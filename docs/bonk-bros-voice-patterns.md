# Bonk Bros Voice Patterns — the "friend, not bullshitting" reference

Calibration doc for the **Friend Test** voice gate (`scripts/friend_test.py`).
Bonk Bros is the north-star reference for dialogue that is *both* trustworthy
and entertaining because it's friends who don't bullshit each other. Reverse-
engineered from verbatim quotes across six episodes (Sea Otter/200th, the Vegan
Cyclist "beef" ep, LeadBoat feed-zone controversy, Unbound/Giardia, Scott's
Portugal recap, Ep.199). Hosts: Dylan "DJ" Johnson, Scott McGill, Tyler
Cloutier, Drew "Dizzle" Dillman, Adam Saban.

Provenance: distilled Jul 2026 from cleaned YouTube auto-transcripts (no speaker
labels; attribution inferred). Quotes are short and verbatim. The point is the
*mechanics*, not the topics.

## 1. The trust mechanics — why it reads as honest

**Conceding a loss without an excuse.** Dylan posted his best-ever power and still bombed, no spin: "I had the best power numbers I've ever had in my life. And I had the worst placing at Sea Otter I've ever had in my life." Pressed with "So you just need to get better" — "Yeah."

**Formal, specific apology when they go too far.** After a rant drew backlash: "Truth without tact is just cruelty... I wanted to apologize for those abrasive words I used last week." No "sorry if you were offended."

**Owning a mistake in exact, embarrassing terms.** "I will eat dicks if you did send the footage that day... I guess I should have followed up, my bad." Specific and low-status — *he* dropped it, not "miscommunication."

**Flagging their own hedges instead of bluffing.** "I could be wrong about this," "I don't actually know how it works," "that's just what the course thing says, but that is dead wrong." They mark the edge of their knowledge instead of performing authority.

**Puncturing their own sponsors' math live.** "The more money you spend, the more money you save with our code... Okay, we get it, we know how math works, dude." Mocking the read they were paid to give makes the ad feel less like a lie.

**Letting a joke go too far, then flagging it.** "No, it's just EPO. I'm on EPO." / "Somebody's going to clip this." / "We might have to edit that part out." The self-catch proves they know where the line is.

**Splitting blame instead of "winning."** After a whole tire-debate segment: "So Dylan's tires were 2 watts faster, but that doesn't account for aero... a wash." / "Well then okay, so then we're both right."

## 2. The entertainment mechanics — why it's fun

**Physical roasting on the spot.** "Show us your dome, Scott." "Dang dude, you look skinhead." "You look so much like Victor Campenaerts right now."

**Running bits with callback payoff.** "Did you say venture that we can endeavor down? ... I'm going to use that from now on. That's good."

**Hyperbolic unrelated analogies mid-argument.** "It's like when you miss a crab when you're running a trotline. You miss one crab? The whole run is screwed." / "How much money would Elon Musk save if he spent all his trillion dollars at Bike Tires Direct?"

**Absurd hypotheticals taken seriously for a beat** (mullet gravel bikes as muscle-car artwork).

**Immediately deflating their own sincerity.** Right after an earnest apology: "I feel like that kind of sums up your whole personality in a nutshell." Sincerity is allowed, never left sitting for more than a few seconds.

**Nickname/costume bits played straight.** Two rivals show up in matching flame shirts by accident: "Flame Hater and Flame Boy in the house."

## 3. The lines they'd never cross

- **Never sell with a straight face for long** — every ad read is undercut within a sentence.
- **Never accept a flex without interrogating it** — a guest's "national championship" claim gets dismantled until he admits "I poach any opportunity to have a flex on social."
- **Never let disagreement curdle into contempt** — "You can be friends with people and disagree... still love you." Loud disagreement, relationship never in question.
- **Never fake certainty to sound authoritative** — they'd rather sound unsure.
- **Never let a mean joke go unflagged** — "somebody's going to clip this" wink separates a bit from an accusation.
- **Never let criticism go unanswered by empathy** — the apology came with a real story, not just contrition.

## 4. Register fingerprint

- **Sentence length:** mostly short, fragmented, interruptive ("Yeah." "Dude." "Okay, cool.") as backchannel; real info arrives in longer run-on bursts once someone gets going.
- **Profanity:** casual intensifiers for emphasis, not shock value; load-bearing on *opinions*.
- **Joke-signaling:** an absurd claim escalated one beat further, then a break ("dang," "oh snap," laughter). The *escalation* is the tell, not a punchline.
- **Transitions:** abrupt, non-sequitur, often self-flagged ("How did we get back on Unbound again?").
- **Filler/tics:** "I don't know," "I mean," "like," "you know" — the opposite of polished copy.
- **Warmth:** shown by immediate return-fire and reassurance after a jab, not by softness.

## 5. Translation to a SINGLE-AUTHOR email voice (Matti writes alone — these are the rules that survive)

1. **Admit the specific failure, not a vague one** — name the exact miss (a plan that flopped, a race you bonked), never generic "we've all been there."
2. **Flag your own pitch before the reader has to** — one aside acknowledging "yes, this is the part where I sell you something" disarms the sales register without dropping the ask.
3. **Hedge in writing, don't bluff** — "I don't actually know if this is right, but here's my read." Works solo; just resist false certainty.
4. **Correct yourself in public, in exact terms** — "I said X last month — that was wrong, here's why," not a quiet revision.
5. **One hyperbolic analogy per email, not a sustained bit** — cross-talk escalation needs two people; a single vivid absurd comparison captures it solo.
6. **Build recurring motifs instead of live callbacks** — seed a recurring phrase/format across a sequence so repeat readers get the callback payoff.
7. **Redirect the roast inward** — no co-host in the room, so self-deprecation absorbs the entertainment function; be the target of your own jokes, never the reader.
8. **Never let sincerity sit unchallenged for long** — after a genuinely earnest beat, a wry aside; unbroken sincerity reads as performance, a self-aware wink reads as a real person.

---

### How the Friend Test uses this
- **Body-Snatcher (voice)** checks an email against §4 register + §1/§2 mechanics — does it sound like this world or like a marketing dept?
- **Tool (tone)** checks against §3 — is it doing something they'd never do (earnest selling, fake certainty, punching at the reader)?
- **Familiarity (premise)** is upstream of Bonk Bros: would this even be *said* — is there a real reason to be talking right now, §4 "real reason to be talking."
