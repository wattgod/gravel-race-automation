# Ultra-Bikepacking Coaching Market & Methodology — Research (2026-07-20)

Deep-research run (102 agents, 3-vote adversarial verification per claim; refuted
claims killed). Purpose: can Gravel God honestly serve ultra prep (Tour Divide class),
and in what product form. Informs docs/specs/road-catalog-migration-spec.md Phase 5.

## Summary

Ultra-bikepacking prep is coached today as long-horizon subscription coaching (6-9+ month engagements) with non-training craft — gear/comfort systems, route and resupply planning, sleep strategy, and pre-scripted mental protocols — packaged INSIDE the coaching product, not as an add-on. The three commercial vendors verified live (Kurt Refsnider/UltraMTB, Peta McSharry/Bikepacking Coach, Samuel Thompson/Acier) converge on the same product ladder: cheap static plans in 4-6 month (never 12-week) formats bundled with substantial prep education, monthly 1:1 coaching at $195-400/month with 3-9 month minimums, and low-priced standalone consults ($45-80 per call) covering the non-training side — proving the consult SKU is an established format. For a gravel coaching business: the durability-training, TrainingPeaks delivery, race-demand-analysis, and weekly-adaptive coaching machinery transfers directly, but the 12-week plan product specifically breaks down (every practitioner's minimum format is 4+ months, and the flagship coach recommends 9 months for Tour Divide because the horizon must also cover route work, gear systems, and mental prep). The honest extension path is: 1:1 coaching yes (with new non-training competence built or partnered in), a longer 4-6 month plan format with a bundled prep guide, and a consult product as the low-cost entry point — not a rebadged 12-week plan. Caveat: the verification process refuted most claims about the ultra-specific physiological training model itself (intensity distribution, sleep-deprivation protocols), so the training-methodology delta remains the least-evidenced part of the picture.

## Verified findings

### F0 [high]
The 12-week plan format is too short for ultra-bikepacking events by every verified practitioner's revealed preference: Refsnider (UltraMTB) sells only 4-month ($65) and 6-month ($75) static plans and requires 4+ weeks of prior base (~5-7 months total build); Peta McSharry (bikepackingcoach.cc) recommends a minimum of 9 months of coaching for Tour Divide; Acier imposes a 6-month minimum commitment on all coaching tiers with annual periodisation; Refsnider's 1:1 coaching has a 3-month minimum as its floor. No verified vendor sells a 12-week ultra product as standalone race prep — bikepackingcoach.cc's only short product (CHF 95 Foundation Track, 3-4 months) is explicitly framed as a bridge into 1:1 coaching, not standalone Tour Divide prep.

Evidence: Verbatim from live pages: "I recommend a minimum of 9 months coaching to prepare for Tour Divide" (McSharry); "4-Month Ultra Training Program - $65.00 ... 6-Month Ultra Training Program - $75.00 ... at least four weeks of general base training before starting any of these programs" (UltraMTB); "Minimum 6 month commitment ... an annual, periodised training plan built around the goal event(s)" (Acier); "$400 per month (No setup fee; 3-month minimum)" (Refsnider coaching). Merged claims 0, 3, 7, 8, 11 (votes 3-0, 3-0, 2-1, 3-0, 3-0). Qualifier from verifiers: the 9-month horizon explicitly covers non-fitness content, so it evidences total preparation horizon, not that 12 weeks of pure training is insufficient for an already-fit athlete; 4 months is only modestly longer than 12 weeks, so the inference leans on the 6-month option and base prerequisite.

Sources: https://bikepackingcoach.cc/race-plan-tour-divide-2027, https://bikepackingcoach.cc/race-plans, https://www.ultramtb.net/trainingplans.html, https://ultramtb.net/coaching/, https://www.acier.cc/full-season

### F1 [high]
Non-training preparation is packaged as a core, structured component of commercial ultra coaching, not an afterthought: McSharry's Tour Divide product delivers 'comfort systems' (bike fit, contact points, hygiene), route research/road book construction, resupply mapping, and pre-written quitting rules / a 'Mental Bedrock Document' via workshops and a planning portal alongside TrainingPeaks training, positioned around the thesis that DNFs are rarely fitness failures; Acier folds event strategy, self-supported logistics, bike setup, equipment, nutrition, and sports psychology inside its single monthly coaching fee; UltraMTB bundles a 60+ page, 9-chapter prep guide (nutrition, mental training, strength, recovery, plus HR/power analyses by bikepacking race style) into its static plans.

Evidence: Verbatim: "The riders who don't finish are rarely the least fit in the field. They're the ones who ran out of mental scaffold"; "Comfort systems — bike fit, contact point protection, hygiene protocols. Pain ends races."; "your quitting rules, written in advance for the version of you that can no longer think straight" (McSharry). "Event strategy, execution, logistics (respecting self-supported principles when applicable) ... Bike setup, equipment choices, nutrition considerations ... Sports psychology" (Acier). "A 60+ page preparation guide ... 9 chapters ... sports nutrition, mental training ... analyses of heart rate and power data from different styles of bikepacking races" (UltraMTB). Merged claims 1, 4, 9, 13 (all 3-0). Note: a stronger version — that non-training prep is 'the core' and training peripheral — was refuted 0-3; the verified position is that they are packaged alongside, not that training is secondary.

Sources: https://bikepackingcoach.cc/race-plan-tour-divide-2027, https://www.acier.cc/full-season, https://www.ultramtb.net/trainingplans.html

### F2 [high]
The commercial landscape has three named, currently-live vendors with a consistent three-rung product ladder — static plan / monthly coaching / one-off consult: (1) Kurt Refsnider (UltraMTB; Tour Divide winner, 2x AZT750 wins, ITI win): PDF plans $65-75, 1:1 coaching $400/mo (3-mo min, weekly email, monthly call, TrainingPeaks review) but roster FULL for 2026, consults $45/30min or $80/60min; (2) Peta McSharry (bikepackingcoach.cc; BC L3, Tour Divide + Silk Road finisher): 1:1 at CHF 195-345/mo over 9-12 months plus CHF 95 Foundation Track; (3) Samuel Thompson (Acier): Full Season £175/€200/mo and Stainless £260/€300/mo, both 6-mo minimum, consults £30-50. All three deliver training through TrainingPeaks-style subscription coaching, none through fixed-length plan-only products for the flagship races.

Evidence: All prices verified verbatim on live pages fetched 2026-07-20. Refsnider: "$400 per month (No setup fee; 3-month minimum)", "Kurt is not currently taking on any new athletes as his coaching roster is full for 2026" — evidence the segment is supply-constrained at the credible-practitioner level. Merged claims 2, 3, 8, 11, 12 (votes 3-0 except one 2-1). Caveat: McSharry pricing detail came from a claim that failed verification 1-2 on some specifics but was corroborated at the tier level by the verifier of claim 0 (Fundamental CHF 195/mo, Advanced CHF 295-345/mo on bikepackingcoach.cc/race-plans).

Sources: https://ultramtb.net/coaching/, https://ultramtb.net/consulting/, https://www.ultramtb.net/trainingplans.html, https://ultramtb.net/about/, https://bikepackingcoach.cc/race-plans, https://www.acier.cc/full-season, https://www.acier.cc/coaching-consultations

### F3 [high]
A low-cost standalone consult is an established commercial format for ultra prep, run by at least two independent vendors: Refsnider sells 'race prep & adventure consultations' at $45/30min or $80/60min covering route/race logistics, gear, bike setup, race strategy, nutrition, mental prep, and body maintenance, with clients typically buying 2-4 calls layered on top of his static plans; Acier sells consults at £30/€35 per 30min or £50/€60 per hour covering event selection, bike setup, in-event maintenance, equipment, event nutrition, psychological prep, route familiarisation, and pre-event logistics.

Evidence: Verbatim: "Consulting rates are $45 for 30 minutes or $80 for 60 minutes"; "I frequently do between 2 and 4 calls with folks looking to tailor the training plans"; "These consultations can be a fantastic addition to preparation for folks following one of my training plans" (Refsnider). "£30 / €35 for 1/2 hour consultation ... £50 / €60 for 1 hour consultation" (Acier). Merged claims 5, 6, 10, 12 (votes 2-1, 3-0, 3-0, 3-0). Qualifier: both consult menus also include training topics, so consults are not purely non-training; and the cross-sell pattern (consult layered on plan) is as important as the standalone framing.

Sources: https://ultramtb.net/consulting/, https://www.acier.cc/coaching-consultations

### F4 [medium]
Ultra races demand competencies with no equivalent in single-day gravel racing, treated by the community as discrete learnable skills: in-race sleep strategy (DotWatcher publishes a dedicated guide — bivvy/hotel selection, no-booking-ahead rules, pre-race sleep-system testing) and free-route navigation/route planning (TCR riders design their own route between controls 500-1,200km apart; DotWatcher built a guide around TCRNo8 3rd-place finisher Pawel Pulawski's approach). The race-demand profile is also categorically different: Tour Divide prep is framed around daily repeatability — e.g., averaging 100 miles and 5,550 ft of climbing per day, every day, for 24 days on a loaded bike — rather than single-day performance.

Evidence: Verbatim: "Sleeping on an ultra-distance race is often a mystery to the uninitiated ... how to sleep during a bikepacking race" (DotWatcher, Kitty Dennis guide); "what it takes to plan a free-route for an ultra-race with 3rd place finisher of TCRNo8" (DotWatcher/Pulawski); "I'll need to average riding 100-miles and 5,550 ft of elevation gain per day on a loaded bicycle" (Swallow, 24-day target, math verified against route totals). All 3-0 votes, but sources are secondary/editorial (DotWatcher) and a self-coached racer's blog (Swallow), not coach methodology — hence medium. Counter-signal from refuted claims: an elite record-setter's (Leveika) sleep management was verified as improvised real-time decision-making, and the claim that it is a pre-plannable protocol failed; sleep strategy transfers as a prep-and-adapt skill, not a script.

Sources: https://dotwatcher.cc/features/guides, https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/follow-your-own-adventure, https://sarahjswallow.com/stories/2021/5/18/how-i-am-preparing-for-the-tour-divide

### F5 [medium]
Verdict for the business decision: a competent gravel-endurance practice can honestly extend to ultra prep via (a) 1:1 coaching — the delivery machinery (TrainingPeaks, monthly subscription, weekly-adaptive feedback, durability focus, race-demand analysis) matches exactly what verified ultra coaches use, and the market is supply-constrained (top coach's roster full); (b) a 4-6 month plan format bundled with a prep-education guide (the proven UltraMTB template at $65-75, i.e., a premium-priced longer SKU is plausible); and (c) a $45-80 consult SKU as low-cost entry. What does NOT transfer: the 12-week plan format itself, and the non-training competence stack (pack/sleep systems, resupply and route logistics, multi-day mental protocols) which must be built, hired, or credibly sourced — every verified vendor's credibility rests on the coach having personally finished or won these races.

Evidence: Synthesis finding — inferred from the verified product structures rather than stated by any source. Supporting pattern: all three vendors converge on subscription coaching + long plans + consults; all three coaches are themselves ultra finishers/winners (Refsnider: Tour Divide 1x win; McSharry: Tour Divide/Silk Road finisher; Thompson: ultra racer), suggesting founder credibility is a load-bearing part of the offer. The training-model transfer claim is the weakest link: claims asserting a distinct ~100-hour 'fatigue resistance' construct beyond gravel durability were refuted 0-3, as were claims that standard 80/20 polarized doctrine transfers unchanged (0-3) — so the actual physiological delta between gravel-durability training and ultra training was not established either way by this research.

Sources: https://ultramtb.net/coaching/, https://bikepackingcoach.cc/race-plan-tour-divide-2027, https://www.acier.cc/full-season, https://www.ultramtb.net/trainingplans.html
