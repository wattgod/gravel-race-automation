# Social Creative Direction — "inform them and make them smile"

Synthesis of the Jul 2026 research (meme-native brand playbook + cycling
social landscape). Matti's thesis, verbatim: value = "inform them and make
them smile... impishly, with cards and memes — the language of the
internet." And: "If a brand I find entertaining, aspirational, or informing
me without rubbing sales in my face, I'll buy from them without asking."

## The one-line playbook

**Be Merriam-Webster with Letterboxd's architecture and Wendy's target
list, wearing Liquid Death's straight face — and never be Duolingo.**

## Why brands get loved (the mechanism, from the research)

The accounts that LASTED (Nat'l Park Service, Merriam-Webster, Ryanair,
Letterboxd) all run one engine: **authority delivering truth with a smirk,
where the joke carries real information.** The data is the punchline, not
the decoration. Chaos-based accounts (Duolingo) decay into stunt
dependence; budget-based ones (Liquid Death) are un-copyable solo.
Brand-love = repeated small proofs that the brand gives value without
asking — the sale happens because it was never demanded (Matti's own
purchase behavior, stated verbatim above).

## The formula (NPS caption structure, proven solo-operator scale <1M→6M)

**joke hook → real information → resource, released with a smile.**
Every post passes the two-question gate: did they learn something real?
did it make them smile? Both or don't post.

## What we uniquely own (white space — nobody does this)

1. **Score reveals** — "We scored [hyped race]. It got a 61." Gap between
   hype and score = built-in mild controversy with receipts. A search for
   honest race review database returns ONLY gravelgodcycling.com.
2. **Ranked carousels** — "most overrated vs most underrated," "T3 races
   charging T1 prices," "best races nobody's heard of." Carousels = ~10%
   engagement, 3x saves vs Reels; rankings force comments.
3. **RIDERS SAY receipt cards** — rider_intel quotes vs the race's own
   marketing copy (Letterboxd screenshot-culture, ours to build).
4. **Countdown utility** — "opens Tuesday, sells out in 4 hours, scored 84."
5. **The honest counter-voice** — every race account is a hype machine;
   the Wirecutter-of-races position is structurally vacant. Holy Spirit of
   Gravel (~7K, outsized influence) proves the irreverent-commentary
   audience exists; nobody has data behind it.

## The Nihilist Arby's mechanic (Matti-requested addition)

@nihilist_arbys (Brendan Kelly, 2015 — a PARODY, not Arby's; the brand
wisely embraced it with sandwiches and a puppy instead of lawyers). Two
things it proves that we steal:

1. **A rigid comedic formula compounds.** Every post is identical
   architecture: bleak cosmic observation → flat "Eat Arby's." The FORMAT
   is the mascot — followers show up for the next iteration of the same
   joke. We steal the ARCHITECTURE only: **hook → real information →
   number/verdict**, same shape every time, recognizable at scroll speed.
2. **The ANGLE is taken (Matti's ruling, Jul 2026).** Nihilist-deadpan is
   Brendan Kelly's owned territory — wearing it would be borrowed
   personality, the exact cringe the email council was built to kill.
   The rigid formula runs in MATTI'S registers instead: math-as-weapon,
   racing-as-reality-test ("200 miles of Flint Hills tells you the
   truth"), warm irreverence, permission, inversion. His Substack
   memento-mori register is adjacent but it's HIS version — use his,
   never Kelly's costume.

Corollary from Arby's-the-brand: if parody/roast accounts ever aim at us,
feed them — never fight them. The gift-bag response outperformed any ad.

## Cycling's shared joke vocabulary (use these, they're canon)

Suffering-as-identity · N+1/financial-ruin math · the dentist bike (mock
wallets, never people) · Strava-as-scoreboard-of-the-soul · mid-pack
self-roast (always "we", never "you") · gravel-vs-roadie costume jokes ·
satire of rules-culture gatekeeping (never actual gatekeeping).

## Hard guardrails

- **Punch at hype, prices, and marketing copy — never riders, volunteers,
  or communities.** Every roast resolves to a score we can defend
  (Wendy's fresh-never-frozen rule).
- **Wit, not chaos.** 45-54 is the largest money cohort; flaw-forward
  chaos indexes to Gen Z (76% vs 43% boomer preference). Dylan Johnson /
  Phil Gaimon register = the ceiling. Deadpan-dry also keeps a serious
  gear (Duolingo's chaos couldn't switch registers and it burned them).
- **Never break the neo-brutalist straight face** — the design IS the
  deadpan (our Liquid Death skull can). The copy gets to be funny because
  the frame never smiles.
- **Never self-roast the database** (it's the trust asset). Self-deprecate
  only about business smallness (Surreal's "our mum said we should grow up").
- Credit community jokes always; comprehension-test recurring bits
  (must land for someone who's never seen the site); sincerity posts mixed
  in — expert who's funny, not comedian with a spreadsheet.

## Format mix (IG)

2-3 Reels/wk (reach engine, non-follower ~33%) + 1-2 carousels/wk
(depth/saves) + quip cards as the workhorse + reply-guy energy
opportunistically. Solo-sustainable cadence = a few excellent posts weekly
(NPS model), NOT chaos-brand volume.

## Card v2 direction

Keep the neo-brutalist frame; the QUIP becomes the star — a deadpan
one-liner under the giant score, written per-race (the low-score verdicts
in race-data are templated and not funny; quips are authored, the score
provides the deadpan authority). Prototype:
Scratch Ankle · 36 · "It's cheap, it's pleasant, it's gravel. Do not book
a flight." Quips are OPINION → they flow through the human approval queue,
not auto-publish, per the automation boundary.
