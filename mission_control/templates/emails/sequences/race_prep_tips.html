Race prep isn't just training — it's everything between now and the start line. Here are three things most riders overlook:

1. Pre-ride the nutrition plan. Your long rides should test your race-day fueling strategy. If you're taking in 80g of carbs per hour on race day, you need to train your gut for that load weeks in advance.
2. Know the terrain before you see it. Study the course profile. Identify the climbs where you'll need to conserve and the flats where you can push. Having a pacing plan beats winging it every time.
3. Taper with intention. The final two weeks aren't about squeezing in more fitness. They're about arriving fresh. Reduce volume by 40%, keep intensity short and sharp.
