1. Your gut is trainable, and almost nobody trains it. If you plan to eat 80g of carbs an hour on race day, you need to have practiced 80g an hour on long rides — weeks out, repeatedly. Race day is the worst possible time to discover your stomach's opinion on the matter.

2. Make the pacing plan before you see the course. Study the profile. Decide where you'll be patient and where you'll spend. The riders who blow up at mile 80 almost always made that decision at mile 20.

3. The taper is not bonus training. The last two weeks aren't for cramming fitness — that ship has sailed. Cut volume around 40%, keep a little sharpness, arrive bored and fresh. Bored and fresh wins.
