Race week is where good training goes to die. Your fitness is locked in — it was decided weeks ago. The only thing left to win this week is arriving rested, organized, and calm. And yet.

Things people do during race week that I am begging you not to do:

One more hard session. Thursday's “fitness test” doesn't add fitness. It just spends Saturday's legs. And if you feel sluggish on an easy spin mid-week — that's the taper working, not fitness leaving. Heavy legs on Thursday are normal.

A massage on Friday. If deep tissue work isn't already part of your routine, the day before a race is a strange time to start. Book it for the Monday after, when you'll have earned it.

New equipment. New tires, new saddle, new bag. Every “upgrade” is an experiment, and race day is the worst laboratory.

Sleep banking the wrong night. You can't sleep 12 hours Friday and fix a bad week — sleep works cumulatively. Aim for 8–9 hours every night starting Monday. The night before, everyone sleeps badly. That one matters least.
