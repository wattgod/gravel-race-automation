A question with a numeric answer: how many gels does a 7-hour gran fondo actually require?

Most riders would guess eight. The arithmetic says roughly 21: 75g of carbohydrate per hour for 7 hours is 525 grams. Most start with four and a plan to “grab something at the feed zones.”

WHAT THE DATA SAYS

Trained guts absorb 60–90g of carbohydrate per hour; untrained guts considerably less, and they announce it. Absorption capacity is trainable — the intestinal transporters that move carbs out of your stomach roughly double their throughput after about two weeks of consistent practice at target rates. Glycogen stores alone hold about 90 minutes of race-pace work. The rest of your day is imported.

WHAT IT MEANS FOR YOU

The bonk at kilometer 140 is usually decided at kilometer 20, when the pace was easy and eating felt unnecessary. Fuel on a clock, not on appetite — appetite is a lagging indicator. Practice your race-day intake on long training rides, starting weeks out, at 40–50g/hr and building. Race on exactly what you trained on. The feed-zone tables are not a plan (they are a lottery).

KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. Do the arithmetic for your race's expected duration.
2. Train your gut like you train your legs — progressively, on a schedule.
3. Nothing new on race day. Nothing.
