Race week adds nothing. It can only subtract. Your fitness was decided weeks ago; the remaining job is arriving with it intact. Accordingly, a list of don'ts:

Don't test your fitness. Thursday's “openers that became a full effort” doesn't measure anything except how Saturday will now feel (worse). Heavy legs on an easy mid-week spin are the taper working, not fitness leaving.

Don't debut equipment. New tires, new saddle, new chainring, new shoes. Each one is an experiment, and the race is the worst possible laboratory.

Don't get your first-ever massage on Friday. If deep tissue work isn't already routine, the day before a race is a strange time to introduce it. Book it for Monday, as a reward.

Don't bank sleep on Friday night. You can't. The night that matters is two nights out; Friday-night insomnia before a race is normal and costs less than you fear.

Don't carb-load with foods you've never eaten. The fueling rule has a race-week corollary: nothing new at the pasta party either.

The one do: make your pacing plan while you're still rational, then obey it while you're not. Your race's profile marks where patience pays: the database.
