Week 1 is a measurement week. There is exactly one way to fail it: adding effort the plan didn't ask for.

Targets are targets, not floors. 200W for 10 minutes means 200, not 220-because-it-felt-good. Overshooting doesn't accelerate the plan; it corrupts the baseline every later week is computed from.

“Too easy” is the expected reading. The load rises from here, and rises correctly because the starting point was measured honestly. Exception worth reporting: if prescribed Zone 2 watts feel genuinely hard, reply — that's a zones problem, and week 1 is the cheap time to fix it.

Rest days are load-bearing. Adaptation happens during recovery, not during work. The plan says rest, you rest. This is the instruction most often ignored and most expensive to ignore.

Proper check-in at the end of week 2. Until then, replies are read — “done, felt fine” is a complete report.
