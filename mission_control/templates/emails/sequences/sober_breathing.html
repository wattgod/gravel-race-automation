When panic or pain spikes mid-race, your breathing shortens and your decisions get worse. The countermeasure is mechanical: inhale for 6 seconds, hold for 2, exhale for 7. The long exhale stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system — the same system that slows your heart rate when you sleep. Three to five cycles is usually enough to get your head back.

Two rules. First, practice it on easy rides now — mile 100 is a bad place to try anything for the first time. Second, pair it with a pre-planned phrase. When “I can't do this” shows up, your brain treats it as permission to quit; replace it with direction — “smooth circles,” “fuel now, decide later.” Focus on execution rather than pain.
