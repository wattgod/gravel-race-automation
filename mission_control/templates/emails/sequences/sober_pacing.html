Every long gravel race follows the same three-act structure. The terrain varies; the pattern does not.

Act one, the opening 10–15%: chaos. Fresh legs, nervous energy, a crowded course. The race cannot be won here, but it can be lost here. Your job is restraint.

Act two, the middle 50–60%: order returns. Groups stabilize and the pace starts to feel manageable, sometimes easy. Do not be fooled — this is the setup for act three. Your job is rhythm: fuel on schedule, hold your numbers, make no emotional decisions.

Act three, the final 25–30%: the bill comes due. Under-fueled riders go from holding 18mph to struggling at 12. If you did acts one and two correctly, this is where you pass them. Your job is resolve.

One sentence to keep: survive act one, execute act two, capitalize in act three.
