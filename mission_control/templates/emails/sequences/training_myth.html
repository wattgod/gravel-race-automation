The most common training advice for gravel riders is also the worst: "Just ride more."

Volume matters. But unstructured volume is how you end up 16 weeks into a training block with the same FTP you started with. You've been riding a lot. You haven't been getting faster.

What actually drives gravel fitness:

Specificity. If your race has 8,000 feet of climbing, your hardest workouts should simulate repeated climbs at race pace — not flat interval sets on a trainer.

Periodization. Your body adapts to stress, then needs new stress. A plan that progressively overloads and then recovers is what turns base fitness into race fitness.

Recovery that's actually recovery. Zone 2 on a recovery week means zone 2. Not "easy but I felt good so I pushed it." If your recovery weeks look like your build weeks, you're not recovering.

This is what a structured plan gives you — every workout designed for the race you're actually doing.
