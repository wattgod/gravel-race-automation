# 114 GRAVEL RACE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Course Details

**First UCI Gravel Event Connecting Two Countries**

The 114 Gravel Race is unique as the first UCI Gravel World Series event connecting two countries. The race starts in Elvas (Alentejo) in Portugal and finishes across the Spanish border in Badajoz (Extremadura). 

**Course Profile:**
- **Distance:** 123km (some sources mention 120+ km)
- **Elevation:** 864m total elevation gain
- **Terrain:** 65% gravel, 35% paved roads
- **Surface:** Fast gravel roads, farmland, sandy trails along the Guadiana River

**Route Description:**
- First 20km on Portuguese soil starting from Parque da Piedade in Elvas
- Crosses Portuguese-Spanish border after 20km near Badajoz
- 100km loop south of the finish location
- Course crosses towns of Valverde de Leganes, Saint Raphael of Olivenza, Saint Francis of Olivenza, and Villarreal
- Finish in Guadiana Park on the right side of the Puente de las Palmas in Badajoz
- Major climbs in the middle of the course
- Final 30km are flat

**Specific Climbs:**
- Short 1km climb at 6-7% average starting after 1.4km from the start
- Longest climb: 4km at km30 with 4-5% average gradient
- Course located between 300m (start) and 150m (finish) above sea level
- No major sustained climbs

**Race Report:**

**Matthew Holmes [ELITE]** used his former road professional experience to claim victory in a sprint finish against Jose Maria Sanchez Ruiz after 3 hours and 13 minutes. The finish was extremely close with only a 1-second gap.

**Lucía González Blanco [ELITE]** "Very unexpected. I felt so bad out there. I was dropped on the first main climb. The two girls went away and somehow I found some legs and just got into pursuit mode. I caught Talia and I thought 'ok cool, I'm in it for the second' and then I did not expect to catch Casia and it was literally in the single track that we came together." González Blanco carved out a small gap to runner-up Annabel Fisher within the final five kilometres, winning by just 21 seconds after 3 hours, 36 minutes and 14 seconds.

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Participation Stats:**
515 riders registered from 30 different countries for the 2025 event. Notable participants included former MotoGP star Aleix Espargaro alongside gravel specialists like Annabel Fisher, Lucia Gonzalez, Freddy Ovett, Jose Maria and Mario Fernandez.

**Cultural Context:**
The race showcases the medieval charm of Elvas (UNESCO World Heritage List for its exceptional fortifications) and the historic city of Badajoz (founded in 875 with its massive Alcazaba fortress). The course passes through "seemingly untouched nature reserves and farmed pastures" with scenic views of the archipelago region.

(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/114-gravel-race-first-uci-gravel-event-connecting-two-countries/, https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/elvas/)

## UCI Gravel World Series Context

The 114 Gravel Race serves as a qualifying event for the UCI Gravel World Championships. In the 2025 series, it was held on March 29, 2025 (sixth qualifying round for the 2025 Championships). For 2026, it's scheduled for March 28, 2026.

**Qualification Rules:**
- Top 25% of finishers in each age group category qualify for the World Championships
- Qualification based on number of starters, not finishers
- First three in all categories qualify regardless of field size
- Elite riders must qualify in Elite category (finishing top of age group doesn't count if entered as Elite)
- Requires full UCI license issued by National cycling federation
- Adequate liability insurance required
