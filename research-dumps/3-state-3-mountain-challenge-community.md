## Weather Experienced

The Cycling Addiction blogger: "As soon as I turned on the road my legs began to burn like crazy as the sun suddenly comes out and began baking down on my neck." (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**2007 ride:** Delayed start due to thunderstorm, wet first hour, then sun came out. (https://www.bikeforums.net/southeast/498360-what-were-your-best-rides-2008-a.html)

**Rain frequency:** indysteel notes "Rain is often an issue that time of year" (early May), mentioning they "got rained out" one year and a friend had an accident when "another rider's brakes failed on a descent" in the rain. (http://forums.teamestrogen.com/showthread.php?t=51493)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Climb sequence:** The 100-mile route includes three major climbs: Suck Mountain (first), Sand Mountain (second), and Burkhalter Gap (final). (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Suck Mountain:** "5-mile climb with a typical gradient of 4% to 6%." Described as "wasn't that bad" by one rider. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Sand Mountain:** "Shorter and steeper than the first." One rider noted: "I don't remember much about the climb" suggesting it's challenging but passes in a blur. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Burkhalter Gap approach:** "A straight two-mile section of road before hitting the wall." The Cycling Addiction blogger: "The gradient didn't look all that steep, but with this climb located 80 miles into the ride I believe most cyclist like myself were becoming pretty fatigued." (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Burkhalter Gap final section:** "20% to 22% road gradient for the last 500 yards." With cowbells and cheers from spectators. Multiple riders seen walking. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Post-Burkhalter Gap:** "As soon as you pull out of the sag stop you are immediately faced with another climb that looks pretty bad, but as I climbed this hill felt pretty insignificant compared to what we just encountered." (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**First 40 miles:** "Flatter than I remember it being. The first 40 miles is flatter than I remember it being. Oh, to have flat stretches like that around here. It's not often that I get to cruise at 20 mph or so for that long." (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

**Lookout Mountain descent:** "I tried my best to keep my speeds from reaching the 40's on the wet roadway." Reaches speeds in the 40s mph on descent. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Rolling terrain after climbs:** "The rolling stretch atop Lookout Mountain has more climbing than I recalled. Or my legs were just tired." (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

**Marion County section:** "That Marion County mud hole sure did a number on my cleats." (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

**Alabama roads:** "Alabama roads still suck." (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**The Cycling Addiction blogger [UNKNOWN]:** "I kept thinking about what Gary had told me a ton of times. Do your own pace and don't use up too much too early. I had the gps map zoomed in fairly tight and I had a summit symbol preloaded so I had an idea that I was getting closer." Advice for Burkhalter Gap at mile 80+. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**The Cycling Addiction blogger [UNKNOWN]:** "After spending a few minutes chunking down some fruits and cookies at the sag stop my legs were now calmed down and my group patiently waited for me to regain a little strength." Post-Burkhalter Gap recovery strategy. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**The Cycling Addiction blogger [UNKNOWN]:** "A lot of people go too hard climbing the first two hills and do not leave enough in the tank for the last one. Pace yourself early and leave something for climbing Burkhalter Gap." Advice given to a Florida rider preparing for the event. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Finish time [UNKNOWN]:** Velo Vol: "I finished in roughly 6:08 of riding time" for the 100-mile route. (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Mass start:** "2,000+ riders makes for a slow start. I don't think I got much above 10 mph until the 3rd mile." (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

**Burkhalter Gap spectators:** "I could hear the cowbells and cheers from those in front" and "cheers from those on the other side of the road" during the final climb. (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**City finish:** "As we entered Chattanooga the police had the intersections blocked off and we were sustaining 20 mph speeds though the traffic. As we entered the stadium parking area the ride officials were getting our numbers to register our ride times there were people lining both sides of the road cheering and from the crowd I began to distinguish voices of friends cheering as well." (https://thecyclingaddiction.blogspot.com/2008/05/3-state-3-mountain-challenge.html)

**Traffic management:** "By and large, they do a great job managing traffic (though I'd feel differently if I was waiting at an intersection in a vehicle)." (https://www.bikeforums.net/4381823-post47.html)

**Organizer:** "Organized by the Chattanooga Bicycle Club" with partnership from "Litespeed Bicycles and George Hincapie" for the 30th Anniversary. (https://wheelbrothers.com/3-state-3-mountain-challenge/)

**KOM/QOM Competition:** "All Century riders participate. Male & female winners receive LITESPEED T5 frames!" (https://wheelbrothers.com/3-state-3-mountain-challenge/)

**Event longevity:** Described as "This is the 26th year for the event, which speaks well for it" in 2013. (http://forums.teamestrogen.com/showthread.php?t=51493)

**Race description:** Pedal Wench: "It's a wonderful ride, great support, and really interesting route." (http://forums.teamestrogen.com/showthread.php?t=51493)

**Distance options:** "Choose the Century Challenge with 6883' of climbing or take on the 64 mile course with 4295' of elevation. The 25 mile route tours 2 states with a profile that is more beginner friendly." (https://wheelbrothers.com/3-state-3-mountain-challenge/)

**Event status:** YouTube description notes "The 3 State 3 Mountain challenge was a great bike ride, fondo, cycle sportive, or whatever you want to call it, in Chattanooga Tennessee that shut down in 2017." (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jgMCnqLWS5o)
