# 6 HOURS OF SYLLAMO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Travis Liles [UNKNOWN]:** "Solid start to the year! I wont go into all the details but I kept things in control, had a great time, ran solid, and did not puke once." Describing the 3 Days of Syllamo running event. (https://lilestrials.com/blog/race-report-3-days-of-syllamo)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "I really like to race mountain bikes. I like it enough that I'd rather suffer than quit most times. I'm sure this has something more to do with pride or ego than it has to do with lust for racing. Or maybe I'm just a foolish girl." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "Syllamo's Revenge, a 50 mile marathon and a 125K NUE race that takes place in the stunning Ozark Hill Country surrounding Mountain View, Arkansas. Known as the Fiddle capital of the World, Mountain View isn't exactly a tourist town." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "Rain the day before would set the tone for the race... the Montgomery Bell trail is also great for a championship race because it drains really well and holds up nicely to traffic in wet conditions. However, since 99% of the trails I ride don't follow that rule... I don't often get the opportunity to ride wet roots and occasional greasy corners." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on wet conditions:** "Holy crap, I felt like I was walking on ice. Every rock, every root had a wet slimy surface from the morning dew and it was almost impossible to even walk on it, especially if it was off-camber. F-get about it. So I just hiked as quickly as I could until the trail smoothed out. It was this way for a good mile. On-off. On-off. Cuss-cuss, lift the bike." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "The trail itself is made of stones and gravel. There's not much dirt really so I was constantly on guard, trying to navigate my way to the first check point. I got there around 1:30, about the same as last year, so that was a good feeling." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "At one point I had to stop when I saw a group go one way even though some trail markers were up a few feet off the trail to my left. I just happened to see them so I stopped and pointed it out to a group that was close behind... It turned out the trail hooked back up to trail we were just on, only it was a piece of shit with down trees." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on Syllamo's character:** "These are trails that can chew up and spit out even the most well-prepared rider and equipment. If you can ride there, you can ride anywhere... It's 50 miles of the most remote, rocky, and beautiful singletrack that I've come across in my relatively few years of riding off-road." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on Syllamo rock:** "The shale that makes up the rock gardens is unique to the area and is a challenge in and of itself in two ways: 1) It's sharp as all getout and will rip holes in even the toughest sidewalls, and 2) when it gets wet, it's quite possibly one of the slickest surfaces known to man." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "It's impossible to go to the Syllamo trails and ride 'easy,' because it's a constant challenge in some way, shape, or form. No matter how strong, skilled, and lucky you are, the Syllamo Trails can crush you and/or your equipment. That's why I love them more than any trails I've ever visited." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on Syllamo's Revenge race:** "I felt awesome, had a great start, and was totally rocking the lead. I had a rear flat super early, but didn't sweat it too hard since it was 15 minutes in to a 5-6 hour day. However, getting back into a race on a narrow, technical bench trail is super hard and ends up being physically and mentally exhausting because you have to yell and beg and punch your way around a couple of hundred people in conga-line groups of 3-10 at a time." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on wet rocks:** "Basically, in those spots, you'll spin-coast your brains out while geared riders go flying past you in their biggest gear... I realized that all off my practice and learning how to ride the rocky sections were in vain… the rain overnight had left them incredibly slick." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on technical riding:** "I made it through a few tough spots, but soon took a couple of hard falls that shook my confidence a bit. If there's one thing I've learned about rocks, it's that you can't hesitate or be indecisive when it comes to riding them." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on the White River Bluff section:** "With the exception of a couple of switchbacks and the rocks along the White River Bluff, it's pretty flowy... Matt called us the fail train" after she and several riders walked the entire bluff section. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on the climbing section:** "You have to be in the right mindset for the next sections of trail... The trail goes up. It gets steep and impossibly rocky. It includes stairs (made of rocks). It's not really that long of a climb distance-wise, but it can break you if you let it. I passed several racers who were sitting on the side of the trail looking as if they didn't know whether to keep going or to fling themselves back down the hill." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "Even though the red loop is not technical, rocky, or steep, its length (~13 miles) nearly drives some people insane. I forced myself to not look at my bike computer and to just keep hammering as best I could." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "60 miles of gravel, road, huge rocks and some of the sweetest bench-cut single-track I've ever ridden." Describing the Ouachita Challenge, which shares terrain with Syllamo area trails. (http://www.markgullett.com/2012/04/2012-ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "I may never crack that top 5 but I'll keep coming back and trying. Big thanks to the friendly/helpful volunteers at the aid stations and road crossings." On the Ouachita Challenge, noting the importance of aid station support. (http://www.markgullett.com/2012/04/2012-ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "the most well-prepared rider and equipment. I get the same vibe from the Pisgah-loving people out East. It's a mix of respect (for the trail and its ability to destroy) and an undercurrent of the self-confidence gained from successfully riding trails that cause a disproportionately large percentage of the people who visit to swear they'll never come back." (http://www.markgullett.com/2010/03/2010-spa-city-6-hour-race-recap.html)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on tire pressure:** "I probably had too much air in my tires but ya walk a fine line in these conditions. Low enough to grab but not so low that you pinch flat. I opted for more air but since I couldn't ride most of the technical stuff anyway it worked out." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on mechanicals:** "So many riders were trail side fixing flats and other things." Noting the prevalence of flat tires during the race. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on creek crossings and drivetrain:** "When I got to the Hwy 5 Aid Station, Todd H. was there with my drop bag (including a spare derailleur!) and some chain lube. I refilled, had a snack, then headed on my way up the hill." Noting the need for drivetrain maintenance mid-race. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] on creek strategy:** "The next bit of trail was the orange and blue down to the first Livingston Creek and Hwy 5 crossing... those crossings were very sandy, so even though they were rideable, I carried my bike across to keep the drivetrain from getting fouled up." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE]:** "I stopped and laid my bike down in the creek in order to clean out the drivetrain" during the race. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

## Weather Experienced

**2013 - Rain before race:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] reported: "Rain the day before would set the tone for the race" and "morning dew" made rocks and roots extremely slippery. Also noted "the morning was very foggy and humid, both of which are results of water in the air." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**2013 - Race day warming:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "Around 7am, Rafal was off with the 125K racers. Thirty minutes later, it was our turn" suggesting a 7:30am start with cool morning conditions that warmed throughout the day. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Syllamo's Revenge race (year unclear) - Rain aftermath:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] reported rain overnight before the race, leaving rocks "incredibly slick." She also noted being stung by something on her forehead and getting "small slices all over our arms and legs from the weeds." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Post-race weather:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The next day, Matt and I built a fire pit and rode forest roads for a couple of hours" suggesting variable spring weather in the Syllamo area. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course overview:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] described Syllamo's Revenge as "50 miles of singletrack" with multiple named trail sections including Orange, Blue, Yellow, Red, and Green trails. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Opening climb:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The opening climb is such a dick waving contest. I dreaded it... Spring rains caused large ruts half-way up and I didn't want to get stuck or wedged into one. As we approached the climb, our 10-rider wide wave narrowed to half the size in order to make the sharp right turn up the service road." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**First technical section:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "Within the first mile, a sharp 180 up loose, wet rock had many off the bike. I yelled out slowing so that the bulls behind me wouldn't take me out. I managed to ride up the first few steps and past a couple of hikers but then it got rooty and tight and I spun out." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Overgrowth issues:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The next section was deeper into the woods. Spots of the trail were over grown and I, along with everyone else, had small slices all over our arms and legs from the weeds. One got caught in my mouth and pleasantly sliced my upper lip as I rode 15mph down a hill." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Stairway to Heaven:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] mentioned "the 'Stairway to Heaven' an absolute hike a bike unless you were Danny Macaskill" as a notorious section. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Deadfall:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "by the time I'd gone down the first descent, I'd stopped and hauled my bike over at least 5 downed trees. It was tedious to say the least." During a night ride recon. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Trail composition:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The terrain includes steep stuff, flowy stuff, and lots of rock gardens." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Specific trail sections from race report:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] described the course starting "with about 8 miles of pavement/gravel to the Big Brushy section of the Ouachita Trail," followed by "Blowout mountain" which she called "the hardest of the day," then "Chalybeate Mountain" with significant climbing. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Rock gardens on Blowout:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The shear size and number of them just beat me into submission. But I survived it, refueled at the aid station and started making my way up Chalybeate Mountain." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Road sections between trails:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "As I was fiddling with my bike I was jockeying back and forth with Corey Godfrey... When we popped out on the gravel road for an 8 mile ride to the Womble I told him I'd like to work together so we could make good time on the road." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**The Womble trail:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "As we approached the Womble trail Corey said to go on because he was starting to cramp, so I took off, feeling as good as I'd felt all day." About 5 miles into the Womble she caught other riders. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Start positioning:** "I lined up about 5 rows back. One pro woman, Andrea Wilson, was on the front row, a single speeder who was a machine! Not much fan fare and we were off to races, as they say." Also: "Within the first mile, a sharp 180 up loose, wet rock had many off the bike." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Checkpoint timing:** "I got there around 1:30, about the same as last year, so that was a good feeling. I grabbed a couple of oranges filled up a bottle and was off." At first checkpoint. Later: "After I made it to check 2, I wasn't even half-way done. That can really reap havoc on the brain." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Riding solo strategy:** "During the whole race I wasn't really ever alone. I'd go back and forth with riders, drop some, catch others and vice versa." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Navigation issues:** "So I'm wondering if I'm going the right way. I don't see anyone. I saw the leader of the 125k leave on another trail but I thought it was b/c he was doing the 125 and would be going a different way. I see a photographer and felt relieved." She had asked "which way to the end for the 50 miler" which was "the WRONG question to ask." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Race pace and finish:** "I was riding pretty well and feeling strong. I hadn't seen any women until at one point" suggesting she was riding near the front of the women's field. Her finish time and exact placement are not specified in the excerpt. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Flat recovery strategy:** "I felt awesome, had a great start, and was totally rocking the lead. I had a rear flat super early, but didn't sweat it too hard since it was 15 minutes in to a 5-6 hour day." She ended up flatting twice and eventually DNF'd, making "the decision to cut my losses based on the proximity of TSE and how hard I would have chased & destroyed myself trying to get back to a podium spot." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Power data from early climb:** "I averaged 301 watts for 8 minutes going up the first climb, and wasn't blown up at the top" before her mechanical issues began. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Confidence management:** "Every time I'd approach the wet rocks, I'd tense up a bit and mentally prep myself to stop and unclip if I needed to. That, of course, led to stalling and unclipping. It was like taking 50 steps back from where I'd worked myself up to as far as technical skill." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Andrea Wilson [ELITE] - Red loop pacing:** "I settled in at a good effort and just stayed in it for about 45 miles running with friends and chatting. I hit the last 5 hard" during what appears to be a different Syllamo-area event. Finished in 6:04 for first place. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Flat tires - critical risk:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] experienced two flats in one race: "I had a rear flat super early... I'd worked my way through about 3/4 (I think) of the field and was settling back in to a good rhythm when I flatted my front tire and ended up at the back of the pack again. I fixed it and bailed at the next trailhead." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Passing difficulty after mechanicals:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "getting back into a race on a narrow, technical bench trail is super hard and ends up being physically and mentally exhausting because you have to yell and beg and punch your way around a couple of hundred people in conga-line groups of 3-10 at a time." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Weather-related flat risk:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "I would have been content to finish the race with him, but on the last gravel climb of the day, he said he was blown and I went on by myself. I wanted to finish in under 5 hours so I put my head down and rode hard to the finish. I ended up crossing the line at 5:01:52 in 8th place... having a bunch of air in my front tire was super sketchy because the rocks & roots were already wet & muddy, which makes them a type of slick that you just have to experience to really appreciate." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Heat and cramping:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE] noted one rider "was cramping and having heat-stroke symptoms so after riding with Brandon and I for awhile, he pulled over and told us to go on." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=28)

**Getting lost:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "Casey knew. She got caught in it the day before. Got lost in it. Cussed and cried in it." During pre-ride recon. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Mental challenge of navigation:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "I realized that my name was nowhere on the results board, so I protested. He told me that they'd give me an award for women's singlespeed... since I was a single racer in the women's singlespeed category, that means that I'm not worthy of getting an official result posted anywhere." Describing post-race frustration from a different event, showing organizational challenges. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=3398)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Social nature of the event:** Travis Liles [UNKNOWN]: "This race is a lot of fun. Being able to interact with other runners for 3 straight days makes this event worth checking out. Each day after the run you hang out, cheer in the other runners, eat, and hang out some more. That is a really unique opportunity that you wont find at other runs." (https://lilestrials.com/blog/race-report-3-days-of-syllamo)

**Cabin accommodations:** Travis Liles [UNKNOWN]: "We were lucky enough to snag the magical 'Dreamcatcher' cabin. Exactly what 3 guys who would be our running in the woods for 3 days needs, a cabin tucked away in the Ozark Mountains with a jaccuzi tub and a giant dreamcatcher above one of the beds... the cabins down there are a good setup. They are priced well to split 3 ways and everyone gets their own bed to sleep in, clean towels, along with full kitchen and a hot shower." (https://lilestrials.com/blog/race-report-3-days-of-syllamo)

**Mountain View, Arkansas:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "Known as the Fiddle capital of the World, Mountain View isn't exactly a tourist town, which is why we stayed close to nature at the Syllamore Creek Campground." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Camping at the venue:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "We stayed there last year, in a hand-built cabin, made form locally sourced logs. It had all of the amenities one would want to have to prepare for and celebrate after a big race like Syllamo's Revenge. Just steps from our cabin." (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Volunteer support:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "I grabbed some food, fill bottles and ask which way to the end for the 50 miler" at checkpoint 3, showing active aid station support. (http://www.dirtgirldiary.com/2013/05/silly-slippery-syllamo.html)

**Trophy quality:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The trophies for this race are pretty sweet (as is the cash in the included envelope!)" with a photo showing a custom trophy with what appears to be a libation. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?p=1885)

**Regional reputation:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "I can only think of two trail systems- Syllamo and Pisgah- that go above and beyond techy/difficult/epic to achieve Infamy. These are trails that can chew up and spit out even the most well-prepared rider and equipment." Comparing Syllamo to Pisgah's legendary status. (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Local lore:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "The name 'Syllamo' comes as a tribute to a troublesome Creek Indian who inhabited the Sylamore Creek area in the early 1800s. It's said that he used to steal horses an support raids of settlers' homesteads, and, while he was eventually shot, it's rumored that he still haunts his territory to this day. If there's ever a legend that I'll believe, it's this one." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)

**Respect for the terrain:** Andrea Wilson [ELITE]: "When people come to the Syllamo trails for the first time, they're often not prepared for what's there." And: "Even though the trail won out this weekend, there were hints of awesome in my very short time racing. I don't think it's possible to be more ready for Trans-Sylvania than I am now." (http://www.brickhouseracing.com/?paged=14)
