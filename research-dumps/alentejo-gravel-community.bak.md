## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile:** The main 119-120km route features 2,100-3,182m of climbing (sources vary slightly). The shorter 84-87km route has 1,500-2,515m elevation. Approximately 76% of the route is off-road.
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/alentejo-gravel-wraps-up-the-2025-season/ and https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/11/10/uci-alentejo-gravel-results-malezsewski-kloser-win-in-portugal/)

**Course Characteristics:** The terrain is described as "rolling, with five climbs between 1–2 km long and gradients of 4–7%." The profile is described as "shark-tooth" with "flat sections far and few between" indicating constant rolling terrain rather than sustained climbs.
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/alentejo-gravel-wraps-up-the-2025-season/ and https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/11/10/uci-alentejo-gravel-results-malezsewski-kloser-win-in-portugal/)

**Start Character:** Rosa Klöser [ELITE] noted "the start was quite hilly and technical" which caused the field to split quickly within the first 15km.
(https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/alentejo-gravel/results/)

**Course Layout:** The race begins with a northern loop, passing near the finish after 25km, then heads south. The final kilometer is uphill to the finish line.
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/alentejo-gravel-wraps-up-the-2025-season/)

**Gravel Quality:** One rider specifically noted it was "realmente un terreno gravilla" (really gravel terrain), suggesting authentic gravel surfaces rather than hardpack.
(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UlSon3yD93Y)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early Race Dynamics [ELITE perspective]:** Rosa Klöser [ELITE] found herself in a group of 15-20 men after just 15km due to the hilly, technical start. She described it as "a fast race" that concluded with an uphill sprint finish.
(https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/alentejo-gravel/results/)

**Winning Times [ELITE]:**
- Men's 120km: Lukas Malezsewski won in 3:20:29-3:29:29 (sources vary)
- Women's 120km: Rosa Kloser won in 3:45:25
- Men's 85km: Bruno Silvestre won in 2:42:55
- Women's 85km: Jenny McCauley won in 3:11:03
(https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/11/10/uci-alentejo-gravel-results-malezsewski-kloser-win-in-portugal/ and https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/alentejo-gravel/results/)

**Breakaway Tactics [ELITE]:** The men's race came down to a two-rider breakaway with an uphill sprint finish, suggesting that strong climbers who can position themselves early have an advantage.
(https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/alentejo-gravel/results/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**International Participation:** The inaugural 2025 event attracted over 700-800 riders from 29-37 countries (sources vary), with strong European representation, particularly from Spain, Portugal, Belgium, and the Netherlands.
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/alentejo-gravel-wraps-up-the-2025-season/ and https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UlSon3yD93Y)

**Mixed Field Racing:** Unlike some gravel events with separate elite waves, Alentejo Gravel mixed elite women with age-group men, creating interesting race dynamics. Rosa Klöser [ELITE] specifically noted racing with the men as good training.
(https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/alentejo-gravel/results/)

**First Edition Success:** Multiple sources describe it as "inesquecível" (unforgettable) and note it was Portugal's first UCI Gravel World Series event.
(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=133XwMLel_U and https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/alentejo-gravel-wraps-up-the-2025-season/)

**Organizer Experience:** The event is organized by Cabreira Solutions, who also run the successful Granfondo Coimbra Region (part of UCI Gran Fondo World Series), suggesting professional organization.
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/ourique/)

**Post-Race Amenities:** The event includes "a dedicated area for socializing, catering, exhibitors and activities for all ages — transforming the day into a true festival dedicated to gravel and cycling."
(https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12849/alentejo-gravel)

**UCI Qualification:** Riders finishing in the top 25% of their UCI age/gender category automatically qualified for the 2026 UCI Gravel World Championships in Nannup, Australia.
(https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/11/10/uci-alentejo-gravel-results-malezsewski-kloser-win-in-portugal/)
