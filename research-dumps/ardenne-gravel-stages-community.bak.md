## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Stage 1 tactics [ELITE level]:** Trouwborst took early control on the muddy 70km course around Vielsalm, winning the opening stage. (https://mountainbiketeam.com/en/marielle-trouwborst-wins-3-day-ardenne-gravel-stages/)

**Stage 2 tactics [ELITE level]:** On the 110km second stage, Trouwborst took an early lead but mechanical problems cost her the advantage. Joyce Vanderbeken was able to break away and win the stage in the final. (https://mountainbiketeam.com/en/marielle-trouwborst-wins-3-day-ardenne-gravel-stages/)

**Stage 3 tactics [ELITE level]:** The 90km final stage saw Trouwborst puncture early but she managed with "a plug and a few pumps" to reach the tech zone, got a new rear wheel, and still finished second, securing overall victory. (https://mountainbiketeam.com/en/marielle-trouwborst-wins-3-day-ardenne-gravel-stages/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Stage 1:** 70km muddy course around Vielsalm with 1,370m elevation gain (https://mountainbiketeam.com/en/marielle-trouwborst-wins-3-day-ardenne-gravel-stages/ and https://www.vojomag.com/news/ardenne-gravel-stages-une-epreuve-gravel-par-etapes-inedite-en-belgique/)

**Stage 2:** 110km stage from Centre Adeps d'Engreux with 1,800m elevation gain (also offered 70km/1,150m option for single-day Dusty Gravel participants) (https://www.vojomag.com/news/ardenne-gravel-stages-une-epreuve-gravel-par-etapes-inedite-en-belgique/)

**Stage 3:** 90km from Baraque Fraiture with 1,620m elevation gain (https://www.vojomag.com/news/ardenne-gravel-stages-une-epreuve-gravel-par-etapes-inedite-en-belgique/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical issues [ELITE level]:** Trouwborst experienced mechanical problems during Stage 2 that cost her the stage win, and punctured early in Stage 3 but was able to continue with tire plugs and reaching tech support. (https://mountainbiketeam.com/en/marielle-trouwborst-wins-3-day-ardenne-gravel-stages/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event positioning:** Described as "la toute première course gravel à étapes de 3 jours en Belgique" (the very first 3-day gravel stage race in Belgium), positioned as an "évènement iconique" (iconic event). (https://www.ardennegravelstages.com/en/inscriptions)

**Flexibility for participation:** The race offers multiple entry options - riders can participate for 1, 2, or 3 days, solo or in duo format, making it accessible to different commitment levels. However, organizers recommend doing the entire weekend to "fully experience the unique Ardenne Gravel Stages." (https://www.ardennegravelstages.com/en/faq)

**Integration with existing series:** Stage 2 was part of the Dusty Gravel series (4th and final round), bringing additional competition from riders only participating in that single race. (https://mountainbiketeam.com/en/marielle-trouwborst-wins-3-day-ardenne-gravel-stages/ and https://www.vojomag.com/news/ardenne-gravel-stages-une-epreuve-gravel-par-etapes-inedite-en-belgique/)

**Accommodation options [UNKNOWN level]:** For riders participating in at least two stages, lodging is available at Centre Adeps d'Engreux (location of Stage 2) including dinner and breakfast, with limited places. (https://www.le-sportif.com/calendrier/215146/ardenne-gravel-stages-2025-vielsalm/gravel/)

**Race format:** Real race with mass start and timed results. Each stage starts and finishes at the same location (different location each day). For duos, timing is based on the second rider crossing the finish line. (https://www.ardennegravelstages.com/en/faq)
