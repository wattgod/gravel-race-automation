# BACK FORTY HIGHLANDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The course features **single lane treed gravel roads** through North Frontenac and Lanark Highlands. The area is known for **marble from the nearby Tatlock Quarry**, which gives many roads a **distinctly white hue, similar to the Italian cycling classic Strada Bianche** (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander-2024).

Highlighted challenging sections include: **Ranger Camp, North Shore, Clarendon** (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander).

The landscape features **rolling hills of forests**, with views of the **Mississippi River and Dalhousie Lake**. The area's history includes logging and colonization, meaning riders traverse **old logging roads** that are now quiet (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander-2024).

One source describes it as having **"12 km of mud followed by technical singletrack, as well as a short paved portion"** for the Tour of Kincardine hybrid format, though this may be conflating events (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events).

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE level data]:** The 2025 Epic Course (108.5 km) winner **Michael Joannisse** finished in **3:13:32** at **33.64 km/h average speed**. The top 8 elite men all finished within 4:32 of each other, between 3:13:32 and 3:18:05, averaging **31.73-33.64 km/h** (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).

**[ELITE level data]:** Top Elite woman **Jennifer Stephenson** completed the Epic Course in **4:05:58** at **26.47 km/h** (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).

**[UNKNOWN level data]:** The Epic Course (108.5 km) saw finish times ranging from **3:13:32 (elite)** to over **6:47:49**, indicating the course accommodates a wide range of abilities (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).

**[UNKNOWN level data]:** For the Medio Course (70 km), winning time was **2:20:05** at **29.98 km/h**, with the field finishing between 2:20 and 4:38 (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).

**Staggered starts by distance:** Epic (108.5 km) starts at 9:00am, Medio (70 km) at 9:30am, Petit (35 km) at 9:45am (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander-2024).

## DNF Risk Factors

In the 2025 event, there were **5 DNFs in the Epic Course Men's category** out of 36 starters. Reasons noted include:
- **Philippe Guindon**: "Shorter course?" 
- **Todd Thompson**: "Rode with daughter"
- **Douglas Van Den Ham**: "Went off course did 79km"

This suggests **navigation errors** are a real risk factor, with at least one rider going significantly off course (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).

**1 DNF in Medio Course Women** out of 10 starters, and **6 total DNFs + 9 DNS** (did not start) across all categories in 2025 (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).

## Weather Experienced

The 2024 event experienced **"not ideal" weather** but participants still "managed to have an amazing time" according to the YouTube video description (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZvDCMD-Bpe0).

The Northern Ontario Travel guide warns that the **May 25 Melrose Gravel Tour** can be **"mucky, depending on the weather"** and recommends **bug repellent**, suggesting similar conditions could occur at Back Forty depending on seasonal timing (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events).

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event takes place at **Back Forty Artisan Cheese Farm**, a "picturesque location" on the shore of the **Mississippi River with the calming distant sound of waterfalls**. Post-race, riders **"relax and reminisce with food and drink"** at this scenic venue (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander-2024).

The area is described as **"beautiful"** with **"maple syrup as the most abundant natural resource"** and riders are **"spellbound by views"** of the natural landscape (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander-2024).

This is a **marquee event** in the Gravel Cup Canada series, positioned as one of the premier gravel events in the region (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/back-forty-highlander-2024).

The event is organized by **Brendan Gorman** and accommodates **112 total participants in 2025** across multiple categories and distances (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A3-2025.html).
