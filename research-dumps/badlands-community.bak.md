# BADLANDS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Sarah Scozzaro [ELITE]:** "I felt stronger now and more relaxed than at the start. THIS is a great feeling! The weather was warm, but not too hot, but 99% of the course is exposed, and the sun beating down can take a toll. Staying cool, calm and hydrated is key with races like this." (https://drtyrunner.com/2016/08/23/badlands-50k-race-report/)

**Sarah Scozzaro [ELITE]:** "I have been really working diligently this past year on my nutrition and hydration, after suffering lots of tummy issues and flash crashes in the past that have really impacted my ability to run a race well. I really feel that I have nailed this aspect of my racing; I use a Carbopro and Skratch Labs combo and my goal is to consume a bottle each hour." (https://drtyrunner.com/2016/08/23/badlands-50k-race-report/)

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "Even after riding for close to 750 km and without any sleep for the final 48 hours, I don't remember any moments of panic. But I think our heads have a crazy strength and can do things that we can't even imagine." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "There's a difference in events of about 48 hours where the tendency is to start very strong – almost like a Gran Fondo – and then find a pace that allows you to advance. In multi-day races you look for regularity and minimising your stops. You can gain or lose hours each day depending on your strategy for eating and sleeping. But it's a delicate balance and you don't always get it right." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "I don't like to lose and I'm prepared to dig deep if I find someone stronger than me in my way. But I was definitely not born with extraordinary gifts. I've worked hard and made sacrifices to be who I am now. And I'm not just talking about being strong on the bike." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "It all stems from the head. If you really want something, you have to go for it. Listening to your body and training makes a significant difference. But you have to be hungry!" (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Saskia Martin [COMPETITIVE]:** "My friendship with Cat grew through working together at Rapha. From Regent's Park laps to weekend bivvying, we're always searching for our next cycling adventure and have a shared Excel spreadsheet permanently detailing our packing lists. All sub-categorised, a tick underneath each heading tells us who's bringing what." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Saskia Martin [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was commuting by bike but even that was exhausting. A physical tiredness but also an emotional sense of weariness that was devastating—I was basically going through a break-up with my bikes." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'd be lying to you if I said I was taking this on for the adventure. I was heading to Badlands to race and I wanted to do well. I'd prepared myself for long stretches on the saddle, minimal stops, little sleep and, at times, suffering from the heat and dehydration." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "At hour three I decided to back it off and ride my own race... I continued on and glanced every now and again back to appreciate the view." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was cruising just outside the top 10 with the village of Gor in my sights, about 50km away. I remember this part so vividly. I was riding solo, and the 'climb' section came up on my Wahoo, with the climb length stating '39km'. All I could do was laugh, literally out loud, at the sheer length of such a climb." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I rushed into the cafe, but I didn't take the time to sit down and have a proper meal, I was so focused on keeping my stop time down to a minimum, I just asked for a couple of bocadillos to go and got going as quickly as I could, that was a rookie error and something I learned the hard way." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Ed P. [ELITE]:** "I spent an hour first thing in the morning sessioning every section of the course, altering the amount of air in both tyres until I found the right balance between traction and impact resistance. Asked what pressures I was running I replied 'squishy front, bouncy back' so prob about 20psi front/26psi back." (https://pbcyclecoaching.co.uk/category/race-reports/page/2)

**Ed P. [ELITE]:** "From then on it was a full on test of strength, skill, exhaustion and excitement. Many things happened and I won't bore you with them all, but by goodness it was a bloomin marvellous race and I was ecstatic to cross the finish line in 3rd place." (https://pbcyclecoaching.co.uk/category/race-reports/page/2)

**James Mark Hayden [ELITE]:** "I pulled out my 40c gravel bike, the Canyon Grail out of the van and went riding. The Grail is fast, if the off-road is smooth and the distances not excessive. The tarmac up to Pico Veleta soon ended and the path went from dust to rocks. Within a few metres my mind went to the hardtail I had in the van, my Canyon Exceed." (https://jamesmarkhayden.eu/articles/badlands)

**James Mark Hayden [ELITE]:** "I know what a beating my body will take if I try to race 700 km of mixed terrain flat out on a 40c rigid bike. And with the esteemed company, it will be flat out. This stuff is hard enough already, no need to make it harder. I don't want to be off the bike recovering for 4 weeks, like I was after Italy Divide in 2019." (https://jamesmarkhayden.eu/articles/badlands)

**Chris Hall [ELITE]:** "For me personally, Badlands is so much more than just a bike race. It's an experience with an incredible community of bikepackers exploring one of the most beautiful and brutal parts of Southern Spain. I keep coming back every year for the community, to see people I've seen at previous editions and reconnect with that absolutely crazy landscape. The views never get old." (https://www.siestacampers.com/blog/badlands-gravel-challenge)

I think I always knew it was a big ask to be able to complete both especially when I was so fatigued. I think as early as the Badlands social rides in the lead up to the race I had a feeling it maybe a stretch too far. I just felt exhausted and hollow. No matter how much I ate or drank I just had zero energy." (https://www.siestacampers.com/blog/badlands-gravel-challenge)

**Chris Hall [ELITE]:** "I was completely at peace with the idea of stopping. I find it really interesting trying to learn and figure out the limitations of my body and it turned out that was the limitation this time. It's not often that you pull out of these kinds of races so relaxed so it was actually quite a nice way to pull out." (https://www.siestacampers.com/blog/badlands-gravel-challenge)

**Pol Tarrés [RECREATIONAL]:** "I didn't expect it but the skin of my hands was irritated from the shaking of riding on the gravel roads. **Pol Tarrés [RECREATIONAL]:** "It also impressed me how fast you can go on the gravel roads when its flat, those bikes are stiff and if you push you fly. Even if its rough, sometimes its better to go fast than slow." (https://www.poltarres.com/2023/05/18/gravel-changed-my-mind/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**40c Gravel Tires vs. MTB Setup - Level-Specific:**

**James Mark Hayden [ELITE]:** Reports race manual stated "40c tyres are perfect" but after testing the course, decided: "I'll race my hardtail again. Why? Because I think it'll be both fastest across the terrain but importantly most forgiving on my body." His reasoning: "The tarmac up to Pico Veleta soon ended and the path went from dust to rocks. Within a few metres my mind went to the hardtail I had in the van." (https://jamesmarkhayden.eu/articles/badlands)

**Ed P. [ELITE]:** For his third-place finish: "Tyre pressures today were silly low, I'd guess around 15psi. Could feel the tyres folding under on the tarmac corners but needed all the grip I could get for the churned up mud and grass that made up over half of the course." (https://pbcyclecoaching.co.uk/category/race-reports/page/2)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I genuinely thought I would see myself arrive at Gor in no time, I guess that serves me right for going into an ultra race having done little research into the route." Also noted: "I had messed up with my gears, a 40T chainring with an 11-42T cassette on the back, my knees were in for a long and grindy few days." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "I always carry a tube of superglue when I'm racing to help fix things and seal up any open wounds." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

## Weather Experienced

**2023 Edition - Storm Conditions:**

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "Usually, all the pre-race chat is about kit and bike set-up but, this year, it was about the weather. Not the extreme heat that usually tests the riders, no. Instead, a storm was due to hit Granada and the surrounding areas for 24 hours following the race start." The course was modified to avoid flash flood risk areas in the Gorafe desert. "Being a Brit, used to riding in cool temperatures and rain, I actually welcomed the sight of high 20s and rain, over the usual high 30s and burning sun." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** Day 1: "Most riders had decided to start with no rain jacket, but I took a bet and decided that the skies looked dark and the rain was coming, and stuck with the rain jacket." Later: "About 10km into the climb I find myself next to Rob Britton... I was already struggling and hanging on with all my might. Overheating and overgeared, I struggled to hang on to the front pack." The heavy rain arrived: "I did find myself on a flat road section, head down next to Ulrich Bartholmoes, just as the heavens well and truly opened." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Ed P. [ELITE]:** During his race: "A big race for me... It was on home turf... with a course that would not look out of place on a world cup event. We are now fully into the 'cross season proper, with leaden skies and mud on the ground." His tire pressure strategy: "20psi front/26psi back" for muddy conditions. (https://pbcyclecoaching.co.uk/category/race-reports/page/2)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "The first section of Badlands is a 20km climb with 850m elevation, climbing through El Sacromonte." Then: "Around 65km the route transitions from the forest to the desert, into the natural plain of La Hoya de Guadix. Not long after entering the plain, you hit The Mirador del Fin del Mundo, an iconic climb that has an average gradient of 15 per cent, ramping up to a maximum of 25 per cent." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** Night riding conditions: "The route continued up and into the mountains as we headed towards the Sierra de los Filabres. I really can't say much about the view as it was genuinely pitch black, all I remember was the loneliness, in the middle of the forest, dark clouds with thunder and lightning above me. One of the main points of interest during this middle section is the Calar Alto Observatory, sitting at the highest point on the route at 2,168m." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "The village of Gor is where Badlands started to take its victims. Of the first front 12 riders that arrived in Gor, only six or so finished the race." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Ed P. [ELITE]:** "The course was very traditional with lots of wide, muddy track and a big hill to go up twice per lap." And: "This was a big race for me... with a course that would not look out of place on a world cup event." The main hill: "Main photo is the big hill, one of 4 forced dismounts we had to do each lap. There were 6 laps in total and this baby really took it's toll on all the competitors." (https://pbcyclecoaching.co.uk/category/race-reports/page/2)

**Pol Tarrés [RECREATIONAL]:** "After that, a fast descent to go into the Gorafe desert loop, the most beautiful nature landscape of the weekend. We didn't stop much since we were enjoying the downhill but you can get the idea of how it is with the couple of pictures we took. Made it to the bottom and rode for a couple of km riding into the canyon, then it was time to go up again where the loop started. It was steep but we were enjoying the views while climbing." (https://www.poltarres.com/2023/05/18/gravel-changed-my-mind/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite-Level Race Start - CRITICAL LEVEL TAG:**

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "As soon as the police pulled off, it was full gas, and straight away I knew the jacket needed to go." About pacing with elites: "About 10km into the climb I find myself next to Rob Britton, eventual winner of Badlands, who turns to me and mutters 'If this is the pace of the race, we'll be laughing and on for an easy ride'. Hearing this gave me no confidence boost, as I was already struggling and hanging on with all my might." 

**POWER DATA - ELITE LEVEL:** "For all you number crunchers out there, I can't share my power data as my power meter decided to pack it in the day before the race but my buddy Tristan (who placed one place in front of me at the end) was in the front pack with me at the start and mentioned that the first three hours were ridden at 4w/kg, for a 750km race." He made a critical decision: "At hour three I decided to back it off and ride my own race." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE] - Multi-Day Strategy:** "There's a difference in events of about 48 hours where the tendency is to start very strong – almost like a Gran Fondo – and then find a pace that allows you to advance. In multi-day races you look for regularity and minimising your stops. You can gain or lose hours each day depending on your strategy for eating and sleeping." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Nutrition - Elite Level:**

**Sarah Scozzaro [ELITE]:** "I use a Carbopro and Skratch Labs combo and my goal is to consume a bottle each hour." She consumed "7 bottles during the event. Small Dixie cup of coke at 3 of the AS. NO flash crash, energy dips or tummy issues." (https://drtyrunner.com/2016/08/23/badlands-50k-race-report/)

**Stop Strategy - Competitive Level:**

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I rushed into the cafe, but I didn't take the time to sit down and have a proper meal, I was so focused on keeping my stop time down to a minimum, I just asked for a couple of bocadillos to go and got going as quickly as I could, that was a rookie error and something I learned the hard way." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Mental Resilience - Elite Level:**

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "Even after riding for close to 750 km and without any sleep for the final 48 hours, I don't remember any moments of panic." He references "always keeping a dollar in his pocket. A reference to leaving enough in the tank that, whatever the eventuality, he can marshall reserves even when sleep deprived and at the limits of his endurance." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Post-Race Fatigue Timing:**

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "The village of Gor is where Badlands started to take its victims. Of the first front 12 riders that arrived in Gor, only six or so finished the race. The first 12 hours over that first 250km were hard racing, and it was a true turning point for me, too." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Inadequate Recovery Before Race:**

**Chris Hall [ELITE]:** After riding 7,000km around Britain one week before Badlands: "The gap between finishing the perimeter of Britain project and Badlands was just over 1 week. I think I always knew it was a big ask to be able to complete both especially when I was so fatigued... I just felt exhausted and hollow. No matter how how much I ate or drank I just had zero energy." He withdrew on day two: "I was completely at peace with the idea of stopping. I find it really interesting trying to learn and figure out the limitations of my body and it turned out that was the limitation this time." (https://www.siestacampers.com/blog/badlands-gravel-challenge)

**Equipment Failure:**

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** At 2021 Badlands: "A crash in the town of Murtas saw Mattia dusting himself down and continuing without realising he'd lost his tracker. But rather than any sense of despair when it did finally sink in that his progress wasn't being recorded, the Italian gravel racer calmly shared his location with the race organisers over WhatsApp before pushing on for the win." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Saskia Martin [COMPETITIVE]:** "Mentally exhausted and with holiday plans in disarray, Saskia Martin looked to the desert wilderness of Andalusia to force a reset. Heading south to ride the Badlands route with her friend Cat Karalis, the redemption Saskia sought proved elusive but she did discover a sense of self and a way of once again moving forward." Context: "As a senior product developer for Rapha, my job is to make our designer's dreams and concepts into a reality. But as it's a very fast-paced role – which I love because I thrive in chaos and under time constraints – that brings with it a certain degree of pressure and I was close to burn out." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Mattia de Marchi [ELITE]:** "This sense of camaraderie between competitors is often mooted as a significant difference between the professional world of road racing and the privateer model of the gravel calendar. And looking back at his own road career, Mattia well remembers how his teammates all sat wearing headphones on team bus transfers – himself included – and that sharing a few beers was reserved for the final evening before flights the following morning." About gravel: "The idea was born from what fundamentally makes us happy—riding a bicycle and sharing this joy with people from all walks of life... And if you look at gravel, it isn't just dirt. Gravel is something that every person can experience in a way they like it best. There's fewer of the boundaries that still persist in the world of road cycling. In gravel, we all line up together." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/badlands/)

**Chris Hall [ELITE]:** "For me personally, Badlands is so much more than just a bike race. It's an experience with an incredible community of bikepackers exploring one of the most beautiful and brutal parts of Southern Spain. I keep coming back every year for the community, to see people I've seen at previous editions and reconnect with that absolutely crazy landscape." And: "It has such an incredible community around it, I don't really know any other race quite like that. That's what pulls me back each year." (https://www.siestacampers.com/blog/badlands-gravel-challenge)

**Brad Shenton [COMPETITIVE]:** "Arriving in Gor was such a special moment and something I will cherish forever. Music is pumping, the village is out for support and Santiago is there with his trusty handycam to capture it all." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/badlands-2023-my-epic-journey-through-europes-toughest-gravel-race-13439)

**Sarah Scozzaro [ELITE]:** "I highly recommend this race; it has a wonderful grassroots feel, well stocked aid stations, a friendly and laid back RD, cool finishers coin and a GORGEOUS course. Plenty of runnable sections and some longer climbs that make this a fun race!" (https://drtyrunner.com/2016/08/23/badlands-50k-race-report/)

**Chris Hall [ELITE] - Advice for Newcomers:** "Start small. When I started I used to ride on a loaded bike to my mates and then camp in the garden and ride back. Also it's important to remember to listen to your body. Pain is a signal not to be ignored for example. Most of all take the pressure off, enjoy the journey and the experience." (https://www.siestacampers.com/blog/badlands-gravel-challenge)
