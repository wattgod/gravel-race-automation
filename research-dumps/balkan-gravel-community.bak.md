# BALKAN GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "After last year's Bohemian Border Bash Race, I've adopted a different strategy for this year: enjoy the scenery and the ride more, ride less empty, take more care of myself. And I'm sure I'll do pretty well with that. I originally planned 6 days for the route. But I don't know the area or all the challenges of the route and the expected weather. In total, I have until the 8th of June (I'm travelling back on the morning of the 9th). That means I have a maximum of 9 days at my disposal." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "I don't want to repeat the mistakes I made in Bohemia and then have to give up physically exhausted and with a sore bum just before the finish." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Die noch auf kroatischem Gebiet verlaufende Strecke direkt an der Grenze führt durch völlig menschenleeres Gebiet" [The route still on Croatian territory directly at the border leads through completely uninhabited territory] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Vor dem Krieg gab es ein paar Orte von denen nur mehr Ruinen übrig sind - jetzt gibt es nur mehr Natur und davon viel." [Before the war there were a few places of which only ruins remain - now there is only nature and lots of it.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Kurzes Resümee: + im Vergleich zur EU sehr günstig, freundliche Leute, für europ. Begriffe erstaunlich große menschenleere Gebiete - war weniger Gravel als gedacht/geplant. Die Planung ist gar nicht so leicht, da die div. Karten keine zuverlässigen Schlüsse auf den Strassenzustand bzw. Untergrund zulassen. Ausserdem wird (in den bewohnten Gebieten) viel asphaltiert - auch kleine Straßen und Wege." [Brief summary: + very cheap compared to EU, friendly people, surprisingly large uninhabited areas by European standards - was less gravel than thought/planned. Planning is not that easy, as various maps do not allow reliable conclusions about road condition or surface. Also, a lot is being asphalted (in inhabited areas) - even small roads and paths.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

**Tenul [UNKNOWN]:** "Vor allem die Jüngeren, die in der Gastronomie und im Tourismus arbeiten (mit denen haben wir logischerweise am meisten zu tun), sprechen alle sehr gut Englisch. Und mit dem Bergbauern, der uns gestern interessiert beim Schlauchpicken zugeschaut hat, haben wir uns eigentlich auch ohne Englisch oder Deutsch ganz gut unterhalten." [Especially the younger people who work in gastronomy and tourism (with whom we logically have the most contact) all speak very good English. And with the mountain farmer who watched us with interest yesterday while fixing a flat, we actually got along quite well without English or German.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Die Leute vom Hotel raten ab, weil schlechte Straße, unbewohnt, Kriegsschäden, Bären, Wölfe und Wildschweine etc. Und empfehlen die Hauptstraße um den Berg herum... Dieser Ratschlag macht (vor allem mich) noch neugieriger und wir fahren die schlussendlich sehr gut zu fahrende und auch nicht steile Naturstrasse über den 1180 m hohen Pass Karaula. Die Landschaft und der Untergrund sind mit einer Forststrasse in der Stmk vergleichbar - nur mit mehr Ruinen aber dafür ohne spinnerten Förster und Jäger." [The hotel people advise against it because of bad road, uninhabited, war damage, bears, wolves and wild boar etc. And recommend the main road around the mountain... This advice makes (especially me) even more curious and we ride what is ultimately a very rideable and not steep natural road over the 1180m high Karaula Pass. The landscape and surface are comparable to a forest road in Styria - only with more ruins but without crazy foresters and hunters.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

### Surface Mix

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "According to Komoot, the Trans Balkan Race has a good mix of all surfaces: Natural surface: 81.5 km, Loose surface: 322 km, Gravel: 328 km, Paved road: 188 km, Tarmac: 435 km" (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** Route "hat von perfekten Asphalt über Waschbrettpiste bis zu grobem Schotter alles geboten" [offered everything from perfect asphalt to washboard to coarse gravel] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

### Specific Sections

**Golo [UNKNOWN] - Karaula Pass:** "Die schlussendlich sehr gut zu fahrende und auch nicht steile Naturstrasse über den 1180 m hohen Pass Karaula. Die Landschaft und der Untergrund sind mit einer Forststrasse in der Stmk vergleichbar" [Ultimately very rideable and not steep natural road over the 1180m high Karaula Pass. The landscape and surface are comparable to a forest road in Styria] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN] - Travnik area:** "Nächste angenehme Überraschung: vor und nach Travnik gibt es eine offizielle und meist mit feinem Kies belegte Radroute auf einer alten Bahntrasse." [Next pleasant surprise: before and after Travnik there is an official cycle route, mostly covered with fine gravel, on an old railway line.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN] - Kozana National Park:** "Der höchste Punkt auf fast 900 m Höhe... unbedingt durch den Nationalpark fahren, gute Straße und verkehrsfrei" [The highest point at almost 900m altitude... definitely ride through the national park, good road and traffic-free] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN] - R408b route:** "Die Strasse R 408b hinauf aus der Schlucht ist ein besserer Forstweg (obwohl gelb auf mapy.cz eingezeichnet- wie manche Hauptstraße) und dann 10 km Traumstrecke quer über die Hochebene bis zur Hauptstraße" [The R 408b road up from the gorge is a better forest road (although marked yellow on mapy.cz - like some main roads) and then 10 km dream route across the plateau to the main road] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN] - Route to Drvar:** "Die gelb eingezeichnete Straße nach Drvar ist zur Hälfte nicht asphaltiert und eine super Strecke zum Graveln" [The yellow-marked road to Drvar is half unpaved and a great gravel route] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

### Elevation & Difficulty

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "A total of 27,000 metres of altitude difference have to be overcome over the 1,380 km. And a glance at the route profile shows that this will be no mean feat... on the last 340 kilometres, you not only cross the border from Bosnia-Herzegovina to Montenegro, but also have to climb 6,700 metres in altitude. From the profile, it looks like an eternal up and down – a good test of character after you've already covered 1,000 kilometres and over 20,000 metres of altitude." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "The Trans Balkan Race has a ratio of around 2,000 metres of elevation gain per 100 km (1,956 m to be precise)... on paper it has more vertical metres than the Bohemian Border Bash Race with 1,846 vertical metres per 100 km" (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

### Safety Considerations

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "You should pay particular attention to the minefields, which are clearly indicated in the road book. A distinction is made here between 'Warning of mines' and 'High Danger'. You should avoid travelling through at night (although this will not always be possible) and in any case you should stay on the track and not bivouac or take a break off the trail." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** Hotel staff warned about route: "schlechte Straße, unbewohnt, Kriegsschäden, Bären, Wölfe und Wildschweine" [bad road, uninhabited, war damage, bears, wolves and wild boar] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

### Uninhabited Sections

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Vor dem Krieg gab es ein paar Orte von denen nur mehr Ruinen übrig sind - jetzt gibt es nur mehr Natur und davon viel" [Before the war there were a few places of which only ruins remain - now there is only nature and lots of it] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Es gibt große unbesiedelte Gebiete und auch Gegenden, wo die eingezeichneten Ortschaften nicht mehr existieren. Dort, wo nur mehr Ruinen stehen, gibt es nichts zu kaufen." [There are large uninhabited areas and also regions where the marked settlements no longer exist. Where there are only ruins, there is nothing to buy.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

### Route Planning Challenges

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Die Planung ist gar nicht so leicht, da die div. Karten keine zuverlässigen Schlüsse auf den Strassenzustand bzw. Untergrund zulassen. Ausserdem wird (in den bewohnten Gebieten) viel asphaltiert - auch kleine Straßen und Wege." [Planning is not that easy, as various maps do not allow reliable conclusions about road condition or surface. Also, a lot is being asphalted (in inhabited areas) - even small roads and paths.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

### Time Planning - Recreational Level

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "I originally planned 6 days for the route. But I don't know the area or all the challenges of the route and the expected weather. In total, I have until the 8th of June (I'm travelling back on the morning of the 9th). That means I have a maximum of 9 days at my disposal. That should be enough, even if something comes up." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN] - Daily distances over 8 days:** Varied between 68-113km per day with 700-1700m elevation, suggesting a recreational touring pace rather than racing. Example days: "100 km with 900 HM", "85 km with 1300 HM", "94 km with 900 hm", "98 km with 1400 HM" (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

### Elite Results

**Historical winners (2022):** "Lieven Schroyen of Belgium took first place with a time of 5 days and 6 hours. Lael Wilcox was the first woman to finish, arriving in the Bay of Kotor in southwestern Montenegro about a day later." (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**NOTE:** Elite finishers complete in 5-6 days; recreational tourers report planning 6-9 days. This represents a substantial difference in daily mileage and intensity.

### Mental Approach

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "After last year's Bohemian Border Bash Race, I've adopted a different strategy for this year: enjoy the scenery and the ride more, ride less empty, take more care of myself... In the last few days before the race, I will only do a few short interval sessions and give my body a rest. This time I've put a bit more energy into my mental preparation" (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

### Resupply Strategy

**Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]:** "It gives me plenty of spare space to store enough food and drink on the way and to cover longer distances without resupply... In the top tube bag I carry food and my multitool at the front... The full frame bag... offers a lot of space and is not fully packed. It gives me plenty of spare space to store enough food and drink on the road and to cover longer distances without resupply." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Besonders im muslimischen Teil kommt man aber öfter an gefassten Quellen vorbei, das Wasser kann man bedenkenlos trinken." [Especially in the Muslim part you often pass tapped springs, you can drink the water without hesitation.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

### Route Modification

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Heute etwas problematisches Wetter weshalb wir die ursprünglich geplante Route Richtung Drvar abgeändert haben" [Today somewhat problematic weather which is why we changed the originally planned route toward Drvar] - demonstrates that recreational riders adapted route based on conditions (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

### Race Growth

"The Trans Balkan Race has been increasing in popularity each year despite a challenging route packed with long, remote sections, limited water access, and massive climbs. This year, there are almost 160 riders signed up for the race." (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

### Notable Participants (2025)

"Some familiar names include Leanne Bentley of Carrie Gere, 2024 Tour Divide winner Justinas Leveika, 2023 Ascend Armenia winner Adrien Liechti, two-time SRMR winner Jenny Tough, Sofiane Sehili, and others." (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

### Local Hospitality

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Eigentlich fällt mir auf die Schnelle jetzt kein Land in Europa ein, das besseres P/L-Verhältnis bietet. Appartments (meist sehr groß) gibts von 18 Euro aufwärts, Hotel mit Frühstück ab 40 für 2 Personen." [Actually I can't think of any country in Europe that offers a better price/performance ratio. Apartments (usually very large) are available from 18 euros upwards, hotels with breakfast from 40 for 2 people.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** "Im Vergleich zur EU sehr günstig, freundliche Leute" [Very cheap compared to EU, friendly people] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

**Tenul [UNKNOWN]:** "Und mit dem Bergbauern, der uns gestern interessiert beim Schlauchpicken zugeschaut hat, haben wir uns eigentlich auch ohne Englisch oder Deutsch ganz gut unterhalten." [And with the mountain farmer who watched us with interest yesterday while fixing a flat, we actually got along quite well without English or German.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

### Cultural/Historical Context

**Tenul [UNKNOWN] - Jajce:** "Die Stadt bietet viel Sehenswertes auf einem winzigen Hügel, von der prächtigen Burg aus dem 14. Jahrhundert samt geheimnisvollen Katakomben über eine Kirche, mehrere schöne alte Moscheen, eine bedeutende Mithras-Kultstätte und einen großartigen Wasserfall bis zu einem gut gemachten kleinen Museum über die Gründungskozeption Jugoslawiens noch während des laufenden Krieges." [The city offers much to see on a tiny hill, from the magnificent 14th century castle with mysterious catacombs to a church, several beautiful old mosques, an important Mithras cult site and a great waterfall to a well-done small museum about the founding conception of Yugoslavia during the ongoing war.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

### Environmental/Waste Issues

**NoNick [UNKNOWN]:** "Was mir nicht nur hier aufgefallen ist, ist dass es zwar eine wunderschöne Landschaft gibt und davon auch recht viel, jedoch jeder mit Auto od zu Fuss gut zugängliche Ort als Mülldeponie oder Klo benützt wird. Kein Fleck, der nicht aussieht als wär ein Müllwagen explodiert." [What I noticed not only here is that although there is a beautiful landscape and quite a lot of it, every place easily accessible by car or on foot is used as a garbage dump or toilet. Not a spot that doesn't look like a garbage truck exploded.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)

**Golo [UNKNOWN]:** Acknowledged the waste issue but noted: "Wenn man da nicht nonchalant drübersteht, fällt halt ganz Südeuropa (und generell weite Teile der südlichen Erdhälfte) als potentielle (Rad)Reiseziele weg." [If you can't be nonchalant about it, all of Southern Europe (and generally large parts of the southern hemisphere) are eliminated as potential cycling destinations.] (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/page/2/)
