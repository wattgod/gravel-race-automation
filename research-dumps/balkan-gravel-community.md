## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tires - Elite Riders:**
- **Vittoria Mezcal 2.25-2.35"** dominates among top finishers (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **Maxxis Forekaster 2.4"** (Valerio Cecchi [ELITE]) with Tannus Armour inserts running tubeless (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)
- **Continental Race King 2.35"** (multiple riders) (https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/bikes-of-trans-balkan-race-2025)
- **Schwalbe Racing Ralph/Ray 2.35"** (Leanne Bentley [ELITE]) (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Bike Setup - Elite/Competitive:**
- **29" wheels** are the dominant choice (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **Full suspension**: 24% of riders opted for full suspension MTB (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **Suspension travel**: Most riders use 100mm or 120mm front suspension (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **Frame material**: Carbon is favored, followed by steel and titanium (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)

**Drivetrain - Cross-Level:**
- **30T or 32T chainrings** preferred for the difficult terrain (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **10-52T or 10-51T cassettes** most common (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **12-speed dominates**, with SRAM preferred over Shimano (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- Only 8% using SRAM Transmission in 2025 (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)

**Lighting & Power:**
- Only 37% rode with hub dynamos at TBR 2025 (lower than other ultra races - 50% at Atlas Mountain Race, 53% at SRMR 2024) - attributed to longer daylight hours in June (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)
- **SON28 dynamo hubs** popular among dynamo users (multiple riders) (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)
- **Sinewave Beacon 2** commonly paired with dynamos (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Aerobars - Cross-Level:**
- **55% of participants fit aerobars** despite high MTB rate (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)

**Bags:**
- **Tailfin** most popular brand, followed by Apidura, then Ortlieb (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2025/05/31/bikeanalytics-evaluation-of-the-trans-balkan-race-2025-bikes/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Altitude Ratio Analysis (Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]):**
- Trans Balkan Race: **1,956m of elevation per 100km** - categorized as a "blue" difficulty race (1,800-2,300m/100km range where "it really gets down to business")
- For comparison: BBBR has 1,846m/100km, Atlas Mountain Race 1,700m/100km, Silk Road Mountain Race 1,600m/100km
- Higher difficulty races (orange category) have 3,000-3,500m/100km
- "On the BBBR I rode around 5,000 metres of climbing over 200 km per day and during the Orbit with Stephan 3,000 metres of climbing over 100 km. Both were challenging." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Time Allowances:**
- **10 days maximum** from start to finish cutoff (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)
- **Elite times:** Justinas Leveika [ELITE] finished in "a little over four days" for the 2025 FKT (https://escapecollective.com/a-winning-trek-that-suggests-full-suspension-gravel-bikes-are-coming-back/)
- **2022 winning time:** Lieven Schroyen [ELITE] finished in 5 days 6 hours; Lael Wilcox [ELITE] arrived about a day later (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Route Planning (Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]):**
- Originally planned 6 days, had up to 9 days available as buffer
- Strategy shift: "enjoy the scenery and the ride more, ride less empty, take more care of myself"
- Used multiple navigation tools: Komoot app for next sections, track transferred to Google Maps with supply points and accommodation marked, Epic Ride Weather app for weather tracking
- Created printed roadbook with supply points and minefield information (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Golo's Daily Distances [UNKNOWN] (2023 tour, recreational pace):**
- Day 1: 100km, 900m elevation
- Day 2: 85km, 1,300m elevation  
- Day 3: 94km, 900m elevation
- Day 5: 98km, 1,400m elevation
- Day 6: 98km, 900m elevation
- Day 7: 113km, 1,700m elevation
- Day 8: 68km, 900m elevation (shorter day, premium gravel)
(https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Pack Weight (Martin Moschek [UNKNOWN]):**
- Total system weight: 19.5-20kg without water, including bike and 1kg of gels/bars
- Packed "very loosely" with storage space still available for carrying food/drink on the road (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Minefield Danger Zones:**
Clear markings in roadbook distinguish between "Warning of mines" and "High Danger" areas. Critical safety rules: avoid traveling through at night when possible, stay on track, do not bivouac or take breaks off the trail. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Environmental Challenges:**
- "Long, remote sections" with "limited water access" (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)
- "Massive climbs" (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)
- Temperature swings of 20Â°C in just a couple hours in karst areas (https://www.bombtrack.com/events-database/tbr2025)
- "Flourishing forests, where paths are often muddy and slippery" (https://www.bombtrack.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Mental Challenge:**
The final 340km with 6,700m climbing after already having 1,000km and 20,000m in the legs described as "a good test of character." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2024/05/26/my-next-adventure-trans-balkan-race-2024/)

**Local Warnings (Golo's experience [UNKNOWN]):**
Hotel staff warnings about routes included: bad roads, uninhabited areas, war damage, bears, wolves, and wild boars - though the route turned out to be rideable. (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Growing Popularity:**
"The Trans Balkan Race has been increasing in popularity each year despite a challenging route" - nearly 160 riders signed up for 2025, up from over 100 at the 2022 inaugural. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Notable Participants (2025):**
Leanne Bentley, Justinas Leveika (2024 Tour Divide winner), Adrien Liechti (2023 Ascend Armenia winner), Jenny Tough (two-time SRMR winner), Sofiane Sehili. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Cultural Context:**
"In the common imagination, the simultaneous proximity and distance of the Balkans depict an exotic 'other' Europe. It evokes images of war, chaos, and wild territories. Yet, these memories left today an enigmatic region, ready to be explored." (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**Photo Documentation:**
"The organizers at Exploro.cc brought in photographer Giuliano Koren to capture the riders' rigs photos before the event started" - became a tradition in recent years. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/rigs-of-the-2025-trans-balkan-race/)

**On-Ground Support:**
Organizers note "The organization does not provide any support â€“ other than two supervised Checkpoints and GPS tracking to follow the race." (https://www.bombtrack.com/events-database/tbr2025)

**Language & Locals (from Golo's touring experience [UNKNOWN]):**
- Older people often speak German, younger people speak good English
- Communication with non-English speakers possible with hands and feet
- "Die Leute hier auch relativ gut Englisch und Deutsch" [People here speak relatively good English and German]
- Managed to communicate with a mountain farmer "eigentlich auch ohne Englisch oder Deutsch ganz gut" [actually quite well without English or German]
- Younger people in gastronomy/tourism "sprechen alle sehr gut Englisch" [all speak very good English]
(https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)

**Historical Sites:**
Route passes through Jajce, described as offering "viel Sehenswertes" [much worth seeing]: 14th-century castle with mysterious catacombs, church, several old mosques, Mithras cult site, waterfall, and museum about Yugoslavia's founding during WWII. (https://bikeboard.at/forum/topic/262848-balkan-gravel/)
