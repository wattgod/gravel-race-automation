## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Setup - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
- Habanero titanium cyclocross bike with disc brakes
- Avid BB7 Road disc brakes with organic compound pads
- Shimano Ultegra 6700 10-speed components
- Zipp 303 carbon clincher disc brake wheels
- **Gearing regret:** "My gearing was not substantial enough, with a 50/36 set of chainrings and 11-27 cassette. With climbs reported over 20% grade... you will need some serious gear... Even if I am fitter going in to the race next time around, I'll either use a smaller chainring, a larger cassette, or both."
(https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**Tire Choice - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
- Used Michelin Jet 700x30 cyclocross tires at 42psi
- "For dry courses, they are my favorite 'cross tire – great ride quality, great grip, and seemingly great puncture resistance"
- **Important caveat:** "Next time around, however, I would put Michelin's Mud 2 model on my front wheel – unless the course happens to be substantially dryer. I didn't crash on the Jets, but there were a few moments that I would've appreciated a little bit more grip – downhill off-camber turns and other pucker-worthy moments."
(https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**Brakes - Multiple sources [ELITE/UNKNOWN]:**
- "Disc brakes were a common sight at the race" - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]
- "Disc brakes are a plus in poor weather, but not mandatory" - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]
(https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**Bike Type - Race Description [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Participants use a variety of bicycle types (cyclocross, mountain, road, fat), depending on course conditions and individual preferences"
- Mountain bikes work well, but will cost you speed on pavement sections
- Fat bike specific categories offered in 18 & 36 mile races
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

**Spares & Tools - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
- "Bring at least one tube and a C02"
- "I highly recommend pre-installing sealant into your inner tubes to prevent punctures"
- "Make sure it hasn't been neglected; I even saw folks with simple single speed bikes having mechanical issues"
(https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition [OFFICIAL]:**
- "Most years, Barry-Roubaix consists of rolling gravel roads (80%), pavement, one mile of rough two-track, rocks, sand, mud, and possibly snow and ice, along with 2200 feet of climbing" for the 62-mile race
- 100-mile: ~7,000+ feet of elevation
- 62-mile: ~3,800-4,000 feet of elevation  
- 36-mile: ~2,200 feet of elevation
- 18-mile: ~1,200 feet of elevation
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

**Named Signature Sections [MULTIPLE SOURCES]:**
- **The Three Sisters:** "First set of hills within the first 2 miles of the race" (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)
- **Sager Road:** "A one mile stretch of sandy, rutted uphill two track" - "Eye of the Tiger climb, Sager Road, and the Three Sisters" mentioned as challenging sections (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix & https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)
- **The Wall:** "You can guess why" (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)
- **The Killer:** A specific hill that "forced the majority of participants to walk" per Greg Kopecky [ELITE] (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**Course Scenery [RACE OWNER]:**
"The area is so scenic with dense tree lined roads, a beautiful abundance of lakes, streams and bogs and of course the HILLS!" (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)

**Mud Conditions - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
"With all of the mud pits, I saw more than one person on the side of the road tending to a jammed drivetrain. This isn't a short-course 'cross race with power washers and bike exchanges – you must start and finish on the same machine." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**2016 Heat Effect [OFFICIAL]:**
"Dry and dusty course conditions made for a fast race which took on the character of a road race with large pelotons staying together until the finish. The top four riders all had the same finishing time (2:54:56)" (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

**2017 Conditions [ELITE]:**
"The event was notable due to the freezing rain that had come in shortly after the race began" (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Hydration Strategy - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
"There are a few aid stations on course, which feature food, water, and sports drink. You must re-fill your bottles (e.g. they don't hand you brand new bottles). I used a hydration pack and two frame bottles, and only had to refill one bottle with water." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**Early Season Fitness Reality [RACE OWNER]:**
"The event is the first major race and test of cyclists' early season condition." (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)

**Gearing for Climbs - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
With climbs over 20% grade, standard compact gearing (50/36 with 11-27 cassette) was insufficient even for an elite rider who pushed through The Killer climb when most walked. Recommendation: smaller chainring and/or larger cassette needed. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**2011 DNF Rate [OFFICIAL]:**
"Over 1000 participants started the race, but only 896 finished" - approximately 10% DNF rate in cold conditions (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

**2022 DNF Rate [OFFICIAL]:**
"4006 registered racers / 2281 finished" - approximately 43% DNF rate in "cold and wintry mix" conditions, the harshest documented DNF rate (https://barry-roubaix.com/results)

**Mechanical Failures - Greg Kopecky [ELITE]:**
- Jammed drivetrains from mud
- "I even saw folks with simple single speed bikes having mechanical issues"
- Recommends starting with "a perfectly clean and tuned drivetrain" which "paid dividends"
(https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/barry-roubaix-recap/)

**2013 Medical Incident [OFFICIAL]:**
"The race almost lived up its 'Killer' name, as a participant collapsed on the course but was later revived by a medical team" (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-Race Party [RACE OWNER]:**
"The race remains true to its roots — delivering an exceptional racing experience crowned by an epic after-party filled with hearty food, shared stories, laughter, and, of course, Blackrocks beer." (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)

**Social Aspect [RACE OWNER]:**
"After a long winter of 'hibernation' from your friends, teammates and competitors, the event serves as a reunion and a day of racings, socializing and catching up with everyone from the cycling community." (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)

**Growth & Scale:**
- Started in 2009 with 274 racers (248 finishers)
- 2025: 5,673 registered / 4,476 finished - world record
- Named to Global Cycling Network's "Top Five Gravel Events" and "Nine Coolest Races of 2018"
- Known as "World's Largest Gravel Road Race"
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix & https://barry-roubaix.com/results)

**Recognition [OFFICIAL]:**
"On 26 Jan 2017, the tourism and economic development impact of Barry-Roubaix was recognized as the event tied for The Destination Award, an annual award given by the Barry County, Michigan Chamber of Commerce and Economic Development." Annual economic impact grown to over US$500,000 as of 2016. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

**Youth Support [RACE OWNER]:**
- 18 and under race FREE (since 2018, previously $25)
- Youth team competition with $1,000 prizes for winning organizations
- Program grew from "20-40 youths racing in the earlier years" to "almost 300 in 2025"
- "We pay Barry County organizations... over $38,000 annually" to support race day with 350 volunteers
(https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/barry-roubaix)

**Course Accessibility [OFFICIAL]:**
"In January 2016, 65 permanent road signs were installed in Barry County to mark the three Barry-Roubaix course routes for race participants and recreational cyclists who visit the area to train on the course year-round." (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix)

**Race Day Logistics [OFFICIAL]:**
- Sheriff-escorted neutral rollout (2.5 miles as of 2010)
- Start/finish in downtown Hastings, Michigan
- Awards and after-party with live music, food, bonfires
- Over $40,000 in cash and prizes awarded
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry-Roubaix & https://barry-roubaix.com/results)
