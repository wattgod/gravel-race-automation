# BATTENKILL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**CJ Rudy [COMPETITIVE]:** "To throw the term 'flahute' around is reserved for extreme occasions, and the 2025 Battenkill Race earned itself the label... I rolled out of bed early race morning and stepped outside my Saratoga Springs hotel room just in time to watch the race horses clop-clop down the road on their way to the race track across the street. It was raining. It had rained heavily all night. In fact, it had been raining all week." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**CJ Rudy [COMPETITIVE]:** "Ferguson Road will haunt me for years to come. It appeared immediately after the second feed zone... The road was deep thick mud of toothpaste consistency. There appeared more than one hundred potholes of varying distances plus two gigantic puddles that likely concealed crocodiles... The mud was deep enough to conceal the inner edge of the deep dish rims. Years, I tell you, years." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**CJ Rudy [COMPETITIVE]:** "What Battenkill does really well is provide remarkable scenery for those who were shot out the back. This region of New York is simply cycling paradise. The views are beautiful. There are rolling hills that defy imagination." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**MDcatV [COMPETITIVE]:** "that was a pretty awesome race. the gravel is what everyone talks about, but i didnt think it to be particularly significant in the outcome, the relentless 6K feet of 1 to 3 minute climbs, that's another story... suffered, did a bunch of fat guy slip back climbing, descended like an insane person, attacked at mi 65, got dropped at mi. 70 (meetinghouse road for those who watched the utube above) followed the SRAM car back onto the group, got dropped again on the next climb, and finished otb. no result to speak of, but a great way to spend my 40th birthday." (https://www.bikeforums.net/33-road-bike-racing/725376-battenkill-roll-call-print.html)

**GirlAnachronism [ELITE]:** "I got a flat (said in Charlie Brown voice)." (https://www.bikeforums.net/33-road-bike-racing/725376-battenkill-roll-call-print.html)

**FormerRower [ELITE]:** "Crashed hard early in the Cat 2 field, managed to get back into the final group of 15 or so (before 10 more bridged), and then ended up top 20. Wish I did not have to spend so much energy early post crash, but that is racing." (https://www.bikeforums.net/33-road-bike-racing/725376-battenkill-roll-call-print.html)

**FrankTuna [UNKNOWN]:** "I've done this ride a few times on my gravel bike and you could absolutely do it on a road bike (preferably with wider tires). Nothing wrong with the Trek Marlin (I have one!) but a road/gravel bike will be faster. It's a fun ride with some beautiful scenery too." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/the-great-north-battenkill-valley/105123)

**Saddlesaur [UNKNOWN]:** "Of course you can ride it on your Marlin, but there's nothing at Battenkill that requires an mtb. A gravel race bike would be optimal. You'll just take a little longer to complete it than on an optimal bike." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/the-great-north-battenkill-valley/105123)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire/Bike Choice [COMPETITIVE - Strambecco]:** "Despite the risk of punctures, most riders go with road vs. gravel bikes. The gravel sections are, by and large, hard-packed and relatively smooth, although there are some loose sections to watch out for." (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

**Official Event Guidance [COMPETITIVE]:** "In order of faster to slower: road bike, gravel bike, mountain bike. Reverse these if you're looking to prioritize comfort. Racers will be using road bikes with 28-32mm tires, and everyone else will ride whatever they have or makes them happy." (https://anthemsportstours.com/battenkill)

**Tire Preparation [COMPETITIVE - Strambecco]:** "The list of DNFs isn't overly long, but the dirt sections can often result in punctured tires, so be sure to bring plenty of spares, pumps, CO2 cartridges, or perhaps tubeless tires, sealant, or extra-big rubber." (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

**Rain Gear [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "I geared for warmth in the first two hours and likely over dressed for the remainder of the race. This is precisely what happened." When anticipating rain stopping mid-race, consider that overdressing for cold/wet conditions early can lead to overheating later. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Water Bottle Cage Maintenance [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "I did snap a single picture from the summit of Juniper Swamp aftering having to tighten the water bottle cage bosses." The muddy gravel conditions can loosen bottle cage bolts. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Race Day [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "It was raining. It had rained heavily all night. In fact, it had been raining all week... It was cool enough to play havoc on attire... With an hour to go in my day, the rain stopped. The clouds loosened and the sun shone down in that blinding way it tends to do after a soaker." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**2025 Gravel Conditions [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "These gravel roads differ from those in southeastern Pennsylvania and western New Jersey. Today's unpaved roads appeared slick but firm. The relentless rain had saturated the top levels of the road. Typically firm and fast, the Battenkill gravel sectors were mushy and spongy, sapping any power." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**2011 Course Conditions [ELITE - Racer Ex]:** "Recon indicates that the roads are molasses." Pre-race report from 2011. (https://www.bikeforums.net/33-road-bike-racing/725376-battenkill-roll-call-print.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Gravel Sector Reality [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "What appeared to be pebbled tracks were just as absorptive as the middle of the road. Every inch of a cyclist's facade was covered in soot. In no time at all, we could feel that flahute ranking sliding into our jersey pocket." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Juniper Swamp Climb [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Juniper Swamp, a steep gravel climb that hits 14% early into the race, was a struggle. Stand up and risk losing traction or remain seated and feel the gravel reaching up and holding forward motion ransom. The bike began to protest by making unique noises. Grinding, clicking, and generally loud unhealthy sounds emanated from the mechanism." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Ferguson Road (Critical Section) [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Ferguson Road will haunt me for years to come. It appeared immediately after the second feed zone. It came as an innocent right hand turn after the route went up through a cow farm. What Washington County revealed to the Battenkill Race was something out of a disaster. The road was deep thick mud of toothpaste consistency. There appeared more than one hundred potholes of varying distances plus two gigantic puddles that likely concealed crocodiles. I avoided every divot as best as possible. The roadway was full of bottles, likely from the front groups flying into Ferguson, fresh off the feed zone, and everyone taking evasive maneuvers to remain competitive." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Opening Miles [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Like Anthem Sports' other event, Tour of the Catskills, Battenkill Race features a slightly downhill start for nearly seven miles. These opening miles are perfect for dialing in the day. Nutrition is explored amidst the pack. Things will get dicey after the covered bridge... The field slowed the roll to approach the covered bridge, arguably due to the pinch point as well as the darkness within." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Course Characteristics [COMPETITIVE - MDcatV]:** "the gravel is what everyone talks about, but i didnt think it to be particularly significant in the outcome, the relentless 6K feet of 1 to 3 minute climbs, that's another story." (https://www.bikeforums.net/33-road-bike-racing/725376-battenkill-roll-call-print.html)

**Gravel Percentage [COMPETITIVE - Dieter Drake]:** "Each course will be about 20% gravel" according to Race Director Dieter Drake. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/tour-of-the-battenkill-founder-revitalises-elite-one-day-races-in-upstate-new-york-after-seven-year-absence/)

**Gravel Description [Official]:** "There's no chunky gravel, no single track, no couches, no river crossings, no dismounting for mud or trees or anything like that. In Washington County we just call them 'dirt roads' and we've been riding them on bikes before there was anything called 'gravel.' You'll love them." (https://anthemsportstours.com/battenkill)

**Total Course Stats [COMPETITIVE]:** "The main Gran Fondo course comes in at ~75 Miles and ~5,500 feet of elevation gain. The route features many rollers and several notably steep climbs on gravel with pitches reaching 18% grade." (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

**Gravel Sectors [COMPETITIVE - Strambecco]:** "Battenkill's 12 dirt sections spanning 18 miles are its trademark" (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

**Railroad Tracks Start [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Ten feet in, the slick railroad tracks were overcome without drama." Note that railroad tracks are encountered immediately at the start when wet. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Race Start Dynamics [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Riders fidgeted with rain capes to show numbers as the one minute warning was called out... Everyone rode as close as possible for warmth but as far as possible in case of catastrophe." Starting strategy in wet conditions from 2025. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Where Race Begins [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Battenkill veterans knew what to expect after the bridge: a few punchy climbs and the first gravel sector. This is where the race begins, and this year was no different." The covered bridge marks the transition from neutral/easy to racing. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Feed Zone Positioning [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** Ferguson Road "appeared immediately after the second feed zone" - riders need to be cautious about hitting difficult sections right after feed zones. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Nutrition Failure [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "With an hour to go in my day, the rain stopped... I had consumed only half of a water bottle and no nutrition. The bottles were caked with grime and I was too focused on maintaining control of the bike that I never went into the back pocket for gels or pictures." Example of how technical terrain can derail nutrition plans. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Race Dynamics [COMPETITIVE - MDcatV]:** Rider described "attacked at mi 65, got dropped at mi. 70 (meetinghouse road)... followed the SRAM car back onto the group, got dropped again on the next climb" - indicating selection happening in the 65-70 mile range. (https://www.bikeforums.net/33-road-bike-racing/725376-battenkill-roll-call-print.html)

**Race Starting Groups [Official]:** "Riders are grouped by ability at the starting line and begin simultaneously at 8:30 a.m. with a neutral rollout... The first handful of miles feature a full police escort and rolling road closure." (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Vibe [COMPETITIVE - Strambecco]:** "a one-of-a-kind race for those seeking a serious challenge shrouded in pastoral beauty. The result is that everyone who finishes, even everyone who starts, goes home with rich stories to tell." (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

**Course Support [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Battenkill excels in the dedication of its volunteers and first responders. Numerous parts of the course had ambulance presence. Major intersections featured police officers or state troopers. The remaining roads had volunteers eagerly guiding riders on their way back to Cambridge where the finish party was getting started. The entire event felt safe; the riders were well cared-for." (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**Multi-Format Appeal [COMPETITIVE - CJ Rudy]:** "Eventually it was my turn to be overtaken. My old race category came rampaging by. I wanted to shout out my credentials of the past for some recognition, but they raced over the next climb and were never seen again. Things have changed at Battenkill. I've changed, too... I rolled through the finish with the rider who had commented about road bikes and Ferguson Road." Mix of race categories and fondo riders sharing the course creates interesting dynamics. (https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)

**After Party [Official]:** "The event has a very nice after-party with great food, entertainment, and libations." (https://strambecco.com/event/tour-of-the-battenkill/)

**Included Amenities [Official]:** "Everyone gets the famously popular Battenkill tee, a custom Battenkill pint glass, SAG & mechanical support, stocked aid stations, and police & volunteer support at critical intersections on the course. While USAC category racers get official support & limited field sizes, gran fondo riders get (1) free beer & a meal voucher at the Battenkill Expo & After Party." (https://anthemsportstours.com/battenkill)

**Historic Significance [COMPETITIVE - Verge Sport]:** "Before gravel riding became cycling's hottest trend, there was Battenkill. For years, this legendary race sent riders bouncing across the unpaved back roads of upstate New York, earning its nickname as 'America's Queen of the Classics.' Then it vanished. Now, after a seven-year silence, the Queen is ready to reclaim her crown." (https://www.vergesport.com/blogs/news/battenkill-gravel-race-custom-cycling-jerseys)

**Organizer Philosophy [Official]:** "Is it a corporation-run event? No way. Grassroots cycling forever." From Anthem Sports FAQ. (https://anthemsportstours.com/battenkill)

**Community Impact [Official]:** "The event will also include several community outreach events, fundraising opportunities for local organizations, live entertainment, food trucks, and a race expo for vendors and community groups next to the finish line in Cambridge." (https://anthemsportstours.com/battenkill)

**Field Size History [Official]:** "more than 3,000 riders from 48 States & 20 countries" at the event's peak under original organizers. The 2025 return "hosted well over one thousand cyclists." (https://anthemsportstours.com/battenkill and https://www.creakybottombracket.com/home/2025/5/12/events-battenkill-race-2025)
