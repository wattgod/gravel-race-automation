# BEAR HOWARD GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Course Overview & Distances

The Bear Howard Gravel offers three distances:
- **100 miles**: Described as "the crown jewel" with "just over 6k of total elevation gain" including "a cool section of single track." The course is "sprinkled with a little asphalt but mostly gravel." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)
- **60 miles**: "Just over a 60 mile loop staying west of the peaks" with "2,800 ft of climbing" (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)
- **30 miles**: Described as "Simply the Bud Light of Bear Howard. You get the start and end of the big loops, just not the filling of the middle." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

All routes start in Flagstaff, AZ and finish at Lowell Observatory.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Difficulty Assessment [COMPETITIVE]:** The organizers emphasize: "the 100 mile course is NO JOKE. It is both challenging in its features, it will test your bike handling as well as your fitness. It is not the distance of UNBOUND, we continue to get feedback that this is epic and certainly no walk in the park...this is a challenging course and should not be underestimated." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Specific Features:**
- The 100 includes "a challenging out and back climb up Saddle Mountain" with a $250 cash prize for the segment winner (determined by Strava) (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)
- Course includes "a few sections of technical gravel" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/9471/bear-howard-gravel-race)
- One section runs on state highway 180 with posted volunteers to direct traffic (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

## Aid Stations & Support

**Number of Aid Stations:**
- 100 mile: 3 aid stations (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)
- 60 mile: 2 aid stations (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)
- 30 mile: 1 aid station (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**What's Provided:** "Neutral water, nutrition and snacks" at all aid stations (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Important Aid Station Etiquette:** "Be Kind to the aid volunteers, they are not your personal mechanic or even your personal support, they are friendly and will assist how they can, so be aware they are also assisting other racers." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Dietary Restrictions:** "If you have dietary restrictions, please consider packing enough food on your bike for the entire ride." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

## Navigation & Course Marking

**Course Marking:** "The course will be fully marked with large green and yellow signs at every turn and questionable intersections. Full GPS maps are available to download." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**GPS Usage:** "Yes! While we embrace the spirit of the frontier, we recognize we are in the 21st century. We have upped our game in course marking and will have arrows ribbon and paint on the road to show you the way. Also a GPS files." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Navigation Warning:** Organizers note: "please make yourself familiar with the course and major intersections or turns, we do the best we can and continue to improve but missing turns happen." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

## Race Rules & Important Notes

**Traffic Laws:** "Obey all the traffic laws, the road is open to traffic...stay on your side of the road...If you do encounter a car on the gravel roads, PLEASE get over to let them by...please, please for the future of the event obey all traffic laws." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**DNF Policy:** "Did Not Finish. That's okay, there is always next year and now you know what to expect!" (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**No Refunds:** "We will not be offering refunds or comp'd entries for the next year of any kind." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:** "In the spirit of adventure and exploration, like Bear Howard himself, we came up with this event to leave you feeling legendary (if only in your own mind). Sure the stories that follow around the campfire will be exaggerated and inflated, fueled by whiskey and grandeur, but we promise you adventure and a good time with this classic ride in the shadow of Flagstaffs peaks." (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Primary Rule:** "DON'T BE A JERK. You know what that means. Don't cheat, cut the course, punch the guy next to you in the finish line chute, yelling at volunteers etc. This is supposed to fun remember!" (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Finish Location:** Race finishes at Lowell Observatory, which "has just completed a state of the art observatory and visitors center" with "a Bear Howard discount" available for families/spectators. (https://www.sableevents.com/events/bearhowardgravel)

**Race Swag Included:** "Race number, socks, finishers glass, and Awards and finishers party at Lowell Observatory" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/9471/bear-howard-gravel-race)

**Community Mission:** Sable Events states: "At Sable Events, we believe that gravel racing is more than just a sport – it's a community. We are committed to fostering a welcoming and inclusive environment where riders can come together to push themselves and each other to new heights." (https://www.sableevents.com/about)
