# BEAVER DAM GRAVEL GRINDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Basics & Location Details

**What:** Nevada's only gravel grinder event, held at Beaver Dam State Park in Lincoln County
**When:** Annual event in mid-June (2025 date: June 21)
**Distance Options:** 35-mile and 45-mile routes, plus a 10-mile option for novices
**Registration Fee:** $40-$60 (has increased over the years)
**Participant Cap:** Limited to 60 participants

**Getting There:**
- 3.5 hours northeast of Las Vegas via I-15 N to US-93 N to Beaver Dam Road East
- Final 30 miles is on gravel road to reach the park
- 2 hours west of Cedar City, Utah
- Park is at approximately 5,000 feet elevation

**What's Included:**
- Park entry
- Continental breakfast (fruit, muffins)
- Lunch at historic Barclay Schoolhouse
- Post-ride BBQ dinner
- Water and snack stops along route

(https://beaverdamgravelgrinder.com/, https://parks.nv.gov/events/gravel-grinder-2, https://lincolncountynevada.com/events/spring-events/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Mark Orton, USA Cycling certified Level 1 coach [COMPETITIVE]:** "Gravel grinders are rides, (sometimes races) that combine riding on surfaces such as asphalt, gravel dirt, some single-track trails and maintenance or B roads. The courses vary from hard pack dirt to softball sized rocks. That pretty much describes this ride to a T." (https://eurekasentinel.com/2017/06/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder-june-17/)

**Course Characteristics:**
- Mostly dirt and gravel backcountry and jeep roads
- "Some rocky patches that can be considered technical, but we have tried to keep those to a minimum"
- Rain events can change terrain and sometimes force route changes
- Sections can be sandy
- Route winds up through pines and junipers
- Rides along the rim of the park with views of volcanic formations
- Passes old dams and ranching communities
- Lunch stop at one-room schoolhouse in Barclay
- Described as "Gently rolling terrain with one or two short hills"

**Technical Note:** One section formerly had a beaver dam but "is now an improved gravel road" as of 2017

(https://www.cyclingwest.com/gravel-biking/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder-comes-south-east-nevada-june/, https://lincolncountynevada.com/events/spring-events/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder/, https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2017/9/24/recap-candyland-a-gravel-adventure)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Requirements [ALL LEVELS]:**
"This ride is not skinny-tire road bike friendly. If you have a fat-tire bike, mountain bike, cyclocross bike, or adventure/all-purpose bike you will be able to do this ride."

**Self-Sufficiency Required [ALL LEVELS]:**
"Though we do offer some support, this is a gravel grinder and not a gran fondo, it emphasizes radical self reliance and features few amenities. You need to be technically savvy and bring any gear you might need to replace or fix tires, chains, or any other possible pitfalls."

**What to Bring:**
- Tire repair equipment (for both technical rocky sections and sandy areas)
- Chain repair tools
- Personal food/drink preferences (basic water and snacks provided at stops)

(https://beaverdamgravelgrinder.com/about-the-gravel-grinder/, https://lincolncountynevada.com/events/spring-events/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy - Dawn Andone, Park Interpreter/Organizer [UNKNOWN]:**
"This is one of the reasons we started the ride – to get people into the beautiful backcountry. It's a side of Nevada most people do not experience."

"So come join us! Laugh, exercise, socialize, talk, laugh again, meet new people and create new friendships, but most of all RIDE!"

**Local Community Involvement:**
"In speaking with Dawn, we learned that the local residents were absolutely crucial in pulling off the ride. During their first year, many stood on the sidelines curious about the strange event. A few years later, they were clamoring for another event to bring in more visitors."

**Return Riders:**
"Many return year after year for that very reason alone" - referring to the hospitality and backcountry experience

**Post-Ride:**
BBQ dinner served "under the pavilion in the park where the ride starts and ends"
Ends with "food" and community gathering

(https://eurekasentinel.com/2017/06/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder-june-17/, https://beaverdamgravelgrinder.com/about-the-gravel-grinder/, https://www.pathlesspedaled.com/2015/05/bicycle-tourism-gravel-the-next-big-thing-in-nevada/)

## Lodging & Weekend Planning

**Camping (Recommended):**
"Stay at the Park! Come camp in one of two campgrounds at the park. The ride starts at 7:30 am and the closest town is 35 miles down a gravel road. Camping is your best option. Stay Friday and Saturday night."

**Nearby Towns (if not camping):**
- Caliente: Closest town with motel accommodations, 35 miles from park
- Also available: Panaca, Pioche, and Alamo
- "We recommend looking into reservations as soon as possible because the summer months tend to book up fast"

**Other Nearby State Parks:**
- Kershaw-Ryan State Park
- Cathedral Gorge State Park
- Four other state parks in the area

**Weekend Activities:**
- Hiking, biking, fishing in the park
- Pioche: old mining town
- Ghost towns in the area
- Trilobite fossil digging site

(https://www.cyclingwest.com/gravel-biking/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder-comes-south-east-nevada-june/, https://lincolncountynevada.com/gravel-grinder-challenge-lincoln-countys-ultimate-ride/)

## Natural & Historical Features

**Geology:**
"Millions of years of volcanic activity formed the dramatic geologic outcrops of igneous and sedimentary rock, including pink rhyolite, dark red and grey andesite and white ash-fall tuff. Obsidian and volcanic glass nodules may also be found in the park."

**Flora:**
- Pinyon pines and junipers dominate
- Ponderosa pines near springs
- Gambel and shrub live-oak
- Sumacs and small-tooth maples (fall color)
- Willows and cottonwoods along wash

**Wildlife:**
Native beavers present in the area

**Historical Features:**
- Historic one-room Barclay Schoolhouse (lunch stop)
- Old homesteads along route
- Isolated ranches
- Old dams
- 2,393-acre park in "rugged canyon country"

(https://www.cyclingwest.com/gravel-biking/beaver-dam-gravel-grinder-comes-south-east-nevada-june/)
