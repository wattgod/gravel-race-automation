## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The Bend Dirt Fest appears to be part of the Oregon Triple Crown Series, a gravel racing series that has been running since 2013. According to promotional materials: "What makes the Oregon Triple Crown Series great is YOU! Whether you smash out 20mph for over 6,000ft of climbing or take it a bit easier with the hills and the speed on our shorter loops, you'll have an unforgettable day of fun and camaraderie on the bike." (https://cascadiamtb.com/2025/11/20/oregon-triple-crown-2026/)

The series emphasizes inclusivity: "Whether you are new to the sport or an experienced racer, we welcome you to our OTC family!" (https://cascadiamtb.com/2025/11/20/oregon-triple-crown-2026/)

**General Oregon Triple Crown community sentiment [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Multiple riders in the Girls Gone Gravel survey cited Oregon Triple Crown events among their top gravel destinations, suggesting these events have a positive reputation within the gravel community. ## Event Details

**Location:** Bend, Oregon

**Date:** July 11, 2026 (https://cascadiamtb.com/2025/11/20/oregon-triple-crown-2026/)

**Series Context:** The Bend Dirt Fest is Race #7 in the Oregon Triple Crown Series. Other races in the series include:
- Real West Gravel (March 15)
- Sasquatch Duro (April 11)
- Oregon Coast Gravel Epic (May 2)
- West Coast Gravel (May 3)
- Oregon Gran Fondo (May 16)
- Takelma Gravel Grinder (June 27)
- High Cascades 100 (July 18)

The series is produced by Mudslinger Events and involves collaboration with local rural towns and land agencies. (https://cascadiamtb.com/2025/11/20/oregon-triple-crown-2026/)

**2025 Event Success:** "Inaugural Bend Dirt Fest a Big Success" - headline from July 13, 2025, suggesting the event had a positive first year. (https://cascadiamtb.com/2025/11/20/oregon-triple-crown-2026/)

## Bend Area Context

Bend, Oregon is described as having "three most popular trails in the area—Lower Phil's, Funner/Tiddlywinks, and North Fork/South Fork" for mountain biking. (https://cascadiamtb.com/tag/bend/)

The area has a significant professional cycling presence, with pro riders including Carson Storch, Reed Boggs, Kyle Jameson, and Kate Meyer living in Bend. (https://cascadiamtb.com/tag/bend/)

Bend hosts multiple cycling events including:
- Mt. Bachelor Bike Park with lift-served downhill trails
- Black Sage freeride mountain bike events at Oregon Dirt Park
- High Cascades 100 gravel race (featuring "Moon Dust & Mountains")
