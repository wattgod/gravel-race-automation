# BERRYMAN EPIC — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Mitch "MashOr" [ELITE]:** "This year the field was stacked with top level riders, weather was perfect and the trail conditions have never been better... This new trail and the stellar conditions allowed a new unofficial course record of 4 hours 9 minutes to be set by Kansas native and long time pro Steve Tilford." (https://gorctrails.com/blog/berryman-trail-epic)

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "The course was super dry and loose. So loose that descending was much trickier than with water running down the trail. And climbing was only a seated motion. It was nearly impossible to stand without spinning your rear wheel." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "I flatted on nothing. I mean there was barely a rock to be seen. And that was the end of the story. It just got worse from there. Once you have to put a tube in riding here, it is nearly impossible to keep air in it." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "57 miles...5 hours 43 minutes...1 broken water bottle cage...1 flat tire...53rd place overall...7th place in 20-29 age grouping...1st overall finisher under the age of 25." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "I used every single stretch of gearing in this race, from granny-granny to big ring/smallest cog. A bike with full suspension and gears may be in my future... So this race is 57 miles, 46+ of which is singletrack, the other 9ish miles are on fire/gravel roads." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "I rolled into the Berryman campground at the second checkpoint in 1:57. Mia told me that I was 17 minutes off the leaders and roughly in the top 25... I had just made it through the rough section and was rolling along a creek bed (mile 41) when I heard the dreaded Psssssss! and saw Stan's spewing out my rear tire." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "Then at mile 46 my rear tire exploded!! Not again! The Gods must have been angry... From the tread almost to the bead! The 'white' is an empty hammer gel packet. I had managed to lose my patch during the last tire change." Rolling in at 5:33:04 for 2nd place women. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "The descent to the Brazil Creek crossing was a sloppy muddy mess but there was a temporary bridge built across the water which was nice. My legs were covered in mud by that point but anything that kept my toes from freezing water was appreciated." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "When I got off my bike and straightened my legs in the cold water they started cramping and I couldn't move for about 20 seconds. It was extremely painful... I crossed the finish line after 5 hours, 30 minutes and 51 seconds of racing; in 21st place. 16 seconds away from that top 20 I was after." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Selection - CRITICAL for this course:**

**Jim C. [UNKNOWN]:** "Try running a WTB Nano on the front, and a Maxxis CrossMark on the rear… both tubeless at about 25-28 psi. I've run this combo for years with no problems… In my opinion, the Nano is the perfect front tire, except in clay mud situations where it will pack up. It floats in sand, rolls extremely well on hardpack, and steers well in almost any condition. I even used this combo last year at Berryman." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/22/berryman-epic-t-12-hours/)

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "I definitely need to keep more up to date on the tires that are available for rock stuff like this. I had a slit right in the very top of my tread... I have the heaviest tires I own on my bicycle now. I've never raced a MTB bike this heavy since I won the NORBA Nationals in 1983. I can't do anything else. I have a cup of latex in each tire." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/ and https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/22/berryman-epic-t-12-hours/)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** Ran tubeless with Stan's sealant but had catastrophic failure: "Then at mile 46 my rear tire exploded!! [...] From the tread almost to the bead!" Used an empty Hammer gel packet as a tire boot to limp to finish. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Bike Setup:**

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "SC let me borrow his Mad Max bike fully geared with baby wheels. I used every single stretch of gearing in this race, from granny-granny to big ring/smallest cog. A bike with full suspension and gears may be in my future..." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Scott Henry [COMPETITIVE]:** Rode "only a single 36 tooth ring in front and a 36 in the back, so with 29'r wheels, he was a bit over geared" and "was doing little interval sessions up the climbs" as a result. (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Hydration Systems:**

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "I decided to ride with my camelbak for the race so I'd be able to carry more water. I've only got one bottle cage on my bike and didn't think it would get me through to the checkpoints." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "I broke the water bottle cage probably about 20 miles in so had to stop for like 10 seconds to stuff it into my jersey." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:**

**Mitch "MashOr" [ELITE]:** "55 mile mountain bike race... 24 mile loop that is the Berryman Trail... an additional and spectacular 15 miles of the 45 mile Courtois (pronounced code-away) section of the Ozark Trail along with 16 miles of rolling gravel roads." (https://gorctrails.com/blog/berryman-trail-epic)

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "57 miles, 46+ of which is singletrack, the other 9ish miles are on fire/gravel roads. The race begins with a pretty easily graded gravel climb to the first part of single track." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Rock Character:**

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "The trails are rocky. Really rocky... The next 15 miles are the rockiest parts." Also noted "This course is so rocky, that even half-pint Catherine burped her tire and had to reinflate it." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** Describing Brazil Creek section: "lots of sketchy riding on sandy/loose gravel... some of these rock gardens and roots and killer climbs was taking a toll." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Trail Improvements:**

**Mitch "MashOr" [ELITE]:** "The Ozark Trail Association has spent countless hours re-routing the west side of Berryman... Removed were sections of trail that followed creek beds and had developed into unsustainable washes that would rattle your teeth. Added are flowing sections of benchcut trail that crosses the same creeks as before, but on solid rock faces that will stand the test of time." (https://gorctrails.com/blog/berryman-trail-epic)

**Mitch "MashOr" [ELITE]:** "There are plans to continue this work on the Berryman with a major re-route that will eliminate the only unridable section that drops you down 4 steep switch backs to Brazil Creek. The new trail will head over to hwy W on a less steep hillside and no longer will you have to cross the often deep Brazil Creek. This was tough to do in the winter time and keep dry feet." (https://gorctrails.com/blog/berryman-trail-epic)

**Topography:**

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "These alternate between ridge tops and creek bottoms. The topography is gently rolling; the longest climb was probably 0.2 miles and at a relatively easy grade. This, combined with dry conditions, made for a superfast course. I spent a lot of time in the big ring." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "None of the hills were all that steep but many of them were several minute climbs and when you combine that with the slick conditions it starts to wear on you." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Specific Technical Sections:**

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "On one of the side cut sections in the first 11 miles, my front tire washed out and down I went... I rode most of the horsed-up switchbacks down to Brazil Creek and the first checkpoint." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "I encountered a 30 yard sand trap (which I ran through), a couple steep loose climbs, and a lot of 'horsed-up' sections of trail." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "The first couple miles [after final checkpoint] was technical with ledgey drops and lots of roots." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "What made it technical in areas was the loose rocky soil, horse damage, and erosion on some of the climbs/descents." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Course Navigation Quirk:**

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "When I looked left I saw there were tracks in the ditch beside the highway so I turned into the ditch and kept going. That ditch was horrible. It had standing water and the weeds were so thick it felt like I had everything pushing against me as I rode... When I looked at the highway that I'd just ridden beside I got really pissed off. I could have ridden on the shoulder of the road and avoided the hell of the ditch!" (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Strategy:**

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "The race starts on a road and goes up a mile long loose gravel climb. At the top of the climb, there were only 4 of us left... I planned to enter the first single track in the lead, but Scott Henry sprinted like crazy and dove into it ahead of me. Afterward, I found out there was a $50 prime for the first guy into the single track." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "The start was FAST and hectic. I rubbed wheels with more than one racer... There were 3 gradual miles climbing on loose gravel before the single track so I settled into a slightly higher effort than I wanted, but knew that I could recover from... Settling into 'stealth' mode I took it easy and watched my competition for any signs of weakness." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "I managed to line up within the first few rows (250 racers in all)" - positioning matters at mass start. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Pacing Through Race:**

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "I made a pass when she bobbled on a sketchy climb, dug deep to open a gap, and settled into a 5 hour race pace after about 15 minutes... 11 miles in :58:47; on pace with the pain cave no where in sight." Target was sub-5 hours. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "I was still feeling pretty good so I kept pushing myself on the flats and hills... As I rode up I was told that only 'about' 15 people had come through ahead of me. That picked my spirits up quite a bit and made me want to get back on the trail as fast as possible." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Working With Others:**

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "Not long after getting back on the bike I was able to catch up with Rock and we rode together for the next 12 miles or so. We were both tired and sore but at the same time were able to go faster as a team than we would have alone. I was working to keep up and Rock was working to stay ahead of me." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Final Push:**

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "I glanced down at my watch and it read 3:27:14! Oh yeah, a sub-5 hour was on the horizon. I had 16 miles to go, with 6.5 of that being a slightly rolling to mostly descending fire road back to the finish." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "Once we got to the place we'd turned to get to the trail at the start I knew how much riding was left and decided to make one last push... It turned out the final gravel road section was about 8 miles." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Checkpoint Strategy:**

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "I grabbed a zip tie and blew through, didn't refill bottles or grab any food..it was ok though, weather was mid-70s, kind of cloudy still and a decent breeze. I found out about 3 miles later from a guy that the zip tie area was the aid station and we had like another 12-13 miles to go to station 2." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "Even though my feet were wet and cold I didn't want to take the time to change socks so I just filled my camelbak with gatorade, grabbed a few gu's and took off." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Drop Bag Contents:**

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "I loaded my bag with GU, dry socks and dry gloves. I hoped I'd only need the GU." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Failures - Flats Dominate:**

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "I flatted on nothing. I mean there was barely a rock to be seen. And that was the end of the story. It just got worse from there. Once you have to put a tube in riding here, it is nearly impossible to keep air in it... When I got back to the last feed zone/checkpoint with 19 miles to go I had decided that if I was less than 5 minutes back I would put in a big effort... I made it about 5 miles, going pretty good, before I flatted again... I rode a couple more miles and flatted in a creek crossing." Three flats total, finished off podium despite having winning time pace. (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "At about mile 48, I found an empty Hammer gel packet. Thank God for litterbugs! I deflated my tube, popped the bead, and inserted the Hammer get packet. For the third time I pumped 250 strokes! Unfortunately for my upper body and mental soundness, I did not seat the patch perfectly and it poked through the hole. I let the air out again, repositioned, and pumped 250 more times!" Still finished despite catastrophic tire failure. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** Multiple riders mentioned flatting, including Zdenek early in race. (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Equipment Failures:**

**Rasta Rider [ELITE]:** "I broke the water bottle cage probably about 20 miles in." (https://kcrastarider.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic.html)

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "My left pedal's tension was way too loose and every time I hit a big lip, my left foot would come unclipped and I'd have to dab." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)

**Physical Failures:**

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "When I got off my bike and straightened my legs in the cold water they started cramping and I couldn't move for about 20 seconds. It was extremely painful." This was at mile 56 of 57 - final water crossing. (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Critical Supplies:**

**Carey Lowery [RECREATIONAL]:** "Fortunately a racer named Rock stopped and gave me one of his patches... Rock came by me once again and this time gave me his only tube; I had refused before as I did not want him without. He did not give me the opportunity to refuse this time, saying as he rode by and dropping the tube at my feet, 'I am tired of you passing me.'" Community support can save your race. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2010/10/berryman-epic-race-report.html)

**Mark Gullett [ELITE]:** "Bob Arnold of DRJ Racing on the side of the trail. He had just fixed a flat and was having problems with his CO2. I loaned him mine and then headed back down the trail." (http://www.markgullett.com/2009/10/berryman-epic-recap.html)

**Mental/Motivation:**

**Steve Tilford [COMPETITIVE]:** "I wasn't going that good on this section. I had lost motivation pretty much... I saw Ryan, the promoter out on the trail and he told me I was 7 minutes back. I rode a couple more miles and flatted in a creek crossing. The last 6 miles are on gravel. I couldn't see anyone behind me, so I just cruised in." (https://stevetilford.com/2010/10/25/berryman-it-was-epic-in-a-different-way/)
