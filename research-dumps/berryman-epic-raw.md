## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**

The race begins with **gravel climbing** (approximately 3-4 miles of gravel grinding[4][5]), transitions into **singletrack** on the Berryman and Ozark Trails, with **connector road sections**[1][6]. One participant described the singletrack as featuring "technical roots and rocks but also lots of sandy areas along with smooth dirt sections"[4].

**Key Course Features**

- **Opening climb**: A hard gravel climb starting at the finish line, roughly 1 mile long, followed by several more miles of gradual uphill gravel[5]
- **Berryman Trail section**: Rocky singletrack with sustained climbs and rocky descents[2]
- **Bass River Resort turnaround**: Riders pass through Bass River Resort with approximately 10-15 miles remaining[3]
- **Final 8 miles**: Includes a steep road climb out of Bass River Resort and notoriously difficult terrain; riders pass the finish line but must continue 8 more miles before the final loop[4]
- **Trail surface character**: The terrain is described as "very tight," with sections completely covered in dry leaves during fall, featuring smooth rocky singletrack that allows riders to build speed[3][4]

**Difficulty Level**

The Berryman Trail is designated as an **IMBA Epic Trail**[1][2]. One YouTube race recap describes it as having "very grally single track the whole trail was covered in rocks but it's like smooth" with a "green" difficulty rating[6][7].

## COMMUNITY & REPUTATION

**Race History**

The BT Epic has been held **annually since 2008**[7]. As of 2023, it was in its **16th year**[9]. The race was created by **Scott and Jake from Springfield, Missouri**, who have deep knowledge of the Berryman Trail area[3].

**Course Evolution**

The race has been modified over time. As of 2015, organizers removed "a far loop and added a loop closer to the Bass River Resort," which improved race logistics (support access) though not necessarily the rider experience[3].

**Notable Participants**

Documented riders include professional cyclist **Steve Tilford**, who competed in 2013 and 2015[3][5]. Tilford competed against regional riders including **Garet Steinmetz**, **Brian Jensen**, and others[3].

**Community Perception**

The race is described as "pretty epic" and "amazing"[3][4]. One YouTube reviewer called it "the hardest MTB race in Missouri" and strongly recommended it: "just sign up for this event... just do it"[4][6]. The IMBA Epic designation and trail quality contribute to its reputation.

**YouTube Coverage**

Two YouTube race recaps are available documenting the BT Epic experience, providing first-person race footage and commentary[4][6].

## RACE REPORTS & RIDER EXPERIENCE

**Steve Tilford's 2015 Race Report**

Professional rider Steve Tilford provided detailed commentary: "The race starts directly up a climb" and competitors immediately enter competitive action on tight, leaf-covered singletrack. Tilford noted that "the trail is very tight and was mostly covered completely with dry leaves." He described the final push: "I rode back into the Bass Resort with about 8 miles left... I felt pretty great and thought I could just maybe make that up." During the steep road climb out of the resort, he gained 45 seconds on the leader over approximately one mile[3].

**2012 Race Report**

Tilford's 2012 account described: "The race starts straight up a hard gravel climb, probably a little less than a mile long. Then a couple more miles of gradual uphill and gravel"[5].

**Course Difficulties from Participant Perspective**

One YouTube participant reported bailing out at the Berryman campground section in a previous year but returned to finish the race in 2024[4]. The final 8 miles are "notoriously hard for two reasons: one you ride right past the finish line and have to go eight miles out and two there's a steep hill and very technical terrain once you get on the single track"[4].

**Equipment & Conditions Insights**
