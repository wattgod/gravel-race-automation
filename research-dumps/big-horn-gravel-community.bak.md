## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Recommendations [ALL LEVELS]:** "Eagle County does not have much 'Champagne Gravel', think more rugged backcountry forest roads. Some of these rugged sections will be low speed, uphill climbs, but some will be high speed ripping descents. For these sections we recommend a minimum tire width of 38mm and running a tire-liner for added protection." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Bike Choice [ALL LEVELS]:** "In some sections a mountain bike might be your best tool for the job. No judgment here. The hardtail mountain bike is the original gravel grinder after all! That said, the vast majority of riders will be on modern gravel bikes, like the Pinarello Grevil." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Ben Delaney [ELITE/COMPETITIVE]:** Created an equipment-focused video from the 2023 race (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**Test Equipment [ELITE]:** BMC URS with Campagnolo's 13-speed Ekar group tested at inaugural 2022 Bighorn Gravel (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WnqAdNTiLKE)

**Test Equipment [COMPETITIVE/ELITE]:** Specialized Epic HT hardtail tested at Bighorn Gravel: "Bighorn Gravel in Gypsum, Colorado is a rowdy race with nearly 10,000 feet of climbing at elevation and a few miles of singletrack. I borrowed a sweet, high-end hardtail" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Zx58wCVnGTI)

**Mandatory Equipment [ALL LEVELS]:** "Cycling computer or GPS device, minimum of 40oz of water or sports drink, two spare inner tubes, air pump or inflation system, and a cell phone to contact the 'outside world' should you need help. There are many sections of the course where there is no cell service!" (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Highly Recommended Equipment [ALL LEVELS]:** "GPS system to communicate your exact location to rescue personnel in the event of an emergency, small rucksack or hydration pack, waterproof jacket, extra thermal layer, emergency blanket, tire levers, puncture repair kit, chain tool, allen wrench set, spoke wrench, chain lube, handlebar map, compass, Chamois Butt'r, first aid kit, FOOD (energy bars, gels, chocolate, etc)." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 Race Day:** "Unlike 2022, race day dawned clear and perfect in 2023, and rider stoke was high" (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**General Weather Warning [ALL LEVELS]:** "Although some blacktop roads will be necessary from time to time, the vast majority of the course will be on dirt, gravel and forest service roads. Some of these roads receive little to no maintenance throughout the year and can be quite primitive in nature. In the event of inclement weather, gravel and dirt roads can become mud roads." ## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile - Ram's Horn Escape:** 85 miles, 10,000 feet of climbing, reaching 11,000 feet elevation. Features "rugged singletrack, alpine mountain roads, and backcountry dirt" (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Course Profile - Little Bighorn:** 50 miles, 5,700 feet of climbing, "the core elements of the full course" (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Course Profile - Gravel Curious:** 20 miles, 2,000 feet of climbing, "2,000' vert entry into the off-road scene, with a big climb, technical but manageable two-track, and an action-packed descent" - E-bikes allowed on this course only (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Specific Sections - 2025 Clockwise Direction:** "Even though the gradual Gypsum Creek ascent is now a buttery smooth downhill, both the 50 and 85-mile courses cater to the climbers. Brumbaugh said one will experience the same zen going up to the Peter Estin Hut on Spring Creek Road or taking Brush Creek to Sylvan Lake. 'It's in the shade, you're cruising along, you see wildflowers, moose,' he said. 'You get the harshness out of the way early and then you get to just settle in.'" (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Technical Sections:** The course includes "a hike a bike or two, descents that will have you begging for mercy" and "some single track" (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Early Course Sections [2025]:** Getting "some of the Spring Creek and Love Connector (trails) in there early when people are fresh and feeling good is important" - reduces likelihood of running into general public users with early-morning arrival (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Singletrack Section:** "We set up our signature cable cam to get some insane action video on the rowdy singletrack descent. Most riders made it through the rock gardens unscathed" (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**High Point Location:** One aid station was moved "a little past Yeoman Park Campground, closer to the course's 11,000-foot high point" (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Final Section [2025]:** "For the second-straight year, the course will run in a clockwise direction. This time, however, the final lollipop will ascend Red Hill Road and come down the paved Cottonwood Pass into the finish." (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Terrain Variety:** "blending long gravel climbs with nasty, twisting singletrack descents, and covering environments from 11,000-ft. high alpine forest to sandy, scrubby rolling hills with forest service doubletrack and red earth" (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**Remoteness Warning:** "The White River National Forest region of Colorado is an extremely remote area. Always be aware of the distance to the next checkpoint and be prepared to travel that distance with the supplies you carry." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Strategy - Michaela Thompson [ELITE]:** Won 2023 women's race by skipping the final aid station, passing Olympian Erin Huck and 2022 champion Alexis Skarda (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**Bike Choice Strategy [COMPETITIVE/UNKNOWN]:** "Just because a race is advertised as a 'gravel race' does not necessarily mean that a gravel bike is the right bike for the job. At Big Horn gravel I had to think outside the box" - rider chose MTB for the course (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e4vCk7NpMMg)

**Aid Station Locations - Ram's Horn Escape:** Mile 14, Mile 28, Mile 45, Mile 69, plus one neutral water zone (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Aid Station Locations - Little Bighorn:** Mile 14, Mile 28, plus one neutral water zone (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Support Restrictions [ALL LEVELS]:** "NOTE... support crews are NOT allowed on the course. All nutritional support is 'neutral' and has to be provided by Bighorn Gravel volunteers or staff, so plan accordingly with the food and supplies you carry." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Course Direction Evolution:** Originally planned to alternate directions every two years, but "the current consensus is to keep it this way [clockwise] for good" with occasional throwbacks. The clockwise direction allows Spring Creek and Love Connector trails early when riders are fresh. (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

## Calendar Positioning & Training Use

**Training Calendar Sweet Spot [ELITE/COMPETITIVE]:** "Bighorn Gravel is perfectly positioned in the off-road calendar for athletes training for marquee races like Crusher in the Tushar, the Leadville 100 or Steamboat Gravel. It's one reason why Wells has been fielding calls from athletes in the LifeTime Grand Prix, the premiere gravel racing series in the country. 'It's the first opportunity to get some high-altitude racing in,' he continued. 'So I think it lines up well for those riders.'" ## Race Logistics & Support

**Start Time - Long Course:** Ram's Horn Escape rolls out at 7 a.m., led by Eagle County Sheriff escort (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**On-Site Camping:** "Thanks to their [Town of Gypsum] support, we're able to offer onsite camping, complete with showers and bathrooms." (https://www.bighornevents.com/)

**Facilities Access:** "The Gypsum Rec Center offers day rates which include full access to the facility for $7 for kids (18 and under), $13 for adults, and $11 for seniors. They will be open 5am - 9pm on Friday, 9am - 7pm on Saturday, and 10am - 5pm on Sunday." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Traffic Status:** "Yes! All roads are open to traffic and all trails are open to the public. Please follow the rules of the road and be curious to other trail users. Believe me, they don't care that you are 'racing'. Keep safety a top priority!" (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Spectator Policy:** "In order to keep riders safe, we are trying to limit vehicle traffic on the courses, and to honor the adventurous spirit of the event please plan to cheer on your rider at the Start and welcome them back at the Finish." (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Registration Growth:** 2022: 289 riders; 2023: 497 riders (+70% year-over-year). Over 500 cyclists competed in 2024. (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel and https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

## Prize Money & Competition

**2024-2025 Prize Structure - Gravel:** Top 5 finishers in both men's and women's Open class receive cash: 1st = $2,500, 2nd = $1,200, 3rd = $750, 4th = $500, 5th = $300. Total prize purse $10,500 split evenly between men and women. Top male and female single speeder receive prize packs. (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/ and https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**2024 Elite Women's Field:** Included Karolina Migon (fresh off Unbound Gravel win), Emily Newsom, Erin Huck (Olympian), Lauren Stephens (3x Unbound 100 winner), Raylyn Nuss (2x Pan American cyclocross champion), Lauren De Crescenzo (2021 Unbound 200 champion), and defending champion Cecily Decker. Race organizers noted: "it's probably even more impressive on the women's side." (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**2024 Elite Men's Field:** Led by Cobe Freeburn (2023 runner-up), Alex Howes (two-time Tour de France finisher), plus Sam Brown and other Mountain Pedaler teammates. (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Road Race Prize - STRAVA Segments:** Two timed STRAVA segments with top 3 combined times receiving cash. Men and women both: 1st = $500, 2nd = $250, 3rd = $100. Must upload to STRAVA by 1:50pm MST race day and make ride public. (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-road)

**Bighorn Series Prize:** Fastest combined times for Gravel Ram's Horn + both Road STRAVA segments. Men and women both: 1st = Shimano Ultegra Di2 groupset, 2nd = SMITH Prize Package, 3rd = Bikeflights Travel Voucher and Swag Bag (https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-road)

**Raffle Prizes [ALL LEVELS]:** "Every 'official starter' will be entered into a raffle for a chance to win some really cool items" including wheels and GPS computers (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel and https://www.bighornevents.com/race-day-gravel)

**Last Place Prize:** "A one-of-a-kind cash prize to the last riders to complete the course" - unique "Ibex Winners" recognition (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

## Race History & Evolution

**Inaugural Year:** 2022 (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**2022 Results:** Eric Brunner 4th, Alexis Skarda won women's (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/ and https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**2023 Results:** Eric Brunner 2nd, Howard Grotts won men's solo, Michaela Thompson won women's (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/ and https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel)

**2024 Results:** Eric Brunner won, Cecily Decker won women's (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Course Direction Changes:** "For the second-straight year, the course will run in a clockwise direction... even though the original plan was to alternate directions every two years, the current consensus is to keep it this way for good. 'We might throw in the occasional throwback for nostalgic reasons,' [Wells] said." (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Road Race Addition:** September 2024 soft launch with ~75 cyclists; officially added as Bighorn Road in 2025. "The 80-mile ride on Sept. 6 will revive the now-defunct but immensely popular Colorado-Eagle River Ride." (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Capacity Expansion:** "Since Brumbaugh and Wells received increased capacity from the Forest Service for Bighorn Gravel, they're hoping to push their registration numbers up in the coming week. Their main focus, however, is finding the 'sweet spot' number which enhances and preserves overall rider experience. 'I don't feel like bigger is always better,' Wells said." (https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)

**Founder Background:** Co-founded by Mike Brumbaugh (owner of Venture Sports) and Jake Wells (professional cyclocross and endurance racer). Wells and Brumbaugh: "We were like, 'we have equally good if not better terrain to choose from. Why don't we have an event where we live?'" (https://www.thecoyotecollective.com/bighorn-gravel and https://www.vaildaily.com/news/bighorn-gravel-2025-off-road-mountain-bike-race-gypsum/)
