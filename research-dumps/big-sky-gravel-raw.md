# BIG SKY GRAVEL - RAW RESEARCH DUMP

## OFFICIAL DATA

**Race Dates**

The Big Sky Gravel is scheduled for **Sunday, August 30, 2026**[3][4][5]. However, there is a significant discrepancy in the search results: some sources list August 24, 2025 as a past date[1][2], while the current race listing consistently shows August 30, 2026[3][4][5][6][7].

**Start Times and Distance Options**

The event offers three race distances with staggered starts[4][5]:

- **100 Mile Race**: 7:00 AM MDT start
- **50 Mile Race**: 8:30 AM MDT start  
- **22 Mile Race**: 9:30 AM MDT start

There is also a **22 Mile Tour/E-Bike option** at 9:30 AM MDT[5].

**Registration Costs**

Registration fees range from **$70.27 to $107.30**[1], with fees varying by distance. The 22 Mile race costs $70.27 (including a $5.27 fee and BSG Party Shirt), the 50 Mile costs $80.85 (including $5.85 fee and shirt), and the 100 Mile costs $107.30 (including $7.30 fee and shirt)[1]. As of the current date (February 8, 2026), online registration is closed for the 2026 event[5].

**Registration Inclusions**

Registration includes: finish line food, rider gift, swag bag, route nutrition and hydration during the race, SAG/rest stop support, water and electrolytes, and a supported GPS route with marked navigation[1][2][3].

**Start and Finish Location**

The race starts and finishes at the **Roosevelt Center, 519 Broadway Avenue South, Red Lodge, Montana 59068**, located in downtown Red Lodge[4][5].

**Drop Bags (100 Mile Only)**

For the 100 Mile race, drop bags are available at Mile 8, 31, 86 (Drop Bag #1) and Mile 60 (Drop Bag #2). Drop bags must be collected by 8 PM on Saturday or by 6:30 AM on Sunday morning[4].

**Field Size, Prize Purse, Cutoff Times**

The search results do not contain information about the number of participants, prize purse, or official cutoff times for any race distance.

**Course Surface and Features**

The race is described as gravel cycling in the **foothills of the Beartooth Mountains**[2]. The event features GPS-supported and clearly marked routes[1][2].

**Specific Course Details**

The search results mention that the **100 Mile and 50 Mile routes are available** on the official website[4], but detailed elevation profiles, surface breakdown percentages (gravel vs. pavement vs. singletrack), specific climb names, or technical difficulty ratings are not provided in these search results.

**Known Course Element: Cocktail Loop**

On Saturday, August 23, there is a pre-race ride option called the **"Cocktail Loop"** (a local favorite) that covers the end section of the race in reverse. This ride is described as taking "roughly 1.5 hours" and being "mostly downhill"[4], suggesting the final race section includes significant climbing.
