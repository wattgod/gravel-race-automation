# BLACK FOREST GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Important Note: Two Different Events

The sources cover **two distinct events** with similar names:

1. **Black Forest ULTRA Gravel** (Germany) - A non-timed "ride, not a race" event in the Black Forest region near Kirchzarten, featuring 80km and 135km routes
2. **Black Fork Gravel Grinder** (Ohio, USA) - A competitive gravel race near Glenmont, Ohio, with 23, 30, and 53-mile options

---

## Race Format & Philosophy

### Black Forest ULTRA Gravel (Germany) [UNKNOWN LEVEL]
**Event philosophy:** "A RIDE, NOT A RACE" - explicitly marketed as a non-competitive event focused on experience over speed (https://www.ultra-gravel.de/en/)

**Route options:**
- 80km with +1,500m elevation
- 135km with +2,500m elevation
- "A harmonious mix of gravel, trail and asphalt" (https://www.ultra-gravel.de/en/news/)

**Unique features:** Multiple "GenussHotSpots" (enjoyment hot spots) offering "Black Forest tapas, barista coffee, ice cream, Rothaus beer and much more" included in the €70 entry fee. Locations include Johanna's Wagencafé at Hinterzarten at the Adlerschanze and the Rothaus Genusswelt on the long route (https://www.ultra-gravel.de/en/news/)

### Black Fork Gravel Grinder (Ohio, USA) [UNKNOWN LEVEL]
**Competitive structure:** Three distance options - The Ride (23 mile), The Original (30 mile), and The Race (53 mile). Part of the Ohio Gravel Race Series with course records tracked (https://www.blackforkgravelgrinder.com/)

**Course records (53-mile):**
- Men's: Matt Acker, 2:44:44 (2023)
- Women's: Emily Leonard, 3:23:51 (2021)
(https://www.blackforkgravelgrinder.com/results)
