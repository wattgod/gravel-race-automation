# BOHEMIAN BORDER BASH — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

(https://magazinebike.com/2024/10/01/bohemian-border-bash-race-in-search-of-borders/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]** used a Fara F/Gravel bike with:
- Shimano GRX 1X, 11-42t gearing
- Pirelli Cinturato Gravel M 45mm tubeless tires with Silca sealant
- Redshift Shockstop PRO suspension seatpost and stem
- Controltech Sirocco aerobars
- QUOC Gran Tourer II gravel shoes
- Restrap Hike-a-bike strap
- Cyclite Race Backpack, Restrap tool pouch, Fara integrated frame bag, Tailfin top tube pack
- Final bike weight: 16kg
(https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]** notes on gravel vs MTB: "In the case of the Bohemian Border Bash Ultra, a gravel bike was a suitable choice due to the mix of surfaces and long-distance riding. The lightweight setup, comfortable components, and adaptability to both on-road and off-road sections made it a great fit for the race. However, for races with more technical and rugged terrain, a mountain bike with robust suspension and knobby tires might be a better option." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Pavel Novotný [UNKNOWN]** tested the Ghost Asket Pro: "The bike was comfortable and easy to handle on the terrain, providing plenty of stability with its 50 mm tires, which were more than sufficient for the mostly smooth gravel." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/border-bash-bohemia-camp-2024-recap/)

**Anonymous DNF rider [UNKNOWN]** on saddle issues: "I also had a relatively new bib... the padding has probably been changed compared to the previous model and was made a bit thicker. That could also be a reason, although these trousers have been worn in for a few hundred kilometres – but unfortunately not on routes longer than 130km." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

## Weather Experienced

**2020 (scouting trip):** "In the pouring rain Dan, Ondrej and I took off... It was a very tough day that brought us to our limit... As the rain poured down on us without any break or mercy and the temperatures dropping to lower single digits we were fighting for our lives here. The moment we put plastic bags in our shoes and plastic gloves on our hands trying to keep them from freezing, shivering in a gas station..." (https://gravgrav.cc/en/article/bohemian-border-bash)

**2021 (scouting trip):** "Well: It can never be dry on this track can it? We didn't think too much about the rain and enjoyed beautiful roads, gravel and trails. Wow this stretch is beautiful! It rained until late at night, luckily we found shelter at a hunting hut... At the highest point we still rode through some crazy high snow walls." (https://gravgrav.cc/en/article/bohemian-border-bash)

**2024 camp:** "We were really lucky, because the weather was more than fine. If I imagine having to ride parts of the route in the rain, that would have been immensely more challenging... we could enjoy the warm late-summer weather, which lasted throughout the camp." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/border-bash-bohemia-camp-2024-recap/ and https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "Between CP1 & 2: Ghost Bikes Checkpoint... Just before CP2 there was an intermediate CP where you could sleep and resupply food. I arrived there in the middle of the night and decided to take 3-hour nap before heading back into the forest." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "CP2 Upper Palatine Forest: Political turmoil has reshaped this region over the last half-century... Most of this area was vacated after the forced expulsion of its predominantly German inhabitants after World War II... The Ghost checkpoint to checkpoint 2 stretch was a demanding ride through dense forests. I reached checkpoint 2 in the dead of night, where a hike-a-bike section awaited. Alone in the forest, fear gripped me." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "One of the wonders of ultra cycling is the unexpected discoveries along the way. I stumbled upon a hidden gem during my journey, a UNESCO heritage site, Cesky Krumlov, a medieval city steeped in history. It was a pleasant diversion... and allowed me to make a quick stop in a bike shop to fix my derailleur hanger and resupply on sports nutrition." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "Checkpoint 6 to Checkpoint 9: Mountain Peaks and Harsh Conditions. The toughest challenges lay ahead as I reached the highest peaks in the Czech Republic. The harsh conditions and cold weather tested my resolve... CP6 was the last chance to scratch from the race, but I chose to continue. I made it to Poland, found a hotel, and slept a few hours, knowing that the next day would be a rigorous climb." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "On the fifth day, I reached CP10, where a collapsed bridge presented a new challenge. With determination and a hiker bike strap, I crossed the broken bridge, driven by the knowledge that Ursula was chasing me." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "Morocco is more technical and has other surprises, but you can ride much easier. Bohemia was mainly ups and downs, peppered with hiking sections and MTB tracks, followed by asphalt sections and steep climbs again and again. This wore you down and ensured that there were always 'breaks' in the rhythm and then also wore you out." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "A steep ramp, which reminded me a lot of the Old Soviet Road at the Silk Road Mountain Race, led up a 940m high mountain and after a root passage I finally stood in front of the Midpoint of Europe." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "Checkpoint 5 was on the day's schedule. But it wasn't that easy and my Strava entry from that day says 'Just hell'. I found it very exhausting, because in addition to the normal ups and downs, this time the route also led a lot through woods and meadows, with a lot of gravel and on small tracks. In addition, the heat and sun got to me, which cost additional energy." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "CP5 at the Iron Curtain/Zelezna Opona. Here is an abandoned village on the border between the Czech Republic and Austria, which had to make way for the former iron curtain because of the border." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

**Max Riese [UNKNOWN]:** "Some parts of the route were quite tricky to ride either because of technical trails and hike a bikes or just because of soaked and muddy trails." (https://gravgrav.cc/en/article/bohemian-border-bash)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "The first 50km were also a small elimination race. A small group quickly got away from the front, and the winners came out of that group. Behind them was a group of about 25-30 racers and me among them. Little by little the group thinned out and after the first climbs the order of the next days became more and more clear." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "After 270km, I decided to end the day together with Markus, a fellow rider. We spread out our bivouac bags in the cold and moist night." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "Markus got up again at around 3am and left. I needed another 15 minutes and then followed him." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "The advantage was that you didn't see the climbs or how long they were. I always found that very pleasant. And the cool temperatures are also more pleasant to ride in before the heat and sun of the day hit." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Justyna Jarczok [ELITE]:** "I tried to eat better today, or to eat regularly at all. I hadn't been able to do that the previous two days, which of course had an impact on my performance at some point. So I planned fixed meal breaks in larger towns." (https://www.buzzalong.cc/post/bohemian-border-bash-1300km-insights-from-my-first-solo-off-road-ultra-endurance-cycling-race)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "After 220km, I ended the day in a rather noble refuge on a climb somewhere in the forest. A little later Markus arrived, who also had this hut on his list. We were still riding in the top 20-25." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "And a shower will certainly help my butt, too... The next morning, after a few hours of sleep, I was back on the bike and on the road at around 3.40 am." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "The second day was also a day of truth, because that's when you usually notice whether you are well trained, have managed your strength correctly and whether your body is powerful enough. And CP 2 is the right yardstick for that." (https://www.biketour-global.de/en/2023/09/10/a-different-experience-my-bohemian-border-bash-race-2023/)
