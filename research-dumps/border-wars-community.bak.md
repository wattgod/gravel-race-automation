# BORDER WARS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Important Note: Event Name Change

**As of 2025, this event is now called "Homegrown Gravel Adventure"**. The race promoter changed the name due to global tensions: "Originally, the name 'Border Wars Gravel Adventure' came from the light-hearted feud between the border folk of Georgia and Alabama... However, global tensions have continued around the world, humanity simply refuses to play nice. We've come to understand that sailing under our 'Border Wars' flag could cause our participants some internal conflict, which was neither our intent nor a consequence we were willing to accept. So we changed it. Done." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

(https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Hydration [COMPETITIVE]:** Jason Lopez carried "two bottles on my bike and a frame bag with a bladder. I was out of water by the end of the race and could have used a little bit more water, but I managed." For the 100-mile route in 5:58 moving time. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Tire/Equipment Note [ELITE]:** The course has "gravel with random chunks that could cause a pinch flat if hit wrong" - specific attention to tire pressure and tire choice recommended. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 Edition:** "The morning of the race started out a bit chilly at 38° F and was going to warm up to 60° F by mid-day. This made it a bit tough to dress for, but at least this year was rain free. There was a decent head wind coming out of the NNW that was going to make the AL section of the race a little more challenging." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**2024 Edition [ELITE perspective]:** "The day was projected to be sunny and temps in the 60s... the day was sunny and temps were 40s at the start and 60s later. Winds however, had 20-30 mph gusts with mostly headwind on the route. Thankfully much of the route was in tree lined areas though paved sections were out in the open." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Mud & Single-Track Start:** "About a mile into the race, we hit the first off-road sector that was essentially a single-track path with a muddy section at the end. The 2024 Border Wars Gravel race instantly backed up as riders got off their bikes and were going single file to walk around the deepest section of the mud." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Course Profile [COMPETITIVE perspective]:** "The course featured a 23% grade section and quite a bit of climbing, but it also had quite a few fast technical descents... The course had some creek crossings, sketchy bridge crossings, but very minimal muddy sections." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Gravel Surfaces:** "There were all types of gravel too, small compact gravel that was quite fast, and gravel with random chunks that could cause a pinch flat if hit wrong." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Paved Sections:** "While out there it seemed like there was a lot of paved sections, but on paper there was only about 30 miles total. That is a pretty solid job with connecting all the different gravel sectors." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Alabama Sections [ELITE perspective]:** "The gravel varied from packed dirt, to typically what one thinks of gravel roads and then there were what I describe as rock gardens. Rock gardens were on several steep climbs and on a few descents. Most of this section was in Alabama as described in the rider guide/FAQ page." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)

**Mud & Water Crossings [ELITE perspective]:** "There were a few muddy patches on the route with only one, besides the previously stated one, where you had to get off the bike to walk across... you soon came to a descent with a stream crossing at the bottom before an ascent to partially clean off your shoes." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)

**Last-Minute Course Changes:** "A week before the event, a bridge was taken out by raging water from a storm, which caused the race promotor to make some major changes to one of the routes. The 30-mile route was forced to become an out and back, and the other two were shortened by a few miles." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Strategy [COMPETITIVE advice]:** "As we lined up, the organizer gave us some instructions, specifically that there was a neutral roll out because of a muddy section that caused a pinch point at the beginning of the race... The race started out in downtown Franklin, GA and we rolled out of the little town with a few intersections being controlled by the local police... the neutral roll out was blown up and the first group to pass through the mud was already up the road." Critical to position at front before the single-track mud section ~1 mile in. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Chase Effort [COMPETITIVE] (245W normalized):** "A bit frustrated and angry, I chased to see if I could catch the lead group. Initially, I had a few riders with me, but they were quickly dropped... after laying down some serious watts, I caught the front group. Right as I contacted the front group, the grade turned upwards, and they started to pull away. I couldn't hold on after my effort to catch them and they slowly pulled away." Jason Lopez spent significant energy chasing after missing the first split and couldn't hold the group when caught. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Navigation:** "The route is not marked, nor are there cue sheets, but the GPS directions were perfect." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)

**Aid Stations:** "The 60 mile route had 3 rest stops (at 16, 36 and 46 miles) with Oreos at the last stop which always makes me happy." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)

(https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Elite Participation:** "The event also had in attendance the top five finisher from the 2023 edition and few pro and elite riders (Lauren De Crescenzo, Michael Garrison, and Mackenzie Myatt)." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

## Logistics & Registration

**2024 Pricing:** "The 2024 Border Wars event was $85 plus a $6.78 BikeReg Service Fee for a $91.78 total." Registration opened November 12, 2023 and "closed about a week before the event, and there were spots available at the time the event registration closed." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**2025 Updates (Now "Homegrown Gravel Adventure"):** "Field limits have increased from 600 to 800 total registered riders. New prize categories have been added for 40-54 Masters, 55+ Masters, and Singlespeed. The 30-mile ride now has an optional ten-mile extension, creating a 40-mile route. There will be official event camping during the evenings before and after the race, and post-ride showers will be available to all riders." Registration opens August 1 with "$15 early bird discount through September 30, 2024." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8123/homegrown-gravel-adventure-heads-into-fourth-year)

**Parking:** "The main bulk of the Parking for the 2024 Border Wars Gravel was held at the Heard County High School. The school had over 600+ parking spots and room for RVs. There was some on street parking downtown and a small parking lot just south of the main square." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Camping:** "Riverside Park had Primitive camping and RV spaces (no electricity or water hook-ups)." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Hotels:** "There isn't much in terms of hotels within Franklin, so depending on the direction you are coming from you will need to choose a close town with hotels." Franklin is "an hour from Atlanta" with "convenient Friday late afternoon packet pick up near the Atlanta Airport." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/ and https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)

**Start Times (2024):** Staggered starts - "8:00am – 100-mile start, 9:30am – 62-mile start, 11:00am – 30-mile start." This "was a was a great idea because it significantly reduced the number of riders at the start of the race." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

## Course Options & Stats

**2024 Routes (Modified due to bridge washout):**
- 100-mile – 70% gravel – 8000 ft elevation gain
- 62-mile – 65% gravel – 5000 ft elevation gain  
- 30-mile – 60% gravel – 2000 ft elevation gain
(https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Route Distribution:** "2024 Border Wars Gravel is a blend of paved and gravel riding starting in Georgia and crossing into Alabama for half of the race. The race is mostly off-road and traverses the rural roads of GA and AL." For the 60-mile route, "21 miles were in Alabama and the rest in Georgia." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/ and https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2024/2/27/border-wars-gravel)
