# BURGR 95 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Size Evolution [UNKNOWN]:**
- Early years (2013): Race organizer used 700x32c tires: "I'm using the same 700x32 tires that got me across Iowa. Sometimes those aren't quite enough, but overall they are an efficient and durable choice." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)
- Later years (2014): Organizer switched to 700x40c: "I used to do it on 32c tires. They will get the job done. I switched to 40c tires this year and I'm not going back." (underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2014/05/burgr-95-3.html)

**Bike Type [UNKNOWN]:**
"This is a cross bike ride. Mountain bikes would make it, but without compromise tires they will be tougher to ride that far. Road bikes will not survive some fire road segments." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Navigation Equipment Required [UNKNOWN]:**
"You will need an accurate computer unless you plan on never getting dropped from the group. You will need a way to hold your cue sheet whether a stem bag with a window, clip board, binder clips, or the cuff of your shorts." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Water Capacity [UNKNOWN]:**
"Depending on temperatures, start with 3 or 4 bottles and put the same in your drop bag." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:**
- 95.5 miles total including 66.2 miles on gravel (underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2014/05/burgr-95-3.html)
- Includes "rocky fire roads" and "fast single lane road which are usually fast" (underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2014/05/burgr-95-3.html)
- Later edition (BURGR99) described as: "Rocky, Fast, Firelane, Twisty, Remote, Pavement, Machine-Built Singletrack, Smooth Country Gravel, Two Mile-long Descents, That part at mile 70 that will Really Test You" (underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2018/07/burgr99-and-fork-horners-47-details.html)

**Environmental Conditions:**
"You will be in bear and wolf territory most of the time. Your phone will work most of the time." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Remoteness:**
"You will be on the road for 5 to 7 hours and will not pass through any town ever." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Specific Terrain Features (2018 version):**
- Mile 36: Water at Jack Lake
- Mile 46: Summit Lake BP station with ice cream
- Mile 70: Particularly difficult section mentioned
- Mile 83: Aid Station
- "Prairie Dells single track" included (underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2018/07/burgr99-and-fork-horners-47-details.html)

**Fork Horners 47 Alternative:**
"FAST and QUIET: This is a journey through wide farm-country boulevards with very light traffic. Roads are often fast granite, with about half paved." (www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

"The Fork Horners 47 course is generally flatter and smoother with none of the rough stuff that might be called B-roads out west." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2019/03/badger-state-burgr95.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:**
"This a self-navigated, unsupported, free 'group ride' through the wilderness dirt roads of Lincoln, Langlade, and Oneida counties. Like with any other training ride, you will be responsible for yourself and should not count on rescue from anyone." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Registration Process:**
"Your comment here is all it takes to be registered. For all legal purposes this is just a friendly group ride through the woods where you are, of course, responsible for you." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

"This event is part of the Ironbear 1000 which highlights Wisconsin's epic gravel." (www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

**Post-Race:**
"Usually folks hang out following the event at the Z's Fork Horners afterwards." (www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

"We like Bambino's downtown for beer, pizza, and more ice cream afterwards." (underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2018/05/saturday-update.html)

**Start/Finish Venue:**
Started at Z's Fork Horners (formerly the Lighthouse Bar & Grill): "They've got great burgers and pizza." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Camping Options:**
"I would camp at the Underdown - cheap and 7.5 miles from the Lighthouse. I expect some trail riders and raspberry pickers on Sunday." (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2013/04/burgr-95.html)

**Old School Character:**
"This a self-navigated, unsupported, old school free 'group ride' so be prepared to support yourself. Use paper cue sheets, gpx, or your phone to navigate because the course is not marked." (www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

**Evolution to Official Event:**
"This old event is now official! Register with the Badger State Games... $20 registration includes BSG shirt and a drink from the bar! BSG medals to top 3 male and female in both" (thunderdown12.blogspot.com/2019/04/badger-state-burgr95-and-fork-horners-47.html)
