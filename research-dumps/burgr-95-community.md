## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Characteristics [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Over 2/3 gravel or worse with several sections of beautiful single lane road which are usually fast" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)
- "Sand and rock will be encountered. Wet feet are a remote possibility" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)
- "Rough fire roads and fast single lane roads with plenty of climbing" (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/)
- Courses run through "wilderness dirt roads of Lincoln, Langlade, and Oneida counties" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

**Fork Horners 47 Course [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Wide open gravel roads through farm country and forest with moderate elevation" (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/)
- "Journey through wide farm-country boulevards with very light traffic. Roads are often fast granite, with about half paved" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)
- "Not much traffic and the gravel roads were in good shape" - **DC411en [UNKNOWN]** regarding the 47-mile route (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/07/burgr-95-5.html)

**Challenging Conditions:**
- The BURGR 95 is consistently described as "the rugged one" compared to the Fork Horners 47 (https://sites.google.com/view/burgr95-and-fork-horners-47/home)
- "No food for sale on course" (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Finishing Times - BURGR 95 [UNKNOWN]:**
From 2016 edition:
- Fast finishers: 6:09-6:18 range (Joel Hynes at 6:09, multiple riders clustered at 6:10-6:18)
- Mid-pack: 7:26 (including fat bike finishers Tim Jennings and Joe Kraznarich)
- Slower finishers: 7:50 (Jason Dostal on fat bike)
- "You will be on the road for 5 to 7 hours" is the expected range (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

**Finishing Times - Fork Horners 47 [UNKNOWN]:**
From 2016 edition:
- David Allen: 3:20
- Brad Gustafson: 3:41

**Support Strategy [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Drop bag will be delivered to the halfway point of the 95 where neutral fluids will also be waiting" (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/)
- "The 47 will have water at about 30 miles" (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/)
- Self-supported format: "be prepared to support yourself" and "you will be responsible for yourself and should not count on rescue from anyone" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Fat Bike Participation [UNKNOWN]:**
- At least two riders (Tim Jennings and Joe Kraznarich) completed the 95-mile course on fat bikes in 7:26 in 2016, with Jason Dostal finishing in 7:50 on a fat bike (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/07/burgr-95-5.html)

**Navigation Equipment [UNKNOWN]:**
- "GPS navigation is required. Courses will not be marked" (https://sites.google.com/view/burgr95-and-fork-horners-47/home)
- "Use paper cue sheets, gpx, or your phone to navigate" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Format:**
- Described as a "self-navigated, unsupported, old school free 'group ride'" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)
- Free registration required online (https://sites.google.com/view/burgr95-and-fork-horners-47/home)
- Part of the "Ironbear 1000 which highlights Wisconsin's epic gravel" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

**Post-Race Scene:**
- "Usually folks hang out following the event at the Z's Fork Horners afterwards" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)
- After party scheduled at 2:00 PM (https://sites.google.com/view/burgr95-and-fork-horners-47/home)
- Fork Horners hosts "Customer Appreciation day during the BURGR95. Specials and a band!" (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/)

**Volunteer Support:**
- "Thanks to Louise, Steve and Brian for being out there supporting us" - 2016 race report (https://underdowngravel.blogspot.com/2016/07/burgr-95-5.html)

**Remote Environment:**
- "You will be in bear and wolf territory most of the time. Your phone will work most of the time" (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)
- "Will not pass through any towns" on the 95-mile course (https://www.ironbull.org/burgr95-fork-horners47)

**Stage Race Integration:**
- The 2022 edition was listed as "STAGE 1 of the THUNDERDOWN GRAVEL STAGE RACE" (https://sites.google.com/view/burgr95-and-fork-horners-47/home)
