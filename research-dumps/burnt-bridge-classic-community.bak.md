# BURNT BRIDGE CLASSIC — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Chad Grice [ELITE]:** "I have done the Burnt Bridge Classic more than any other gravel race, and over the years, the long course has grown in both distance and elevation. What is now the medium course was the long course of only a few years ago." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Chad Grice [ELITE]:** "The second lap starts with a 7 km climb, and about 1 km in, I came completely apart. I have never cracked so hard in my life; I did the longest climb of the race at an average of 190W at 10 kph. It was awful, and I just kept looking back to see how many people were going to pass me." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Chad Grice [ELITE]:** "The final climb, I was even more cracked and only managed to find 166W for 5.2 kms at 10 kph. This is where I started getting caught; five riders had passed me up the climb, and I was just surviving to get to the end. However, the final descent was really technical and had some single track, which was apparently exactly what I needed to get back in the race." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Brenna Pauly [ELITE]:** "Five kilometres into the race we hit the first major climb and I decided to ride tempo up the climb. I ended up riding away from the group and kept the momentum over the climb. The course offered lots of variety of terrain and beautiful scenery which I was able to take in." (https://pacificcyclingcentre.ca/news/brenna-pauly-takes-the-win-at-burnt-bridge/)

**Brenna Pauly [ELITE]:** "With 15 kilometres to go there was a steep loose climb and rocky decent that was the last technical part of the race before six kilometres of rail trail to finish. After 70 kilometres of solo riding, I crossed the finish line winning the women's race." (https://pacificcyclingcentre.ca/news/brenna-pauly-takes-the-win-at-burnt-bridge/)

**Chad Grice [ELITE]:** "There is something about Burnt Bridge that has eluded me since I started racing it. I have never got it right or felt like I had it figured out, this year was no exception. As we left the start line the pace ramped up it wasn't immediate but by the time I realized that we were going too hard it was too late." (https://bookofchad.ca/category/race-reports/)

**Chad Grice [ELITE]:** "Forbidden Gravel Race is my personal favorite. Out of all the gravel races I have entered Forbidden sets itself out to be the gnarliest. This race has the most climbing and the most single track. However, what I think everyone walks away remembering is the loose, long and fast decents." (https://bookofchad.ca/category/race-reports/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Chad Grice [ELITE]:** Used 2.1-inch tires and a 75mm dropper post setup for the Kapoor main descent. He noted: "with my setup of 2.1-inch tires and a 75mm dropper post, I thought that I would probably want to lead down this portion." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Note on mechanical issues:** One rider (Aidan) was rendered single-speed by SRAM equipment failure during the 2025 race, forcing him to abandon after one lap. (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Descent hazards:** The Kapoor main descent features at least one technical corner where multiple riders crashed simultaneously in 2025. Chad Grice [ELITE] described: "I set up for the toughest corner on the descent, turned in, the bike leaned, and I made it to the apex when I heard skidding behind me. As I exited the corner, I peered back to watch the first guy sail straight off the corner into the trees, and everyone behind him slid one by one to the scene of the accident." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Wildlife encounters:** Black bears are present on the course. Chad Grice [ELITE] reported: "I started down the descent, and halfway down, a large black bear decided to join the road in front of me. Luckily, it was quicker than I was and managed to run down and off the road before I could get stopped." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Technical sections:** Brenna Pauly [ELITE] noted "a steep loose climb and rocky decent that was the last technical part of the race before six kilometres of rail trail to finish." (https://pacificcyclingcentre.ca/news/brenna-pauly-takes-the-win-at-burnt-bridge/)

**Final sections:** The course includes technical single track near the end, followed by a "final 10 km flat time trial to the finish." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Climb characteristics:** The long course features a 7 km climb at the start of the second lap, followed later by a 5.2 km final climb. (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Chad Grice [ELITE] - 2025 Long Course (120.86 km, 2106m elevation):**
- Overall stats: 5:08:10 finish time, 185W average power, 210W normalized power
- First major climb: "When we hit the bottom of the climb and my power numbers went well north of 400w, I knew that I needed to shut that down and ride at a pace I could sustain for the entire first climb."
- Second lap 7km climb (complete bonk): 190W average at 10 kph
- Final 5.2km climb [ELITE] (severely depleted): 166W at 10 kph
- Final 10km flat TT to finish: 190W at 32 kph
(https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Group dynamics strategy:** Chad Grice [ELITE] emphasized the importance of group work to avoid solo riding: "I was very vocal in the group, trying to keep it working together to not only help me go forward but especially to prevent the 60km solo ride I had last gravel race. The group was great; actually, it seemed to work really well, and we kept the speed up for well over two and a half hours together." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Brenna Pauly [ELITE] - Women's Win Strategy:** "Five kilometres into the race we hit the first major climb and I decided to ride tempo up the climb. I ended up riding away from the group and kept the momentum over the climb... I set a steady pace, passing riders that started in the earlier categories." Resulted in 70km of solo riding to the win. (https://pacificcyclingcentre.ca/news/brenna-pauly-takes-the-win-at-burnt-bridge/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical failures:** SRAM equipment failures have occurred, rendering riders single-speed and forcing abandonment. (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Crash zones:** The Kapoor main descent has a particularly technical corner where multiple riders crashed in 2025, with search and rescue mobilized to the scene. (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Bonking risk:** Even elite riders experience severe depletion on the second lap. Chad Grice [ELITE] reported his worst bonk ever, dropping from 400W+ climbing power to 166-190W. He noted: "I was thinking about quitting and turning back; at this point, I was so wrecked and hardly moving. I just kept eating and kept turning the pedals." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**DNF recorded:** At least one DNF listed in 2025 results (Chandra Kalke, Masters 35-44 Female, Impulso 40km course). (https://www.webscorer.com/racealldetails?raceid=390636)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Brenna Pauly [ELITE]:** "It was a great day of racing for all, with many smiling faces crossing the finishing line!" (https://pacificcyclingcentre.ca/news/brenna-pauly-takes-the-win-at-burnt-bridge/)

**Race support:** Search and rescue teams are positioned on course. When Chad Grice reported a crash, "the search and rescue team... mobilized immediately." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Neutral start:** The 2025 race featured a neutral start behind David Dallin on a motorcycle, though he "took the second corner a little too hot, slid in the gravel, and spun slightly, stalling the bike. Honestly though, props to him for keeping it upright." (https://bookofchad.ca/2025/05/19/burnt-bridge-classic-gravel-2025/)

**Multi-category field:** Faster riders pass those who started in earlier categories throughout the race. (https://pacificcyclingcentre.ca/news/brenna-pauly-takes-the-win-at-burnt-bridge/)

**Series context:** Part of the BC Gravel Series/Trek BC Gravel Series, with multiple events across Vancouver Island. The 2026 edition will host the Canadian Gravel Championships. (https://www.bcgravelseries.com/)
