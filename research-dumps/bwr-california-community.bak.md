# BELGIAN WAFFLE RIDE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "I didn't expect to be at the start line of the 2025 Belgian Waffle Ride in San Diego. Between a new job, family-wide respiratory illness, and a never-ending sleep deficit, this wasn't exactly the ideal lead-up to a 100-mile gravel race... We were two of just 21 women to finish the Waffle that day. Only about two-thirds of the 300 men finished." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "At Station 3, around mile 60, volunteers told us future aid stops might not be manned. We stuffed our pockets with bars, gels, and Coke and committed to the final push—ready or not... Just when we thought we were done, the course served us one last cruel combo: a dam, a lake, an eternal set of switchbacks, and the choice between a hike-a-bike or a creek crossing. We chose the creek—and slammed our bikes into hidden boulders. I went full submersion." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** "Less than one week before the race, the organization had to shorten the course because snow had rendered two sections unrideable. On race day, we enjoyed a late start at 9:00 AM, allowing the temperature to rise above 0°C... After 10km of easier riding, I timed an attack perfectly and broke away solo, ideally positioning myself to enter the single track first." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** "A higher commitment to getting all the details right makes the experience more enjoyable. Gravel races are long, and my default approach was to try to have fun for at least part of it—this quest included looking at the scenery, joking with fellow riders, and everything else I do in training to make it more fun. Last weekend at BWR Utah was the most fun I've had in a gravel race in a long time, and I committed to strict focus throughout the race." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Keegan Swenson [ELITE]:** "Today's race was a true test of endurance and strategy. The competition was fierce, and I'm thrilled to have come out on top. The BWR series is unlike any other, and I'm excited to continue racing in this incredible environment." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8408/villafañe-and-swenson-dominate-at-the-3rd-edition-of-belgian-waffle-ride-arizona)

**Sofia Gomez Villafañe [ELITE]:** "Winning at BWR Arizona is a fantastic way to start the season. The support from the fans and the camaraderie among the racers make this event special. I can't wait for the next challenge." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8408/villafañe-and-swenson-dominate-at-the-3rd-edition-of-belgian-waffle-ride-arizona)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "I'm not a very emotional bike racer but it felt like this huge weight that I'd been carrying since 2019 had been suddenly lifted. A sense of brief euphoria that I'd made good on what I'd sold to my sponsors. A very emotional win and something that still gives me a lot of pleasure to look back on." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "I kind of view a race as a test. If I prepare and do everything just right, then I ace the test which translates to me winning. A very analytical approach that I get pleasure out of." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "That moment on the Black Canyon climb where my sense of self-worth and value was shattered seemed like a lifetime ago. I laughed to myself with the thought that every single type of ride I'd ever done was, one way or another, shaping my performance on this one... I wasn't suffering, I was floating." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "I can't remember having as much fun on a bike. The vibe at BWR is 'racy with a hint of irony,' as died-in-the-wool road racers, mountain bikers, cyclocross-istas, and the occasional semi-retired professional triathlete all rub elbows in a challenging yet fun setting." (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Brendan Johnston [ELITE]:** "For me it was a matter of using up the group of five in the long head wind sections, and if I had the legs send it up the main climb of the day from Kanarraville... Fortunately I felt great out there and almost got better in the heat, and was able to really put some time in up the climb." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Brendan Johnston [ELITE]:** "The win is special for me. This has been a season where I have put more investment into than any other and it's had a tonne of challenges. In the periods where I should have been performing at my best ever I've had some of my worst days on the bike. It was essential that I carried on and turned things around." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I actually had to walk, I was cramping so much. I was like 'oh no this is my day, it's over.' But it wasn't and Rollins came over the line in seven hours and six minutes." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Maghalie Rochette [ELITE]:** "I looked around me and saw so many people riding hard. I rode to the crest of the dirt road hill and saw the beautiful views of Vermont. I thought about Heidi and Anthony, the 2 amazingly cool and inspiring organizers of Rasputitsa... Suddenly, I started to smile. I also refused to be sorry for not having the energy to go all-out and fight for the win, and for feeling fatigued. Sometimes, you have to accept what your body is telling you." (https://cxhairs.substack.com/p/freeroading-report-belgian-waffle)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** Used Rule 28 Long Sleeve Aero Jersey and Aero Bib Tights for all gravel races in 2024: "I no longer have to choose between staying warm and being aerodynamic." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** Nutrition - "Dates rolled in granola and peanut butter and salt along with the tens of Stryker Gels, NeverSecond drink mix, and my LMNT salts and some gummies along the way!" (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** "The people who are stressed and unable to recover are the ones who think about their work or sport all the time. They worry, and, in so doing, they deplete their energies and harm their performance." - recommends mental recovery as key technology. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "Old pro habits die hard [laughs]" - starts riding at 10:00am, not early morning. Elite-level training schedule flexibility. (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** On bike choice for mixed terrain: "There's also all this heavy sand on the track, so when it levels off just a bit, you have to pedal like hell." - describing road bike challenges on BWR's varied surfaces. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "Your understanding of race fueling. Triathlon is more of a logistical and mental challenge than a pure athletic challenge, and if you've come up with a successful fueling plan for a half-ironman or Ironman, you're going to be prepared for the 'out there all day' nature of these rides. Eat and drink early, but learn how to pee while riding your bike (sorry, ladies), so you're not stopping all the time." (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

**2024 BWR Utah:** Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE] noted: "Less than one week before the race, the organization had to shorten the course because snow had rendered two sections unrideable. **2025 BWR Arizona:** Race took place in desert conditions with 103.5 miles through "rugged desert terrain surrounding Scottsdale." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8408/villafañe-and-swenson-dominate-at-the-3rd-edition-of-belgian-waffle-ride-arizona)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**BWR California specific sectors:**

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "11 miles in and hit the first bottleneck... That's below us once we dropped down a bit and got stuck again. At least the weather's cool. Oh Highland Valley, I will never forget you!!! Somewhere in the chaos, I dropped my chain, lost Keri, missed a trail, and somehow ended up ahead of her." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "Just when we thought we were done, the course served us one last cruel combo: a dam, a lake, an eternal set of switchbacks, and the choice between a hike-a-bike or a creek crossing. We chose the creek—and slammed our bikes into hidden boulders. I went full submersion. Honestly, it felt amazing on my burning feet. The final three gravel hills were so steep that I had to push my bike." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**BWR Arizona specific sectors:**

**2025 BWR Arizona:** "103.5 miles through the rugged desert terrain surrounding Scottsdale, showcased the grit and determination of the athletes as they tackled 7,062 feet of climbing. A mix of mountain and desert with a smattering of good times and fast racing!" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8408/villafañe-and-swenson-dominate-at-the-3rd-edition-of-belgian-waffle-ride-arizona)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "Imagine a roller coaster at full downward tilt, but without any safety gear, with tracks coated with Crisco, and your roller coaster actually only has one track, and you have to balance on that track and not crash into any other roller coasters that may have the balls to pass you. There's also all this heavy sand on the track, so when it levels off just a bit, you have to pedal like hell." - describing Black Canyon descent. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "The first 20km were straightforward until we turned left and encountered a strong crosswind. The lead car, inadvertently while taking photos, helped create a front selection by blocking the wind." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**BWR Utah specific sectors:**

**Brendan Johnston [ELITE]:** "For me it was a matter of using up the group of five in the long head wind sections, and if I had the legs send it up the main climb of the day from Kanarraville. From there it's possible to make it to the finish." The 128 mile course "starts and finishes in Cedar City, is punctuated by nine road sections which make up just 8% of the race, has 7,500 feet of climbing (2,286 metres), single track, water crossings and often stiff headwinds." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I actually had to walk, I was cramping so much" on the final big climb after crashing earlier and "bending her derailleur and losing access to her easiest gear." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**BWR General Terrain:**

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "BWR begins with an eleven mile 'neutral' roll-out that devolved, unsurprisingly, into an all-out sprint for the first section of dirt. I knew positioning would be important, and did what I could to be near the front of the pack, but 300 riders makes for a dynamic, shifty field!" (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "This section of dirt did what it is supposed to do: break the 300-person field up into groups of 30-40 riders." Describing Lemontwistenberg singletrack section. (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "At the second station, Keri took off, determined to beat the 11:30 a.m. deadline. I chased her through endless steep hills, my legs protesting every 15–17% gradient with increasing rage... We were two of just 21 women to finish the Waffle that day." Finished just under the 10-hour cutoff. (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** "Fear nothing, respect everything / fear no one, respect everyone" - his key mental mantra throughout race. Focused on "constantly redirecting my attention to the moment." BWR Utah finish time: 166km race. Intensity Factor: 0.781, TSS: 299, Calories: 4,508. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** "After 10km of easier riding, I timed an attack perfectly and broke away solo, ideally positioning myself to enter the single track first." Strategic positioning before key technical sections. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Keegan Swenson [ELITE]:** Won 2025 BWR Arizona in 5:21:56, only 3 seconds ahead of Russell Finsterwald - indicating sustained threshold effort for entire race. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8408/villafañe-and-swenson-dominate-at-the-3rd-edition-of-belgian-waffle-ride-arizona)

**Sofia Gomez Villafañe [ELITE]:** Won 2025 BWR Arizona women's race in 6:04:39, nearly 21 minutes ahead of second place - indicating ability to sustain solo effort after selection. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8408/villafañe-and-swenson-dominate-at-the-3rd-edition-of-belgian-waffle-ride-arizona)

**Brendan Johnston [ELITE]:** "For me it was a matter of using up the group of five in the long head wind sections, and if I had the legs send it up the main climb of the day from Kanarraville." Won BWR Utah 2023 in 6:25:38, nearly 5 minutes ahead of second. Strategy: conserve in groups through headwind, attack on key climb at 110 miles. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnson-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** Won BWR Utah 2023 women's race in 7:06:08, 10 minutes ahead of second despite mechanical issues. Lost easiest gear after crash but still managed final climb. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** On 2021 BWR repeat win: "I'd put all this debilitating pressure on myself to do well at BWR 2021—especially seeing as Canyon was the headline sponsor—and then the race went so poorly with stupid issues that I thought I was out of it five times or more." Still won despite multiple setbacks. (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "Caught in a suboptimal position, I had to exert nearly maximum effort for 4 minutes to catch up" after bottleneck. Later: "This was when things got real. My power was fading, and I struggled to keep up... I was cracked. Bonked. Off the back." Demonstrates how early over-efforts can doom race at elite level. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "In the gravel races I've done so far, I've discovered that as long as I keep pedaling steadily, the road racers around me (not used to the constant muscle tension that triathlon requires) tend to simply fall away as the day progresses." Triathlete-specific pacing advantage in ultra-endurance gravel. (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "Only about two-thirds of the 300 men finished" the 2025 BWR California Waffle distance. "We were two of just 21 women to finish the Waffle that day." Indicates ~33% DNF rate for men, much higher for women. (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** Early bottlenecks cost them "nearly 45 minutes before the race even really began" - putting them immediately at risk for cutoff times. Multiple cutoff deadlines throughout race. "At the second station, Keri took off, determined to beat the 11:30 a.m. deadline." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "At Station 3, around mile 60, volunteers told us future aid stops might not be manned." - mid-race aid station closures add risk for slower riders. (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** Mechanical/crash: "Somewhere in the chaos, I dropped my chain, lost Keri, missed a trail, and somehow ended up ahead of her." Also: "A guy swing wide out of a corner, heading into my path definitely out of control with no recourse to stop. He shoulder checked me and sent me down hard onto my right." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** Course cancellation risk: "Less than one week before the race, the organization had to shorten the course because snow had rendered two sections unrideable." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "I was cracked. Bonked. Off the back. The group dropped me in what felt like the slowest, cruelest way possible... I felt like screaming and suddenly I hated everything about what I was doing, why I was there, what I had done wrong." Even elite riders face bonk risk. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** Crash on descent while passing slower riders: "I saw a guy swing wide out of a corner, heading into my path definitely out of control with no recourse to stop. He shoulder checked me and sent me down hard onto my right, so fast that I didn't even put a hand out. I landed with a thud and a sharp pain on my side. I sensed immediately I'd broken a rib." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** Mechanical issues even for winners: "Rollins had crashed in a single track section, bending her derailleur and losing access to her easiest gear." Still finished first but had to walk sections. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/belgian-waffle-ride-melisa-rollins-and-brendan-johnston-claim-victory-in-utah/)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** Navigation risk: "I managed to be in the middle third of the field (I think) when we first hit the dirt, which separated me from the front of the field (not that I would have been able to stay up there on the road sections, either), but meant I wasn't too far back once we completed the first two miles of single-track." Position in field before singletrack determines entire race outcome. (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Yun [RECREATIONAL]:** "Gravel isn't just harder than road—it's way more dynamic, and honestly, a lot more fun... It was brutal. It was suffering. And somehow, it was fun." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle)

**Adam Roberge [COMPETITIVE]:** "More Focus, More Fun - A higher commitment to getting all the details right makes the experience more enjoyable... Last weekend at BWR Utah was the most fun I've had in a gravel race in a long time." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/04/563/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "I mean, it's waffle themed and it starts and ends in a brewery. You have WorldTour riders going flat out on bike paths still open to the public on Sunday strolls." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "The vibe at BWR is 'racy with a hint of irony'... Through it's evolution, it's become one of the most hotly contested single day races in the 'gravel' scene. I was admittedly nervous as hell for it, but equally drawn to the vibe, media, hype and general partying associated with the race." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** On his 2019 BWR win while racing for Trek-Segafredo: "Crossing the line in first place, I guess it was a feeling of relief... But I never expected it to be this catalyst for changing my career. And then afterwards, the attention it got was like this lightbulb moment... A week later as I'm going up a climb in the Tour of California—swinging off after helping Richie Porte—and people are yelling, 'There's the Belgian Waffle winner!'" (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/belgian-waffle-ride/)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "I can't remember having as much fun on a bike. The vibe at BWR is 'racy with a hint of irony,' as died-in-the-wool road racers, mountain bikers, cyclocross-istas, and the occasional semi-retired professional triathlete all rub elbows in a challenging yet fun setting." (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Chris Bagg [COMPETITIVE]:** "After a mariachi band send off, our wave of maybe 400 people rolled off the start... This was admittedly the most terrifying part of the day." (https://caffeineandwatts.com/blogs/news/caffeine-and-watts-tackles-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "That's a big part of these events, it seems. We rode smooth for the next 30 miles until we hit the base of the Black Canyon climb. I looked around and realized I'd dropped everybody... I laughed to myself that my first actual ride with him [Eric Marcotte] was on this crazy thing." Describing the mix of riders and informal camaraderie. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Race growth:** "Set in the San Diego County foothills, BWR was started in 2012 with a rumored 100 participants, and since has grown to over 1500." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/5/13/self-discovery-in-a-waffle-stephen-recaps-belgian-waffle-ride)

**2025 Del Mar venue:** "The 14th Annual BWR California is moving to the scenic Del Mar Polo Fields in Surf Sports Park... the venue will transform into Southern California's largest cycling festival... With Belgian waffles, craft beer, and family-friendly fun, the event offers more than just cycling—it's a celebration for all." (https://strambecco.com/articles/update-on-belgian-waffle-ride-2025-expanding-the-gravel-frontier-and-moving-to-del-mar-polo-fields/)

**Charity component:** "All proceeds will benefit Curebound, a nonprofit dedicated to advancing cancer research. Every mile ridden and every festival moment contributes to this critical cause." (https://strambecco.com/articles/update-on-belgian-waffle-ride-2025-expanding-the-gravel-frontier-and-moving-to-del-mar-polo-fields/)
