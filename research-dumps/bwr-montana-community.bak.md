# BWR MONTANA — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Russell Finsterwald [ELITE]:** "Dax and I went to the line [together]. Lance was closing in pretty quick on us, so I had to launch my sprint a little early, which I think was good because I have a decently long sprint. That worked in my favor... I'm a big fan of diverse courses like that where you have to use your technical skills, have to use group dynamics and tactics. So it's kind of a course that challenged you in all aspects, which I think is neat." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/belgian-waffle-ride-series-2025/bwr-montana/results/)

**Russell Finsterwald [ELITE]:** "I'm really glad I came up to Bozeman, it was a super fun course. I just love doing new events because there are so many unknowns. The singletrack, in the end, pleased me the most." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/belgian-waffle-ride-series-2025/bwr-montana/results/)

**Stella Hobbs [ELITE]:** "That was a really fun ride. It broke on that first big climb, and I just spammed the gas." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**Luke Mosteller [ELITE]:** "We cooked up a plan the night before. I bridged to the break, went all in, and got to the singletrack. But that finish... it was chaos." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "Elevation: Don't forget Bozeman is at 4800 ft, and you ride up to 5500 feet numerous times which you will notice if you don't normally ride that high... Many long sustained climbs, with a total of 7900 feet of elevation gain. I had a total of 19 climbs on my head unit, with only 1 punchy section that was 11 percent, but it comes around mile 90." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "Course: The waffle section (north loop) is beautiful, the views are incredible, its much more scenic than the wafer and wanna sections of the course (you complete all three). 60 % is unroad, lots of gravel country roads." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "This was advertised as being awesome, but It was open to the public, and was pretty rocky. Got caught up with some riders walking the steep section in the middle, ended up having to walk, and a few non racer were using the trail riding against the flow." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**ToddAMelton [COMPETITIVE]:** "The elevation was definitely a challenge for a number of people. The level of competition was higher than what I've seen in other events. It seemed as though a good percentage of the racers had participated in other BWR events this year." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**ToddAMelton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I found the gravel roads to be of much higher quality than what I've seen anywhere in Oregon or Northern California; very few potholes, some loose gravel but not scary thick, no big sharp rocks, a little bit of washboarding but more baby washboards, and some sections were near paved quality." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**ToddAMelton [COMPETITIVE]:** "The single track near the end was pretty special... outside of a couple short chunky sections on the single track felt like it was the perfect tire choice." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**BH107 (local resident) [UNKNOWN]:** "I live in Bozeman and it has been pretty dry this spring. Count on fast graded county roads and only 1 real single track section which is downhill on a groomed trail. There are some punchy climbs including one paved section over 10%. Should be a really fun course and I wish I was in shape to ride it." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**Flavia Oliveira Parks [ELITE]:** "Despite the mechanical hiccup, I think Bozeman was by far the most amazing course they have ever put together. Perfect distance and elevation, the 'unroad' conditions were superb, the single track was amazing (I'm shocked I'm saying that), and the views were unreal! It was a super fast course despite the elevation gain." (https://firstendurance.com/blogs/articles/flavia-oliveria-q-a)

**Flavia Oliveira Parks [ELITE]:** "Racing at altitude is a bit trickier. Perhaps it's just me, but I tend to drink more at altitude, and it can't be just water. Additionally, I aim to replenish electrolytes and stay on top of my carb intake because you just burn more at elevation." (https://firstendurance.com/blogs/articles/flavia-oliveria-q-a)

**Siena Hermon [ELITE]:** "The next 40 miles were more of the same fast descents followed by steep climbs. None as long as the first but sustained enough that I felt out of my comfort zone. I started to wonder how I'd ever compete with the top women at Leadville, knowing there will be even more sustained climbing." (https://www.sienacycles.com/blog/luz8t56r37q3gqrezovgfbnymskouq)

**Siena Hermon [ELITE]:** "After the final climbs, we hit the section I was most looking forward to: singletrack! This is where I felt the most confident. I was smiling ear to ear, descending through grassy fields lined with purple and yellow wildflowers." (https://www.sienacycles.com/blog/luz8t56r37q3gqrezovgfbnymskouq)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "I liked about 100 of those 110.6 miles." When asked if she'd return next year, paused: "Right now? No. But tomorrow? Yeah." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "A lot of it was loose over hard gravel, most where riding in the thinned out tire ruts or over to the far right edge for there was little gravel. If you got out of the ruts you were surfing around in the soft stuff." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "The last 4 ish miles is single track. This was advertised as being awesome, but It was open to the public, and was pretty rocky. Got caught up with some riders walking the steep section in the middle, ended up having to walk, and a few non racer were using the trail riding against the flow." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**Race organizer Michael Marckx [ELITE]:** According to BWR founder Michael Marckx, "it was the closest top five finish in BWR history, as only 24 seconds separated the riders." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/belgian-waffle-ride-series-2025/bwr-montana/results/)

**Siena Hermon [ELITE]:** "At mile 23, I got a pack hand-up from my mom, little did we know it was at the bottom of a 900-foot climb. Not ideal for me already a heavier rider compared to some of the other women I didn't want to carry extra weight up that climb." (https://www.sienacycles.com/blog/luz8t56r37q3gqrezovgfbnymskouq)

**Stella Hobbs [ELITE]:** "It broke on that first big climb" - referring to where the women's race split early in the event. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**Flavia Oliveira Parks [ELITE]:** "Bozeman... the altitude, fast gravel, and elevation, along with some pretty exposed, windy parts meant that you had to be really strategic about racing and burning too many matches." (https://firstendurance.com/blogs/articles/flavia-oliveria-q-a)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Russell Finsterwald [ELITE]:** Made his winning move after 105 miles on technical singletrack, launching his sprint early to hold off Lance Haidet who was closing fast. Finished in 5:03:22. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/belgian-waffle-ride-series-2025/bwr-montana/results/)

**Stella Hobbs [ELITE]:** "Stormed away from the field on the first climb and riding solo across the line" - built an 11+ minute lead by attacking early and never looking back. Time: 5:31:33. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/belgian-waffle-ride-series-2025/bwr-montana/results/)

**Siena Hermon [ELITE]:** "At mile 23, I got a pack hand-up from my mom, little did we know it was at the bottom of a 900-foot climb" - learned that accepting feeds at the bottom of big climbs is not ideal when you're already a heavier rider. (https://www.sienacycles.com/blog/luz8t56r37q3gqrezovgfbnymskouq)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** Aid stops "Were well stocked and appropriately placed, roughly at 22, 39, 67, 98. Had cold coke which was a bonus." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**Flavia Oliveira Parks [ELITE]:** "Hydration and calorie intake have been major areas of focus for me, and it's been an ongoing trial and error situation, particularly when you have to deal with stopping for feeds, where I'm not always able to always have my 'own' First Endurance nutrition. However, that comes with the territory in gravel racing, and being able to adapt or make the most of it is part of the game as well." (https://firstendurance.com/blogs/articles/flavia-oliveria-q-a)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Flavia Oliveira Parks [ELITE]:** "Oh boy, definitely having a little mechanical malfunction at mile 40 in Bozeman. I was stuck in my 10 cog and I thought my race was over, which was a major bummer. I lost the momentum of being in the group, but luckily after fiddling with it and swapping batteries, I was able to get going again, only to have the same issue happen again at around mile 97. This time I wasn't able to fix it, and was forced to finish 'fixie' style, but at least this time I was in my 17!" (https://firstendurance.com/blogs/articles/flavia-oliveria-q-a)

**Race report [ELITE]:** An unexpected train stop added four minutes to some riders' times in the women's race, though "The riders seemed to take the unexpected stop in stride, and battled on for the final spots on the podium." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/belgian-waffle-ride-series-2025/bwr-montana/results/)

**ToddAMelton [COMPETITIVE]:** "The elevation was definitely a challenge for a number of people" - suggesting altitude was a dropout factor for some riders. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)
