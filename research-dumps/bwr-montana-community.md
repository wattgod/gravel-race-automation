## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "Race Organization: Check in and parking were excellent. Elevation: Don't forget Bozeman is at 4800 ft, and you ride up to 5500 feet numerous times which you will notice if you don't normally ride that high. Course: The waffle section (north loop) is beautiful, the views are incredible, its much more scenic than the wafer and wanna sections of the course (you complete all three). 60 % is unroad, lots of gravel country roads. Many long sustained climbs, with a total of 7900 feet of elevation gain. I had a total of 19 climbs on my head unit, with only 1 punchy section that was 11 percent, but it comes around mile 90." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "The last 4 ish miles is single track. This was advertised as being awesome, but It was open to the public, and was pretty rocky. Got caught up with some riders walking the steep section in the middle, ended up having to walk, and a few non racer were using the trail riding against the flow." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "Aid stops: Were well stocked and appropriately placed, roughly at 22, 39, 67, 98. Had cold coke which was a bonus." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "Finish line: Decent vibes, nothing that makes you hang out. You get a beer and coozy. Overall I enjoyed the event, I would do it again." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**ToddAMelton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I really enjoyed the event as well as spending in and around Bozeman. I am already planning to do it again. The elevation was definitely a challenge for a number of people. The level of competition was higher than what I've seen in other events. It seemed as though a good percentage of the racers had participated in other BWR events this year." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**ToddAMelton [COMPETITIVE]:** "I found the gravel roads to be of much higher quality than what I've seen anywhere in Oregon or Northern California; very few potholes, some loose gravel but not scary thick, no big sharp rocks, a little bit of washboarding but more baby washboards, and some sections were near paved quality. The single track near the end was pretty special." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**BH107 [COMPETITIVE] (local Bozeman rider):** "I live in Bozeman and it has been pretty dry this spring. Count on fast graded county roads and only 1 real single track section which is downhill on a groomed trail. There are some punchy climbs including one paved section over 10%." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**Luke Mosteller [ELITE] (4th place):** "We cooked up a plan the night before. I bridged to the break, went all in, and got to the singletrack. But that finish... it was chaos." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**Stella Hobbs [ELITE] (1st place women):** "That was a really fun ride. It broke on that first big climb, and I just spammed the gas." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**Unidentified rider [UNKNOWN]:** "I liked about 100 of those 110.6 miles." When asked if she'd return next year, she paused: "Right now? No. But tomorrow? Yeah." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**Russell Finsterwald [ELITE] (1st place men):** "BWR Montana was an unforgettable experience. The course was challenging, but the community support made it all worthwhile. I'm honored to take home the title and can't wait for the next challenge." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8604/stella-hobbs-and-russell-finsterwald-take-victory-at-the-inaugural-2025-bwr-montana)

**Stella Hobbs [ELITE]:** "Winning here in Bozeman, surrounded by such passionate fans and fellow cyclists, is a dream come true. I'm grateful for the support and can't wait to return next year." ## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**MikeMckinney [COMPETITIVE]:** "A lot of it was loose over hard gravel, most where riding in the thinned out tire ruts or over to the far right edge for there was little gravel. If you got out of the ruts you were surfing around in the soft stuff." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-montana-june-29th/103804)

**Course architect Michael Marckx [ELITE]:** "We're at mile 22, and this is where it starts to get real. A really good climb coming up, and the leaders are looking... a little too good." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**From race report [ELITE perspective]:** The inaugural race featured "champagne gravel, blistering climbs, fast descents, singletrack surprise" with "Bozeman's sneaky singletrack" in the final sections. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-montana-2025-bwr-montana-brings-the-pain-to-bozeman)

**Official course details (2025):**
- Waffle: 111-115 miles | 7,900-8,240 ft climbing | 60% unroad
- Wafer: 69 miles | 4,212 ft climbing | 35% unroad
- Wanna: 30 miles | 1,200 ft climbing | 30% unroad
(https://www.belgianwaffleride.bike/pages/belgian-waffle-ride-montana)

**From race description:** "smooth valley roads and winding forest tracks to rolling gravel climbs and high-speed descents through Big Sky Country." (https://www.belgianwaffleride.bike/pages/belgian-waffle-ride-montana)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Russell Finsterwald [ELITE]:** Made his decisive move after 105 miles, breaking away on singletrack to secure victory in approximately 5:03:22. The top 5 men finished within 24 seconds of each other. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8604/stella-hobbs-and-russell-finsterwald-take-victory-at-the-inaugural-2025-bwr-montana)

**From elite race narrative:** "The men's race was a slow-burn thriller, with early breakaways, group chases, and a finish tighter than your legs after 8 hours in the saddle."
