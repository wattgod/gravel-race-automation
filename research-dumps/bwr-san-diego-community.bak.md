# BELGIAN WAFFLE RIDE SAN DIEGO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "I didn't expect to be at the start line of the 2025 Belgian Waffle Ride in San Diego. Between a new job, family-wide respiratory illness, and a never-ending sleep deficit, this wasn't exactly the ideal lead-up to a 100-mile gravel race... We were two of just 21 women to finish the Waffle that day. Only about two-thirds of the 300 men finished." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "With 1,500 riders rolling out together, we got caught behind bottlenecks on early singletrack—losing nearly 45 minutes before the race even really began... At the second station, Keri took off, determined to beat the 11:30 a.m. deadline. I chased her through endless steep hills, my legs protesting every 15–17% gradient with increasing rage." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "Just when we thought we were done, the course served us one last cruel combo: a dam, a lake, an eternal set of switchbacks, and the choice between a hike-a-bike or a creek crossing. We chose the creek—and slammed our bikes into hidden boulders. I went full submersion. Honestly, it felt amazing on my burning feet." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Chris Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** Took the Spezia to victory in the Wanna event, with Troy Templin [COMPETITIVE] finishing second on a Spezia (both finishing times not listed, but placed three Litespeeds in top 10). (https://litespeed.com/blogs/litespeed-stories/2024-belgian-waffle-ride-san-diego-dusted-belgian-style)

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "The race kicked off at 7am and hits a 1.7-mile road climb at mile 4 to break the field up before descending into the first dirt sector. My goal was to make it over the climb in the 2nd group and I was right where I wanted to be after an 8-minute effort at 440 watts." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "My only option to avoid [a stopped rider] lead me to crash into a wood post fence... There was a puncture on the tread and near the tire bead. The sealant eventually worked but I had to air up multiple times and hold my bike sideways to get the sealant to pool at the puncture before sealing properly. I still had 15 miles of dirt to go until I saw pavement again." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "Once I hit the Double Peak climb, I couldn't push my target power of 300 watts. I was pushing 270 and getting passed more than I should have... I crossed the finish line in 7:23, in 91st place." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Peter Stetina [ELITE]:** "I wrote myself off about 10 times today. I did not have a clean run by any means... There were a few odd mechanicals, no major issues, but something would go wrong due to the bumps. My derailleur worked its way loose and I had to stop and screw it back in. I was crashed on an uphill in soft sand." Finished in 6:10:20 to win the 2021 edition. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Katerina Nash [ELITE]:** "I'm beyond excited to take the win at Belgian Waffle Ride. The intimidating distance of 132 miles, the heat and the competition kept me focused. I put it all together and saved enough energy for final climb attack." Won the women's race in 7:15:09 in 2021. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "You need someone to have your back when shit hits the fan. The mechanical stuff, we're all supposed to be dealing with that on the course—that's expected. But if you're racing at the top level, that is probably the biggest disparity between someone finishing 20th and someone finishing first or in the top five." Won 2022 BWR California. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/features/how-to-be-a-gravel-pro-vermeulens-top-10-tips/)

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "At BWR, Willie was not in the lead selection, but Vermeulen was. He attacked on a climb about 50 miles from the finish, and only current WorldTour rider Alex Howes was able to keep his pace. Vermeulen then attacked coming up on 10 miles to go to get away for the win." (https://cxhairs.substack.com/p/freeroading-report-belgian-waffle)

**Flavia Oliveira [ELITE]:** "Gravel's a compromise. I never know what the right set-up is... If I had a bigger tyre, not a 38 inch [sic], if I had gone with a 35[mm], I think that would have been enough because you can roll faster on pavement. That's where I was hurting, especially as it got windier. I chose the Specialized Crux, but I suffered a lot more on the road." Finished 2nd in 2022. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/flavia-oliveira-embraces-gravel-its-you-against-the-terrain/)

**Brendan "Trekky" Johnston [ELITE]:** "I'd been out there [in Leadville] for three weeks, and ridden down Powerline probably ten times. I was following Torbjørn, which was fine—he's a good rider—and it just happened that he passed this rock, and it dislodged from the bank and tumbled across the track. It didn't even hurt my rear tyre; it just smashed into my rear wheel. It was like a big boulder, and it just destroyed my wheel, instantly." DNF'd in 2024 Life Time GP at Leadville due to mechanical. (https://flowmountainbike.com/features/brendan-trekky-johnston-on-racing-the-lifetime-grand-prix/)

**ndmufc [UNKNOWN]:** "I rode it last year on my road bike with IRC Serac CX Sand 30's fitted. Although these felt great on the road sections, for me, they weren't worth the payoff with how much of a slog I felt it was on the gravel (granted the heat did not help last year). This year, with the increase in the gravel sectors, I'll be riding my gravel bike with something like 36's with slicker centres fitted purely for my own piece of mind." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

**whatsername [UNKNOWN]:** "I've done the BWR CA 3 times (waffle distance). Last year I did it on my road bike with Hutchinson Sector 30mm tubeless tires. My gearing is 52/36 with an 11-28. I'm signed up for this year and plan on using the same bike (My gravel bike is way heavier and I'd be spun out on the road sections too). I'm planning to run 32mm tires this time, likely the IRC serac." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Choice:**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** Chose ENVE Melee with 48/35 chainrings, 10-36 cassette, and 35c Pirelli Cinturato Gravel H tires mounted on Zipp 303 Firecrest wheels at 44 PSI. "I thought my setup would be quick on the pavement and have enough tire volume to be comfortable on dirt." Post-race reflection: "I would run a mullet setup with a 48T chainring and a 10-52 cassette. This would allow me to run the 38c Specialized Pathfinder Pro tires and have a lower gear for some of the +20% gradient climbs near the end of the ride." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Flavia Oliveira [ELITE]:** Used Specialized Crux with 38mm tires but regretted not going with 35mm: "If I had gone with a 35[mm], I think that would have been enough because you can roll faster on pavement. That's where I was hurting, especially as it got windier." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/flavia-oliveira-embraces-gravel-its-you-against-the-terrain/)

**Chris Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** Used Ultimate G2 to win Wanna event. Troy Templin [COMPETITIVE] used the new Spezia (clearance to 35mm tires) and finished second. (https://litespeed.com/blogs/litespeed-stories/2024-belgian-waffle-ride-san-diego-dusted-belgian-style)

**Tire Recommendations:**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "I didn't have a lot of cornering and braking confidence with the 35c tires on the loose over hard single track we spent a lot of time on. The wider tire would let me run lower pressure and have some better grip in those sectors." Recommended 38c for better traction. (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**jfranci3 [UNKNOWN]:** "I tried using Conti Terra Speed 38c tires and just couldn't put any power down going up the dirt hills, but I'm not fast. I'd say these are the minimum tire off road... The loose dirt on top of the sand just slips under the tire as you climb, similar to climbing in mud or snow... If you have FRESH Terra Speeds and you're not trying to win, I'd bring them. If you're not trying to run 28-30c tires, just go straight to 38c+." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

**jfranci3 [UNKNOWN]:** "I wouldn't mess with something with a smooth center, you need center knobs; I wouldn't mess with a textured tire (G-One Allaround; GK SK) as it won't be able to bite through the dust. Terra Speeds or Challenge Gateway would good choice for the mixed race." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

**Gearing:**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** Post-race: "I would run a mullet setup with a 48T chainring and a 10-52 cassette" for better climbing on +20% gradients. (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**whatsername [UNKNOWN]:** Successfully completed 3x Waffle distance on road bike with 52/36 chainrings and 11-28 cassette. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

**Mechanical Preparation:**

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "Learn how to use them [tools] in your garage when you're not stressed. I pride myself on being able to do all of that... You have to know how to use all the tools you have. Cam Wurf flatted and didn't really know how to use a tire plug. Sealant fixes most things on the road. That is not the case for gravel. Most times you flat, you're going to have to plug it." Carried "six plugs taped on my bike, I had two plugs in my pocket. I had duct tape, I had super glue, all the little things that are light and easy. One thing I had happen two years ago at Unbound, I used my CO2 head so much the gasket blew out. I had to carry two CO2 heads this year." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/features/how-to-be-a-gravel-pro-vermeulens-top-10-tips/)

## Weather Experienced

**2021 Edition:** Described as having "unprecedented conditions" with "90-degree temperatures" and "brutal conditions." Peter Stetina [ELITE] mentioned battling "leg cramps throughout the day" in the heat. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**2023 Edition:** "Grey skies are generally frowned upon in (typically) sunny Southern California, riders were met with cloudy but mild skies that warmed slightly through the day—perfect temps for hard riding without overheating." (https://litespeed.com/blogs/litespeed-stories/2024-belgian-waffle-ride-san-diego-dusted-belgian-style)

**2025 Edition:** Yun [UNKNOWN] noted: "At least the weather's cool" during the race. (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**General Warning from jfranci3 [UNKNOWN]:** "There's ZERO water out there and the SD weather forecast lies to you (it'll say 70, but it's 90 after the first set of hills)." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Early Bottlenecks:**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "With 1,500 riders rolling out together, we got caught behind bottlenecks on early singletrack—losing nearly 45 minutes before the race even really began." At 11 miles: "hit the first bottleneck." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "The race kicked off at 7am and hits a 1.7-mile road climb at mile 4 to break the field up before descending into the first dirt sector." Made the second group after "an 8-minute effort at 440 watts." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Highland Valley:**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "Oh Highland Valley, I will never forget you!!! ...endless steep hills, my legs protesting every 15–17% gradient with increasing rage." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Pamo Valley (aka "Death Valley"):**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "Somewhere in the chaos, I dropped my chain, lost Keri, missed a trail, and somehow ended up ahead of her. Once we reunited, we began the soul-crushing climb through what I now call Death Valley—aka Pamo Valley. A fierce headwind met us on the return." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Creek Crossing:**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "Just when we thought we were done, the course served us one last cruel combo: a dam, a lake, an eternal set of switchbacks, and the choice between a hike-a-bike or a creek crossing. We chose the creek—and slammed our bikes into hidden boulders. I went full submersion." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Final Climbs:**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "The final three gravel hills were so steep that I had to push my bike. Keri? She fishtailed up them like a legend." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "Before Double Peak we raced 12 miles of technical single track with a lot more sharp-edged rock. My tire was still low, and I felt the rim many times, so I had to take it easy." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Surface Conditions:**

**jfranci3 [UNKNOWN]:** "The surface has a layer of loose dust over hard pack. The hills are all the way steep 16-20%. Traction is an issue everywhere... The loose dirt on top of the sand just slips under the tire as you climb, similar to climbing in mud or snow... Braking is also a concern as they decents are -18%. It really is XC MTB territory." Also noted: "Sand - bring some lube or something to clean the chain mid ride. Not wax based (prewax = yes)." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

**Course Layout (2023):**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "126 mile, 10,500 feet of climbing race with 85 miles of road and 41 miles of dirt, much of it being single track." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Strategy:**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** Targeted 440W for the initial 8-minute climb at mile 4, aiming for second group. Later targeted 300W for Double Peak climb but could only sustain 270W and got passed. Total time: 7:23:00 for 91st place. (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Peter Stetina [ELITE]:** "In the race's second half the field appeared to wilt in the heat and Stetina made his move" around mile 70, attacking with Russell Finsterwald and John Borstelmann. "On the final climb of the day, the ascent of Double Peak, Stetina dropped his companions and then caught and dropped Floren, eventually descending to the finish." Finish time: 6:10:20. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** On nutrition: "Guessing that the first feed zone at 77 miles is going to be about three and a half hours, for Unbound, I had two bottles with a full serving of SuperFuel, which is either 400 calories or 100 grams of carbs, and two litres of plain water on my back, and I had food." He adjusted based on conditions: "Maybe I didn't eat enough as much as I needed and I need three bottles coming out of this feed zone because I'm having a much easier time drinking my calories today." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/features/how-to-be-a-gravel-pro-vermeulens-top-10-tips/)

**Recreational/Unknown Strategy:**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** Chased cutoffs all day: "At the second station, Keri took off, determined to beat the 11:30 a.m. deadline. I chased her through endless steep hills." At Station 3 (mile 60): "volunteers told us future aid stops might not be manned. We stuffed our pockets with bars, gels, and Coke and committed to the final push." Finished "beating the 10-hour cutoff by the skin of our teeth." Food: "Dates rolled in granola and peanut butter and salt along with the tens of Stryker Gels, NeverSecond drink mix, and my LMNT salts and some gummies." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Key Tactical Points:**

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "You don't have a director who's gone out and driven the course to tell you about that climb that's 20km in and that's going to split the field, you need to have ridden that or driven it or know it. So either you get really good at Google Maps and studying things, or you spend a lot of time out on course. Very rarely do I get to ride the whole course, but there are not any courses I don't ride at least the first 20 and last 20." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/features/how-to-be-a-gravel-pro-vermeulens-top-10-tips/)

**Feed Zone Support:**

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "If I go, I'm paying someone to be full support in the feed zone. We're in the Unbound feed zones for less than 45 seconds on the long end. You can't do that alone. That's part of the race." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/features/how-to-be-a-gravel-pro-vermeulens-top-10-tips/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Finish Rates (2025):**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "We were two of just 21 women to finish the Waffle that day. Only about two-thirds of the 300 men finished." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**General Attrition:**

Event founder Michael Marckx noted: "The BWR typically sees over 40% attrition" according to Source Endurance camp materials. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Common Mechanical Issues:**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "There was a puncture on the tread and near the tire bead. The sealant eventually worked but I had to air up multiple times and hold my bike sideways to get the sealant to pool at the puncture before sealing properly. I still had 15 miles of dirt to go until I saw pavement again... My tire was still low, and I felt the rim many times." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**Peter Stetina [ELITE]:** "There were a few odd mechanicals, no major issues, but something would go wrong due to the bumps. My derailleur worked its way loose and I had to stop and screw it back in. I was crashed on an uphill in soft sand." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Early Bottleneck Time Loss:**

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** Lost "nearly 45 minutes before the race even really began" due to singletrack bottlenecks with 1,500 riders starting together. This put significant pressure on cutoff times throughout the day. (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Pinch Flats & Sharp Rocks:**

**Nick Leeper [ELITE]:** "Before Double Peak we raced 12 miles of technical single track with a lot more sharp-edged rock." (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/nick-leeper-bwr-ca)

**jfranci3 [UNKNOWN]:** Emphasized: "rocks (pinch flats, use TLR)" as a major concern. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/bwr-california-bike-tire-choice/70453)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Peter Stetina [ELITE]:** "These are my A races now. This is it's own legitimate discipline... We are creating our own discipline and I'm so proud to be a part of it. This is fun—now I get to have beers with 4,000 of my friends." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6230/katerina-nash-and-peter-stetina-fastest-at-the-2021-belgian-waffle-ride)

**Flavia Oliveira [ELITE]:** "I find it's the only area of cycling that gives women equal everything. We have the same course, same distance, same prize money. You don't find that in many other areas of cycling, and it's very appealing to me. I even preferred the mass start with the men. It is a lot more fun for us women. I think gravel is an inviting place, especially for women." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/flavia-oliveira-embraces-gravel-its-you-against-the-terrain/)

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "I met the Opicure Foundation team on a pre-ride, we ended up having dinner together, and I rode with three of them at different points on race day. I spent hours riding and talking with Remko Rinkema. At the end of the weekend, I have seven more friends that I'm sure I'll see again at future events. I caught up with numerous others at the expo and post-race that I met in previous years. The community grows and strengthens with every event and it's amazing." (https://flowmountainbike.com/features/brendan-trekky-johnston-on-racing-the-lifetime-grand-prix/)

**Pre-Race Festival:**

Saturday includes "the BWR Unroad Expo, teeming with live music, a beer garden, ample waffles, and the festive spirit of San Diego in the Spring. You can also expect pre-rides and a comprehensive rider briefing. Sunday, race day, starts bright and early at 5 am with waffles and coffee to fuel the riders." (https://strambecco.com/event/belgian-waffle-ride-san-diego-california/)

**Post-Race:**

"Post-race celebrations include revealing the winner of the Canyon Bike/OpiCure bike giveaway and loads of Rouleur's signature Badass Ale." Equal prize money awarded across three categories (M/W/Gender Diverse). (https://strambecco.com/event/belgian-waffle-ride-san-diego-california/)

**Yun [UNKNOWN]:** "Gravel isn't just harder than road—it's way more dynamic, and honestly, a lot more fun... Belgian Waffle Ride California Del Mar 2025 was equal parts chaos and catharsis. From chasing cutoffs and creek crashes to unexpected trail magic and camaraderie, it was a ride I'll never forget. Sleep-deprived, saddle-sore, and smiling at the end—that's gravel for you." (https://thresholdcycling.com/blogs/training-resources/from-doubt-to-dirt-my-grit-fueled-ride-at-belgian-waffle-ride)
