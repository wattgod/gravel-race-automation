# BWR UTAH — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** "BWR Utah is where my gravel journey began back in 2020 with a 9th-place finish. Since then I've added three more top-10s: 9th in 2022, 4th in 2023, 6th in 2024. And in 2025, it finally all came together as I achieved my long-term goal of winning this race." (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p2jtN5LC32Q)

**Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** "I wasn't thinking about the finish. Just trying to stay out of trouble, eat, drink, and push when it mattered... I was holding a power I wasn't sure I could sustain, but I sensed it was my moment. I just kept going." On his 2025 victory at BWR Utah. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**Flavia Oliveira Parks [ELITE]:** "I feel like I got the monkey off my back. It's my first win at BWR, and it feels amazing... I wasn't thinking about the win. I was just trying to stay out of trouble, eat, and drink as much as possible. It never lets up out there." After winning the women's race in 2025. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**Whitney Allison [ELITE]:** "This was our race" — after winning BWR Utah 2021, finishing with over 7 minutes on 2nd place. Her husband supported at every aid station. (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021)

**Lindsay Goldman [ELITE]:** On the rocky Turnpike section: "Goldman had no idea what the rocks could do or would feel like, so she just rode them. That split-second decision and in portion naiveté solidified 2nd place for Goldman" — finished 2nd at 2021 BWR Utah. (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021)

**One rider [LEVEL UNKNOWN]:** "Too much sanding it—bumpy out there" — commenting on the mile 52 singletrack section with jump features in 2025. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**One rider [LEVEL UNKNOWN]:** "Still climbing... still got a ways to go" — as temperatures crept toward the 80s during the race. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I was actually pretty frustrated with gravel because I didn't feel like I was strong enough to really race. I was always kind of a participant and finished in that middle-pack range. So, I took a step back from gravel and really focused on that higher-end power earlier this year." After winning BWR Utah 2023. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "Once we were able to make contact with them [the pro men's group], we got to sit up and it was kind of nice for a minute. Then we got into the notorious single-track section, which actually was pretty rugged. I am a mountain biker, or at least I like to consider myself a mountain biker, and it is pretty technical terrain for a gravel race. I felt completely under-biked." (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I crashed in the single track and I bent my deraillage hanger, losing my easiest gears. [It] was fine for the most part because the course is pretty flat, except for the last climb at 110." (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I instantly started cramping in both of my legs, both quads, both adductors, and my calves were going too, which I've never had before. I'm curious if that's because I haven't ridden longer than four hours this year and this was at hour six or so. But once it flattened out and I was able to put down some more power, the cramps went away." (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Courtney Sherwell [ELITE]:** "They are leading the way with gravel events in the U.S., and it was just something that, because I love gravel so much and it suits me, I wanted to experience. There is so much participation in the US, and even just the spectators there as well so, I think it'd be really good to get the vibe and experience ... and hopefully also get some good results too." Australian rider planning to compete at BWR Utah. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/chasing-the-gravel-experience-a-year-of-branching-out-for-australias-courtney-sherwell/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Width [ORGANIZER RECOMMENDATION]:** "The suggestion for tires at least 40mm in width" — from the official race guidance. Given the 75% off-road terrain including sand, rocks, and technical singletrack. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2020-bwr-cedar-city-utah-routes)

**Bike Setup — Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** "His setup was all-in for speed: aero wheels, fast tires" — used for his 2025 victory, particularly effective on the downhill attack at mile 86 and in the tailwind finish. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**Music for Training — Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** Uses H2O Audio TRI 2 PRO bone-conduction headphones with dual-mode functionality (Bluetooth + Memory) for long training rides to maintain "focus, rhythm, and mental toughness." (https://h2oaudio.com/blogs/news/from-fire-sparked-to-finish-line-joe-goettl-wins-bwr-utah)

**Mechanical Vulnerability:** Multiple riders experienced issues. Heidi Franz had a slow leak to her front tire in 2021. Melisa Rollins bent her derailleur hanger in a singletrack crash, losing easiest gears. (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021 and https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Technical Setup Considerations [ELITE]:** Melisa Rollins felt "completely under-biked" for the singletrack sections despite being a mountain biker, suggesting the technical terrain exceeds typical gravel bike capabilities. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Race Day:** "The cool morning air hovered around the low 50s" at start, then "As the temperatures crept toward the 80s, the race lived up to its 'hell of the west' reputation. Riders battled not only each other, but the sun and dry desert air." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**General Conditions:** The course features "strong headwinds from approximately mile-42 to mile-97 will have a definite bearing on whether people finish or not" according to 2020 course description. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2020-bwr-cedar-city-utah-routes)

**2025 Finish Conditions:** Joe Goettl benefited from "a ripping tailwind" during his solo breakaway after the Kanarraberg climb. (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**The Tolweg (Turnpuke) Section:** Appears at mile 116, described in the official Bijbel as "a dirty, dastardly and diabolical sector 4.4-miles in length and twists and turns--sweeping and swooping--leaving riders weeping or whooping, depending on their single track skills and whatever energy they may have left... This definitive sector is a single track that contours Iron Hills, just above the valley floor, with its most prominent features being rocks, three dozen turns, boulders, junipers, sage brush, pines and more rocks." (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/september-legs-singletrack-bwr-utah-143428100.html)

**Mile 52 Singletrack:** "At mile 52, near feed zone two, the course featured a rowdy singletrack section complete with jump features" in 2025. One rider noted it was "Too much sanding it—bumpy out there." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

**Singletrack Variety:** Michael Marckx (race founder): "We go into a mountain bike park for seven miles and wind our way through that on really flowy, nice, singletrack. With bridges and fun terrain stuff. Not long after that, we literally go onto a motocross course. We do the full motocross course and then get back out onto gravel roads." (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/september-legs-singletrack-bwr-utah-143428100.html)

**Kanarra Mountain KOM/QOM:** "Appears on the horizon until around mile 108. There, riders tackle a roughly three mile climb that averages 10 percent." (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/september-legs-singletrack-bwr-utah-143428100.html)

**Rocky Canyon Section:** In 2021, "As the riders entered a narrow canyon with rocks from one side to the other, Goldman rode the segment, and Franz opted to carry her bike and run. Franz being the more experienced dirt rider, knew the treachery of the rocks ahead, so she took a more conservative approach." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021)

**Little Salt Lake Section:** In 2021, "As the women neared the first dirt section coming off the gravel road, the chasing men of the wafer caught the group. This happened at a perfect spot before the road kicked up and became more technical through Little Salt Lake." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021)

**Terrain Variety:** "What's unique about that particular setting is we go through a few different types of terrain. Yeah there are gravel roads, but there's red hills and trees near Salt Lake City. Then you're back out onto the high desert and you get 10 miles of incredible rolling hills and gravel. Then, a bunch of sand and other stuff." (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/september-legs-singletrack-bwr-utah-143428100.html)

**Mt. Kanarraberg Climb [2025]:** Described as "a brutal climb stretching over 2.6 miles at punishing gradients" where Joe Goettl attacked and went solo. (https://h2oaudio.com/blogs/news/from-fire-sparked-to-finish-line-joe-goettl-wins-bwr-utah)

**Course Changes:** For 2026, organizers note: "This year's course removes technical single-track in favor of flowing dirt and gravel sections that reward power, skill, and strategy" and features "an all-new route across gravel, sand, and rock, at altitude, with a dynamic array of rippable unroad spiciness (no crazy single-track this time)." (https://www.belgianwaffleride.bike/pages/utah)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Pacing — Whitney Allison [ELITE]:** "There was never one big out of the saddle attack or decisive technical descent for Whitney. She just would not let up. As the gradient increased, she hunkered down and would not relinquish her pace. The separation began to happen at mile 58, halfway through a long moderate grade on gravel." Her gap grew to 9 minutes by mile 100. (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021)

**Early Race Positioning — Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "Going into the race, my main focus was to try to be present at the front because last year when I raced gravel, I always struggled with that... I was super excited to see how the start would go, and it actually went really well. I was able to follow wheels better than I ever had before." She was "the last person to not make the lead men's group" after the first main climb. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Working Strategy — Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** After missing the lead group: "I sat up as I cruxed the top and noticed that Tiffany was just 10 seconds back, so I was like, 'Okay, here's our race. Let's start, let's go'. The two of us found another man to work with and the three of us actually ended up pulling back the pro men's group at mile 45…after 10 miles of dying." (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Climb Strategy — Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** "Joe's plan was to stay patient, make moves when the course demanded it, and deliver on climbs." On the Mt. Kanarraberg climb, "By the halfway point of the climb, he was solo off the front. With over 30 seconds on the nearest chaser and a ripping tailwind, Joe leaned into his aero setup and pushed the advantage all the way to the finish line." (https://h2oaudio.com/blogs/news/from-fire-sparked-to-finish-line-joe-goettl-wins-bwr-utah)

**Downhill Attack — Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** "At mile 86, Joe Goettl made his move on a downhill section—'believe it or not'—and it stuck." (https://almsthre.com/blogs/news/bwr-utah-2025)

## Nutrition & Fueling Strategy

**High-Carb Strategy — Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I switched over to high-carb fueling [in the bottles]. I used Skratch Superfuel and realized I was actually able to tolerate a lot more carbs an hour than I ever was doing before. I was doing 60g/hr before and when I started using the Superfuel I could get into 90-100 grams an hour pretty easily." For BWR Utah specifically: "I started out targeting 100-110 an hour in the first three hours, then I diluted the Skratch a little bit. I counted afterward how many carbs I ended up consuming an hour and it was ~120g/hr. That was through a mix of Superfuel and high carb energy gels." Note: This was for a 6+ hour effort by an elite rider. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Fueling Challenge — Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "Belgium Waffle Ride was actually kind of a test. I was scared because I had only tried 120 grams of carbs per hour for three hours and had no idea what happened beyond that." The race extended beyond her training duration. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Feed Zone Availability:** The 2023 Waffle course had "4 Feed Zones" spaced roughly every 20-30 miles; the Wafer had "3 Feed Zones"; the Wanna had "2 Feed Zones." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021 and https://strambecco.com/event/belgian-waffle-ride-bwr-utah/)

**Aid Station Support — Joe Goettl [COMPETITIVE]:** "His wife, Kendra, was there for every aid station, helping him execute a flawless race-day strategy." (https://h2oaudio.com/blogs/news/from-fire-sparked-to-finish-line-joe-goettl-wins-bwr-utah)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Failures:** Heidi Franz (elite) suffered "a slow leak to her front tire" which took her out of contention in 2021. Griffin Easter, after "what looked like a guaranteed sprint between he and Stetina last year, the 30-year-old punctured in the notorious Tolweg section" in 2020/2021. (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021 and https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/september-legs-singletrack-bwr-utah-143428100.html)

**Cramping Issues — Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "I instantly started cramping in both of my legs, both quads, both adductors, and my calves were going too, which I've never had before. I'm curious if that's because I haven't ridden longer than four hours this year and this was at hour six or so." She managed to continue, but noted cramping forced her to walk when her derailleur threw into the spokes on the final climb. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Crashes:** Multiple crashes reported. Tiffany (last name not specified) "wiped out pretty hard" on the descent at mile 85 in 2023, leaving Rollins solo. Melisa Rollins crashed in the singletrack section, bending her derailleur hanger. (https://thefeed.com/insider/gold-for-rollins-at-bwr-utah)

**Navigational Issues:** Flavia Oliveira Parks "had a major navigational issue in North Carolina" which affected her Quadrupel Crown standing, suggesting navigation can be a factor in BWR events. (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/september-legs-singletrack-bwr-utah-143428100.html)

**Chase Group Motivation Fade:** "The fatigue of the chase was wearing on the group and with the leaders so far out of sight, motivation was waning. It's a flip of the coin to work hard or rotate in harsh desert conditions, knowing the lion's share of the climbing was still ahead, and you may be acting as the catalyst for a competitor to launch their attacks." — describing the 2021 women's chase group dynamics. (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/race-report-bwr-utah-2021)

**Headwinds:** "The headwinds from approximately mile-42 to mile-97 will have a definite bearing on whether people finish or not" — official course warning. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2020-bwr-cedar-city-utah-routes)

A united group who would soon begin throwing watts at each other, but of their own volition—not because the men were riding harder into the headwind.
