# CAMP MICHAUX GRAVEL GRINDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Evan McGregor [ELITE]:** "Since attending the BaseCamp gravel camp in Michaux Forest back in October, I've really wanted to get to one of the races there, and Camp Michaux has been a top priority race for me since then. Unfortunately, I suffered a training related injury at the end of February that then kept me off the bike for 2 months and in PT for the past 4 months... I was with the front of the race, until I realized I overspent and was unable to follow the front 5. I dialed things back and paced my effort. A bad cramp on Big Pond Hollow cost me a few minutes off the bike... A sidewall slash on my rear tire ended my ride. I didn't bring a tube and nothing was plugging that, though I tried for about 4 minutes. Being so close to the finish still in the top of the field was agonizing... so I picked up my bike and ran. The aptly named Birch Run Road served as my 4k running finale. With my bike in hand and cycling shoes on, I gave it what I had for the last 2.5 miles... Almost 25 minutes of running with a handful of riders passing me (including the AG winner) brought me to the line... I finished in second place by 12 seconds in my AG, and 10 minutes down on the category winner." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**Evan McGregor [ELITE]:** "The BaseCamp gravel camp in Michaux helped build the skills I needed for descending to no longer be a liability or outright weakness." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**Leo Hamilton's Dad [RECREATIONAL]:** Context from video title/description: "Dad Is Now a BIG Gravel Guy!! Michaux Gravel Grinder" - described as "our first gravel bike race! My brother Brian and I ride with our Dad at the Camp Michaux Gravel Grinder. This was our Father's Day gift to him this year" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bt-dxNUM810)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Tire setup warning:** Evan McGregor experienced a sidewall slash that could not be plugged with standard plug kit. He noted: "I didn't bring a tube and nothing was plugging that, though I tried for about 4 minutes." This suggests carrying a tube as backup for the Michaux terrain is advisable, even for experienced riders. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Skills preparation:** Evan credited attending the BaseCamp gravel camp in Michaux Forest for improving his descending skills, noting that "descending -- which used to be a liability for me -- was much improved from the skills camp, and I was ripping along." This suggests technical descending skills matter for competitive placement. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Big Pond Hollow:** [ELITE] Evan McGregor noted suffering "a bad cramp on Big Pond Hollow" that cost him "a few minutes off the bike," suggesting this is a particularly demanding section of the course. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**Birch Run Road finish:** [ELITE] Described as the final approach, it's where Evan ran 2.5 miles (about 4k) with his bike after a flat. He was "audibly cursing every time I got passed," suggesting it's visible/exposed with other riders still coming through, and likely gradual enough that running with a bike is physically possible though painful. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**Single track section near the end:** [ELITE] Evan noted "The race fell apart for me on a bit of single track near the end" where he got his sidewall slash. This indicates there's technical single track included in the course, not just gravel roads. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**Technical descending:** [ELITE] Course includes descents technical enough that they were "a liability" for an experienced rider before skills training, and improving descending allowed him to be "ripping along" with the front group. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Early pacing caution:** Evan McGregor: "Since it was my first race of the year, I had a lot of energy and excitement to let out, and I took that out on the pedals for the first hour of the race... I was with the front of the race, until I realized I overspent and was unable to follow the front 5. I dialed things back and paced my effort." This suggests the front group stays conservative enough in the first hour that it's possible to overextend yourself and then pay for it. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical failure in final section:** [ELITE] Sidewall slashes can happen on the single track section near the end of the course. Evan's tire could not be plugged and ended his competitive ride despite being "still in the top of the field" and "so close to the finish." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**Cramping risk:** [ELITE] Even fit riders can cramp on Big Pond Hollow - Evan lost "a few minutes off the bike" to cramping. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

## Course Logistics

**Three distance options:** Full Series (64.4 miles, ~7,211 feet elevation), Half Series (45.2 miles, ~5,241 feet elevation), and Short Series (23.8 miles, ~2,718 feet elevation). (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/cmgg)

**Cutoff times enforced:** Full and Half courses have aid station cutoffs: Aid Station #1 at 11:30 AM, Aid Station #2 at 1:00 PM, Aid Station #3 (full only) at 3:00 PM, with 9:00 AM race start. Short course has no cutoff. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/cmgg)

**Aid station placement:** Half course has aid stations at miles 18.5 and 32. Full course adds one at mile 51.5. Short course has one at mile 18.5. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/cmgg)

**2024 Half Course results:** Winning time was 3:09:45 (Sam Becker). Times ranged from 3:09:45 to 5:54:19 across 57 finishers, with the last finisher at 5:54:19 (Paul Spencer). (https://www.gravel-results.com/race/21364)
