## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Pacing mistake - going too hard early:** Evan McGregor [ELITE], coming off a 2-month injury layoff, noted: "Since it was my first race of the year, I had a lot of energy and excitement to let out, and I took that out on the pedals for the first hour of the race... I was with the front of the race, until I realized I overspent and was unable to follow the front 5. I dialed things back and paced my effort." This led to cramping later at Big Pond Hollow. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Late-race tactics:** Lynn Botelho [ELITE] describes being caught with 3 miles to go and having another rider "attack" on a gentle incline with 2 miles remaining. She "tried, but I had no more to give and she pulled away," suggesting the final 2-3 miles on road present an opportunity for stronger riders to distance competitors. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/lynns-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Short prep recovery protocol:** Lynn Botelho [ELITE] successfully raced with only 2 weeks of focused prep after being "sick in bed for a week" and having "a 16 days holiday in the UK" by focusing on: "Good food; no drink. Mini training camp over a long weekend. Good sleep. Solid work outs. Good rest." She finished 2nd in her age group within 2 minutes of 1st. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/lynns-story-camp-michaux-gravel-grinder)
