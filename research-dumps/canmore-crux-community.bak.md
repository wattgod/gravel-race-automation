# CANMORE CRUX — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Format & Structure Evolution

**Major Format Change for 2026:**
The race is transitioning from a traditional distance-based format to a **4-hour AMLAP (As Many Laps As Possible)** format on a new 16 KM loop (https://www.canmorecrux.com/).

**Previous Format (2024-2025):**
- Two distance options: 80 km and 32 km courses
- 5-lap format on double-track trails
- 1,800m elevation gain over 83km (2024 data)
- Mass start at 9:00 AM
- Approximately 200-250 participants
(https://cms.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/canmore-nordic-centre-pp/events/events/canmore-crux-2025/, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7CXE4vtE0RY)

**2026 New Format Details:**
- 16 KM loop blending "smooth pavement with crunchy gravel"
- Solo or Duo team options (partners can split laps)
- Solo riders can "throw in the towel" at any point with "no judgment, just high-fives at the finish"
- Described as "smoother and more approachable than ever" with "faster" terrain, though "the 4-hour clock is relentless"
- Registration opens February 1, 2026, closes June 3, 2026 or at sellout
- Limited to 250 participants
(https://www.canmorecrux.com/, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10497/canmore-crux)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**2024-2025 Course (being replaced):**
Used double-track trails at Canmore Nordic Centre including:
- Olympic
- Meadowview
- Silvertip
- Banff
- Bow
- Grey Wolf Lower
- Banff Loop
- Coyote
(https://cms.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/canmore-nordic-centre-pp/events/events/canmore-crux-2025/)

**2026 Course:**
- 16 KM loop with mixed pavement and gravel
- "Some solid climbing along the way to keep the lungs honest"
- Smoother surface allows riders to "really enjoy the descents"
- Course map available at event website
(https://www.canmorecrux.com/course)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Type Flexibility [ALL LEVELS]:**
The organizers explicitly welcome non-traditional gravel setups: "No gravel bike? No sweat. We love mountain bikes, 'Franken-bikes,' and whatever rig makes you feel confident on the climbs and gravel. Bring the bike you trust and a thirst for adventure." (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)

*Note: This inclusive approach suggests the course is rideable on various bike types, likely indicating relatively moderate technical demands.*

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event emphasizes **inclusivity and personal challenge** over pure competition:

- "Out here, the struggle is our shared language, and everyone is invited to the conversation" (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)

- The new format explicitly removes pressure: "the challenge is exactly as big as you make it - throw in the towel after your first lap if that is all you have in the tank" (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)

- "No judgment, just high-fives at the finish" for riders who stop early (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)

- Positioned as a **"season opener"** and **"wake-up call"** event (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)

- Described as "the most stunningly brutal and beautiful day you'll have on two wheels all year" (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)

**Venue:**
Located at the "legendary Canmore Nordic Centre" (1988 Olympic Way, Canmore, AB, T1W 2T6), described as "the heart of the Rockies and our favorite proving ground" with "Epic Views" throughout (https://www.canmorecrux.com/, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10497/canmore-crux)

**Post-Race:**
Event wraps up around 2:30 PM (based on 2025 data). Includes finisher tee, swag, bike number plates, post-event meal, changing rooms, toilets, and results within 24 hours (https://cms.albertaparks.ca/parks/kananaskis/canmore-nordic-centre-pp/events/events/canmore-crux-2025/, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10497/canmore-crux)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Format-Specific Strategy [ALL LEVELS - 2026 FORMAT]:**
The 4-hour AMLAP format creates unique strategic considerations:

- The "4-hour clock is relentless" despite "easier" and "faster" terrain (https://www.canmorecrux.com/)
- Duo teams can strategically split laps, potentially allowing for harder individual efforts with recovery between turns
- Solo riders have full flexibility to end their race at any lap completion without penalty
- The smoother, faster course may encourage riders to push harder pace than sustainable for 4 hours

*Note: No specific pacing data or nutrition strategies were found in sources. The AMLAP format is relatively uncommon in gravel racing, so traditional distance-based pacing may not directly apply.*
