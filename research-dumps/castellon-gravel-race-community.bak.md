# CASTELLON GRAVEL RACE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Jorge Padrones [COMPETITIVE]:** "The race was not a walk in the park. Castellon is the second most mountainous region in Spain, something you don't realise until you arrive in Llucena, the starting point, and see big mountains around you as the only exit from the village. It means we knew we will have to climb during the race - more than 2600 meters of elevation to be exact in 97 km, but a hard test is the mark of a World Cup race like this." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

**Jorge Padrones [COMPETITIVE]:** "This was not a very technically demanding race, if we can say that descending a gravel path at 65 km/h with some sharp corners is not technical! What I mean is that there are no singletrack or rocky sections to pass. It was a fast course, with a good surface in which the first flew at more than 25 km/h average." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

**Jorge Padrones [COMPETITIVE]:** "The organisation had prepared a nice party with food and drinks for all the participants while we could see the prize ceremony and enjoy it with other fellow riders chatting about how our day in the saddle was. I want to give a big shout out for the organisation of this Castellon Gravel World race as it was flawlessly organised making it a real party in all senses. It has all the ingredients to be considered as a reference gravel event in Europe - good weather, good course and good organisation." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Edition:** "The weather was nice the day of the race, bit chilly in the morning but with temperatures around the 15 degrees Celsius along the race itself." - Jorge Padrones [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:** 72% gravel roads with 28% paved roads, mainly located in the first 7km and a longer section just after halfway through the course (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/castellon/)

**Elevation Profile:** 2,500-2,600 meters of elevation gain over 97km, with the shorter 85km course totaling 2,100m+ (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon and https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/castellon/)

**Specific Climbs from Official Profile:**
- Start: 1.2km at 4.9% average to leave town
- 9.8km mark: 6.3km at 5.4% (longest sustained climb)
- 24.9km mark: 9.4km at 3.8% 
- Final climb: 1km at 9.9% average, with the summit only 300m before the finish line (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/castellon/)

**Key Course Features [COMPETITIVE perspective]:** "The parcours included fast descents, with a good surface and big climbs... descending a gravel path at 65 km/h with some sharp corners" - Jorge Padrones noted speeds reaching 65 km/h on descents (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

**Final Section:** The course features a "final 6km descent in Llucena and a stiff uphill finish into Llucena" - the last steep climb in town towards the finish is 1km at 10% average with its top at only 300m before the finish (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/alejandro-valverde-and-carolin-schiff-ride-to-solo-elite-titles-at-castellon-gravel-race/ and https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/castellon/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Men's Strategy [ELITE]:** Alejandro Valverde finished the 97km route in 3:27:53 (averaging approximately 28 km/h). He rode with a lead group through the mid-point holding a 10-second margin, then "used a final acceleration on the uphill finish to distance Ferreira and ride solo across the finish with 12 seconds in hand" (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/alejandro-valverde-and-carolin-schiff-ride-to-solo-elite-titles-at-castellon-gravel-race/)

**Elite Women's Strategy [ELITE]:** Carolin Schiff rode solo for the entire second half of the race, finishing in 4:20:08 with a four-minute margin. "Schiff carried a four-minute margin over the Spanish duo... as she sped away on the second half of the course" (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/alejandro-valverde-and-carolin-schiff-ride-to-solo-elite-titles-at-castellon-gravel-race/)

**Key Race Selection Point:** The final climbing section is where the race was decided in both elite categories. "Once on the final climbing section, Valverde and Ferreira continued with Fontana now in the lead trio. Valverde then used a final acceleration on the uphill finish" (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/alejandro-valverde-and-carolin-schiff-ride-to-solo-elite-titles-at-castellon-gravel-race/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Scale:** The race sold out at 800 participants, with approximately 250 people traveling from abroad, making it "the most internationally popular gravel event so far in Spain" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

**Post-Race Experience:** "The organisation had prepared a nice party with food and drinks for all the participants while we could see the prize ceremony and enjoy it with other fellow riders chatting about how our day in the saddle was" - Jorge Padrones [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)

**UCI Worlds Qualification:** A major draw is qualification for the Gravel World Championships - "Participants who wish to race at the World Championships need to finish in the top 25% of each category of this UCI Gravel World Series event to qualify... riders finishing in the top 25% of their UCI age/gender category automatically qualify" with participants "taking home the appreciated golden UCI medal that is only given to the ones who qualified" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon and https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/02/16/2025-uci-gravel-worlds-qualifying-old-man-valverde-wins-at-castellon-again/)

**Elite Field Quality [2025]:** Women's race included Carolin Schiff, Marta Romeu, Maddy Nutt, and defending champion Sofía Rodríguez. Men's field included 44-year-old Alejandro Valverde (now Spain's national cycling coach), Tiago Ferreira, Luis Angel Mate, Jan Bakelants, and 41-year-old UCI Gran Fondo World Champion Johnny Hoogerland (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon and https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/02/16/2025-uci-gravel-worlds-qualifying-old-man-valverde-wins-at-castellon-again/)

**Location Appeal:** "Many of the participants came encouraged... attracted by the mild weather we are used to having in the Mediterranean area at this time of the year" - Jorge Padrones [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-uci-gravel-world-series-castellon)
