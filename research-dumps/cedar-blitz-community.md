## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Surface [UNKNOWN]:** "Fast, no chunky gravel" - noted by multiple riders as a positive characteristic distinguishing it from rougher gravel races (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/beths-story-cedar-blitz)

**Elevation Profile [UNKNOWN]:** "No giant hills" - described as relatively flat compared to other gravel races (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/beths-story-cedar-blitz)

**Wind Exposure [ELITE]:** Starting around mile 26, riders encounter significant headwinds that "seemed to be everywhere you turned" through the farm country sections. This appears to be a critical point where riders going solo face difficulty. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz)

**Scenery [ELITE]:** "Beautiful farm country" and "scenic backroads, forests, and farmland around Cedar Springs, Michigan" with miles of open farmland (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz, https://dragonadventuresbasecamp.com/2025/07/18/cedar-blitz-gravel-race-2025-highlights-and-interviews/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Pacing [ELITE]:** Tracy Gravlin noted "The start was fast and felt great" - elite riders should expect a quick opening pace (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz)

**Mid-Race Challenge [ELITE]:** Mile 26 appears to be a critical juncture where headwinds begin and riders on their own face increased difficulty. Staying with groups through this section may be advantageous. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz)

**Warmup Caution [UNKNOWN]:** Beth Huffaker humorously noted being "worn out on our warmup" before the race start - riders should be strategic about pre-race efforts (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/beths-story-cedar-blitz)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-Race Celebration:** "The post-race hangout, and great local food made the finish line feel like a festival" with Cedar Springs Brewery providing craft beer for finishers. The race is described as having "big smiles" and "post-race high-fives" (https://dragonadventuresbasecamp.com/2025/07/18/cedar-blitz-gravel-race-2025-highlights-and-interviews/, https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz)

**Community Vibe [ELITE]:** "This race isn't just about watts and finish times‚Äîit's about sharing the day with other riders who love gravel as much as we do. The post-race hangout, and great local food made the finish line feel like a festival, and that's the kind of energy we love to see in cycling!" (https://dragonadventuresbasecamp.com/2025/07/18/cedar-blitz-gravel-race-2025-highlights-and-interviews/)

**Race Organization:** Described as "well-organized, high-energy gravel race" and "well supported race" (https://dragonadventuresbasecamp.com/2025/07/18/cedar-blitz-gravel-race-2025-highlights-and-interviews/, https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz)

**Growth:** 2024 brought "500+ riders" indicating the event is expanding (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oq5ch_rV4cA)

**Regional Significance:** "The Cedar Blitz has quickly become a must-do event in the regional gravel scene" and is "One of the fastest growing gravel races in the region" (https://dragonadventuresbasecamp.com/2025/07/18/cedar-blitz-gravel-race-2025-highlights-and-interviews/, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rgtWKhZV2P4)

**Inclusivity:** Multiple distance options "ranging from beginner-friendly routes to leg-burning challenges for experienced racers, there's something for everyone" with routes from 18 miles to 128 miles (https://dragonadventuresbasecamp.com/2025/07/18/cedar-blitz-gravel-race-2025-highlights-and-interviews/)

**BaseCamp Community [ELITE/UNKNOWN]:** "The BaseCamp community has given me so many cycling friends from across the country. Beth came up from Kansas!" - riders travel from multiple states to participate (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/tracys-story-cedar-blitz)

**Venue:** Start and finish in downtown Cedar Springs with easy access to celebration at Cedar Springs Brewery (https://ilovegravelracing.com/event/cedar-blitz/)
