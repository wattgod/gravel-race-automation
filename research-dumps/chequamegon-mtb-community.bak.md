# CHEQUAMEGON MTB — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Jenna Rinehart [ELITE]:** "So many mixed emotions about my 11th Chequamegon race. This years race format was the first time they started the women's field separately from the men. This really changed the dynamics of the women's race. It was much harder to break away. I put in a bit of an effort in Rosies Field and Melissa Rollins was the only one to come with me. We worked together for the first half of the race to build up a gap. 30 miles in we hit Fire Tower climb and I rode at a firm effort knowing that while Fire Tower is hard the race gets even harder when you hit the Birkie trail after that. Once on the Birkie trail we both started to put the pressure on each other up the steep grassy climbs but neither of us could manage to break away. I got a couple small gaps on the atv trail after that but Melisa managed to come back to my wheel when we hit the gravel road. I sat up on the last gravel road knowing it was going to come down to the last few miles of ski trail. With 1.5 miles to go my front tire slipped out on a rut and I found myself on the ground watching Melisa ride away. I got back up as quickly as possible but was feeling discouraged that I had just made a major mistake. I started to pedal a bit more and rounded a corner and realized I could still see Melisa up ahead. I gave it everything I had up the last 20 second climb before we rounded the corner to a big downhill finish. Unfortunately it wasn't quite enough time left and I ended up finishing 1 second back." (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Melisa Rollins [ELITE]:** "We were a pretty big group coming through the first feed zone and I tried to stay patient. The race blew up at mile 25 on the Firetower climb and I was feeling really good. I got a little gap over the top of it but wasn't able to hold it. I got caught by three riders and then went again shortly after and knew that I was holding my momentum really well, and managed to hold them off until the finish." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/chequamegon-mtb-festival-2025/ltgp-4-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**Alexey Vermeulen [ELITE]:** "I started trying to make my moves with around seven miles to go. I could see the group was splintering, and guys were chasing to regain contact, and I knew the rubber band would eventually snap and gaps would start to open up so I just bided my time. I could tell that anybody who got on my wheel wasn't willing to work and it was still quite a long way to the finish. I didn't think anybody could go solo initially, but in the end I just trusted myself, saw the gap happen, and just committed." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/chequamegon-mtb-festival-2025/ltgp-4-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**Kyan Olshove [ELITE]:** "It's taken a lot of discipline and consistency in training. Lots of fun days but a lot of sacrifice too. It feels really good to have everything come together on a day like this to achieve the best result of my career so far. I'll be competing in two more Life Time GP events this fall as well as Iceman. I'm hoping to keep the momentum going and end the season on a high note." (https://www.gomuskox.com/blogs/news/kyan-olshove-chequamegon)

**Mike Hall [COMPETITIVE] (support crew for Jeff Hall):** "Jeff flatted about 25-26 miles in. Sharp rock slashed a gaping hole in sidewall. Pack just started ramping it up and Jeff was at the front with a couple other riders. A large pack the first half of the race. Took a while for his legs to get going but he was feeling pretty good after the first hills just past OO. I got the news after the Fire Tower. That's where I was going to hand up his final bottle. Bummer to flat but that racing!" (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Jay Richards [COMPETITIVE]:** "As far as fall riding goes, gosh darn the month of September is going down as one of the best ever that I can remember. Temps have been running a bit warmer, the fall colors have exploded in the northern locales, and the dirt has been pretty much as good as it gets with just enough moisture to keep things fresh and magical." (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

The question remains unanswered in the thread, but the context suggests recreational participation ("not competitively"). **No Aero Bars Allowed:** Official rules state "No aero bars allowed. We understand that aero bars have been seen at this event in the past, but our official stance is that the use of them is prohibited. ANY PARTICIPANTS CAUGHT USING AERO BARS WILL NOT BE ALLOWED TO START THE RACE." (https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

**Bike Categories:** The race allows standard mountain bikes, single speed, fat bikes, tandems, and e-bikes (pedal assist only, open category with no awards). (https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition [from multiple sources]:** The Chequamegon 40 is notably NOT a traditional mountain bike race - it contains NO technical singletrack whatsoever. The course consists of:
- Much of the route follows the Birkebeiner ski trail with "constant punchy rollers following fast flowing and wide grassy cuts through the forest"
- Several gravel and double track sections
- Forest roads and snowmobile routes
- "Ever-present are the epic Seeley Fire Tower climbs and leg burning rollers near the end" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/lutsen-99er-vs-chequamegon-40-which-life-time-event-is-the-better-northwoods-challenge and https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Elevation Profile:** 61.4 feet of climbing per mile over 40 miles (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/lutsen-99er-vs-chequamegon-40-which-life-time-event-is-the-better-northwoods-challenge)

**Race-Defining Sections [ELITE perspective]:**
- **Rosie's Field (Mile 2):** Early in the race where breakaway attempts begin; "irregular, rough-mowed field" (https://www.cheqmtb.com/pro-riders/)
- **Fire Tower Climb (Mile 25/30):** Multiple sources identify this as where the race "blows up." Jenna Rinehart noted: "I rode at a firm effort knowing that while Fire Tower is hard the race gets even harder when you hit the Birkie trail after that" (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)
- **Last 1.5 Miles:** Technical enough that Rinehart's front tire "slipped out on a rut" causing a crash (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)
- **Final Miles:** Includes "last 20 second climb before we rounded the corner to a big downhill finish" (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**2025 Course Change:** "The final two miles were changed this year for the pro field, following the American Birkebeiner Ski Trail instead of the Sleigh Trail section used by mass start participants, which allowed for additional space with passing on the ramp to the finish." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/chequamegon-mtb-festival-2025/ltgp-4-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**Aid Station Locations:**
- Gravel Pit Aid Station: Mile 12
- OO Aid Station: Mile 18
- High Point Aid Station: Mile 30
(https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Separate Women's Start Impact [ELITE]:** Jenna Rinehart noted that 2021 was "the first time they started the women's field separately from the men. This really changed the dynamics of the women's race. It was much harder to break away." Previously, elite women could work with the larger men's field to create separation. (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Pacing Through Fire Tower [ELITE]:** Rinehart's strategy: "I rode at a firm effort knowing that while Fire Tower is hard the race gets even harder when you hit the Birkie trail after that." This suggests restraint on the major climb knowing harder efforts come later. (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Late Race Tactics [ELITE]:** Vermeulen's winning move came "with around seven miles to go" when he recognized "the group was splintering, and guys were chasing to regain contact, and I knew the rubber band would eventually snap." He noted riders on his wheel "weren't willing to work" so he committed to a solo effort. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/chequamegon-mtb-festival-2025/ltgp-4-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**First Half Group Dynamics [ELITE]:** Multiple sources note large packs staying together through the first half. Mike Hall described "A large pack the first half of the race" before things separated. (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Race Characterization:** The Chequamegon 40 is described as "The Drag Race from Hayward to Cable" and known for "fast-paced intensity" being "more of a sprint for many riders" compared to longer endurance events. With no technical singletrack, it becomes "one long all-out effort for the entire 40 miles." (https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/ and https://www.thenxrth.com/post/lutsen-99er-vs-chequamegon-40-which-life-time-event-is-the-better-northwoods-challenge)

**Start Line Strategy:** The race uses a 6-gate system with placement based on previous Chequamegon results from 2019-2025. "Participants must hold their position for the entirety of the roll-out. Passing or weaving through other participants will result in disqualification without warning." There's also a police escort to Rosie's Field at approximately 20mph. (https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

**Pro Start Times:** Pro Women start at 12:30pm, Pro Men at 1:00pm, with Age Class riders starting at 10:00am, meaning elite riders will encounter age-group riders later in the race. (https://www.cheqmtb.com/pro-riders/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues [ELITE]:** Jeff Hall, one of the race favorites, "flatted about 25-26 miles in. Sharp rock slashed a gaping hole in sidewall" right as "Pack just started ramping it up and Jeff was at the front." The timing and location (around Fire Tower area where racing intensifies) made this particularly costly. (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Crashes [ELITE]:** Jenna Rinehart crashed with 1.5 miles to go when her "front tire slipped out on a rut," ultimately costing her the win by 1 second. This indicates technical sections in the final miles despite the generally non-technical course. (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Cut-Off Times:** Official cut-off times exist at multiple checkpoints:
- 11:45 AM at Aid Station 1 (Mile 12)
- 12:33 PM at Aid Station 2 (Mile 18)  
- 2:15 PM at Aid Station 3 (Mile 30)
- 4:00 PM Course Cutoff (all riders must be complete)
"If you do not make the cut-off time, your number will be pulled and you can choose to be transported via sag vehicle to the finish or ride a short-cut route (after you sign a waiver.) Additionally, any finish time after 6 hours (4:00PM) will be listed as a DNF." (https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

**No Outside Assistance:** "You may not accept assistance outside of aid stations or approved spectator areas. DNFs must be reported to timing." (https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Festival Atmosphere:** "Chequamegon is deeply rooted in community tradition and delivers a full-on celebration of mountain biking. Hayward and Cable rally around the event with cheering crowds and strong volunteer presence. With the race starting at 10am, riders come in hot in a short time frame and the party escalates quickly. The festival atmosphere is vibrant, with music, an expo, and vendors all adding to the experience." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/lutsen-99er-vs-chequamegon-40-which-life-time-event-is-the-better-northwoods-challenge)

**Family-Friendly Weekend:** "Gather your friends and family for a weekend of epic racing through the Northwoods of Wisconsin, accompanied by a festival atmosphere, live music, craft beer, and savory food truck eats." The event includes races for all levels including "Little Loggers" for kids. (https://www.cheqmtb.com/)

**Post-Race Scene:** The race director notes "THANK THE VOLUNTEERS!" in the official rules. The finish line is described as "packed with the rest of the Cheq riders, cheering you on and waiting anxiously to see the winners — creating an awesome finishing experience for the Pros." (https://www.cheqmtb.com/pro-riders/ and https://www.cheqmtb.com/chequamegon-40/)

**CAMBA Support:** In 2021, "One amazing aspect of the day was the announcement by the organizers of Chequamegon MTB Festival of a $20,000 donation to CAMBA" (Chequamegon Area Mountain Bike Association). (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848)

**Fall Colors:** Multiple sources emphasize the timing in September provides "fall colors" and the "Northwoods Wisconsin" setting creates "a classic Midwest biking atmosphere." Jay Richards noted the 2021 event had "fall colors have exploded in the northern locales." (https://www.skinnyski.com/racing/display.asp?Id=50848 and https://www.thenxrth.com/post/lutsen-99er-vs-chequamegon-40-which-life-time-event-is-the-better-northwoods-challenge)

**Historical Significance:** "Chequamegon is one of the oldest mountain bike races anywhere in the United States. It began during the early days of mountain biking, long before the sport reached its current popularity and was dreamed up by Telemark resort employees as an effort to create a post-season tourism draw. The first year had 27 races and the event quickly grew into a beloved tradition." Now in its 42nd edition. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/lutsen-99er-vs-chequamegon-40-which-life-time-event-is-the-better-northwoods-challenge and https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/chequamegon-mtb-festival-2025/ltgp-4-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**Life Time Grand Prix Integration:** As the 4th or 5th stop (sources vary by year) in the Life Time Grand Prix series, the event attracts top national and international talent, with "Defending champion Keegan Swenson (Santa Cruz), wearing his new rainbow stripes as UCI marathon mountain bike world champion" among participants. Prize money for Pro division: 1st place $2,500, 2nd $1,000, 3rd $750, 4th $500, 5th $250. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/chequamegon-mtb-festival-2025/ltgp-4-elite-women-and-men/results/ and https://www.cheqmtb.com/pro-riders/)

**Spectator Experience:** Multiple approved spectator locations are spread throughout the course with specific access instructions, including Rosie's Field (mile 2), "OO" and Birkie Trail (mile 15.92), and several others. "A great place to watch as the early drama of the lead riders develops." (https://www.cheqmtb.com/pro-riders/)
