## OFFICIAL DATA

### Event Overview
- **Full Name:** Chequamegon MTB Festival (formerly Chequamegon Fat Tire Festival)
- **Location:** Hayward to Cable, Wisconsin (Chequamegon-Nicolet National Forest)
- **2025 Date:** September 13, 2025 (41st Edition)
- **2026 Date:** September 19, 2026 (42nd Edition)
- **Current Status:** Part of the Life Time Grand Prix Series

### Race Distances

#### Chequamegon 40 (Main Event)
- **Distance:** 40 miles (Elite route: 38.3 miles)
- **Participant Limit:** 2,100 riders
- **Entry Fee:** $120
- **Start:** Downtown Hayward, WI (American Birkebeiner Trailhead)
- **Finish:** Derksen Family Great Hall / Telemark Resort in Cable, WI
- **Course Type:** Point-to-point
- **Cut-off Time:** 6 hours (4:00 PM finish = DNF)

#### Short & Fat
- **Distance:** 16 miles
- **Participant Limit:** 1,000 riders
- **Course Type:** Loop starting and ending in Cable
- **Description:** "All the same challenges of the 40 jam packed into a bite sized version"

#### Little Loggers
- **Distance:** Kids event
- **Entry Fee:** FREE

### Total Registration
- **Combined Limit:** 3,100 participants
- **Registration Method:** Lottery system (see below)

### Registration & Lottery System

**How It Works:**
- Against strong long-time participant opposition, a registration random lottery was established in 1999
- Entry submission deadline: March 15 (postmark date)
- Known as "March Madness" in the mountain bike community
- Life Time Club-Access Members (6+ months membership) get priority entry or guaranteed lottery registration
- Can register up until September 1st, but best odds require registering 6+ months in advance

**Deferral Policy:**
- Can defer entry to following year for guaranteed spot
- Must pay 50% of entry fee for deferral
- Original entry fees are non-refundable
- Deferrals only valid for following year, same event

**Military/Medical Exemptions:**
- Active duty military deployed or recalled: full refund or free deferral with proof of orders
- Pregnant/postpartum mothers: full refund or free deferral with medical note

## RACE HISTORY

### Founding (1983)
- **First Year:** 1983
- **Original Participants:** 27 cyclists
- **Founders:** Telemark Resort employees Tom Kelly, Dan Hunt, Mike Cooper, Phil VanValkenberg and Phil Rasmussen of Lakewoods Resort
- **Original Concept:** Bicycle race on the American Birkebeiner ski trail to extend tourism season beyond summer
- **Original Course:** Point-to-point 40 miles from Main Street Hayward to Lakewoods Resort in Cable

### Gary Crandall "The Fatman"
- Despite popular belief, did NOT dream up the event concept
- Raced in the first year and finished 12th (out of 27)
- Became long-time event director

### Early Recognition
- NORBA (National Off Road Bicycle Association) sanctioned Chequamegon 40 as first Midwest Off Road Championship within a couple years of founding
- This official recognition fueled rapid growth

### The Greg LeMond Era (1990-1991)
- **1990:** Three-time Tour de France champion Greg LeMond entered and WON his first-ever mountain bike race
- Race was rain-shortened to 35 miles
- LeMond also caught a world-record bass in Lake Owen that weekend
- **1991:** LeMond returned to defend title in another weather-shortened 35-miler
- Era became known as "LeFat, LeMud, LeMond"
- LeMond's participation brought massive media attention

### Notable Participants (Historical)
Mountain Bike Hall of Fame Inductees who have raced Chequamegon:
- Joe Breeze
- Ned Overend
- Steve Tilford
- David Wiens
- Greg LeMond (3x Tour de France champion)

### Growth Timeline
- **1990:** Rider limit set at 2,250
- **1991:** Limit raised to 2,500 (1,700 for 40-miler, 800 for Short & Fat)
- **1999:** Lottery system implemented due to overwhelming demand
- **Current:** 3,100 total participants

### Sustainability Efforts
- Self-imposed limits set early to preserve forest resources
- Organizers recognized that uncontrolled growth could damage trails in the fickle September weather

### Course Records
- **Men's Record (original):** Doug Swanson (Trek/VW) - 2:07:11 in 2003
- **Men's Record (current):** Brian Matter - 2:02
- **Women's Record:** Lea Davison (Olympian) set new course record

### Recent Winners

**2025:**

- Women: Melisa Rollins (Liv Racing Collective) - 4:32 winning margin, repeat of 2021 success

**2024:**

## TERRAIN & COURSE

### Course Overview (Chequamegon 40)
The race traverses multiple terrain types through the Northwoods:

**Terrain Mix:**
- American Birkebeiner Ski Trail (grassy, wide paths with rolling hills)
- Forest roads
- Snowmobile trails
- Fire roads
- ATV trails
- Some smooth singletrack
- Wooded paths in Sawyer and Bayfield counties

### Terrain Breakdown (Approximate)
- **Birkie Trail:** Wide (30-foot) grassy ski trail with rolling hills - majority of course
- **Fire Roads:** Fast gravel sections
- **Doubletrack:** Forest service roads
- **Singletrack:** Limited, smooth sections - NOT technical switchbacks
- **Gravel Roads:** Fast pavement-to-gravel transitions

### Elevation Profile

**Total Climbing:** 2,500-3,000+ feet (varies by measurement method and course modifications)

**Key Climbs:**
1. **Seeley Fire Tower Climb** (Mile ~28.5, 11.5 miles from finish)
   - The pivotal point of the race
   - Steep and technical requiring good traction
   - Usually clogged with people walking if not in first 100 riders
   - "Knockout punch" - elevation gained then lost

2. **High Point on Birkie Trail** (7 miles from finish)
   - "Yet another knockout punch"
   - Climb back after losing Fire Tower elevation

**Course Character:**
- "Long and fast" - leaders average nearly 20 MPH
- "Never ending profile fluctuations wear down even the fittest participants"
- "Roller coaster ride" with constant 25-50 foot climbs
- Characterized as a "POWER course" - favors fittest riders, not most technical
- Linear nature benefits strong cyclists over technical specialists

### Surface Conditions

**When Dry:**
- Deep sandy stretches
- Many embedded rocks with sharp edges
- Some loose climbs (especially Fire Tower)
- Fast rolling sections

**When Wet:**
- "All about survival"
- Mud holes throughout course
- Erosion creates additional challenges
- Historical races shortened due to weather (1990, 1991)

### Short & Fat Course (16 miles)
- Starts in Cable, heads south on Randysek Road
- Uses logging roads surrounding Telemark Resort
- Returns to Cable using American Birkebeiner Ski Trail and backwoods paths
- Same terrain challenges as 40, compressed distance

### Course Marking
- Course usually marked by Labor Day weekend
- Some sections cross private land (easement only on race day)
- Shared motorized trail sections - caution advised when pre-riding

## FORUM INSIGHTS

### Race Experience Quotes

**On the Festival Atmosphere:**
> "It is the only race where I've had bratwurst and beer at the feed stations."

> "It's something that you should do at least once. Everyone I met or rode with seemed very nice; it's an event that really makes you feel like you're part of a family."

**On the Course:**
> "If you're looking for legit singletrack there won't be much in this race... If you enjoy gravels, distance, and rollers you will find a happy place."

> "More about the festival atmosphere of it all and the rollout of 1700 bikes in a mass start."

**On the Lottery:**
> "Over a thousand riders were turned away as the Chequamegon Festival continues to hold its total registration to 2500 riders."

> "Hopeful participants learned to check their local banks to see if their checks had been cashed, a sure sign of acceptance into the event."

### Bottleneck Issues

**Start & Early Miles:**
> "When the racers made a left onto the trail system, the pace damn near stopped, as 2 paved lanes of bicycle traffic made a left onto the narrow ski trails climb."

**Fire Tower Climb:**
> "The one difficult climb at Firetower is usually so clogged with people walking that if you aren't amongst the first 100 riders through, you'll be walking too."

> "At the Fire Tower climb, it was a little congested with a line on the right that was walking and a line on the left that was climbing."

**Mud Sections:**
> "Around mile 5, a major bottleneck happens around a big mud hole."

> "I ripped off my leg and arm warmers here, since I had to get off the bike and do some standing and walking through the bottleneck anyways."

### Bike Setup Discussions

**Hardtail vs Gravel:**
- Peter Stetina (pro) finished 7th on Canyon Grizl gravel bike
- Course favors "Fitness and Speed race, rather than technical and skills race"
- Rigid bikes hard on hands and lower back after 3+ hours

**Tire Recommendations:**
- When dry: Low rolling resistance with decent float for sand
- Popular choices: Racing Ralphs, Kenda SB8s, IRC Mythos 2.1"
- Crows front and rear = fast but risky on sweeping corners
- 50-52mm clearance works well on gravel setups

**Gearing:**
- Single-speed: 34x19 or 34x18 viable
- Gravel: 46-36 chainrings + 11-40 cassette
- "Get a frame as light as you can afford (to make pushing the bike up firetower hill easier)"

### Training Recommendations

> "End of season form from racing road & TTs all year along with the build up for CX. A mixture of longer 2x20 minute intervals on the road with some 3-4 minute climbing VO2 type intervals each done once a week."

## BLACK PILL

### The Brutal Truths

**1. You Probably Won't Get In**
- Lottery system means hundreds/thousands rejected every year
- "Over a thousand riders were turned away"
- Must register by March for September race
- Life Time membership gives priority but isn't guaranteed

**2. Unless You're Elite, You'll Walk Fire Tower**
- "The one difficult climb at Firetower is usually so clogged with people walking that if you aren't amongst the first 100 riders through, you'll be walking too."
- Not a fitness issue - pure logistics of 2,000+ riders on narrow trail

**3. First Half Can Be a Parking Lot**
- Massive field (2,100+) funneling from paved start to narrow trails
- "The pace damn near stopped"
- Unless you're in first few gates, expect crawling speeds

**4. Not "Real" Mountain Biking**
- "If you're looking for legit singletrack there won't be much"
- Mostly wide grassy ski trails and fire roads
- Technical riders may be disappointed
- "Characterized as a power course, the linear nature favors the fittest and not necessarily the most technically accomplished rider"

**5. Lodging is a Nightmare**
- Small town infrastructure overwhelmed by 3,100+ racers plus support crews
- Book immediately upon lottery acceptance (6+ months out)
- Some lodging books up before lottery even happens

**6. Weather Russian Roulette**
- "Fickle weather month of September"
- Can go from "scorching predictions" to mud overnight
- Historical races shortened due to conditions
- Train for mud regardless

**7. Your Body Will Hurt**
- "Upper body takes a beating"
- Rigid bikes brutal after 3+ hours
- "Never ending profile fluctuations wear down even the fittest"
- Hands, lower back, legs all suffer

**8. You're Racing a 40+ Year Old Course**
- Not modern flow trails or purpose-built MTB course
- Nordic ski trail adapted for bikes
- "Rolling hills through the woods" - functional, not exciting

**9. Cut-off Times Are Real**
- 6-hour limit enforced
- First aid station cutoff: 11:45 AM (Mile 12)
- Miss it = pulled number, sag wagon, DNF

**10. It's a "Festival" as Much as a Race**
- If you want pure competition, might be disappointed
- Heavy emphasis on atmosphere, beer, bratwurst
- "More about the festival atmosphere of it all"

### Who Should Skip This Race:
- Technical singletrack purists
- Anyone who can't commit to March registration
- Riders who hate mass-start chaos
- People without flexible lodging options
- Anyone expecting a modern purpose-built MTB course

- 12+ campgrounds within an hour

**Critical Lodging Note:**
- Book IMMEDIATELY upon lottery acceptance
- Most lodging fills months in advance
- Official website offers free Hotel Planning tool

### Race Day Logistics

**Transportation:**
- Bus transport from Great Hall parking lot to Hayward start line
- Small fee required
- Must register in advance for bus

**Parking Options:**
- Great Hall parking lot (Cable)
- City lot on Highway 27/63 (Hayward)
- Hayward Primary School
- People's National Bank
- Hayward Wesleyan Church

**Festival Location:**
- All festivities at finish line in Cable
- Derksen Family Great Hall

### Aid Stations

**Products Available:**
- Neversecond Sports Drink (Citrus/Unflavored) - 60g CHO per 750ml
- Neversecond C30 Energy Gels (Citrus, Fruit Punch, Passion Fruit, Berry, Orange)
- C30 Fuel Bars (Chocolate & Berry)
- Water

**Aid Station Rules:**
- May NOT accept assistance outside aid stations
- Team/personal support allowed at official aid stations only
- Leave no trace - no garbage on course

**Cut-off Times:**
- Gravel Pit (Mile 12): 11:45 AM
- Final finish: 4:00 PM (6 hours)

### Local Resources

**CAMBA (Chequamegon Area Mountain Bike Association):**
- 135+ miles of singletrack
- 70 miles of groomed winter trails
- 200+ miles of mapped gravel routes
- Day use pass: $5
- Annual pass: $30

**Trail Clusters:**
- Hayward Cluster: Beginner-friendly
- Seeley Cluster: Family-friendly with amenities
- Cable Cluster: Old school, technical
- Ashwabay Cluster: Elevation, Lake Superior views

### What to Pack

**Essential Gear:**
- Helmet (required, chin strap must be buckled)
- Bike in good working order
- Spare tube/patch kit
- Multi-tool
- Nutrition (familiar products - train with race nutrition)
- Hydration system
- Weather-appropriate layers

**Prohibited:**
- Aero bars (will be disqualified)
- Littering (disqualification)

### Pre-Ride Information

- Course marked by Labor Day weekend
- Some sections cross private land (race day only)
- Sections on shared motorized trails - use caution
- Locals don't expect bike traffic except race weekend

## GRAVEL GOD RATINGS

### Overall Event Rating: 8.5/10
One of the most iconic and storied MTB races in North America. The history, atmosphere, and challenge make it a bucket-list event despite its quirks.

### Category Ratings

| Category | Rating | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| **Historical Significance** | 10/10 | Founded 1983, one of oldest MTB races in US. LeMond, MTB Hall of Famers raced here. |
| **Course Challenge** | 7/10 | 2,500-3,000ft climbing over 40mi, but not technical. Power > skill. |
| **Singletrack Quality** | 4/10 | Mostly ski trails and fire roads. Technical riders will be underwhelmed. |
| **Festival Atmosphere** | 9/10 | Beer, brats, live music. Community feel. "Do it at least once." |
| **Organization** | 8/10 | 40+ years of experience shows. Aid stations, volunteers excellent. |
| **Accessibility** | 5/10 | Lottery system frustrating. Remote location. Lodging limited. |

| **Scenery** | 8/10 | Northwoods of Wisconsin beautiful, especially early fall colors. |
| **Pro Competition** | 9/10 | Life Time Grand Prix stop brings elite field. Vermeulen, Swenson, Villafane. |
| **Beginner Friendly** | 6/10 | Short & Fat option good, but lottery, logistics, pace challenging for newbies. |

### Best For:
- Endurance cyclists seeking iconic bucket-list event
- Gravel/XC racers who favor power over technical skill
- Cyclists who appreciate history and community
- Those who enjoy festival atmosphere with racing

### Skip If:
- You want technical singletrack
- You can't handle lottery uncertainty
- You need last-minute planning flexibility
- You dislike mass-start chaos

### Pro Tips for Success:

1. **Enter lottery immediately** when registration opens
2. **Book lodging** the second you get lottery acceptance
3. **Train power**, not technical skills
4. **Start conservatively** - first 10 miles are chaos
5. **Accept you'll probably walk Fire Tower** unless elite
6. **Bring mud tires** or fast-rolling tires with decent traction
7. **Train with race nutrition** (Neversecond products)
8. **Pre-ride if possible** (respect private land restrictions)
9. **Embrace the festival** - it's not just a race
10. **Plan for weather** - anything from 50s and rain to 90s and dust
