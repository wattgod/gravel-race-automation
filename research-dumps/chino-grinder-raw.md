**Course Route**

The route follows the **Overland Historic Road Trail**, described as "the oldest continuously used road in the United States (since 1000 AD)"[3][4]. The course runs from Chino Valley, Arizona to Whitehorse Lake near Williams, Arizona, before returning to Chino[3][4].

**Technical Difficulty**

According to race organizers, "this is a gravel bike course, there is nothing technical about it. What makes it difficult is the climbing and change of altitude"[3][4]. The challenge is primarily about endurance and elevation gain rather than technical skill.

**Key Features**

## COMMUNITY & REPUTATION

**Race History**

The Chino Grinder was **founded in 2014**[3][4] and is "Arizona's longest running (circa 2014) and original Gravel Grinder"[1]. It is "celebrated as Arizona's oldest and most iconic gravel grinder" and has "earned its reputation as the toughest gravel race in the West"[2].

**Organizer**

The race is organized by **Shendo Cat Racing**[2][3][4].

**Community Philosophy**
