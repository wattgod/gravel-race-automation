# CIRREM — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Zachary Schuster [UNKNOWN]:** "The annual CIRREM gravel event in Central Iowa embraces the suck of February in Iowa. The 2019 race featured treacherous icy roads and a lot of DNFs." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/tag/cirrem)

**Dave Mable [UNKNOWN]:** "CXM contributor Dave Mable tackled Iowa's CIRREM 100k gravel race on a singlespeed. He provided his thoughts on the event for this irreverent race report." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/tag/cirrem)

**Drew Wilson [ELITE]:** "Everyone wants to be in Paris-Roubaix. That's what you dream about. But the USA Cycling-sanctioned road races have too many set-up costs and rules to stage a race on this same scale. So these gravel rides are as close as we can get to having classics-style courses." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Jeff Frane [UNKNOWN]:** "Regular racing is extremely cost prohibitive. But with gravel races you don't have to worry about getting your money's worth, because getting your money's worth is just about having fun. And if you had fun, you clearly got your money's worth. There's something about removing all the red tape and restrictions and regulations that brings cycling back to its roots in another way." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Jeff Frane [UNKNOWN]:** "If you have a mechanical problem, ain't nobody coming to pick your ass up. And there's something to be said for a person surviving by their wits, even if it's only for a couple of hours during a bike race." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Jim Cummings [UNKNOWN]:** "Grown men cry like babies at the finish line at Dirty Kanza. And it's easy to assume the reason they're crying is because they've been suffering and the pain is over. But I don't think that's it. I think they cry because it gives them a chance to go inside themselves to a place where they don't normally get to go, and they like what they find there." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Frank Bures [RECREATIONAL]:** "At about mile 45, I looked down at my chain to find it completely encased in frozen mud—the morning snow had melted and the afternoon roads were a cold, dirty swamp. Dead tired, and with only one gear working, I stopped to chip my cables loose." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Race organizer [UNKNOWN]:** "CIRREM is not for the faint of heart, but for those who want to test their grit!" (http://cirrem.com/)

**Race organizer [UNKNOWN]:** "All kidding aside, please take care to be safe, for the benefit of yourself and the riders around you. It can be slippery, the gravel can be loose, you'll hit high speeds, and the roads are open to cars... There is also a bridge (or bridges) with spaces between the boards that eat wheels for lunch. Seriously, a friend separated his shoulder last weekend on it." (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[UNKNOWN level] - Multiple bikes work:** "Your guess is as good as mine on this one. The winners have ridden singlespeed mountain bikes, fender'ed cross bikes, and the normal 'cross fare. You'll see mountain bikes, cross bikes, ss's, fenders, fixies, fat bikes and even road bikes. No recumbents or unicycles yet... Point is, ride what you feel comfy on, because chances are you're faster that way." (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**[RECREATIONAL level] - Fat bike category exists:** Results show a dedicated fat bike category with times ranging from 4:54 to 23:35 (https://www.gravel-results.com/race/20280)

**[UNKNOWN level] - Salsa Warbird gravel-specific design:** "Companies have started coming out with gravel-specific bikes, like Salsa's Warbird, which has a longer wheel base, a lower bottom bracket, a taller head tube, and sealed cables to keep dust out. It also comes with three water bottle cages in case you get thirsty out around mile 150 or so." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**[RECREATIONAL level] - Fenders consideration:** "Several riders pulled their fenders off mid-ride because of the peanut-buttery build up causing tire-rub" during Year 2 conditions. (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**[RECREATIONAL level] - Mechanical preparation critical:** "If you have a mechanical problem, ain't nobody coming to pick your ass up" - self-supported race requires full preparation (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**2021:** sunny mid 50's, 5-10 mph wind, thawing gravel (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**2020:** sunny mid 50's, 15-20 mph wind, dry gravel (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**2018:** sunny mid 50's, 15-20 mph wind, dry gravel (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**2017:** sunny mid 60's, 10-15 mph wind, dry gravel (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**2014:** sunny, mid 20's-30's, snow/ice gravel with a lot of melting soft gravel (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**Year one (2009):** "25-34 degrees sleet/rain/snow windy and wet/soft gravel. Temps started in the mid 30's & dropped steadily throughout the day, until the freezing rain turned to snow. It was cold & wet, the gravel was squishy" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/ and http://cirrem.com/results/)

**Year two (2010):** "Colder temps at the start, but as the temps rose from the teens into the upper 30's, the frozen snow on the roads melted and turned smooshy & muddy" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Year three (2011):** "Started with temps in teens, and the overcast conditions kept temps in the 20's for the day. Two inches of snow the night before" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course length:** 100 km (62 miles) with over 5,000 ft of climbing (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQnmfWxv8Hw)

**Location specifics:** "Cold, nasty, hilly gravel roads" around Cumming, IA and Madison County (http://cirrem.com/)

**Road surfaces variable:** Course conditions in late February can include frozen gravel, ice, mud, slush, or dry gravel depending on weather (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**Hazardous bridge(s):** "There is also a bridge (or bridges) with spaces between the boards that eat wheels for lunch. Seriously, a friend separated his shoulder last weekend on it." (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Pile-up location:** "In the lead pack, a rider broke away and the others started to chase him. Nearly all of them went over, too. We were just a few miles into" the race. "The roads were sometimes unpredictable in spots, and caused a small pile up in the first half of the race among the leaders" in year three. (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/ and https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Mid-race checkpoint:** "Checkpoint is roughly in the middle" of the 62-mile course (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Course consistency:** "The course will be hilly, and very similar to past CIRREM courses. In fact, it may be very similar to past CIRREM courses. In fact, it might actually be the same as past CIRREM courses." (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE level] - Winning times:** Men's record: 3:06:38 (Dillon McNeill); Women's record: 3:31:23 (Marisa Boaz); Men's Masters record: 3:27:38 (Ted Longnecker) (http://cirrem.com/results/)

**[ELITE level] - 2025 podium times:** Top finishers ranged from 4:14:50 to 4:37:20 in Men's Masters category (https://www.webscorer.com/racedetails?pid=1&raceid=381373&did=502211&cid=2689059)

**[RECREATIONAL level] - Extended finish times:** "The winners generally finish around 4 hours, with others on the course for more than 6 hours" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**[RECREATIONAL level] - Ride with buddy if slow:** "Ride with a buddy if you think you'll be out there a while" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**[ALL levels] - Self-supported:** "This is a self-supported race, meaning you should start the race with everything you need to finish it" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**[ALL levels] - Schedule:** "8:00AM – Breakfast, 8-9:45AM – Rider check-in, 10:00AM – CIRREM begins, 3:00PM – Awards" (http://cirrem.com/)

**[ALL levels] - Awards timing:** "Awards are generally done a couple hours after the winners come in, but still before everyone is off the course" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Multiple DNFs recorded:** 2025 results show multiple DNFs across categories - 4 in Men's Masters alone, plus others in Men's Open and Singlespeed categories (https://www.gravel-results.com/race/20280)

**Mud/ice mechanical failures:** "At about mile 45, I looked down at my chain to find it completely encased in frozen mud—the morning snow had melted and the afternoon roads were a cold, dirty swamp. Dead tired, and with only one gear working" (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**2019 conditions brutal:** "The 2019 race featured treacherous icy roads and a lot of DNFs" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/tag/cirrem)

**Early crashes:** "Not far out of the gates... riders started going down on the dirt road in the middle of Iowa. A big guy on my left spilled hard and almost took me out. Another one up front went over and slammed his helmet into the ground." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Year three pile-up:** "The roads were sometimes unpredictable in spots, and caused a small pile up in the first half of the race among the leaders" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Must notify if dropping:** "There will be a number that you must text if you drop from the race. I spent my Saturday evening driving around Madison County last year looking for 2 riders-who-shall-remain-nameless... So if you drop out & don't text there will be consequences." (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Self-extraction required:** "If you aren't going to finish, you need to figure out how to get home" (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Rapid sell-out:** "CIRREM made no opening announcement but filled up in 24 hours" (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Registration intensity:** "2026 Registration is live Nov 28 @ 8pm!" with note that registration typically fills in minutes (http://cirrem.com/)

**Post-race scene:** "I walked across the finish line (the bar's front door), and a guy at a table asked for my race number and shouted out my time. The bar was packed with dirty, happy cyclists, laughing, talking, and, this being a bar, drinking. I wandered around and found a few of the people I'd ridden with. I asked some of them how they did, but no one seemed all that concerned. Most of the riders just shrugged. After all, it didn't really matter how fast anyone went. What mattered, in the end, was just the ride." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Venue atmosphere:** "Please stay around afterwards and have a good time at the Tap as long as you like. The Tap is pretty awesome, and it's in Cumming, for crying out loud." (https://cirrem.com/race-info/)

**Inclusive categories:** Categories include mens, womens, singlespeed, fat bike, womens 50+, mens 50+, tandem (http://cirrem.com/)

**Low-key entry:** "Most gravel rides are free, or close to it, which is part of the gravel scene's everyman ethos. Just get on your bike and ride: At CIRREM, I saw mountain bikes, cylcocross, fat bikes, and even tandems rolling through the Iowa hills." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)

**Food included:** "Pre-ride breakfast, & post-race beer & food provided with your registration fee" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5135/cirrem)

**Strict policies:** "Absolutely no transfers and no refunds" (http://cirrem.com/)

**OG status:** "So at the OG of winter gravel races, CIRREM (a 16-year-old 64-mile hilly gravel race in the south central Iowa town of Cumming)" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kOw1SgaJ_vo)

**Growth of gravel scene:** "EVER SINCE 2005, IT'S been on the upswing. It's been astounding to see the growth of it." - Mark "Guitar Ted" Stevenson, noting the number of gravel races was up by 50 percent (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/groad/)
