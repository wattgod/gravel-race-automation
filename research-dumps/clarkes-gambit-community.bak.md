# CLARKES GAMBIT — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Format

**Clarke's Gambit** is a two-day gravel overnighter held in Nelligen, NSW (on the banks of the Clyde River near Batemans Bay). The event is organized by Graveleur and is part of their Bushranger Series. Named after the Clarke gang—bushrangers Thomas and John Clarke who operated in southern NSW in the 1860s.

The event emphasizes **social riding over racing**, designed for small groups of similar ability. According to the official description: "The overnighter is not a race, but rather a social cycling experience designed to be enjoyed in small groups of cyclists of similar ability." (https://raceroster.com/events/2026/112569/clarkes-gambit-2026)

**Course Options (2026):**
- **Short Course:** 35km each day (~850m climbing Day 1, ~787m Day 2)
- **Medium Course:** 55km each day (~1,030m climbing Day 1, ~1,150m Day 2)
- **Long Course:** 95km Saturday only (~1,925m climbing Day 1, ~1,150m Day 2)
- **One Day Option:** Available for those riding Saturday OR Sunday only

The 2023 inaugural event attracted almost 200 cyclists. (https://www.batemansbaypost.com.au/story/7950149/clarkes-gambit-gravel-race-comes-to-nelligen/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Quality:** Multiple sources describe the roads as "champagne gravel," "groomed gravel roads," and "mint gravel backroads" on maintained state forest roads. The official description emphasizes "Type 1 Fun only" and "No back breaking climbs." (https://graveleur.cc/events/clarkes-gambit-2024)

**River Crossing Highlight:** Day 1 features a signature river crossing at Shallow Crossing. **[UNKNOWN] Angus Calder (Event MC)** reported: "The water over the concrete causeway can vary in depth from ankle deep to waist high (and deeper!). Early on it was rideable but as the tide crept up, by 1pm riders were hoisting the bike CX style and wading through knee deep water for about 100m. With temperatures peaking at over 30 degrees, there was not a single rider regretting the opportunity to cool off while they made their way to the feedzone awaiting at the campground on the other side." (https://rideonmagazine.com.au/clarkes-gambit-gravel-good-times-great-mates/)

**Route Locations:**
- Day 1: North Loop heading west of Batemans Bay
- Day 2: Southern route (described as having "calmer energy")

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Event (reported by MC Angus Calder [UNKNOWN]):**
- **Day 1:** Hot and bright sunshine, temperatures "peaking at over 30 degrees" despite a forecast of 24°C
- **Day 2:** "Dark clouds building in the west... looked set to bucket down, but the weather pulled its punches, delivering only a light breeze, a gentle drizzle and excellent riding conditions." (https://rideonmagazine.com.au/clarkes-gambit-gravel-good-times-great-mates/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Official Participant Requirements [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
According to event organizers, to complete the activity cyclists need to:
- "Understand how to fuel their body for an extended ride and be at a good level of fitness to complete their chosen distance at an average speed of 15-20 kph"
- "Be experienced at navigating and following a GPX route"
- "Have a reasonable level of mechanical know how in case you need to perform running repairs on your bicycle e.g. punctures, ripped tyres, broken chains, non-functioning gears" (https://raceroster.com/events/2026/112569/clarkes-gambit-2026)

**Pacing Context:** The 15-20 kph target speed suggests this is recreational-to-moderate pacing. No specific wattage or nutrition data was provided in the sources.

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**[UNKNOWN] Angus Calder (Event MC)** provided extensive commentary on the event atmosphere:

"There's a particular kind of buzz that fills the air at a Graveleur event. It's the kind of energy that comes from a community who genuinely know each other, from riders who show up not only for their own adventure, but to share it with all the other fans of the emerging gravel scene who join them on the startline."

"Clarke's Gambit is one of the more intimate events on the NSW cycling calendar... With a smaller field comes a tighter community. Riders working together, helping to fix any mechanical issues, and making sure everyone has the best experience possible. It also means Mike Israel knows a surprising number of people by name. Riders give him feedback, he listens, and you often see adjustments made by the next day."

"It's one of the best things about having a great finish line location, being able to hang out in style with the other riders and de-brief on the day." (https://rideonmagazine.com.au/clarkes-gambit-gravel-good-times-great-mates/)

**Event Director Philosophy [UNKNOWN] Mike Israel:** "We like to push the boundaries and we like it to be really social" and "We want to support local towns." (https://www.batemansbaypost.com.au/story/7950149/clarkes-gambit-gravel-race-comes-to-nelligen/)

**Social Infrastructure:**
- Start/finish at Steam Packet Hotel Nelligen
- Post-ride beers in the beer garden
- Camping setup available across from the hotel
- "First entered, best prize basis" for sponsor prizes
- Belt buckles awarded to riders who complete all three Bushranger Series events
- Professional event photography by ReadyAimMedia included

## Event Logistics

**Base Location:** Nelligen, NSW - "a historic town upstream from Batemans Bay on the NSW south coast. Nestled between The Budawang National Park and the banks of Clyde River" (https://graveleur.cc/events/clarkes-gambit-2024)

**Start Times:**
- Saturday morning: 8am departure from Nelligen Bridge (expect traffic delays for motorists)
- Early registration available Friday night at Steam Packet Hotel

**Accommodation Options:**
- Car camping at BIG4 Caravan Park Nelligen (mention Graveleur for 10% discount on 1+ night stays: 02 4478 1076)
