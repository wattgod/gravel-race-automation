# COAST TO COAST — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## IMPORTANT NOTE: TWO DIFFERENT RACES

The sources reference **two completely different races** both named "Coast to Coast":

1. **Kathmandu Coast to Coast (New Zealand)** - A multisport race with running, cycling, and kayaking across New Zealand's South Island
2. **Coast to Coast Gravel Grinder (Michigan, USA)** - A 204-mile gravel bike race across Michigan's Lower Peninsula

These are analyzed separately below.

# COAST TO COAST GRAVEL GRINDER (MICHIGAN, USA)

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Selene Yeager [UNKNOWN] from Bicycling.com:** "I have no idea why I thought it would be easy... Roughly 185 miles into it as the f-bombs I was dropping vanished in the shifting sand beneath my sinking tires, it dawned on me (again) that this was not easy." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20770672/michigans-coast-to-coast-210-gravel-grinder/)

**Selene Yeager [UNKNOWN]:** "Some 300 of us awaited the 6:12 sunrise start of this inaugural event. Riders were lining up, snapping selfies as they dipped their tires in Lake Huron's Saginaw Bay for the ceremonial beginning of their adventure." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20770672/michigans-coast-to-coast-210-gravel-grinder/)

**Selene Yeager [UNKNOWN] on the crash:** "No sooner had I responded, 'We have 200 miles to go! I'm good right here,' than I heard the terrible, unmistakable sound of locking brakes, skidding tires, and bikes and bodies tumbling. Someone had washed out in a soft rut; the chain reaction had begun... My rear wheel kicked up, cartwheeling me into the chaos. I untangled my bike and emerged with exactly one scratch on my left shin." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20770672/michigans-coast-to-coast-210-gravel-grinder/)

**Selene Yeager [UNKNOWN] on the forest section:** "Manistee National Forest is where wood fairies come from. That might not be true. But I'm going with it, because everywhere I looked as we dove through a series of snowmobile tracks beneath the heavy canopy was crisp, green ground cover speckled with tiny white flowers." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20770672/michigans-coast-to-coast-210-gravel-grinder/)

**Selene Yeager [UNKNOWN] on mile 150:** "Until mile 150-something, when as if in a David Lynch film, the Mitten folded upon itself and became a boxing glove and the high-five state turned into the punch-in-the-face state, pummeling those who dared try to cross it with exposed, soul-sucking false flats, endless rollers covered in loose-gravel, and did I mention more deep sand?" (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a20770672/michigans-coast-to-coast-210-gravel-grinder/)

**Sarah Porch [COMPETITIVE]:** "We rode at a neutral pace until reaching the outskirts of town then, after a quick turn, we were off. Expect the unexpected: The tailwind the usually helps push riders along for the first section did not occur this year." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sarahs-story-coast-to-coast-gravel-grinder)

**Sarah Porch [COMPETITIVE]:** "The sun. The heat. The humidity. It sapped energy. We found some shade to lower the body temp and stretched. We both nearly ran out of hydration despite each carrying two Bivo insulated bottles and a full hydration pack." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sarahs-story-coast-to-coast-gravel-grinder)

**Sarah Porch [COMPETITIVE]:** "The kindness…we're in Michigan, folks. The Midwest. Several residents set up water and cooling stations for the racers. One kind family put up a sunshade, chairs, and a table so racers could stop and take ice and cold bottled water, or play in the sprinkler as some do." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sarahs-story-coast-to-coast-gravel-grinder)

**Sarah Porch [COMPETITIVE] on the forest section:** "We peered down at the Pine River, a Nationally-designated Scenic River. It looked inviting – especially as we had time to admire the shimmering water as we trudged through deep sand. More than a few areas were not rideable – or at least when weighing the risk of navigating deep, wheel-eating sand." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sarahs-story-coast-to-coast-gravel-grinder)

**Sarah Porch [COMPETITIVE] on the mosquitos at Checkpoint 3:** "We entered the Big M Recreation Area and were greeted by the mosquitos. So many that you almost needed to hold your breath. Bug spray? They couldn't care less. It was humorous trying to eat, grab hydration, and put on the lights while being buzzed and bitten." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sarahs-story-coast-to-coast-gravel-grinder)

**Jackie Gregory [UNKNOWN] on her first attempt (2021 - 100 mile):** "I had no clue what I was getting myself into... It rained the night before, and it rained up until the start... I hit the sections with a lot of sand. I think it's called the Big M Rec Area. The sand was so thick, we walked. There was a good section that we walked maybe a mile and a half, two miles, because it was like the beach. It was terrible." (https://www.cantuwheels.com/behindthewheels/2025/6/27/michigan-coast-to-coast-adventures-with-jackie-gregory)

**Jackie Gregory [UNKNOWN] on getting lost:** "I got lost. They put us on snowmobile trails in the forest. You could barely see anything, but trees on each side of you. I think I was following tracks of other people that got lost. So, I was in the middle of nowhere. I think I went about three miles... Every time I heard something in the trees, I was like, 'It's a bear. It's a bear.'" (https://www.cantuwheels.com/behindthewheels/2025/6/27/michigan-coast-to-coast-adventures-with-jackie-gregory)

**Jackie Gregory [UNKNOWN] on being stopped at mile 80+:** "By the time they told me to quit, I was like, well, 'I'm just done.' **Jackie Gregory [UNKNOWN] on single speed motivation:** "I don't have to think about shifting. I don't have to worry about if I'm gonna break something. I just ride." (https://www.cantuwheels.com/behindthewheels/2025/6/27/michigan-coast-to-coast-adventures-with-jackie-gregory)

**Jackie Gregory [UNKNOWN] on confidence from Flint Hills:** "After that day, I felt so good. I think this is okay. I think I could do it."
