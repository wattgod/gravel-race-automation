# COLLEGIATE GRAVEL NATIONALS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Details & Course Information

**2026 Inaugural Event:** The inaugural USA Cycling Collegiate Gravel National Championships will be held on **March 7, 2026** at Valley of Tears Gravel in Texas. The championship will be contested on the **Medium Course featuring approximately 50 miles** of racing across gravel roads, old rail trail, and select singletrack within Caprock Canyon State Park. (https://www.valleyoftearsgravel.com/2026-collegiate-gravel-national-championships)

**Eligibility Requirements:** Competitors planning to race must review eligibility documentation and complete the Collegiate National Championship Eligibility Verification Form in order to gain eligibility to compete. (https://www.valleyoftearsgravel.com/2026-collegiate-gravel-national-championships)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The course includes a mix of **gravel roads, old rail trail, and select singletrack within Caprock Canyon State Park** — an unusual combination for a gravel race that includes actual singletrack MTB sections. (https://www.valleyoftearsgravel.com/2026-collegiate-gravel-national-championships)

## Related Collegiate Racing Context

**Jake Rytlewski [COMPETITIVE]** on collegiate racing strategy: "My experience with collegiate racing is sometimes the races can be short, even if they aren't short I would look to get more riding before or after the races. Early on this is a great way to build up your CTL... I remember riding back to school from races, or even doubling up the collegiate race with the category races. Racking up as much as two 80 mile races in a row. But that effort, with proper recover paid off to some great results come nationals and into my summer pro racing schedule." (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/collegiate-road-nationals-training)

Jake's credentials [COMPETITIVE]: "4-Time MWCCC Conference Champion 2003-2006, 2-Time Marian University Track National Championship Team member 2002, 2003, 2nd - 2005 Collegiate Road Race / Omnium" (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/collegiate-road-nationals-training)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The 2026 event represents a **significant expansion** of USA Cycling's collegiate championship offerings, being highlighted as an "exciting addition" to the 2026 championship calendar alongside established road, track, and MTB nationals. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KV9DlVGRGF4)

The race is being hosted at an existing gravel event (Valley of Tears), suggesting the collegiate championship will benefit from established event infrastructure and the broader gravel racing community atmosphere. (https://www.valleyoftearsgravel.com/2026-collegiate-gravel-national-championships)
