# COWBOY CRUSHER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Location
The Cowboy Crusher is held in Glenrock, Wyoming, nestled at the base of the Rocky Mountains at the confluence of Deer Creek and North Platte River. The town served as a mail and stage stop along the Oregon Trail. The event is described as "Central Wyoming's Grassroots Gravel Event and One Heck of a Shindig" with a strong emphasis on the "Cowboy Tough" ethos. (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/)

**Next Event Date:** July 11, 2026 (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/)

All race activities start and finish at Glenrock Town Park, which hosts the start/finish line, awards, music, food, beverage, and significant swag distribution. (https://www.cyclingwest.com/gravel-biking/cowboy-crusher-central-wyomings-grassroots-gravel-event-and-one-heck-of-a-shindig-to-be-held-july-13-2024/)

## Course Options & Distances

**Cowboy 121:** 121 miles, 9,000+ feet of elevation gain. Route features Mormon and Boxelder canyons, Shirley Basin views, La Prele Creek drainage descent, approximately 20 miles of pavement, Ayers Natural Bridge Park area, and a significant final climb up Windy Ridge Road. Cash purse awarded for top 3 male, female, and non-binary finishers. (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

**Broncobuster 75:** 75 miles, 6,500 feet of elevation gain. Described as a "Butt Kicker" that ascends Mormon Canyon, includes rollers, descends into Boxelder Creek drainage ranchlands, passes through Ayers Natural Bridge Park, then climbs back up Windy Ridge Road before descending Boxelder Canyon to finish. (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

**Buckaroo 40:** 40 miles, 3,200 feet of elevation gain. Takes in portions of both the Cowboy 121 and Broncobuster 75, featuring Mormon Canyon and Boxelder Creek ranchlands before descending Boxelder Canyon. (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

**Mutton Buster 17:** 17 miles, 1,000 feet of elevation gain. Described as "fast and fun right out of the gate," following the Cowboy and Broncobuster routes up into the foothills before turning around and descending back to Glenrock. (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

E-bikes are allowed in the Mutton Buster 17 category only. NO E-BIKES ALLOWED in the Cowboy 121 or Broncobuster 75 courses. (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

Course surfaces are described as "mixed surfaces and anything from champagne gravel to whiskey wielding roads with some two-track." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/gravel-biking/cowboy-crusher-central-wyomings-grassroots-gravel-event-and-one-heck-of-a-shindig-to-be-held-july-13-2024/)

The Cowboy 121 rider guide specifically notes: "Be sure to save a little in the tank as this climb is a doozy" when referring to the final climb up Windy Ridge Road and lower Boxelder Canyon. (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

The Broncobuster 75 description includes self-talk during Mormon Canyon climb: "Wow, beautiful scenery...Wow, shut up legs. Just keep pedaling and before you know it you are out of the canyon and feeling like you are on top of the world." (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

GPS devices are required for the Cowboy 121 and strongly encouraged for the Broncobuster 75. "You'll spend a large part of the day outside of cell coverage." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Mandatory Carry List** [ALL LEVELS]:
- Helmet (required at all times when on bike)
- Cycling computer/GPS (required for Cowboy 121, strongly encouraged for Broncobuster 75)
- Rear taillight visible from at least 50 feet (front headlight not required but recommended)
- 2-3 water bottles and/or hydration pack
- Food: energy bars, gels, nuts, chews, salt tabs
- Spare tubes, CO2 cartridge, pump or inflation system, tire levers, multi tool
- "We strongly encourage you to carry extra butt butter also"
(https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

**Additional Recommended Gear** [ALL LEVELS]:
- Extra clothing: arm/leg warmers, wind vest and/or rain jacket ("anything can happen in the mountains")
- Post-ride clothes change: "Your bottom will like you better if you don't walk around in a sweaty, stinky chamois for hours after being in your kit all day"
(https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

Participants are advised to "know basic repair skills" and come prepared with their own pump and extra tubes. (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

## DNF Risk Factors

Outside support is NOT allowed except at designated aid stations. "Receiving assistance (other than immediate emergency medical needs) from a support crew, or any other non-participant, at any point other than the official aid stations/checkpoints will result in immediate disqualification from the event. There are no exceptions to this rule." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

Support crews and spectators are NOT allowed on course. "If you are going to abandon the race you must make it to an official aid station/checkpoint, and then call for your support crew to come get you or take a ride back to Glenrock with a SAG wagon when it is available." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

You will receive a DNF if you do not complete the race distance you are registered for. (https://cowboycrush.itsyourrace.com/)

Cell service is limited throughout most of the course. "If there is an emergency, please call 911 if you have cell service. Given that there is limited cell service, try to get someone to the nearest Feed Zone or find a course marshal to assist in helping you." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

## Aid Stations & Support

**Mutton Buster Course:**
- Mile 8.5: Fully stocked aid station at turnaround point

**Cowboy 121, Broncobuster 75, and Buckaroo 40:**
- Mile 10: Mormon Canyon Water Stop (water only - "this is a little early, but you climb 1,700 ft. to get here")
- Mile 18: Boxelder Road Aid Station (where Cowboy 121 departs from Broncobuster/Buckaroo)

**Broncobuster Specific:**
- Windy Ridge Road Aid Station: Fully stocked with porta potty (riders pass twice)
- Four Corners: Water stop only (riders pass twice - Mile 29 @ 3,039 ft. gain and Mile 53 @ 5,165 ft. gain)
- East End Windy Ridge Rd & Cold Springs Rd/WY 91: Fully stocked with toilet

**Cowboy 121 Only:**
- Mile 33 (Balsh Road): Fully stocked, Checkpoint #1, no restroom ("use the greater outdoors")
- Mile 45 (Campbell Creek Campground): Fully stocked, USFS vault toilet available
- Mile 60 (Cold Springs Rd/WY 91): Water stop only
- Mile 80 (Spring Canyon Rd): Fully stocked, Checkpoint #2
- Mile 97 (Four Corners): Water stop only
- Mile 104 (Windy Ridge): Fully stocked, Checkpoint #3, porta potty available

Aid stations will have electrolyte mix, water, energy gels, chews, bars, real food, and mechanical and medical supplies. Each station will have an air pump and basic supplies. "Although there will be an array of snacks at each feed zone, we strongly encourage you to have some 'safety snacks' in your pockets just in case." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event emphasizes its grassroots nature and community atmosphere. "We are super stoked to have you visit the wonderful town of Glenrock, Wyoming. We've worked hard to bring you an epic gravel experience that will keep you coming back year after year and bringing your dirt lovin, grit lovin, and gravel lovin friends!" (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

The park venue includes a playground for kids and music and beverages for adults. Organizers encourage participants to "bring your friends and family to stay and play." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

The park has ample parking but NO CAMPING allowed. (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

The race philosophy emphasizes self-sufficiency in the spirit of grassroots gravel racing: "Providing support to a rider while on course is not in the spirit of self-sufficiency of grass roots gravel racing. It is also unfair to other participants, and increases the risks to other participants when there is more traffic on the road." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

## Rules of the Road

This is NOT a closed course. Required conduct:
- Obey the rules of the road at all times
- Obey flow of traffic
- Stop at stop signs
- Yield to pedestrians
- STAY TO THE RIGHT SIDE OF THE ROAD for safety
- Respect riders around you at all times

"Let's keep this fun and accident-free." (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)

**Leave No Trace:**
"Keep it clean, please! DO NOT throw your gel packets, paper, plastic wrappers or any other garbage along the route. Pack it In…Pack it out!" (https://www.cowboycrusher.com/faqs)
