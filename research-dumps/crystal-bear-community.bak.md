## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**B&E's Trees [RECREATIONAL]:** Navigation setup that failed: "I really like paper maps, but completely failed here. In hindsight, had I responsibly approached this race I would have *at least* used the USFS maps I unknowingly had stashed back in the van. A gazetteer has nowhere near the resolution required when riding forest service roads, truck trails & unmapped singletrack." Lesson learned: "Like many bikepacking routes, this one was best navigated by GPS for safety, speed & convenience. On Day 1 I was dependent on my kind group of riders to wait and help me find my way. On Day 2 I relied on Brent to navigate part of the ride, and then used my phone while trying to find service & conserve battery." (https://www.embarkmaple.com/blog/crystal-bear-run-what-ya-brung-amp-make-up-the-rest)

**B&E's Trees [RECREATIONAL]:** Comfort setup: "A few weeks earlier I had installed some old bar-ends on the inside of my brake levers to give me more hand & seating positions. Moving around in the saddle saved my back on my too-small bike with a seized too-low seat post... I have also had heal problems since running cross-country in high school, and early in the ride I removed my right shoe heal cups since they made my right leg slightly longer, which seemed to be triggering knee pain on previous rides. My back and knees had no complaints!" (https://www.embarkmaple.com/blog/crystal-bear-run-what-ya-brung-amp-make-up-the-rest)

**Laura Hrubes [ELITE]:** Bike setup for Crystal Bear: "My gravel/off-road/adventure bike... is a full titanium Bearclaw Thunderhawk. I've got two wheelsets for it, a set of Velocity Blunts with 50mm Donnelly Xplor off-road tires and a dynamo hub wired up to a light and battery pack, and another set of Velocity Ailerons... and skinny Teravail Cannonball gravel tires. Both sets in purple... It's all 12 speed sram electronic shifting and set up 1x with an absolute black oval chainring." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/triple-crown-tandem-with-dad-a-barbie-dream-bike-laura-hrubes-foodieknitter-s-epic-2022)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Design [UNKNOWN]:** "The Crystal Bear is a two-day 194-mile bikepacking trip from Laona, WI to Crystal Falls, MI to Laona, WI. It was designed so that you only need to pack camping gear, no mess kit. The route passes by a bar and grill on the way to Crystal Falls. Includes: campground fee, tasty pizza and cold beer in Crystal Falls on Saturday night and breakfast buffet Sunday morning in Crystal Falls to fuel your ride back to Laona." Rules: "Self-sufficiency, unsupported, no SAG." 74 rider limit. (https://www.thebear100.com/crystal-bear)

**B&E's Trees [RECREATIONAL]:** First-timer experience: "I arrived in Laona Friday night and learned that Gary, whom I had met two weeks earlier at the Bear 100, volunteered to shuttle beer & coffee. Chris informed me I was no longer needed as a volunteer, and thus I was now obligated to race. WHAT!? I secretly wanted to ride, but coming from an MTB & off-road adventuring background I never really participated in long road/gravel events." (https://www.embarkmaple.com/blog/crystal-bear-run-what-ya-brung-amp-make-up-the-rest)

**B&E's Trees [RECREATIONAL]:** Saturday night atmosphere: "In somewhat of a daze I 'switchbacked' my way up and rode through town to pizza, beer and every other racer already at camp. My goal was to finish Day 1, my first official century, but in true Embark spirit went over top and managed DFL!!" (https://www.embarkmaple.com/blog/crystal-bear-run-what-ya-brung-amp-make-up-the-rest)

**B&E's Trees [RECREATIONAL]:** Day 2 morning: "I got out of my tent and took a few photos of camp while my gear was laid out to dry in the light mist. **Race philosophy [UNKNOWN]:** "The Crystal Bear... recaptures the original spirit of bikepacking and exploration.
