## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Format:** 
- Overnight point-to-point from northern Wisconsin to Upper Peninsula Michigan
- Self-supported bikepacking (carry overnight gear, food, navigation tools)
- No aid stations—riders navigate and resupply independently
- Mix of marked route and turn-by-turn navigation
- Finish whenever you arrive (typically 15-30 hours depending on rider)

**Surface Composition:**
- Northern Wisconsin and UP Michigan forest service roads
- Mix of hard-packed gravel, sandy sections, and technical terrain
- Some paved connector roads between gravel segments
- Technical sections with embedded rocks, roots at trail crossings
- Variable surface quality—sandy loose sections common
- Stream crossings and wet sections possible

**Terrain Character:**
- Northwoods terrain: rolling with short, punchy climbs
- No sustained "mountain" climbing but constant undulation
- Dense forest cover throughout most of course
- Remote sections with minimal towns or services
- Wildlife common (deer, black bear, coyotes)
- Darkness riding required—night navigation skills essential

**Route Highlights:**
- **Laona, WI start** - northern Wisconsin forest town
- **Nicolet National Forest** - remote forest roads through WI section
- **Wisconsin-Michigan border crossing** via forest roads
- **Upper Peninsula terrain** - transition to Michigan's rugged UP landscape
- **Crystal Falls, MI finish** - small UP mining town

**Technical Elements:**
- Sandy sections can bog down wheels and sap momentum
- Root crossings and embedded rocks in forested sections
- Night navigation with limited lighting (remote areas = no light pollution but no landmarks either)
- Stream/water crossings possible depending on spring runoff
- Rough gravel can cause flats and mechanical issues

## WEATHER & CLIMATE

**Mid-May in Northern WI / Upper Peninsula MI:**
- Typical temperatures: 40°F-65°F
- Nighttime lows: 30°F-45°F (frost possible)
- High variability: early May can bring snow; late May can be warm
- Rain common—spring is wet season in Northwoods
- Black flies and mosquitoes emerging (can be intense)
- Daylight: ~14.5 hours (5:30 AM sunrise, 8:00 PM sunset)

**Historical Conditions:**
- May weather is unpredictable—prepare for cold, rain, mud
- Overnight temperatures require warm sleeping gear or bivy
- Mud and wet conditions common after spring snowmelt
- Black fly season peaks late May—insect repellent essential
- Wind not major factor (dense forest buffers)

**Climate Challenges:**
- Cold overnight temperatures when stopped or riding slowly
- Rain can turn sandy/loose sections into energy-sapping slogs
- Insects (black flies, mosquitoes) during brief stops
- Darkness riding—approximately 6-10 hours of night riding depending on pace
- Limited services for resupply in remote areas

## SUFFERING ZONES

**Zone 1: Miles 30-60 "Wisconsin Northwoods Marathon"**
Early fatigue sets in as sandy, loose sections sap energy. Still daylight but realizing the remote, lonely nature of the route.

**Zone 2: Miles 80-100 "Darkness Arrives"**
Sunset around Mile 80-90 depending on pace. Transition to night riding. Fatigue + darkness + navigation challenges compound. First real mental test.

**Zone 3: Miles 120-150 "Midnight Grind"**
Deep into overnight hours (midnight-4 AM). Coldest temperatures. Riding alone in pitch-black forest. Sleep deprivation starts hitting. Mental battle peaks.

**Zone 4: Miles 170-192 "Dawn Survival Shuffle"**
Sun rises but body is wrecked. Final 20 miles feel endless. Mechanical issues at this stage are crushing. Just want to be done.

## DNF FACTORS

**Mechanical Failures:**
- Flats from rough gravel and embedded rocks in remote sections with no support
- Drivetrain issues (chain breaks, derailleur hangers) end races with no quick fix available
- Light failures during overnight hours = dangerous to continue

**Sleep Deprivation + Cold:**
- Riders underestimating overnight cold stop to bivy/sleep and don't restart
- Hypothermia risk if stopped too long in wet/cold conditions without proper gear
- Cognitive decline from sleep deprivation leads to navigation errors or crashes

**Navigation Errors:**
- Getting lost adds miles and drains morale/energy reserves
- Missing a turn in darkness can mean 10+ mile detours before realizing error
- Limited cell service makes GPS reliance critical; dead batteries = problems

**Underestimating Self-Sufficiency:**
- Running out of food/water between resupply points
- Not carrying enough warm layers for overnight cold
- Inadequate lighting for 8-10 hours of night riding

**Mental Breakdown:**
- Loneliness and darkness compound fatigue around Mile 100-130
- No external support or cheering crowds—purely internal motivation
- Quitting is easy: just stop pedaling and call for pickup

## EQUIPMENT CONSENSUS

**Bike Setup:**
- Bikepacking-capable gravel bike or rigid MTB
- 40-50mm tires (or wider) for sandy/rough sections—tubeless mandatory
- Wide-range gearing (1x with 10-50t minimum, or 2x)
- Frame bags + seatpack + handlebar roll for overnight gear
- Fenders recommended (May mud common)

**Lighting:**
- High-lumen headlight (1000+ lumens) for 8-10 hours night riding
- Backup light mandatory
- Battery redundancy: extra battery packs or dynamo hub setup
- Taillight for visibility if riding with others

**Overnight Gear:**
- Bivy sack or ultralight tent (if planning sleep stop)
- 30-40°F sleeping bag or quilt
- Insulated jacket (puffy) for cold overnight hours
- Rain jacket and pants—May weather demands it
- Warm hat, buff, gloves

**Navigation:**
- GPS device (Garmin, Wahoo) with loaded route
- Phone with offline maps as backup
- Paper cue sheet (electronics fail)
- Extra battery packs for devices

**Food/Water:**
- 2+ bottles on bike; 3-4 liter capacity total if using bladder
- Food for 20-30 hours (primarily calorie-dense: bars, gels, nuts, candy)
- Cash for resupply at small-town gas stations
- Water filter or purification tabs for stream water if desperate

## LOGISTICS

**Start Location:**
- Laona, Wisconsin (northern WI, Nicolet National Forest area)
- ~1.5 hours north of Green Bay, WI
- ~3 hours north of Milwaukee, WI

**Finish Location:**
- Crystal Falls, Michigan (Upper Peninsula)
- ~2 hours west of Marquette, MI
- ~4.5 hours north of Green Bay, WI

**Transportation Logistics:**
- **Major Challenge:** Point-to-point format requires shuttle/logistics planning
- Options:
  - Leave car at finish, get ride to start
  - Leave car at start, arrange pickup from finish
  - Coordinate with other riders for car swap
- Rental car one-way between WI-MI possible but expensive
- Some events provide shuttle service (verify with organizer)

**Airports:**
- **Green Bay (GRB)** - 1.5 hours south of Laona start
- **Marquette (MQT)** - 2 hours east of Crystal Falls finish
- **Milwaukee (MKE)** - 3 hours south of start
- **Rental car required** for any airport option

**Lodging:**
- **Pre-race:** Laona, WI has limited motels; Rhinelander, WI (30 min south) has more options
- **Post-race:** Crystal Falls, MI has small motels; Iron Mountain, MI (20 min south) has more options
- Camping available at area campgrounds if preferred

**Resupply Points:**
- Few reliable options—plan based on route
- Small-town gas stations (stock shelf-stable food, not race nutrition)
- **Critical:** Verify open hours; northern WI/MI towns close early
- Typical gaps: 40-60 miles between services

**Race Day Logistics:**
- Start time: Typically morning (7:00-9:00 AM) to maximize daylight before dark
- Packet pickup: Day before or race morning at start
- No aid stations—purely self-supported
- Tracking: GPS tracker required for safety
- Finish: Whenever you arrive (15-30 hour range)

**Pro Tips:**
- Scout resupply points on route beforehand
- Test overnight gear in training (don't discover your sleeping bag is too cold at 2 AM)
- Bring spare derailleur hanger, tube, patch kit, chain links, multi-tool
- Download offline maps—cell service spotty in Northwoods
