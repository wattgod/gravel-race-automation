## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile [Official sources]:**
- 110km total distance with 1800m of ascent
- Mix of "punchy climbs, fast descents and long flatter sections to really get the power down"
- Described as "parcours akin to the original gravel grinder races in the US"
- Surface: Dalby Forest access roads and linking trails, described as "perfect Dalby gravel" and "pristine gravel routes"
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info, https://cycletechreview.com/2026/news/dalby-grit-2026/)

**Specific Sections [Official course description]:**
- First 6km: Flatter warm-up section after neutralized start, good opportunity to work in a group
- 16km mark: "Infamous White Cliff Rigg" - described as a long climb, topping out at 21km
- After first feed station: "Fast, well surfaced undulating terrain" heading east
- "Super fast descent into the Deep Dale traverse" followed by "short testing climb up Bickley Gate"
- Final 15km: "Retains most of its height and winds its way through the fast trails above Crosscliff Wood" with "short, sharp kickers and some technical corners"
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**One rider's experience [ELITE]:** Race "absolutely lived up to the hype" but noted they "quickly ran out of steam on the relentless..." [quote appears cut off in source] (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kkT3HxL2XkY)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Feed Station Strategy [Official info]:**
- Three feed stations at approximately 23km, 53km, and 76km (to be finalized)
- Stations provide: water, sports hydration, bananas, flapjacks, sweet treats, and some sports nutrition
- Official guidance: "We strongly recommend that riders do not rely solely on the feed station. In the spirit of gravel riding, we encourage riders to remain self-sufficient throughout the event."
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Early Race Tactics [Official course notes]:**
- Initial flatter 6km section described as "good opportunity to work in a group" but warning: "Don't let the initial flatter sections fool you"
- After first feed station around 23km, there's a section described as potentially "a chance to take on food, consolidate a lead or share some work in a small group"
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Elite Category Racing [Official format]:**
- Lead vehicle/motorbike guides Elite men's and women's categories
- "Riders overtaken by the lead vehicle must keep to the left-hand side of the trail to allow faster riders to pass on the right"
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Required/Recommended Kit [Official requirements]:**
- Minimum self-sufficient kit required:
  - Water bottle or hydration pack with at least 500ml
  - Snacks or sports nutrition for full duration
  - Spare inner tubes (even if tubeless)
  - Tyre plugs (if tubeless)
  - Tyre levers
  - Multitool and chain splitter
  - Split link for chain repair
- Helmet mandatory at all times (must meet current safety standards)
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Bike Restrictions [Official regulations]:**
- Permitted: Any bike type except tandem or recumbent (road, mountain, hybrid, cross, etc.)
- Prohibited: E-bikes, time trial bikes, and aero bars
- Same bike frame must be used for entire event (wheel changes permitted)
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Mechanical Support Available:**
- Neutral mechanical support via self-service station at feed zones
- "Riders should be prepared to handle their own bike repairs, though mechanics will be on hand to assist where possible"
- Note: "There may be a charge for any parts used"
- External technical assistance not permitted (only from other riders or official staff)
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

## DNF Risk Factors

**One Elite rider's experience:** Started well but "quickly ran out of steam on the relentless..." course, suggesting the sustained climbing and pace can catch out even strong riders (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kkT3HxL2XkY)

**Course Warnings [Official description]:**
- "Don't let the initial flatter sections fool you" - suggesting riders who go too hard early may struggle
- "Relentless" nature of climbs mentioned by multiple sources
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kkT3HxL2XkY)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Scale & Reception:**
- Inaugural 2025 edition "drew hundreds of riders across multiple age and ability categories"
- 2026 entries "sold out within 36 hours" for the closed community launch in late January
- Described as "standout 2025 debut" and "STAND-OUT GRAVEL RACE of 2025"
(https://cycletechreview.com/2026/news/dalby-grit-2026/, https://www.dalbygrit.cc/)

**Event Village & Support:**
- Industry partner support at feed stations, event village, and for mechanical assistance
- Confirmed partners: TORQ Nutrition, Wahoo, Fenwicks
- On-site showers and facilities
- Food and drink available for purchase
- Podium ceremony for all classes
- Free day parking for full weekend included
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info, https://cycletechreview.com/2026/news/dalby-grit-2026/)

**Race Format:**
- Championship-style format with podium places for top 3 male and female finishers in each age category
- Professional timing with splits
- Split over two days:
  - Saturday: All women's categories including Elite, plus men's 50+
  - Sunday: Elite Men and all remaining men's categories up to 49
(https://news.paceupmedia.com/championship-style-gravel-racing-returns-to-dalby-forest-this-summer-as-dalby-grit-2026-entries-open)

**Organizer Pedigree:**
- Run by Focal Events, organizers of "hugely popular Dirty Reiver" (UK's biggest gravel event)
- Part of Focal's full gravel portfolio including Gravel Rocks and SCOTT Frontier 300
- Described as bringing "best in class" events approach
(https://cycletechreview.com/2026/news/dalby-grit-2026/, https://news.paceupmedia.com/championship-style-gravel-racing-returns-to-dalby-forest-this-summer-as-dalby-grit-2026-entries-open)

**Accommodation Options:**
- Limited on-site camping (£10 per night for tent)
- Camper van slots (£18 per night, no hook-ups)
- Teepee Village available for "more luxurious option"
(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**UK Gravel Context:**
Co-Race Director Bryan Singleton: "Last year showed us that riders are craving more high-level gravel racing in the UK" - positioning Dalby Grit as part of growing UK gravel scene alongside events like Dirty Reiver, The Gralloch (UCI World Series), and British National Gravel Championship
(https://cycletechreview.com/2026/news/dalby-grit-2026/)
