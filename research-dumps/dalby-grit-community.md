## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile:** 110km with 1,800m of elevation gain using access roads and forest trails within Dalby Forest (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Course Description from Organizers:** "Close racing on punchy climbs, fast descents and long flatter sections where you can really get the power down" - described as "akin to the original gravel grinder races in the US" (https://news.paceupmedia.com/championship-style-gravel-racing-returns-to-dalby-forest-this-summer-as-dalby-grit-2026-entries-open)

**Specific Course Sections (from official route info):**
- **0-6km:** Neutralized section on road, then "initial flatter sections" on gravel - "a good opportunity to work in a group"
- **16-21km:** First major climb up "the infamous White Cliff Rigg," topping out at 21km
- **Post-feed station:** Fast, well-surfaced undulating terrain heading east - "a great chance to really power along"
- **Deep Dale traverse:** "Super fast descent" followed by "short testing climb up Bickley Gate"
- **Final 15km:** Maintains height, winds through fast trails above Crosscliff Wood with "short, sharp kickers and some technical corners"

(https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

## Race Format & Categories

**Race Structure:** Split over two days
- **Saturday:** All women's categories including Elite, plus men's 50+ age categories
- **Sunday:** Elite Men and all remaining men's categories up to 49 years

**Categories Available:** Pro/Elite (18+), then age groups: 19-34, 35-39, 40-44, 45-49, 50-54, 55-59, 60-64, 65-69, 70-74, 75-79, 80-84

**Championship Format:** "Podium places are awarded to the top three male and female finishers in each category" (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

## Race Support & Logistics

**Aid Stations:** Three feed stations at 23km, 53km, and 76km (to be finalized). Stations provide water, sports hydration, bananas, flapjacks, sweet treats, and some sports nutrition. Organizers note: "We strongly recommend that riders do not rely solely on the feed station. In the spirit of gravel riding, we encourage riders to remain self-sufficient throughout the event." (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Mechanical Support:** Neutral mechanical support available via self-service station with mechanics on hand to assist, though "there may be a charge for any parts used" (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Required Kit:** Helmet (must meet current safety standards), water bottle/hydration pack (minimum 500ml), snacks/sports nutrition, spare inner tubes, tire plugs (if tubeless), tire levers, multitool and chain splitter, split link for chain repair (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Event Partners:** TORQ Nutrition, Wahoo, Fenwicks, fi'zi:k, Maxxis, Chamois Butt'r, Enduro Bearings, Orange Seal, Ride with GPS (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8610/mitchell-and-clayton-take-victory-at-inaugural-dalby-grit)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Format:** Full weekend experience with Friday course recce opportunity, Saturday racing, and Sunday recovery rides to "shake out the legs and enjoy the scenery at a less frenetic pace" (https://www.granfondo.com/events/dalby-grit-gravel-race/2025-06-27/6901/cycling)

**Organizer Background:** Run by Focal Events, the team behind Dirty Reiver (UK's biggest gravel event), Gravel Rocks, and SCOTT Frontier 300. The organization has "a wealth of experience" and "a dedicated team of passionate riders who have explored the far reaches of the globe on two wheels" (https://news.paceupmedia.com/championship-style-gravel-racing-returns-to-dalby-forest-this-summer-as-dalby-grit-2026-entries-open)

**Event Village:** Access to brand expo, on-site showers and facilities, food and drink available for purchase (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**Racing Philosophy:** Distinctly positioned as "for the racers" and "championship-style gravel racing" - contrasts with Dirty Reiver which is more of a challenge event. Described as having "close racing" and competitive atmosphere (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/news/one-for-the-racers-introducing-brand-new-uk-gravel-event-dalby-grit)

**Camping Available:** Limited on-site camping and campervan slots. Campervan: £18/night, tent: £10/night (cheaper than barrier exit rates). No hook-ups available. Luxury teepee village option also offered (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**2025 Reception:** Inaugural event drew "hundreds of racers" and was described by organizers as having "a fantastic turnout" with riders embracing "the spirit of gravel racing" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8610/mitchell-and-clayton-take-victory-at-inaugural-dalby-grit)

## Entry Information

**Cost:** £87.50 (+ fees) includes: professionally signed race course, custom race number, professional timing with splits, three feed stations, on-course mechanical support, on-site showers and facilities, event village and brand expo access, professional medic teams on course, podium ceremony for all classes, free day parking for full weekend (https://www.dalbygrit.cc/event-info)

**2026 Entry Response:** "We launched entries to our closed community late January and had a really fast response" - suggesting high demand (https://news.paceupmedia.com/championship-style-gravel-racing-returns-to-dalby-forest-this-summer-as-dalby-grit-2026-entries-open)

## UK Gravel Racing Context

**Position in UK Calendar:** Dalby Grit sits among a growing roster of major UK gravel events including:
- Dirty Reiver (UK's biggest, 65-200km options)
- The Gralloch (UCI Gravel World Series Round 18)
- Graean Cymru (UCI Gravel World Series Round 41)
- Gravel Fondo Cymru (Welsh National Championship, open to non-Welsh)
- British National Gravel Championship

"Dalby Grit's blend of fast racing and technical forest terrain gives it a distinctive place in the calendar. The race's rapid uptake suggests it's becoming a must-ride fixture for competitive gravel racers." (https://cycletechreview.com/2026/news/dalby-grit-2026/)
