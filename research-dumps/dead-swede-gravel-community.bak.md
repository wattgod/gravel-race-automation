# DEAD SWEDE GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Heidi [ELITE - 2nd place women's 40-mile]:** "It's been a long time, or even never, since I have been this proud of my race effort and finish. I raced smart in the opening pavement miles, hiding in the pack. I railed the descents, and made sure to stay steady on the climbs. I put forth a solo time trial effort over most of the course, much of it into a hot headwind all by myself." (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Heidi [ELITE]:** "I hate mass starts, and I hate them even more in a crowd mostly filled with people that have never road raced or ridden in a peloton." (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Heidi [ELITE]:** "And all done on a 2012 Specialized Crux with canti brakes and relatively narrow tires in comparison to today's trends, and hamburger seat bag. I thumb my nose at you, industry marketing tactics!" (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Jeffrey Cory [ELITE - 64th of 65 finishers in 100-mile]:** "My goal was to finish, as this was my first gravel 'hundo.' Spoiler alert — mission accomplished... I ended up riding almost all of the hundred miles solo." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Jeffrey [ELITE]:** "Due to the fact that Sheridan on Memorial Day got 2.5 inches of rain (yep, 7th highest single-day total there, on record — ever, like, in recorded history) thus putting wet heavy snow up in the Big Horn mountains, that single track course portion had to be scratched. That meant that we did one loop of the main course two times instead of a planned once, for the 100-miler." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Jeffrey [ELITE]:** "The picture of the 'can we still be friends' sign is from the steepest hill, near the top. I'd say it was 9-12% max grade for about a quarter mile. I guess it was still my friend, as I like climbing, and in fact, I'm happy to report that I actually rode every foot of all the climbs (except about 10 feet on the penultimate climb of the day due to cramps)." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Jeffrey [ELITE]:** "Kind of a sort-of-demoralizing reminder of being DFL. But he was a nice guy (kept talking to me when he stopped 'with me' at aid stations). Still, a 3/4-ton rig putt-putting along behind ya kinda crimps a guy's solitude out there. New personal race goal: stay ahead of DFL, don't have the sweeper truck slowly follow YOU." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "I loved that the event started and finished at Kendrick Park, right there in the heart of town. These lovely creatures [horses] were grazing on the hillside overlooking the park as we lined up at the start line." (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Heidi [ELITE]:** Successfully completed 40-mile course on a 2012 Specialized Crux cyclocross bike with cantilever brakes and 32mm tires, despite descending at 43mph on loose gravel. Notes this was "relatively narrow tires in comparison to today's trends." (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Jeffrey [ELITE]:** Rode a Lauf True Grit bike with AXS electronic shifting for the 100-mile race. Battery lasted the entire race. Used chamois butter and reapplied mid-race from single-use packets. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

## Weather Experienced

**2019 (Heidi's race):** Hot and sunny during the race. Heidi notes: "The still air combined with my black skin suit and hot sun made me want to melt" on the first climb. Around hour one, she was "cognizant of the fact I needed to be taking on the calories and hydration in the sun." (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**2022 (Jeffrey's race):** "Weather was amazing. Low 50s to start, mid 70s in afternoon. No T-storms developed till evening. Hardly any wind, head or tail." However, the Friday before Memorial Day (race day), Sheridan received 2.5 inches of rain with wet heavy snow in the Big Horn mountains. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**2024 (Anonymous rider):** Course turned north "onto the gravel nestled along the base of the Bighorn Mountains" with conditions that minimized dust. Rider notes: "I'm not sure why this was — perhaps it rained the day before, perhaps they use oil on the roads?" (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**40-Mile Course:**
- **Heidi [ELITE]:** "8 miles of pavement to get us warmed up... After eight miles we hit the gravel and immediately the first big climb of the day." Describes "Super, crazy fast descents" and "long grindy uphills." Notes a "rolly, falsely flat" section with "mild headwind" after Dayton. Last handful of miles on pavement before finish. (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

- **Anonymous [UNKNOWN]:** "The course headed west on a paved gradual climb for about nine miles. Then the course turned north onto the gravel nestled along the base of the Bighorn Mountains and the biggest climb of the route began." (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

**100-Mile Course:**
- **Jeffrey [ELITE]:** Course featured 6,500+ feet of climbing (would have been more with original single-track section). Describes a hill with "9-12% max grade for about a quarter mile" marked with a "can we still be friends" sign. "Super, crazy fast descents" and "long grindy uphills." The "bacon" aid station at mile 49 was "at the top of a long hill" followed by "mainly downhill(ish) back to Sheridan for 10 miles." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Course Conditions:**
- **Anonymous [UNKNOWN]:** Roads had minimal dust compared to typical gravel roads, possibly due to recent rain or oil treatment. (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Heidi [ELITE - 2:26 finish for 40 miles]:** "I raced smart in the opening pavement miles, hiding in the pack." Made it to top of first big climb as first female but then "eventually dropped off" from the lead group. "Time to maintain this second overall female position! ... I put forth a solo time trial effort over most of the course." Finished 13th overall out of 330+ racers. (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Jeffrey [ELITE - 8:45 total time, 7:45 ride time for 100 miles, 64th of 65 finishers]:** 
- Stats: 103 miles; 6,516 feet elevation gain; 485 TSS; HR zones: Z2 AE 32%, Z3 Tempo 52%, Z4 Threshold 15%. Normalized Power in Tempo zone, "well, well below my FTP."
- "I hate mass starts... pretty soon (um, less than a mile into the race) I was back of the pack... I couldn't burn all my matches right at the start."
- "By about 20 miles in, I was In. Fact. DFL."
- Took 60 minutes total at aid stations for resting and fueling across the 8:45 total time.
- "Around hour one I started sipping my Tailwind, cognizant of the fact I needed to be taking on the calories and hydration."
- Notes he "did better with eating necessary fuel than I often do on long rides."
(https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Aid Station Strategy:**
- **Jeffrey [ELITE]:** At bacon station (mile 49), took first bathroom break and got "cold Coke or Pepsi" at nearly each aid stop. Had "a couple shots of pickle juice for the cramps" and "handful of Swedish fish" at final aid station (mile 90). (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

- **Heidi [ELITE]:** "I blew past" the beer and bacon aid station around mile 30-something of the 40-mile course. (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Course Cutoff Times:**
- Multiple sources confirm there are cutoff times at designated aid stations for rider safety. Jeffrey [ELITE] notes he arrived at the bacon aid station (mile 90 of 100) "About 1.5 hours before the DNF cutoff at that station." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Mechanical Issues:**
- **Sam (teammate of Heidi) [LEVEL UNKNOWN]:** Got 3rd in his age category "even after two flats" on the 40-mile course. (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Weather-Related Cancellations:**
- **2022:** Single-track mountain section was eliminated due to 2.5 inches of rain and wet snow, forcing 100-milers to do two loops of the main course instead. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Cramping:**
- **Jeffrey [ELITE]:** Experienced cramps on the penultimate climb, walked "about 10 feet" before "realizing riding staved off the cramps more than walking." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Sweeper Truck:**
- A "race support" sweeper truck follows the last rider to enforce cutoff times and provide sag wagon service for those who don't make cutoffs. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Mass Start:**
- **Heidi [ELITE]:** "I hate mass starts, and I hate them even more in a crowd mostly filled with people that have never road raced or ridden in a peloton. The incredibly short neutral roll out took place, and then bam!" Start featured 330+ racers in the 40-mile distance alone (2019). (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Aid Stations:**
- **Jeffrey [ELITE]:** "Fun socializing with aid station volunteers... The vols... were super supportive, all cheers and cowbells and offers to feed me." At the second visit to the bacon station, volunteers "got all whoop-y and jumpy" when they realized he was attempting the full 100. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

- **Anonymous [UNKNOWN]:** "The aid stations were positioned about every 10-12 miles or so. The second aid station offered pickle juice shots, and the third aid station offered bacon and PBR." Describes them as "very well stocked." (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

**Finish Line:**
- **Heidi [ELITE]:** "Beer at the finish line!!" Received trophy mug for podium finish. (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

- **Jeffrey [ELITE]:** "After crossing the finish line, racers were greeted by cheering crowds, a band in the amphitheater, and the smell of delicious barbecue... the free race beer at the finish party tasted pretty good." He "went under the finish banner just as they were getting the afternoon podium awards going." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jeffreys-story-dead-swede-2022)

**Course Support:**
- **Anonymous [UNKNOWN]:** "The organizers clearly communicated that cars shouldn't be on the course providing services to riders — that's what the aid stations were for. This was for rider safety, and to minimize the dust in the air. I thought this was brilliant and would love for other gravel events to take a similar stance." (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

**Women's Field:**
- **Heidi [ELITE]:** Notes the event "has crazy huge women's fields!" (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)

**Event Organization:**
- **Anonymous [UNKNOWN]:** "Overall, it was a very well marked course, communications were optimized, and the process from registration to packet pickup to post meal collection was seamless. Registration fees included a very cool Dead Swede t-shirts, sticker, and pint glass. I highly recommend this event!" Notes that packet pickup was day-before only, "which pretty much requires that participants book some form of lodging for one night." (https://2wheeledinspiration.com/2024/06/19/the-dead-swede-a-race-review/)

**Event Philosophy:**
- **Heidi [ELITE]:** "So yeah... I like this gravel stuff. It's a whole new different style of racing. It has some roadie tactics without the 15-20 hour a week training commitment Colorado women's cat 3 road racing seems to command just to not get dropped in the first fifteen seconds. It has a need of skills that crosses over from cyclocross and mountain biking. It has crazy awesome courses on little travel roads." (https://heidiridesbikes.com/2019/06/09/race-report-the-dead-swede/)
