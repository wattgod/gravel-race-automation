## Weather Experienced

**2021:** "Year one had near-freezing conditions with bouts of rain and sleet-like scuds." (https://theradavist.com/australian-gravel-national-championships)

**2022:** "Year two served up near gale-force winds." (https://theradavist.com/australian-gravel-national-championships)

**2023:** "A night of thunder and rain precedes the race, as we gather in the chilly predawn to sign in." Intermittent showers and downpours throughout. "Sliding out of the mountain mist we are greeted with a break in the weather and a blue bell sky." The torrential rain "muddied the course" significantly. (https://theradavist.com/australian-gravel-national-championships, https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**2024:** "It was icy cold with frost settling on the trees" at the start. "Derby looked like it had a thick blanket of fog thrown over it." However, it was "a record breaking super fast race" with no torrential rain like 2023. The event took place in "sunny conditions although it was still a cool and misty start." (https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/, https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/)

**2025:** Race day was May 10, 2025 with over 360 riders. Conditions were "sunny" though it was "a cool and misty start for the event in the southernmost state of Australia." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:** "87% gravel, 2,300m of climbing" over 106km long course. "The gravel isn't what riders would normally call 'champagne gravel' which is usually smooth 'road like' gravel. The gravel found in Derby is more like a rocky mountain bike trail in most parts." (https://burgh.cc/gravel-goes-global-the-devils-cardigans-uci-debut-in-derby-tasmania/, https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**Three Major Climbs:**
- **Mutual Valley:** 8.3km at 4.2% average gradient (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/)
- **Ralph Falls:** 10.7km at 5% average, with maximum gradient of 16% (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/)
- **"Un-named Climb of Hell":** 3.5km at 8.1% average, "but has a descent early in the climb so actually runs at 9% through most of the climb and has a maximum gradient of 15%." Also described as "Unnamed Climb of Hell (frightening no?)" (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/, https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**Key Sections:** "There is also another small but, with around 100km of racing in the legs, challenging kicker within the final 10km." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/)

**Dead Horse Hill Descent:** "The first of the big descents was called Dead Horse Hill Descent which goes straight into the first feed station. This became a pivotal point of the race because it was where the remaining elite men's field became strung out forming several smaller groups." It was causing "some pre-race favorites to puncture over the rough and rocky terrain." (https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**Single Track Section:** "The previous days of heavy rain left its mark on the single-track portion of the route and turned it into one steady trickling creek." (https://theradavist.com/australian-gravel-national-championships)

**Final Section:** "One final challenge is the final road, bordering on a country path, which greets us, our tired bones and grinding gears over, providing the last splatter of mud over bikes and bodies to the finish." (https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/tassie-gravel-the-devils-cardigan/)

**Road Surfaces:** 13% bitumen over four short sections, rest is various grades of gravel, logging roads, and fire roads. The course has "just 13% paved roads." (https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/tassie-gravel-the-devils-cardigan/, https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**2025 Elite Men's Race [ELITE LEVEL]:**
- "The elite men's race consisted of 47 quality riders from all over Australia. The men started fast and a large group of riders formed on the first climb up Mutual Road. The pace was fast, pushing well into threshold territory for the first 15 minutes."
- Brendan Johnston attacked on Ralph Falls Climb "approximately 35km into the race" and "extended this gap into a sizeable lead of 1:15 minutes at the top of the final climb where he rode the final 40kms into Branxholm" to win in 3:24:12.
- "A group of 10-15 elite men formed on the roads before the second climb" and "the group halved in the first 5 mins as the two Marks put the pressure on."
(https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**2025 Elite Women's Race [ELITE LEVEL]:**
- "The women's elite field had a separate start to the men's race and they set a solid tempo up the first climb, Mutual Valley."
- "The group of 13 ladies in the women's elite field dwindled down to just four by the top of Mutual Valley."
- Tiffany Cromwell had a puncture "around half way through" while racing with Talia Appleton.
- Winner Talia Appleton finished in 4:03:15.
(https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**Scott Mattern's XL Cardigan Strategy [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
- Started at 4 a.m. to complete 50km warm-up lap before 8 a.m. long course start
- "Fifty kilometers down and 100 to go; what started as short and tight was soon to be long and loose. With a brief window, I grabbed a warm drink and a second breakfast before the final lap."
(https://theradavist.com/australian-gravel-national-championships)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanicals:** Multiple elite riders experienced punctures during the 2024 and 2025 races. "It was even causing some pre-race favorites to puncture over the rough and rocky terrain" on Dead Horse Hill Descent. Tiffany Cromwell had "a mid-race puncture" and Cassia Boglio "punctured on the rough rocky terrain" of Dead Horse Hill Descent. (https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/, https://burgh.cc/gravel-goes-global-the-devils-cardigans-uci-debut-in-derby-tasmania/)

**Course Difficulty:** "The Devils Cardigan is no roll around the park." 2022 champion Tasman Nankervis described it as "a course built for the brave." (https://www.devilscardigan.com.au/race-reports, https://burgh.cc/gravel-goes-global-the-devils-cardigans-uci-debut-in-derby-tasmania/)

**Weather:** Pre-race discussions included concerns about "snow above 800m" and challenging conditions. The combination of cold, rain, and technical terrain creates compounding difficulties. (https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-Race:** "Over the line, a cold craft beer and a moment to sit in the winter sun. A shower, bike clean, hot local pub dinner and the final results await." Winners receive "a sack of potatoes and handmade wooly cardigan winners trophy to keep the celebrating riders warm at the pub in Derby on Saturday evening." After the race, "everyone flocked to the Dorsett Hotel in Derby for a well-deserved and eagerly anticipated banger of a dinner, chat with mates and another beer (or 3) by the fire." (https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/tassie-gravel-the-devils-cardigan/, https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/the-devils-cardigan/results/, https://www.ambmag.com.au/feature/2024-devils-cardigan-gravel-national-609264/)

**Local Community Support:** "The North East Lions Club! Each year this passionate group of locals come together and cook an incredible amount of soup and food to nourish your post race depleted souls! This year it was 145 Litres of Soup complimented by the tastiest sausages this side of the sideling." A portion of race entries goes to the Lions Club. "No race or event can be organised without the help of an army of volunteers." POC Sports provided hoodies to keep volunteers warm. (https://www.devilscardigan.com.au/2023-race-recap-1)

**Vibe:** "There is an undoubted community of gravel riding which, I think, in part creates the attraction to this style of riding." "There is a rare bond formed in shared experiences—make no mistake, this ride is an experience." "It wasn't just a race – it was a celebration of resilience, community and adventure. From elite riders to first-time gravel grinders, the spirit was unmistakable: challenge accepted, and conquered." (https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/tassie-gravel-the-devils-cardigan/, https://burgh.cc/gravel-goes-global-the-devils-cardigans-uci-debut-in-derby-tasmania/)

**Event Photography:** Two world-class cycling photographers document the race: Kristina Vackova (KiPhotoMedia) and Beardy McBeard. "It was a real privelge to have their artistic talent and energy on course." (https://www.devilscardigan.com.au/2023-race-recap-1)

**Special Events:** In 2025, Burgh Cycling sponsored "a brilliant Q&A session featuring Tiffany Cromwell, Mark O'Brien and Grace Brown. Their candid conversation on gravel racing, mindset and progression in cycling brought extra heart to the festival atmosphere." Olympic Champion Grace Brown served as event ambassador. (https://burgh.cc/gravel-goes-global-the-devils-cardigans-uci-debut-in-derby-tasmania/, https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/26796/)

**Unique Atmosphere:** Derby was "jam-packed with riders telling their mates about what happened, like stories of flat tyres, aching sore bodies, lost water bottles and nasty pinchy climbs that lasted forever."
