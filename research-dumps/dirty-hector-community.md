## Race Overview & Basic Facts

The Dirty Hector is a gravel race held in Hector, NY, near Trumansburg. The course is approximately 40 miles with around 3,500-3,700 feet of elevation gain, starting and finishing at Silver Queen Farm. Mass start at 11am with all categories starting together. One aid station at approximately the halfway point with water and electrolyte/race mix available.

**Course Character:** This is explicitly NOT a gravel "road race" - organizers describe it as "gravel meets cyclocross with lots of hills." The course includes technical trail, mud, grass, water crossings, and fence crossings in addition to dirt roads. The route winds through cow fields, creeks, forests, and trails in the Finger Lakes National Forest.

The 2025 edition served as the NYS Gravel State Championship race with 131 finishers.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The organizers emphasize this is a challenging, technical course: "This race is CHALLENGING and FUN!! It may be MUDDY, it will be HARD, it is BEAUTIFUL and will take GRIT to get through." (https://cycle-cny.com/event/dirty-hector/)

The 2025 race conditions were particularly difficult due to weeks of wet weather leading to "extremely muddy" conditions with "rutted back roads and technical singletrack." (https://nysbra.com/2025/06/30/gravel-champions-dirty-hector-2025/)

Course includes: cow fields, creeks, mud, forests, technical trail sections, water crossings, fence crossings, and lots of climbing. GPS and downloaded route "HIGHLY recommended due to poor reception throughout the route." Roads are open with marshals at busier intersections. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12417/dirty-hector)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Performance [ELITE]:** Jack Bernhard's winning time of 2:17:07 represents an extremely strong performance, averaging approximately 17.5 mph over technical terrain with 3,700 feet of climbing. His cyclocross background was noted as key to his success on the technical sections. The front group raced together through much of the course before Bernhard soloed to victory.

**Mid-Pack Times [COMPETITIVE]:** Masters category winners finished in the 2:22-2:40 range for men, suggesting strong recreational to competitive-level riders should plan for 2.5-3+ hours depending on conditions.

**Recreational Pace [RECREATIONAL]:** Finish times extending to 4+ hours indicate the technical nature and difficulty of the course, particularly in muddy conditions. Riders should be prepared for a significantly slower pace than typical gravel road races due to technical sections.

## Course Information & Logistics

- Start/Finish: Silver Queen Farm, 5286 Stillwell Rd, Trumansburg, NY 14886
- Mass start: 11:00 AM
- One aid station at approximately mile 20 with water and Flow Formulas electrolyte/race mix
- Course marking: Signage provided but GPS/downloaded route highly recommended
- Cell reception: Poor throughout most of the route
- Roads: Open to traffic with marshals at busy intersections
- Prizes: 3-deep for each category
