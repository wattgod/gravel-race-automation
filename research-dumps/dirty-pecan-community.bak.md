## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Red clay roads:** "Soon the whole gaggle was gently escorted down a few miles of traffic free paved roads. Fifteen minutes later the pack swarmed onto the first red clay road and the pace picked up. Then it picked up some more. Then the hills got longer and steeper. Then the red clay became sticky and criss crossed by washouts." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Surface composition:** "Along the way we had a few stretches of quiet pavement but mostly it was packed sand" on Day 2 of the 2025 ride (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/04/dirty-pecan-day-2-monticello-florida/)

**Sand sections:** "At some point in these middle wastelands of effort...it seemed like 12 miles of slippery, dead flat wet sand. Like Sidewinder snake tracks we could read the desperate flailings of those ahead seeking hard soil. No magic here, just keep up an adequate terra-planing speed to survive. Every stroke was an effort. Drafting an inch off was still virtually useless" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Mixed surfaces:** "Random red mud spot" followed by "The last 15 miles was paved but back into a stiff head wind" on the 80-mile route (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/04/dirty-pecan-day-2-monticello-florida/)

**Scenery:** "Mile after mile we rolled up and down rusty red clay hills through countless bucolic mossy oak tunnels – occasionally breaking into green meadows and gorgeous historic farmsteads. Virtually no traffic. So much openness and so few people. So quiet you could hear a cotton boll drop." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Final miles:** "And then the hills began… scores of hills" in the final 5-10 miles on pavement (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early pace warning [UNKNOWN level]:** "Guys?" I yelled, as I puffed chasing up a clay hill. "I'm pretty sure this pace isn't sustainable for another 140 miles." The 150-mile riders found the early pace unsustainable after ~10 miles. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Nutrition [UNKNOWN level]:** "Mrs. K-Dogg (a scientifical person) advised me to 'consume 200 kcal/hour or a big lunch or suffer the consequences.'" K-Dogg ate Clif Bars plus "Coke and cookies" at the convenience store stop, later used "Double Espresso Gu" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Ibuprofen [UNKNOWN level]:** "Note to self… Ibuprofen is the fourth food group" — K-Dogg noted this helped him feel better in the later miles (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Wrong turn advantage [UNKNOWN level]:** "One of our dudes made a wrong turn. At that point, I was happy to turn off the gas and turn on the camera" — taking a break when someone went off-course allowed recovery (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/04/dirty-pecan-day-2-monticello-florida/)

**Route selection [UNKNOWN level]:** "I was perfectly fine just doing 80 so we split" — Dave chose the 80-mile option knowing he'd likely be dropped by the 100-mile group (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/04/dirty-pecan-day-2-monticello-florida/)

**Teamwork in headwind:** "I eventually met up with another 80 miler and we swapped pulls back to camp" for the final 15 paved miles into a headwind (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/04/dirty-pecan-day-2-monticello-florida/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Bonking:** "With five miles to go I drifted off the back on one particularly long hill. A black curtain began to lower as an extreme bonk set in" — K-Dogg nearly DNF'd at mile 145 of the 150-mile route (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Mechanical/crashes:** "Pfaff Daddy tried to change lanes through a 12″ high crusty clay ridge and bobble-swept Dr. Pain's front wheel out from under him" in red clay bottom land. "Dr. Pain's rear derailleur and white arm covers looked the the losing side of a Cheese Wiz fight but otherwise the bikes were fine." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Sand sections:** The 12-mile flat wet sand section appeared to be a major attrition point: "Every stroke was an effort. Drafting an inch off was still virtually useless" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

**Rider implosion:** "The Aussie goes down under. JOM finally imploded on a punchy little hill and vanished backwards around a corner" after staying on the front too long (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/ride-report-dirty-pecan-150-mile-dirt-road-epic-what-were-we-thinking-by-k-dogg/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Participation growth:** "Now in its 10th year, Dirty Pecan has more than doubled its attendance and is getting close to $200,000 raised for 4-H" with "Over 400 cyclists" participating in 2025 (https://www.wtxl.com/news/local-news/in-your-neighborhood/monticello/pedaling-for-a-purpose-at-dirty-pecan-gravel-ride-in-monticello)

**National draw:** "Cyclists come from all over the country to take part in the two-day event" — Sarah Carothers and Randy Honcik drove over 400 miles from Sylva, North Carolina (https://www.wtxl.com/news/local-news/in-your-neighborhood/monticello/pedaling-for-a-purpose-at-dirty-pecan-gravel-ride-in-monticello)

**Registration cost:** "Registration is only $25 - yes, you read that right!" (https://www.dirtypecan.com/)

**Charitable mission:** "Your participation in the Dirty Pecan Gravel Ride directly contributes to supporting 4-H kids in our community" with proceeds funding "overnight camps where kids can develop important leadership skills" and nearly $30,000 donated in 2025 (https://www.wtxl.com/news/local-news/in-your-neighborhood/monticello/pedaling-for-a-purpose-at-dirty-pecan-gravel-ride-in-monticello)

**Unsupported nature:** "Remember, this is an UNSUPPORTED ride (not a race): there is NO support, NO sag stops, NOR support vehicles. The course will NOT be marked, and there will be NO printouts prior to the ride. YOU are 100% responsible for YOU, YOUR welfare, YOUR equipment, YOUR navigation, and YOUR own food and drink. If you need a juice box and a hug every 20 miles, this is not the ride for you, please stay home." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5234/dirty-pecan)

**Post-ride hospitality:** "The Blackwater Bros invited me back to their rental house to have dinner and camp in the driveway. The post ride activities where just what you might expect, theraguns, air legs, talk of the day's ride with occasional bad smells." (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/04/dirty-pecan-day-2-monticello-florida/)

**Event format:** "This one-day event is perfect for all adventure seekers out there. With 75% gravel routes, you can ride at your own pace and enjoy the scenic views." Multiple distance options: 40, 60, 80, 100, 150, 200 miles (https://www.dirtypecan.com/ and https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5234/dirty-pecan)

**Local character:** Routes take riders "through bike trails, pecan orchards, and canopy paths" in Jefferson County, the "Pecan Capital of the World" (https://www.wtxl.com/news/local-news/in-your-neighborhood/monticello/pedaling-for-a-purpose-at-dirty-pecan-gravel-ride-in-monticello)

**Venue:** Start located at "2729 West Washington Street, Monticello, FL 32344" at the "University of Florida extension service area" with on-site camping, observatory, and port-o-lets (https://davefaitlemonde.com/2025/03/03/dirty-pecan-day-1-monticello-florida/ and https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5234/dirty-pecan)

**Limited amenities:** "A juice box and a hug... ;-) This is a YOYO ride. We do not provide maps or markers. No rest stops. It is you, the bike and this beautiful part of north Florida." Though they do provide "free parking and port-o-lets at ride start" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5234/dirty-pecan)
