# DIRTY REIVER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Rob Weaver [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's one thing to rip along the road in a tightly packed group with your mates, but riding in a bunch on a slippery, unpredictable surface at speed with a bunch of strangers is, at times, terrifying... With mud, gravel and slippery embedded rock under your tyres, you find your face being pummelled with dirt from the rear tyre of the rider in front (it was very wet during this year's edition)." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "The hills were literally relentless, not so much steep on the whole but sooo long and they just kept on coming, we did the 130km and it was over 8000 feet of climb and the descents were brutal too, rattling about on rough tracks... Some of the gravel was very rocky and you had to follow a narrow 'path' between the rocks and there was a big boulder field which was not much fun, to be honest." (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "I was happy to be at the event at all in truth as we thought we might have to cancel as my back went into spasm 3 days before the event and I had been in agony and hadn't even been on a bike. I had to ask Clive to stop at least 4x mid-ride to massage my back to relieve the pain and for me to take more ibuprofen." (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "It was a little intimidating (for me) at the start I confess as I have unlike Clive, not 'raced' and I didn't like the crowds with all the riders packed in and setting off all together behind the car (neutralisation zone) and I was worried someone might bump my bike... Also initially it was really packed on the trails which I didn't really enjoy if I'm honest but after a time it all shook out and settled down." (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Rob Weaver [COMPETITIVE]:** "At about the halfway point, around 2 hours 30 minutes in, the wind got up and it rained quite heavily. To say I was pleased with my over-the-top choice of layers would be an understatement. While others shivered, I continued to stay warm and dry." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "Unless you are in the leading group of riders the segment to the first feed station is spent jostling for space as people get into their rhythm. This can be fun but also frustrating as I had to excuse my way past many riders on climbs only to later be passed on the descents just to repeat the process again." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "18 minutes in one of my water bottles fires out onto the floor on a rough descent, the speed is too high to stop with a group of people behind me... (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** "After some big efforts to keep the pace high I find out that we are the leaders so all I've been doing is narrowing down the lead group which is now maybe 10 riders... I was shivering on the start line it was so cold, and was pretty warm for the first 20 minutes but once it started raining I was the perfect temperature." **Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** "One bottle, another 2 hours till the next feed zone. This is suboptimal! As a coach I plan out people's nutrition strategies for races on a regular basis. Today would be a long way from the strategy but at least I have bars and gels in my back pockets. Those two bottles I lost though had 240 grams of carbohydrate from the 560 gram total I'd planned on consuming." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/dirty-reiver-an-introduction-to-gravel)

**Ben Thomas [ELITE]:** "With about 50 kilometres to go Micky stopped with a slow puncture, the group was then just three. A few kilometres later Nico dropped off the back after a steep descent having punctured. It was then just Ingvar and I at the front... In the end we rolled towards the castle climb together. I already knew this was going to be a tough but gave it everything, 20 seconds at 790 watts with a max power of over 1100 watts. This was post 6 hours 34 and at almost 6500 kilojoules!" If you dig in too deep in a 7.5 hour race, then you're going to pay the consequences. Getting dropped probably did me a favour as I could settle into my own rhythm, and I caught several riders back up." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/dirty-reiver-take-10-with-Josh-Ibbett)

**Josh Ibbett [COMPETITIVE]:** "The conditions can vary. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/dirty-reiver-take-10-with-Josh-Ibbett)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tires - Rob Weaver [COMPETITIVE]:** "I ended up wearing a baselayer, Windstopper jersey, fleece-lined gilet and Windstopper jacket for the full 130km ride. In similar conditions on a mountain bike, I'd probably wear a baselayer and Windstopper jersey, only pulling out a jacket if the weather really turned." Tire choice: "While gravel bikes are spectacular bits of kit, many of them (especially their gravel tyres) are relatively fragile compared to modern mountain bikes." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**Tires - Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "I was very happy with my tyre choice of Terravail Rutland - Clive also went for the same though he said he would have used the WTB Ventures ideally with it being dry but I was very happy with my tyres. We saw literally hundreds of people with punctures, particularly after the big descents." (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Bike Setup - MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** Fixed gear setup: "I was back on my trusty Surly Steamroller but this time it featured a couple of key upgrades over last years setup. A carbon fork and front disk brake lightened the ride and offered plenty of braking power when required. I ran 40c WTB Nano's this year. These worked well with no issues in regards to grip or rolling resistance. I did find the ride harsher than last year although this might be down to other factors. If I was to choose I would probably opt for Panaracer Gravel King SK's in a 35c/40c size." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Bottle Cages - MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "18 minutes in one of my water bottles fires out onto the floor on a rough descent... Another 4 minutes down the track and the next bottle fires itself out of the race... Lesson 2, get decent bottle cages. I usually run Mount Zoom cages but this is the one bike without them." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Bike Setup - Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was riding a 46 tooth Rotor Q ring with a 10-44t cassette, this was fine for the rolling hills." Tire pressure: "On those wheels I ran Schwalbe G One R's in 45c width at 30 psi with tyre inserts front and rear." Wheels: "The Strada Gravel Ultra Plus wheels which providing an aero setup for the fast gravel roads of Kielder Forest." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/dirty-reiver-an-introduction-to-gravel)

**Bike Setup - Ben Thomas [ELITE]:** "The Lauf Seigla with the Smoothie handlebar and Grit fork with its 30mm of travel was incredible once again. The bike gave incredible confidence on the descents, along the rougher gravel sections and through any muddier sections... I finished the race in a faster time and feeling a lot fresher than I would have done on another bike with a rigid fork." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-really-dirty-reiver)

**Bike Setup - Josh Ibbett [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'd have to say my bike. I'm a big fan of the Shimano GRX Di2 2x groupset because you really need those big gears. I think my top speed on one of the descents was something mad like 90 km/h (56 mph) as I was chasing to get back onto the group... Make sure you've got fast-rolling tyres—you don't need much tread at all—and probably a 42 or 45 mm width depending on how much comfort you demand." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/dirty-reiver-take-10-with-Josh-Ibbett)

**Essential Spares - Rob Weaver [COMPETITIVE]:** "Carry the essentials, but be sure to pack extra tyre plugs, CO2 canisters (and a pump), tubes and a decent tyre boot, along with the usual kit... The number of people stopped by the side of the fire road fixing punctures was staggering. I couldn't believe how many people I saw with wheels out and tubeless sealant everywhere, frantically pumping away in the hope they'd soon be back on their bike." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**Clothing - Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** "I raced in waterproof overshoes, thick socks, thick Kalas RainMem leg warmers which are water and windproof, thick Kalas Shark water and windproof bibshorts and softshell long sleeve jacket, a warm base layer, insulated gloves, a neck warmer and winter cap. I carried my Kalas eVent waterproof jacket but despite the persistent rain actually stayed dry enough thanks to everything else I was wearing." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-really-dirty-reiver)

**Single Speed Setup - Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "I'd be riding my Surly Steamroller, with a few key changes to make it a more suitable for the rough surfaces and extended climbs... a change from drops to flat handlebars, a new set of wheels, with Halo Evaura rims, Halo rear hub and a dynamo front hub, wrapped with a set of Schwalbe G One Allroads in 38c... And a change to a 38 – 19 gear set-up (2:1)." (https://grvlcc.wordpress.com/2019/04/18/thedirtyreiver2019/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Characteristics - Rob Weaver [COMPETITIVE]:** "Typical Kielder forest roads were a narrow two track with fist or larger size rocks embedded in them. They were less like Midwest and Central Plains hero gravel and more like Rocky Mountain forest roads. The whole route reminded me of riding in parts of northern Montana, particularly the Swan River Valley section of the Great Divide Route." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**Rocky Sections - Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "Some of the gravel was very rocky and you had to follow a narrow 'path' between the rocks and there was a big boulder field which was not much fun, to be honest. I didn't want to batter my bike so I (we) went carefully through any rough bits, I chose my lines really carefully too, as I love my Bokeh too much!" (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Climbing Profile - Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "The hills were literally relentless, not so much steep on the whole but sooo long and they just kept on coming, we did the 130km and it was over 8000 feet of climb and the descents were brutal too, rattling about on rough tracks." (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Lakeside Section - Tiggy [UNKNOWN]:** "Particularly nice was around the lake which looked like a rough sea in the wind but was so pretty. The trails around there were much gentler too! It's a lovely isolated and very wild part of the world and there is not even any phone signal or wifi." (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**Stage 2 Description - MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 2 spared no prisoners. Straight into a climb that hoisted us up to 1500ft and the highest point in the course only to descend straight down the other side... However 13 miles down the road there was a nasty surprise, a short climb barely a mile long but so steep that we struggled to keep moving. Each pedal stroke felt like the last." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Twisty Shore Path - MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "The course left the road and now followed a twisty shore path that ducked and weaved through the trees. The twisty corners were a lot of fun but as riders bunched up on the narrow path it was difficult to overtake and progress was hampered by tired riders ahead." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Stage 3 Exposure - MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's hard to quantify but Stage 3 seems to be the hardest... This year the route took us South of Kielder Water and into an exposed landscape. Wind whipped across the land forcing riders to fight to keep moving forwards, hunched low over their handle bars with no where to hide." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Lauf Special Stage - MOSTLY BY BIKE author [COMPETITIVE]:** "This year there was another update to the Dirty Reiver in the form of a special timed stage sponsored by Lauf. A cruel 8km segment that featured some of the roughest terrain of the whole course... Tired legs turned reluctant cranks as the loose gravel surface tried to break traction and rhythm." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**Surface Variety - Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** "Surface varies from smooth gravel, to very loose rocks (often on corners), to big rough arm pumping rocky descents. I was riding the new Schwalbe G One R tyres... The rubber is soft giving lots of grip and the tread rolls fast and offers predictable traction. I had enough confidence to rail the corners which I didn't expect to be the case this kind of surface on this bike." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/dirty-reiver-an-introduction-to-gravel)

**The Bombtrack Climb - Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "The Bombtrack Climb... Now, this still sticks in my head vividly... a short climb barely a mile long but so steep that we struggled to keep moving. Each pedal stroke felt like the last as thoughts of dismounting and pushing ran through my head... I have never, in my life been so happy to see a fully grown man in a costume, ringing a giant bell." (https://grvlcc.wordpress.com/2019/04/18/thedirtyreiver2019/)

**Single Track Section - Liam [UNKNOWN]:** "This continued for a few mile's even tightening into a pretty fun section of single-track weaving between the tree's, undulating up and down making my sharp handling Steamroller feel very at home, and was one of the more enjoyable parts of the day." (https://us.alpkit.com/blogs/deeds/liams-dirty-one-thirty)

**Final Section - Ben Thomas [ELITE]:** "We keep ticking off the kilometers, the wheels keep turning... The terrain is breaking people now, each climb sees someone drop from the group... I'm not sure if we could have gone much quicker but I'm content to ride to the line. Plus, as we enter 20km, 15km, 10km, the route gets busier and busier with other trail users." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-really-dirty-reiver)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[COMPETITIVE] Starting Position - Rob Weaver:** "I line up near the front eager not to get caught up in the inevitable carnage further back in the pack... Some bikes had almost bike packing size bags strapped to them carrying the minimum kit required by the organiser, not sure what else they were carrying, maybe a pizza in case they couldn't reach a feed zone or a cuddly toy in case the whole thing just got too much!? Others were running much more aero lightweight setups like me." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**[COMPETITIVE] Group Riding Strategy - Rob Weaver:** "Finding a group of riders you can tag along with helps with not only your pace, but morale, too. These are your Guardians of the Gravelty... Etiquette when riding in a group is important. Following another rider offers shelter from the wind and helps you to save energy, but be prepared to take your turn on the front when it comes." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)

**[UNKNOWN] Conservative Pacing - Tiggy:** "We stopped to take quite a few photos as it was really just so beautiful there and seemed a shame not to!... I didn't do very much chatting!! The atmosphere was really amazing with people cheering and ringing cowbells as we passed which was lovely! It really gave such a boost!" (https://masoncycles.cc/blog/dirtyreiveride)

**[COMPETITIVE] Early Pace Setting - MOSTLY BY BIKE author:** "The legs are feeling sprightly so I set the pace on the opening climbs, the pace begins to create a lead group... After some big efforts to keep the pace high I find out that we are the leaders so all I've been doing is narrowing down the lead group which is now maybe 10 riders." (https://mostlybybike.com/2019/06/03/the-dirty-reiver-2019/)

**[COMPETITIVE] Chasing Back - Ben Thomas:** "I had to stop at feed zone two to refill my two bottles. I was only stopped for 35 seconds but I had to work hard to catch back up. 6 minutes 30 at 403 watts normalized, which is just below threshold, got me back to lead group. This was the hardest 6 to 10 minutes of the race. This effort happened after 3 hours 19 and 3300 kilojoules of work." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/dirty-reiver-an-introduction-to-gravel)

**[COMPETITIVE] Hydration Planning - Ben Thomas:** "Two people started with two bottles and a hydration pack which would take them through the whole distance. This was my original plan but talked myself out of it as I didn't want to carry the extra weight... In a normal situation I'd be aiming for 80 grams of carbs per hour." Actual intake: "Total carb intake was 620 grams, 95 grams of carbs per hour." Total fluids: "3 litres of energy drink for 6.5 hours was minimal even for these chilly temperatures and I was feeling pretty thirsty by the finish line." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/dirty-reiver-an-introduction-to-gravel)

**[ELITE] Late Race Tactics - Ben Thomas:** "I tried hard on the last 19 kilometres to get rid of Ingvar but along the path next to the Kielder Water it was impossible to open a gap, he was riding tactically and was glued to my wheel. On any short rise I would put in an acceleration but the climbs were all sub 30 seconds but this short sharp effort is my weakness and suited Ingvar far better as he does a lot more cross country racing than I do. I did my best 30 second power of the day, 654 watts, after 6 hours 29 in one last ditch effort to open a gap." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-really-dirty-reiver)

**[ELITE] Final Sprint - Ben Thomas:** "In the end we rolled towards the castle climb together. I already knew this was going to be a tough but gave it everything, 20 seconds at 790 watts with a max power of over 1100 watts. This was post 6 hours 34 and at almost 6500 kilojoules!" (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-really-dirty-reiver)

**[COMPETITIVE] When Dropped Strategy - Josh Ibbett:** "When I got dropped from the lead group, I knew I wasn't the only one that was suffering - I was just the first to crack. I made a conscious decision to keep on the pedals, ride my own pace and have confidence that I could reel them back in. If you dig in too deep in a 7.5 hour race, then you're going to pay the consequences. Getting dropped probably did me a favour as I could settle into my own rhythm, and I caught several riders back up. You can't overdo it in a long race." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/dirty-reiver-take-10-with-Josh-Ibbett)

**[COMPETITIVE] Breaking It Down - Josh Ibbett:** "My advice is to divide it into small sections. The good thing about the Dirty Reiver is the number of feed stations—you can break the race down into each feed section and tick off the climbs within each part. I have the route on my bike computer, showing the elevation, and I tick off the climbs so there's always a sense of achievement and I use the feed stations as waymarkers along the way." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/dirty-reiver-take-10-with-Josh-Ibbett)

**[RECREATIONAL] Late Field Advantage - Cycling Weekly author:** "The bunch spreads out quite quickly, so you could always hang back at the start or even pick a later start time when the bulk of people have set off. After the first climb, the field will have split up, so you don't have to be surrounded by people if you prefer to ride in peace." (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/routes/7-tips-to-nail-your-first-gravel-event-taking-on-britains-dirty-reiver-challenge)

**[UNKNOWN] Single Speed Pacing - Anonymous rider:** "For the first 15 or so miles the going was good. Loads of rolling hills, with most of the climbs and descents being reasonable and not giving us too much grief, after the 20 mile mark we got onto the the first of the longer climbs, with a few bits that pushed my legs and gearing for the first time of the day." (https://grvlcc.wordpress.com/2019/04/18/thedirtyreiver2019/)

**[UNKNOWN] Managing Cramps - Anonymous rider:** "Duncan had suffered with a really bad cramp, again and there was a possibility that he would need to be taken back in one of the Bomb track vans... another chap had turned up and give me the gift I didn't know I needed, a pickle... Apparently good for cramping." (https://grvlcc.wordpress.com/2019/04/18/thedirtyreiver2019/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Failures - Rob Weaver [COMPETITIVE]:** "While the best gravel bikes are spectacular bits of kit, many of them (especially their gravel tyres) are relatively fragile compared to modern mountain bikes. As a result, pummel one over rough ground for five to eight hours and you'll more than likely encounter some kind of mechanical hiccup... The number of people stopped by the side of the fire road fixing punctures was staggering. I couldn't believe how many people I saw with wheels out and tubeless sealant everywhere, frantically pumping away in the hope they'd soon be back on their bike." (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/opinion/five-things-i-learnt-riding-dirty-reiver)
