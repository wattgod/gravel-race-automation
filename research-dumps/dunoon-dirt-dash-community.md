## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**James [UNKNOWN]:** Rode a Sonder Broken Road Pinion bike, which he "found perfect for the trip." (https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

**Brett Cotter [UNKNOWN]:** Used a Deviate Highlander II mountain bike as a loaner. Other riders commented: "We know who's going to be the fastest downhill," with jealous eyes on the Deviate Highlander II. (https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

**Minimum tire size requirement:** 45mm minimum recommended tire size for mountain bikes & gravel bikes, which must be in good working order. E-bikes are allowed. (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Mandatory kit list:** Helmet, front and rear lights, sufficient water, emergency blanket or sleeping bag, waterproof jacket, first aid kit, phone or GPS for navigation, multitool, spare tube, tire levers, puncture repair kit, tent/bivvy bag/tarp. (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Brett Cotter [UNKNOWN]:** Purchased "a handy tick removing kit" and SMIDGE at the local supermarket in Dunoon before the event, specifically mentioning "the terror ticks I'd only heard about and yet to experience." (https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course profile [2024 edition]:** 137 kilometres with 2,350 metres of climbing and "gnarly descents" over two days. Day 1: 65km with ca. 1,300m of climbing; Day 2: 70km with ca. 1,000m of climbing. Some technical sections. (https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/ and https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**2023 route:** The 2023 event had a different route: 62km on Day 1 and 46km on Day 2. The route is "fine-tuned" each year. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-photo-essay-from-the-2023-dunoon-dirt-dash)

**Brett Cotter [UNKNOWN]:** "After a mass start, the participants slowly thinned out as we weaved our way up through the skinny streets of Dunoon to the hills and forests above the township, complete with outstanding views down over the peninsula." (https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event philosophy:** "It's not a bloody race" and "Don't be a dick" are the two things riders must know about the event. (https://mostlybybike.com/tag/dunoon/)

**Format:** Non-timed, self-supported gravel cycling event described as a "reliability trial" with the mantra "Stay cool, be nice, don't hurt yourself or others." (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Camping location:** Carrick Farm, situated right next to a Scottish sea loch and the historic 14th-century Carrick Castle. Dinner and breakfast provided by local catering provider Winston Churchill. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-photo-essay-from-the-2023-dunoon-dirt-dash and https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

**Participant diversity:** The 2023 event "attracted riders from across the UK and internationally, as far as the US, Canada and France." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-photo-essay-from-the-2023-dunoon-dirt-dash)

**Brett Cotter [UNKNOWN]:** "Everyone was stoked for the event, waiting to hear the introduction and induction from Markus of where we were going to be riding and what the two days held ahead. This is the third rendition of the DDD... There was plenty of good banter and amusement going around." (https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

**Charlie (co-organiser) [UNKNOWN]:** "Just like down in Dorset the vibes will be good, the food will be venison, and the beer will be very much deserved. Oh and we are camping next to a bloody castle!!!" (https://bikepacking.com/news/surly-dunoon-dirt-dash-gravel-event/)

**Registration:** Takes place Saturday morning from 7:30am-8:45am at Argyll Hotel in Dunoon. All riders MUST attend a rider briefing at 8:45am. Mass start at 9am. (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Special participants [2023]:** Para-cycling team members Steve Bate and Jon Gildea rode the first ever Dunoon Dirt Dash. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_dsE55VYQcg)

## Access & Logistics

**Public transport access:** Dunoon is "easily accessible by public transport from Glasgow." Ferry services run from Gourock to Dunoon. The organizers "highly encourage you to take the train or cycle to the event." (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/ and https://bikepackingscotland.com/dunoon/)

**Ferry options:** Two ferry services available:
1. Passenger ferry from Gourock to Dunoon Pier (Calmac - limited bike spaces)
2. Regular ferry service from Hunter's Quay to McInroy's Point (Western Ferries - takes cars and bikes)
(https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Train connection:** Train to Gourock connects with the ferry to Dunoon. Glasgow is the nearest major city. (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Navigation:** "The course is not marked, you are responsible for your own navigation." Route provided electronically via Komoot (official route sponsor). (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/ and https://bikepacking.com/news/2024-dunoon-dirt-dash-recap/)

## Environmental Requirements

**Tree disease prevention:** Riders must "wash bikes and shoes at the stations provided and clean all equipment when you get home" to help prevent the spread of "Phytophthora ramorum," a tree disease. (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Leave No Trace:** "Please don't drop litter!" and if not using public/campsite toilets: "use a trowel to bury your s** and stay well away from water for your comfort break." (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)

**Scottish Outdoor Access Code:** When cycling off-road, riders must "exercise due care to all other users, according to the Scottish Outdoor Access Code." (https://dirtdash.cc/dunoon/)
