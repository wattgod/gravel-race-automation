# EDITION ZERO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Ant [RECREATIONAL]:** "Strange things happen in one's mind when the distances are that long. Whilst the 70km to the first bag drop seemed to, and in fact did, take hours, once I reached the second and final bag drop, I felt that I was nearly home even though there was still 80km left to ride." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Ant [RECREATIONAL]:** "At 248km it was my longest bike ride ever, as well as my first gravel race in the spectacular and remote landscapes of the South Island." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Ant [RECREATIONAL]:** "I was almost begging Ant to agree that we should ride separately as I was reasonably confident that if I paced it well I could complete the course, and I knew that Ant was the stronger rider. He refused, saying he was unlikely to perform better than previously and that he would be happy to ride with me." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Zoe [RECREATIONAL]:** "Zoe was an enduro mountain-biker and had never done a gravel race… or a ride this long." She finished as 4th woman overall. (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**George Bennett [ELITE]:** "Not one of the stresses overlapped, compared to riding in Europe. I was worried about aid stations; I was worried about getting there in time for sign on. I don't know what you take on a gravel race. Do you take a pump, do you take tubes? What do you take?" (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**George Bennett [ELITE]:** "My biggest fear was getting a puncture early on and being stuck riding on my own. I was shitting it about riding 250 kilometres on gravel. No matter what, no matter how fit you are or where you're riding, being on a bike that long is hard." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**George Bennett [ELITE]:** "When we hit the first feed zone, I didn't realise the pace that we'd go through those at and I dropped into the third group coming out of it. After that the race was on." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Caleb Bottcher [ELITE]:** "My plan there was just to be dropping my Camelbak. I'd still have two bottles and full pockets. When I got there my mind blanked. I still had the bag clipped up and the hose strapped across me." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Caleb Bottcher [ELITE]:** "I started to really panic. This group was not working well together, and we were watching the gap just get bigger and bigger to the two groups in front. I thought I'd blown it." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Caleb Bottcher [ELITE]:** "In an eight hour race, there's going to be dark patches, and there's lots of time to think, so how you shape that has a huge impact. Bad things are going to happen in that time, so it's how you manage those moments and your reactions to them which is what matters." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Caleb Bottcher [ELITE]:** "George pulled a turn, I pulled a turn, and then no-one pulled through after me. I didn't want to look back, but I glanced under my shoulder and there was no-one there…I just thought, 'you guys are going to regret that', and started turning the screw." This attack came with 130 kilometres still to go. (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Caleb Bottcher [ELITE]:** "I'd run a couple of scenarios in my head before the race…I wasn't sure how it was going to play out, but I was open to going early. It wasn't Plan A, but if an opportunity arose, I wasn't going to turn it down. Morale was a bit low; no-one pulled a turn after me… I just decided to take it on." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Charlotte Clarke [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was buzzing with excitement and counting down the days until the race. I kicked the week off with a trip to Darren at DC Cycles, trading a flat white for a corner of the workshop. I gave my bike a quick service and threw on some fresh rubber (criminally mismatching my tyres)." (https://charlottelucyclarke.substack.com/p/edition-zero-gravel-2025)

**Charlotte Clarke [COMPETITIVE]:** "It ended up being a pretty lonely day out there for me. I wasn't really expecting to spend so much of the race alone. It was a bit nerve wracking not knowing what the race situation was like behind me, but I stayed calm and backed myself. I used my knowledge of the roads to my advantage - knowing where to push the pace, and where I could back it off and recover a bit." **Charlotte Clarke [COMPETITIVE]:** "With around 5km to go, I was caught by a group including Michaela and I had to adapt the plan. She sent a super strong attack into the final descent of the day and I chased her back down. I made the decision to back myself in the sprint, and just like that all 247km of racing came down to a dramatic sprint finish." Won back-to-back. **Charlotte Clarke [COMPETITIVE]:** "Although I won the race, I didn't quite tick off all the goals I'd set for myself, so it felt a little bittersweet. I guess as athletes we're pretty hard on ourselves! But overall, it was awesome to finish my season on a high, and I was really happy with how strong I rode and how steady my mental game was throughout the entire race!" ## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Mandatory Gear - All Levels:** All Mains and Entrée riders must carry one emergency blanket large enough to provide complete shelter. Emergency bivi blankets were available for sale at the expo. (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Navigation - Mains riders:** GPS navigation device is required for the Mains course as it is not fully marked with signs. "You will need a means of navigation for the Mains. DO NOT use any files from 2022, 2023 or 2024." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2024-newsletter-one)

**Tools & Spares - All Levels:** Riders should carry "all the relevant tools and spares to ensure forward progress" including consideration for pumps and tubes. George Bennett [ELITE] specifically questioned what to bring, highlighting this is important even for pros. (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/ and https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Tire Considerations - [RECREATIONAL]:** Ant and partner took it easy on the rocky descent of Myers Pass to "not risk a puncture or a crash, as Ant had experienced a side-wall puncture on the descent previously." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile - All Levels:** Main Course is 248km with 3500-4000m of vertical ascent (depending on bike computer). 72% of the Main (about 180km) is on gravel roads. No towns on route. (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Three Key Passes:** Mackenzie Pass (takes you west), Hakataramea Pass (southern leg of the loop), and Meyers Pass (pushing east back towards Waimate). (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Mackenzie Pass [RECREATIONAL]:** "An excruciating straight headwind section led to the pass." The climb has a double-headed nature - "a short road descent, we were back on the gravel for another long climb. That had not been Mackenzie Pass!" It's a four kilometre climb with an average gradient of 6.3%. (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/ and https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Hakataramea Pass [RECREATIONAL]:** "We now had a tailwind. That make the conditions surprisingly hot and there was no drafting benefit. It felt harder than it looked as the road climbed gently and the gravel was a bit heavier." Followed by "the nicest section of the ride. 20km of tailwind and gravity assisted fun on a smooth gravel road." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Gravel Conditions - [RECREATIONAL]:** "In general these comprised of fast secure gravel or hard packed dirt, but over the course of the ride we were also treated to freshly laid, deep slow gravel (the worst part of this was on a section near the start of the route that Lill and the other riders of the Snack had to cover it in both directions), unexpectedly deep gravel that you wouldn't notice until your bike started to fishtail on a descent, gravel spiced up with rocks, and numerous stream crossings that required one to ride over rounded wet slippery stones." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Stream Crossings - Multiple Levels:** "The course crosses a number of fords, please take care when crossing, even if there is no water, the surface will be rough." [RECREATIONAL]: "We had a couple of minor crashes in the fords we crossed, one involving Ant. He mentioned that sitting in the cold water helped the cramp he was starting to suffer from." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2024-newsletter-one and https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Gates:** "There are a couple of gates on the Mains course, please leave these as you find them." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2024-newsletter-one)

**Open Roads - All Traffic:** "All roads are open, this means you can expect to find other road users at all times, locals, tractors, maybe even some stock. KEEP LEFT at all times and be prepared to make room, make way or even stop." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Initial Kilometres:** "The sun was still low, with strung out lines of riders casting long shadows as we raced along the initial tar-sealed kilometres. As we flew along a straight false flat, the field split and by the time we reached the initial gravel sections (after 6km), the lead bunch was already a long way ahead." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Remoteness:** "It's 250 kilometres with no towns. There are smatterings of dairy farms, but it's limited to the very start and end of the course. There isn't a lot of traffic going up and down the roads every day, so the gravel is pristine." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early Pace - [ELITE]:** George Bennett described the early pace as "easy" but noted: "When we hit the first feed zone, I didn't realise the pace that we'd go through those at and I dropped into the third group coming out of it. After that the race was on." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Feed Zone Strategy - [ELITE]:** Feed zones operate more like triathlon transitions than road racing. "It's one of professional cycling's unwritten rules that you don't attack through the feed zone, but in gravel events the feed zone is more like the transition of a triathlon; valuable time, or your position in a strong group, can be lost through poor planning or execution." Caleb Bottcher lost position at the first feed due to fumbling his Camelbak drop. (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Early Attack Strategy - [ELITE]:** Caleb Bottcher attacked solo with 130 kilometres and three major climbs still to go. "I'd run a couple of scenarios in my head before the race…I wasn't sure how it was going to play out, but I was open to going early. It wasn't Plan A, but if an opportunity arose, I wasn't going to turn it down." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Local Knowledge Advantage - [COMPETITIVE]:** Charlotte Clarke: "I used my knowledge of the roads to my advantage - knowing where to push the pace, and where I could back it off and recover a bit." She noted "The first half of the route winds along my usual training roads, the kind I know down to the kilometre - the exact angle of every corner, the rhythm of each climb, where to push and where to recover." (https://charlottelucyclarke.substack.com/p/edition-zero-gravel-2025)

**Riding Together Strategy - [RECREATIONAL]:** After dropping from lead group at ~50km: "Ant was riding more strongly than I and could have stayed with the group I think. However, I was happy he didn't and we rode together to the first bag drop... We made introductions figuring that, all being well, we would be together for the rest of ride." They finished together after full course. (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Mental Management - [ELITE]:** Caleb Bottcher on recovery from setback: "In an eight hour race, there's going to be dark patches, and there's lots of time to think, so how you shape that has a huge impact. Bad things are going to happen in that time, so it's how you manage those moments and your reactions to them which is what matters." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Nutrition - [RECREATIONAL]:** "I had been feeling bloated for the last couple of hours and was really craving something that wasn't sugary" near the end. "At the final drink stop (after the descent of Myers Pass) Ant stopped to stretch out his cramping quads and we both stuffed a few handfuls of potato chips in our mouths." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Pre-Race Nutrition - [COMPETITIVE]:** Charlotte Clarke: "At 3 a.m. on Saturday, my obnoxious alarm went off and I stumbled down the hallway into the kitchen - more specifically, straight to the fridge - to grab my container of rice, brown sugar, pancakes, and sauce, plus a bottle of orange juice... Then I crawled back into bed for one more cosy hour before my 4 a.m. alarm." (https://charlottelucyclarke.substack.com/p/edition-zero-gravel-2025)

**Drop Bag Planning - [ELITE]:** Each rider receives drop bags at registration to fill with food, nutrition, and spares. Mains riders receive two drop bags. Caleb Bottcher's plan: "just to be dropping my Camelbak. I'd still have two bottles and full pockets." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/ and https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues:** George Bennett's "biggest fear was getting a puncture early on and being stuck riding on my own." Ant [RECREATIONAL] had experienced "a side-wall puncture on the descent [of Myers Pass] previously" which influenced their cautious approach. (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/ and https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Cramping - Multiple Levels:** [RECREATIONAL]: "During the final 30kms to the finish... Ant was complaining about cramp" and earlier "Ant stopped to stretch out his cramping quads." [RECREATIONAL]: "As the terrain became lumpier I started to feel the odd pre-cramp twinge from repeated efforts to close gaps over the tops of the punchy ascents." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Mental Challenges - [ELITE]:** Caleb Bottcher after losing position: "I started to really panic. This group was not working well together, and we were watching the gap just get bigger and bigger to the two groups in front. I thought I'd blown it." Also heard "murmurs from a few of the other guys in the group, saying things like 'oh shit, we're not even halfway'." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Feed Zone Errors - [ELITE]:** Caleb Bottcher nearly ended his race at first feed: "When I got there my mind blanked. I still had the bag clipped up and the hose strapped across me... I started to really panic." George Bennett also dropped position here. (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Descent Crashes - [RECREATIONAL]:** "We had a couple of minor crashes in the fords we crossed" and concern about "unexpectedly deep gravel that you wouldn't notice until your bike started to fishtail on a descent." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Distance Intimidation - [ELITE]:** George Bennett: "I was shitting it about riding 250 kilometres on gravel. No matter what, no matter how fit you are or where you're riding, being on a bike that long is hard." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Illness - [RECREATIONAL]:** "I had been coughing the previous day but as I had slept reasonably well (after taking a Lemsip) I wasn't sure if I was suffering from pre-race health paranoia, hay-fever or was genuinely sick." Rider completed course despite concern. (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Protocol for Withdrawal:** "If for any reason you cannot continue on and you wish to withdraw from the event, please check in with the Tail End Charlie, the Aid station crew or the finish line village. Please do not just abandon the course and disappear." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Philosophy:** Andy Chalmers: "Andy is insistent that the primary reason for the event, its rurality, and its difficulty, is that the challenge helps to forge a community. But, when you put a load of people together on bikes and point to a finish line, you've got a race." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Three Pillars:** "Edition Zero is set up as an adventure, this means things might well be different from your usual events." The event focuses on "Adventure. Challenge. Community." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one and https://www.squirtcyclingproducts.com/2025/10/edition-zero-gravel-adventure-challenge-community/)

**Post-Race Scene - [COMPETITIVE]:** Charlotte Clarke: "The post-race vibes were immaculate. Everyone was buzzing, and everyone had a story to tell. Prizegiving was super cool - the medals had the course elevation profile on them, such a detailed touch - and the pink winner's jerseys were absolutely stunning. The celebrations carried on at the afterparty, where I refuelled with some very good pizza and kept catching up with everyone!" (https://charlottelucyclarke.substack.com/p/edition-zero-gravel-2025)

**Velodrome Finish - [RECREATIONAL]:** "As we entered the velodrome for two laps before the finish, Gus, the MC, was trying goad us into a sprint but we decided to cross the finish line together. It was satisfying to complete such a hard course and nice to ride and finish it together. It felt like a mountain bike pairs stage race stage in that regard." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Velodrome Finish - [COMPETITIVE]:** Charlotte Clarke: "The atmosphere inside the velodrome with the crowd was unreal." (https://charlottelucyclarke.substack.com/p/edition-zero-gravel-2025)

**Historic Venue:** Waimate Velodrome was "built in 1891" - five years before the first Paris-Roubaix and more than 40 years before Roubaix's concrete track. "Between 1930 and 1950 the town hosted the start of the annual 233km race from Waimate to Christchurch, and from 1951 to 1954 the race was run in reverse." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Gravel Expo:** "During event registration we have the EZG Expo going down. The expo is full of cycling brands, vendors and locals setting up to meet you, have some fun (goodies and giveaways aplenty) and share in the welcome that EZG is known for. Do not miss it." Friday 3-7pm. (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Registration & Briefings:** "Everyone must register for the ride, there is no Saturday event registration for the Mains riders. Registration on Friday opens at 3pm and closes at 8pm." "Event Briefings: These take place 15 minutes prior to your ride starting, everyone must attend, they will be located on the Velodrome track - #dontbelate" (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Name Tradition:** "At the event centre in Victoria Park, the names of the riders for all the routes were chalked onto the asphalt." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Start Time:** 6am for Mains riders. "6am saw the Mains riders roll onto the neighbouring street for the official start of the race." (https://www.climbbikeski.com/2025/12/12/edition-zero-gravel-2025/)

**Finish Village:** "Once out of the Velodrome, enjoy our village where the expo vendors will have refreshment flowing to help with your carb replacement requirements." Prize giving at 5pm. (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Community Impact:** The event is "focused on creating a community of riders, participants and supporters while having a positive impact on the host community." (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Unsupported Nature:** "Given this is an adventure and people are travelling from all over to attend, this event is strictly unsupported. This means that there is no personal on course support. No one other than official event staff will be allowed into the Aid Stations!" (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Littering Policy:** "Rubbish: Littering of any kind is totally unacceptable! There will be rubbish bags for you at the aid stations and finish village, otherwise be sensible and respectful, carry all your rubbish with you!" (https://www.greatkiwigravel.com/post/edition-zero-2025-newsletter-one)

**Growth & Prestige:** "In just its third year, Edition Zero has quickly become one of New Zealand's most prestigious bike races." Andy Chalmers: "This year it was very evident that people had targeted it. It wasn't just another race on their calendar. It was something they were putting specific effort towards." (https://www.nzcyclingjournal.com/inside-edition-zero/)

**Community Description:** "What really sets Edition Zero apart is the people. Riders from all over Aotearoa come together not for podiums, but for connection. You will see it at the start line, in the mid-ride encouragement, and over a post-ride yarn back at the velodrome. It is the kind of community that keeps the sport grounded and growing."
