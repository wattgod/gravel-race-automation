## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The **115km race** is structured around two distinct loops:

**Start Loop (Luxembourg side - ~25km):**
- Begins with a spectacular cobblestone climb through Vianden's historic center to the iconic Vianden Castle
- Progressive 10km forest climb reaching the course's highest point at 510 meters altitude
- Descent alternates between tarmac and forest paths
- Includes a playful, fast section along the Our River on winding paths
- Described as the harder portion with more climbing and more paved sections (https://www.eislekgravelluxembourg.com/en, https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-with-a-cross-border-challenge/)

**German Loop:**
- Softer landscapes with less demanding gradients
- More gravel, less elevation compared to Luxembourg loop
- Long sections under tree canopies where riders can pick up speed
- Smoother rhythm, more fluid riding
- After completing, riders cross back into Luxembourg on a greenway and elevated path overlooking the Our River (https://www.eislekgravelluxembourg.com/en, https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-with-a-cross-border-challenge/)

**Course structure:** The 115km route consists of 2.5 laps of a 46.8km circuit, with riders crossing the finish line in Vianden multiple times. The 70km masters race (M60+, W50+) covers 1.5 laps. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-with-a-cross-border-challenge/)

The 2026 edition introduces "a new 'Gravel Circus' section – fast, technical, and packed with atmosphere" (https://allevents.in/diekirch/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-2026/200029253717054)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite women's race dynamics [ELITE]:**
Rosa Klöser made a last-minute decision to enter less than a week after finishing the Tour de Suisse on the road. She joined Axelle Dubau-Prévot and Marie Schreiber to form a leading trio. After Schreiber punctured, Klöser attacked solo and built a 6-minute gap to the finish. Larissa Hartog caught and passed Dubau-Prévot for second place. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/eislek-gravel-luxembourg/results/)

**Elite men's race dynamics [ELITE]:**
Started with a lead group of 20 riders. Mathijs Loman, Petr Vakoč, and Mees Hendrikx broke away from this group and worked together to maintain their gap, setting up a three-way sprint finish. All three were credited with the same finishing time of 3:27:40. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/eislek-gravel-luxembourg/results/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The inaugural 2025 edition **sold out with 1,500 riders** from around the world. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/06/27/luxembourg-hosts-uci-gravel-worlds-qualifying-eislek-race-results/, https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-with-a-cross-border-challenge/)

**Setting and access:** The race is set in Vianden, "one of Luxembourg's most iconic towns," nestled in the Our Valley and famous for its medieval castle and picturesque old town. Spectators can access privileged viewpoints via chairlift to the castle and meet riders at various checkpoints throughout the course. (https://www.eislekgravelluxembourg.com/en, https://allevents.in/diekirch/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-2026/200029253717054)

**2026 expansion:** The second edition (June 20-21, 2026) expands to a two-day event:
- **Saturday, June 20:** "Eislek Gravel Experience" — an untimed exploration day open to all riders with multiple route options, featuring a Gravel Village with brands, exhibitors, local food, music, and activities
- **Sunday, June 21:** UCI Gravel World Series race with a "vibrant finish line in Vianden, worthy of the world's best professional races" (https://allevents.in/diekirch/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-2026/200029253717054)

**Elite field [ELITE]:** The 2025 race attracted former Olympic champion Greg Van Avermaet, plus Niels Vandeputte, Petr Vakoc, Hugo Drechou, Joris Nieuwenhuis, Laurens Sweeck, Felipe Orts, Toby Perry, Niki Terpstra, and Paul Voss in the men's field. Women's field included Axelle Dubau-Prévot, Larissa Hartog, Carolin Schiff, Marie Schreiber, Annabel Fisher, Tessa Neefjes, Wendy Oosterwoud, Annemarie Worst, and Nicole Frain. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/eislek-gravel-luxembourg-with-a-cross-border-challenge/)
