## Race Overview & Context

The Fairfield Harvest Rush is part of the **Iowa Gravel Series** and serves as the **championship race for the series**. The event features a **cash purse for the podium** and has "lots of support from the community that is so excited to invite gravel adventurers from all over the midwest and the world" (https://iowabicyclecoalition.org/event/fairfield-harvest-rush-iowa-gravel-series/).

**Date:** October 4, 2025 (Saturday), with start time at 6:30am or 7:30am depending on source

**Location:** Fairfield, Iowa - starts/ends at Fishback and Stephenson Cider House, 1949 Pleasant Plain Road

**Distance Options:**
- 50K/30 mile fun ride
- 100K/62 mile gravel distance
- 100 mile/120 mile race

The race takes place in **Jefferson County in SE Iowa, where "we have more dirt roads than any neighboring county"** (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10605/harvest-rush-gravel-grinder).

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Difficulty:** Described as "Difficult terrain with longer, steeper hills" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10605/harvest-rush-gravel-grinder)

The course features the "backroads of Jefferson County" with emphasis on gravel and Class B roads. Sources consistently highlight "lots of gravel to grind on" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/News/9474/ride-the-backroads-of-jefferson-county-on-the-harvest-rush-gravel-grinder).

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event is described as **"friendly to both new and experienced racers"** and part of a series "where you can invite your family or buddy that's new to gravel grinding or group riding and know the route will be well marked and the people friendly" (https://www.fairfieldiowa.com/calendar/harvest_rush_gravel_grinder/).

**Post-race venue:** Fishback and Stephenson Cider House is described as a "top-notch venue" and "premier place to host" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/News/9474/ride-the-backroads-of-jefferson-county-on-the-harvest-rush-gravel-grinder).

The race is described as **"unsanctioned"** despite being part of the Iowa Gravel Series (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10605/harvest-rush-gravel-grinder).

**Fairfield is described as "unique"** with the Harvest Rush reflecting that character - "Fairfield is unique and so is the Harvest Rush" (https://iowabicyclecoalition.org/event/fairfield-harvest-rush-iowa-gravel-series/).

## Race Support & Logistics

**What's Included:**
- SAG Support - "if you break down or just can't finish the route call our SAG team and they will come rescue you" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/News/9474/ride-the-backroads-of-jefferson-county-on-the-harvest-rush-gravel-grinder)
- Water & Snack Stations along each route
- Swag bag for all registered riders
- Chip timing (for grassroots and higher registration tiers)
- Marked course
- Pro photography

**Mandatory Equipment:** "Helmets, lights/flashers are mandatory and will be checked at the start" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10605/harvest-rush-gravel-grinder)

**Roll-out Window:** Past editions ran 7:00am - 9:00am rolling start (https://www.bikeiowa.com/News/9474/ride-the-backroads-of-jefferson-county-on-the-harvest-rush-gravel-grinder)

## Prize Categories & Cash Purse

**120-mile/100-mile race:** "$1,000 cash prize for the top male and top female rider" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10605/harvest-rush-gravel-grinder)

Prizes also awarded for winners of the fun rides (30-mile and 60-mile options).

As the series championship race, there is a "cash purse for the podium" beyond just the top male/female (https://iowabicyclecoalition.org/event/fairfield-harvest-rush-iowa-gravel-series/).

## Registration Pricing

**Grassroots Registration:** $40 - includes chip timing, marked course, pro photography and aid stations

**Lunch & Shirt:** $60 - adds lunch and Iowa Gravel Series shirt to standard racing package

**Full Season Pass:** $399 - includes all 7 races in the Iowa Gravel Series, t-shirt, and "one of a kind Iowa Gravel Series jersey" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/16029/fairfield-harvest-rush)

Day-of registration available with cash or card (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10605/harvest-rush-gravel-grinder)

## Iowa Gravel Series Context

The Harvest Rush is one of seven races in the 2025 Iowa Gravel Series:
- Kalona Horseshoe - May 10th
- Silver City Century - May 17th
- Waukon 100 - June 21st
- Albia Holy Cow - July 12th
- Glenwood Superbloom - August 9th
- Soldier Cutoff Hillduro in Pisgah - September 20th
- Fairfield Harvest Rush - Sept. 28th (note: conflicting dates exist; Oct 4 also cited)

Full season registration includes "All meals included: you won't have to worry about packing your own food or spending money on eating out" and a "Limited edition jersey designed by Rad Custom Cycling" (https://www.fairfieldiowa.com/calendar/harvest_rush_gravel_grinder/)
