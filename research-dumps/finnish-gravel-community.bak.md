# FNLD GRVL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Graham Cottingham [ELITE]:** "It was clear from the start this was definitely a race, as very quickly the riding was flat out as racers jostled for positions on the first climb. I managed to get away with a fast group, strong pulls kept the pace high and we averaged almost 35kph over the undulating twisting Nordic ski paths... with my average heart rate at over 170bpm for the first 30 minutes I was starting to have doubts that I could hold on for much longer." Finished in 6hrs 3mins for 177km. (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Graham Cottingham [ELITE]:** "The picturesque smooth gravel tracks may have been technically easy to ride but the almost constant false flat effect of the very gently rolling course had been deceptively demanding... One thing is for sure though, if you are after flat-out gravel racing FNLD GRVL is fckng fst." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Suvi Loponen [ELITE]:** "The climbing felt relentless, the downhills needed bravery and the flat… someone forgot to include any flat parts." On the 40km Forest Route. (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/fnld-grvl-why-the-shortest-distance-is-often-the-sweetest-12795)

**Suvi Loponen [ELITE]:** "I made a huge effort to look up from my handlebars, take in the views, make memories and take pictures. The route was worth the hard work, hands down... Although I am no pro gravel racer, I have done enough gravel events and long rides to say that the 40km with my sister was more special on multiple accounts than any of those long, gruelling rides." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/fnld-grvl-why-the-shortest-distance-is-often-the-sweetest-12795)

**Miranda [COMPETITIVE]:** "I enjoyed the challenge of having to race 177km, a distance that is usually just a big endurance-training day for me. Racing is completely different! (https://www.sram.com/es/life/stories/here-there-everywhere-fnld-grvl)

**Miranda [COMPETITIVE]:** "I've never raced gravel before and to maximize my time in this new country, I thought I better tune up the legs a bit, so, fueled mostly by tasty Korvapuusti, I rode my bike 165km from Helsinki to the race site in Lahti, on perfect smooth, gravel roads and paths." (https://www.sram.com/es/life/stories/here-there-everywhere-fnld-grvl)

**Blogger [ELITE]:** "Just when we thought we had tackled all the climbing, came the finish line which if I remember correctly is one of the ski slopes in winter. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/fnld-grvl-a-premier-gravel-event-in-the-making/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile:** 177km Midnight Sun route has approximately 1,900m elevation gain. The elevation comes primarily from rolling terrain rather than sustained climbs, with one notable steep ramp 13km from the finish. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/fnld-grvl-a-premier-gravel-event-in-the-making/)

**Surface Quality:** Multiple riders described the gravel as "pristine," "smooth," and "champagne gravel." Graham Cottingham [ELITE] noted the roads were "quintessential smooth Finnish gravel roads that make up much of the local infrastructure... very different from the rugged and harsh, heather-lined Scottish gravel tracks I am accustomed to." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Course Variation:** The 40km Forest Route included "a beautiful variation of gravel, tarmac and everything in-between... smooth forest roads, a little bit of singletrack, and perfectly paved cycle paths." (https://www.keepitsimpelle.com/fnld-grvl-review/)

**Technical Sections:** "The FNLD GRVL routes knitted together a lot of the cross-country skiing tracks of the area, making the riding quite roller-coaster-like with repeated fast descends followed by punchy climbs." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/fnld-grvl-why-the-shortest-distance-is-often-the-sweetest-12795)

**Nordic Ski Trails:** The course starts and ends on "undulating twisting Nordic ski paths that neighbor the Lahti Sports Center" before opening up to "quintessential smooth Finnish gravel roads." The last 15km returns to "rolling ski trails... punchy climbs and technical corners" that "could very easily punish wearier legs and trip up tired minds." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Deceptive Difficulty:** Despite smooth surfaces, riders noted "the almost constant false flat effect of the very gently rolling course had been deceptively demanding." The "sheer vastness of the landscape skewed distance and time. Looking at my Garmin did little to help either as the numbers seemed to tick by slowly no matter how hard I pedaled." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Feed Stations:** First 55km were entirely off-road. Coffee stop provided by Kahiwa Coffee Roasters "in the middle of nowhere" with local korvapuusti pastries. 100km feed station for the 177km route. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/fnld-grvl-a-premier-gravel-event-in-the-making/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early Race Intensity [ELITE]:** Graham Cottingham averaged "almost 35kph over the undulating twisting Nordic ski paths" in the opening with a fast group, maintaining "average heart rate at over 170bpm for the first 30 minutes." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Group Dynamics [ELITE]:** "Finding a fast group that worked well together was the key to success." One rider noted: "I jumped over to another pair, the woman of the two was on a mission out of the feed station and with another larger group on the horizon, I was optimistic that we could bridge to a larger group. We both burned a few matches but much to my dismay it didn't work out." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Solo Riding Difficulty [ELITE]:** "I rode by myself for a long time with hardly another rider in sight... Progress was fast but the sheer vastness of the landscape skewed distance and time... I was starting to become properly unraveled on the flat roads." Getting "swept up" by groups was described as "a welcome respite from my own struggling legs and mind." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Pro Times [ELITE]:** Toms Skujins finished in 4hrs 46mins; Tiffany Cromwell in 5hrs 14mins for the 177km course. (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Mid-Pack Strategy [COMPETITIVE]:** Miranda's approach: "I tried my best to hang on to a fast group for as long as I could, then settled into a pack that matched my pace." (https://www.sram.com/es/life/stories/here-there-everywhere-fnld-grvl)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues:** Graham Cottingham [ELITE] experienced a dropped chain at 25km: "an aggressive front shift dropped my chain and it became well and truly jammed between the small ring and the frame. There was little more I could do than watch groups ride past as I tried to wrestle it free. After five minutes of frustrating fiddling and the chain was back on." This cost him his front group. (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Punctures:** Carolin Schiff experienced "3 punctures, causing her to lose contact with the front group." (https://www.maurten.com/es/magazine/fnld-grvl-carolin-schiff)

**Mental Challenge:** The "sheer vastness of the landscape skewed distance and time" creating psychological difficulty. One rider noted being "properly unraveled on the flat roads" despite smooth conditions. (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Clothing Preparation [ELITE]:** "FNLD GRVL organisers made it really clear that the weather in Lahti could be unpredictable... They literally said to pack 3 pairs of gloves… one for every season and I did that." The blogger packed: warm layers, padded shorts, base layer, jersey, wind/rain jacket, casquette/cycling cap/ear warmers, but noted "the rain subsided after Thursday and we got to enjoy plenty of sunshine." (https://www.keepitsimpelle.com/fnld-grvl-review/)

**Bike Transport:** Riders had the option to fly bikes or hire locally. One blogger flew a Liv Brava and borrowed another bike from Liv Cycling Finland. Bike boxes were mentioned as necessary for air travel. (https://www.keepitsimpelle.com/fnld-grvl-review/)

## Weather Experienced

**2023 Conditions:** Despite organizers warning about unpredictable weather and rain, riders experienced "plenty of sunshine" after Thursday, with enough heat to cause sunburn: "we got a tan in Finland, who would have thought?" (https://www.keepitsimpelle.com/fnld-grvl-review/)

**2023 Test Event (August):** Described as a "warm day" during pre-event course testing. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/fnld-grvl-a-premier-gravel-event-in-the-making/)

**2023 Race Day:** "Racing was fast, furious, and very dusty" - dust indicating dry conditions. (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Daylight:** "This was my first experience of visiting a country with 'the midnight sun' and living in a sort of Forever 2 pm was strange." Southern Finland gets "19hr of daylight on average each day" in June. (https://www.sram.com/es/life/stories/here-there-everywhere-fnld-grvl)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Inclusive Multi-Distance Format:** Three distances offered (40km Forest Route, 77km Lake Course, 177km Midnight Sun) attracted "every type of gravel rider, from moonlighting off-road World Tour Pro to have-a-go party pace hero." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**International Draw:** "About a third of the participants had flown across from the US and, in total, the event attracted riders from 27 countries." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/fnld-grvl-why-the-shortest-distance-is-often-the-sweetest-12795)

**US Gravel Spirit:** Co-founded by F1 driver Valtteri Bottas and Amy Charity (SBT GRVL founder), with co-owner Tiffany Cromwell. The event's tagline 'the spirit of gravel' aimed "to create a gravel event in Europe that wasn't just a race, but also a fun experience." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Local Support:** "Quaint wooden houses painted either red or yellow were dotted along the course, their occupants at the side of the road waving and cheering riders on as they passed." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Post-Race Scene:** "Tired bodies milled around the finish area, inhaling their post-race meal and exhaling stories from the road." The blogger noted finishing "dusty, battered, and exhilarated... Very ready for a cold beverage." (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Competitive but Inclusive:** "The competitive nature of the event is a little bit of a double-edged sword." One rider's sister "expressed her doubt about 'fitting in' with the WorldTour pros" but afterwards "said competitiveness was the aspect that pushed her to complete the ride." The atmosphere was described as "full of racing buzz but simultaneously relaxed." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/fnld-grvl-why-the-shortest-distance-is-often-the-sweetest-12795)

**Prize Money:** €20,000 prize purse "equally split between the women's and men's podiums" for pro categories, plus "prize packs for the top three riders in all categories, including non-binary and para-cyclists, for both the 177km and 77km routes." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/fnld-grvl-why-the-shortest-distance-is-often-the-sweetest-12795)

**Multi-Day Festival:** "The proceedings spanned four days with plenty of shakedown rides, yoga, and other activities" including VIP rides, Zip and Sip Ride, Fjallraven Hill Climb, SRAM shakeout ride, Canyon Pro recon, Pro Panel, Race Briefing, Kids Race, Sponsor Appreciation Dinner, and Post-Event Party. (https://www.bikeperfect.com/features/fnld-grvl-ride-report)

**Professional Atmosphere:** Alexey Vermeulen's presence was noted - he "likes to add some cycling-related leisure to his calendar" and saw the event as "a great opportunity to hang out with people from the industry and create media content." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/fnld-grvl-a-premier-gravel-event-in-the-making/)

**Unanimous Course Praise:** "Back in Lahti's sports center, there was a common comment; it was one of the best gravel courses we had ever ridden. The varied surfaces and landscapes along the route amazed each one of us." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/fnld-grvl-a-premier-gravel-event-in-the-making/)
