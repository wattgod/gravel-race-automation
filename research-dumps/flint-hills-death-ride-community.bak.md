# FLINT HILLS DEATH RIDE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

**Burnsey [ELITE]:** "I can't believe I paid the big bucks for this so called race when in reality it was a group ride in the heat and dust of the Kansas plains. The finish line was non existent and a total let down; no cameras, no cheers, no cowbells...nothing. I had to ask around town just to see where I was to go and if I needed to check in somewhere." (https://singlespeedpirate.blogspot.com/2009/07/flint-hills-death-ride.html)

Well, I am going to have to call bullshit on that one... ## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Single Speed Setup [ELITE - Burnsey]:** Rider completed the 80+ mile course on a 29er single speed that was "WAY under-geared" (appears to be 32-19 gearing based on comments). He noted: "I was lucky to keep a 12 mph pace, and that was seriously making me spin like a gerbil on cocaine!" This was NOT his preferred setup - he was forced into it due to mechanical issues before the race. (https://singlespeedpirate.blogspot.com/2009/07/flint-hills-death-ride.html)

**Note:** The under-geared single speed experience suggests proper gearing is important for maintaining reasonable cadence on the gravel roads, though this rider still finished successfully despite the handicap.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Conditions:** The course is described as "gravel road ride" rather than technical mountain biking terrain - "the 80+ miles of 'dirt' was nothing more than a gravel road ride." (https://singlespeedpirate.blogspot.com/2009/07/flint-hills-death-ride.html)

**Distance:** Referenced as both "80+ miles" and "77.4 miles" in different sources. One YouTube description notes "5000' of elevation gain" across the course. (https://singlespeedpirate.blogspot.com/2009/07/flint-hills-death-ride.html and https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gpiDxLl8lEA)

**Current Route (UNDEAD Death Ride successor):** The modern version is described as "about a 70 mile loop between Madison and Matfield Green, KS" with "two 35 mile legs" where riders may start in either town. (https://discovergravel.com/the-undead-death-ride)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Organization Issues:** Multiple riders reported significant organizational problems with the original event. The start was notably informal: "The mass of riders were there listening to an older fella who described himself as a 6th grade school teacher announcing the race business. This took all of two minutes and without hesitation we heard a 'GO'! All of the riders kind of looked at each other like 'Is that the start? Do we go now? WTF?'." (https://singlespeedpirate.blogspot.com/2009/07/flint-hills-death-ride.html)

**Positive Element:** "All of the volunteers at the SAG stations were super nice and helpful!" (https://singlespeedpirate.blogspot.com/2009/07/flint-hills-death-ride.html)

**Event History:** The original Flint Hills Death Ride appears to have ended amid organizational issues including unreturned jerseys and canceled events. The current "UNDEAD Death Ride" is explicitly "loosely based on the routes of the historic Flint Hills Death Ride" and organizers state "Discover Gravel LLC is not associated in any way with the FHDR. I simply am a fan of local gravel history." (https://discovergravel.com/the-undead-death-ride)

**Current Event Format:** The successor event (UNDEAD Death Ride) is described as "a simple group ride through the Flint Hills of Kansas" and "First and foremost the UDR is a group ride out in the Flint Hills" - emphasizing its non-competitive, community ride nature. There is a "$20 entry fee" with "Any and all gross profit will 100% be given to entities in Madison and Matfield Green." (https://discovergravel.com/the-undead-death-ride)

**Modern Alternative:** The Flint Hills Gravel event appears to be a separate, more established event series now operating in the region with multiple distance options (20, 40, 80, 120+ mile routes) and running options, described as "Gravel event- family style! Small town love for the backroads!" based in Americus, KS. (https://flinthillsgravelride.com/)
