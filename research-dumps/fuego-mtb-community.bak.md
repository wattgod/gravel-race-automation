# FUEGO XL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Brendan Johnston (Trekky) [ELITE]:** "I wasn't sure where I'd slot into their scene... I feel like ok I'm winning things here, I can win most things here on the road I can have good results here, on the mountain bike I can have good results here but can I go there and be competitive? That was something that was pretty unknown for me." After racing, he came "seventh at the 100km Fuego XL, despite having to fight his way back up the field after missing the early split of seven riders." (https://www.mediaboss.net/seo-training/brendan-johnston-a-15-year-pro-racing-quest-with-a-gravel-resolution-cyclingnews.php)

**Peter Stetina [ELITE]:** "The local excitement around this is already palpable and I cannot convey how difficult and majestic this route is. It is the hardest one-day course in America, hands down." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/leipheimers-growler-gran-fondo-turns-pro-luring-top-riders-to-majestic-route/)

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE] (pre-riding 2023 course):** "Really great course. I haven't had my Scalpel for a year yet, but the more I get it dialed in, the faster I can go and the more fun I have. Fort Ord is a great place to do it." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE] on difficulty:** "Two laps is going to be a test even for the Pros/Lifetime racers." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Jolyzara [COMPETITIVE]:** "I will say this… once the course gets on craters (one of my favorites) it is back on track to near perfect... As mentioned it is a tough course. I don't think anyone will hit the sub 3 hour mark for the gold corral at Leadville." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Jolyzara [COMPETITIVE] on conditions:** "Kinda tough to describe the trail conditions right now because it's such a mixed bag (and will likely change by race day anyways). Vernal pools are full and overflowing onto the trails in some places (trail 18, 57, 56, etc). Other places it is straight up hero dirt. Expect LOTS of ruts and sand (because, well, that's Fort Ord) but if we get a break in the rain the trails could very well be the best you'll ever see out here. If we get hit by a few more atmospheric rivers expect a muddy mess." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Selection [COMPETITIVE level]:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** Initially considered running Thunder Burts but reconsidered after pre-ride conditions. "I road the whole thing. It's sandy at the bottom, but all the rain has made it ridable when it starts to pitch up." Ran Ray/Ralf combo for the course and "they were great." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**fidracer [COMPETITIVE] discussion:** Asked if "fast XC tire (Aspen, Race King, Fast Trak/Renegade, etc.)" would work or if conditions warranted something else. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Bike Choice [COMPETITIVE level]:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE] on mountain bike vs gravel bike:** "I'm not convinced that a drop bar bike is the best choice for the gravel course. I may kit up my XC bike for it instead. I'd like to finish under my own power rather than get flown out in a helicopter after going over the bars because my drop bar handling skills aren't as good as my XC skills." Noted that "MTB has full bounce, less aero, smaller water bottle capacity (2x500ml vs 2x800), wider tires (Nanos are my tire of choice for the course in a 53mm vs 40mm)." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Bike Setup:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "Both bikes weigh about the same and have droppers, so that part is a wash." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Length & Climbing:**
- 100km distance (approximately 66 miles)
- "8000ft Fuego XL course" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PaFPWZP72HM)

**Specific Technical Challenges [COMPETITIVE level observations]:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's still going to have lots of wet and goopy parts. I think there was only one puddle/crossing that was so deep it stopped my momentum. The rest just required one to keep a good spin going until the other side. Lots of places where there is water running right down the middle of the trail. That's the line you want to take, because either side of that is mud." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Sand Sections:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "Second part of the course has a LOT of deep speed sucking sand washes. Like a dozen at least. There's one stretch about mile 25 where you take a hard left off of a fire road descent BOOM 200m sand wash right into a climb." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Jolyzara [COMPETITIVE]:** Criticized course design: "One of the worst parts is they have you going up 69. It's a rutted beach. I guess if you like a mile of off & on uphill sand you'll enjoy the challenge." Noted this section is "the sandy uphill known as 'the beach'" out to eucalyptus. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Descents & Technical Sections:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "Ruts on longer descents and climbs going parallel with the trail. There was one descent like that where there was a single line. The rest of the drop is just crap and it's going to freak less experienced riders out. The rutted climbs are going to be a handful. as they do have a couple of lines, but you're gonna need to keep momentum as some of them are steep enough that a bump you don't have enough to keep rolling over, you're not going to be able to just remount in the middle of that particular freakshow." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Final Climb:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "The last 2.7 mile climb is going to be the test. about the first half of it is a sand wash riddled single track climb, that will drop you on to the same final fire road climb as last year, but the fire road is ultra rutted with a couple sections that have a single line because the road is best described as gone with ruts that are about 5 feet deep. So that's lovely." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Paved & Fire Road Sections:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "Paved and fire road climbs/descents abound. There are a couple that have hard turns off into single track so managing the transition is going to be critical as momentum is still the name of the game." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Course Design Critiques [COMPETITIVE level]:**

**Jolyzara [COMPETITIVE]:** "XL course is good… could be better. I will say they got the first 11 miles right but then starts skipping some of the best trails in the park for cement road. Why ride down on Jacks road instead of descending Red Rocks? Why then go up Jacks instead of going up and over 49 to 82?... Not sure why but they have you ride up 18 (it is a full on stream right now and will be for a long time) then 17 rather than take you up Itchy McScratchy → 51 ->52 → 23 → 16 → 56 to machine gun." Suggested they "should have gone up Watkins Gate to Watkins Spur then cut over on 70 & up 92 and Mars hill. But again…trail choice… as they choose road and sand over some of the best xc singletrack out on the north side of the park." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Passing Opportunities [COMPETITIVE level observations]:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "Passing opportunities on singletrack = slim to none. You do it, it's going to be a ballsy move." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Elite Racing Context:**

**Brendan Johnston [ELITE]:** Came into race believing "Something like Unbound is really going to be in my wheelhouse and hopefully I can navigate my way through that race to a really strong result, especially in the Life Time Grand Prix field... I think over that period of time it's a big unknown ... anyone can kind of come in and be competitive in the final if you've done the work so I'm definitely eyeing off the win, that's for sure." His Sea Otter result (7th) helped calibrate expectations. (https://www.mediaboss.net/seo-training/brendan-johnston-a-15-year-pro-racing-quest-with-a-gravel-resolution-cyclingnews.php)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Size & Participation:**

The race is part of the Life Time Grand Prix series. For 2025, "The field size is reduced to 25 men and 25 women initially, with three wild cards added after UNBOUND." (https://rouvy.com/blog/life-time-grand-prix)

**Pre-Race Activities:**

**webdev511 [COMPETITIVE]:** "There was a high school XCC racing going on that over lapped the course. We went through in the gaps, but it was a perfect example of the course marshall asking us it go around knowing full well that there was no detour to be had. It was easy enough to stay out of the way of racers. Should be a better experience for all classes on race day this year." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)

**Format Changes:**

For 2023, the race maintained the same course as the XC race but modified format. As part of Life Time Grand Prix: "wave starts for each age group in all XC and Gravel races." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/sea-otter-classic-2023-fuego-la-gravilla-et-al/82332)
