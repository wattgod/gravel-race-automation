# GALACTIC GRINDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Location Details

**Location:** Aztec/Cedar Hill, New Mexico (Four Corners region, near Farmington)

The Galactic Grinder is a gravel cycling event hosted by Aztec Adventures, founded by Neil Hannum in partnership with Fort Lewis College graduate William Farmer, Haden Cox, and volunteers starting in 2021. The event philosophy is **"Go Fast, Go Slow, Just Go"** — explicitly welcoming all abilities.

**Distances offered:** 9-12 miles, 35-37 miles, 50-55 miles (sources vary slightly on exact distances)

The race concludes with riders experiencing the **Alien Run trail system**, which gives the event its space-themed name and costume tradition. Multiple sources emphasize riders are encouraged to **"Race through the Aztec Arch in your best intergalactic alien costume"** and to **"PLEASE DRESS UP!!!!"**

The region is described as **"Often overshadowed by the famed trails and cycling community in Durango, Colo., San Juan County is a hidden paradise of mountain biking and gravel riding just waiting to be discovered."** (https://triathloninsight.com/insight-update/third-annual-galactic-grinder-challenges-cyclists-2/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Composition:**
- Almost all dirt/gravel with minimal pavement
- Includes **3.8-4 miles of singletrack** on the Alien Run trail system
- Multiple sources warn: **"These roads may be rougher than other gravel event roads"** (https://farmingtonnm.org/events/galactic-gravel-grinder)

**Elevation & Gradient:**
- 50-mile course: **4,500+ feet of total elevation gain**
- Includes **"steep punchy climbs up to 12%"** (https://farmingtonnm.org/events/galactic-gravel-grinder)
- Described as **"Hilly terrain"** throughout

**Surface Conditions:**
- **"Some sections of the lunar landscape were sandy and challenging at times"**
- Other sections on roads were **"'glazed' by traffic and then baked under the New Mexico sunshine. This made for a fast pace in those areas of the galaxy."** (https://triathloninsight.com/insight-update/third-annual-galactic-grinder-challenges-cyclists-2/)
- **"mesas, washes, and singletrack"** characterize the route

**Navigation:**
The 37 and 53 mile routes require **"MANDATORY navigation device with the route you are riding"** with the explanation: **"There are a bunch of tricky turns out there and we don't want anyone getting lost. The course will be marked the best we can but if we use the combination, we should avoid lost riders."** (https://farmingtonnm.org/events/galactic-gravel-grinder)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Choice [ALL LEVELS]:**
Multiple sources state: **"Make sure your gravel bike is up for the burly roads NM has to offer! If you don't have a gravel bike a mountain bike is perfectly acceptable."** (https://aztec-adventures.com/galactic-grinder/ and https://farmingtonnm.org/events/galactic-gravel-grinder)

The explicit acceptance of mountain bikes, combined with 3.8 miles of singletrack and "burly roads," suggests this is rougher terrain than typical smooth gravel events.

**Required Equipment [37 & 53 MILE RIDERS]:**
- GPS device or smartphone with loaded course (mandatory for navigation)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Aid Station Locations:**

*37-mile course:* Aid stations at **miles 23 and 31** (https://farmingtonnm.org/events/galactic-gravel-grinder)

*53-mile course:* Aid stations at **miles 23 and 46** (https://farmingtonnm.org/events/galactic-gravel-grinder)

Note: The 37-mile course has only a 6-mile gap between final aid and finish, while the 53-mile has a 7-mile gap — relatively close support throughout.

**Pacing Considerations:**
The mix of **"fast pace"** sections on hard-packed roads and **"sandy and challenging"** sections plus **"steep punchy climbs up to 12%"** suggests highly variable pacing demands. The singletrack finish means technical bike handling at the end when riders may be fatigued.

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:**
The organizers' ethos is **"Go Fast, Go Slow, Just Go"** with explicit encouragement that **"Aztec Adventures encourages participants of all abilities to partake in its events"** (https://triathloninsight.com/insight-update/third-annual-galactic-grinder-challenges-cyclists-2/)

**Costume Culture:**
The space/alien theme is taken seriously, with race materials including **"PLEASE DRESS UP!!!!"** and **"Race through the Aztec Arch in your best intergalactic alien costume."** (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10288/galactic-grinder)

**Series Context:**
Galactic Grinder is part of a three-event gravel series by Aztec Adventures:
1. Galactic Grinder (April)
2. Gas Field Grinder (June)
3. Holy Grit (location: Wines of the San Juan in Blanco)

Riders registering for all three events receive **20% off registration**. (https://triathloninsight.com/insight-update/third-annual-galactic-grinder-challenges-cyclists-2/)

**Venue Details (2024):**
**"The new location in Cedar Hill allowed everyone to spread out and warm up with some of the local trail lines on the property."** This suggests a more relaxed pre-race atmosphere with practice opportunities. (https://triathloninsight.com/insight-update/third-annual-galactic-grinder-challenges-cyclists-2/)

**Regional Context:**
The event is positioned as part of economic diversification efforts: **"Northern New Mexico is looking toward outdoor recreation as a future economic diversifier, and events like Roost the Ridges, Galactic Grinder, HOLY GRIT with more to come, are already blazing trails for cyclists to enjoy for generations to come."** (https://www.outdoorsportswire.com/third-annual-galactic-grinder-challenges-cyclists-northeast-of-aztec-n-m/)
