## Course Details & Terrain

**2025 Course Profile:**
- Long route: 117.4km (110km listed on some sources), 1,220m elevation gain
- Short route: 76km (78.68km on some sources), 653m elevation gain
- Surface mix: 66.90km dirt road, 43.60km asphalt (on long route)
- One major climb per lap from sea level to 343m elevation

**Course Description from Official Sources:**
The race is "essentially divided into a flat coastal section on very smooth and fast roads, with some asphalt sections (it runs alongside inland ponds, crosses a fishpond, and runs alongside the Flumendosa River), and an inland section that is virtually entirely dirt, with a few ups and downs and only one long, but never difficult, climb. The entire course is technically straightforward and follows easy, very fast roads." [COMPETITIVE perspective]

**Specific Route Details:**
- Start: Hotel Villaggio Colostrai, between beach and internal pond
- Gravel begins immediately alongside the pond
- "After circling the pond, the route alternates between asphalt and dirt before entering the fishpond area, crossing a striking barrage between inland and marine waters"
- Circuit structure: Elite & younger age categories do 2 laps of 50km circuit; older age categories do 1 lap
- "Fast dirt road" after SS125 stretch leading to first feed zone
- "Long dirt road" begins the main climb (343m) with "panoramic views of Colostrai Valley"
- Descent is "partially paved"
- Second feed zone near Muravera after long dirt section through Flumendosa River delta
- Finish returns to Hotel Villaggio Colostrai

**Technical Assessment:**
Course described as "technically simple and very fast" with surfaces that are "very smooth" in coastal sections (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/orosei/, https://www.girosardegna.com/girosardegnagravel-uci-gravel-world-tour/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Context - Part of Larger Festival:**
The Giro Sardegna Gravel is integrated into the week-long GiroSardegna road racing event. The broader event is described as having:
- "High quality" hotels with "unique camaraderie fostered among participants"
- "Convenient schedules, impeccable roads, and picturesque natural surroundings"
- Philosophy: "at the end of the week, every participant feels victorious"
- "Personal connection riders establish with their opponents from the first stage"
- Over 500 athletes annually (70% Italian, 30% from other European and global nations)

**Race Hub:**
Hotel Villaggio Colostrai serves as central hub with:
- Beachfront position with private beach access
- Outdoor pool, restaurant, bar
- Kids' club and family-friendly activities
- Evening entertainment
- Registration, start, and finish all located here

**Unique Aspects:**
- "Logistics-free" setup - stay in one resort all week, races start/end nearby
- Described as feeling "more like a vacation with a competitive edge"
- Awards ceremony at 4:00 PM in hotel amphitheater
- UCI winners receive UCI winner's jersey
- World Championship qualifiers receive qualification medal

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Pace Profile [ELITE perspective]:**
- 2025 winning times suggest ~3.5 hour finish for 117km = ~33.5 km/h average for elite men
- Elite women's winning time: 4:16:18 for 117km = ~27.4 km/h average
- 76km short course elite times: Men 50-59 winner at 2:32:12 = ~30 km/h; Elite women at 2:51:18 = ~26.5 km/h

**Race Dynamics [ELITE]:**
- 2025 men's race came down to 2-up sprint after nearly 3.5 hours
- 2025 women's race was also a 2-up sprint, won by just 0.25 seconds
- Third place finishers were 5 minutes (men) and 1 minute (women) behind, suggesting selective race with small lead groups

**Feed Zones:**
- 2 feed zones for short course (1 lap)
- 4 feed zones for long course (2 laps)
- Located: one after SS125 on fast dirt road section, one near Muravera

**No Time Limit:** Race has no official cutoff time
