# GLENGARRY GAMES GRAVEL RUN — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The course uses rolling gravel roads around Maxville in the United Counties of Stormont, Dundas & Glengarry, with a distinctive finish: "It then finishes with a 3/4 lap of an oval track in front of the giant covered grandstands in front of hundreds of spectators" (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/glengarry-gravel-run). This unique finish takes place at the Glengarry Highland Games grounds.

**Shane Hotchkiss [UNKNOWN]:** Described the Tour of Kincardine (different race) as "more like an adventure than a race" - suggesting these Ontario gravel events emphasize experience over pure competition (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events).

The Epic Course covers 114.0 km with finishing times ranging from 3:08:23 to 5:14:00 in 2025 (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html). In 2024, the Epic Course was 112.0 km with winning times starting at 3:02:03 (http://timeral.info/Archive/A2-2024.html).

The Medio Course is 83.0 km with 2025 winning times around 2:23:47 (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html).

The Petit Course is 42.0 km with 2025 winning times around 1:31:19 (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html).

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Epic Course - Elite Level Performance [ELITE]:** The top finishers in 2025 completed 114 km in approximately 3:08:23, averaging 36.31 km/h. Stephen Chapman, Bayden Pritchard, and William Goodfellow finished within one second of each other, suggesting pack racing dynamics at the front (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html).

**Epic Course - Competitive Level Performance [COMPETITIVE]:** Mid-pack finishers (places 15-25) completed the course in 3:13-3:35 range (33.62-31.75 km/h average), showing approximately 5-27 minutes back from the leaders (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html).

**Medio Course - Elite/Competitive Performance:** 2025 top finishers completed 83 km in 2:23:47-2:23:51, with the top 5 separated by only 4 seconds, again suggesting tactical pack racing (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html).

## DNF Risk Factors

In 2025, there were 4 total DNFs across all categories:
- Epic Course 19+ Men: 3 DNFs out of 49 starters (6% DNF rate)
- Petit Course Men: 1 DNF out of 11 starters (9% DNF rate)
(http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html)

In 2024, there were 3 total DNFs:
- Epic Course: 2 DNFs (Mark Andrews from Ottawa, Matthew Cloutier)
- Medio Course: 1 DNF (Darcy Bluethner, listed as "Injured")
(http://timeral.info/Archive/A2-2024.html)

The relatively low DNF rate (3-6%) suggests the course is challenging but manageable for prepared riders.

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The race is "in partnership with the Glengarry Highland Games" (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/glengarry-gravel-run), giving it a unique cultural festival atmosphere rather than just a standalone cycling event.

The finish at the Highland Games grounds means riders complete "3/4 lap of an oval track in front of the giant covered grandstands in front of hundreds of spectators" (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/glengarry-gravel-run) - creating a spectator-friendly finale unusual for gravel events.

**2025 Participation:** 128 total participants across all categories (58 in the 9am Epic start, 47 in the 9:30am Medio start, 23 in the 9:45am Petit start), showing solid community support for a regional event (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html).

**2024 Participation:** 96 total participants, indicating growth year-over-year (http://timeral.info/Archive/A2-2024.html).

The event is organized by Brendan Gorman with timing by Allan Hawley, suggesting a consistent organizational team building event reputation over multiple years (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A2-2025.html and http://timeral.info/Archive/A2-2024.html).
