# GRASSHOPPER ADVENTURE SERIES — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "Imagine with me a place where people show up with their bikes, in a parking lot, early on a weekend morning... Now, travel with me to one perhaps less familiar. It's still a parking lot, and still pretty damn early. But first, find yourself among redwoods, green hills, and a quiet town... Replace any impression of ego with conversation about past rides or the day ahead. Some here have shown up to race, some to ride hard, and some to finish." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "Where else will you line up with the North American Cyclist of the Year (Katie Hall), former WorldTour pro and current gravel 'pro' (Ted King), living legend in the cyclocross, endurance & enduro MTB (Geoff Kabush) and an O.G. gravel master and advocate (Yuri Hauswald)? What other bike race has the assumption that everyone will go as hard as they want to, which generally means ridiculously hard, but in the most casual way possible?" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "This route featured about a 20 minute road climb from the gun, which usually sets an interesting scenario in these events. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "The valley was dark under redwood canopies, still wet from last week's rain, and a minefield of pot holes. ## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike choice is critical and highly terrain-dependent:**

**[ELITE] Stephen Vogel:** At Low Gap, noted a Bear Dev Team rider on a full mountain bike (2.3" tires) was able to stay with gravel bikes on 22 miles of pavement, then excelled on the dirt: "I didn't expect him to roll 2.3 mountain bike tires at our clip over 22 miles of pavement... Mountain biker kid now in his element, he had ample watts and grip to work with every time he got out of the saddle." The rider (Sandy Floren, U23 XC national champ) won the race. (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**[UNKNOWN] Sara:** At Old Caz, "Sara was rocking her FS MTB" (full suspension mountain bike). (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2018/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-old-caz)

**Official race description:** "Lake Sonoma – Different Bike. Same Grasshopper. As if to hark back to its routes, the hard tail mountain bike might just be the best for this event. Steep gradients both up and down, ribbons of single track." (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

**Tire pressure strategy [ELITE]:** Stephen Vogel and Ted King "decided to make a pit stop to drop some pressure in the tires" after hitting dirt at Low Gap (a calculated mid-race adjustment for traction). (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**[UNKNOWN] Amy Cameron:** "This was my first year riding my Santa Cruz Stigmata CC, though, and it definitely made a difference. Plus I've been riding dirt a little bit more than usual, so I was a *wee bit* faster downhill this year." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2018/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-old-caz)

## Weather Experienced

**2023 Huffmaster:** Described as "mud-caked" by race reports, suggesting wet/muddy conditions. Miguel Crawford called it "like a five hour cyclocross race." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/katerina-nash-ian-lopez-de-san-roman-win-mud-caked-huffmaster-hopper/)

**Low Gap (year unspecified):** "The valley was dark under redwood canopies, still wet from last week's rain." Road descent featured "damp hairpins." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Huffmaster general conditions:** Official description warns riders to "be prepared for everything!" due to February timing in California, with "mixed bag that is California weather." (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Low Gap [ELITE perspective]:**
- "22 miles of road followed by 22 miles of dirt" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)
- Opens with "20 minute road climb from the gun" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)
- "Downhill, one lane, pot-holed road with grades up to 12% and plenty of damp hairpins... a minefield of pot holes" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)
- "We were still in the trees, so climbing turned a heavy grind through the ruts and often mud. Mountain biker kid now in his element, he had ample watts and grip to work with every time he got out of the saddle. The smaller descents between climbing, rutted and wet, made it difficult to really recover." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)
- "Twenty minute dirt climb to go before the nine mile descent" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)
- "The descent of the ridge wasn't too bad, although a fair number of washboard bumps and potholes kept it interesting." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Old Caz [UNKNOWN perspective]:**
- Starts with Coleman climb, immediately putting riders "into the red from the gun" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2018/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-old-caz)
- Features creek crossing: "slosh across the creek" (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2018/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-old-caz)
- Reminder to "keep your mouth closed" on descents (likely due to dust/debris) (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2018/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-old-caz)

**General terrain characteristics:**
- Official: "Unless you've ridden the course before (unlikely because of how remote these places are), 'road' and 'dirt' can mean any combination of things, from pristine mountain highway to bombed out chip seal, or from smoothly packed gravel to rutted, rooty, and muddy jeep tracks." (https://www.grasshopperadventureseries.com/)

**Lake Sonoma:**
- "Steep gradients both up and down, ribbons of single track all with the shimmering, sparkling Lake Sonoma in view" (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

**Ukiah-Mendo Gravel Epic:**
- "Private trails that are typically closed year round to the public... near traffic free ride and spectacular scenery as the course gains elevation and glimpses of the coast." (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early race dynamics [ELITE perspective]:**

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "This route featured about a 20 minute road climb from the gun, which usually sets an interesting scenario in these events. Will the front group act cool and casual, staying together and chatting it up? It's not a race, after all. Or will someone go rogue and lay down the hammer?" At this particular Low Gap, someone attacked early on a road bike, and the chase group formed: "I set a good pace to reel him in. Mind you, I decided not to go full on roadie attack, because this isn't a race." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Feed zone strategy [ELITE]:**

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** At Low Gap feed zone (also the transition to dirt): "In the Hoppers, these are funny spots in the race...er, ride. There's ample Osmo and often lots of snacks laid out in enticing fashion. Do you stop, chat it up, refill bottles, pound a few mini cinnamon rolls? Or do you count precious seconds and go for just a handup? We chose the latter, but shortly after decided to make a pit stop to drop some pressure in the tires." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Pacing philosophy across skill levels:**

Official race description emphasizes flexibility: "Some riders push for their best finish, while others are there simply to enjoy some really cool places on two wheels. We have a podium for the top 3 finishers in all categories and cash prizes for the top 3 Pro M/W, but if you're just in it for adventure and exploration, we say ride on." (https://www.grasshopperadventureseries.com/)

**Self-sufficiency requirements [ALL LEVELS]:**

Official guidance: "Hoppers encourage self-reliance. It's important to be self-sufficient; able to navigate using the downloadable GPS routes, capable of fixing a flat, and willing to push yourself... Bring essentials: extra tubes, tire boots, CO2 and a pump, food, water, warm clothes, a course map or GPS, space blanket." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/discover-grasshopper-adventure-series/)

**Aid station support [ALL LEVELS]:**

"Well-stocked rest stops providing Skratch hydration and assorted snacks (be sure to stop for the hot grilled cheese)" (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/discover-grasshopper-adventure-series/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical preparedness:**

Official warning: "We'll have an ambulance and SAG (Support and Gear) vehicles for injuries only. If you're tired or cramping, you'll need to make your own way back. Carry tubes, a pump (not just CO2), chain tools." (https://www.grasshopperadventureseries.com/about-us)

**Navigation challenges:**

"The routes are marked, however you'll rely on a GPS device for most routes. GPS files are provided in advance for each event, and riders are encouraged to pre-ride if possible." (https://www.grasshopperadventureseries.com/about-us)

**Bike/tire choice mismatch:**

**[ELITE] Stephen Vogel:** Observed a roadie drop out on technical descent: "This is where we quietly bid adieu to our roadie friend, whose bike couldn't keep up with our smashing of California Cobbles." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Weather exposure:**

Huffmaster specifically warns: "Be prepared for everything!" due to February timing and variable California weather. (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Pre-race atmosphere:**

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "Multiply the number of cars & people by about five. Add in a sponsor expo with swag, free nutrition, and generally stoked looking people happy to chat. Replace any impression of ego with conversation about past rides or the day ahead. Some here have shown up to race, some to ride hard, and some to finish. Some roll with the newest 39.75c 20-million TPI flat resistant cotton sidewall wet weather file treads, some with grip-shift 26-inch mountain bikes. It's a Grasshopper Adventure Series... when over 400 people show up on a given Saturday in rural Mendocino county to ride (or is it race?) their bikes on Miguel Crawford's road and gravel du jour, there's gonna be some variety." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Post-race scene:**

Official description: "Post-ride party with food and drinks... post-race beer and burrito in hand!" (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/discover-grasshopper-adventure-series/ and https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

**Inclusivity and community values:**

"Join a passionate community of riders that shares your love for cycling and exploration. Hoppers are as much about forging new friendships as they are about pedaling through uncharted territory. All levels of riders, from those new to gravel to World Cup pros, are welcome and neither USA Cycling nor UCI licenses are required; everyone is able to register! Podiums for male, female and non-binary categories are offered and all participants are celebrated." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/discover-grasshopper-adventure-series/)

**Elite field quality but welcoming atmosphere:**

**Stephen Vogel [ELITE]:** "The aforementioned idols of mine were all present, chatting it up with any one of the cheery 400 others crowding the lane at the start line." (http://www.teammikesbikes.com/blog-2020/2019/2/5/grasshopper-adventure-series-numero-uno-low-gap)

**Women's mentorship initiative:**

"'My Mentor Gave Me the Confidence to Try a Gravel Race in the First Place:' Year 2 of Low Gap Hopper Initiative a Success — 20 mentors rode the 50-mile gravel race in California with 20 U19 girls." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/my-mentor-gave-me-the-confidence-to-try-a-gravel-race-in-the-first-place-year-2-of-low-gap-hopper-initiative-a-success/)

**Community support for nonprofits:**

"A portion of each registration is donated to a variety of nonprofits working to improve lives and the communities where Hoppers are held. Organizations receiving financial support from the 2024 Hoppers include the NorCal Interscholastic Cycling League, Redwood Trails Alliance, Adventure Therapy Foundation, Be Good Foundation, Redwood Forest Foundation and various high school mountain bike teams." (https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/biking/discover-grasshopper-adventure-series/)
