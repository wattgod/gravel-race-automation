## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Huffmaster Course:**
- Maxwell, California starting point
- "Riders on this stretch frequently battle headwinds" for the first 25 miles of flat paved roads
- Mile 25-40: gradual climb
- Mile 40-43: "punchy gravel climb" topping out at 1,700ft on County Road #303
- "The day's fastest and most challenging gravel descent" from the summit
- Approximately 25 miles of gravel total
- Described as having "less climbing than a typical Hopper"
- Can experience extreme weather conditions requiring Plan B route (https://mtbgirlsmagazine.com/huffmaster-race-2-grasshopper-adventure-series/)

**Low Gap:**
- "A course of two halves, the first of which is comprised of broken tarmac and is climbing focused. The second half is a challenging undulating, redwood lined roller coaster. Short in distance, high in intensity." (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)
- Half pavement and half dirt (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/grasshopper-adventure-series-2025/king-ridge-hopper-5/results/)

**King Ridge:**
- 91 miles with 9,116 feet of elevation gain
- 96% paved despite being in a "gravel heavy series"
- Three timed segments: Joy Road at mile 17.5, Meyers Grad/Hwy 1 at mile 43, and Hwy 1/Kruse Road at mile 60
- Segments account for 22.7 miles and 4,781 feet of vertical gain
- "Remote mountain roads with steep climbs, twisty descents and coastal views of the Pacific Ocean" (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/grasshopper-adventure-series-2025/king-ridge-hopper-5/results/)

**Lake Sonoma:**
- Mountain bike event with "steep gradients both up and down, ribbons of single track"
- "Hard tail mountain bike might just be the best for this event" (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

**Ukiah-Mendo Gravel Epic:**
- "Private trails that are typically closed year round to the public"
- "Near traffic free ride"
- Course gains elevation with glimpses of the coast
- Features redwood forests and turquoise oceans (https://www.sportful.com/US/en/stories/cycling/grasshopper-adventure-series)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-race scene:** "Relax and celebrate with fellow riders at the post-ride party featuring food, drinks, and music" with "hot grilled cheese" at rest stops. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Series/GrasshopperAdventure)

**Inclusive atmosphere:** "All levels of riders‚Äîfrom beginners to World Cup pros‚Äîare welcome, and all can compete without needing a USA Cycling or UCI license. Hoppers feature podiums for male, female, and non-binary riders, and every participant is celebrated for their effort and spirit." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Series/GrasshopperAdventure)

**Community support:** "Roaming course marshalls, course preview videos, and well-stocked rest stops" with Skratch hydration provided. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Series/GrasshopperAdventure)

**Team zones:** "Each event will include a designated Team Zone where teams can set up tents, banners, and a home base. This is your spot to gather, support your riders, and bring your team's vibe to the day." (https://www.grasshopperadventureseries.com/)

**Mentorship initiative:** Low Gap features mentorship program where "20 mentors rode the 50-mile gravel race in California with 20 U19 girls." **Giving back:** "A portion of each registration is donated to organizations including the NorCal Interscholastic Cycling League, Redwood Trails Alliance, Adventure Therapy Foundation, Be Good Foundation, Redwood Forest Foundation, and various high school mountain bike teams."
