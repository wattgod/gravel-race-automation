# GRAVEL AND GRANITE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**The Range Rumble (91km, 2,335m elevation):**
- 59% gravel, 41% bitumen
- Surface mix includes gravel roads, fire tracks, and rough service roads
- Creek crossings present on course
- The crux comes at approximately 50km: "you'll climb for about 10km, including an absolute wall with grades in excess of 15%!"
- Course passes through Basket Swamp and Boonoo Boonoo National Parks
- Described as designed for "Type 2 fun" with extended climbs, fast descents, and rough technical challenges
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**The Wellington Rocks (66km, 1,562m elevation):**
- Nearly half gravel despite being a third shorter than Range Rumble
- Features "spicy downhill sections"
- Passes through Basket Swamp National Park but cuts out the back section through Boonoo Boonoo National Park
- Finishes with "a fast descent down Mt Lindesay Road"
- Sweeping views of the Great Dividing Range
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Basket Swamp Bash (38km, 453m elevation):**
- 54% gravel, 46% bitumen
- Starts with quick downhill section followed by short climb
- Features 5km uphill grind as the crux
- One creek crossing with concrete base, "usually about 25-30cm deep"
- Point-to-point course requiring shuttle or drop-off at start
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Mt Mackenzie Frenzy (24km, 710m elevation):**
- Hill climb race from showground to Mount Mackenzie Lookout
- 12km of climbing with 710m elevation gain
- Narrow sections on descent
- Riders encouraged to "take it easy on the way down"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Curry's Gap Dash (11km, 163m elevation):**
- Gravel is "smooth and approachable for youngsters and riders still finding their feet"
- Less technical compared to longer courses
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Course Evolution Note:**
**Caitlin Reid (event organizer):** "Many of the roads that we have used in previous years are now sealed, so we had to create completely new courses. In 2025, Gravel 'n Granite will feature five new courses designed to cater to riders of all levels"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Types Welcome [ALL LEVELS]:**
- Gravel bikes, mountain bikes, e-bikes, and adaptive bikes/hand cycles are all permitted
- Despite being advertised as a gravel event, organizers explicitly welcome mountain bikes
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c7DtPN9-EbE)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Aid Station Placement [ALL LEVELS]:**

*The Range Rumble:*
- Four fully stocked feed stations at 29km, 46km, 63km, and 73km
- Volunteers strategically placed along course with food, water, and first aid supplies
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

*The Wellington Rocks:*
- Three aid stations at 29km, 38km, and 48km
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

*Basket Swamp Bash:*
- One fully stocked aid station at 20km from start
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Pacing Consideration [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
- For Mt Mackenzie Frenzy: "It's a race to the top, but riders are encouraged to take it easy on the way down as it gets pretty narrow in places"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:**
**Caitlin Reid (event organizer) [COMPETITIVE]:** "While there is a racing aspect to the event with prizes for the fastest two riders in each category, on each course, the focus is on the adventure and the landscape around you"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Caitlin Reid [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's organised to be as welcoming as possible to all riders, regardless of their FTP"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Weekend Experience:**
**Caitlin Reid [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's a weekend for the whole family, starting Friday night in the main street of Tenterfield. Riders can register in town, enjoy live music, and eat at local restaurants offering special events for everyone"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Race Village:**
- Features DJs, food trucks, and local beer and wine at Tenterfield Showgrounds
- Post-race party at Race Village after finish line
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/, https://www.nsw.gov.au/visiting-and-exploring-nsw/nsw-events/gravel-n-granite-tenterfield)

**Accommodation:**
- Camping available at Tenterfield Showgrounds for $15 for whole weekend (book during registration)
- Other accommodations within walking or riding distance
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Off-Bike Activities:**
**Caitlin Reid [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's the perfect spot for those bringing friends and family, with plenty of hiking, swimming holes, and the Railway Museum for kids... There are heaps of things to do – you will never be bored. Gravel 'n Granite is a great opportunity to experience what the town has to offer"
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Event Accessibility:**

## DNF Risk Factors

**Basket Swamp Bash - Logistical Challenge:**
- Point-to-point course requires shuttle bus or personal drop-off arrangements
- Organizers note: "the Saddler's MTB club is working on a shuttle bus, but for now, they are recommending folks have a friend or family member drop them off at the start"
- Could create issues for solo riders without support
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)

**Adaptive Rider Consideration:**
- Creek crossing on Basket Swamp Bash has concrete base "usually about 25-30cm deep" — depth may vary and could be challenging for hand cycles
(https://flowmountainbike.com/post-all/gravel-n-granite-is-returns-in-2025/)
