# GRAVEL AND TAR CLASSIC — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Context

The Gravel and Tar Classic is a UCI 1.2 registered road race held annually in the Manawatu region of New Zealand (between Palmerston North and Feilding), specifically in the Pohangina Valley. The race starts and finishes in the village of Ashhurst.

**Race Format:**
- Men's Classic: 155km with 41.2km of gravel
- Women's La Femme: 91km with 23.1km of gravel
- Approximately 75% sealed roads, 25% gravel roads
- Team-based format (four riders per team)
- Categories include UCI Continental Teams, Trade Teams, Club Teams, Under 19, Elite, and Over 35
- Team Time Trial held on Saturday (20-30km), with points counting toward Sunday's team competition

The race has been held since 2016 and takes place in mid-November (typically around November 16-19). It's considered "one of the toughest one-day cycling events in the Oceania region" according to event organizers (https://greasychain.com/events/gravel-and-tar/).

**Prize Money:** The event notably offers equal prize money for men's and women's races, earning recognition from Cycling New Zealand with the Shona Smith Award for contribution to women's cycling (https://greasychain.com/events/gravel-and-tar/).

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

### Tire Selection [ELITE LEVEL INSIGHTS]

**Jordan Rapp [ELITE]** provides extensive tire testing relevant to gravel racing conditions:

**For Rough/Technical Gravel (ALL-road approach):**
- **Specialized Trigger Pro 38mm:** "The tire I'm glad I chose for Dirty Kanza, where it has a multitude of wins on both the men's and women's side." Ran at 42psi for the race. Notes: "I found there to be a noticeable difference between the feel and performance of the tire off road between 38, 42, and 46psi respectively." At 50psi (initially thought of as low), the tire "felt almost unrideable once I got used to the grip of the tire at 42psi." However, "the Trigger is certainly not designed for pavement" — costs about 25w at 30kph/20mph compared to road-oriented tires. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/gravel-from-the-ground-up-tires-pressure/)

**For Mixed Terrain with More Road (all-ROAD approach):**
- **Compass Snoqualmie Pass 44mm (Extralight):** "Pretty close to the perfect tire" for mixed conditions. Tested by Tom Anhalt "to roll as fast as a Continental GP4000S both on and off road." Optimal at 35psi — "it just rolls over everything, on road and off." WARNING [ELITE]: The Extralight version is "prone to damage when used in MTB-esque terrain with pointy rocks" — "the area where the tread meets the sidewall seems prone to damage." Requires "regular top-ups of sealant" and does have some sidewall bleed-through. "If you can steer clear of pointy and sharp objects, it's pretty close to the perfect tire." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/gravel-from-the-ground-up-tires-pressure/)

**Tire Pressure Insights [ELITE]:**
Rapp notes the importance of precise pressure: "I realized that difference was — in terms of percentage — roughly equivalent to the difference between 110 and 100psi for a 23mm road tire, something I'd definitely notice." During Dirty Kanza, his pressure peaked at 45.5psi in heat, dropped to 38psi by finish due to temperature change and slow air loss over the long day. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/gravel-from-the-ground-up-tires-pressure/)

### Tire Pressure Monitoring [ELITE]

**Quarq TyreWiz** recommended by Jordan Rapp [ELITE]: "Being able to monitor my tire pressure in real time during Kanza was huge peace of mind." Key benefits: 1) Easy field testing of different pressures during training, 2) Real-time monitoring during races to know if you've punctured and how much pressure you've lost, 3) Answers the question "Can you ride it or do you need to stop and air up?" Critical for long events where terrain varies constantly. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/gravel-from-the-ground-up-tires-pressure/)

### Gravel Bike Setup Examples [ELITE]

**Momsen R355 (South African brand):**
- Carbon frame and rigid carbon fork
- Tire clearance: 27.5×2.10 / 700x50c / 29×2.10
- SRAM Force1 1×11 drivetrain with MTB cassette and 40T chainring
- Anti-shock carbon seatpost for comfort
- Stan's ZTR Grail MK3 MTB wheels
(https://sparkbikereview.com/new-gear-momsens-gravel-bike-series/)

**Momsen GP300 (Alloy version):**
- Same tire clearance as R355
- SRAM Apex 1×11 drivetrain
- Tektro Spyre mechanical disc brakes (reviewer notes preference for hydraulic)
(https://sparkbikereview.com/new-gear-momsens-gravel-bike-series/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Jordan Rapp [ELITE]** on gravel racing fundamentals: "Gravel riding is almost entirely about two things: tire choice and tire pressure. Front-end geometry is an important third factor." He emphasizes that "gravel riding is not about the bike. It's about the tires." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/gravel-from-the-ground-up-tires-pressure/)

**Pressure Testing Strategy [ELITE]:**
Rapp recommends "bleed-down testing — starting with obviously too high pressure and testing subsequently lower pressures until I was happy and then going even lower until I was unhappy again" during training to dial in optimal race pressure. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/industry/gravel-from-the-ground-up-tires-pressure/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The gravel cycling community is described with terms like "trauma-bonding" that "speaks to the shared struggle and triumph experienced on gravel rides. Enduring long stretches of rough terrain, shaky descent, battling headwinds, riding through clouds of dust, and weaving up steep climbs foster a unique form of camaraderie. Riders develop an unspoken, helmet-bopping understanding for one another, and it's precisely this shared suffering that brings the community closer together." (https://wildairsports.com/the-growing-gravel-riding-culture/)

**Community Culture:** "The sport is hard, there's no sugar-coating to it, but it's the stories of time spent on the saddle that promote the sport (no matter how harrowing the narrative)." The community attracts "a particular type of cyclist – a purist, dirt-devotee, maverick, endurance-junkie, grit-master, and just someone who is simply insane." (https://wildairsports.com/the-growing-gravel-riding-culture/)

**Event Organization:** The race is organized by the Greasy Chain Charitable Trust, which aims to help "all New Zealanders become fitter through cycling." Support comes from Palmerston North City Council and Manawatu District Council. (https://greasychain.com/events/gravel-and-tar/)
