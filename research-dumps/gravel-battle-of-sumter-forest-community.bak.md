# GRAVEL BATTLE OF SUMTER FOREST — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Marni (Trimarni) [RECREATIONAL]:** "I had no idea what to expect. When I reached the gravel section, it was so dusty that I could barely see anything. This freaked me out a bit and I found myself slowing down to stay safe... Once I went past the railroad crossing (which occurred after a downhill), I was just riding along (famous last words) until I found myself rolling into a deep hole (more like a crater) on a pavement-like crossing. The hole caught me by surprise and it was too late for me to do anything as I don't have the skills to bunny hop holes yet and my reaction times are a work-in-progress. When my front wheel hit the hole at 21 mph, the force of my body weight on the handlebars caused them to rotate forward." (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Karel [RECREATIONAL/COMPETITIVE]:** "Karel had a lot of fun but he said it was crazy - his first gravel race felt much like a road race. Karel finished 3rd in his AG (40-49) and missed the first spot by just a few seconds as he was outsprinted. Karel had nothing left as he cramped near the end." (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Claire Colbert and Kathryn Taylor [UNKNOWN]:** "Being around that many people for the first time in probably over a year was weird, to say the least. I think I can speak for both of us when I say that being around that many people for the first time in probably over a year was weird, to say the least... I've never been to a gravel race where I didn't leave with new friends; the same can't be said for the many triathlons I've raced." (https://feisty.co/p/2021-3-17-we-went-to-gravel-race/)

**Anonymous racer [RECREATIONAL]:** "The Sumter Forest Gravel Event brings us through the best gravel roads around. There was no traffic, the course was very challenging, and the food afterwards hit the spot. This event had me mark down every Southeast Gravel event on my calendar." (https://strambecco.com/event/gravel-battle-sumter-forest/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Structure:** "You do a few miles of gravel and then a few miles of road - rinse and repeat for 72 miles. Although there are a few technical areas (and a river crossing, a few steep hills and a tight rocky turn)" - Marni [RECREATIONAL] (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Key Features:**
- **I-26 Tunnels:** "One of the most iconic shots of the Sumter Forest course is the tunnels that traverse under I-26. Now both the long course and short course get to have fun in those tunnels." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)
- **River Crossing:** "Same downtown start/finish venue in Whitmire, SC. This allows us to be closer to the good gravel, and means that fun river crossing gets to be hit from each direction." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)
- **Finish Through Cornfield:** Warm-up included "riding through a corn field, onto gravel and then a pitchy climb to the finish (an area that we hadn't ridden before because it's on an active shooting range)" (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Difficulty:** "You may think that with Clinton being in the Piedmont region of South Carolina that the terrain means an easier ride. Don't be fooled, everyone who finishes this event knows it was much harder than they anticipated." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)

**Surface Conditions:** "The roads seemed to have a bit more loose gravel than what I was used to on this course so I had to be extra careful and attentive on the hillier sections, especially when there's holes, sand and rocks to dodge" - Marni [RECREATIONAL] (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Pace [COMPETITIVE/ELITE]:** "When the event started, it felt like any other bike race. It was fast, a bit chaotic... Karel was near the front and for the first 6 mile section of gravel, his group average 23.9 mph! His HR was in the mid 160's and pretty much stayed there for the rest of the race. For the next 50 miles, Karel's group averaged 21.5 mph!" (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Dust Management:** "What I didn't expect was the intensity of the group when we hit the first section of gravel (around mile 6). The pace didn't slow down - if anything, it got faster. Because this was all very new to me, I had no idea what to expect. When I reached the gravel section, it was so dusty that I could barely see anything." - Marni [RECREATIONAL] (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Hydration Setup [RECREATIONAL]:** "Since I had two bottles on my bike of sport nutrition along with a Camelback of 1.5L of sport drink, I didn't need to make any stops." - Marni (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Aid Stations:** 
- Long Course: Mile 21, 40, 63
- Short Course: Mile 21, 40
- "You can expect water, carb drink mix, gels, pickle juice, coke, and lot's of other fun snacks!" (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Size:** "With nearly 400 cyclists registered for either the long or short course, there was a lot of energy in the air that people were itching to race." (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Community Vibe:** "I think more than the race itself, an event brings a community together - a community of like-minded individuals who love to ride bikes. It was great to see some familiar faces and to see so many people who just love to ride off road. It was great to see so many female riders there as well." - Marni [RECREATIONAL] (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Social Atmosphere:** "Gravel events are basically just a party with a bike ride to kick things off... You can ride them as hard or as easy as you want on whatever bike you want (for the most part lol). And then everyone gets to share a beer (or two or three!) and usually some good food at the end." (https://feisty.co/p/2021-3-17-we-went-to-gravel-race/)

**Equal Prize Purses:** "With equal pay for both the men and women (which is not typical in many cycling events), this brought more pro women to the event." (https://trimarni.blogspot.com/2021/03/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest-race.html)

**Post-Race:** "Go as hard as you want, and know that a hot meal and a cold refreshing beer will be awaiting you at the finish line. Stick around and cheer on fellow riders who are finishing." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)

**Town Support:** "Whitmire was super excited to bring bike racing to their downtown area... Whitmire was thrilled to be able to bring a bike race into their quaint downtown. Finish right on Main Street and see what the town has to offer." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)

**Camping:** "The town of Whitmire has agreed to allow racers to boondock downtown Friday night. If you have a sprinter van or a small camper, you're free to camp at the Whitmire Community Center Friday night. There will be no hookups of any kind, just primitive camping in a parking lot." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/gravel-battle-of-sumter-forest)
