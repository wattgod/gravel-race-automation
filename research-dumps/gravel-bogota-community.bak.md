# GRAVEL BOGOTÁ — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Context

Gravel Bogotá is part of the UCI Gravel World Series, scheduled for **Sunday, May 24, 2026** in Chía (approximately 10 km north of Bogotá). The event is organized by the same team running UCI Gravel World Series races in Medellín, Brazil, and Chile, plus Gran Fondo events across South America. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravel-bogota/)

**Location Details:**
- **Elevation:** Chía sits at 2,564 meters above sea level
- **Nickname:** "Pueblo de la Luna" (Town of the Moon), named after the Moon goddess in Muisca mythology
- The area is about 10 km north of Bogotá in Cundinamarca department
- Known as an upscale suburban area with proximity to the capital

This is a **UCI qualifier race** where the top 25% of finishers in each age group qualify for the UCI Gravel World Championships. Elite riders compete alongside amateurs in the same events. (https://www.uci.org/article/2026-gravelking-uci-gravel-world-series-a-comprehensive-season/XWSarTMx2S2EhfkqJvKMW)

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

### From TransCordilleras (Multi-Stage Colombian Race)

**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** "Colombians live and breathe cycling like no other nation in the world. Bogota is easily the city with the most bike shops worldwide... Even more impressive is the Ciclovia on Sundays. In all of the big Colombian cities, some of the main roads are closed for cars and cyclists take over the city." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** On Colombian racing style: "Colombians have an incredible amount of 'grinta' which translates as pluck, grit, determination or resolve. They simply love to race like no one else. There was no holding back or saving any kind of energy for the next day during the stage. It felt like they were just thinking of the moment, not what was still coming on the rest of the stage or even the next days. They love to attack whenever they can." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** On Colombian gravel surfaces: "Colombian gravel is not comparable to the smooth surface most of us are used to in Europe or the US." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**Cory Wallace [ELITE]:** On arrival in Bogotá: "Arriving in Bogota with two days to spare before our trip began, we had a chance to explore the local riding scene. I'd always heard that Colombians are passionate about cycling but I never expected to see so many people on bikes. As we merged onto a busy highway at 6am on a Sunday morning we could have been mistaken for thinking we'd entered a Gran Fondo. Hundreds of cyclists on all kinds of bikes zipped casually along." (https://steedcycles.com/blogs/steed-cycles/podcast-colombia-dreaming-part-two)

**Rich Costello [ELITE]:** On Colombian drivers: "For the most part though, the drivers we encountered were friendly and gave plenty of room when passing, often sharing an encouraging honk and a wave. Many Colombians' love cycling and we felt very welcome in the country." (https://steedcycles.com/blogs/steed-cycles/podcast-colombia-dreaming-part-two)

**Rich Costello [ELITE]:** On Colombian cycling culture: "Our final day in Bogota coincided with the final stage of the Tour of Colombia and we pitched up three hours early at the finish area to watch the riders cross the line. The scenes that day will live long. People of all ages chanting the names of their heroes 'Rigo, Rigo, Nairo, Nairo, Egan, Egan'." (https://steedcycles.com/blogs/steed-cycles/podcast-colombia-dreaming-part-two)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Cory Wallace [ELITE]:** Used a Kona Ouroboros gravel bike equipped with:
- 2.25" Maxxis Aspen tires
- 50mm Fox suspension
- Noted: "The Ouroboros with 2.25 Maxxis Aspen tires and 50 mm of Fox suspension was built for this and we rock and rolled passing a number of riders" on a rugged 1000-meter descent through jungle. (http://corywallace.com/2025/04/early-season-racing-25/)

**NOTE:** This is an elite-level setup for multi-day self-supported racing. Wider tires (2.25") and suspension suggest rougher conditions than typical European/US gravel.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**High Altitude Considerations:**
- Chía is at 2,564 meters elevation (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravel-bogota/)
- For reference, nearby climbs in the region reach 3,200-4,150+ meters
- Col d'Iseran (highest paved pass in Europe) is 2,764m — over 1,000 meters lower than many Colombian climbs (http://corywallace.com/2025/04/early-season-racing-25/)

**Surface Conditions:**
**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** "Colombian gravel is not comparable to the smooth surface most of us are used to in Europe or the US." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**Cory Wallace [ELITE]:** Described Colombian conditions including "sticky mud," "huge potholes," and surfaces that get "sun baked" onto bikes after rain. Noted: "The entire afternoon was taken up either eating or washing stuff" after stages. (http://corywallace.com/2025/04/early-season-racing-25/)

**Traffic & Road Conditions:**
**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** "The race takes place in open traffic with huge potholes, dogs chasing you and cows standing in your way all on your daily menu." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**Cory Wallace [ELITE]:** "The last 3 kilometres were then a crazy hunt in open traffic. Mauricio Ardila attacked first. We passed cars left and right, sprinted in front of oncoming buses, cut off a police car to then sprint it out in the last meters." (http://corywallace.com/2025/04/early-season-racing-25/)

## Weather Experienced

**Cory Wallace [ELITE]:** TransCordilleras experience (February-March timeframe):
- "Waking up at 4:30 am... to a solid rainstorm"
- "typical Colombian afternoon rainstorm" when crossing a 3200m pass
- Heavy rain and cold temperatures at high altitude: "Heavy rain and cold temperatures greeted us at the startline... pouring down again with temperatures of less than 5 degrees Celsius at the top" (at 3,850m elevation)
- Hot and exposed conditions at lower elevations: "a hot and exposed 1500 vertical meter climb"
(http://corywallace.com/2025/04/early-season-racing-25/)

**NOTE:** Gravel Bogotá is scheduled for late May (May 24), which may have different weather patterns than February-March experiences.

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Colombian Racing Style:**
**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** "Colombians... simply love to race like no one else. There was no holding back or saving any kind of energy for the next day during the stage... They love to attack whenever they can." This resulted in "Stage two was one to forget for me" and "Stage 5 was a hot and mainly flat one. We wrestled the field down to 8 riders... The last 3 kilometres were then a crazy hunt in open traffic." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**[ELITE] Nutrition Failure:**
**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** "I don't know what I was thinking before stage 1, but the idea to only bring nuts as my on-bike nutrition turned out to be costly on the last 15km of the stage. By that point I was leading the race with Brayan, the brother of Esteban Chaves, when I completely bonked. Losing 10 minutes on the last 15km. The tank was empty and another lesson learned." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

**WARNING:** Elite riders need substantially more on-bike nutrition than nuts alone. Recreational riders should plan accordingly for their own power output and duration.

**Cycling Culture:**
**Cory Wallace [ELITE]:** "Colombians live and breathe cycling like no other nation in the world. Bogota is easily the city with the most bike shops worldwide." (https://steedcycles.com/blogs/steed-cycles/podcast-colombia-dreaming-part-two)

**Sunday Ciclovía:**
**Janosch Wintermantel [ELITE]:** "Even more impressive is the Ciclovia on Sundays. In all of the big Colombian cities, some of the main roads are closed for cars and cyclists take over the city. You find everything there, from professional riders on top notch bikes, to families riding with their kids, to people on old rusty cruisers. It is incredibly diverse, regardless of one's socio-economic or cycling background, everyone seems to come out for it to enjoy cycling together." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/transcordilleras-colombian-adventure)

## Tourist Attractions in Chía

From the official race page:
- Puente del Común: Historic colonial bridge
- Castillo Marroquín: French-style castle (built 1898)
- Capilla Valvanera: Scenic chapel on mountainside with panoramic views
- Parque La Montaña del Oso: Eco-tourism park for hiking
- Peña de Juaica: Mystical mountain with hiking trails
- Festival of the Moon: Celebrates indigenous heritage
- Cuisine: Famous for Colombian dishes like ajiaco; home to iconic restaurants such as Andrés Carne de Res
- Nearby: Salt Cathedral of Zipaquirá and Laguna de Guatavita
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravel-bogota/)
