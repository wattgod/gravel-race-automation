# GRAVEL BRAZIL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Key Details

**Location & Course:**
- Held in Balneário Camboriú/Camboriú, Santa Catarina, Brazil
- First UCI Gravel World Series event in South America (inaugural 2025)
- 90% gravel roads with only small paved sections in the middle (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)
- Course designed with first and last 36km following the same roads, with a southern loop in the middle (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)

**Distances & Elevation:**
- **Granfondo:** 111-120km with 1600-2000m elevation gain (sources vary slightly)
- **Mediofondo:** 60-69km with 900-1117m elevation (qualifier for men 60+, women 50+)
- Time limits: 6h30 for Granfondo, 4h30 for Mediofondo (https://www.ticketsports.com.br/e/2026-uci-gravel-world-series-brazil-73922)

**2025 Results:**

*Men's Elite [ELITE]:*
- Winner: Simon Pellaud (Switzerland, Li Ning Star) - 3:44:43 at 29.37 km/h average
- 2nd: Facundo Jesus Perez Costa (Argentina) +6:53
- 3rd: Daniel Oss (Italy) +14:30
- Notable: 7-minute winning margin, 15+ minutes to third place (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/pellaud-and-nutt-celebrate-in-brazil/)

*Women's Elite [ELITE]:*
- Winner: Madeleine Nutt (UK) - 4:42:50 at 25.48 km/h average
- 2nd: Tamires Fanny Radatz (Brazil) +12:53
- 3rd: Luiza Euzebio De Souza (Brazil) +15:50
- Dominant 13-minute winning margin (https://cyclingflash.com/race/uci-gravel-world-series-gravel-brazil-we-2025/result)

**Participation:**
- ~400 riders registered for 2025 inaugural edition (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/uci-gravel-brazil-first-event-in-south-america/)
- 600 athlete limit for 2026 (https://www.ticketsports.com.br/e/2026-uci-gravel-world-series-brazil-73922)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Race Conditions:**
- "Bright sky with hot temperatures" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/pellaud-and-nutt-celebrate-in-brazil/)
- March 9, 2025 race date confirms early autumn/late summer conditions in Southern Hemisphere

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Climbing Profile:**
- 4km climb starting after 19km with 3.7% average gradient (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)
- "Three short but steep climbs varying between 1 and 2km with gradients up to 10% in the middle part of the course" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)
- Significant 5km climb averaging 7% at the 75km mark (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/uci-gravel-brazil-first-event-in-south-america/)
- "Back side of the climb which was done in the first half" - riders face same climb twice (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)

**Course Design:**
- 90% gravel surfaces (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)
- First and last 36km share the same roads (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)
- Mediofondo avoids the three steepest southern climbs (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/camboriu/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Pacing Data [ELITE]:**
- Simon Pellaud averaged 29.37 km/h (32.07 km/h per alternate source) over 120km with 2000m climbing (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/pellaud-and-nutt-celebrate-in-brazil/)
- Madeleine Nutt averaged 25.48 km/h (23.34 km/h per alternate source) over same distance (https://cyclingflash.com/race/uci-gravel-world-series-gravel-brazil-we-2025/result)
- Large time gaps suggest decisive selection on climbs - 7+ minute men's gap, 13+ minute women's gap

**Event Experience:**
