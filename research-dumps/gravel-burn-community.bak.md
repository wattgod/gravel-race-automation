# GRAVEL BURN — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Key Facts

Gravel Burn (officially "Nedbank Gravel Burn") is a 7-day, 750-800km gravel stage race in South Africa's Eastern Cape region. The inaugural edition ran October 26 - November 1, 2025. The route starts in Knysna (coastal forests), traverses the Great Karoo semi-desert, and finishes at Shamwari Private Game Reserve (Big 5 wildlife territory) with approximately 10,500-11,000m of climbing.

**Prize purse:** $150,000 total (largest in gravel racing history), split equally between men's and women's Pro categories. No prize money for Age Group categories.

**Format:** Solo race with Pro and Age Group categories. All riders complete the same course with mass starts. Full-service race includes tent setup, meals, bike cleaning, and nightly "Burn Camps."

**Stage distances:** Vary between 90-140km per day (2026 specifics to be announced in May). The 2025 race was approximately 800km total.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface conditions:** The race explores "South Africa's most dramatic, remote, and under-explored landscapes" with varying gravel including "wide, open 'Champagne Gravel' to remote and narrow paths." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-stage-race)

**Key geographic sections:**
- Start: Knysna coastal forests and "rolling hills" 
- Middle: Great Karoo semi-desert with "expansive plains, rugged passes, and breathtaking vistas"
- Finish: Shamwari Private Game Reserve "African bushveld" with Big 5 territory

The route passes through towns including "Knysna, Avontuur, Willowmore, and Graaff-Reinet." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-stage-race)

**Technical difficulty:** Described as "750km over 7 days on relatively smooth, non-technical gravel roads. While the demands of the course are lighter than those of a mountain bike stage race, the cumulative fatigue of racing for multiple days shouldn't be underestimated." (https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)

**Wind hazard:** Andri Frischknecht's crash during a neutralized section was caused by "eine seitliche Windböe" (lateral wind gust), indicating significant wind exposure in some areas. (https://www.ride-mtb.com/de/news/sturz-bei-gravel-premiere-schluesselbein-durchsticht-frischknechts-lunge)

## Weather Experienced

**October 2025 conditions (inaugural race):**
- "Extreme Wetterbedingungen" (extreme weather conditions) forced one stage to be neutralized (the stage where Frischknecht crashed on October 31). (https://www.ride-mtb.com/de/news/sturz-bei-gravel-premiere-schluesselbein-durchsticht-frischknechts-lunge)
- A significant storm hit: "After a week lost to stormy weather, the fleet scrambles to make up for lost time" (Stage 3 description). "The first storm of summer arrives to rock the fleet" (Stage 2 description). (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3Tx4dzclTY0)

**Expected October conditions (typical):**
"October marks late spring in South Africa, bringing mild and warm conditions."
- Knysna area: "daytime temperatures typically range between 68-77°F, with cooler evenings"
- Great Karoo: "more extreme conditions: daytime highs can reach 95°F, while temperatures drop sharply after sunset, creating pleasantly cool evenings"
- Recommendation: "Riders should prepare for these fluctuations with sun protection, hydration strategies, and warmer clothing for nights in the Burn Camps."

(https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-stage-race)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Serious injury incident:** Andri Frischknecht suffered a severe crash during a neutralized section on Stage 6 (October 31, 2025) caused by a lateral wind gust. Initial diagnosis: collarbone fracture and concussion. Later discovered a bone fragment punctured his lung, causing left lung collapse. Required operations on both hand and collarbone, plus lung re-inflation. Recovery prognosis: "Sechs Wochen darf ich nicht aufs Mountainbike, aufs Rennrad kann ich aber sicher schon früher" [Six weeks no mountain bike, but road bike probably sooner]. He was in 9th place GC when he crashed. (https://www.ride-mtb.com/de/news/sturz-bei-gravel-premiere-schluesselbein-durchsticht-frischknechts-lunge)

**Weather-related challenges:** Stage neutralization due to "extreme Wetterbedingungen" indicates weather can force course modifications or stoppages. (https://www.ride-mtb.com/de/news/sturz-bei-gravel-premiere-schluesselbein-durchsticht-frischknechts-lunge)

**Time cutoffs:** "riders are required by race rules to finish within the maximum allowed stage time each day" — though specific cutoffs are not detailed in the sources. (https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**"Karoo Nights" in Burn Camps:** "The Burn Camps are more than just overnight recovery stops—they are the heart of what makes Gravel Burn unique. Designed to balance comfort, relaxation, connection, and camaraderie, the camps provide a welcoming refuge after a demanding day in the saddle. Riders retreat to spacious, tented accommodations, where chef-prepared meals and the warmth of communal campfires await. The camps cater to all: there's space for racers who want to prepare optimally for the next stage, but the prevailing atmosphere is one of camaraderie, storytelling, and shared adventure. With massage services, bike technicians, and other essential amenities on hand, riders can choose how to spend their time—whether relaxing, connecting with others, or gearing up for the challenges of the day ahead." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-stage-race)

**Local cultural integration:** "At each overnight Burn Camp, participants enjoy true local hospitality—from live music and storytelling to dishes inspired by and sourced from the surrounding region. Gravel Burn partners with local businesses, farms, communities, and outreach programmes to deliver a memorable rider experience while promoting sustainable tourism and economic opportunities in these often-overlooked rural areas." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-race)

**Pro-am integration:** "It's a rare opportunity for all to ride and live among Olympic and world champions – the finest athletes in the sport – in this true pro-am race." More than 20% of registered riders for the inaugural edition were women. (https://gravel-burn.com/racing/ and https://diverge.info/2025/06/20/gravel-burn-alison-jackson-to-light-up-the-inaugural-race/)

**Rider support during emergencies:** When Frischknecht crashed, he thanked "allen anderen Fahrerinnen und Fahrern für ihre Fairness und dafür, dass sie angehalten und sich um mich gekümmert haben" [all other riders for their fairness and for stopping and taking care of me]. (https://www.ride-mtb.com/de/news/sturz-bei-gravel-premiere-schluesselbein-durchsticht-frischknechts-lunge)

**Event philosophy:** Race rules "emphasises fair play, respectful behaviour and active environmental consciousness." (https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)

**Who it's for:** "Gravel Burn welcomes both competitive cyclists and those drawn to gravel riding for the adventure, experience, and scenery rather than the podium. Elite athletes and seasoned gravel racers will relish the challenge of the course and the competitive field, while fit adventure enthusiasts will discover their own rhythm and joy in the landscapes, camaraderie, and immersive journey." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-stage-race)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike requirements:** "Gravel bikes only" — "We have designed a pure gravel route that is ideally suited for a classic gravel bike (off-road tyres to handle terrain and with drop bars for speed). When we all ride gravel bikes, it levels the playing field and makes it more enjoyable when riding in groups... And wide, straight MTB-style handlebars are perhaps a little less safe when riding at speed amongst drop-bar bikes in a peloton." (https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)

**Gravel suspension debate:** A podcast episode featured "the controversial topic of gravel suspension. Is it a game-changer or an unnecessary addition?" with timestamps at "22:00 - The debate over gravel suspension: Is it worth it?" The show notes asked: "Controversy alert: Should gravel bikes even have suspension? Some argue it's overkill, while others swear by its benefits." (https://ngontinh24.com/article/giant-factory-tour-gravel-burn-bike-tech-with-kevin-bekenstein)

**Evening clothing needs:** Due to temperature swings (up to 95°F daytime in Karoo, "sharply" dropping after sunset), riders need "warmer clothing for nights in the Burn Camps." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-stage-race)

**What's included in entry:** "Full-service race, Gravel Burn offers comprehensive support to riders, encompassing tent setup, meal preparation, bike cleaning, and more, allowing participants to concentrate on the ride and the experience." On-site services include "massage services, bike technicians, and other essential amenities." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/from-the-northwoods-to-south-africa-meet-the-world-s-newest-and-wildest-gravel-race)

## Race Organization & Logistics

**Founder:** Kevin Vermaak, who previously founded the Cape Epic mountain bike stage race. "20 years ago, Kevin Vermaak founded what is now widely regarded as one of the world's premiere mountain bike races. During a 2-year sabbatical after selling the event, he was inspired by his gravel bike expeditions in the Swiss Alps." He sold Cape Epic to The Ironman Group in 2017 and exited in 2022 before launching Gravel Burn in July 2024. (https://gravel-burn.com/ and https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)

**Title sponsor:** "A newly secured multi-year title sponsorship" was announced for unveiling on February 13, 2025 (this enabled the $150,000 prize purse). As of the sources, the title sponsor is Nedbank. (https://capovelo.com/gravel-burn-stage-race-ups-the-ante-to-150000-in-prizing-money/)

**2026 route changes:** "With a New Year comes new improvements. Nedbank Gravel Burn 2026 features an improved route allowing riders more time to soak in the Karoo" with "Start town, Burn Camp locations and stages confirmed" as of January 15, 2026. Start location for 2026 is Graaff-Reinet (changed from Knysna in 2025). (https://gravel-burn.com/)

**Wildlife safety:** "Before we reach Shamwari Private Game Reserve, whenever our gravel roads traverse land with dangerous animals, there will be a game fence between us and the animals. On the final stage into Shamwari, there is a comprehensive safety plan for managing the riders' safety. Shamwari employs the largest team of qualified game rangers in all of South Africa and they will ensure the safety of all our riders on this final short section through Big 5 territory." (https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)

**Media production:** "With world-class television production and media coverage, the event will showcase elite athletes against the breathtaking landscapes of the region, placing Gravel Burn firmly on the international stage." (https://capovelo.com/gravel-burn-stage-race-ups-the-ante-to-150000-in-prizing-money/)

**Entry categories:**
- **Pro category:** Open to all; required to compete for prize money; men and women race in separate start batches
- **Age Group categories:** 19-34, then 5-year increments from 35 (35-39, 40-44, 45-49, 50-54, 55-59, 60-64, 65-69, 70-74); compete for leader jerseys and trophies, no prize money

(https://gravel-burn.com/racing/)
