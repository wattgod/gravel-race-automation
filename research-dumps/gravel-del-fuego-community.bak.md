## Weather Experienced

**April 2025 (250km):** Race started "just as the sun rose, in that chilly dawn light" with "cold morning air." The cold intensified: "in the last 50km, when darkness began to fall again and the cold started biting at my face, hands, and feet." Described as "freezing wind" and temperatures requiring significant cold-weather gear. (https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/gravel-del-fuego-2025-landscapes-too-big-to-frame)

**General timing rationale:** "The race takes place in April, a month with lower wind speeds than spring or summer and a reduced likelihood of rain. While the cold is present, with the right gear, athletes can take on the challenge without major concerns." (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)

**Wind conditions:** Described as one of "the windiest and most remote places you could imagine a gravel race" with riders "hoping for prevailing winds and favourable weather." (https://dotwatcher.cc/race/gravel-del-fuego-2024)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**250km course highlights:** "We started just as the sun rose, in that chilly dawn light. And then, something shifted — I dropped into total connection... I felt every piece of that autumn landscape pour in through my senses — the colours, the wind, the stillness... teñidos de rojizo por el otoño (tinged reddish by autumn)." (https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/gravel-del-fuego-2025-landscapes-too-big-to-frame)

**Specific difficult sections [RECREATIONAL perspective]:** "The headwind at times felt endless... I was afraid I'd break. And I did — at the Milodón sector." CP4 described as brutal: "I was on the edge of another breakdown. The cold, the exhaustion — it all made me doubt. Dust, crashes, freezing wind." (https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/gravel-del-fuego-2025-landscapes-too-big-to-frame)

**250km route description:** "Begins in Puerto Natales, ventures deep into the majestic Torres del Paine National Park, passes through the iconic Milodón Cave, and returns to Puerto Natales via scenic dirt roads." Features "the Almirante Nieto hill, the Cuernos del Paine and the majestic Paine Grande... the end of the Southern Ice Fields with the Grey Glacier, pass through Villa Serrano." (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/ and https://mapmagic.app/blog/2024/01/24/gravel-del-fuego-2024/)

**1000km route:** "Shares the initial 250 kilometers but then heads south into the vast Patagonian steppe, reaching the border with Argentina. From Villa San Gregorio to the ferry crossing into the Isla Grande of Tierra del Fuego, riders journey through breathtaking landscapes and iconic landmarks such as Porvenir, the King Penguin Reserve, Cameron, Lago Blanco, Pampa Guanaco (in the Timaukel commune), and Karukinka Park—culminating at Caleta María." (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)

**Strait of Magellan crossing (1000km):** "Ferry runs every hour, crossing time is approximately 30 minutes, operating hours are from 8:30 to 22:30." Riders must "carry money to pay for passenger and bicycle" and should "be prepared for cold and wind while waiting." (https://graveldelfuego.com/?page_id=10127)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**250km time cutoff:** "Often completed in under a day" with an 18-hour cutoff for the Baqueanos 250km distance. (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)

**1000km time cutoff:** 6.5 days (some sources say 5.5 days) to complete the route. (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)

**Solo riding reality [RECREATIONAL]:** "Riding solo can be beautiful, but it also plays tricks on your mind... I found myself alone, which was more than half the race." (https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/gravel-del-fuego-2025-landscapes-too-big-to-frame)

**Mental breakdown management [RECREATIONAL]:** "I cried because I needed to — in order to keep going... Reaching CP4 was brutal. I was on the edge of another breakdown... But the warmth of the helping hand at the checkpoint gave me strength. That kind of strength that shows up just when you think there's none left." (https://dotwatcher.cc/feature/gravel-del-fuego-2025-landscapes-too-big-to-frame)

**Self-sufficiency requirements:** "Nonstop format where the clock never stops. Athletes embark on an epic odyssey, relying on self-sufficiency: organizing their own food, rest, and strategy. Some opt to sleep each night, while others pedal through without pause." (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)

**Critical planning note:** "Not all checkpoints provide food. Some shelters have maximum stay limits. There are areas with very limited or no services... Do not rely exclusively on kiosks or restaurants. Some minimarkets operate only during daytime. Food may not be available at night." (https://graveldelfuego.com/?page_id=10127)

**Emergency shelter rule:** "The Hosh Backcountry Dome is for emergency use only. Entering the dome or requesting assistance results in disqualification." (https://graveldelfuego.com/?page_id=10127)

## Logistics & Access

**Getting there:** "Your journey starts with a flight to Santiago, the Capital of Chile (SCL). From there, you can fly directly to Puerto Natales (PNT)." Alternative: "If you arrive in Punta Arenas, you need to reach Puerto Natales, buses depart directly from the airport approximately every hour. Travel time: ~3.5 hours, Cost: ~USD 13, Bike transport: ~USD 10." (https://graveldelfuego.com/?page_id=10127)

**Finish locations:**
- 170km & 250km: Start and finish in Puerto Natales
- 1000km: "Finishes in Porvenir... The award ceremony takes place in Punta Arenas (PUQ)" (https://graveldelfuego.com/?page_id=10127)

**Drop bag service (1000km only):** "Maximum allowed size is 10 liters... Deliver the drop bag during race kit pickup... luggage transport is available. On Friday, during the technical briefing, you may leave your bags with us for CLP 9,000 (approx. USD 10). We will deliver them to Punta Arenas." (https://graveldelfuego.com/?page_id=10127)

**Registration cost:** "$105" listed (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)

## Race Philosophy & Background

**Founder's background [UNKNOWN]:** "I began doing mountaineering... then I understood there was something called trail running and I became an auto runner... around Leadville, 100. I did it. I got the big buckle... Then one person told me that if you had a bicycle, I could go super far over 200 Ks." (https://thegravelride.bike/gravel-del-fuego-race-a-patagonian-adventure-with-tito-nazar)

**Event inception:** "I have like a deep passion for ultra stuff... graveling is a new thing in Chile and even more in Patagonia... I want to show the world a different perspective, even to myself. Like I know my region climbing, ice skating, skiing, but graveling is a new thing." (https://thegravelride.bike/gravel-del-fuego-race-a-patagonian-adventure-with-tito-nazar)

**Historical context:** "I'm a very, I want to believe I'm a big fan of history... I think Patagonia is powerful because of our, of the aesthetics, the mountains, of course, but the history that surrounds the mountains is something that is hard to grasp... I was born and raised there. So I want to believe that I have a deep connection with my land." (https://thegravelride.bike/gravel-del-fuego-race-a-patagonian-adventure-with-tito-nazar)

**Positioning:** "The southernmost ultra-cycling race in the world" and "considered one of the most beautiful races in the world." (https://bikepacking.com/event/gravel-del-fuego-2026/)
