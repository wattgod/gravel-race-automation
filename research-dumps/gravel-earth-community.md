## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Ranxo Gravel Course:** "The 160 km Ranxo course starts almost immediately with the day's toughest climb, 5,4 km on gravel with an average gradient of 5.2% and some sections above 15%." Total elevation: 180km with 3,400m climbing, with "more than 1500 meters climbing still to be covered" after km 110. "Half of the elevation gain was concentrated in just the 60 final km, so the script was saying this would be an elimination and survival race." (https://thegravelstack.substack.com/p/recapping-ranxo-and-the-2025-gravel)

**Ranxo Terrain Character:** "The rolling fitness friendly farm roads were linked with singletrack connections and also cyclocross dismount moments. It was much rowdier than anyone expected." Photos make it look like "endless rolling terrain similar to midwest American gravel, but once recon was finished, it was anything but." Course included "rocky sections and technical trails." (https://thefeed.com/insider/race-roundups-with-pete-stetina-the-gravel-earth-series-final)

**Course Selection:** "The early parts [of the first climb] are very steep at around 15% and there are only two viable lines. If you are too far back, you can lose the race right there." After 10 minutes into the race, "the field had already shattered." The course became "more straightforward" by halfway, allowing groups to settle. (https://thegravelstack.substack.com/p/recapping-ranxo-and-the-2025-gravel)

## Series Structure & Controversy

**2026 Calendar Changes:** The series underwent significant restructuring for 2026, reducing from 25 races to 12 events. "Downsizing the series was the right call. The 2025 calendar was bloated: 25 races, little coherence, and no clear storyline. Cutting it down to 12 gives it room to breathe." Age-group racing was introduced. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/gravel-earth-2026-a-series-of-unfortunate)

**Scheduling Controversy:** "Ranxo, a race that's gained a reputation as the GES finale and beloved for its proximity to Girona, has been moved to the week after Unbound. The week after Unbound?! Erm, why? Unbound is the Tour de France of gravel... Holding a race in the backyard of most riders just eight days after the 200-mile Kansas event is, frankly, ridiculous." One week after Ranxo, Lost and Found is scheduled in California. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/gravel-earth-2026-a-series-of-unfortunate)

**Iceland Controversy:** "As if losing The Rift wasn't enough, the creation of Cuckoo Gravel - a Klassmark event in Iceland - has left a sour taste in a lot of mouths. It's scheduled just two weeks before The Rift and, from what's been shared, appears to take place in a similar region and on a similar course... reportedly, The Rift organiser only found out about this new race in the past few weeks." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/gravel-earth-2026-a-series-of-unfortunate)

**Rider Reactions to 2026:** "Within minutes of posting my thoughts about the calendar, I had a large percentage of the GES Top 10 in my DMs. The sentiment was near-unanimous: disappointment... These latest moves seem corporate and calculated." One pro stated: "I feel like we are going to see Life Time eat Gravel Earth Series' lunch as the wealth gap increases." Life Time announced a $590,000 prize pot for 2026. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/gravel-earth-2026-a-series-of-unfortunate)
