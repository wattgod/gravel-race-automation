## OFFICIAL DATA

### Series Overview
The Gravel Earth Series (GES) is the world's leading gravel race circuit, created by Klassmark, a Girona-based events company. The series debuted in 2023 and has grown to become one of the most prestigious gravel racing circuits globally. The series was founded to preserve the adventurous essence of gravel cycling in response to the UCI's formalization of the discipline.

**Founding Vision:** "To provide more of an experience than a competition." - Cristina Freixes, Klassmark Co-owner

**Key Leadership:**
- Gerard Freixes - CEO of Klassmark
- Cristina Freixes - Co-owner of Klassmark

### Scoring System

**Exponential Scoring Structure:**
- Base events: 1,000 points to divisional winner
- +10% events: More challenging races (e.g., The Traka 200)
- +20% events: Most difficult races (e.g., The Traka 360)

**Points Distribution:**
- Points awarded from 1st through multiple positions based on finishing order
- Non-finishers receive no points
- Final ranking based on top 4 results of the season

**2026 Eligibility Requirements (PRO Category):**
- Must finish at least 3 official scoring events
- Must complete The River by The Traka (series finale)
- Must attend closing ceremony at finale to receive awards
- Prizes awarded only in rankings with at least 50 participants

**Age Group Requirements:**
- Must complete at least 2 events with Age Group classification
- Must complete The River by The Traka
- Top 3 scores from Age Group events count toward ranking

### Registration Information
- Registration is handled through individual race websites
- Entry fees vary by event (e.g., Gravel Weekend 2025: starting at EUR 60)
- Oregon Trail Gravel Grinder 2026: Registration currently open

## RACE HISTORY

### Series Evolution

**2023 - Inaugural Season:**
- Series debut with 8 events
- Started April 29 in Girona, Spain with The Traka 360
- Original events: The Traka (Spain), Migration Race (Kenya), The Rift (Iceland), Nordic Gravel Series (Sweden), Octopus Gravel (Switzerland), Nature is Bike (France)
- Points system developed with input from pros Nathan Haas and Lachlan Morton

**2024 - Expansion:**
- First year of official series (some sources list 2024 as inaugural full season)
- Expanded to 20 events
- Added races in USA and Chile
- Champions: Mattia De Marchi (Men) and Annabel Fisher (Women)
- Karolina Migon won Ranxo Gravel finale and overall series (women)

**2025 - Global Growth:**
- 26 events in 16 countries across 4 continents
- Prize purse increased to EUR 37,500
- Added 9 North American races including: Festivus of Gravel, The Range (Canada), Gravel Mexico, Lost & Found, CORE4, Rebecca's Private Idaho, Oregon Trail Gravel Grinder, Lauf Gravel Worlds, Last Grizzly Gravel
- Champions: Rosa Kloser (Women, Germany, Canyon Factory Racing) and Magnus Bak Klaris (Men, Denmark, PAS Racing)
- Simen Nordahl Svendsen (Norway) claimed 2024 title

**2026 - Consolidation:**
- Reduced to 12 events in 7 countries (13 races total)
- Focus on quality over quantity
- Removal of The Rift (Iceland) - replaced by Cuckoo Iceland by The Traka
- 5 of 13 races now operated directly by Klassmark

## TERRAIN & COURSE

### Course Difficulty Classification
The series uses a weighted points system based on course difficulty:
- **Base events:** Standard scoring (1,000 points to winner)
- **+10% events:** Moderate difficulty increase
- **+20% events:** Most challenging courses (e.g., The Traka 360)

### Flagship Event: The Traka (Girona, Spain)

**The Traka 360:**
- Distance: 335km (original 360km, may be shortened for conditions)
- Elevation: 3,700m
- Terrain: 85% gravel, 15% paved
- Features: Forest trails, rocky descents, punchy climbs, technical singletrack
- Start: 5:50 AM
- Typical field: ~800 riders
- Course sections: Flowing terrain outside Girona, high climbing section with sea views, single track, some hike-a-bike sections

**The Traka 200:**
- Distance: 200km
- Scoring: +10% points multiplier
- Terrain similar to 360 but shorter

**The Traka 100:**
- Distance: 100km
- Elevation: 1,500m
- Terrain: 79% unpaved

**The Traka Adventure (560km):**
- Ultra-distance event (cancelled in 2024 due to weather)
- Single track, hike-a-bike climbs, rocky descents

### Migration Gravel Race (Kenya)
- **Reputation:** "The hardest and wildest gravel race in the world"
- **Format:** 4-stage race
- **Total Distance:** 450-650km
- **Total Elevation:** 8,000m
- **Average Altitude:** 1,900m (equatorial location)
- **Terrain:** Single track, game trails, red clay, rough hard-pack gravel, cattle tracks, wildlife paths
- **Notable Features:**
  - River crossings
  - Wildlife encounters (giraffes, zebras, wildebeest, elephants)
  - Maasai villages
  - "Dutch Mountains" headwinds on open plains
  - Laterite ground (rich red color from iron/aluminum oxides)
  - Climbs to 2,800m at escarpment
  - ~20% completion rate (40 of 200 registrants finished in 2024)

### The Rift (Iceland) - Removed from 2026 Calendar
- **Distance:** 200km or 120km
- **Elevation:** 2,600m (200km route)
- **Terrain:** Lava fields, volcanic sand, tectonic rift zone, moss-covered fields, glacial rivers
- **Features:**
  - Water crossings (half a 700c wheel depth, 5-10m wide)
  - Hike-a-bike climbs
  - Wind-blasted plains
- **Scoring:** +10% bonus points
- **Conditions:** Extreme - horizontal rain, sudden weather changes, 10C (50F) average

### Oregon Trail Gravel Grinder (USA)
- **Format:** 5-day stage race ("Grand Tour of Gravel")
- **Total Distance:** ~580km (350 miles)
- **Total Elevation:** 25,000-33,000 feet
- **Location:** Cascade Mountains, Oregon

## WEATHER

### Regional Weather Profiles

**Girona, Spain (The Traka, La Santa Vall, Ranxo):**
- **General:** Mediterranean climate, mild year-round
- **Average highs:** Always double digits (Celsius)
- **Average lows:** Never below 2C even in winter
- **Summer:** Average highs of 29C
- **Peak season:** April-June and September-October
- **Race conditions:** Variable - 2023 was idyllic, 2024 saw severe storms

**The Traka 2024 Weather Crisis:**
- Pre-race: Heavy rain, muddy trails, flooded sections
- Adventure distance (560km): Cancelled for safety
- Other distances: Proceeded with modified routes
- Course shortened from 360km to 344km
- Start delayed 1 hour
- Aftermath: Muddy, creek crossings at trail junctions
- Recovery: Weekend distances held under "radiant sun"

**Iceland (The Rift / Cuckoo Iceland):**
- **July average:** 10C (50F)
- **Conditions:** Highly unpredictable
- **Possible weather:** Strong winds, horizontal rain, sudden hail, snow
- **Visibility:** Can be limited by low-hanging clouds
- **Sun can become snow in minutes**

**Kenya (Migration Gravel Race):**
- **Temperature range:** 65-70F (18-21C) typical
- **Conditions:** Variable - hot and dusty OR muddy with rain
- **Altitude effect:** Temperature drops with elevation gain
- **Challenges:**
  - Intense heat on open plains
  - Strong afternoon winds
  - Flash rain storms
  - Black clay mud when wet
  - Limited shelter from heat

**Oregon, USA (Oregon Trail Gravel Grinder):**
- **Season:** Spring (mild temperatures)
- **Terrain variation:** "From sand to snow"
- **Challenges:**
  - Potential snow on course requiring route modifications
  - Possible extreme heat waves
  - Start times may be adjusted for excessive heat

### Weather Preparation Tips
- Always check weather apps frequently in days before race
- Understand course geography (where climbs are, terrain types)
- Prepare gear for multiple weather scenarios
- Be ready for sudden changes (especially Iceland, Kenya)

## FORUM INSIGHTS

### Rider Community Perspective

**The Traka Experience (from cycling forums):**

**Positive Feedback:**
- "The most scenic gravel I've ever ridden"
- "Well-organized with great community feel"
- "Europe's flagship gravel event"
- "You can just turn up with your flat bar mountain bike and baggies or your aero gravel bike and lycra and just ride your own style"

**Critical Feedback:**
- "360 kilometers is a long way" - lessons learned the hard way
- "Riding around Regent's Park means nothing when you have 2,500m of off-road climbing"
- Training specificity matters - need long, punchy off-road climbs

**Gravel Bike General Forum Sentiment:**
- Some riders find gravel bikes limiting: "I absolutely hated my gravel bike experience"
- Terrain suitability is key: "A gravel bike can REALLY suck off road on rough, unmaintained doubletrack"
- When used appropriately: "Great rides when I knew the route and surfaces"
- Wider appeal: "Ideal for riders who enjoy exploring diverse terrains"

### Strategy & Tips from Community

**Start Positioning:**
- "Get to the start as early as possible so you don't end up at the back from the get-go"
- Mass-start events create bottlenecks if caught mid-pack

**Training:**
- "People have cracked the code and realized that you just have to train like a pro road rider and then ride the gravel bike a little bit"
- Need specificity: long off-road climbing, uneven surfaces

**Race Day:**
- Quick transitions through aid stations
- "Know what you need, get it fast, and roll out"
- Eat every 45 minutes, hydrate constantly
- Practice fueling strategy in training

**Tire Selection:**
- Wide, supple tires are "secret weapon" on rocky, loose descents
- "Go as wide as your frame allows" - Lachlan Morton
- 44mm Vittoria Mezcals popular choice
- In mud: Some size down to 38mm for aero profile and mud-cutting

- "It felt human. It felt independent. It felt like ours. Built on passion."
- Strong YouTube storytelling and live streams
- Genuine sense of community

**Concerns (2026 Calendar):**

## BLACK PILL (Critical Analysis)

### Series Criticisms & Controversies

**2026 Calendar Problems:**
1. **Schedule Conflicts:**
   - Ranxo Gravel moved to week after Unbound Gravel
   - "Unbound is the Tour de France of gravel. Holding a race just eight days after the 200-mile Kansas event is, frankly, ridiculous"
   - Forces riders to choose between premier events

2. **Loss of Key Races:**
   - The Rift (Iceland) dropped from calendar despite being "one of Europe's gravel monuments"
   - Featured in GES since beginning
   - Gravel Worlds (Nebraska) also absent - "American classic with six-figure prize pot"

3. **Cuckoo Gravel Controversy:**
   - New Klassmark event in Iceland, scheduled 2 weeks before The Rift
   - "Similar region and similar course"
   - The Rift organizer reportedly only found out about new race weeks prior
   - "Optics aren't good here"
   - "This move feels like something dreamed up in a corporate boardroom, not a family business"

4. **Consolidation Concerns:**
   - Reduced from 23 to 13 events
   - 5 of 13 races now directly operated by Klassmark
   - Perceived shift from collaborative to corporate

**Competition Threat:**
- Life Time Grand Prix: $590,000 prize purse for 2026 (vs EUR 37,500 for GES)
- Pro rider quote: "I feel like we are going to see Life Time eat Gravel Earth Series' lunch as the wealth gap increases"
- Life Time offers $50,000 to series winner; GES offers EUR 2,500

**The Traka 2024 Issues:**
- Adventure distance (560km) cancelled due to weather
- Course modifications required for safety
- Some participants criticized cancellation of ultra-distance event
- Debate: "Should an endurance and ultra-distance test be suspended due to weather conditions?"
- Penalty controversy: Winner stripped of victory for infraction

**DNF & Completion Challenges:**
- Migration Gravel Race: ~20% completion rate (40 of 200 finished in 2024)
- Extreme conditions contribute to high attrition
- Most gravel races don't report DNF statistics
- Long-distance events (360km+) naturally have higher dropout

**Prize Money Disparity:**
- GES total purse: EUR 37,500
- Life Time Grand Prix 2026: $590,000
- Unbound Gravel 2026: $60,000 single event purse
- Gravel Burn: $150,000 single event purse
- Lauf Gravel Worlds: $100,000 purse

**Jet Lag Calendar:**
- Events span 4 continents
- Irony noted: "No Nature, No Future" slogan "plastered across a calendar that gives you jet lag just looking at it"

### Structural Weaknesses
- Reliance on single organizer (Klassmark) for multiple key events
- Regional focus shifting (strong Europe, growing NA, limited elsewhere)
- No clear pathway for amateur-to-pro progression compared to UCI events

## GEAR RECOMMENDATIONS

### Bike Setup

**Tire Recommendations:**
| Width | Best For | Popular Choices |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| 45mm | General Traka/GES racing | Pirelli Cinturato Gravel M (most popular at Traka) |
| 44mm | All-around performance | Vittoria Mezcal |
| 42mm | Faster courses | Specialized Pathfinder |
| 38mm | Muddy conditions | Vittoria Terreno Mix (better mud cutting) |
| 50mm | Rough terrain | Maximum comfort, practical for rough sections |

**Tire Pressure Guidelines:**
- 40mm tyre on 700c: ~40psi starting point
- 45mm+ on 650b: ~35psi starting point
- Lower pressure = more comfort and grip

**Wheel Recommendations:**
| Wheel Size | Best For |
|------------|----------|
| 700c | Mixed terrain, speed priority, smoother gravel |
| 650b | Technical terrain, wider tires, more traction |

**Top Wheelset Picks:**
- **Best Overall:** Zipp 303 Firecrest
- **Climbing/Comfort:** Campagnolo Levante
- **Race Performance:** DT Swiss GRC 1100 DICUT 50
- **Budget Pick:** Hunt 4 Season Gravel Disc X-Wide
- **Carbon Budget:** Prime Orra V2 Carbon

### Electronics & Navigation
- **Recommended:** Garmin Edge 1040 Solar (40 hours battery)
- **Alternative:** Wahoo ELEMNT Ace (large display, powerful battery)
- **Garmin Edge 540:** 27 hours battery

### Puncture Protection
- Tubeless setup mandatory for serious racing
- Dynaplug or Stan's Dart plugs (most popular systems)
- Carry needle and thread for large tears

### Gearing
- Compact crankset recommended
- Wide range cassette for steep off-road climbs
- Stay seated on loose climbs to maintain traction

### Long Distance Equipment (360km+)
- Lights required for The Traka 360
- Aero clip-on bars for efficiency on road sections
- Cargo bib shorts for accessible nutrition storage
- Additional frame/saddle bag storage

### Nutrition & Hydration
- Eat every 45 minutes
- Hydrate constantly (even when not thirsty)
- Pre-race: ~900g carbohydrates over 3 days
- During: 180g+ carbs per bottle possible
- Carry caffeine gels/bars for late-race boost
- Electrolyte supplementation essential

### Clothing
- Lightweight jerseys
- Cargo bibs (snack accessibility)
- Protective mitts
- Prepare for variable conditions (layers)

### Who Should Race Gravel Earth Series

**Ideal For:**
- European-based gravel racers seeking premier competition
- Adventure-focused riders wanting unique global experiences
- Riders seeking strong community atmosphere
- Those wanting alternative to Life Time / UCI racing
- Age group competitors seeking meaningful rankings

**Less Ideal For:**
- Prize money-focused professional racers
- Riders unable to travel internationally
- Those seeking primarily USA-based racing
- Riders needing consistent, predictable conditions

### Final Assessment

The Gravel Earth Series represents the soul of gravel racing - adventure, community, and global exploration. While it cannot match Life Time's financial resources, it offers something arguably more valuable: authentic, human-scale racing in some of the world's most spectacular locations.

**Strengths:**
- Unmatched course variety and scenery
- Strong community and inclusive atmosphere
- Unique events like Migration (Kenya) and The Rift (Iceland)
- European stronghold with The Traka as crown jewel
- Sustainable, values-driven organization

**Weaknesses:**
- Significantly lower prize money than competitors
- 2026 calendar controversies (schedule conflicts, lost events)
- Increasing Klassmark control may reduce independence
- Logistical complexity for international travel
- Competition from better-funded series

**Trajectory:** At a crossroads. The 2026 consolidation and controversies will test whether GES can maintain its identity while competing with deep-pocketed rivals. Success depends on staying true to the adventure-first ethos that made it special.
