## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN]:** Upgraded to Fox 32 TC gravel suspension fork after struggling with vibrations in previous years: "Les premiers tests sont plus que concluants : ce n'est plus le même vélo. L'efficacité en tout terrain est bien meilleure. Fini les vibrations, la roue reste en plus collée au sol et les montées caillouteuses sont bien plus faciles, le vélo ne perdant plus de vitesse en buttant sur les gros cailloux."

Translation: The first tests are more than conclusive: it's not the same bike anymore. The efficiency on all terrain is much better. No more vibrations, the wheel stays glued to the ground and rocky climbs are much easier, the bike no longer loses speed by hitting big rocks. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN]:** Tire choice for potentially wet conditions: Schwalbe RX Pro 45mm tires (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN]:** Drivetrain setup for the Ultra 500: Switched to 1x (single chainring) with 36t front and 9-46 MTB cassette rear. "Le mono-plateau facilite les changements de vitesse rapide en tout terrain. Le pignon de 9 dents me permet de conserver un développement adapté descente, pendant que le grand pignon de 46 me permettra de grimper les raidars de la forêt de Chinon."

Translation: Single chainring makes quick gear changes easier in all terrain. The 9-tooth cog allows me to maintain appropriate gearing for descents, while the large 46-tooth cog will let me climb the steep sections of the Chinon forest. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN]:** Previous attempt with Vecnum suspension stem: "très efficace mais loin d'une fourche VTT" (very effective but far from a MTB fork). Also tested doing ultra gravel on a hardtail MTB (semi-rigid) which provided excellent comfort. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN]:** For overnight riding, brought thermal jersey in drop bag at CP1 for the cold night sections. This proved adequate for overnight temperatures. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Edition (September 26-28):** Cold and wet conditions. KHS91 reported: "il ne fait plus de 12 degrés dehors" (no more than 12°C outside) during the day on Friday. Night temperatures required thermal clothing. Weather described as "dans la grisaille et l'humidité" (in greyness and humidity) on Friday, with sun finally appearing on Saturday morning. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**2024 Edition:** KHS91 mentioned not having cold issues during the 2024 night unlike the previous attempt, suggesting variable year-to-year conditions. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Evolution:** The Ultra distance has increased each year: 400km in first edition, 500km in 2024, 540km in 2025 with an additional loop in the Alpes Mancelles after CP1. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Ultra 500 Route (2025):** Starts from Meudon (near Paris Velodrome), passes through Chevreuse Valley, crosses Beauce region and Orléanais at night, follows Loire riverbanks past Château de Chambord and Château d'Amboise, goes around medieval city of Loches and Brenne natural park, finishes at La Manu in Châtellerault. Total 540km. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Checkpoint Locations:**
- CP1: Bellème (km ~200)
- CP2: La Bazoge (km 320)
- Finish: Châtellerault (~540km)

**Difficult Sections:** KHS91 notes: "La partie entre le CP1 et le CP2 est la plus difficile, dans le Perche puis les Alpes Mancelles, avec de longues montées dont celle du Belvédère de Perseigne."

Translation: The section between CP1 and CP2 is the most difficult, in the Perche then the Alpes Mancelles, with long climbs including the Belvédère de Perseigne. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**New Feature in Alpes Mancelles:** Chain-pulled ferry crossing over the Sarthe River called "Bac le Passage" - riders pull themselves across on a chain. KHS91 reports it's not physically demanding: "il suffit de tirer la chaine et le bac avance sans gros effort" (you just pull the chain and the ferry moves without much effort). (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Le Mans Section:** Much improved routing in 2025 compared to 2024, staying on greenways and riverbanks throughout the city. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Post-Le Mans Terrain:** "de belles allées roulantes, c'est du vrai gravel sur cette partie très agréable à rouler avec des paysages de vallées et de collines dans les champs" (beautiful rolling paths, it's real gravel on this very pleasant section to ride with valley and hillside landscapes in the fields). (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**First 150km:** Described as having no notable difficulties. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Terrain Character:** French gravel events often use MTB trails, making them rougher and more technical than typical US gravel events. The jarring/vibrations are significant without suspension. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Elevation:** Ultra 500 listed as 3400m D+ for 500km version. (https://gravelfever.fr/en/500-km/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN] - Pacing Strategy:** "depuis le départ je tiens le rythme que je commence à maitriser et dont je sais que je peux le tenir et même accélérer jusqu'à la fin" (from the start I hold the rhythm that I'm starting to master and that I know I can hold and even accelerate until the end). After CP2, when following a fast rider: "je suis à 200W, ce n'est pas raisonnable, je le laisse filer" (I'm at 200W, it's not reasonable, I let him go). This suggests his sustainable pace was below 200W. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN] - Night Riding Performance:** Climbed significantly in rankings overnight, recovering ~40 places between CP1 and CP2, suggesting many riders started too fast and faded. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN] - Checkpoint Timing:** 
- CP1 arrival: "un peu avant 20h" (shortly before 8pm) - approximately on schedule
- CP1 stop: "un peu moins d'1h de pause" (a bit less than 1 hour pause)
- CP2 timing: dawn arrival (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**KHS91 [UNKNOWN] - Food/Rest Strategy:** Planned to resupply at Tréon (Carrefour Contact) mid-day on Friday, took 40-minute stop including hot coffee. Took advantage of hot soup at CP1 which "me redonne plein de forces" (gives me back so much strength). Did not sleep at CP1 as wasn't feeling tired. Later took a critical 15-minute nap on a bench by the river when feeling drowsy after CP2: "il suffit de quelques minutes pour se sentir bien mieux" (just a few minutes are enough to feel much better). (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Drop Bag Strategy:** Event provides drop bags - one goes to CP1, another to finish in Châtellerault. KHS91 used CP1 bag for resupply of bars/gels and a thermal jersey for night riding. Race bag system allows carrying items needed at first checkpoint and finish. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Notable Elite Performance:** Victor Bosoni, 23-year-old winner of 2025 Trans Continental Race, participated in 2025 edition. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues - Early Flats:** KHS91's teammate Yann suffered a major tire tear around km 150 on relatively non-technical terrain - "tesson caché sous les feuilles" (glass hidden under leaves). The tear was too large for tire plugs to seal. Lost 1.5 hours finding a bike shop in Versailles to replace the tire, nearly abandoned. Another teammate Benjamin also flatted 5km later but was able to seal it with preventive sealant. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Pacing Errors:** KHS91 observed many riders starting too fast in 2025: "Il semble que beaucoup sont partis très fort cette année mais commencent à le payer. Sans doute un manque d'expérience de la distance" (It seems many started very hard this year but are starting to pay for it. Probably a lack of experience with the distance). He began passing struggling riders once the real climbs started in the Perche region. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Sleep Management:** Riders found sleeping on the course - KHS91 mentions "quelques concurrents endormis sur la terrasse d'une maison" (several competitors asleep on a house terrace) during the night section. Those who don't manage sleep/drowsiness risk safety issues. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Water Availability at Night:** Water resupply can be challenging overnight when everything is closed. KHS91 specifically planned a stop at Saint Leonard des Bois for water refill where he found an automatic vending machine. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Organization Quality:** Consistently praised across sources. KHS91: "Organisation toujours au top, c'est la raison qui fait que je reviens chaque année, Jean-Christophe Savignoni et son équipe sont aux petits soins pour nous, et cela fait vraiment plaisir"

Translation: Organization always at the top, it's the reason I come back every year, Jean-Christophe Savignoni and his team take great care of us, and it really is a pleasure. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Start Experience:** Coffee service at La Loggia at the foot of Meudon Observatory before the Ultra 500 start. Briefing by race director JC Savignoni before departure. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Checkpoint Support:** Hot food at all checkpoints including hamburgers, soup, sandwiches, cakes, drinks, plus rest rooms for sleeping. The vermicelli soup is particularly appreciated by returning riders. Showers available at finish regardless of arrival time, plus rest room for night finishers. (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Event Venue - La Manu:** Historic former weapons manufacturing site from 1819 in Châtellerault, now cultural venue with two large chimneys as landmarks. Houses automobile and bicycle museum, ice rink, skatepark, and circus school. Panoramic platform on towers overlooks the city. (https://en.3bikes.fr/2023/08/30/le-gravel-fever-en-octobre-un-evenement-a-ne-pas-manquer/)

**Festival Atmosphere:** Multiple distance options (50km, 80km, 120km, 150km, 200km, 500km), food trucks, free entertainment, BMX and skateboarding, DJ sets. Described as "a gravel festival for everyone." (https://gravelfever.fr/en/)

**Growing Prestige:** 2025 edition hosted the very first FFC French Gravel Championships. Event is gaining recognition - 3bikes.fr noted: "Signe que l'épruve devient très connue" (Sign that the event is becoming very well known). (https://khs91-passionvtt.blogspot.com/2025/09/vendredi-26092025-la-gravel-fever-ultra.html)

**Course Design:** Sylvain Chavanel (former pro cyclist from Châtellerault) designed parts of the courses, particularly the Race 160. (https://en.3bikes.fr/2023/08/30/le-gravel-fever-en-octobre-un-evenement-a-ne-pas-manquer/)

**Inclusive Approach:** Non-FFC members can participate in French Championships distances in their category and be ranked, just not eligible for the podium. Multiple non-timed/unranked distance options available. (https://gravelfever.fr/en/)

**Support Services Included:** GPS tracking for safety, luggage transport from Meudon to Châtellerault, race bag transport to checkpoints, numbered handlebar plate, Matchy accessory, accident insurance, free guarded bike parking, free luggage storage. (https://gravelfever.fr/en/500-km/)
