# UCI GRAVEL SUISSE / THE MAJESTICS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Details & Course Profile

**The Majestics** (formerly "Gravel Suisse") was first organized in 2024 and has been awarded the UCI Gravel World Championships for 2029. The race takes place at the doorsteps of the UCI headquarters in Aigle, Switzerland, in the mountains of the Vaudois Alps.

**Course distances:**
- 104km granfondo (qualifier for men 19-59, women 19-49): 3440m total elevation
- 72km mediofondo (qualifier for men 60+, women 50+): 2380m total elevation

**Course profile (granfondo):**
- Start: UCI headquarters in Aigle at 380m elevation
- First 10km: Flat paved roads toward Ollon
- First climb (km 10-15): 5km offroad climb at 10% average gradient
- Valley section until km 50, where "real climbing begins"
- Second half: Mostly offroad through woods, climbing in stages from valley to finish
- Highest point: 1819m elevation (11km before finish)
- Route des Chaux (after 79km): Very steep 20% section
- Finish: Col de la Croix near Les Mazots ski station
- Last 2km: Challenging road to Col de la Croix

**Elevation distribution:** First half has 1180m of climbing; second half has 2300m of climbing.

(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The course is described as "pure gravel" that keeps riders "really in nature, with no traffic, proper gravel climbs and technical descents." The race is characterized as having less tarmac than some UCI gravel events that "feel more like road races."

The course is "constantly up and down" with technical, fast gravel downhills. The final gravel downhill before the finish is noted as "technical, fast, and then you drop down towards the line."

The course is "easy accessible for spectators who can ride from one part to another making shortcuts to see the riders on different locations."

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early reconnaissance recommended [ELITE perspective]:**

**Tessa Neefjes [ELITE]:** "I like to arrive early and do proper recons. I study the climbs, the downhills, the gravel and the lines... I want to know where time can be gained and where attacks might happen. That preparation is really important." (https://www.grallochgravel.com/journal/tessa-neefjes-on-defending-her-gralloch-crown-belief-gravel-and-coming-back-stronger)

**Fueling strategy [ELITE perspective]:**

**Tessa Neefjes [ELITE]:** "It's constantly up and down, and it can be easy to forget to drink and eat enough. Train your fuelling so you still feel strong at the finish... If you know the course, you know where the top of the climb is. You know it's just one more minute. That really helps in hard moments." (https://www.grallochgravel.com/journal/tessa-neefjes-on-defending-her-gralloch-crown-belief-gravel-and-coming-back-stronger)

*Note: Specific fueling quantities and pacing data not provided in sources. The above advice comes from an elite-level rider and may need adjustment for recreational finishers.*

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The race is described as having a festival-like atmosphere. The start area relocates to the UCI headquarters in Aigle, creating a unique setting.

**Tessa Neefjes [ELITE]:** "It feels like a festival before and after the race. The start area is really nice, the people are kind, and everything just comes together. That's a big reason why I love coming back to Switzerland... You finish and there are happy people everywhere. You can sit on the grass, eat, drink, relax. It feels like a celebration, and that makes the experience really special." (https://www.grallochgravel.com/journal/tessa-neefjes-on-defending-her-gralloch-crown-belief-gravel-and-coming-back-stronger)

**Cross-community connection:**

**Tessa Neefjes [ELITE]:** "After the race, you can talk with amateur riders. You've all ridden the same course, so you can really share the experience... For me, that connection is a big part of what makes gravel racing so unique." (https://www.grallochgravel.com/journal/tessa-neefjes-on-defending-her-gralloch-crown-belief-gravel-and-coming-back-stronger)

*Note: The interview quote references "The Gralloch" but appears in the UCI Gravel Suisse/Majestics source context, suggesting possible confusion in source material.*

## Location & Tourism Context

**Villars-sur-Ollon** is located in the canton of Vaud at 1,300 meters altitude. The village is accessible via scenic train ride from Aigle with views of surrounding mountains.

**Regional attractions:**
- Part of the Villars-Gryon-Les Diablerets ski area (120+ km of slopes)
- Summer hiking/mountain biking through alpine meadows and forests
- 18-hole golf course with views of Dents-du-Midi and Mont Blanc
- Thermal baths of Lavey-les-Bains nearby
- Proximity to Montreux Jazz Festival
- Bex-Villars-Bretaye rack railway offering panoramic views

The area offers year-round appeal as both winter sports destination and summer outdoor recreation hub.
