## UCI Gravel Suisse / The Majestics

### Official Website and Registration

- **Official Website:** https://ucigravelsuisse.com/en/
- **Alternative Site (The Majestics branding):** https://themajestics.ch/en/
- **Registration Page:** https://ucigravelsuisse.com/en/inscriptions/
- **UCI Event Page:** https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/

### Race Dates

#### 2025 Edition
- **Date:** June 28, 2025 (Saturday)
- **Location:** Villars-sur-Ollon / Aigle, Switzerland
- **Event Type:** Part of UCI Gravel World Series

#### 2026 Edition (The Majestics)
- **Date:** July 12, 2026 (Sunday)
- **Location:** Villars-sur-Ollon / Aigle, Switzerland
- **Full Weekend Schedule:**
  - Friday, July 10: Time Trial
  - Saturday, July 11: Road Race (Gran Fondo)
  - Sunday, July 12: Gravel Race
- **Registration Opened:** November 18, 2025

### Course Details

#### Long Route (Granfondo / "The Summit")
- **Distance:** 104-110 km
- **Elevation Gain:** 3,300-3,440 m
- **Starting Elevation:** 380 m (Aigle, at UCI headquarters)
- **Highest Point:** 1,819 m (Col de la Croix area, reached 11 km before finish)
- **Terrain:** Mix of paved roads and gravel/off-road sections
- **Surface Split:** Approximately 50% paved / 50% unpaved
- **Qualification:** For all age groups seeking UCI Gravel World Championship qualification

#### Medium Route (Mediofondo)
- **Distance:** 61-72 km
- **Elevation Gain:** 1,800-2,380 m
- **Qualification:** Official qualifying distance for men 60+ and women 50+

### Course Profile and Terrain

**First Half (relatively easier):**
- Start at UCI World Cycling Center in Aigle
- First 10 km on flat paved roads towards Ollon
- At Ollon: First 5 km off-road climb at 10% average gradient
- Return via Ollon after 20 km
- Stay in Rhone Valley until km 50
- Total elevation first half: ~1,180 m

**Second Half (challenging):**
- Real climbing begins at km 50
- Mostly in woods and off-road
- Very steep 20% section on Route des Chaux after km 79
- Total elevation second half: ~2,300 m
- Finish at Col de la Croix

**Route Path:**
Aigle - Saint-Triphon - Ollon - Verschiez - Plambuit - Panex - Ollon - Antagnes - Lavey-les-Bains - Lavey - Bex - Fenalet-sur-Bex - Les Posses-sur-Bex - Plans-sur-Bex - Solalex - Frience - Taveyanne - Ensex - Bretaye - Col de la Croix

### Location and Start/Finish Points

- **Start Location:** UCI World Cycling Center, Aigle, Switzerland
- **Finish Location:** Col de la Croix / Villars-sur-Ollon area
- **Region:** Canton of Vaud, Vaudois Alps
- **Altitude of Villars:** 1,300 m above sea level

### Entry Fees and Requirements

#### Entry Fee Structure
- Early registration discounts available (deadline typically June 1)
- Specific prices displayed on the registration platform at ucigravelsuisse.com
- Route change fee: CHF 10.00 (plus price difference if upgrading)
- Postponement to following year: CHF 35.00 (requires medical certificate)
- Protest deposit: CHF 100.00 (refunded if allowed)

#### Member Discounts
- Swiss Cycling members: 20% discount (email info@rd-cycling.com with membership number)
- #fastandfemaleSUI members: 15% discount (email info@rd-cycling.com with membership number)

#### Requirements
- Participants must be 18+ (or turning 18 in the calendar year with parental consent)
- No specific license required for age group participation
- Must be medically fit to participate
- Gravel bike or similar suitable equipment

#### What's Included
- Welcome bag
- l'Echappee beer
- Partner gifts
- PDF and GPX roadbook of the route
- Frame plate and bib number
- Feed zones with refreshments
- Mechanical and medical support

### Cutoff Times

- Specific cutoff times are determined by local organizers in coordination with authorities
- After a specified time frame (typically 30 minutes to 1 hour after course closure), riders are passed by a motorcycle with a green flag
- Riders passed by the green flag moto must respect road traffic regulations
- Exact cutoff times communicated via website, briefings, and at the start

### Aid Station Information

#### UCI Gravel World Series Requirements
- Feed zones provided every 25-30 km
- Races under 90 km: Minimum 2 feed zones
- Races over 90 km: Minimum 3 feed zones
- Signs placed 5 km ahead of each zone
- Feed zones provide drinks, food, and basic tools
- Beverage provided at finish line

#### Volunteer Support
- Over a dozen volunteer sites along the route
- Mechanical assistance available
- Medical support available

### Accommodation and Travel Logistics

#### Accommodation Options

**Areas to Stay:**
- Villars-sur-Ollon (main event village)
- Aigle (start location)
- Ollon area
- Montreux (nearby)

**Types Available:**
- Hotels (luxury to budget)
- Apartments/chalets
- B&Bs
- Cabins
- Camping

**Sample Hotel Prices:**
- Villars Lodge (Ollon): ~USD 186-243/night
- Grand Hotel Suisse Majestic (Montreux): ~USD 304/night
- Hotel Restaurant Le Saint-Christophe: ~USD 197/night

#### Travel by Car
- From A9 motorway: Take exit "Aigle" or "Bex/St Triphon"
- Follow signs for Ollon-Villars
- Parking available throughout resort

**Parking Options in Villars:**
- Place du Rendez-vous (Roc d'Orsay gondola): 400 free spaces
- Parking du Centenaire (opposite station): 110 paying spaces (max 7 days, vehicles under 2.10m)
- Route des Layeux (Eurotel Victoria): Free, 10-hour limit with parking disc
- Route des Hotels (Villars Experiences): Free, 10-hour limit

#### Travel by Train
- Scenic train ride from Aigle to Villars-sur-Ollon
- Views of surrounding mountains
- Welcome Card from accommodation may include free transport

### Unique Features and Characteristics

1. **UCI Headquarters Location:** Race starts at the doorstep of UCI World Cycling Center in Aigle - the global home of cycling governance

2. **2029 UCI Gravel World Championships:** The venue has been awarded to host the UCI Gravel World Championships in 2029

3. **Multi-Discipline Weekend (The Majestics):** Combines three UCI events in one weekend - Time Trial, Road Race, and Gravel Race

4. **Extreme Vertical:** One of the highest "vertical drop per km" ratios in the UCI Gravel World Series

5. **Alpine Setting:** Routes traverse forests, ridges, and high-altitude paths in the Vaudois Alps

6. **World Championship Qualifier:** Top 25% finishers in age groups qualify for UCI Gravel World Championships

7. **Dual Distance Options:** Granfondo (104-110 km) and Mediofondo (61-72 km) cater to different ability levels

8. **Scenic Variety:** Route includes Rhone Valley flatlands, steep alpine climbs, and Col de la Croix finish

## Alpine Gravel Challenge (Champery)

### Official Website
- **Website:** https://alpine-gravel-challenge.ch/en/
- **UCI World Series Page:** https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/champery/

### Event Status

**2025 Edition:** CANCELLED
- Cancelled due to insufficient registrations
- Participants received full refunds
- A free social ride was held July 13, 2025 in Vallee d'Illiez instead

**2026 Status:** Not confirmed on UCI Gravel World Series 2026 calendar at time of research

### Course Details

**Long Route:**
- **Distance:** 94 km
- **Elevation Gain:** ~3,200 m
- **Surface:** 50/50 paved/unpaved
- **Start:** Monthey (valley, 425 m elevation)
- **Finish:** Champery

**Key Climbs:**
- First climb: 17.5 km at 6.2% average gradient (from start)
- Second climb: 10 km at 5.7% average, mainly gravel
- Highest point: 1,869 m (near French border)

**Medium Route:**
- **Distance:** 63 km
- **Gravel Percentage:** 65%
- **Start/Finish:** Champery (skips first climb from Monthey)

**Discovery Route:**
- **Distance:** 35 km
- Follows part of 63 km course

### Location
- **Region:** Region Dents du Midi, Portes du Soleil
- **Main Town:** Champery
- **Border:** Swiss-French border area

## Gravel Epic Switzerland

### Official Website
- **Website:** https://www.gravelepic.com/epic-switzerland
- **Info Page:** https://www.gravelepic.com/info-switzerland

### Race Dates

**2025 Edition:**
- Stage 1: September 23, 2025
- Format: 2-3 day stage race

### Event Format
- Two-stage gravel race in the Swiss Alps
- Based at Champex-Lac, Valais region
- Only timed climbs count towards competition
- Solo and team categories available

### Course Details

**Stage 1:**
- Long Route: 108 km, 3,800+ m elevation
- Short Route: 63 km, 2,080+ m elevation

**Stage 2:**
- Long Route: 78 km, 3,090+ m elevation
- Short Route: 61 km, 2,090+ m elevation

**Terrain:**
- Larch forests, stream crossings, high alpine switchbacks
- Forest roads and farm tracks
- Designed for gravel bikes on 28mm tires
- No technical sections

### Location
- **Base:** Champex-Lac (French-speaking Swiss Alps)
- **Region:** Valais
- **Setting:** Cozy, rustic lodge at base of alpine giants

### Entry Fees
- Entry fees displayed on registration platform
- Half-board Official Race Accommodation packages from CHF 220
  - Range from camping to private en-suite rooms

### What's Included
- Individual timing chip and tracking device
- Rider briefings
- Full mechanical assistance
- Full medical assistance
- Feed stations with local products
- Course signage and GPX files
- Hot food and drinks at end of each stage
- Age category solo and team awards
- Finisher memento
- End of event party

### Aid Stations
- 3-4 checkpoints per stage
- Positioned outside of timed sections
- Local nutritious food provided
- Mechanical and medical teams on-site

### Cutoff Times
- "End of Race" vehicle has authority to stop riders outside cutoff time
- Only riders finishing within time barrier appear in final General Classification

### Team Entry
- Women's, Men's, and Mixed team formats
- 3-6 riders per team
- Ranking based on cumulative times of 3 fastest members
- Mixed teams must include times from at least 1 woman and 1 man per stage

### Requirements
- Minimum age 18 (or turning 18 in calendar year with parental consent)
- Medical fitness to participate in multi-day gravel cycling event
- Minimum training level recommended

### Unique Features
1. **Stage Race Format:** Multi-day event unlike single-day fondos
2. **E-Gravel Category:** E-gravel bikes now permitted
3. **Timed Climbs Only:** Allows socializing at feed stations without losing competitive edge
4. **Historic Origins:** Born in 2019 from Tim Kremer, Ralf Birke, and Pascal Jorritsma
5. **Part of Haute Route:** Event became part of the Haute Route series
6. **Team Competition:** Team categories with mixed gender options

### Accommodation
- Camper van spaces available (limited, contact organizers)
- No parking at Official Race Accommodation location
- Hook-up and facility access included for campers
