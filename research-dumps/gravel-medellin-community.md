# GRAVEL MEDELLÍN — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Context & Organization

**Event Status:** Gravel Medellín is a **new event** debuting on August 9, 2026 as part of the UCI Gravel World Series. No rider reports or race experiences exist yet since the inaugural edition hasn't occurred.

**Organizer Background [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** The event is "organized by the same team behind UCI Gravel races in Brazil, Chile, and Bogotá" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravelking-uci-gravel-world-series-2026-a-record-45-events-worldwide/). This organization also runs "the UCI Gravel World Series in Bogota (24 May) and the UCI Granfondo World Series race in Bogota in December plus the gravel and granfondo events in Brazil and Chile" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravel-medellin/).

## Location Details

**City Information [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
- **Location:** "Capital of Antioquia department, situated in the Aburrá Valley in the Andes mountains"
- **Elevation:** "Approximately 1,495 m above sea level"
- **Population:** "Around 2.5 million, making it Colombia's second-largest city"
- **Climate:** "Known as the 'City of Eternal Spring' due to its mild year-round temperatures (18–28°C)"
- **History:** "Once associated with conflict and cartels, Medellín has transformed into a hub of innovation, culture, and urban development"

(All location details from https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravel-medellin/)

## Regional Gravel Cycling Context

**Colombia Gravel Scene [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** One tour operator describes the region: "Welcome to the coffee-scented mountains of the Western Colombian Andes. Filled with brightly colored towns and enchanting landscapes, Colombia's main coffee-growing region is like something out of a fairytale, and perfect to discover Colombia by bike" (https://www.eatsleepcycle.com/tour/colombia-gravel-adventure/).

The same operator notes about Medellín specifically: "Discover Medellín by bike, the 'city of eternal spring' nestled on a high plane in the Andes mountains" and highlights riding "through Colombian coffee plantations & taste the best coffee in the world at its source" (https://www.eatsleepcycle.com/tour/colombia-gravel-adventure/).

**Regional Terrain Character [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** A multi-day tour in the region includes routes with significant elevation changes. One example day features "60 km, 845 m" of climbing, while another "Queen stage" packs "a leg-sapping 2,500 m of elevation into just 55 km of riding" with routes described as "Climb high into the mountains on a mix of gravel & paved roads" at altitudes reaching 2,500m (https://www.eatsleepcycle.com/tour/colombia-gravel-adventure/).
