# GRAVEL ROLL - PECAN SHAKER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Format & Timing Structure
The Gravel Roll uses a unique **timed-segment format** rather than traditional point-to-point racing. According to the official site, "Each of our courses [has] between one to four timed race segments and the race results are based on each racer's total time completing these segments. Rather than just racing from point A to B, take advantage of being able to ride our gravel bike races with friends of different fitness and skill levels, relax at fully stocked Gravel Roll aid stations, and pause for pictures along the scenic routes. The Gravel Roll focuses on fun and competition, using a timed-segment bike race format covering between 30 to 50 percent of each course." (https://gravelroll.com/)

This means **only 30-50% of the course is timed**, allowing riders of different abilities to ride together during untimed sections.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)
The official description notes riders will encounter **diverse surface conditions** specific to Georgia gravel: "you'll speed past pecan groves and through fish hatcheries, private timberlands, watermelon, sunflower and peanut fields and over a wide variety of surface conditions. Similar to the terrain on our Bird Dog 100 course, you'll encounter Georgia red clay, red dirt, white kaolin, loam, dust over crust, and of course....the loose stuff!" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10569/pecan-shaker)

**Difficulty rating:** Listed as "Difficult terrain with longer, steeper hills" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10569/pecan-shaker)

## Weather Experienced
**2024 (Spring race):** The event moved from fall to spring in 2024. According to **Samson Large [ELITE]**: "Pecan Shaker 2024 wasn't without tragedy, as we did have quite the rain before raceday, making for soggy conditions on certain areas of the course." (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/pecanshaker24recap)

One rider required medical attention during the 2024 event: "with the exception of one gentleman who was promptly attended to by medical staff." (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/pecanshaker24recap)

## Course Distances
Three distance options available:
- **Short Course:** 41 miles (66 km)
- **Intermediate Course:** 72 miles (116 km)  
- **Long Course:** 106 miles (170 km)

(https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10569/pecan-shaker and https://apuama.com/events/gravel-roll-pecan-shaker-116659)

## Race Support & Amenities
According to the event listing, participants receive:
- "Law enforcement and course marshals at key intersections including a rolling enclosure out of town"
- **"Private on course medical support. We aren't solely relying on 911 to help us"** — notable emphasis on having dedicated medical rather than just 911
- "Fully stocked aid stations with electrolyte mix from S.O.S Hydration energy product and food"
- "Roving Mechanical Support"
- Post-race food, beverage, and entertainment
- "Community Raffle" with prizes from partners
- Finisher awards for all participants
- "All timed segments broken down by age group, open categories get podiums prizes, everyone is eligible for the raffle"

(https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10569/pecan-shaker)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere
**Samson Large [ELITE]** describes the event philosophy: "Whether you're on the podium, or coming in DFL, the gravel roll is really about the vibes. Having a good time with friends of all skills, ages, backgrounds, and bikes. We all love cracking a cold one after the ride and sharing stories from the trail." (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/pecanshaker24recap)

The organizers emphasize community over pure competition: "Our community-focused races leave traditional-format gravel grinders in the dust." (https://gravelroll.com/)

The event gives "a huge shoutout to the town of Hawkinsville for being such great hosts each year" indicating strong local community support. (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/pecanshaker24recap)
