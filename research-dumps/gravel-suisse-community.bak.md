# GRAVEL SUISSE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Sophie Wright [ELITE]:** "I dream of days like this...today, it became reality. UCI World Series win. Feeling good as hell!!!" (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/gravel-suisse/results/)

**Olivier Godfroid [ELITE]:** Belgian former shoe salesman and gran fondo rider won the 2024 edition. Just two years prior he was an amateur selling shoes before winning Mallorca 312 and earning a pro contract with BALOISE TREK LIONS. He won in 3:32:21 on a muddy, wet and gritty course. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2024/07/07/fondo-rider-shoe-salesman-turned-pro-wins-uci-gravel-suisse-worlds-qualifier/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The 2024 race featured "muddy, wet and gritty" conditions on course (https://granfondodailynews.com/2024/07/07/fondo-rider-shoe-salesman-turned-pro-wins-uci-gravel-suisse-worlds-qualifier/)

**Route Profile (104km "The Summit"):**
- Starts at UCI headquarters in Aigle at 380m elevation
- First 10km on flat paved roads towards Ollon
- First major climb: 5km offroad climb at ~10% average gradient after Ollon
- First 50km relatively easier through Rhone valley
- Second half is "mostly offroad" in woods with real climbing beginning at km 50
- Route des Chaux (after 79km) features "a very steep 20% section"
- Highest point: 1819m elevation, reached 11km before finish
- Last 2km on Col de la Croix road is challenging
- Total elevation: 3,440m over 104km
- First half: 1,180m elevation vs second half: 2,300m elevation
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

The course is "easy accessible for spectators who can ride from one part to another making shortcuts to see the riders on different locations" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

**72km Mediofondo option:** Skips km 4-30 of the granfondo, finishes after 72km with 2,380m elevation. This is the qualifier distance for men 60+ and women 50+. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**2024 Men's Race [ELITE level]:** "Three riders got away early in the race to reach the highest points on course before attacking the long downhill towards the valley around the UCI headquarters." Godfroid was not in the leading group but moved to second spot halfway through with a 30-second gap behind Petr Vakoc for the final 10km. He "surged past Vakoc in the final stretch" to win by 1:25. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/the-uci-gravel-suisse-decided-on-the-final-climb/)

**2025 Men's Race [ELITE level]:** Andreas Seewald won solo after "five climbs shattered the field," finishing in 3:37:47. Second place Mathijs Loman was over 5 minutes back. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/gravel-suisse/results/)

**2024 Women's Race [ELITE level]:** Karolina Migon, "building on her earlier victory at La Indomable in Spain with a similar profile, showcased her mountain skills once again." The women's race had "constant jockeying for position, but Migon capitalized on the final climb to create a decisive gap," winning by 1:12. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/the-uci-gravel-suisse-decided-on-the-final-climb/)

**2025 Women's Race [ELITE level]:** Sophie Wright won solo in 4:25:00, with a gap of over 7 minutes to second place. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/gravel-suisse/results/)

**KEY INSIGHT:** The race is consistently "decided on the final climb" with the ability to handle the steep climbing in the second half being the determining factor for elite results.

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The race is described as "tough with lots of climbing and beautiful vistas" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/the-uci-gravel-suisse-decided-on-the-final-climb/)

Over 550 riders participated in 2024, with top 25% of each age/gender category automatically qualifying for UCI Gravel World Championships (https://granfondodailynews.com/2024/07/07/fondo-rider-shoe-salesman-turned-pro-wins-uci-gravel-suisse-worlds-qualifier/)

The race has been awarded to organize the UCI Gravel World Championships in 2029 (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

Location setting: "Takes place at the doorsteps of the UCI headquarters in Aigle and takes on difficult roads in the mountains of the Vaudois Alps" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

Multiple YouTube videos describe it as "beautiful" while racing through the Swiss Alps, with one rider [UNKNOWN] calling it "The most beautiful yet disappointing race of the season" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pi4O41Ginrc)
