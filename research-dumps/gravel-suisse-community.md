## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile - 2025 Edition:**
- **Distance:** 100km for "The Summit" (long distance)
- **Elevation:** 3,300m+ of climbing on the long distance route
- **Route:** From Aigle (UCI headquarters) to Col de la Croix, through the Vaud Alps
- **Key characteristic:** "Climb heavy" - the race is decided on climbing ability (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/gravel-suisse/results/)

**2026 Course Details (The Majestics):**
- **Gran Fondo (104km):** 3,440m elevation gain, starting from 380m elevation at UCI headquarters in Aigle, highest point at 1,819m reached 11km before finish
- **First half:** Relatively easy with 1,180m elevation
- **Second half:** Brutal - 2,300m of climbing, including a very steep 20% section on Route des Chaux after 79km
- **First 10km:** Flat paved roads toward Ollon
- **At 20km:** First 5km off-road climb at 10% average grade
- **After km 50:** "The real climbing begins" - mostly off-road through woods
- **Last 2km:** Challenging on the road of Col de la Croix
- **Spectator-friendly:** Easy for spectators to make shortcuts and see riders at multiple locations (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

**Surface Conditions:**
The course "linking the Rhone plain to the Col de la Croix" combines paved roads, gravel forest trails, and alpine paths through "forests, ridges and high altitude paths" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**When the race gets decided [ELITE level]:**
- **2025 race:** "The five climbs shattered the field" - race was decided on the climbs, not the finish (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/gravel-suisse/results/)
- **2024 race:** "The UCI Gravel Suisse decided on the final climb" - Olivier Godfroid wasn't in the early leading group but "moved up to second spot halfway the race to start with a 30 seconds gap behind Petr Vakoc for the final last 10km towards the finish line" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/the-uci-gravel-suisse-decided-on-the-final-climb/)

**Key tactical moment [ELITE]:**
In 2024, Godfroid "surged past Vakoc in the final stretch, securing the victory with a 1 minute and 25-second advantage" despite not being in the early lead group (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/the-uci-gravel-suisse-decided-on-the-final-climb/)

**Women's winning strategy [ELITE - 2024]:**
Karolina Migon "capitalized on the final climb to create a decisive gap" after "constant jockeying for position" throughout the race (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/the-uci-gravel-suisse-decided-on-the-final-climb/)

**Winning times [ELITE benchmarks - 2025]:**

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event evolution:**
The race "were organized for the first time in 2024 under the name Gravel Suisse and have been awarded to organize the UCI Gravel World Championships in 2029" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

**Location significance:**
"The race takes place at the doorsteps of the UCI headquarters in Aigle" giving it special significance in the gravel racing world (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

**Setting:**
"Wild, demanding and infinitely alpine. From the World Cycling Center in Aigle to the heights of Villars, the Majestics Gravel crosses forests, ridges and high altitude paths, between silence and intensity." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/11723/uci-gravel-suisse)

**Regional tourism context:**
The race is set in Villars-sur-Ollon, described as "a charming and picturesque village that embodies the beauty and charm of Switzerland" at 1,300 meters elevation. The area offers skiing, hiking, thermal baths at nearby Lavey-les-Bains, and proximity to the Montreux Jazz Festival. The village features "picturesque chalets, welcoming cafes and charming boutiques" with "authentic Swiss ambiance" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/villars/)

**Ribble Outliers team stats for 2025 season:**
- Countries raced: 16 (including USA, Spain, England, Scotland, Wales, Kenya, Namibia, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, Switzerland, Germany, Iceland, Latvia, Lithuania, Mexico)
- Total team podiums: 16 (Sophie Wright: 9, Hayley: 1, Meth: 1, Jenson: 4, Harry: 1)
- Approximate distance raced per rider: 2,000-2,500km (Meth highest at 3,056km)
- Longest race by distance: Dorset Divide 530km (https://lecol.cc/en-us/blogs/content/ribble-outliers-gravel-season-recap)
