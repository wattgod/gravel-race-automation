## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**
The course utilizes **gravel sections and quiet mountain roads**[3]. The Big Loop specifically includes "small farm roads, forest roads, and easy singletrack"[3]. An exact percentage breakdown is not provided in available sources.

**Key Course Features and Named Climbs**

The Big Loop route is characterized by[3]:
- Pastures linking Villars, Les Diablerets, and Gryon
- A climb toward the authentic village of **La Forclaz**
- A long, steep descent to Les Diablerets and its Sports Park
- A forest road climb toward **Panex and Les Ecovets**, described as "likely give the best riders the chance to make the difference for the final victory"
- A completely flat 15 km section along the **Rhône** in Aigle
- Ascent through forest toward Verschiez (for the Small Loop)

The region features notable mountain passes including **Col des Mosses, Col de la Croix, Col du Pillon, Col de la Givrine, Col du Marchairuz**, and **Col de la Croix**[5].

**Technical Difficulty Level**
Described as featuring "short, steep climbs" with "plenty of vertical meters"[2]. The course is characterized as requiring "regular changes in pace"[3]. No formal difficulty rating is provided.

**Elevation Profile Description**
The Vaud Alps setting provides consistent climbing terrain. The Big Loop ascends from Villars into the alpine region with sustained climbs, descents into resort areas, and a notable flat section along the Rhône valley before returning to elevation gain near the finish[3].

## COMMUNITY & REPUTATION

**Race History**
The UCI Gravel Suisse **debuted in 2024** on the UCI calendar[5]. It is part of the **UCI Gravel World Series** and qualifies riders for the UCI Gravel World Championships. The 2026 World Championships will be held in **Nannup, Western Australia on October 10-11**[2].
