# GRAVEL UNRAVEL - BON JON PASS OUT — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Course Details & Distances

**Three Distance Options:**
- **Short Course:** 32 miles, ~4,000 feet elevation gain, 50% gravel
- **Medium Course:** 55 miles, ~6,000 feet elevation gain, 60% gravel  
- **Long Course:** 80 miles, ~9,000 feet elevation gain, 70% gravel

**Start Times (staggered):**
- 8:00 AM - Long Course
- 8:30 AM - Medium Course
- 9:00 AM - Short Course

(https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/bon-jon-pedal-bender/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Key Feature - Bon Jon Pass:** The main challenge is described as an "endless ascent" through Olympic National Forest. The climb coincides with peak rhododendron bloom in June, with flowers "spotlighted against the deep green background of the forest."

**Long Course Bonus Suffering:** Long course riders climb Bon Jon Pass twice - once on the way out, then again on the return. Medium and short course riders only do it once.

**Surface Conditions:** Organizers specifically note "RideWithGPS surfaces are incorrect" and provide their own gravel percentage estimates (50-70% depending on distance). Remaining sections are paved or "rustic" paved.

**Technical Section:** All medium and long course routes include the Lords Lake Loop section after the main Bon Jon Pass climb.

(https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/bon-jon-pedal-bender/)

## Logistics & Support

**Aid Station Locations:**
- Short Course: Miles 11 and 22
- Medium Course: Miles 11, 22, 33, and 45
- Long Course: Miles 11, 22, 33, 45, 55, and 70

All aid stations include hydration, snacks, and portable toilets.

**Critical Tech Warning:** "There is essentially zero cell coverage on the course." Organizers strongly recommend downloading GPS tracks for offline use before the race. Free RideWithGPS event membership provided to all participants for offline downloads.

(https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/bon-jon-pedal-bender/)

## Race Categories & Format

**Competitive Categories (for each distance):**
- Open
- Open Masters (45+)
- Open Super Masters (60+)
- Women's Open
- Women's Masters (45+)
- Women's Super Masters (60+)
- Non-Binary
- Juniors (under 18)

Awards 3 deep in all categories.

**Non-Competitive Option:** Gran Fondo style available for short and medium courses. E-bikes allowed as a separate category in non-competitive division only.

**Field Size:** Limited to 400 riders total.

(https://www.bikesignup.com/Race/WA/Quilcene/GravelUnravelBonJonPassOut)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The village of Quilcene is described as "synonymous with hospitality." Race base is at Worthington Park in downtown Quilcene.

**Post-Race:** Hot food, beer garden (purchase), awards ceremony, and door prizes included. Gravel Unravel t-shirts available for purchase at registration.

**Timing:** RFID chip timing provided. Results posted within 24 hours.

**Local Support:** A portion of proceeds benefits the Quilcene Historical Museum/Worthington Park and Count Me In for Quilcene (local non-profits).

## Weather & Timing

**Event Date:** Mid-June (June 13, 2026 for next edition)

**Seasonal Conditions:** Timed to coincide with wild rhododendron peak bloom in Olympic National Forest. June represents the "green season" in the Pacific Northwest with lush moss and ferns.

**2024 Weather Impact:** The long course was shortened from 80 miles to 55 miles "due to severe weather" in 2024, indicating that June conditions can still be challenging in the Olympic Mountains.

(https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/bon-jon-pedal-bender/)

## Lodging & Camping Options

**NEW for 2026:** Worthington Park allows 10 vehicles for parking lot camping (van/car camping only, no large RVs/trailers/tents). $20 donation to Historical Museum. Sanicans and drinking water on-site. No campfires. Reserve during registration - limited spots.

**Preferred Option:** Twana Springs (Lake Leland) camping arranged with private landowner, 10 miles from race start. Organizers "highly recommend camping at this beautiful property."

**Other Options:**
- County campground behind Community Center (9 sites, first-come first-served)
- Dosewallips State Park (12 miles away, reservations required)
- Port Ludlow Resort offering 20% off for Gravel Unravel participants

(https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/bon-jon-pedal-bender/)
