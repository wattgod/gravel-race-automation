## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**ACree [COMPETITIVE]:** "I use a 29er hardtail, with a rigid fork, and fast mtb tires. I've never regretted it on courses like this. Actual gravel bikes are faster on some of the climbs for sure, and the pavement. But there is enough very rough gravel that I don't think I'd be any faster on a drop bar bike. I do ride a regular gravel bike for the other Vicious Cycle events." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)

**broshaughnessy [COMPETITIVE]:** Planning to use a Cervelo Aspero with 40mm tires for the long course (78 miles, 8,200' elevation). (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)

**zkravey [UNKNOWN]:** Planning to use a Domane with 35mm Gravelking tires. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)

**Official recommendation:** "Minimum recommended tire width is 40mm" with approximately 95% gravel surface. (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**ACree [COMPETITIVE]** notes "there is enough very rough gravel" on courses like this to justify using a 29er hardtail with fast MTB tires over a drop bar gravel bike, despite gravel bikes being faster on climbs and pavement. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)

The course includes "one mile of single-track that is the Maidenhair Falls Trail" around Wynoochee Lake. (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

The area features "older growth forest offering pristine views of the region and lake" with Hemlock, Spruce, Douglas Fir, and Western Red Cedar. (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

The 23-mile loop course has "just under 2,000' of climbing per lap" and goes around Mount Fitzgerald on the "Big Creek Loop" - described as "peaceful, lush and green." (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**broshaughnessy [COMPETITIVE]** planned CTL of 71 with a TSB of +10 for race day, treating the long course as a training race for Gran Fondo Winthrop. FTP: 282W at 74.45kg = 3.79 W/kg. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)

New for 2026: "Mass Start at 9:30 AM. Whether you are riding 1 lap or 3, everyone rolls out together." This creates a looped format where "friends and family can see you and cheer you on as you pass through Satsop Center between loops." (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Critical safety concern:** "This area is remote and rugged with the nearest cell reception being nearly 40 minutes away unless you are up on the highest ridges." (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

"Important: there is essentially zero cell coverage on the course – you will want to download the tracks onto your phone ahead of time. We have a RideWithGPS event membership, which means that you will be able to download routes for offline use for FREE." (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

The rough gravel conditions led competitive rider ACree to choose a hardtail MTB over a gravel bike for this type of course. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event includes "post-race hot food and cold drinks" with "a beer garden at the race finish should you want to purchase a frosty bevvie." (https://www.bikesignup.com/Race/WA/Hoquiam/GravelUnravelWhyNotChee)

The event emphasizes camping options: "Camp with us, ride, stay for camaraderie and fun!" with onsite camping at Satsop Center plus free dispersed camping allowed on Olympic National Forest land. There's also Coho Campground on Wynoochee Lake (3.5 miles from Race Central) for those wanting a traditional campground experience. (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

"All participants receive a professional, well-organized event, RFID timing, a well-marked course, on-course snacks and medical support, mechanical support, a finisher token of badassness, and post-race hot food and drinks." (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

The area has deep historical significance: "The Wynoochee Lake area represents some of the deepest logging roots in the state, if not the country, with the last of the company-owned logging camps in the continental United States, Camp Grisdale, located just south of the lake" on "Chehalis tribal ancestral lands." (https://peninsulaadventuresports.com/events/gravel-unravel/why-not-chee/)

**ACree [COMPETITIVE]** describes the promoter (Vicious Cycle) positively: "I did the Hohly roller by the same promoter a couple years ago... I'm sure it will be a good event." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/why-not-chee-gravel-unravel/85508)
