## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** Trek CheckPoint with Bontrager 40mm GR1 tires. SRAM AXS drivetrain with 36T chainring and 10-52 cassette. Chose NOT to use a hydration pack, ran 2x 28oz bottles. Bontrager Ion 200 RT front light (didn't throw much light but sufficient for 20-minute dark start). (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Rosa Klöser [ELITE]:** SRAM Transmission system proved critical for mud sections: "Due to the SRAM Transmission system, I could push it in the mud without losing the derailleur." Also noted she started with a hydration pack but dropped it during the race. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

**Mathew Wollmann [UNKNOWN]:** "Super light setup with two aspero bags. A top tube bag with essentials such as plugs, CO2, lights, battery, etc. Things I would need quickly. My saddle bag contained a light rain jacket, two extra embark maple pouches, and my shaded oakelys. I started the race with clear lenses on so I didn't have to stop and transition between the two. I had done this the year prior and found it to be very beneficial. I had my Red Shift aero bars mounted up and everything felt fantastic. I had also switched out my normal saddle for my Sworks Power saddle. This turned to be a great decision. I run two bottles on the bike frame with Embark maple mix, and a USWE Rush 8L Bike Hydration Vest with Nuun electrolyte tablets." (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

## Weather Experienced

**2023 Race (Carey Lowery):** "Friday night we went to bed knowing that there was a 40% chance of rain. Well, those thoughts changed when I was rudely awakened to thunder and rain beating against the hotel window. In 3 hours, Lincoln was hit with 2 1/2 inches of rain! Aye yai yai!" Started at 69°F and raining. "It was still raining at the start and the temperature was a chilly 69 degrees." Later: "this is also where I engaged my mental game. I smiled and gave words of encouragement as I passed those who had passed me earlier in the day. Most were grateful; a few were rather irritated that an old lady was 'chicking' them." Day began heating up after mile 80-100. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**2024 Race - 300 Mile (Mathew Wollmann):** "Race weather proved to be gorgeous with a tailwind most of the night. A chance of rain depending where you were on the course closer to morning, but I wasn't worried." Later in the race: "At mile 270 we stopped at a VERY well stocked aid station... Topped off on water, I strictly used my bottles now to spray over my body to keep cool. It was HOT... At mile 287 I began to feel my first cramps... I began to stop sweating, developed a headache, and the cramps got worse." Temps rose above 100°F with heat indexes up to 105°F. "It was starting to heat up and temps were projected to rise above 100 with heat indexes up to 105." (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**2024 Race (pre-race conditions):** "Boy, it was hot! 100+ degrees and the gravel was dusty! The gravel here is small pea gravel/sand mixture." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**2025 Race (Matt Beers):** Pre-dawn start with wet conditions from overnight precipitation. "Adding to the darkness was the weather that rolled in the night before dousing portions of the course with plenty of precipitation." Started at 5:55 AM with "a crisp" temperature. Later: "the heat, which was a concern for many of the favorites heading into the race" but "plenty of cloud cover and the winds were cool all day." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Gravel Surface:** "The gravel here is small pea gravel/sand mixture... The water does not run off the roads here like back home, but rather the fine gravel/sand/dirt mixture soaked it up like a sponge." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Mud Sections (MMRs):** "Eventually the sun came out. The roads were still sludge, but I knew eventually they would dry out and the fast lines would reappear... When I went to get a swig of water from my bottle, it squirted out all over me. The volunteer had cross-threaded the lid!" The course featured multiple B-roads (minimum-maintained roads) that became "dark chocolate ribbons" when conditions improved: "I hit the first B-road around mile 70 and I felt like I was on a single track ribbon of dark chocolate. There was a 18 inch wide path of firm ground on this road, but to either side it was sticky mud. As long as I held the line, I was fine. It was groovy!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Bohemian Alps Section:** "North of Lincoln, the area is called the Bohemian Alps, named after the Czech immigrants who settled here. Full of rolling hills, one after another after another. None are long, but all are steep. This year's course, compared to last year's, had more of this... Death by a thousand rollers." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**MMR Impact on Racing:** "Each time the race hit an MMR, the lead group changed, shaving off riders for crashes, mechanicals, and mud clogging the gears and frames of the main men." The 2025 race had three critical MMR sections in the first 30 miles that "defined the race." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

**Course Characteristics:** "The gravel roads were halfway decent, with only a few with big chunky rocks. We were beginning to get into the roller portions of the course, which were mentally draining." (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**Unique Features:** "There was a short, 0.4 mile cross course I had to do, including riding through a barn, before I hit the aid station." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] - 150 Mile Strategy:** Mile 54 checkpoint reached in 4:30 (vs 3:25 previous year in dry conditions). Second checkpoint at mile 108 reached in 8:37. Finished in 11:34 total time. Stopped at both checkpoints for ~4 minutes each. "Miles 80-100 were the hardest for me, as I was beginning to tire from the monotony. But this is also where I engaged my mental game. I smiled and gave words of encouragement as I passed those who had passed me earlier in the day." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] - Nutrition:** "Just get through the next 25, I told myself, as the remaining 17 are all downhill (and with a tailwind)... At about mile 130, I caught the boys, who were now on the struggle bus, having expended all their matches... The last 20 miles seemed to fly by; it might have something to do with the 200mg caffeine pill I took at the last CP as well as the 3 gels I had consumed, each having an additional 90mg of caffeine. Vroom vroom! My effort may not have been any harder, but it sure felt easier!" Total caffeine load: 200mg pill + 270mg from gels = 470mg in final 42 miles. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Mathew Wollmann [UNKNOWN] - 300 Mile Strategy:** Finished in 21:53 with 106 minutes off-bike time. Goals: Sub-24 hours (achieved), Top 20 overall (19th overall), Sub-1 hour off-bike time (missed). "My goal of staying on the bike, and making my gas station stops fast AF was paying off. I stopped at miles 57, 131, and took only a bit longer at 189 because I needed an 8-minute snoozer and needed to wait for the store to open." (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**Mathew Wollmann [UNKNOWN] - Night Strategy:** "I hung with the front group until about mile 12. From years past I knew better than to try and hang with them. Another 5 of us stuck together until about mile 40. After that, it was pretty much solo work with a passer-by to chat with the entire night... That night I was all over the road. Literally yelling out loud to wake up." Took an 8-minute nap around mile 189 before sunrise. (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**Elite Women's Strategy [2025]:** "The obvious plan was that none of the females would truly work until the men's group came by and we could latch onto their groups." Rosa Klöser and Geerike Schreurs worked together for ~120 miles after breaking away at mile 25-30: "We had a quick chat and we decided we would commit together because we knew with our power on the flats and on those rolling hills we could drive it home." They stopped to fuel at both aid stations together. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/rosa-kloser-wins-gravel-worlds-after-epic-breakaway-with-geerike-schreurs/)

**Elite Men's Strategy [2025]:** "13 dwindled to nine heading into a crucial aid station where the leaders would have to choose who needed to stop for water versus those who could carry on without stopping. Since Gravel Worlds is staunchly self-supported, there is no outside assistance and no handups... six of the leaders carried on — including Beers and Swenson — while Jones, Roberge, and Brendan Johnston stopped for water and fuel. While it was risky, the gap didn't last." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mud-Related Mechanicals:** "More than once I thought about just tucking tail and turning back, after seeing many people off their bikes looking at their drivetrains and playing with their chains." However: "Sniper, my steed, was plowing through this like a champ. I was having zero drive train issues." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Brake Wear:** "My brakes were taking a beating, as the road grime was splashing up on the rotors and wearing down my brake pads, even though I hardly used them." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Heat/Dehydration (300 Mile):** "At mile 287 I began to feel my first cramps... I began to stop sweating, developed a headache, and the cramps got worse. Uh oh, I've been here before." Required IV treatment at finish. Note: Heart rate data showed significant elevation as temperatures increased through the day. (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**Nutrition Mishap:** "John had a little mishap when half of his nutrition fell into the porta potty as he was making race weight. He told me he actually thought of retrieving it... if only for a second." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Early MMR Selection:** In 2025 women's race: "Not only did it bring certain contenders to the fore, it also seemed to eliminate a few key challengers who would typically play a role in Gravel Worlds: Lauren Stephens, minutes back; Morgan Aguirre, minutes back; Sofia Gomez Villafañe, minutes back; Lauren De Crecenzo, minutes back. At just 25 miles into the 150-mile race, the women's lead group was just six riders after the first major mud selection." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

**Sleep Deprivation (300 Mile):** "That night I was all over the road. Literally yelling out loud to wake up. Digestively I was great. I always felt full and had no cramps. Things were going very smoothly." The 300-mile race starts at 5 PM Friday requiring overnight riding. (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Jason Strohbehn (Race Director):** "Jason Strohbehn (the face of Gravel Worlds) was there to give me a fist bump. Volunteers handed me an ice cold Coke and a wet towel." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I love the vibes of Gravel Worlds and how no one is more important than another. We are all equal here! The race organizers are wonderful people and have their shit dialed! They work tirelessly for days to make sure our experience is a positive one." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2023/08/gravel-worlds-race-report.html)

**Race Mission:** "Gravel Worlds® Mission: to create a fun, kick-ass gravel event for all levels of athletes to enjoy... We didn't ride gravel because it was the cool thing to do, we simply rode gravel because it was what we had and it was fun. So that is what we will always strive for our events, keeping gravel FUN!" (https://www.gravelworlds.com/about)

**First to Last Initiative:** "From 'first to last,' we're elevating the experience of every participant... the 'First to Last' initiative underscores Gravel Worlds' dedication to recognizing the efforts of every participant, regardless of placement. Much in the vein of the Mid South's iconic DFL party that celebrates the last rider to finish the Oklahoma race, Gravel Worlds' will welcome its final 150-mile course participant with a raucous party after dark." Cut-off time extended from 9:00 PM to 9:59 PM for 2024. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/garmin-gravel-worlds/)

**Randy Gibson Memorial:** "Randy Gibson was one of the greatest humans in our Gravel Family. We lost Randy to a drunk driver in 2017... It didn't matter if it was 110° or -10°, Randy was on the bike and there was no place he'd rather be. He helped bring Gravel Worlds to where it is today and we will never forget the Friend, Husband, and Father he was. We miss you Randy and we will always #RideForRandy." There is a Randy Gibson Climber Jersey awarded. (https://www.gravelworlds.com/about)

**Gravel Worlds Crew Letter:** "We are humbled to know some of our riders spend their hard-earned money and vacation to come to Lincoln, NE on a hot August weekend to experience something special, and we work to make sure you get what you came for... We love celebrating the peak of human performance, but we care just as much about the everyday Joe and Jill who come to our events to challenge themselves and find something in themselves through gravel." (https://www.gravelworlds.com/about)

**Pre-Race Activities:** "We started by eating at Napoli's Haymarket... A couple of glasses of wine later and a belly full of pasta, we decided to explore a bit. We soon found an Arcade Bar about a block away. A few drinks there and some amazing arcade games, we then hit up the Starlite Lounge next door. A VERY cool 1940's style lounge, also, with amazing drinks... By this point, I was not focused on hydration by any means. I hit the bed around midnight and indeed, slept in past or near noon. No regrets. I felt amazing and carefree for the first time on race day." (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**Unique Start:** "Gravel Worlds has many unique elements, but the most surreal of them all is the pre-dawn start. While many gravel races roll off the line at sunrise, none of the big gravel prizes start before the sun beyond Gravel Worlds. With a crisp 5:55 AM start for the men, there is a full hour of racing before the sun emerges." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

**Aid Stations:** "At mile 270 we stopped at a VERY well stocked aid station. Almost anything you could ask for." (https://www.mathewwollmann.com/post/garmin-gravel-worlds-300-2024-race-recap)

**Self-Supported Rule:** "Since Gravel Worlds is staunchly self-supported, there is no outside assistance and no handups. That means you must either start with everything you need or stop at the neutral aid stations." And: "Gravel Worlds is very clear that no team support is allowed out on course." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/results-matt-beers-and-rosa-kloser-win-lauf-gravel-worlds-2025-in-a-duo-of-two-up-sprints/)

**2025 Prize Purse:** "$100,000 prize purse split evenly between the top five men and women racing the 150-mile course" with $17,500 to the winner. The race also committed "$30,000 payout to gravel cycling-adjacent charities." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/garmin-gravel-worlds/)
