## Weather Experienced

**2025 Edition:** Race day experienced cold, rainy conditions with no blue skies. The official race summary described it as showing "its wild side" with challenging weather that "soaked riders to the bone." This was noted as a significant departure from expected conditions. (https://www.gravelista.cc/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile - Seymour Location (2024-2026):**
- 83% quality gravel riding with wide open gravel roads
- Course starts at Seymour Racecourse with 2.5km neutral paved start
- **Key Challenge - Wicket Hill Climb:** Located at 30km mark (and again at 73km for long course). Only 1km length but gains 125m at 11% average gradient. This is described as "the crux of the course"
- Elevation ranges from 155m to 520m (at Wallaby Gully Flora Reserve)
- Total elevation: 1372m for 95km course, 1892m for 135km course
- Course is "mostly rolling" with short paved relief sections interspersed
- Described as "some of the finest, fastest & least-known gravel in the country"
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/seymour/ and https://www.gravelista.cc/info)

**Previous Beechworth Location (2023-2024):**
- 120km course with 85% gravel
- Elevation ranged from 180m to 837m at highest peak
- Cumulative elevation gain of 1892m
- Featured two longer climbs
- Described as "demanding" and featuring "renowned fine gravel of Victoria's beautiful outdoors"
(https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravelista-grows-to-over-500-riders/)

**Terrain Description:** "Rugged, beautiful and, at times, unforgiving gravel routes winding through rolling farmlands, taking in vistas of the serene Goulburn Valley and Strathbogie Ranges" (https://raceroster.com/events/2025/101242/gravelista-2025)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Race Results [ELITE]:**
- **Brendan Johnston (winner, 33 years old, Giant Australia):** Completed 135km in 3:46:57, winning by just 3 seconds over Mark O'Brien in 2025. Also won 2024 edition, making him a repeat champion.
- **Sharlotte Lucas (women's winner, 34 years old, 2025 NZ Cyclocross Champion) [ELITE]:** Completed 135km in 4:41:01, with 3:21 margin over second place
- **Tim Jamieson (95km winner) [ELITE]:** 2:51:01 for the medio course

The extremely close men's finish (3 seconds) and the description of the 11% Wicket Hill Climb as "the crux" suggests this is where the elite race was likely decided.

(https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/10/28/uci-gravelista-results-brendan-johnston-repeats-down-under/ and https://cyclingflash.com/race/uci-gravel-world-series-gravelista-we-2025/result)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:** "Whether you joining us to race for a UCI Age Category podium, or simply to roll with mates, we can't wait to welcome you to Seymour, Victoria" — indicates the event welcomes both competitive qualifiers and recreational riders. (https://raceroster.com/events/2025/101242/gravelista-2025)

**Community Recognition:** Despite challenging 2025 weather conditions, the race organizers wrote: "Seymour — thank you. Again. You may have soaked us to the bone, but your warmth shone through. Even drenched, your gravel remains world-class." (https://www.gravelista.cc/)

**Event Village:** The race features an event village with "fine food & drink, local produce and activities for supporters as well as riders" plus at-event camping available for $30 per site (max 6 people). (https://raceroster.com/events/2025/101242/gravelista-2025 and https://www.gravelista.cc/info)

**Growth:** The event has grown significantly, with "over 500 enthusiastic participants" by 2023, marking "a significant increase compared to its inaugural edition." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/gravelista-grows-to-over-500-riders/)

**UCI Qualification Significance:** As the first qualifier event globally for the UCI Gravel World Championships, top 25% finishers in each category automatically qualify. The first three riders in each official age category qualify automatically regardless of field size. This adds competitive significance for age-group racers. (https://www.gravelista.cc/info)

**Recreational Options:** E-bikes are explicitly "welcome, and encouraged" in recreational events, showing inclusive approach to non-UCI categories. (https://www.gravelista.cc/info)
