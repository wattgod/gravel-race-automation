# GRAY DUCK GRIT — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

(https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

**Brian Turk [ELITE]:** "I battled the cold and rain. I battled the 30 mph head and cross winds that took out a lot of riders. I had no mechanical issues. A lot had to go right." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

## Weather Experienced

**2022 Race (222-mile):** Started at 9:00 PM Friday through below freezing temps, transitioning into 17mph headwinds at the end (https://www.reddit.com/r/gravelcycling/comments/y5o64a/completed_the_gray_duck_grit_2022/)

**2023 Race (50-mile):** Blue skies, perfect temps in the 60's, with a breeze that evolved into a headwind (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/horsetracks-cowbells-blue-sky-gravel-pat-moore-s-gray-duck-grit-recap)

**2023 Race (333-mile) [ELITE]:** Cold and rain, with 30 mph head and cross winds that caused multiple rider DNFs (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Driftless Region Character:** The gravel south and east of Northfield is the driftless region of Minnesota, described as "the most beautiful and most challenging in the state" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-collective-passion-for-gravel-gray-duck-grit-rounds-the-corner-into-year-2)

**Surface Conditions [ELITE]:** "Limestone gravel, and LOTS of rollers" in Southern Minnesota (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/grey-duck-grit-in-minnesota/74859)

**Elevation Profile [ELITE]:** "They're getting elevation by dropping in and out of the canyons along the Mississippi River valley. The ones near Frontenac are not something I went out of my way to climb" - describes climbing strategy as entering/exiting river valleys (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/grey-duck-grit-in-minnesota/74859)

**50-Mile Course:** "A healthy mix of forest and farmland, popping in and out of the woods and occasionally riding along the Cannon River... a wonderfully fast descent" in the final 10 miles (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/horsetracks-cowbells-blue-sky-gravel-pat-moore-s-gray-duck-grit-recap)

**Specific Course Marker:** 380th Street goes over Highway 52 and was familiar terrain from the 2023 course (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/horsetracks-cowbells-blue-sky-gravel-pat-moore-s-gray-duck-grit-recap)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**333-Mile Support Strategy [ELITE]:** Brian Turk (2nd place, 333-mile): "The most important difference in this race was having support. She was able to meet me at every aid station every 55 miles. This meant I had all the food and hydration I needed when I needed it... It meant I had every clothing option I needed for changing conditions. I could add and drop batteries and lights as needed." Aid stations were spaced every 55 miles. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

**222-Mile Logistics [COMPETITIVE]:** DingleTower noted: "I wouldn't have a support crew though which is a downside. I'm sure I could manage especially with the cooler weather." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/grey-duck-grit-in-minnesota/74859)

**Mental Approach [RECREATIONAL]:** Paul Palecek: "I have struggled with mental health myself and I've never found a medication or counselor that is as effective at reducing anxiety as a long bike ride" - emphasizing the mental health benefits of participation (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

**Race Philosophy [UNKNOWN]:** Event emphasizes "Ride Your Ride" - no boundaries or restrictions on pacing approach (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Weather-Related [ELITE]:** In the 333-mile race, "30 mph head and cross winds took out a lot of riders" - wind was the primary cause of DNFs (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

**Cold and Exhaustion [ELITE]:** Shawn Wynn dropped from 4th place at 110 miles "beaten by the cold and wind and figured he couldn't podium so dropped out" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

**Night Riding Challenge [ELITE]:** The 222-mile race starts at 9:00 PM, requiring overnight riding capability (https://grayduckgrit.com/)

**Navigation [RECREATIONAL]:** Paul Palecek mentioned taking "about a mile's worth of wrong turns" on the 69-mile route (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Philosophy:** "The driving force behind this challenge is the sincere desire to do more than simply ride or challenge ourselves. Giving back to the communities in which we live is at the core of Gray Duck Grit and it's passionate team of volunteers." All proceeds go to Fraser of Minnesota (autism and mental health resources). (https://grayduckgrit.com/)

**Inclusivity:** "We strive to make GDG accessible to anyone who has a bike and wants to get after it by offering Non-Binary/Gender Non-Conforming categories for all our competitions and distances." (https://grayduckgrit.com/)

**Race Director's Approach:** Mark Jesse: "How fast you ride, when you ride, what gear you use, and where you do so is up to you, and we respect that. Inviting ALL is our approach. It's the simplest and easiest approach of 'the more, the merrier.'" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-collective-passion-for-gravel-gray-duck-grit-rounds-the-corner-into-year-2)

**Finish Experience:** "Crossing the finish line to the amazing sound of the cowbell was as satisfying as always. The finish area at the Cannon Valley Fairgrounds was perfect. A food truck, music, and sunshine always seem to make for a good time." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/horsetracks-cowbells-blue-sky-gravel-pat-moore-s-gray-duck-grit-recap)

**Start Line:** "The start of the 50, 69, and 111-mile races took place on Sunday morning on a horse race track which admittedly was a first for me." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/horsetracks-cowbells-blue-sky-gravel-pat-moore-s-gray-duck-grit-recap)

**Community Support [ELITE]:** Brian Turk noted unexpected community support: "Shawn and Casey stayed with Candace through the overnight hours. Their support to help Candace was such an unexpected help." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gray-duck-grit-first-look-3-riders-share-3-perspectives-of-3-races)

**Rider Feedback:** Race organizers received "overwhelmingly positive feedback" with riders saying they're "proof that perception can be reality" and "changing gravel cycling" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-collective-passion-for-gravel-gray-duck-grit-rounds-the-corner-into-year-2)

**Local Support:** "The city of Northfield along with law enforcement were all on board and provided excellent support from the beginning stages of GDG planning through the final seconds of the inaugural event." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-collective-passion-for-gravel-gray-duck-grit-rounds-the-corner-into-year-2)

**Origin Story:** Race was founded by Level Be Adventures LLC, created by cyclists who organized casual weekly no-drop group rides starting in 2021, emphasizing "being present and showing up" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-collective-passion-for-gravel-gray-duck-grit-rounds-the-corner-into-year-2)
