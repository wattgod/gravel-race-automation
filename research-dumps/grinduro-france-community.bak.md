**Paul Errington [ELITE]:** Carried spare chain links and successfully made a roadside repair after snapping his chain during Stage 4. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Julien Sommier [UNKNOWN]:** Pre-race preparation included: "J'ai rempli mes bidons, et vérifié la pression de mes pneus, tubeless oblige." Also removed rain jacket from saddle bag after checking weather. (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 Edition:** Weather started favorable but changed dramatically. Paul Errington [ELITE] noted: "My day would be spent riding with Martin from Leatt... The orage aura été menaçant sur la fin de la sortie mais heureusement nous sommes passés entre les gouttes." Later: "While we were eating, the added drama of a brutal rain storm rolled in, which luckily had had the foresight to ensure the riding portion of the day was done before it arrived. As quickly as it started the rain ended." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Julien Sommier [UNKNOWN]:** "Un orage violent nous a forcés à nous abriter, mais la chance a été de notre côté jusque-là." The storm hit during the evening festivities, not during the riding. (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Layout:** Two 50km loops totaling approximately 96-100km with 2,200+ meters of elevation gain. The format allowed riders to return to event village at midpoint for lunch. (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/ and https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Timed Segments:** Four timed stages total, described by Paul Errington [ELITE]:
- Stage 1: 3km climbing stage starting on tarmac, transitioning to gravel
- Stage 2: Fast downhill stage on rough double track (confusingly placed after Stage 3 on course)
- Stage 3: Technical singletrack descent through "the canyon" - high banked singletrack with quick succession of turns
- Stage 4: "Up and down stage" - long drag followed by rough gravel double track descent (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Terrain Type:** Paul Errington [ELITE]: "When Grinduro was dreamt up as an event it was always meant to find the absolute limits of a gravel bike and this stage definitely aimed to do that. Finding myself in one piece at the end felt like a win." The technical singletrack stage was particularly challenging on drop bars with 40mm tyres. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Geographic Features:** Course went through Réserve Naturelle Nationale du Frankenthal-Missheimle and Réserve Biologique Dirigée de la Haute-Meurthe. Maximum elevation reached approximately 1,100m twice during the event. (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)

**Feed Stations:** Multiple feed stations positioned between timed segments. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Paul Errington [ELITE]:** Strategy for Stage 1: "Let a few other riders go to have something to aim for. A seated 40 km/h as the back wheel shifts violently side to side through deeper looser gravel. Pass three riders, but then one comes back round. Someone to work with at least." Measured effort to catch riders ahead, then finish strong. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Paul Errington [ELITE]:** On Stage 2 descending: "On a 40mm tyre and drop bars speed is your friend as you try and stay on top of everything... My time loss through this stage is palpable and towards the end I have to give up my line for a faster rider." Acknowledged descending was not his strength. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Paul Errington [ELITE]:** Stage 3 climbing approach: "Out the saddle from the start then settle into a pace just on the uncomfortable side but maintainable. Plenty of riders on the climb to work through and one that seemed to be a much slower catch, so they were the focus." Targeted specific riders to stay motivated. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**General Format Advantage:** The enduro format allows strategic recovery between stages. Julien Sommier [UNKNOWN]: "Pédaler à fond, sans oublier de savourer les bons moments... Grâce à son format Enduro, Grinduro permet d'apprécier le parcours tout en donnant le meilleur de soi sur les segments chronométrés." (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)

**Nutrition Timing:** Extended lunch break at event village mid-race. Paul Errington [ELITE]: "It's an extended stop as we wait for the afternoon's stages to be open, so there's no harm in enjoying another coffee while waiting." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Pre-Stage Prep Observed:** Paul Errington [ELITE] noted other riders' preparations before Stage 4: "As I rolled up on the start line, riders were prepping - emptying their bottles and lowering tyre pressures." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues:** Paul Errington [ELITE] experienced a dropped chain that turned into a snapped chain during Stage 4, but was able to continue with a spare link. Carrying spare chain links proved essential. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Crashes on Technical Sections:** During Stage 3 (technical singletrack), Paul Errington [ELITE] witnessed: "Not more than a bike length after the timing box, he hits a rock square on, sending him flying over the bars. Though up on his feet and off riding again..." This caused other riders to walk the technical opening section. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-france-goes-down-a-storm-in-the-vosges)

**Event Village Setup:** Base camp located at Lac de Longemer (121, route de la plage) featuring: detente zone, DJ stage and concerts, bar, sponsor booths (Shimano, WishOne, Odlo), bike wash area, and large communal dining tent. All meals included in registration. (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)

**Friday Arrival Vibe:** Julien Sommier [UNKNOWN]: "Arrivé en fin d'après-midi au domaine de Longemer, l'ambiance était déjà bien festive. Certains participants jouaient au cornhole tandis que d'autres profitaient de l'apéro." (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)

Julien Sommier [UNKNOWN]: "Le DJ a repris le son, et la piste de danse s'est animée." Post-race featured live music, DJs, and dancing. Festive, non-traditional race start atmosphere. (https://weelz.ouest-france.fr/jetais-au-grinduro-france-une-epopee-gravel-dans-les-vosges/)
