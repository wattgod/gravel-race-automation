# GRINDURO ITALY — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "From day one the Grinduro vision was simple - how far can you push the limits of gravel bikes and at what point would a mountain bike be faster? Every round of the Grinduro Global series has this in mind and if a rider gets to the finish line still not knowing what the perfect bike would have been, then Grinduro consider that a win!" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 1 - the flat-out gravel road. Let a few other riders go to have something to aim for. A seated 40 km/h as the back wheel shifts violently side to side through deeper looser gravel. Pass three riders, but then one comes back round. Someone to work with at least. Make a left-hand turn and pass again the rider, but they throttle back so now alone, the road points slightly uphill, so dig a bit deeper." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 2, the 'Canyon', a singletrack descent. This is where any time I have made on the previous stage can be comfortably lost for me. I haven't pre-ridden the course, so I have no idea what is about to come next between the start and the finish line... I watch ex World Cup XC MTB rider Marco Fontana sprint into the stage on his flat bar Scott gravel bike. Maybe this stage is less techie than I thought? The stage is initially a little wider than singletrack and is rocky. On a 40mm tyre and drop bars speed is your friend as you try and stay on top of everything. Then the canyon part starts. A high banked singletrack trails snakes downhill in a quick succession of turns. My time loss through this stage is palpable." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 3, the road climb. Though not the 'funnest', this was likely to be my best stage. A road climb of just short of 4km at a gradient that afforded a decent gear. Out the saddle from the start then settle into a pace just on the uncomfortable side but maintainable." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 4, the XC vibe stage. Let's get this wrapped up. As I rolled up on the start line, riders were prepping - emptying their bottles and lowering tyre pressures. I opted for a more casual approach and just dived straight in. After a few metres I was already hanging a foot out to make an off-camber turn, then sprinting when the track straightened. Without the descending prowess of many, my approach was more brute force. As flat out as possible when the terrain allowed to average out the slower more considered approach to the technical elements or slower turns. I was deeply confused when confronted by a hard right turn into a river crossing." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was blown away by how much great singletrack had been packed in to the route. Everything had such a great flow to it and with a little rain in the preceding days, the dirt was just perfect." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Claudia Gerosa [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 1 – La Zinghera Hill Climb: This 2.8 km climb with 160 metres of vertical gain was in the forest on rough and pretty loose gravel. As the first hill of the day, I think everyone was stoked to get up it, regardless of their pace. I took my time, banishing thoughts of the results list from my mind as I stopped to take photos." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Claudia Gerosa [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 2 – Marsiliana Down Hill: A 3.7 km fast, techy descent on rough gravel and churned-up stones, this was a test of line choice that put both bike and wrists through the wringer. I am a big fan of descents and even though I took a few risks, I loved every metre of it – In fact, I even won this segment in my category!" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Claudia Gerosa [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 3 – Montioni Jungle Singletrack: At 3.8 km, this segment had a bit of everything - up and down through the woods, a jungle of emotions with real flow. I've never tried enduro, but definitely think it could become my thing as I really enjoyed threading the bike over roots and between the trees." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Claudia Gerosa [COMPETITIVE]:** "Stage 4 – Le Api Down Country: Fast and dusty, this 1.7 km segment demanded full concentration. With exposed roots and lots of sand, it was signature Mediterranean style riding." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Claudia Gerosa [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'm not sure what the rest of the participants were expecting, but if you'd come to Grinduro Italy thinking you'd be cruising exclusively along classic Tuscan strade bianche you would have been in for a shock. As promised, it delivered virtually every surface imaginable... Sand, rocks, wooded bits, flow sections, stony trails and tarmacked back roads." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Swiss Rider (fitfortrails.ch) [UNKNOWN]:** "I had to fall first before I could win the race afterwards... When I rode a special line at the very bottom [of the Canyon], I went down... Unfortunately, I had scraped my right shin and knee again. Exactly the same place as a year ago when I crashed during the prologue in Punta Ala. Luckily, the wound wasn't quite as extensive this time. That's racing. Only those who seek or exceed the limit know where the limit is!" (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Swiss Rider (fitfortrails.ch) [UNKNOWN]:** "Stage 1 was flat. Very flat... For me as a poor roller, this should be the stage where I absolutely must not lose too much time." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**JOM [UNKNOWN]:** "Temperatures on event day (June 14, 2025) were very hot, around 94F / 34C. Combined with the course's technical demands featuring tough climbs, tricky descents, and plenty of mountain bike single track, one had to maintain focus for much of the ride." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/2025-grinduro-italy-crazy-italian-gravel-race-party/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** Changed vorbau from 100mm to 90mm "to take a more compact position and to make it easier to lift the front wheel." Tires: Pirelli Cintuatro Gravel M 45mm front, Pirelli Cintuatro Gravel RC 45mm rear (higher side knobs for more grip in the limit range). Tire pressure: "very low in the descents, 23 PSI front and 25 PSI rear." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** Defended his Pirelli tire choice against Schwalbe stand comments: "I told the guys that I finished the race on the podium last time with exactly these tires." Notes that grip and puncture protection are his first priorities. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE]:** "On a 40mm tyre and drop bars speed is your friend as you try and stay on top of everything" in rocky sections. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Ex-World Cup XC MTB rider Marco Fontana [ELITE]:** Rode a flat bar Scott gravel bike for the technical stages. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Riders at Stage 4 start [UNKNOWN]:** "Riders were prepping - emptying their bottles and lowering tyre pressures" before the final XC-style stage. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Swiss Rider (fitfortrails.ch) [UNKNOWN]:** After discussing tires with another rider concerned about rough terrain: "I explained to him that I ride wide tires with rather a lot of tread, as the ground is quite rough in some places. Grip and puncture protection are always my top priorities." The other rider "had his tires changed at Schwalbe to also be on the safe side." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Length Discrepancy:** Official distance listed as 100km with 1500hm, but actual GPS showed "95 km and 1850 hm. Pretty crisp..." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**The Canyon Trail:** "The Canyon is the probably most famous trail in Tuscany. I also know every centimeter there. When I was still a bike guide, I rode the canal up to three times a week. And at Strava I used to have the KOM, 2 seconds ahead of Nino Schurter and other well-known names." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**The Canyon Trail (continued):** "As I arrived at the trailhead, there was, as usual, a bunch of mountain bikers there. I waited a long moment to have a clear ride. I should have waited longer, though, because even before half of the trail I had traffic in front of me. Unbelievable, I was riding faster with the unsuspended gravel bike than the mountain bikers with their enduro bikes..." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Canyon Trail Description:** "A high banked singletrack trails snakes downhill in a quick succession of turns." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Spaghetti Climb (2023 route):** "Well-known... looks exactly like it sounds – hairpin after hairpin, with some flat sections thrown in. I took it easy - the first part didn't have any shade, but the second part went in and out of the woods with a lot of zig-zags. It led us up Monte Arsenti, which is where all the local MTB descents start." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Stage 1 Specifics:** "A flat Schotterweg with only three 90 degree curves and 5 km long. A time trial as it is in the book." One rider called it "almost flat" and noted wind direction matters: "The wind came from slightly behind on the first straight, but that also meant that it would come from the front at some point. I tried not to let the speed drop below 40 km/h. After the first turn there was a slight climb... After the next turn came the final straight, which was almost 2 km long. The wind now blew from the front and the speed suddenly dropped to 30 km/h." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Kapannenberg (Stage 3):** "The asphalted climb is 3.8 km long, has 180 hm and an average gradient of 6%. Perfect to race up there at full throttle." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Cippo Trail (Stage 4):** "The 'Cippo' Trail is super fast in the upper part and has some long-drawn gravel curves. Down it is then completely flat with fluent curves, which can be driven with a lot of train." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Singletrack Density:** "The singletrail share was pleasingly high!" and "I was blown away by how much great singletrack had been packed in to the route." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**2023 Route (Punta Ala):** Started on coast, went inland into Maremma hills. "Under our tyres was a real mix of surfaces, with everything from champagne gravel to never-ending singletrack in the woods and barely-used, typically Tuscan back roads." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**2024 Format Change:** Unlike 2023's single big loop, 2024 had "a lap in the morning and one in the afternoon with 2 stages each. This made the logistics a bit easier as I could get back to the car at noon to eat and drink and get the bike back on track." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE] - Stage 1 Pacing:** "Seated 40 km/h as the back wheel shifts violently side to side through deeper looser gravel" with strategy to "let a few other riders go to have something to aim for." Speeds hit 45km/h but dropped to 30km/h when headwind hit on final straight. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE] - Stage 3 Climbing:** "Out the saddle from the start then settle into a pace just on the uncomfortable side but maintainable. Plenty of riders on the climb to work through and one that seemed to be a much slower catch, so they were the focus. Final out the saddle effort as the finish comes in to sight." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Paul Errington [COMPETITIVE] - Stage 4 Approach:** "Without the descending prowess of many, my approach was more brute force. As flat out as possible when the terrain allowed to average out the slower more considered approach to the technical elements or slower turns." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Pre-Riding Strategy [COMPETITIVE]:** Paul Errington pre-rode the course on Tuesday (event on Saturday), noting: "As a Maremma Local I knew almost the entire circuit. Only one section was new to me, where the first stage was at the same time." He specifically scouted each stage to understand where he could gain or lose time. (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Prologue Strategy:** Riders get three attempts at the ~1km prologue course, with best time counting. Strategy is to use first lap for reconnaissance, then push harder on attempts 2 and 3. (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**General Event Pacing Philosophy:** "When transitioning between stages at a Grinduro you can get lost in the mix a little. The transition sections are not timed so you can relax, chat to other riders, take in the views, spot another Fiat Panda 4x4 for the 100th time that day." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Lunch Break Strategy:** "An extended stop as we wait for the afternoon's stages to be open, so there's no harm in enjoying another coffee while waiting." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**James Deane (Grinduro Global):** "Grinduro has always been a destination event, promoting the unique regional identity of its locations and encouraging riders to consider staying not only for the event weekend, but stretching out their travel plans to explore further." (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/grinduro-celebrates-10th-birthday-in-scotland-and-returns-to-italy-in-2025)

**Event Philosophy:** "Perfect party to race ratio" is the defining characteristic. "Rather than being solely a race, Grinduro set itself apart from rivals by being a combination of race and party over 3 days." (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/grinduro-celebrates-10th-birthday-in-scotland-and-returns-to-italy-in-2025)

**Paul Errington's Summary:** "Even though the years have passed since the creation of Grinduro, the vibe still remains the same. Arrive, have a coffee, have a beer, meet old friends, make new friends, enjoy another beer, listen to some cool music, get matching tattoos, ride some bikes. It's the Grinduro way. Performance with party. Competitively social!" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Expo & Village:** "Populated by the series supporters, with Shimano also taking care of the coffees for the weekend with a top-notch barista. Also in attendance was the round's host brand Scott who showcased a performance flat bar gravel bike... Leatt, Odlo, Wahoo, Gravel Union and Crank Brothers were also in attendance." Free coffee all weekend. (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**First Feed Station (2024):** "Hosted by a beautiful agriturismo and riders were offered espresso and cake, along with stunning views out to the Mediterranean." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)

**Lunch at Morisfarms (2023):** "Stopping for lunch in the middle of a ride felt like a very foreign concept – it was definitely more substantial than the usual rushed mid-ride coffee. But you know what, I could get used to it. It was a lesson in taking time to enjoy a well-earned break in a stunning location with great food. So, why not indulge in a glass of wine from the very same ground where you're riding too? Again, no one was in a hurry." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Aid Station Quality (JOM):** "Good times, great support, excellent aid stations with great food, hydration, and more!" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/2025-grinduro-italy-crazy-italian-gravel-race-party/)

**Post-Race Scene:** "The vibe back at the finish was really welcoming, with everyone waiting for the rest to finish. Ice cream and beer were basically on tap and everyone seemed really happy with how the day went." Saturday night features "dinner and the disco on the beach." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Sunday Hangover Ride:** "Suited those who had enjoyed a bit more of a good night's sleep (such as me) – or are simply indefatigable – and gave the perfect opportunity to spin the legs socially on some local singletrack. Wahoo, one of the event's sponsors, provided coffee and croissants at a gorgeous spot beside the harbour in Portiglione." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Rider Interactions:** Story of meeting Henry from Frankfurt at the expo: "He had also a mountainbike background and we understood each other right away. We chatted a bit and decided to ride the entire route together." Later, "Laughing I had to, as Henry ran me later again over the way with a large bandage on the shin. He had managed what I managed last year: Fall in the prologue..." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Overall Feeling:** "One natural split happened to form two groups and it was clear that some of cycling's usual competitiveness was inevitable" but "it felt as though this wasn't the main priority for most of the participants and for that, I was pretty happy. This is where Grinduro brings such a refreshing attitude compared to many other gravel events - it's more about jaw-dropping scenery, great conversations with the other riders and top-rate feed stations than all-out competition." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-grinduro-italia-2023)

**Mass Start Atmosphere:** "The tross on the way to Stage 1... The field moved together comfortably towards the first stage. The route led over beautiful gravel roads and some cool trails." (https://fitfortrails.ch/blog/rennbericht-grinduro-italy-2024)

**Skills Park & Pump Track:** Event village at Massa Vecchia had "an onsite MTB skills loop and pump track" showing "this was definitely not going to be your stock gravel event." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-la-dolce-vita-meets-the-purple-party-at-grinduro-italy)
