# GUNNI GRINDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Nick Gould [ELITE]:** "If he has a hard day, we're all gonna have a hard day" - describing the course difficulty (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**Nick Gould [ELITE]:** Posted Strava data showing the course as "one of the hardest days" on a bike (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**Sarah, 34 [COMPETITIVE]:** "I love racing bikes, but I'm not a natural athlete. After several years of riding, I wasn't building fitness or getting faster... Within a few months, I found myself on the podium of the Gunni Grinder — an outcome that I always wanted but didn't consider possible." (https://simpleendurancecoaching.com/biking-and-running/)

**Laurel Darren [ELITE]:** "I finished sixth overall among females and No. 1 in my age group" at the Canon City Crippler after training in Colorado. Later noted finishing the Gunni Grinder but "missed a turn, so I did an extra 10 miles. I was proud of myself in that race, though, because I caught the tail end of the back-of-the-pack 60ish riders. I was able to pass 4-5 riders, turn around at the aid station and come back and pass 6-7 more." (https://www.wildbunchdesertguides.com/blog/Just-what-doctor-ordered-Phoenix-mountain-bike-tours-next.cfm)

**plindley [UNKNOWN]:** "I attempted the 120 last year and made it 30 miles before I turned around on my final plug. Had 4 flats in those miles and didn't want to attempt the rest of it with no more plugs. Some really nice smooth sections of gravel at the beginning but then there were hard packed spots with SHARP rocks that kept tearing my tires open. I saw a lot of folks with flats besides me." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**plindley [UNKNOWN]:** "Overall though it was pretty although I heard the people who completed it mentioned it was a lot of the same over and over but idk that's most gravel races imo." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**DMC [UNKNOWN]:** Describing second-hand report: "Basically climb for 20-30 minutes of rough gravel, descend down rough gravel, go through sand, do it again 3-4 times." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**Angy, 47, School Librarian [COMPETITIVE]:** "Coach Paul's approach really worked for me because he understood my goal of taking racing more seriously, but also gave me the freedom to be spontaneous and have fun when I needed to. This balance allowed me to enjoy the journey while still making big improvements in my riding." Iowa state masters cyclocross champion, Big River Gravel overall champion, second Core 4 age group. (https://simpleendurancecoaching.com/biking-and-running/)

**From The Ground Up riders [RECREATIONAL]:** Completed the Gunni Grinder 60 mile course as their "longest ride to date" during training. "It was a day of ups and downs and pushing through mental and physical limits." Route described as "60ish miles with over 5,000ft of climbing through varied terrain near Hartman Rocks southwest of town. The course alternated climbing and descending through pristine gravel roads and exhilarating doubletrack that tested our riders technical skills." (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gunnison-gravel-biking-from-the-ground-up/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**plindley [UNKNOWN]:** "If I were to do it again I would definitely use inserts and maybe up the PSI and maybe ride more carefully" after experiencing 4 flats in 30 miles. Notes that "SHARP rocks" were tearing tires open on "hard packed spots." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**General recommendation [SOURCE: Official/tourism materials]:** "Rocks and sand are commonly encountered here, so wider tires are recommended" for Hartman Rocks area. (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gravel-biking-in-gunnison-colorado-with-nick-gould/)

**General note [RECREATIONAL perspective]:** The From The Ground Up project notes that some of the Gunnison trails at Signal Peak "proved just how beginner-friendly some of these Gunnison trails are" when riders got through on gravel bikes, though this refers to training rides, not the Gunni Grinder course itself. (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gunnison-gravel-biking-from-the-ground-up/)

## Weather Experienced

**jamesdanielosborne [UNKNOWN]:** "It can also still be very hot in Gunnison this time of the year" referring to early September race date. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**oldandfast [COMPETITIVE]:** Noted weather "does seem 7-10 degrees higher from where I live" when comparing to their home location, prompting them to do "panic sauna sessions" for heat adaptation before the race. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**General context [SOURCE: tourism/official materials]:** Gunnison area "usually is the first area in the Gunnison Valley to dry out in the springtime, and rides well through late fall" with "bountiful sunshine year-round." "With daytime highs rarely exceeding 90 degrees" in summer months. (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gravel-biking-in-gunnison-colorado-with-nick-gould/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course composition [SOURCE: Official]:** "About 83%" of the 120-mile course is on gravel and dirt roads. "Some of these roads receive little to no maintenance and can change drastically after heavy rains and/or snowfall." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**plindley [UNKNOWN]:** "Some really nice smooth sections of gravel at the beginning but then there were hard packed spots with SHARP rocks." Describes terrain pattern as repetitive. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**DMC [UNKNOWN]:** Second-hand description: "climb for 20-30 minutes of rough gravel, descend down rough gravel, go through sand, do it again 3-4 times." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**Neil Beltchenko [UNKNOWN]:** Bikepacking.com route description notes "80 miles of the route consist of well-graded and well-maintained gravel roads, leaving riders with only 33 miles of two-tracks, routes, and steep roads to navigate." The route features "rolling hills and mountain passes" with "high desert flora" transitioning to "high alpine forests." Specific sections mentioned: "ascent of Los Pinos Pass provides a high alpine experience... At 10,500 feet, Los Pinos Pass is the high point of the route, but the climb is manageable and rideable." Also notes "more than 10 miles" along the Great Divide Mountain Bike Route before "a rougher turn for approximately 20 miles. This section features two-tracks with steeper gradients, including the most demanding ascent out of Highway 114 and up Lick Park Road." (https://bikepacking.com/routes/gunni-grinder/)

**From The Ground Up [RECREATIONAL]:** On the Gunni 60 course: "alternated climbing and descending through pristine gravel roads and exhilarating doubletrack that tested our riders technical skills." For their second training day: "10+ mile, 2,300ft climb" at Lost Canyon described as "the longest, toughest gravel road climb of their lives" followed by "a more adventurous, technical section of road." (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gunnison-gravel-biking-from-the-ground-up/)

**Altitude context [SOURCE: route guide]:** "Gunnison itself sits at 7,700 feet" and "higher altitude... might pose a challenge for folks coming from out of state." (https://bikepacking.com/routes/gunni-grinder/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**jamesdanielosborne [UNKNOWN]:** Characterizes it as "one of the hardest days they've ever had on a bike. Relentless climbs (including that big one near the end)." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**Laurel Darren [ELITE]:** Training approach for summer 2021: "started from scratch" in Colorado in June, initially "could not even get up the freaking ski hill on my mountain bike." Built up over the summer by "showing up every time" for group rides and attempting to "stay within a mile" of training partner. Successfully completed Alpine 50 "in sub-6 hours" with 50 miles and 2 major mountain passes. This was after recovering from hormonal imbalance by backing off extreme training ("not the crazy, extreme, 8-hours-per-day regimen I was following in 2016") and following medical guidance. (https://www.wildbunchdesertguides.com/blog/Just-what-doctor-ordered-Phoenix-mountain-bike-tours-next.cfm)

**Neil Beltchenko [UNKNOWN]:** Route assessment notes "Covering 119 miles over two days is a demanding endeavor" and rates overall difficulty as "6 out of 10 for overall difficulty if ridden as an overnighter. Stretch it to three days and that goes down a notch or two." Notes "sufficient water resupply points along the way" and Cathedral Cabins as "a convenient food resupply option in the middle." (https://bikepacking.com/routes/gunni-grinder/)

**From The Ground Up [RECREATIONAL]:** Training progression: Started with Gunni Grinder 60 as "longest ride to date" featuring 5,000ft climbing. Next day did Signal Peak trails followed by "10+ mile, 2,300ft climb" at Lost Canyon where riders "found and pushed beyond" their limits. Route design had "sole purpose of exploring a wide variety of Gunnison's gravel offering while ensuring they would all have an opportunity to 'fail', learn, and adjust their training ahead of race day." (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gunnison-gravel-biking-from-the-ground-up/)

**Cathedral Ranch Cabins option [SOURCE: route guide]:** "At mile marker 51, you can opt to stay at Cathedral Ranch Cabins courtesy of Brad and Annette. Whether you decide to spend the night or just grab some snacks, the cabins provide a delightful pause, enabling you to travel lighter without carrying a sleep system, if you so choose." (https://bikepacking.com/routes/gunni-grinder/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**plindley [UNKNOWN]:** DNF'd at 30 miles after 4 flats, running out of plugs. "I didn't want to attempt the rest of it with no more plugs." Noted seeing "a lot of folks with flats besides me." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**Elevation/altitude [SOURCE: route guide]:** "Another factor to consider is the higher altitude, which might pose a challenge for folks coming from out of state, as they'll likely feel the effects of the elevation." (https://bikepacking.com/routes/gunni-grinder/)

**Road conditions [SOURCE: Official]:** "Some of these roads receive little to no maintenance and can change drastically after heavy rains and/or snowfall. Riders are encouraged to be fully prepared for any and all conditions." Route is described as "remote." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

**DMC [UNKNOWN]:** Comparison note: "Can't be worse than Co2ut?!" and plindley's response: "My skin did not peel off my hands and didn't feel like it was going to anytime soon, so yes I'd say better than Co2Ut" - suggesting Co2ut has a reputation for extremely rough descents causing hand issues, but Gunni Grinder does not reach that level. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/gunni-grinder-2023-colorado/86583)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**From The Ground Up [RECREATIONAL]:** Training trip included stops at local spots: "finished by the roaring Gunnison River with Jermaine's sandwiches, which in our opinion is just about the best way to finish a long bike ride." Celebrated at "Garlic Mike's overlooking the Gunnison River sharing stories of the weekend. The next morning we packed up, grabbed breakfast at the Backcountry Cafe" - describing supportive local infrastructure and community gathering spots. (https://gunnisoncrestedbutte.com/blog/gunnison-gravel-biking-from-the-ground-up/)

**Joel Grimmett [Race creator, UNKNOWN level]:** On course development: "We spent all of our vacation time for more than two years riding, driving, studying maps and running into lots of dead-ends and roads that were just a little too challenging for a gravel event. During this time, we met many great people and really grew to like Gunnison. I think the route that we use showcases the area and has a great amount of unpaved surface variety with as little pavement as possible." (https://bikepacking.com/routes/gunni-grinder/)

**Laurel Darren [ELITE]:** Post-race: "enjoyed a post-race wine festival" after Canon City Crippler with training partners, describing community social atmosphere around Colorado gravel events. (https://www.wildbunchdesertguides.com/blog/Just-what-doctor-ordered-Phoenix-mountain-bike-tours-next.cfm)

**General context [SOURCE: tourism materials]:** Gunnison described as "a hub of outdoor recreation, higher education and entrepreneurship" with "quickly growing business community." The area promotes itself as having "more than 1,000 miles of Forest Service roads in Gunnison County that are rideable on a gravel bike." Race benefits noted: "This event was made possible, in part, by a grant from the City of Gunnison" and "A portion of the race profit helps support Gunnison Trails." (https://www.thegunnigrinder.com/)

**Prize purse [SOURCE: Official]:** "$5,000 prize purse" mentioned for the event. (https://www.thegunnigrinder.com/)
