## Race Overview & Key Facts

**Location:** Nelma/Long Lake, WI (northern Wisconsin)

**Distances:** Three options available:
- 85 miles (formerly called "Century"/100 miles, adjusted to 85) with ~7,500 feet of climbing
- 50 miles (formerly 100K/62 miles, adjusted to 50) with moderate climbing
- 26 miles (formerly 39 miles, adjusted to 26) - shortest option with challenging climbs

**Format:** Self-supported gravel ride on forest roads. GPS routes and cue sheets provided via RidewithGPS. Riders must carry their own water, nutrition, and tools.

**Fundraiser:** Proceeds donated to Alvin Volunteer Fire Department (also listed as Town of Alvin Volunteer Fire and Rescue Department).

**Start Times:** 
- 85-mile route: 8:00am or 9:00am CDT (sources vary)
- 50-mile and 26-mile routes: 9:00am CDT

**Cut-off Times:**
- 85-mile: 5:00pm CDT
- 50-mile: 4:00pm CDT  
- 26-mile: 4:00pm CDT

**Departs from:** Buchanan Memorial Park, County Hwy A, Nelma, WI 54542 (some sources list "16950 WI-55 Long Lake, WI US 54542")

**Minimum donation:** $30 registration fee

**Event dates identified:** September 9, 2022 and September 30, 2023

(https://www.thenxrth.com/events/the-hardwood-hustle, https://www.bikesignup.com/Race/WI/LongLake/TheHardwoodHustle, https://runtrimag.com/race-events/the-hardwood-hustle/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Character:** "Beautiful forest roads" featuring "some of the best gravel the Northwoods has to offer" with both challenging and scenic sections.

**85-mile route specific:** "A hearty challenge even for the seasoned cyclist!" Gas station on route at mile 55 in Hiles, WI.

**26-mile route specific:** "Will still feature great gravel and some challenging climbs" despite being the shortest option.

**Training value:** Multiple sources note this is a "Great opportunity to train for upcoming events such as The Hibernator and Red Granite Grinder."

(https://www.thenxrth.com/events/the-hardwood-hustle, https://www.bikesignup.com/Race/Events/WI/LongLake/TheHardwoodHustle)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Ride philosophy:** The event tagline "Enjoy some slow gravel or get your hustle on!" appears repeatedly, suggesting a welcoming atmosphere for various pacing approaches and abilities.

**Self-supported ethos:** Strong emphasis on self-sufficiency - riders must "carry enough water, nutrition, and tools to provide for themselves" - suggesting a more adventurous, less coddled race experience.

**Community benefit focus:** The charitable component (fundraising for volunteer fire department) adds a community service dimension beyond just the athletic event.

(https://www.thenxrth.com/events/the-hardwood-hustle, https://www.bikesignup.com/Race/WI/LongLake/TheHardwoodHustle)

## Important Note

**CONFLICTING INFORMATION DISCOVERED:** The majority of sources for "Hardwood Hustle" refer to a **basketball coaching podcast and youth basketball training program**, NOT a gravel cycling event. The gravel cycling event appears to be a smaller, regional Wisconsin event with limited online presence and rider reports. No detailed first-person race reports, equipment reviews, or rider experiences were found in the provided sources - only basic event information and registration pages.
