# HAUTE ROUTE GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Details & Format

**Course Structure & Options:**
The Haute Route Gravel events feature multi-day formats with tiered route options to accommodate different abilities. For example, Gravel Epic Switzerland (part of the Haute Route family) offers:

- **Stage One:** Long route (108km, 3,800m+) and short route (63km, 2,080m+)
- **Stage Two:** Long route (78km, 3,090m+) and short route (61km, 2,090m+)
- **Prologue:** Optional Friday warm-up circuit

**Unique Competition Format:**
"Only timed climbs count towards the competition, so you can race against your friends and other competitors if you want too, but it also gives you the chance to relax at the feed stations and meet and ride with other people on route. Top 3 best times are counted towards the podium." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6209/register-now-for-gravel-epic-switzerland)

This format allows riders to race specific segments while maintaining a more social atmosphere on other sections.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Gravel Epic Switzerland - Detailed Terrain Description:**

**Stage One Long Route:**
- Starts with "controlled descent" then transitions to "gravel and farm roads"
- "Riders will wind their way down through the fields and meadows to the village of Sembrancher"
- "The vines planted here for centuries are crisscrossed with old roads, the riders will climb these same routes used by locals dating back to medieval times"
- Passes "ancient salt mines" and "wide open gravel ride through the golden larches"
- Features "Col De Planches" with "incredible views over the Bernese alps, the Rhone river valley, and all the way to Mont Blanc"
- Technical section: "riders will cross streams and roll through dark tunnels carved into the mountain sides complete with rock falls and windows chiselled into the bedrock"
- Climbs "up ski runs and under ski lifts up to the Col de la Croix-de-Coeur overlooking all of Verbier"
- Final challenge: "last climb to the Relais d'Arpette and the finish line"

**Stage Two Long Route:**
- "Riders will climb and ride through farm trails and hay meadows"
- "Pass blackened barns and chalets that have been darkened in the alpine sun over the centuries"
- Follows "ancient bisses, the ingenious Swiss irrigation systems that have supported the alpine farmers for centuries"
- "Keep an eye out for Swiss fortifications that protected the Alpine country from invasion during the Second World War. Faux buildings and bunkers are just a few of the interesting things a keen eyed cyclist can see"
- Village of Liddes is "famous for its local cheese. If you need a food stop look for the cheese vending machine located at the village fromagerie"
- Follows "the ancient trade route from Southern Europe and Rome to Northern Europe. Romans, Celts, and even Napoleon walked the route"
- Climbs "the famous Col du Grand St. Bernard"

(https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/6209/register-now-for-gravel-epic-switzerland)

**Gravel Racing Terrain Variety [ELITE]:**
Testing environment in Nice region: "White gravel roads, winding climbs with sea-view, technical descents through pine forests, loose rock sections and flowing high-speed stretches... On tarmac, hardpack, fine gravel, chunky rocks and muddy trails... Steep ramps tested their climbing prowess, tight corners their handling, fast descents their composure." (https://granfondo-cycling.com/best-race-gravel-bike-review/)

## Event History & Evolution

**10th Anniversary & Flagship Event:**
"The announcement of the acquisition comes as Haute Route celebrates the 10th anniversary of its flagship race, Haute Route Alps, which has earned a reputation as the hardest and best organized multi-stage amateur road cycling event in the world. Since its beginning in 2011, the single event has expanded to a series of races across the world." (https://www.racedirectorshq.com/read/ironman-group-acquires-haute-route-gravel-epic-300/)

**2021 Event Portfolio:**
"In 2021, events range from seven-day race (Haute Route Alps) to five-day events (Dolomites & Pyrenees) and three-day stage races (Ventoux, Crans-Montana and Brazil)." (https://www.racedirectorshq.com/read/ironman-group-acquires-haute-route-gravel-epic-300/)

**Gravel Epic Origins:**
"The Gravel Epic is a new multi-day gravel racing series set in premier destinations like the Atlas Mountains, Swiss Alps and other incredible gravel destinations. Created in 2019, Gravel Epic was acquired by Haute Route earlier in 2021 and currently has events scheduled for Switzerland and Morocco with plans to expand." (https://www.racedirectorshq.com/read/ironman-group-acquires-haute-route-gravel-epic-300/)
