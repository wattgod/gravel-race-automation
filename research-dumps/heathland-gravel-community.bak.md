# HEATHLAND GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Key Details

**Location:** Maasmechelen, Belgium (Limburg province, near Dutch border and Maastricht)

**Dates:** August 7-9, 2026 (3rd edition)

**Distances:** 
- 160km (Official Unbound Gravel Qualifier - requires racing license)
- 110km 
- 70km (highest percentage of off-road)

**Terrain Composition:** 85%+ unpaved/gravel roads with 130km of the 160km route timed across segments (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8239/heathland-gravel-is-back-in-2025)

**Unbound Qualification:** 50 spots for Unbound 2026 available - 25 based on age-group performance, 25 via random lottery among 160km finishers (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/8669/50-participants-qualify-for-2026-unbound-at-2nd-edition-of-heathland-gravel)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Characteristics:**
- Routes through Hoge Kempen National Park, Belgium's first national park covering 127 square kilometers (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8239/heathland-gravel-is-back-in-2025)
- Passes over and between mining slagheaps from Belgium's industrial heritage (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/news/the-impossible-dream-gets-closer-as-unbounds-only-european-qualifier-opens-for-entries)
- Mechelse Heide section blooms purple in August (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8239/heathland-gravel-is-back-in-2025)
- Mix of dunes and forested areas (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8239/heathland-gravel-is-back-in-2025)

**110km specific [RECREATIONAL]:** One rider described it as "110 km, wenig Höhenmeter, aber technisch anspruchsvoll mit Sand, Wurzeln" (110km, low elevation gain, but technically demanding with sand and roots) (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_UzA2wRUPXI)

**70km specific [RECREATIONAL]:** "The shortest distance comes with the highest percentage of off-road tracks" with "breathtaking views along the Meuse" (https://www.heathlandgravel.com/en)

## Event Growth & Status

**Participation Numbers:**
- 2025 edition approached 1,000 participants, marking "substantial growth" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/8669/50-participants-qualify-for-2026-unbound-at-2nd-edition-of-heathland-gravel)
- Maximum 4,000 athletes compete at Unbound, making spots highly coveted (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8239/heathland-gravel-is-back-in-2025)

**Unique Status:**
"Heathland Gravel remains the only European qualifying event for Unbound Gravel" - first designated in the 20-year history of Unbound (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/8669/50-participants-qualify-for-2026-unbound-at-2nd-edition-of-heathland-gravel, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7812/life-time-and-flanders-classics-to-designate-first-ever-unbound-gravel-qualifier)

**Partnership Background:**
Life Time Events chose Flanders Classics based on the organization's "substantial track record in event promotion in Europe" with goal to "grow international participation" at Unbound (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7812/life-time-and-flanders-classics-to-designate-first-ever-unbound-gravel-qualifier)

**Tomas Van Den Spiegel, Flanders Classics CEO [COMPETITIVE]:**
"UNBOUND Gravel is to gravellers what Kona in Hawaii is for triathletes: a monumental sporting goal and exceptional experience. And it is filling up fast; Heathland Gravel in 2024 is the very first and only qualifier event for UNBOUND Gravel 2025. In Maasmechelen, at least 50 participants will be able to claim their spot for next year. Everyone has a chance. Very exceptional." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7812/life-time-and-flanders-classics-to-designate-first-ever-unbound-gravel-qualifier)
