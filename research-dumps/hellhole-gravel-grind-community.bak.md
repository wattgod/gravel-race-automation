# HELLHOLE GRAVEL GRIND STAGE RACE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Jake Andrews [ELITE]:** "Despite Andrew's fast time, his win [in the prologue] yielded him less than a minute lead over his podium mates; a scant margin to hold for 150 miles and would one that prove to be his anchor to carry over the next two stages." Andrews later DNF'd Stage 2 due to mechanical: "While there's no doubt that Jake may have weathered the fight, his rear derailleur had other plans... the landscape of potholes, gravel and mud can take their toll on riders, bikes, and in this case, jockey pulleys." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

**Adam Everest [ELITE], former pro from New Zealand:** Bridged solo to the lead group on Stage 1 - "A local transplant, Adam Everest (racing in the 40+ field) had soloed across the gap (more like an abyss) to join the four at the front... laid the hammer down to bridge up to the group." Finished 1st in Men 40+ category. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

**Dr. Pain (Justin Pfaff) [ELITE]:** "I sort of figured that I could lose more time actually riding the TT than taking the averaged handicap based on those who did ride it." On the course conditions: "The next 20 miles of road racing was characterized by shouts of 'hole', 'big hole', 'hole left', 'hole right'... There was also a cacophony of sounds from chains slapping carbon, aluminum, titanium, and steel. There were also loud cracks, as handlebars twisted in stems." On Saturday: "After coming to crash stop #4 (aided by slamming into some poor chaps thigh… sorry, mate), I took great interest in team mate Pfaff Daddy's flat front tire." On Sunday: "By some complete miracle, I managed to stay about 25 meters behind the front riders. By as firm a reality as the miracle, I was completely gassed and unable to close that 25 meters once we got back to the road. Shortly after I 'sat up' for the second time (now at mile 20)... I was convinced that Pfaff Daddy must have had a mechanical and most certainly needed my help again." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Pfaff Daddy [ELITE]:** On Sunday's race: "His handlebars had suffered that loud cracking sound, and slipped again... he was getting seriously dehydrated or something." Described as being in rough shape at the mile 35 aid station: "Pfaff Daddy just sat down in the gravel. It was a bit shocking, since he usually rides me into the ground and has completed a lot of very hard events." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Chris Moore [ELITE], Race Director:** "We really didn't have a lot of places to train. So we would go out in the Francis Marion forest and ride the gravel roads and the bike, the, the, the hiking trails out there. And that was like I said, probably 2010, 2000, and then that morphed into this would be a great place to put on a gravel race." On the terrain: "Here in the coastal area of South Carolina and it's flat and, and very flat, we, we will go out for a, I think, I think what the 75 mile one day portion of the hell hole is about 400 feet, about 400 feet of climbing... Sometimes our road rides on Saturday. I mean, we can go, we can go 40 and 50 miles and do you know, less than a hundred feet." (https://thegravelride.bike/website/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-south-carolina)

**Jeff [ELITE], Race Co-Director:** "In retrospect, that was just brutal what it does to your body on a cross bike for that distance [in the forest]." On the initial launch: "I think when we, we launched the page within hours, it had over hundred likes and I remember texting Chris and I'm like, what, what did we just get ourselves into here?" (https://thegravelride.bike/website/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-south-carolina)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Types - First Year [ELITE/COMPETITIVE/RECREATIONAL]:** "I think that first year, I think on the podium, I think we had all three. I think we had a road bike, a cross bike, and a mountain bike on the podium that first year." Also notable: "We had a guy show up on a, a 36 inch diameter unicycle... He did, he did 75 miles on that, you know, cycle." (https://thegravelride.bike/website/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-south-carolina)

**Tire Recommendation [ALL LEVELS]:** Official race notes state: "A minimum of 28c wide tires with tread is recommended." (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Equipment Failures [ELITE]:** Jockey pulleys failed on Jake Andrews' rear derailleur due to course conditions. Pfaff Daddy experienced flat tire and handlebars slipping in stem multiple times. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Lights Required [ALL LEVELS]:** For Friday prologue: "The time trial style event will begin just before sunset (6:30 PM) so front and rear lights are required." (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Early Cross Bike Use [ELITE]:** From 2010 era: "We, we hopped on cross bikes and went out there. In retrospect, that was just brutal what it does to your body on a cross bike for that distance." By modern times: "You definitely can tell, you know, everybody is starting to get or ask about, you know, what kind of bike should I get? What kind of gravel bike you know, they're asking about things like tire tire, width, and, and wheels and brakes and all that kind of stuff." (https://thegravelride.bike/website/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-south-carolina)

## Weather Experienced

**2015 Race [ELITE]:** Sunday Stage 2 temperatures reached near 90°F: "The air on Sunday's second and final stage was different. It may have been the near 90°F temperatures or the fact that there were five people within two minutes of the win, but whatever the reason, things were tense." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Elevation Profile [ALL LEVELS]:** "Despite being pancake flat... what the 75 mile one day portion of the hell hole is about 400 feet, about 400 feet of climbing." (https://thegravelride.bike/website/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-south-carolina)

**Surface Conditions [ELITE]:** "The roads of Francis Marion are littered with car sized, bike swallowing potholes... Francis Marion's forest service roads are largely hard packed gravel and pot holes are numerous." Described as "mostly hard-packed gravel, with some grassy doubletrack, Roubaix-rated trail sections along the Palmetto Trail." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Potholes [ELITE]:** Dr. Pain's detailed description: "The next several miles was a blur of pain... all sorts of speeds from the 'teens to brief bursts of 30mph. The bursts of speed came from responses to the fast folk at the front who were attacking each other, and from the chases to get back on after getting stuck behind the numerous crashes... From the cockpit, it felt like a lot of 'dodge left', 'dodge right', 'bunny hop', CLANG, DAMN. From each of these your legs took a hit, your arms took a hit, and your hands got tired." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Mud Holes [ELITE]:** Multiple mud sections described: "We looked up the road a few meters to see a mass pileup and conflagration at the mudhole... Neither of us made it through without 'touching' a foot into foot deep mud. It's rumored that the event moto crashed there on a recon mission earlier." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Singletrack Sections [ELITE]:** Dr. Pain on Sunday's singletrack: "This section was simply brutal. I freely confess that I have absolutely no MTB skills. I don't know what 'technique' might have been used by others, but I could only apply maximum torque and plow through, lurch over, and careen around what ever it was that I couldn't seen in the deep forest underbrush. I heard a lot of futile calls of 'hole' etc. but that was just useless information." The singletrack is described as "hiking trail, full of holes, roots and other MTB stuff" and includes "Roubaix-rated trail sections along the Palmetto Trail." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Navigation [ALL LEVELS]:** Multiple instances of the lead group missing turns: "Stage One started in a panic... the peloton blew past the first turn and was forced to backtrack and chase" and "The race again started with the lead group missing an early turn and being forced to scurry through some difficult trail sections." Race notes specify: "Routes will not be marked. GPS course files will be provided approximately two weeks prior to the event." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Wildlife [ALL LEVELS]:** Course includes "megafauna such as bear, snakes, boar (and chupacabra…)." Race rules note: "Traffic in the National Forest includes horses. Horseback riders have the right of way on trails." (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Remote Location [ALL LEVELS]:** "To say this area is remote is an understatement. Bring with you what you think you may need for before, during, and after the race." And: "There are no services (including most cellular) or utilities at this site [Hellhole Road Primitive Camp for prologue] (one portajohn is provided)." (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Prologue Speed [ELITE]:** Jake Andrews won the 2015 prologue at "a blistering fast 24.5 mph performance" for the six mile course. "Over half of the field laid down times averaging better than 21 mph for the six mile course." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

**Stage 1 Tactics [ELITE]:** After a crash split the field, "the frontrunners simply worked to distance themselves from the rest of the field and gain time... The four worked simply to maintain the status quo and settle their differences on Stage Two." The lead group of four finished Stage 1 "After nearly four hours of racing" with "the five finished in a field sprint." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

**Front Group Positioning Critical [ELITE]:** Dr. Pain: "So, stay to the front, to get through that pinch point. So, Dr. Pain positioned himself about 5 riders from the front. That became 5 riders from the back when we rode past the turn. My first match was burned getting back to the group, then to the front." Multiple crashes and navigation errors emphasized the importance of front positioning. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Aid Station Strategy [ELITE]:** Dr. Pain described the mile 35 aid station stop where Pfaff Daddy was extremely depleted: "As I consumed about everything offered, Pfaff Daddy had to be coaxed to eat, or move, or even respond." Limited support available: "One stocked aid station will be available on each day's course and limited support will be provided along the courses... Bring with you what you think you may need including tools, parts and nutrition." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/ and https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Stage Race Time Management [ELITE]:** Jake Andrews' 15-second prologue lead was under attack all of Stage 2 and ultimately lost to mechanical failure, demonstrating how small time gaps over 150 miles can swing the overall. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

## DNF Risk Factors

**DNF Rate [ALL LEVELS]:** 2015 race had extremely high attrition: "50% of the stage racers ended the weekend with a DNF." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

**Mechanical Failures [ELITE]:** Jake Andrews lost the overall lead on Stage 2 due to derailleur failure from course conditions. Multiple riders experienced flat tires. Pfaff Daddy had handlebars slip in stem multiple times. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Crashes [ELITE]:** "Stage One started in a panic... carnage soon ensued with a large crash that caused a split in the field." Multiple additional crashes throughout: Dr. Pain mentioned "crash stop #4" by mid-race. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Heat and Hydration [ELITE]:** Sunday's 90°F temperatures and Pfaff Daddy's extreme dehydration at mile 35 demonstrate heat management as a critical factor. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Physical Depletion Over Two Days [ELITE]:** The cumulative toll visible in Pfaff Daddy's condition on Sunday: "he was getting seriously dehydrated or something... he usually rides me into the ground and has completed a lot of very hard events." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Cutoff Times [ALL LEVELS]:** Official rules: "Stage Race - Riders must complete each day's stage within 7 hours. 75 Mile Fondo - Riders must complete the course within 7 hours. 45 Mile Fondo - Riders must complete the 45 miles within 5 hours." (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Historical Setting:** "The Francis Marion National Forest... once played host to the Swamp Fox; a Revolutionary War hero that made life hell for British troops." The forest is "named for the revolutionary war hero Francis Marion, who was known to the British as the Swamp Fox. He used these swamps, creeks and forest areas to ambush and hide from the British and was depicted in the movie 'The Patriot'." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Unique Race Format:** "A one-of-a-kind race that begins by showcasing competitors in a night-time prologue... a three-day stage, is the first and longest running gravel stage race in the United States." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report and https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Pre-Race Atmosphere [ELITE]:** "During the pre-race preparations we were serenaded by another racer with an orange plastic horn. I don't think it was a flugelhorn, but I do think he had ingested too much caffeine." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/hell-hole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report-by-dr-pain/)

**Post-Race Food:** "Post-race food (included with registration) will be available from Empanada Cuisines!" (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Mid-Race Gathering:** Schedule includes "6:30 PM - Mid-race gathering at Commonhouse Aleworks" on Saturday evening between stages. (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Finish Line Awards:** "All participants who finish will receive a Hellhole Gravel Grind Finishers Award... Awards/Medals/Prizes will be given to the top 3 finishers in each category of the stage race... Podiums will be presented approximately 20 minutes after each race." (https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Stage Winner Jerseys:** "Lowe and Everest donned stage winner's custom Hellhole Gravel Grind jersey their respective fields." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)

**Event Growth:** Race directors noted the rapid initial interest: "When we, we launched the page within hours, it had over hundred likes" in 2013. The event "is quickly becoming a marquee gravel event and should be on any gravel enthusiast's bucket list!" (https://thegravelride.bike/website/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-south-carolina and https://www.mtpleasantvelo.org/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race)

**Accomplishment Factor:** "Completing it represents a true accomplishment!" with the 50% DNF rate emphasizing the challenge. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/hellhole-gravel-grind-stage-race-report)
