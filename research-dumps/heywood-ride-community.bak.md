# HEYWOOD RIDE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Dustin Hruby Wyttenhove [COMPETITIVE]:** "It is an extremely fun and accessible event based out of Northfield, MN which allows riders to get out and experience some really great gravel riding, without the self-seriousness and pressure of the increasingly competitive modern gravel racing scene." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Dustin Hruby Wyttenhove [COMPETITIVE]:** "My training plan involves bike-commuting on my e-cargo bike 3-4 times per week, trying to use my bike for errands whenever possible, and the occasional party/pastry-paced social ride. So while I ride regularly, and have done a few longer events this spring already, I have not exactly been Training with a capital T." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Dustin Hruby Wyttenhove [COMPETITIVE]:** "I never feel as fast on a bike as when I have been on a long gravel section, dealing with the constant bumps and chatter of the surface and the bike, then suddenly turn off onto a paved road." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Dustin Hruby Wyttenhove [COMPETITIVE]:** "Last year I wasn't able to make it up [the MMR climb], getting stuck behind another rider and spinning out my back wheel before I was halfway up the first pitch. Not wanting a repeat this year I made sure to pick a line that was mostly free of other riders and tried to carry some momentum into the hill." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Atsuko [UNKNOWN]:** Rode a Cake Merveilleux (custom steel 650b CX bike) with Shimano 105 Mechanical, Nitto cockpit, custom Wolftooth finishing kit. Tires: Panaracer Gravel King Slick TLR 750bx38mm. Gearing: Wolftooth 38T Oval x 11-36 11s. No mechanicals reported. (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

**General recommendation:** "Nearly any tire will work, though we suggest staying above 32c." (https://theheywoodride.com/)

**Layering advice [UNKNOWN]:** Atsuko brought and wore multiple layers for 50°F conditions: long sleeve Verge skinsuit, Podiumwear vest, lightweight jacket, kneewarmers, lightweight Wolf Tooth buff. Reports wearing "every single layer of clothing I brought" and finding it "comfortable... Not too hot and not too cold." (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 (May 17):** Dustin's report suggests warm, sunny conditions with significant headwinds (20-30 mph) in the later portions of the ride. Heat was building during the day. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**2025 (May 17):** Atsuko reports "rudely chilly day" with 50°F temperatures, a stark contrast to the "2 weeks of shorts and T-shirt conditions" leading up to the event. Many riders wore "full length tights on, gloves, jackets." Significant headwinds reported (gusted "enough to push me around on the bike"). (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**MMR (Minimum Maintenance Road) Climb:** Dustin describes it as "the main attraction, big-ticket event of the Heywood Ride" with a "seemingly vertical wall of gravel strewn with riders." Features a "bright yellow sign stating 'MINIMUM MAINTENANCE ROAD - Travel At Your Own Risk'" followed by a steep climb with multiple pitches. The top is described as "tree-shaded tunnel of the climb into the bright and open flat." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Shady Lane climb:** Mentioned by Atsuko as a climb requiring walking. (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

**Oxford Mill ruins:** Located at the bottom of the third descent in the early section, "an old grain mill along the river that operated from 1878 to 1905." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Opening section:** "First few sections of the ride were an absolute blast" following the Cannon River northeast towards Cannon Falls. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Mid-course climbs:** After about 10 miles, riders "turned into both the wind and the hills" with "three climbs, all about the same length, each immediately followed by a speedy descent back down." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Valley section:** "Some of the nicest and prettiest of the ride, descending down a valley to the Little Cannon River along winding rural roads surrounded by greenery and farmland, before climbing back out on the other side." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Final approach:** "Long straight back into town" with sustained 20-30 mph headwinds, approximately 5 miles from the turn back to town (though felt much longer to riders). Last few blocks go "past houses and the golf course" to Central Park. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Surface mix:** "There is a little bit of pavement, but only enough to make important connections." Approximately 7 miles of mostly paved road in mid-course. "Our surfaces are generally smooth and the gravel is downright pleasant." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove, https://theheywoodride.com/)

**General terrain:** "Low trafficked, mostly one lane wide and vary from long rollers to short, punchy climbs. You'll pass active farms... You'll also see your fair share of wooded lanes. From easy cruisers to tough climbs and fast descents." (https://theheywoodride.com/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start pacing [UNKNOWN]:** Atsuko notes "As always the start was fast" and found herself doing 18+ mph when expecting to target 14 mph groups. Deliberately slowed and "let myself drift backwards through the crowd" to avoid blowing up. (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

**Average speed [UNKNOWN]:** Atsuko completed 58.39 miles at 11.3 mph average. (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

**Nutrition [UNKNOWN]:** Atsuko's on-bike food for 60 miles: "1 sleeve Clif Shotblock (salted watermelon), a bag of Haribo Twin Snakes, 100 ml Kirkland pure maple syrup." Pre-ride: Kwik Trip breakfast sandwich. (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

**Nutrition [COMPETITIVE]:** Dustin brought an energy bar and filled water at the rest stop. Used an "emergency caffeine gel" near the end when starting to bonk with approximately 3 miles remaining. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**MMR climb strategy [COMPETITIVE]:** Dustin's advice: "Make sure to pick a line that was mostly free of other riders and tried to carry some momentum into the hill before shifting up to the spinny gears." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Rest stop timing [COMPETITIVE]:** Dustin stopped at Urland Lutheran Church (the lone rest stop on 55-mile route) for about 10 minutes, noting that "stopping for any amount of time allowed the adrenaline to wear off, and the heat of the day was starting to build, so by the time I left the church I was already starting to feel pretty drained and slow." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Distance flexibility:** Riders can choose between distances on ride day. Atsuko notes: "What I really appreciate about the Heywood is that there are many distance options, and no pressure to choose your mileage during registration. If I was feeling crappy or underdressed, I could at least do the 30 miler." (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Bonking on final stretch:** Dustin notes the final 5-mile straight back to town with 20-30 mph headwinds was particularly challenging when energy was low: "I was crawling along" and needed an emergency caffeine gel. The lack of distance information (GPS failure) made this section feel much longer mentally. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Weather preparedness:** 2025 conditions caught riders off-guard with 50°F temperatures after weeks of warm weather. Atsuko questioned whether to even start: "Maybe I can just take a nice nap in the car instead?" Adequate layering proved essential. (https://atsukos.day/2025/06/13/2025-heywood-ride-report/)

**MMR climb:** Can force riders to walk or turn around. Dustin couldn't complete it in his first year. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**No podium, no race:** The event has "no sponsors, no podium, is not a race" and focuses on being "a welcoming space for all people." Did you finish first? "Cool! There is no podium." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/no-sponsors-no-podium-the-heywood-ride-at-the-heart-of-grassroots-community-gravel)

**Cost & accessibility:** Registration is just $25 regardless of distance. Originally started as pay-what-you-want. Features a "pay for another rider" program with around 100 riders typically contributing. If you need financial support, "this community fund has your back, no questions asked." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/no-sponsors-no-podium-the-heywood-ride-at-the-heart-of-grassroots-community-gravel, https://theheywoodride.com/)

**Growth:** Grew from 800 riders in 2022 to nearly 1,200 registered in 2024. Organizers believe they could eventually grow to 2,000 while maintaining grassroots approach. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/no-sponsors-no-podium-the-heywood-ride-at-the-heart-of-grassroots-community-gravel)

**Finish line:** "Like much bigger events it even sends riders through a finishing chute before you reach the line, although instead of big branded inflatable arches riders follow the tree-arched sidewalk into the center of park, with friends, fans, and fellow riders cheering them to the finish line at the center of the plaza." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Social atmosphere:** Dustin notes "Being out on bikes in the sunshine, having a good time with your pals is the perfect way to spend a Saturday." Multiple mentions of chatting with friends during the ride. (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Inclusivity:** E-bikes are allowed, including cargo bikes. Dustin gives a "shoutout to the dad I've seen 2 years in a row bringing their toddler along on the back of the e-cargo bike - goals." (https://pie.bike/blog/heywood-55-ride-report-dustin-hruby-wyttenhove)

**Post-race 2025:** Addition of "music and food trucks at the finish line in Northfield's tree-covered park" with emphasis on "creating a welcoming environment where riders can set up tents, enjoy a fun party and cheer on finishers." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/no-sponsors-no-podium-the-heywood-ride-at-the-heart-of-grassroots-community-gravel)

**Origins:** Connection to the Almanzo ride. Chris Skogen (Almanzo founder) moved the event briefly to Northfield in 2019. After COVID pause, Skogen "officially passed the torch" to Heywood organizers Marty Larson, Ben Witt, and Mike Lehmkhul in 2022. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/no-sponsors-no-podium-the-heywood-ride-at-the-heart-of-grassroots-community-gravel)

**Run by 3 people:** Event is run by just 3 core team members plus their spouses and a handful of volunteers. No corporate sponsorships. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/no-sponsors-no-podium-the-heywood-ride-at-the-heart-of-grassroots-community-gravel)

**Free camping:** Free camping offered at Sechler Park (no facilities, but camping normally not allowed so special arrangement). "If you're following the trends and have a Camper van or rooftop tent, we've got you covered! If you're tenting it, you're welcome as well." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/meet-the-heywood-your-new-favorite-pay-what-you-want-gravel-event)

**Northfield town amenities:** "College town of approximately 20,000 people. We have a thriving downtown with cafés, bakeries, and lots of stores with actual personality." Three breweries, a distillery, and a cidery. Nerstrand State Park and Caron Park are ten-minute drive from start line. (https://theheywoodride.com/, https://www.thenxrth.com/post/meet-the-heywood-your-new-favorite-pay-what-you-want-gravel-event)
