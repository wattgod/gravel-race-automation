# HEYWOOD RIDE - RAW RESEARCH DUMP

## OFFICIAL DATA

**2026 Race Date**
The Heywood 2026 is scheduled for **Saturday, May 16, 2026**[1][5]. This is approximately 3 months and 8 days from the current date (February 8, 2026).

**Distance Options**
The race offers **five distance options**, not the single 50-mile distance mentioned in your known info[1][3]:
- 30 miles (beginner-focused)
- 60 miles
- 110 miles
- 165 miles
- 390 miles

**Start Times and Locations**
All rides start from **Central Park, Northfield, MN 55057**[1]. Start times vary by distance[1]:
- 390-mile riders: 8:00 AM (Thursday, May 14)
- 165-mile and 110-mile riders: 9:00 AM
- 60-mile riders: Start time not explicitly listed in search results
- 30-mile riders: Start time not explicitly listed in search results

Participants must **check in at least 30 minutes before their ride time**[4].

**Registration and Entry Fees**
Specific entry fee amounts are **not provided in the search results**. However, the event notes that "after initial registration is closed we'll still offer last-minute signup, right up until the morning of the ride, but at a steeper price of double"[1]. Registration is available through Eventbrite and the official website (theheywoodride.com)[1][4]. The race organizers explicitly state they "do our best to minimize" entry fee barriers, and offer options for donors to "buy an entry for another person anonymously during registration"[4].

**Field Size**
The expected number of participants is described as "many hundreds of your closest riding buddies"[1], but specific historical or projected field sizes are **not provided in the search results**.

**Prize Purse**
**No prize purse information is available** in the search results. This appears to be a community-focused gravel ride rather than a competitive race with prize money.

**Elevation Gain**
Your known info indicates 2,000 ft elevation gain. However, the search results do **not provide specific elevation profiles or total elevation gain figures** for any of the distance options. One source only notes that the roads "vary from long rollers to short, punchy climbs"[4].

**Aid Station Details**
The race provides **minimal guaranteed aid**[4]. Key details:
- Cue sheets will not be provided, but riders can print them from Ride with GPS or download route files to GPS for navigation[6]
- "There MIGHT be unsanctioned aid stations. We don't control where they will go, or if they will show up. Be prepared to not have any!"[6]
- Urland Lutheran Church on Highway 9 is noted as a potential aid/snack stop (mile 33 on 60-mile course; mile 33 off-route on 110 and 165; mile 78 on 110-mile)[6]
- Convenience stores are located just before mile 70 on the 110-mile route (Cannon Falls) and at mile 93.5 (mile 147 for 165-mile riders) with Nerstrand Meats and a convenience store across the street[6]
- **Participants must bring cash** for purchases at these stops[6]
- **No official cutoff times are mentioned** in the search results
