# HOLLY SHELTER GRAVEL GRINDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Key Facts

Holly Shelter Gravel Grinder takes place at Holly Shelter Game Land, 7471 Shaw Highway, Rocky Point, NC 28457. The race is organized by Trans-Sylvania Productions and is part of the North Carolina Gravel Series.

**2026 Race Date:** March 28, 2026
**Race Start Time:** 9:00 AM (Registration opens 7:15 AM, closes 8:30 AM)

**Distance Options:**
- Short Course: 32.5 miles, ~192 feet of elevation
- Long Course: 54.7 miles, ~293 feet of elevation  
- 100 Mile option (offered in 2021, unclear if continuing)

**Aid Stations:**
- Short Course: One aid station at 25 miles
- Long Course: Two aid stations at 25 miles and 47 miles

**2021 Results [ELITE]:**
- 32 Miles Winner: Josh Bennett - 1:45:19 (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=244174)
- 50 Miles Winner: Jonathan Dubel - 2:38:02 (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=244174)
- 100 Miles Winner: Todd Hancock - 5:36:04 (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=244174)

**Registration:**
- Opens December 8, 2025
- Early pricing ends March 15, 2026 ($10 increase after)
- Online registration closes March 26, 2026 at 11:00 PM
- Must register by March 15 to guarantee event shirt
- Race day registration available if not sold out (no shirt included)

**Categories:**
Long Course: Open Men, Open Women, Men/Women 30-39, 40-49, 50-59, 60+, Clydesdale, E-Bike (Non-Competitive)
Short Course: Open Men, Open Women, Men/Women 40-49, 50-59, 60+, Clydesdale, E-Bike (Non-Competitive)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Ray George [UNKNOWN]** provides detailed firsthand terrain description from exploring Holly Shelter Game Land: "Gravel was a mix of sand, mushy sand, gravel and some sections of grass...Temps in upper 70's at start to mid 90's with high humidity" for a 34-mile ride. On a separate 39-mile route: "Gravel was a mix of sand, mushy sand, gravel and some sections of grass. Temps in upper 60's at start to upper 90's with high humidity and no tree coverage." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2019/9/14/recap-holly-shelter-game-land-gravel)

Official course description states: "You will experience all types of road surfaces in this race. You will start out on a short patch of paved roads, then after you get into the woods you'll see everything from gravel roads, dirt roads along with packed and loose sand roads. And a few wet areas depending on what kind of rain we get before the race. It sure isn't a true Gravel Grinder without some mud holes right?" (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/hsgg)

The terrain is described as: "Well-Marked Course" with "Fully Stocked Aid Stations" (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/hsgg)

**Elevation Profile:**
Despite the modest total elevation gain (192-293 feet depending on course), the terrain is characterized as "Moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/9355/holly-shelter-gravel-grinder)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Ray George [UNKNOWN]** encountered a significant course hazard: "At mile 13 I had turn back because the road was washed out over multiple sections and Jack warned me that alligators and snakes are abundant. I did not venture into the water." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2019/9/14/recap-holly-shelter-game-land-gravel)

The game land location presents wildlife risks, with alligators and snakes noted as "abundant" in certain areas, particularly around washed-out road sections.

**Parking Access Issue:** "Parked at the Holly Shelter Gun Range, but the parking lot is limited access during business hours" - race parking may be at a different designated location (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2019/9/14/recap-holly-shelter-game-land-gravel)
