## Weather Experienced

**2025 Race (February 22):** Temperatures plunged into the mid-20s°F at the start, climbing into the 50s°F during the day. The course had received more than seven inches of rain the previous week, which dried into "hero dirt" conditions. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**2025 Race:** "The morning began with temperatures well below the freezing mark... The week before, seven inches of rain soaked the dirt roads and washed out a section of the course along the Chattahoochee River, but riders found limited muddy sections." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/michael-garrison-lauren-de-crescenzo-repeat-as-winners-of-homegrown-gravel/)

**2024 Race (February 24):** Started at 38°F in the morning and warmed up to 60°F by mid-day. "This made it a bit tough to dress for, but at least this year was rain free. There was a decent head wind coming out of the NNW that was going to make the AL section of the race a little more challenging." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**2025 Race (February 22):** "The morning of the race started out a bit chilly at 38° F and was going to warm up to 60 ° F by mid-day... Note the feature photo—most people raced with legwarmers and in full winter jackets!" (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**General Advice [COMPETITIVE]:** "Be prepared for ANY weather. I've ridden in shorts and short sleeves in this area in past years around this time with no problems, but it was COLD on race day." (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:** The 100-mile course contains approximately 8,700 feet of climbing with 80% gravel (some sources say 70% gravel), split roughly 50% in Georgia and 50% in Alabama. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/ and https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**2024 Specific:** "A week before the event, a bridge was taken out by raging water from a storm, which caused the race promotor to make some major changes to one of the routes. The 30-mile route was forced to become an out and back, and the other two were shortened by a few miles." The 100-mile ended up with about 8000 ft elevation gain in 2024, with 7500 ft noted by one rider. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Gravel Quality [COMPETITIVE]:** "There were all types of gravel too, small compact gravel that was quite fast, and gravel with random chunks that could cause a pinch flat if hit wrong... While out there it seemed like there was a lot of paved sections, but on paper there was only about 30 miles total. That is a pretty solid job with connecting all the different gravel sectors." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Course Features:** "The course featured a 23% grade section and quite a bit of climbing, but it also had quite a few fast technical descents." Rider noted having "two top 10s" on downhill Strava segments "with many of the gnarly technical gravel descents not having segments created for them." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Water Crossings:** "The course had some creek crossings, sketchy bridge crossings, but very minimal muddy sections." Multiple sources mention that "Your feet will almost certainly get wet." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/ and https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Sand Sections:** "The sand can definitely catch you out, so keep your head up at all times!" (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**First Mile:** "About a mile into the race, we hit the first off-road sector that was essentially a single-track path with a muddy section at the end. The 2024 Border Wars Gravel race instantly backed up as riders got off their bikes and were going single file to walk around the deepest section of the mud." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Scenery [COMPETITIVE]:** "The course was beautiful, and the weather was perfect. I loved riding through some of the little rural towns, isolated forested sections, and pasture areas... a mix of quiet backroads, rolling hills, and relentless gravel rollers." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Technical Note:** "According to the race director, the 100-mile contains 8,500 feet of climbing and 40-ish gravel segments, including all the secluded pavé, gravel, dirt, sand, and streams you can handle." (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Strategy - Critical [COMPETITIVE]:** "Advice to future self, stick to the front at the beginning of the race. Ride a little bit harder up the hill to stay on the front group." After missing the initial break due to the muddy bottleneck, rider spent significant energy chasing and never rejoined the lead group. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Neutral Start Warning [COMPETITIVE]:** "NEVER TRUST THE NEUTRAL START! Unless there is a lead vehicle keeping riders from passing and controlling the initial race speed, never trust a neutral start!" The 2024 race had a supposed neutral rollout through a muddy section, but "the neutral roll out was blown up and the first group to pass through the mud was already up the road." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Elite Race Dynamics [ELITE]:** In 2025 men's race, "After a three-mile neutral rollout, the men's race began at a controlled pace, with riders mindful of the grueling course ahead." Ian Boswell launched early attacks but the pack kept him in check. "By mile 30, the lead group had begun to splinter." The decisive moves came as the race entered "its most punishing section." By mile 70, it was down to two leaders at the front. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**Women's Elite Tactics [ELITE]:** Lauren De Crescenzo "made her move early. Twenty miles in, as the race entered the more technical Alabama gravel, she attacked" and rode solo for the remaining 80+ miles. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**Hydration [COMPETITIVE]:** "I had two bottles on my bike and a frame bag with a bladder. I was out of water by the end of the race and could have used a little bit more water, but I managed." This rider completed the course in 5:58 moving time. (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Course Knowledge:** "The course, as mentioned earlier, isn't marked, so make sure you have the route loaded to your cycling computer or phone." (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Time Benchmarks:**

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues [ELITE]:** In the women's race, "Only Liza Ray (Solutions Cycling) could match her acceleration, but soon after, Ray dropped her chain, leaving De Crescenzo to ride solo for the remainder of the race." This dropped chain likely cost Ray a chance at the win. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**Weather-Related:** Early season timing means potential for freezing temperatures and weather variability. Multiple sources emphasize being prepared for "ANY weather" including full winter conditions in February. (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Navigation:** Unmarked course requires GPS navigation - riders without loaded routes or with dead batteries could get lost. (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Flat Risk:** Course has "gravel with random chunks that could cause a pinch flat if hit wrong." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Vibe [COMPETITIVE]:** "The vibes and the race itself were great—we highly recommend putting it on your list for gravel races that are worth doing, especially in the early season!" (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Camaraderie [ELITE]:** "It's competitive, but there are no people standing on the start line 45 minutes before trying to get up front. There was a neutral section through the mud and everyone just waited for each other, essentially a sense of mutual respect." - Ian Boswell (https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/michael-garrison-lauren-de-crescenzo-repeat-as-winners-of-homegrown-gravel/)

**Post-Race [COMPETITIVE]:** "The post ride was well hosted. There were quite a few booths of vendors. There were prize giveaways, food, and beer. The raffle required you to be present, which probably increases your chances of winning if you stay. I didn't stay too long afterwards. There was plenty of food and drinks with quite a few varieties. There was a band." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Festival Element [COMPETITIVE]:** "The promoter puts huge effort into the festival element, tons of post-ride food and beverage. The whole weekend is a neat takeover of the small downtown of Franklin, GA." (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Race Amenities:** "Custom finisher beanies for all finishers. Homecooked food thanks to the East Point Track Club, plus beer from Halfway Crooks. Raffle included Wahoo Kickr, three new Ace computers and a bunch of Trackr heart-rate monitors, a S&S Fire pit, 4 person Saris bike rack, Custom Eliel Clothing, Custom Garneau socks, Chandi Bikes swag bags, IRC Tires and more." Custom hand-made trophies and medals made by race organizer. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**Pro Field:** "The race has offered a pretty hefty pro prize purse for a couple of years so increasingly there are more heavy hitters showing up despite the small town and early season timing." $10,000 prize purse with $5,000 for women. (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/ and https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**Participation:** 524 total registered riders in 2025, from 17 states and multiple Canadian provinces. 25 women in the elite 100-mile field. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8396/)

**Charitable Giving:** "Proceeds of this event go to MTB Atlanta, Heard County Food Pantry, and East Point Track Club. This year they are planning to donate about $2000 to MTB Atlanta and Heard County Food Pantry." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Name History:** Formerly called "Border Wars" - renamed to "Homegrown Gravel Adventure" for 2025. Race promoter explained: "Originally, the name 'Border Wars Gravel Adventure' came from the light-hearted feud between the border folk of Georgia and Alabama... However, global tensions have continued around the world, humanity simply refuses to play nice. We've come to understand that sailing under our 'Border Wars' flag could cause our participants some internal conflict, which was neither our intent nor a consequence we were willing to accept. So we changed it. Done." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Small Town Character:** "This event is a hidden gem for gravel racing and has a huge potential to become a premier event. Border Wars is a relatively small event, but it is growing. Now that is held in downtown Franklin, GA, USA, it has plenty of room for growth and can handle a lot more riders." (https://jaylocycling.com/2024-border-wars-gravel-race-report/)

**Local Tips [COMPETITIVE]:** "There's one great Mexican restaurant in the small town of Franklin—Del Rio—that's worth checking out. Massive margaritas and huge servings, plus they're great about the race basically shutting them down for a day!" No coffee shops in town, but coffee trucks at the race. "There are port-a-potties on site, but if you go about 25 meters past them next to the police station, there's a great clean public washroom that's open 24/7. Use that instead of waiting in line—for some reason, no one seems to notice it!" (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)

**Parking:** "Parking at the high school (if you didn't camp the night before) is pretty far from the start/finish, and at the top of a hill. If it's chilly out, I'd suggest riding to the start in some old warm stuff, then stashing it at a vendor booth or near the start/finish area." (https://consummateathlete.com/homegrown-gravel-intel/)
