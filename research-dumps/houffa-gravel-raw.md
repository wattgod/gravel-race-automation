## **TERRAIN & COURSE**

**Surface Breakdown**

The course is approximately **70-80% off-road**[1][4]. The search results describe it as "gravel terrain" but do not provide a precise breakdown between gravel, dirt, singletrack, and pavement.

**Key Course Features and Named Climbs**

The race **immediately challenges riders with the steep Rue du Saint Roch climb**[1], which is "one of the steep road climbs during Liège Bastogne Liège," a famous professional cycling monument[4]. This occurs within the first 5 km of the race[4]. The first gravel section begins after this initial climb[4].

The 2025 course features **loops of approximately 47 km**, with the course being redesigned for the 2026 European Championships to include "less technical downhills"[4].

**Technical Difficulty Level**

The 2025 course is described as **"challenging"** with steep climbs and rolling terrain. For 2026, the redesigned course will have "less technical downhills but is still challenging with 700m of elevation per lap"[4].

**Elevation Profile**

Each lap contains approximately **700 m elevation gain**[1][3][4]. For the full 141 km elite distance (three laps), this totals approximately 2,100 m of elevation gain.

## **COMMUNITY & REPUTATION**

**Race History**

Houffa Gravel was **founded in 2021**[1]. The 2025 edition marks **the fifth edition**[1], and it is **the fourth edition as a UCI Gravel World Championships qualifier on Belgian soil**[1].

**Significance and Prestige**

Houffa Gravel serves as **the third leg of the Bolero Gravel Series**[1][3] and is described as **"a true classic on the gravel calendar"**[3]. It is **"a qualifier for the UCI Gravel World Championships 2025"**[1].

The race has additional prestige because the 2025 edition unveiled the newly redesigned course intended for the **2026 UEC European Gravel Championships**, which will take place at the same location on August 30, 2026[1][4]. This gives riders a strategic advantage to preview the championship course.

Houffalize itself is described as **"a region renowned for UCI Mountain Bike competitions"** and **"a mountain bike capital"** gaining prominence in gravel racing from "hosting World Cups to qualifying events"[1].
