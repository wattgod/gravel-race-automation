# HUNGRY BEAR 100 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Dan (One On One Bicycle Studio) [COMPETITIVE]:** "We missed the start of the race. For the second year in a row. We didn't care. That's part of the allure of gravel racing for us, it's low stress... Plus, fun and adventure always trump competition, particularly when you get to ride through the Chequamegon forest for 6 or 7 hours." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Dan [COMPETITIVE]:** "The race ends with its toughest two stretches of road. Rock Lake Road and Camp Road 38 make up the infamous finishing stretch before spitting you back out to pavement for one final short climb into Cable for the finish... There are around 2k feet of elevation in those punchy, sandy hills and descents stacked into that stretch." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Monica Shoberg [UNKNOWN]:** "In this reflective and deeply personal story, Monica Shoberg shares what it was like to take on the Snacking Bear 60-mile route, how she shaved three hours off her time from last year, and why the true heart of the Hungry Bear isn't about speed, it's about people." **Linda Shydlowski [UNKNOWN]:** "As I rode along the course, two feelings were present with me every mile, and they will forever stay with me: gratitude and joy. **Parker (original course designer) [COMPETITIVE]:** "The course that Parker designed winds its way out of Cable and is a beautiful mixture of forest service roads and country gravel roads, with the occasional stretch on pavement. (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Gravel bike vs MTB [COMPETITIVE perspective]:** "A gravel bike with 40mm tires will likely be fastest but an XC mtb with short travel suspension and firm tires would be much more comfy and faster on the rocky descents. There are also going to be some muddy sections like the 2.5 mile long ATV Trail 8 connector at mile 77/41 (Hungry/Snacking) between Lost Land Lake Rd and Rock Lake Rd on which a 2" mtb tire will be better." (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

**Tire choice from ELITE rider:** "The Salsa Cutthroat on 2.2 Teravails came in handy at mile 80 when the roads turned into rocky..." (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZoCCyzmw5Eg)

**General bike guidance [UNKNOWN level source]:** "This is billed as a gravel ride, but many sections do have baby-head rocks and other smaller glacial erratics that make for a bumpy ride. Our Chequamegon fortified gravel is not as consistent as Iowa or Northfield gravel." (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

## Weather Experienced

**2022 conditions [COMPETITIVE]:** "Most of us underdressed for the day, with the forecast promising sun and temps over 50 all day. We were a bit surprised when it was 34 when we woke up... We were hoping for the sun to break through all day. Finally it did and we welcomed the warmth, with temps just reaching up above 50 degrees." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**2024 conditions [UNKNOWN]:** "Mother Nature did not disappoint" — over 800 riders participated with apparently favorable conditions. (https://blog.newmoonski.com/2024/05/15/2024-hungry-bear-a-photo-recap/)

**Pre-race week conditions [UNKNOWN]:** "As of event week, all sections of all routes of the Hungry Bear are passable by bike but some may be a bit soft from snowmelt and new rain in the forecast. Some small sections still have snow on the road now, but most will likely be entirely melted. A few northern segments had standing water, but shallow enough to ride through. Our soil and the gravel forest roads drain well, so all it takes is a couple warm dry days and things can firm up." (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Final 25 miles [COMPETITIVE perspective]:** "The gravel is perfect, small crushed stone and conditions were good considering the tough storms that hit the region last summer. That is, until you get to mile 75... Camp 38 Road (aka, 'Moonscape' aka, 'Scott's road to Flatsville' aka, 'you're almost at the end' aka, 'you're going to feel it tomorrow')" (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Rock Lake Road & Camp Road 38 [COMPETITIVE perspective]:** "Descending into the corners, which had been formed into tiered berms by the ATV traffic, required concentration, and to our quickly dwindling fitness, felt a bit insane. Hitting a sandy off-camber corner at 30mph is intense enough, not factoring in the extreme level of fatigue in your arms and legs by that point. Once you climb out of Rock Lake Road, you hang a left onto Camp Road 38, and you've got more of the same, quick, punchy hills and descents, this time, you completely throw the idea of 'gravel' out the window. The makeup of that trail is exclusively sand...and big rocks." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Opening miles:** "The first 10 miles on Lake Owen Road" is paved, hilly and curvy with narrow two-lane road with no painted centerline and short sight lines. Race organizers work to get moto-pace lead out due to traffic concerns. (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

**ATV Trail 8 connector:** 2.5 mile long muddy section at mile 77 (Hungry Bear 100) / mile 41 (Snacking Bear 60) between Lost Land Lake Rd and Rock Lake Rd. (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start strategy [COMPETITIVE]:** "We missed the start of the race. For the second year in a row... That said, once we hit the road we were riding pretty hard to try to catch back up to the leaders. We reeled in most of the field over the first 20 or so miles of the race." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Pacing advice for final sections [COMPETITIVE perspective]:** "Having raced the previous year, I knew it was coming, and warned the others to make sure they were saving something in the tank for it. I forgot to make sure I was saving up for it..." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Elite finishing times:** Tim Savre [ELITE] finished in 4:40:58 for the overall win. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JnJYFEWNAHY)

**Time cutoff guidance [UNKNOWN level organizer advice]:** "If you don't think you can finish either of the longer courses by 6pm and want an official time, I suggest you get going before the official 9 am start or transfer to a shorter route." Only "a handful of people who took longer than 9 hours to finish the 100" in previous years. (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

**Water/nutrition strategy [race director guidance]:** "There are no official aid stations on any of the courses. Embark Pure Energy will have an informal aid station somewhere, but don't count on them for water. Carry all you own water of bring a purification filter to get water from any of the many streams, rivers and lakes." Official aid stations listed at mile 26 and mile 67 on the 100-mile route; one station around mile 30 on the 60-mile route. (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/ and https://hungrybeargravel.com/)

**Pre-race food spot [COMPETITIVE]:** Group ate at Norske Nook for "hot breakfast... potato pancakes, some bacon, some eggs, and a good deal of lingonberry jam" — "They're known for their pie." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Flats on Camp Road 38:** Scott [UNKNOWN] got 3 flats in quick succession on Camp Road 38 at 95% completion, "completely derailed by flats." The road makeup is "exclusively sand...and big rocks." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Weather-related:** Cold start (34°F when forecast was 50+) led to underdressing issues for some riders. (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**No support structure:** "This is a self-supported adventure ride with timing. There are no prizes, no aid stations, no sag, no support. You are in charge of you. We will not come help you if you call with a problem and there is little cellular coverage on much of the course. Ride with a buddy!" (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

**Limited cell service:** "Cellular service is spotty at best and many riders will find themselves with no service." Emergency protocol involves Cable Volunteer Fire Department sweeping courses but "They cannot sag you! You are on your own to get back to Cable." (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**DIY/low-key vibe:** "I really liked the DIY vibe and associated low participant cost common to that style of race. When you skip all the age group prizes, podiums, etc., you can keep registration fees low. I also liked the 'family reunion' feel to the events (where everyone knew Tim and Odia as well as many of the other racers.)" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/hungry-bear-gravel-race-2022)

**Inclusivity focus:** "It's all about the inclusivity of going out into the hinterland of your state and putting yourself through the ringer for hours on end, where the only goal is to overcome your own body, mind, and the limits you're trying to push through personally." (https://www.oneononebike.com/blogs/journal/race-report-the-hungry-bear-100/)

**Post-race scene:** "We will also have Ursa Minor Brewing beers at The Rivers Eatery. Ursa Minor is a cool small craft brewer in Duluth with tasty beers... Jennifer Reisch will be performing at the finish by Rivers as well." Tilly's Pies serves as the finish line where riders can pick up pie orders. (https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

**Community impact:** "A portion of the proceeds supports the Cable Volunteer Fire Department, The Steve Tilford Foundation, Hospice, LCO Schools Scholarship, Unruly Women, and other local non-profits and worthy causes." (https://blog.newmoonski.com/2024/05/15/2024-hungry-bear-a-photo-recap/)

**Scale:** 2024 event had "over 800 riders" across all three distances. Race director capped 2022 at 500 riders (increased from original 420 cap). (https://blog.newmoonski.com/2024/05/15/2024-hungry-bear-a-photo-recap/ and https://lifeaboveeight.com/hungry-bear-100/hungry-bear-must-know/)

**Family-friendly:** 11-year-old Molly Ehlers and her dad Aaron completed the 60-mile route, showcasing "determination and the spirit of family adventure." (https://hungrybeargravel.com/)

**Small town charm:** Rivers Eatery walls "are covered with ski and bike jerseys from famous pros, bikes with historic significance hang from the rafters, and Dirt Rag (RIP) and Silent Sports magazines were always on the tables." The venue became "the new unofficial finish line for area bike and ski events." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/hungry-bear-gravel-race-2022)
