## OFFICIAL DATA

**Race Dates**

The Bell's Iceman Cometh Challenge is held on the **first Saturday in November**[2]. For 2026, this falls on **Saturday, November 7, 2026**[1][2][6]. The 2025 race was held on **Saturday, November 8, 2025**[3][4][5].

**Registration & Entry Fees**

Registration for 2026 will open in **early March**[2]. One source lists a tandem race fee of **$140.00**[8], though this appears to be category-specific and may not reflect all entry options. Complete fee structure for 2026 is not yet available in these search results.

**Field Size**

Over **5,400 men and women as well as 300 children in 77 age divisions** participate in the Bell's Iceman Cometh events[1][2]. An additional source states that **approximately 5,000 riders** take to the trail, including Pros and Juniors[6].

**Distance Options**

- **Main Event (Bell's Iceman Cometh Challenge)**: approximately **30-mile point-to-point** cross-country mountain bike race[1][2][3][6]
- **Meijer Slush Cup**: **9-mile course** for beginning riders[1]
- **Meijer Sno-Cone**: race for children **10 & under**[1]

## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**

The course consists primarily of **dirt roads, two-tracks, abandoned railroad beds and the world famous Vasa Nordic ski trail**[6]. It **crosses only one paved road (Williamsburg Rd at mile 17)** as it winds through the **Pere Marquette State Forest in Northern Lower Michigan**[6].

The search results do not provide a percentage breakdown (e.g., 70% gravel, 20% singletrack, etc.).

**Key Course Features**

The course winds through **breathtaking terrain**[6] and is described as **grueling**[3]. The race is noted as "the largest point-to-point mountain bike race in the country"[3]. Specific named climbs or sectors are not identified in these search results.
