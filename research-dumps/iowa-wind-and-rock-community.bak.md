# IOWA WIND AND ROCK — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE]:** "Iowa Wind and Rock is a 340ish mile, single stage, cue sheet navigated, self supported, gravel/dirt road bicycle race... Every year is a new course and is a closely guarded secret, no one other than the race directors know where we are going." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE]:** "Since November 2020 I've logged over 243 hours on the bike and over 4,200 miles(many of these are 'virtual miles' take that how you want) to prepare myself for this race, I have never put this much focus and effort into a race effort before." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE]:** "At mile 44 was, hands down, THE WORST Non-B-Road I have ever seen. I don't know what this county was doing on this road, but it was nothing but wet, sloppy dirt with road rock mixed in just enough that your tires would pick the rocks up and run them through your frame or drivetrain. This road cost numerous people their race with broken derailleurs and chains." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE]:** "Beth was informed this year to not be so willing to come and pick me up. She was instructed that either my bike was to be broken, or I was questioning if I should be calling her or 911 before she would agree to come and pick me up. I wasn't quitting until either I finished or was told to quit." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL]:** "My best description is that it's like a never-ending land of washboard gravel in all direction, and you're about the size of a dust mite… on a teeny-tiny bicycle… ...the cycling part was almost exclusively: total-commit, fly down the best line you can pick on sketch gravel while hitting 30-40 mph, cross a small bridge, dump all that momentum in about 1.7 seconds, and then grind in your lowest gear for the next few minutes over the next crest, only to do it all over again and again and again… for almost 350 miles and over 25,000 vertical feet." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL]:** "Preparing for this race is overwhelming: How do you train for this? Which bike and what set up? What all should you bring? How much food, tools, clothing, safety equipment? How exactly are you supposed to navigate by paper cue sheets in the middle of the night?" (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL]:** "The Friday night registration was underwhelming, to the point I was wondering, 'am I missing something?' It was a simple table at a winery, they checked my name off a paper list, handed me a totally generic number plate, and offered some various colored pipe cleaners to attach it to my bike." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL]:** "It was SO GOOD to have a friend to do this race with as the, 'What the Hell am I doing here?' feelings could've easily totally overwhelmed me." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN]:** "You would think that 340 miles of gravel with 28,000 feet of climb over 31 hours and 16 minutes could not be summed up by one word, but for me it says everything. #Grateful" (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN]:** "Let's be clear, Greg frequently sat on the outside and took the wind… for hours upon hours. I kept having an inner monologue questioning why he kept on with me as he was stronger and I felt inadequate... That draft shield was priceless. I would not have been moving like I was without him" (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN]:** "About 30 miles in, it became abundantly clear to me that my 100 ounces of water and two CarboPro bottles of nutrition were not going to be adequate hydration for nearly 90 miles of hot, brutal terrain. And so began the quest for water." (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Lighting Issue:** "5 minutes before the start I had a problem with my generator hub and light... I had my rechargeable battery powered headlight with me but it's battery life is woefully short of making it through the overnight segment(It might last 6ish hours IIRC)... at Despair... I purchased a $4 flashlight to try and somehow get myself through the overnight." His generator hub mysteriously started working again later. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Wheel Failure:** "Shawn alerted me to a wobble/bend in my rear wheel... A mile or two after this checkpoint... I FINALLY realized my rear rim was totally rubbing, probably for the last 50-75 miles... a broken spoke (I did NOT think to bring extra spokes!)... I had to completely disengage [rear brakes]. And, yes, I WAS one of only two people in this entire race still racing rim brakes!" Completed the final 170+ miles with no rear brake and a bent rim. (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN] - Cold Weather Layering:** "For legs, I chose a very light tight that I'd have to just suck up and deal with during the hot hours of the day. I elected to wear knee warmers on the outside to block the wind then shed them later. This would be my first quasi-mistake. I didn't pull them up high enough and so they kept rolling down... Feet were perfect with a thin wool sock, foot warmers, gravel shoe with toe covers, toe warmers on top of that... and then a full shoe cover over that. All easy things to throw away and not need again going into a second warmer night." (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

**Drop Bags:** "One aspect that stuck from 2020 was the introduction of 'Drop Bags'. At each Checkpoint on the course(of which there was 2) you would have access to what ever you deemed necessary and would fit in a 2 Gallon ziplock bag... One other racer had a serving of lasagna! Another had nothing but a bottle of hydration mix and a rice crispy bar! Mine tended to look like a mini C-Store. A bottle of Coke, bag of chips, beef jerky, Tylenol, etc." [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - First Section:** "80.5 miles and almost 7k feet of elevation gain. Plus 1 B-Road that made me clean and walk and then there was Lewis Ave... mostly North, into the wind. The hills on this section were relentless." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Second Section:** "111 miles with ~8300 ft of elevation gain, no mud and better winds. The B-roads were dry and fast and great to actually ride them, instead of walk in the ditch. Numerous old Iron bridges! The majority of this leg headed West with a significant south section that I got to before the afternoon winds started to die off." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Shelby County Rock:** "A couple of roads on this section in Shelby county had fresh rock laid down for a couple mile long sections. The rock they use is horrendous to ride. They spread it the full width of the road, leaving no good line to ride. The rock itself is a mixture of different sizes and is just a major PITA to ride on. I opted to walk a few of the steeper hills with this stuff." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - B-Roads:** "Hit some of our first B-roads" and later "A mile or two after this checkpoint (in a park), Shawn and I stopped at the second gas station on the route" - indicating only 2 gas stations on entire 347-mile route. (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Bull Run Road:** "Next memory was a right turn onto Bull Run Road... Some because I grew up near the town of Bull Run, IL. The second is because in a race filled with hundreds of ridiculous climbs, this road stood out. It had several of the steepest grades we encountered, and the gravel was super chunky and loose. I felt super-proud simply to not put a foot down on that road!... Strava segment name: 'Misery in Missouri.'" (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - B-Road Bridge:** "I hit a B-road with a VERY sketch bridge with a kind gentleman there monitoring it for us and yelling directions. Something along the lines of, 'Watch the entrance, and stay on the planks in the center…'" (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Packs of Dogs:** "Packs of dogs! The scattered residences typically had dogs and lots of them, sometimes barking and howling from cages or some fenced area in the back, but usually roaming free so in the middle of the night passing a farmhouse, you'd find yourself surrounded by 5-10 of various shapes and sizes. Thankfully, none were especially aggressive." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Gravel Percentage:** "This was a GRAVEL race. They worked hard to keep us on gravel roads, I'm guessing we were off tarmac 96-97% of the race." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Remote Nature:** "It's remote! We never passed a single gas station (or any other business) the entire route other than our pre-planned 3 stops." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN] - Water Scarcity:** "We lucked out to find a farmer that was willing to allow us to fill up at his pump about 40 miles past CP1. That was a savior because I still rolled into CP2 with almost no hydration left and having already drank down 50 ounces at the refill." (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Checkpoint Cushions:** "I got to Checkpoint #1 @ 11:19am, cutoff was 12:03pm. After the first 80 miles I only managed to build a 44 minute cushion... I made it to Checkpoint #2 at 8:44pm, cutoff was 11:08pm. I now had a 2:24 cushion." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Solo Riding:** "Either my fitness was way lower than most everyone else's or they had a 'hotter' start planned than me. Either way, I found myself pretty much riding alone within an hour... No big deal, everyone needs to ride their own pace on something like this, trying to stick with someone that is faster than you will put you in a hole you won't be able to get out of and sticking with someone slower can cost you precious time you don't need to give away." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Rushing Checkpoints:** "Knowing I had only built such a small cushion, I rushed through my Checkpoint routine and got back on the road." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Checkpoint Times:** "Checkpoint #1: Caseys @ mile 95.1, 10:21am, 12th place... Checkpoint #2: mile 170.9, 4:01pm, 12th place... 347.98m, 25,000ft, 27:46 moving time, 32:10 total time, 10th in Open Men, 12/56 OA" (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Post-Mechanical Strategy:** "Initially, I just chugged along at a pretty safe/conservative pace, which was fine... within about 30 minutes, I went from aspirations to start moving up in the race, to questioning if I should even continue. At that point, I was doubtful my rear wheel would survive the next 170+ miles, and I was feeling nervous about navigating the steep sketchy descents with just my front brakes. My next 10 hours or so were 100% alone" (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Group Riding Early:** "I was positioned mid-pack... We were moving at a nice pace. Not too fast, not too slow. Greg's pacing was working great and I felt pretty good... after a while it got pretty dark and eventually it became clear those first 10 miles that it was going to be difficult to stay together up and down the hills, and there would be very few opportunities to draft or help one-another. This will be a solo-effort race." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN] - Partnership Strategy:** "Joe, Greg, and I pedaled away to CP1... We rolled into CP1 and got our new cues... and were back on the road in about six minutes. Not too shabby for this dallier. After CP1... it was with sadness that we said goodbye to Joe around mile 100. Greg and I pedaled on in the wind... We chatted, pedaled in amicable silence" (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

**Heather Poskevich [UNKNOWN] - Checkpoint Duration:** "We refueled and rested for a total of 14 minutes [at CP2]. We picked up the next set of cues, accepted more well wishes from volunteers and rolled out." (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Mechanical Failures:** "At mile 44... This road cost numerous people their race with broken derailleurs and chains." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Navigation Errors:** "One aspect of this race that I had ZERO experience with was navigating with cue sheets... the consequences of an error can be massive, adding hours to your time possibly... It took all of 1 mile before the entire group made a cue sheet error and blew past a turn, literally the second turn!" (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Cory Rood [COMPETITIVE] - Mud on B-Roads:** "We hit the first B-Road and the nerves of if they would be rideable or not were rising. At the start it was tacky, but not sticking, good going, but cautiously watching for build up on the tires. With about 100 yard left, it started sticking, uh oh! At Steve suggestion of going faster would help, I gave that a try. FYI, it didn't work and I had to stop and clean the bike off and walk the ditch to the end." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**2024 Results:** "37 on the last roster, 24 started, 13 DNS, 19 DNF, 5 Finishers!" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/15945/iowa-wind-and-rock)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Self-Doubt After Mechanical:** "within about 30 minutes, I went from aspirations to start moving up in the race, to questioning if I should even continue. At that point, I was doubtful my rear wheel would survive the next 170+ miles, and I was feeling nervous about navigating the steep sketchy descents with just my front brakes." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Navigation Complexity:** "There is no published route and no signage. You get printed paper cue sheets at the beginning of the race and at Checkpoints #1 & #2. This adds so much unknown, significant mental strain and practical complexities (physical placement of cues, reading in the dark, re-routing when you take a wrong turn, doing math factoring in wrong turn mileage, etc.)" (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Origins:** "For 14 years, TransIowa, one of the most difficult gravel races in the US, took place in Iowa at the end of April... The end of TransIowa in 2018 meant that a unique chapter of gravel racing history closed. As Iowans, TransIowa finishers, and people who enjoy stretching personal boundaries, we didn't want to see this unique opportunity for people to challenge themselves disappear." (https://iowawindandrock.com/)

**Race Directors:** "Hosted by Sarah Cooper, Steve Fuller, and Dori Jansma... Sarah told me to just keep moving and see what happens... Met the race directors and volunteers at an old Iron bridge on a B-road and everyone was commenting how good I was looking at that point" [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Volunteer Support:** "All the volunteers at the checkpoint were positive and reassuring me that things were not as dire as I felt they were." [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/a-success-story-iowa-wind-and-rock/57591)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Event Vibe:** "The IWAR vibe and people are cool. Everything is understated, calm, and super simple. They make it clear that you're on-your-own with just about everything. Yet, there is deep respect for anyone that shows up to even toe the line. My sense is it's like the Barkley Marathon of gravel races (not that I've ever done the Barkley Marathon)." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**Wilco [RECREATIONAL] - Start Atmosphere:** "At the start in a grocery store parking lot, I found Jason & Shawn, got my cue sheets and into the line-up with about 45 seconds to spare for the quiet (remember 4am through town), super-chill roll out for the 56 starters." (https://wilcounchained.substack.com/p/iowa-wind-and-rock-iwar-2024)

**CrossTrail Commentary:** "Nonetheless, they decided to create this event, in the spirit of Trans Iowa and with Mark Stevenson's blessing, to offer the cycling community a similar boundary-expanding experience. And they offered it for free... This is the Gravel Family. In action. Doing what needs to be done. And doing it right." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2018/09/iowa-wind-and-rock-gravel-family-steps.html)

**Free Entry:** "Free to enter. Donations accepted" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/15945/iowa-wind-and-rock)

I knew full well that these directors would draw an absolutely tough-as-nails crowd. Their reputations precede them, and their friends are some of the toughest cyclists in the country. Making the lottery left me grateful… and scared." (https://www.hpultracycling.com/iowa-wind-and-rock-2019-heather-poskevichs-race-report/)
