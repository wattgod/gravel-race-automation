# IRON CROSS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "Dark. Cold. The alarm went off at 4:45a. Ugh. Ok. Let's do this... Not a Cyclo-cross race, not a mountain bike race, but somewhere in between with lots of hills, paved roads, gravel fire roads, singletrack, and a bit of rock climbing to kick you in the teeth. Add to this mix of terrain, a cold, steady autumn rain that continued all day, and it makes for an interesting mix of racing." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "It wasn't until I left here [first rest stop around mile 22] I finally started to get into a groove and felt warmed up enough. The hardest parts were yet to come." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "Up. Up. Steeper. Up still. Steeper. Wheels slipping on the wet leaves and rocks. Time to get off and start pushing. This is not something I like to do, I'd rather ride. But that's part of the challenge of this course. Now I was at the 'run-up'. A wall of thick moss-covered loose rocks going straight up to the heavens." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's got a mix of gravel, dirt, paved roads, and even some singletrack. The variety of roads/paths isn't what makes it so hard, it's the terrain, elevation gain, and weather all together that make it a grueling, yet rewarding day on the bike... in 2021, it was 56 miles with over 7,000 feet of climbing." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "The most memorable hard part for me is the switch-back section over an old granite road at around mile 35 that has pitches of up to 9%. Oh wait, I forgot about the hand-over-foot run-up! And, even when you feel like you've just about had enough, the course ends on a kick-in-the-chamois single-track section that goes STRAIGHT UP." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Cole House [ELITE] (2013 winner):** "I had no idea what to expect. I have never done this race. I came out early to ride a couple sections, and I actually drove the road sections. That paid off big time. There was so much climbing - if you don't know how long it is mentally, you can kind of give up. If you know where you are, it's a big advantage." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Cole House [ELITE]:** "Some said it was pretty rocky. On the road sections, 'cross bikes had a bit of an advantage, but you could draft and not do too much work. Racing my mountain bike was how I got my gap for the win - on the trails. I knew that was where I had to push it." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Cole House [ELITE]:** "When they had said 'run-up', I was thinking we were going to run up a little rocky hill. Instead, we ran up a cliff. That was shocker." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Brian Toone [ELITE] (2013 2nd place):** "I ended up on the big climb thinking I should go as hard as possible because I'm not as good on the singletrack, so I went as hard as possible and left the guys I was with. From there until the end, I was by myself, and I ended up catching three of the lead guys to take second." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Ruth Sherman [ELITE] (2013 women's winner):** "I was by myself a lot which always makes me nervous because I'm afraid I might go off course. I did the race four or five years ago, but it was a different course." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Evan McGregor [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's the hardest, most beautifully painful race I've ever done... I finished around 90 seconds down [from 10th]. It's fitting that the rider who has helped me up my skill level significantly was the only one to overtake me today. I was thrilled to see a 12th place finish on the day and an age group podium!" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-iron-cross-gravel)

**Evan McGregor [COMPETITIVE]:** "I paced the climbs pretty well, and on the single track sectors, I kept hearing Nathan Goates in the back of my head from our ride at BaseCamp gravel camp last year coaching me through it all. My efficiency was way higher this year, and I went much faster through the single track." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-iron-cross-gravel)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike choice - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "In our opinion the best bike is a cross bike. We say cross not just because we because the event was designed around a cross bike and every inch of it - except parts of the Wigwam 'Run-Up' are rideable on a cross bike... That said, 29ers didn't exist when we launched this thing and properly set up they make a strong statement as the 'best choice.' A slightly narrower tire on a 29er with a rigid or lock out fork will roll really well at IC." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**Bike choice - Cole House [ELITE]:** "Some said it was pretty rocky... Racing my mountain bike was how I got my gap for the win - on the trails... I just popped over those logs. That's where a mountain bike really paid off, and I got a gap of a minute there over the other two." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Tires - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "I'd run small knobs that roll fast on a tire with a big-ish casing. You don't need a 40mm but you probably do want something bigger than 33. We've seen people be successful on file tread and with less singletrack that style tire could be a better choice than ever... Run higher than 'short race' pressure with most at 45+ psi depending on weight." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**Tubeless - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "Tubeless is ideal for IC. When we first set this thing up we were pinching tires and slicing open tubulars all over the place. NoTubes takes care of an awful lot of those issues." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**What to carry - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "Multi-tool. Chain link. 2 tubes. Mini-pump. Knowledge how to use all of it. At least. Bottle opener in case you run into any trail fairies may be appropriate." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**Hydration pack vs bottles - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "With the new course this year and a reduction in singletrack overall you bottles are a better choice than ever. Hydration packs are always popular though and with the mid-race self-support pit at CP2 you may find it easy to drop a pack from the first half the race on your pass through CP2." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**Clothing - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "'How much' and 'what to wear' was the big question of the day. Full rain pants? What kind of gloves? Glasses or no? If so, which lenses? Trivial decisions when it's dry and sunny, critical in the rain and cold when you are gonna be riding 100k on all kinds of roads." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

## Weather Experienced

**2017 Race - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "Cold. The alarm went off at 4:45a... As I neared Williamsport, the yellow, orange and brown mountains greeted me with white caps of fog... a cold, steady autumn rain that continued all day... Just then the sky opened up and the rain came and wind came in sideways, pelting my face and pushing me side to side." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**2013 Race - Race Report [ELITE]:** "Racers competed in perfect trail and weather conditions, with sunny skies and comfortable temperatures all day." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course overview - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "Calling Iron Cross a gravel grinder is actually a little unfair; they call themselves the 'original North American ultra cross,' which is much more accurate. It's got a mix of gravel, dirt, paved roads, and even some singletrack." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**PA-44 descent - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "Bombing down PA-44 at 40 miles an hour in the wet with cars was 'fun'... I was back at the top of the paved PA-44 road, ready to bomb down once more, this time banging a left at the bottom and and climbing one, more, hill." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**Mid-race terrain - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "More climbing on the ATV trails and then it flattened out, into thick wet mud, with standing water ponds on the double track. No place to go but through. After several of these water crossings, the course turns right at a 90-degree angle, right into single track." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**The Run-Up - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "Now I was at the 'run-up'. A wall of thick moss-covered loose rocks going straight up to the heavens. But I knew the effort to shoulder the OPEN and make it up the hike-a-bike would be worth it." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**Granite road section - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "The most memorable hard part for me is the switch-back section over an old granite road at around mile 35 that has pitches of up to 9%." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Final climb - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "Even when you feel like you've just about had enough, the course ends on a kick-in-the-chamois single-track section that goes STRAIGHT UP." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**100K course description - Official site [UNKNOWN]:** "The first climb through the Williamsport Water Authority provides a great opportunity for early groups to form. Fourth Gap road is where bike handling skills come into play. Walters climb is often where the big separations happen... The final climb is certainly a kicker, but there is no better mountain top finish in PA!" (https://ironcrossgravel.com/routes/)

**Singletrack sections - Cole House [ELITE]:** "I just popped over those logs. That's where a mountain bike really paid off, and I got a gap of a minute there over the other two. From there, I went all out." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early race positioning - Cole House [ELITE]:** "House got a good start, but after backing it off the pace of the initial leaders, he ended up making a wrong turn during the first singletrack section. As he realized his mistake and made the correction, two groups of top riders passed, and he had some work to do to catch back up on the next road section." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Run-up as key selection point - Race Report [ELITE]:** "A group of four... emerged as the leaders after the grueling hike-a-bike and the subsequent aid station #1. They worked well together for a bit to keep their advantage, but the repeated, hard climbing was still to come after the half-way point following aid station #2." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Warm-up time - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "It wasn't until I left here [first rest stop around mile 22] I finally started to get into a groove and felt warmed up enough." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**Climb pacing for non-specialists - Brian Toone [ELITE]:** "I ended up on the big climb thinking I should go as hard as possible because I'm not as good on the singletrack, so I went as hard as possible and left the guys I was with." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Skills development - Evan McGregor [COMPETITIVE]:** "On the single track sectors, I kept hearing Nathan Goates in the back of my head from our ride at BaseCamp gravel camp last year coaching me through it all. **Checkpoint strategy - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "CPs 1 & 3 will be stocked primarily with fluids for you - water and Hammer Nutrition's HEED. There will be some light emergency fuel like Hammer Gels... CP2/Self Support - We will have an area designated for you to place whatever you like (small cooler/bag or smaller) for your pit area... you should be prepared to pit for yourself at CP2... This allows you to use what you need for the race!" (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**General fueling advice - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "Drink often. Eat before you need it. Don't get in a [deficit]." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Flats - Cheryl Sornson [ELITE]:** "One of those sharp shale pieces caught my tire and cut it almost in half. Fortunately I had a number plate from my last race that was Tyvek, so I was able to boot it. It was still sticking out, but I was able to limp back about 10 miles into aid station #2." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Going off course - Selene Yeager [ELITE]:** "Right before we were going to come toward aid station #2, we asked a moto which way, and he said straight so we went straight. We went four miles off course. I didn't see the arrows - I will own it, I'm bad at that. I had good legs today from the time I woke up this morning, so I was disappointed." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Crashes on descents - Stephan Kincaid [ELITE]:** "Thirty miles in, I realized there were just five of us. Figuring out who was where, I turned around to look and a hit a pothole and flew off my bike just before we got to aid station #2. I cracked my helmet, briefly blacked out and initially felt a little dizzy after the incident before he collected himself enough to decide to keep racing." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Mechanical - Carla Williams [ELITE]:** "I was pretty much by myself until right at the end. I was riding in third until near the end when I passed Swan and came up on Ruth near the finish, at the end of that last climb. Williams rode most of the race in her small front chain ring, after breaking her front derailleur on the prologue. 'I could get up all the hills ok; it's just that some of the straight aways were a little tough.'" (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/)

**Mental fatigue - Evan McGregor [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's been a tough year, though. Working back from injury after 2 months off the bike was taxing, and after finishing up the PA Gravel series, I was again mentally done, but I expected this and was better prepared. I built some strong habits through the year that really carried me over the past month and brought me to race day as ready as I could be." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-iron-cross-gravel)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Larry's Tavern - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "I could hear the sound of Angus Young's guitar and smell of campfire. Then a yell of 'YEAH!! WOO!! ALRIGHT!!' With a raucous roar of enthusiasm, an unofficial stop at Larry's Tavern welcomed and cheered riders by passing out beers, pickle/vodka shots and chunks of grilled venison! With a slap on the back, they would let us know you know, you had another 4 miles down the 'flat' gravel road to the next aid station." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**Larry's Tavern - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "Any story about Iron Cross would not be complete with a mention of Larry's Tavern, an 'unofficial' pit stop just after the run-up. Larry and his crew, also named Larry (I asked who was named Larry and they said they are all named Larry), bring BBQ and other questionable bike ride snacks, including Fireball, to brighten the mood just as things are starting to feel grim. Larry and all the other Larrys hand out official Larry's Tavern stickers for your bike and are by far the most enthusiastic cheer squad I've ever encountered." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Overall vibe - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "Iron Cross has been around for at least 18 years, which is well before gravel became Gravel. Its longstanding status as an awesome event attracts every kind of cyclist: roadies, washed up ex-roadies, hipsters, frankenbike types, hardcore gravelers, mountain bikers, and everything in between. Because everyone fits in, it's a lot more welcoming, unintimidating, and laid back than other gravel events I've done." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Event organization - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "From the number pick-up at New Trail Brewing Company the night before the event to the awards ceremony at the Stonehouse, the organizers have thought of everything. Some amazing details: 1) Included with your registration is a free pint glass, a spiffy pair of socks, and a token for a free beer and a free dinner item at the Stonehouse which you'll definitely want to cash in on after the event. 2) The organizers give you a gallon-sized ziploc bag that they carry to the rest stations in case you want to pack dry socks or snacks for yourself and not be bothered with schlepping. 3) The rest stops have great snacks and awesome volunteers. Think: Swedish Fish, coke, pb&j, and volunteers coaxing you into doing a wheelie. 4) You get a gift! In 2021, the organizers gifted us Iron Cross branded dry-bags." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Post-race - McCluskey [COMPETITIVE]:** "After a long time getting out of my wet clothes, I packed up and headed over to The Brickyard Restaurant & Ale House for food and beer and the awards and finisher's gift, a soccer scarf. Great for those cold days coming soon here in the Mid-Atlantic." (https://www.worldwidecyclery.com/blogs/worldwide-cyclery-blog/my-experience-at-the-iron-cross-race)

**Post-race meal - Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "Our bud and 7x ICer Warren Holtzman is cooking up a few hundred pounds of pork for you we'll have bubbling in a cauldron at the finish line when you arrive this year. Macaroni salad, chips, coleslaw and a couple cookies will fill out the meal. Iced tea and lemonade will be available to drink... A post race meal is included in the entry for every racer and you must bring your ticket from reg with you in order to get your grub on." (https://ironcrossrace.blogspot.com/p/ic-faqs.html)

**Scenery - Liz M [COMPETITIVE]:** "I know that late October in Central PA might not sound glamorous, but it's stunningly beautiful. Being in the deep woods with the last breath of fall color on the trees is absolutely magical. Every section of the course feels special for one reason or another - whether it's the mystical fairy land of the single track, the views of the early gravel section, or the glowing leaves of the late gravel section when you're about to bonk, it's really a sight to see." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/why-iron-cross-is-the-best-event-ever)

**Overall experience - Evan McGregor [COMPETITIVE]:** "Gunnar and his team put on an absolutely fantastic event with Iron Cross, and if anyone is looking for something exciting, different, and great bang for your buck, come out and do Iron Cross with me next year! It's beautiful, beautiful pain." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/evans-story-iron-cross-gravel)
