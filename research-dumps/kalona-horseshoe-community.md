## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The race is described as having "rolling hills gravel" through Kalona and Iowa City area. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_7VS-l6yPqA)

YouTube video title emphasizes "Iowa is not FLAT!!" suggesting the terrain defies expectations of flat Midwestern racing. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_7VS-l6yPqA)

The course is characterized as "windy and rolly" with "captivating and quiet" scenery. (https://icorrmtb.org/event/kalona-horseshoe/)

Course terrain is officially listed as "moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/11788/kalona-horseshoe)

Roads are described as "beautiful and rustic without the complexity of asphalt" - maintained by the Amish and Mennonite population in Washington County, same area used by Jingle GX race. (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/15673/kalona-horseshoe)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The race tagline asks: "Ever wondered what it's like to race your bike past a horse-drawn buggy?" This is a real feature of racing in Kalona's Amish/Mennonite community. (https://icorrmtb.org/event/kalona-horseshoe/)

Post-race features local bakeries, with organizers saying "Come for the race, stay for the amazing bakeries!" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/15673/kalona-horseshoe)

Post-race pie is described as "mandatory" with the advice: "Seriously, don't skip the pie." Food includes "food, drinks, and as much pie as you can handle." (https://icorrmtb.org/event/kalona-horseshoe/)

Kalona is known as the "quilt capital of Iowa" and the race proximity to Iowa City "raises the general desire for a simple and more community focused environment." (https://www.racecenter.com/cycling/kalona-horseshoe)

The race is part of the Iowa Gravel Series and was new to the series in 2024 as the season opener, providing "a bit of a preview of the terrain in Southeastern Iowa" before the championship race in Fairfield later in the season. (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/15673/kalona-horseshoe)

Race starts at Kalona Historical Village with check-in opening at 6 a.m., 100K start at 7:30 a.m., 50K at 8 a.m. (https://thenews-ia.com/stories/kalona-horseshoe-gravel-bike-race,178440)

Registration pricing (2024 data): $40 Grassroots (includes chip timing, marked course, pro photography, aid stations); $60 Race Pace (adds Iowa Gravel Series t-shirt and finish line raffle entry); $400 Full Season (all 6 Iowa Gravel Series races plus Superbloom Series jersey). (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/15673/kalona-horseshoe)
