## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**

The Bull Course features **85% gravel**, the Cow Course **76% gravel**, and the Calf Course **64% gravel**.[4]

**Course Characteristics**

The Bull Course (90-100 miles) is described as featuring **"long climbs, exposed gravel flats, and technical descents."**[2]

The Cow Course (54-68 miles) is characterized as delivering **"a perfect mix of grit and flow — ideal for intermediate riders who want a full-day adventure."**[2]

The Calf/Steer Course (28 miles) offers **"a fun, scenic route with rolling elevation,"** while the Calf - Heifer Course (16 miles) is designed as **"perfect for first-timers, casual riders, or those just looking to cruise Colorado's big sky gravel."**[2]

**Technical Difficulty**

KowTown is described as a **"high-altitude, high-energy ride through the remote ranchlands and rolling mountains of Kremmling, Colorado."**[2] The 2026 marketing emphasizes **"new routes with more gravel"** and **"an untapped and untamed gravel adventure."**[4][5]

**Elevation Profile Description**

The event showcases rolling mountains and high-altitude terrain. The Bull Course climbs 10,000 feet total elevation gain over 100 miles, the Cow 5,500 feet over 68 miles, and the Calf 2,000 feet over 28 miles.[4]
