## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course characteristics:** The event features "l'ambiance atypique du gravel « Diois » : un mélange de petites routes goudronnées et de pistes caillouteuses" (a mix of small paved roads and stony tracks) (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/project_category/gravel/)

**Technical challenges from race photos [UNKNOWN riders]:** "Premier kilomètre, certains coureurs se retrouvent déjà à genoux en train de retirer des cailloux de leurs chaussures, se demandant s'ils ont vraiment signé pour ça. À mi-chemin, la compétition devient sérieuse, mais l'ambiance reste bon enfant, avec des coureurs échangeant des regards complices après avoir survécu à une descente périlleuse." (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/photos-la-dromoise-gravel/)

**Terrain variety:** Courses pass through vineyards (vignoble châtillonais), with routes now featuring "les versants opposés au Glandasse" for the 2025+ editions (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/project_category/gravel/)

**2023 course conditions:** References to mud and dust: "s'ils sont prêts à affronter la boue et les cailloux ou s'ils vont finir par ressembler à des bonhommes de neige couverts de poussière" (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/photos-la-dromoise-gravel/)

## Weather Experienced

**Seasonal conditions [UNKNOWN riders]:** "Vent frais pour la saison" (fresh/cool wind for the season) was noted by participants, though this didn't prevent them from enjoying the scenery (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

**2023 conditions [UNKNOWN]:** Sun was present ("Le soleil était au rendez vous") with wind and cold mentioned as factors, though some riders accustomed to plateau conditions found it manageable (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Organization quality [UNKNOWN riders]:** Multiple riders praised the organization as "au top" and "exemplaire" with particular appreciation for volunteers who served with smiles (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

**Cultural integration:** The event runs parallel to "L'Echappée des Rues" cultural/theatrical festival, creating a combined sports and arts experience (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

**Feed station quality:** Volunteers at feed stations were highly praised, though one organizer noted: "Une petit remarque sur les points de ravitaillement, il vous manque quelques bénévoles pour pouvoir distribuer les produits au rythme d'arrivée des participants" (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

**Scenic experience [UNKNOWN]:** "Les vignobles offrent un répit bienvenu, avec leurs rangées ordonnées offrant une illusion de calme avant le prochain tronçon chaotique" - vineyards provide welcome respite before the next chaotic section (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/photos-la-dromoise-gravel/)

**Event scale:** Over 3,600 participants in recent editions, with organizers asking "combien serez-vous en 2026?" (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

**Ambassador/Patron:** Titouan Carod serves as the event patron (parrain), adding local credibility to the event (https://www.cyclodromoise.com)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Feed station locations [ALL LEVELS]:** For the 75km gravel route compared to similar events, feed stations are typically placed around km 30 and km 60 based on standard gravel event structure (https://gfmontventoux.com/en/parcours-2/80-km-gravel/)

**Navigation [ALL LEVELS]:** GPX tracks are provided to participants before the event. Some directional arrows at strategic points, but navigation primarily relies on following the GPX file (https://gfmontventoux.com/en/parcours-2/80-km-gravel/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical challenges [UNKNOWN]:** Rocks/stones in the course cause issues - riders reported having to stop in the first kilometer to remove stones from shoes, suggesting the rocky terrain is immediately challenging (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/photos-la-dromoise-gravel/)

**Technical descents:** References to "une descente périlleuse" (perilous descent) and "descentes escarpées" (steep descents) suggest technical descending skills are needed (https://www.cyclodromoise.com/photos-la-dromoise-gravel/)
