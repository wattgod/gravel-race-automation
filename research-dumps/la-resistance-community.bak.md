# LA RESISTANCE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**VecchioJo [RECREATIONAL]:** "Huddled in the porch of the refuge at the top of the Col de l'Arpettaz everyone is looking at everyone else in dank tangible anticipation that someone will say what everyone is thinking, everyone. Someone just has to mention the 'Bail' word and we can all throw our kit in the car and high-tail it home." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**VecchioJo [RECREATIONAL]:** "Well, on any other day it would be a gravel track but today it's quite, um, puddly. Deep puddly. Downhill puddly. Okay, it's pretty much a river. We splash through where we have to and sneak up the slippery rooty muddy sides where possible but it doesn't take long for feet, overshoes or not, to be soaking wet and cold, a state they would remain in for the rest of the day." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**VecchioJo [RECREATIONAL]:** "My 40mm tyres have definitely come into their own. I feel confident riding at the kind of speeds that mean I'm quickly catching and passing those skinny-tyred whippets who had overtaken me on the Arpettaz climb." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**Stu Bowers [ELITE]:** "My own chosen steed is the 3T Exploro, a bike that claims to be built for precisely this kind of varied terrain. It offers plenty of clearance for voluminous tyres so I'm rolling on 700c, 40mm WTB Nano gravel tyres, which I hope will provide the perfect middle ground – not too draggy, but durable, grippy and comfortable enough for both man and machine to stay the course." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Stu Bowers [ELITE]:** "As I make my way onto the track, gingerly at first, it's not long before I encounter the first of many who have succumbed to the puncture demons, crouched at the roadside fumbling with inner tubes and pumps." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Stu Bowers [ELITE]:** "Aside from the tricky surface, the track is rarely flat and it can be difficult to control speed on the descents while trying to pick the best line through clusters of rocks. When the gradient points up, the challenge switches to maintaining rear-wheel traction on the loose surface. It demands concentration at all times and by the end of its 14.3km my arms and hands have taken as much punishment as my legs." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Stu Bowers [ELITE]:** "On paper, the 6.8km Col des Glières shouldn't be as tough as the first climb, but only a kilometre later I'm not so certain. The path carved into the cliff face is relentlessly steep – 9% average with large chunks over 10%. At every hairpin I feel certain the gradient must ease, but it never does, and by the time the summit eventually comes into view, I can barely appreciate the towering limestone cliffs that stand like sentries on the skyline. My bottles are supped dry and my legs are close to cramp. The Col des Glières has drained my physical reserves." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Width - Critical Choice [ALL LEVELS]:**
- **VecchioJo [RECREATIONAL]** on 28mm tires: "I'm riding a Specialized Diverge with 28mm tyres and it's right on the limit of acceptability on this gravellage section, and I'm plenty used to riding off road on mountainbikes, cross bikes and willfully pointing a road bike across sketchy terrain. If you want to do La Resistance then a bike with 30mm+ tyres might be more confident." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

- **VecchioJo [RECREATIONAL]** recommends: "a file-treaded CX tyre maybe" for 30mm+ setup (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

- **Stu Bowers [ELITE]** on 40mm: "My 40mm tyres have definitely come into their own. I feel confident riding at the kind of speeds that mean I'm quickly catching and passing those skinny-tyred whippets who had overtaken me on the Arpettaz climb." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

- **Observed at start [MIXED LEVELS]:** "Around me I note some riders have simply fitted wider 25mm or 28mm road tyres to their top-end carbon race machines, while others have chosen a far more aggressive gravel set-up. Knobbly tyres on cyclocross bikes seem to be a popular choice." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Bike Choice Trade-offs [ALL LEVELS]:**
"This is where deciding on what compromise you're willing to make on your bike choice comes into play; do you pick a bike with fatter tyres to make the off-road sections easier, knowing that it's going to make the tarmac climbs tougher, or do you opt for climbing speed over dirt fragility?" (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**Tour de la Resistance recommendation [BIKEPACKING]:** "42-45mm tyres with good grip are ideal and some low gears make the climbs more user friendly." (https://www.komoot.com/collection/2300993/tour-de-la-resistance-a-gravel-bikepacking-loop-above-lake-annecy)

**Tubs on Gravel [ELITE with exceptional bike handling]:**
"Those riding this section on 23mm tyres are definitely having more fun, especially for Nick who is on some borrowed Mavic wheels, with tubs. They've survived the Paris-Roubaix so they should get round this, and that palmares certainly helps with the skill level." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**Mechanical Support:**
"Out on the course are several bright yellow Mavic Service Course motos loaded with spare wheels (just like in the Tour de France), to ensure no one is left stranded. Already the indications are that they're in for a busy day." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

## Weather Experienced

**2016 Reconnaissance Ride (worst case scenario):**
- "Yesterday had seen the worst rain Europe had experienced in quite a while. It's less than ideal." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)
- "Today the cloud is down to tree-top level" (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)
- At Col de l'Arpettaz summit: "With no woodwork to shield the wind the chill factor kicks up and every time we swing left on a hairpin we turn into the frigid headwind, which combined with everything I'm wearing now being saturated with damp makes the last few kilometres a cocoon of hardship. I start to lose feeling in my extremities, this is quite grim." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)
- Snow encountered on Route de la Soif: "We round a corner to face a deep snow-bank blocking the trail. I fumble in my back pockets to see if there are any teddies in there that I can throw petulantly out the pram. There aren't so I unclip with everyone else, shoulder the bike and clamber across the snow bank." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**Weather Warning [ALL LEVELS]:**
"This is a mountain environment with the higher trails beyond the tree line and fully exposed to the elements. Come prepared with appropriate clothing for the frequent and sometimes rapid changes in the weather. Even if the forecast is for clear skies, conditions can change daily! Bring some warm clothes for the evenings too." (https://www.komoot.com/collection/2300993/tour-de-la-resistance-a-gravel-bikepacking-loop-above-lake-annecy)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Route de la Soif (14.3km HC Gravel section):**
- "As gravel rides go it appears that the French translation of gravel is quite a lot more rustic than the finely graded miles of hardpacked aggregate that's characterised by the American Marketing Dream of this kind of riding, and by that I mean the it's more rough hewn, chunky and fibrous. Let's call it artisan for advertising reasons." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

- "The Route de la Soif swoops and bends around the contours of the mountain, which is somewhere up there to the left, we can't see it in the cloud but we can definitely feel its brooding presence and it really is spectacular and silently foreboding. This is real mountain riding and not the place to cock it up. Especially in these conditions. On road bikes." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

- "The track is rarely flat and it can be difficult to control speed on the descents while trying to pick the best line through clusters of rocks. When the gradient points up, the challenge switches to maintaining rear-wheel traction on the loose surface." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Col de l'Arpettaz (14.8km HC, 1,165m gain, 8% avg):**
- "It may not be in the Hit Parade of famous alpine climbs but it's HC classified and at 14.8km it does go on a bit, but with an average of 8% gradient it never gets steep and it's an easy climb. Relatively." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

- "For the first 5km the road sticks at 5-6%, but by the middle it has ramped to 7-10%, maxing out at 12%. There are hairpins galore, and when I emerge out of the tree line in the upper reaches, I'm treated to a spectacular view of the Aravis Alps – jagged peaks set in a sea of green fields, accompanied by the gentle clang of cowbells. It's a true gem of a climb, offering similar stats to Alpe d'Huez but with almost twice as many bends and not a single car to be seen." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Col des Glières (6.8km Cat 2, 660m gain, 10% avg) - Full route only:**
- "The path carved into the cliff face is relentlessly steep – 9% average with large chunks over 10%. At every hairpin I feel certain the gradient must ease, but it never does." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Opening Gravel Sector:**
"We turn off the main road on the outskirts of Doussard and the quiet of the bunch is traded for alarmed cries as riders fight to hold a straight line in the loose, dusty gravel." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Gravel Detour Option:**
"For those looking for more adventure, we've discovered some really fun gravel sections at the beginning of the ride. Due to their technical nature we recommend that only people on gravel or cyclocross bikes take on these extra sections. Those riding modified road bikes are recommended to stay on the main route." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/la-resistance)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Line Philosophy [ALL LEVELS]:**
"The scene before the start of La Resistance is unlike any sportive I've ever encountered. For one thing, it's not pre-dawn. The sun is up and breakfast was enjoyed at an almost sociable hour. There are no pens full of riders jostling for position near the front. In fact, as I look around barely anyone has even bothered to gather on the start line yet. Instead most are content to sit on the scattered hay bails or kick back on the grass, sipping a last-minute coffee and chatting." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Event Philosophy:**
"La Resistance is a ride not a race, as we urge people to take some moments at each La Resistance memorial to honour the memory of those who refused to stand down in the face of tyranny, and paid for this with their lives. Whilst La Resistance is not a race, we have set up some segments on Strava so you can measure yourselves against your fellow riders." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5192/la-resistance)

**Pacing Reality [ELITE]:**
"Experience has taught me that every cycling event is united by one thing: no matter how much you insist it's not a race, it always is. Thankfully the pace remains gentle as we roll out of Talloires, heading south along the lake." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Route Choice Decision:**
"Your choice of route is completely open so you can change your mind on the day to suit the strength of your legs!" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/la-resistance)

**Tour de la Resistance Multi-day Strategy [COMPETITIVE/BIKEPACKING]:**
- Day 1: "Fuel well before starting this Tour as there's a hefty amount of climbing on day one for its distance." (96.3km, 4,000m gain)
- Day 2: "More relaxed, with less climbing (although it is steep at times) and distance to cover" (68.7km, 2,780m gain)
- Day 3: "Not dissimilar in altitude gain to day one, but over the longest distance of the three days. You'll be glad to know though that there's more down than up for this return to Talloires!" (https://www.komoot.com/collection/2300993/tour-de-la-resistance-a-gravel-bikepacking-loop-above-lake-annecy)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Punctures [ALL LEVELS]:**
"As I make my way onto the track, gingerly at first, it's not long before I encounter the first of many who have succumbed to the puncture demons, crouched at the roadside fumbling with inner tubes and pumps. It's reassuring to know that out on the course are several bright yellow Mavic Service Course motos loaded with spare wheels." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Navigation Errors [ALL LEVELS]:**
"In the excitement of the descent I somehow miss a route arrow and find myself off course and needing to backtrack up part of the climb. I'm annoyed at my error but equally concerned that this misspent energy will haunt me later." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Extreme Weather Bailout [RECREATIONAL - 2016 recce]:**
The pre-ride briefing resulted in "curtailing the intended full 130km ride and focus on getting round the shorter 90km 'Petite' route and its meagre 2,200m of climbing rather than the full fat's 3,200m. This means we'll miss out on taking on the Plateau des Glieres." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**Physical Depletion [ELITE on full route]:**
"My bottles are supped dry and my legs are close to cramp. The Col des Glières has drained my physical reserves, and looking around at other riders slumped in chairs at the feed station, or draped over benches stretching out hamstrings, I realise I'm not alone in having found it brutal." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Start Line Vibe:**
"It's all very relaxed. I could just as easily be on holiday, rather than preparing to embark on an epic day in the Alps. Talking with organisers Adam Horler and Ross Muir over a beer last night, it was clear this is exactly the vibe they wanted for the event's inaugural running." (https://www.cyclist.co.uk/in-depth/la-resistance-sportive-vive-la-resistance)

**Historical Purpose:**
"Conceived to honour those of the French Resistance who lost their lives in the Alps at the Battle of the Glières in 1944, the inaugural running of the La Resistance celebrates both the high mountains and the bravery of the men and women who fought and lost their lives against the occupying forces by stringing together a mixed terrain route that visits both the National Monument to La Resistance on the Plateau des Glieres and the Necropolis memorial." (https://road.cc/content/blog/200639-la-resistance-close-futile)

**Finish Line:**
"All riders arrive back at the beach in Talloires, where there will be a lakeside party named 'La Guinguette' – in the style of the Parisian riverside drinking establishments at the turn of the last century. Period costume encouraged!" (https://www.freewheelingfrance.com/blog/la-resistance.html)

**Entry Benefits:**
"Entry to La Resistance includes a ticket to La Guinguette and a free Mavic jersey. The first 100 hundred entrants will also get a free Mavic Roadie Cap." (https://www.freewheelingfrance.com/blog/la-resistance.html)

**Limited Entry:**
"Entry is €95 and limited to 463 riders – the same number of Resistance fighters who sought refuge on the Plateau in 1944." (https://www.freewheelingfrance.com/blog/la-resistance.html)
