## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**160km Shimano Round the Lake Course:**
- Total elevation gain: approximately 1,200 meters
- "Rolling hills that rise and fall like a living pulse beneath you"
- Mix of smooth highways and rolling terrain
(https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Notable Climbs:**
- Waihaha Hill Climb: "Wai is water and haha noisy, so 'noisy water' 1km" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ill9qHrJDyk)
- Hatepe Hill Climb: Featured as distinct section (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tuwGfUohMJY)
- Kuratau Forest section: "Pines stand like silent guardians" (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Turangi/taupo-lake-cycle-challenge-turangi-new-zealand)

**Wind Conditions:** "The lake breeze can shift unexpectedly" - "open stretches alongside the lake with variable wind exposure." Afternoon winds noted as "gusty lake winds that stiffen and sap energy later in the ride." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Surface Mix:**
- Mostly paved roads with some gentle to moderate hills
- Forest sections where "ancient trees crowd the shoulders"
- Open lake edge stretches with "vast sapphire surface"
(https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Gravel Grind Course (83km):**
- "Approximately 70% GRAVEL ROADS/TRACKS, 11% Tarseal, 16% Mountain Bike trails and 3% other"
- "Mountain Bike trails are mostly Grade 2 or very easy Grade 3"
- Uses "private roads" not rideable outside event day
- Four aid stations at 16km, 34km, 49km, and 77km
(https://www.cyclechallenge.com/gravel-grind)

**Tineli Huka Hundy (MTB):**
- 100km mountain bike course
- Isaac Schuurman (local) won with 4:45:13, "eight seconds ahead of second place"
- Female winner Milla Phipps: 5:21:59, average speed 18.6 km/h
(https://www.nzherald.co.nz/waikato-news/sport/lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-results-great-lake-100-debuts-top-times-logged/OFA65LKEBREDPNGUOJMWR5X3TM/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues:** Broken spokes noted - Brent James "had a broken spoke, but after some running repairs still clocked in at 5:02." (https://www.kapiticyclingclub.co.nz/newsarticle/160363?newsfeedId=2290504)

**Cut-Off Times (Gravel Grind specific):**
- 12pm at the 45km mark
- 2:30pm at Aratiatia Dam (69km)
- "Management reserves the right to remove riders from the course if it's apparent they will not complete the event by 4pm"
(https://www.cyclechallenge.com/gravel-grind)

**Weather Variability:** "The cold weather" and "the lake's unpredictable winds" noted as challenges. "Shifting shadows and cooler air that can dull senses" in late afternoon. (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Edition:** "Great weather; unlike the last few cooler years, the temperature increased during the day and hydration was important." Described as "The sun's out and spirits are high." (https://www.kapiticyclingclub.co.nz/newsarticle/160363?newsfeedId=2290504 and https://www.tauponews.co.nz/stories/sprint-finish-at-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge)

**Previous Years:** Multiple references to "last few cooler years" and "cold weather" as typical. (https://www.kapiticyclingclub.co.nz/newsarticle/160363?newsfeedId=2290504)

**Temperature Patterns:** "Early October to late March offers the most temperate weather; avoid the colder shoulder seasons when winds off the lake turn from gentle pushes to stubborn barriers." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Wind:** "Central Plateau sun can be relentless even on cooler days" - "sudden wind gusts and variable weather, especially around exposed lake edges" (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Selection [ALL LEVELS]:** "Opt for gravel tires or sturdy road tires with good tread to handle mixed surfaces and minimize punctures over gravel and sealed roads sections." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Footwear [ALL LEVELS]:** "Choose cycling shoes with good pedal grip Essential - Good shoes increase pedaling efficiency and safety while providing some comfort for short off-bike segments." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Clothing [ALL LEVELS]:** "Windproof and layered cycling apparel Essential - Layered clothing adapts to changing temperatures and the lake's unpredictable winds, maintaining comfort on long rides." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Hydration [ALL LEVELS]:** "Hydration pack or bottles Essential - Carry enough water or electrolyte drinks to stay hydrated between rest stops, especially during summer months." "Hydrate Strategically - Begin hydration well before any steep climbs and carry electrolyte mixes to maintain energy and prevent cramps during the ride." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**Tools [ALL LEVELS]:** "Multi-tool and Puncture Repair Kit Essential - Mechanical reliability is essential on long rides with remote stretches; quick repairs keep your ride going without interruption." (https://app.advcollective.com/travel-guides/Taupo/conquering-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-a-riders-practical-guide)

**E-Bikes:** "Electric bikes are permitted as part of this event category [Gravel Grind] and will be timed separately from the non assisted bikes. We will not be supplying charging stations or facilities to store extra batteries around the course." (https://www.cyclechallenge.com/gravel-grind)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Scale:** "Over 3,000 cyclists of all ages, abilities and disciplines" participated in 2025. "Thousands of riders rolled out for the 47th edition." (https://www.tauponews.co.nz/stories/sprint-finish-at-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge)

**Philosophy:** "With a philosophy of inclusion, the Lake Taupō Cycle Challenge offers a wide variety of options to get on your bike and be part of the annual pilgrimage to one of New Zealand's longest and most established events." (https://www.cyclechallenge.com/)

**Post-Race:** "Event Village" atmosphere with prize giving. "Everyone had a great night and any memories of how tough the ride had been were forgotten and thoughts of next year." Club organized after-ride dinner and debrief where "everyone was quickly chatting and sharing the days experiences with each other." (https://www.kapiticyclingclub.co.nz/newsarticle/160363?newsfeedId=2290504)

**Club Participation:** Kapiti Cycling Club example: "KCC riders aimed to start together in different groups, and then get together for the after-ride dinner and debrief. Thanks to the club for providing some drinks and nibbles to kick the night off." (https://www.kapiticyclingclub.co.nz/newsarticle/160363?newsfeedId=2290504)

**Prize Pool:** "Riders who registered for prize giving had the chance to win from an impressive spot prize pool valued at over $50,000; including a 20-month lease of a Suzuki Jimny 5-door JLX Manual, the ultimate Shimano DURA-ACE bike build" and other prizes. (https://www.tauponews.co.nz/stories/sprint-finish-at-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge)

**Community Impact:** "Every year the Lake Taupō Cycle Challenge make contributions to community groups in return for providing volunteers who help make the event come to life. For the 2024 event we were able to contribute over $75,000 to our local community groups by way of volunteer payments." (https://www.cyclechallenge.com/)

**First-Timer Experience:** "Always a special memory and achievement" - special mention for Brent Jarnell and Marc Simpson completing their first round the lake ride. (https://www.kapiticyclingclub.co.nz/newsarticle/160363?newsfeedId=2290504)

**Event Director Quote:** "Today's event was a true celebration of cycling and community. From top athletes to first-time riders, it is inspiring to see so many people challenging themselves and enjoying everything this event, and Taupō, has to offer." - Hayden Dickason (https://www.tauponews.co.nz/stories/sprint-finish-at-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge)

**Atmosphere Description:** "Taupō was buzzing with energy" - "The sun's out and spirits are high; the energy on course and in the Event Village is unmistakable: this is Aotearoa's premier cycling celebration, and it just keeps getting better." (https://www.tauponews.co.nz/stories/sprint-finish-at-the-lake-taupo-cycle-challenge)

**Age Inclusivity:** Blogger aged 81-85 competed in 85-89 age group at related duathlon events, showing multi-generational participation. (https://laketaupocyclechallenge.blogspot.com/)

## Event Categories & Options

**Nine Event Categories (2025):**
1. **Shimano Round the Lake** - 160km, iconic full circumnavigation
2. **Great Lake 100** - 100km, new in 2025, "designed for riders looking for that 'just right' challenge"
3. **Independent Traffic Control Length of the Lake** - ~80km
4. **Tineli Huka Hundy** - 100km MTB
5. **Huka Steamer** - 60km MTB, "most popular mountain biking event"
6. **Huka Teaser** - 30km MTB
7. **Enduro** - 320km (two laps), winners: Drew Christensen 9:11:20 (male), Prudence Fowler 11:13:57 (female)
8. **Gravel Grind** - 83km mixed surface
9. **Lakesider** - 16km family-friendly

(https://www.cyclechallenge.com/ and https://www.nzherald.co.nz/waikato-news/sport/lake-taupo-cycle-challenge-results-great-lake-100-debuts-top-times-logged/OFA65LKEBREDPNGUOJMWR5X3TM/)

**Start Groups (Gravel Grind example):**
- Group 1: Ride time under 3:45
- Group 2: Ride time 3:45 - 4:40
- Group 3: Ride time 4:40+
"Please select a start time based on the length of time you expect to ride"
(https://www.cyclechallenge.com/gravel-grind)

**Age Restrictions:** "The age limit to ride this alone is 15 years of age. If your child is between 12-15 and you will be accompanying them for the full duration of their ride please email info@cyclechallenge.com" (https://www.cyclechallenge.com/gravel-grind)
