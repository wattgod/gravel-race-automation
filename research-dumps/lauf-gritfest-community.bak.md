# LAUF GRITFEST — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Emily Tillett [ELITE]:** "Day 1 covered 73km with 1,700m of elevation and three timed segments — 9.4km, 19km, and 6.7km. It brought both mechanical issues and a stage win — a pretty good summary of gravel: highs and lows in a single ride." (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Emily Tillett [ELITE]:** "There's a noticeably different ethos in gravel racing compared to road racing. It's much more relaxed – wear what works, ride at the pace that suits you, and enjoy the experience... Most of the route is untimed, since racing on UK bridleways isn't allowed. That turns the transfers between stages into rolling social rides – no scrambling for position or elbows-out chaos first thing in the morning." (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "It was at this point I first regretted giving all my attention to packing my clothes and ignoring my bike - perhaps I should have done something to change my usual flat-riding gearing to something more suitable for the inclines of Wales." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "The 6 km of gradual uphill was a torturous lesson to me that I not only should have gone for a smaller chain ring, I probably should have done a bit less chatting and a bit more breathing on the transition climb too." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "The second stage was an epic - nearly 20 km of undulating trails on ever changing grades of gravel. The riders at the side of the trail fixing punctures were a sharp reminder to balance all-out speed with keeping bike and self in one piece." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "Day two was a much drier and shorter affair although the fact that the 60 km route included 1,500 m of climbing meant I continued to curse my relaxed attitude to selecting gearing. At least the day's stages were predominately downhill and so riders could pace their tired legs up the hills." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "The most fun was saved for the last stage with a spectacular fast descent on a winding track through an old lead mine. The only downside is that there was no chance to stop and take it all in." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "Gritfest is a superb event both on and off the bike. The course is brilliantly put together balancing the fun and the challenge. There's a load of other stuff to occupy and entertain you too whether it be the beauty of the scenery, the spot prizes from the sponsors, the gravy on the chips or the dodgy dancing. I'll be back next year, albeit with far more sensible gearing." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Setup - Emily Tillett [ELITE]:** Used a Specialized Diverge (2018 model) with Future Shock suspension (20mm travel), Scribe Race Disc aluminium wheels, WTB Resolute 42mm tyres, and mixed SRAM mechanical components (Red chainset, Force 1 shifters and callipers, Rival rear mech). (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Gearing - Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE - CRITICAL LESSON]:** Multiple mentions of regretting flat-riding gearing for Welsh climbs. She specifically wished she had "gone for a smaller chain ring" and described the experience as "torturous" on gradual uphills. This is COMPETITIVE rider feedback — even strong age-groupers struggled with inadequate climbing gears. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Clothing/Kit - Emily Tillett [ELITE]:** Day 1: Velocio Women's Adventure Cargo Bib Shorts with Merino Luxe Colourblock Jersey. Day 2: Velocio Concept Bibs and Jersey. Started both days with an extra layer (Ultralight Vest or Ultrapack Jacket) due to unpredictable weather, both easy to stash without feeling bulky. Full kit list included arm warmers, ultralight gloves, and base layers. (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Weather Prep - Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** Packed "extra pairs of riding shoes and empty my drawers of kit to give me an almost infinite combination of sleeves and waterproofs" after receiving weather warnings. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Mechanicals Warning [ALL LEVELS]:** Both riders referenced punctures/mechanicals as common. Rachel noted "riders at the side of the trail fixing punctures" as a reminder to balance speed with bike preservation. Emily mentioned mechanical issues affecting her overall result. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022 and https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

## Weather Experienced

**2022 Event - Rachel Sokal [COMPETITIVE]:** "Dry skies early on Saturday lulled the riders into a false sense of security before the heavens opened ahead of the race." Rain came down during Day 1, creating "wet dust splatter" on glasses. Evening brought more heavy rain: "Then the rain really came down, so we hid in our tents until the evening brought out the sunshine." Day 2 was "a much drier and shorter affair." Pre-event packing was done "in temperatures of over 30°C, for a weekend of bike racing and camping when the forecast predicted torrential rain for the first 24 hours." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**2025 Event - Emily Tillett [ELITE]:** "With the weather looking unpredictable both mornings, I started each ride with an extra layer." (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Day 1 Stages:** 73km with 1,700m climbing split into three timed segments: 9.4km, 19km, and 6.7km. First stage includes "gradual uphill" (6km per Rachel's account). Second stage is nearly 20km of "undulating trails on ever changing grades of gravel." Third stage is 8km with "punchy climbs and drifting descents." Route includes scenic sections around Llyn Brianne reservoir. (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643 and https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Day 2 Stages:** 55-60km with 1,300-1,500m climbing, three timed segments: 6.8km, 7.2km, and 6.7km. Stages are "predominately downhill" allowing riders to pace tired legs on climbs. Final stage is "a spectacular fast descent on a winding track through an old lead mine" — described as the most fun stage. (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643 and https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Transition Sections:** Include "a steep gravel track" climb before Stage 1. One transition described as "'rolled' isn't quite the right word for winching up a 20% gradient climb" before Stage 3. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Surface Conditions:** Mix of terrain including fast all-weather tracks in Tywi Forest (Day 1), technical sections, and varying gravel grades. Course is approximately 70% off-road with 40% on timed stages. (https://gritfest.co.uk and https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Notable Features:** Route passes through old lead mine with spectacular views, circles Llyn Brianne reservoir, and includes views over "quiet valleys, remote tracks" in the Cambrian Mountains. (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643 and https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Stage Format [ALL LEVELS]:** Most of the route is untimed due to UK bridleway racing restrictions. Only the designated "special stages" are timed. This creates a "rally format" where "you race at maximum intensity on designated Timed Special Stages, then relax and chat with friends on the untimed liaison sections." (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643 and https://www.breconoak.com/running-and-cycling-events/lauf-gritfest)

**Mechanical Recovery Strategy [ELITE - Emily Tillett]:** "I had a few mechanicals that put the overall out of reach, but the nature of gravel means you're never really out of it. You can go stage-by-stage, target a segment, or just enjoy pushing yourself." Stage-by-stage approach allows recovery from setbacks. (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Speed Management [COMPETITIVE - Rachel Sokal]:** "Sharp reminder to balance all-out speed with keeping bike and self in one piece" after seeing multiple puncture victims on Stage 2. Risk management is critical on the 20km undulating stage. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Pre-Stage Preparation:** At the top of the first climb, "riders congregated: removing layers, demisting glasses and attempting to rid their legs of lactic acid before heading into the first stage." Suggests allowing recovery time before timed efforts. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Inadequate Gearing [COMPETITIVE Level Confirmed]:** Rachel Sokal, a former British 24-hour MTB champion (competitive level), repeatedly cited gearing issues as her biggest struggle. She wished for a smaller chainring specifically for the Welsh climbing. If a competitive-level rider struggles with flat-land gearing, recreational riders should take note. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Mechanicals:** Multiple mentions of punctures on Stage 2's 20km section with "ever changing grades of gravel." Emily's mechanicals "put the overall out of reach." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022 and https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Weather Preparation:** Forecast can predict "torrential rain" while actual conditions vary. Under-packing for weather led to multiple riders bringing extensive gear options. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Social Atmosphere:** "With no phone signal at the campsite, the social side carries on into the evening, with people sitting around and sharing stories from the day. It feels more like a weekend away than a race weekend." (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Event Village:** "Back at event central riders made good use of the bike and person washing facilities and array of food and drink outlets to recover from the day's riding." Evening entertainment included "sunshine, entertainment and some very dodgy dancing in front of a very lively local band." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)

**Food Culture:** Multiple mentions of quality catering including "the gravy on the chips" as a memorable element. Event includes "several catering outlets... good quality, tasty and nutritious food that will keep your energy levels up. There is pizza too and Stokh brewery on site." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022 and https://www.eventrac.co.uk/e/lauf-gritfest-8732)

**Racing Ethos:** "There's a noticeably different ethos in gravel racing compared to road racing. It's much more relaxed – wear what works, ride at the pace that suits you, and enjoy the experience... no scrambling for position or elbows-out chaos first thing in the morning." Timed segments are solo efforts against the clock, not bunch racing. (https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Capacity & Scale:** Event limited to 400 participants. Described as "UK's Premier Gravel Race" and "has become a staple on the UK gravel events calendar." (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022 and https://road.cc/content/feature/why-i-gravel-weekend-lauf-gritfest-velocio-314643)

**Family Friendly:** "Families and dogs welcome!" Live music and entertainment until 10pm Friday & Saturday, with a quiet camping area available "furthest away from the main area." (https://www.eventrac.co.uk/e/lauf-gritfest-8732)

**Event Extras:** "Plenty of atmosphere and entertainment to go alongside the premium route... spot prizes from the sponsors" and various activities beyond just racing. (https://gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report--gritfest-2022)
