# LAUF TRUE GRIT GRAVEL EPIC — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & History

**Corey Godfrey (Race Director) [ELITE]:** The event started in 2007 as the Good Life Gravel Adventure. "I put the event info up on the Pirate Cycling League blog and for some reason 25 people showed up and did it. Some guys from Kansas and I believe Iowa and Missouri showed up. The internets are a powerful thing, I guess. The next year 50 riders showed up. It was apparent that Gravel Grinding was getting more and more popular every year." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** "Over some beers one night, I suggested rebranding the event 'Gravel Worlds' because there wasn't a World Championship for gravel yet, and it was bound to get got by someone, so why not us?" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Multiple Elite Racers [ELITE]:** Vittoria Mezcal tyres were a popular choice among Atlas Mountain Race competitors, with multiple riders running the 2.35" size. Michael Borg used Mezcal 2.35", while Henrik Landergren ran Barzo/Mezcal combination (front/rear) in 2.25". (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/2024-atlas-mountain-race-rigs-part-2/)

**Will Jones [COMPETITIVE]:** On the Vittoria Mezcal: "If someone told you you have to only use one gravel tyre for everything, there's a very strong case to be made that it should be the Vittoria Mezcal. It does so well over such a range of terrains that I'd happily leave them on year-round." He noted they roll fast despite knobby appearance and have been "totally puncture-free, despite plenty of rocky riding." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/reviews/vittoria-mezcal-tyre-review/)

**Will Jones [COMPETITIVE]:** "The carcass is also solid enough to hold air well and not leak sealant through the sidewalls, as I have found with many a lighter tyre. For gravel racing, or if your riding is predominantly hardpack, especially if you can accommodate a wider tyre, I can absolutely see why you'd want something slightly lower profile." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/reviews/vittoria-mezcal-tyre-review/)

**Michael Borg (AMR racer) [UNKNOWN]:** For water capacity on long remote sections: "I'm a thirsty guy, and so have 7L+ of water capacity for the longer sections without resupply." His setup included a 3L bladder in frame bag, handlebar pouch bottles, down tube bottle, and Camelbak backpack with 3L bladder. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/2024-atlas-mountain-race-rigs-part-2/)

**Antoine Dengler (AMR racer) [UNKNOWN]:** Highlighted the Milkit tubeless booster bottle "which I can also use for water" as a dual-purpose gear choice. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/2024-atlas-mountain-race-rigs-part-2/)

**Will Denning (AMR racer) [UNKNOWN]:** Used Tailfinn aero bag and panniers on a full-suspension Santa Cruz Blur CC. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/2024-atlas-mountain-race-rigs-part-2/)

**Justinas Leveika (AMR racer) [UNKNOWN]:** "I am going all in for comfort this time. I will be using Trek Supercaliber. Flat handlebars, but still putting on the clip-on aero bars. Hunt wheels with dynamo paired with a Supernova M99 dynamo light." Used Tailfin custom bags including "a long top tube bag (to carry my baguette)." (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/2024-atlas-mountain-race-rigs-part-2/)

**Ben Lewis (AMR racer) [UNKNOWN]:** Ran a sophisticated lighting setup: "SON Dynamo Hub running a K-Lite 'Ultra Racing lighting system' with USB charger to recharge my battery pack" on a Specialized Epic Evo Pro. (https://bikepacking.com/bikes/2024-atlas-mountain-race-rigs-part-2/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Corey Godfrey (Race Director) [ELITE]:** "The gravel is primarily of the pea-sized variety near Lincoln, though there will be stretches of larger, chunky cherry-tomato-sized gravel on occasion. Some of the roads have become hardpack and are as fast as concrete. There's also a lot of minimal maintenance and dirt roads in the area." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** On elevation: "You can be in the middle of Lincoln and in 10 to 15 minutes be on gravel in the country. It's a beautiful thing. The elevation can also vary. To the northwest of Lincoln are the Bohemian Alps, which have a lot of rolling hills." The course typically has between 8,000 and 11,000 feet of climbing for the 150-mile route. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** "The gravel around Lincoln is deceptive. The hills don't look that challenging, but those hills are relentless. One after another after another." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Will Jones [COMPETITIVE]:** On the Mezcal tyre performance across terrain types: "On techy, rutty, and rocky surfaces - think singletrack blasts and those climbs where line choice is key - they really do excel, and you can tell they are basically a cross-country MTB tyre." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/reviews/vittoria-mezcal-tyre-review/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Corey Godfrey (Race Director) [ELITE]:** On race features: "Riders must either carry everything they need for the day or resupply at our mandatory checkpoints, optional oases, or small rural town convenience stores. We force the riders to interact with volunteers and locals in order to sustain themselves." No outside assistance allowed, unlike some other gravel events. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** "Some have been completely wasted at the finish" regarding elite finishers, suggesting the terrain and distance create significant fatigue even for top riders. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** On 2018 elite racing: "Alison Tetrick is back to defend her title and Kae Takeshita will give her another epic battle. They sprinted for the finish last year in what was the most exciting finish ever at Gravel Worlds." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Corey Godfrey (Race Director) [ELITE]:** On the event philosophy: "The goal of Gravel Worlds was to create an event with a 'polished, grassroots vibe.'" The event deliberately avoids carnival-like checkpoints to maintain grassroots atmosphere. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** "As a promoter, the most satisfying part is seeing someone dig deep and accomplish something they weren't sure they could do at the start. Those moments change lives for the better, and I feel lucky to be a small part of that." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** On the event philosophy: "I guess the whole pirate theme revolved around promoting and participating in events without an association to a sanctioning body or a cycling team. If you wanted to join the team or participate in an event, all you had to do was show up and ride. Don't have a team? Join the Pirate Cycling League!" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** On the UCI rainbow stripes issue: "We were contacted by the UCI last year after the event. They reminded us about their international license on the rainbow stripes. They were very cool about our inadvertent use of the rainbow stripes and kindly asked us not to do it again. Completely our bad... I'd like to visit Switzerland someday, but not because I'm getting sued in international court." The event now gives out Champion's jerseys with rainbow-esque lettering but no stripes. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Race Rule #1 [ELITE]:** "Don't be lame" is the first rule. Godfrey explained: "The rule of not being lame covers a lot of behaviors that we feel are antithetical to the spirit of gravel grinding. Since we're pirates, we can and will disqualify those that exhibit any lame behavior." The event has only 9 rules total, compared to Dirty Kanza's 25. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)
