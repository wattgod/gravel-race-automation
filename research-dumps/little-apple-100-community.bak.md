## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start positioning [ALL LEVELS]:** The race features a neutral roll-out for approximately one mile before racing begins, designed to prevent incidents at the start (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Group dynamics [ELITE/COMPETITIVE]:** Multiple riders mentioned getting dropped early or riding solo. One elite rider popped off the group at mile 11, while a competitive rider found himself riding alone after the initial five miles of climbing (https://www.rawbikefitting.com/blog/2018/08/little-apple-100, https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Partnership strategy [ELITE]:** RAW Bike Fitting rider (elite, finished 2nd/3rd) formed a team time trial partnership after being dropped, which successfully brought them back to a podium finish. They made strategic stops at mile 50 (cooler) and mile 45 (on-course water station) and stopped when partner was cramping (https://www.rawbikefitting.com/blog/2018/08/little-apple-100)

**Working together [COMPETITIVE]:** Around mile 23 of the 50K, Ironman Chris partnered with "Matt" to work together to the finish, trading pulls on different terrain types (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Positioning in turns [ALL LEVELS - from experienced rider]:** "We are taught to take the inside line in a turn always. That's because when a rider falls, they fall away (out) from a turn. If you are on the inside a falling rider will not take you out. If you are on the outside of a rider, and they fall, they will naturally slide into your wheel and take you out too." - dfriestedt (https://discuss.360velo.com/t/little-apple-100-crash/1386)

**Gravel positioning safety [ALL LEVELS]:** Multiple riders emphasized maintaining an exit strategy and avoiding riding boxed in. Coach_Theia stayed "on the far right or far left sides, in the smooth section and close to the grass" to allow escape routes (https://discuss.360velo.com/t/little-apple-100-crash/1386)

**150km winning strategy [ELITE]:** Matt Zalewski (Hardcore Cycling Club) broke away solo for 20 miles to win the open 150km race (https://hardcorecycling.com/hardcore-cycling-club-dominates-little-apple-gravel-state-championship/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface composition:** Course is billed as 88% gravel running through farm areas of southern Chicago area (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Gravel quality variation:** "Someone nicknamed a section of very soft gravel the 'Devil's Gravel,' and that section had some white knuckle moments" - occurred around mile 23 of the 50K. The gravel was described as soft enough that a hard crash to the head "didn't seem to phase him" (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Gravel conditions day-of:** Elite 50K winner noted: "The gravel today was loose and I went down in a corner as well. I promise there are so many different types of gravel and today was just a lot rougher than usual" (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Line choice:** Multiple riders emphasized finding the best line - "usually the car tire-packed-down area of the gravel road. There were plenty of bike tire tracks to use as a guide as well" (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Rough gravel center:** "DO NOT RIDE IN THE ROUGH GRAVEL BETWEEN TWO RIDERS... Only ride in the rough gravel (in the middle of the road) to go between the smooth sections" - the center of the road has rougher gravel than the car tire lines (https://discuss.360velo.com/t/little-apple-100-crash/1386)

**Early course profile (50K):** First gravel section after the first turn includes approximately 5 miles of uphill into headwind (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Final approach:** Course includes paved road sections near the finish. "When we finally got to a paved road again, it was I who took the lead" (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Mental demands:** "Devil's gravel lived up to its name. I think my brain is still tired from staring at the ground trying to find a good-enough line" - described as mentally exhausting (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Wheel overlap crashes:** Multiple crashes documented from riders overlapping wheels in rough gravel. "If you overlap wheels in rough gravel you are asking for trouble" (https://discuss.360velo.com/t/little-apple-100-crash/1386)

**First turn crashes:** "As we got rolling and started racing, we came to the first turn and the first section of gravel, and that is when I saw my first crash. The guy went into the turn a little too hot and wiped out" (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Corner crashes in loose gravel:** Multiple riders went down in corners, including the elite 50K winner. "Around 23 miles into it, I saw a second rider who had just passed me and another rider go down around a corner" (https://anamazingrun.com/2025/08/03/little-apple-gravel-race-50k-race-report/)

**Crash outcomes:** Coach_Theia reported "A few broken bones, but nothing major" from the documented group crash (https://discuss.360velo.com/t/little-apple-100-crash/1386)

**Front brake grabbing:** One crash specifically attributed to rider grabbing "a hand full of front breaks" in rough gravel (https://discuss.360velo.com/t/little-apple-100-crash/1386)

**Mental fatigue:** Elite rider described battling "dark thoughts" at mile 11: "You don't have the fitness. You're tired. Sleep Apnea sucks. It's too hot. I can't hold this pace" - nearly led to DNF before partnering with another rider (https://www.rawbikefitting.com/blog/2018/08/little-apple-100)

**DNF count (2023 results):** 7 DNFs listed out of 64 starters in the Gravel 1-5 category for the 100km race (https://results.bikereg.com/race/17684)

**Early setback recovery:** Witold Zabawski (Hardcore Cycling Club) crashed in the initial miles but "showed true grit and determination, battling through to the finish" (https://hardcorecycling.com/hardcore-cycling-club-dominates-little-apple-gravel-state-championship/)
