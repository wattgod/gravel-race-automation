**Sofía Gómez Villafañe [ELITE]:** "I went solo up the grassy hill at the start and expected Alexis to catch me, but she was riding her own pace. It was a bit lonely out there, but my legs were good today. Maybe too much altitude training—but it worked!" (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/gómez-villafañe-and-jones-go-solo-at-little-sugar-mtb)

**Cameron Jones [ELITE]:** "I had awesome legs today. The plan was simple—be up front in the singletrack, ride my own pace, and stay out of trouble. I cooked it a bit early and started to cramp, but it was good to have that buffer. Fun day on the trails." (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/gómez-villafañe-and-jones-go-solo-at-little-sugar-mtb)

**Cameron Jones [ELITE]** on Little Sugar: "I actually got dropped out of that lead group three times, but each time convinced myself to fight back to it. It was really tough. I actually vomited coming back to the group the third time, but just really wanted to be proud of my last race effort of the year." (https://alliedcycleworks.com/blogs/stories/big-little-sugar-bike-check)

**Cameron Jones [ELITE]** on the bike racing mindset: "You're just literally trying to break the will of someone else, and they're trying to do the same to you. It's kind of primal, and you can see that if you stand in the finish corral of a pro race like this. Everyone is a bit wide-eyed, not really able to put words together yet, trying to reenter the real world." (https://alliedcycleworks.com/blogs/stories/big-little-sugar-bike-check)

**tgoods [COMPETITIVE]** (Bentonville local): "The course is going to be brutal. It's about 85% singletrack, with most of the singletrack being loose over hard with a lot of chatter. The first half of the course is going to be non stop climbs and fast descents, averaging around 130 ft of climbing per mile. There's a lot of little 'kickers' that will require your lowest gear and they really take a toll on legs after an hour so so. If you go out too hard you will pay dearly the next 4 or 5 hours. Unfortunately the way the route starts people are going to be gunning to get into single track in a good position and everyone is going to smash the first couple of climbs. I encourage everyone to try to dial it back the first hour!" (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**MN_XC [COMPETITIVE]:** "The West side loop...there was only one 2-3 mile section that I would consider pedally. Continuous 'up-a-holler' climbs and 'down-a-holler' ripping descents...It's not particularly loamy - mostly gravelly limestone dry dirt mixture. Even the big berms are made of gravel with occasional baseball size baby-heads. The quality of the trail experience was directly correlated with how recent the trail crew had come through to 'buff-out' the trail." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**SoonerNate [UNKNOWN]:** "I can only stand about 20 miles of that type of undulating riding at a time. Concentration is going to be key, because you'll be flying through some creek beds at full speed past rocks and trees one inch from your handle bars and there's a hard turn and now climb over these roots up the other side. Over and over. Yesh." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Greg Marchand [COMPETITIVE]:** "I had a really tough patch about 2 hours into Little Sugar. I'd been battling a bad stomach that morning, and was close to needing to pull over and handle a bit of business. My legs just weren't quite firing with the power required to stick with the front group either. I had to dig pretty deep mentally to keep my head in the game, but things came around and I snagged a top ten just by staying consistent and committed." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/little-sugar-mtb-2024)

**webdev511 [UNKNOWN]:** "That looks rough. Longest climb is 1.5km longest descent is 1.9km. your going to be going up and down non-stop the whole way. Think lots of short sweet spot/threshold efforts." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Alex_NoCo [COMPETITIVE]:** "Yes, this is going to be constant short threshold intervals for 5+ hours haha" (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

### Tire Choices (Critical - Multiple sources agree this is make-or-break)

**Cameron Jones [ELITE]:** Maxxis Rekon Race 2.35 tires on ZIPP 1Zero Hitop SW wheels. "The rubber on those tires is stiff and thick as hell, making them tougher to ride than a more supple tire, but it's worth it on these super sharp OZ rocks. Even with that added reinforcement, I actually cut my front tire about 3 hours in, but luckily the Orange Seal sealed it up." (https://alliedcycleworks.com/blogs/stories/big-little-sugar-bike-check)

**tgoods [COMPETITIVE]** (Bentonville local, 170 lbs): "I will be running Barzo 2.35 Front / Mezcal 2.35 Rear (both with TNT casing) with no inserts...running 21 / 23 PSI. I've had some nasty sidewall cuts on the Vittorias, but mile-for-mile I've had less trouble with Vittoria tires than other brands, especially Maxxis XC tires with EXO. I don't run Maxxis anymore." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**MN_XC [COMPETITIVE]:** "If you bring your racing ralphs, aspens, race kings or other racing-style protection tires with smaller knobs I'd say you are risking 50% chance of flating. The flats are usually not dyna-pluggable. Usually a slice you need to boot/tube with a card or dollar bill." Also: "Funny, I've sliced up two race king protections out there…you would have needed to thread fishing line to fix. Bonk bros suggested Maxxis Rekon (not race version)...Our crew (from MN) changes over from 2.35 Bontrager XR2s to 2.4 XR4s when going to Bentonville." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Kuttermax [UNKNOWN]:** "A Maxxis Ardent up front in 2.4 and either another Ardent in the rear, or perhaps an Ardent Race 2.35 in MaxxSpeed as a faster rolling rear alternative, both with EXO casing would be reasonable. Schwalbe Nobby Nic 2.4s in Super Trail if you favor extra protection would be another way to go." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Power13 [UNKNOWN]:** "They discussed tire choices on the most recent episode of the Bonk Bros podcast…takeaway seemed to be to make sure you have sufficient sidewall protection...2.4 seemed to be the recommended size." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Consensus from multiple sources:** Minimum 2.35" tire width, with 2.4" preferred. Extra sidewall protection essential. Avoid race-weight XC tires without protection. Tire inserts optional but sidewall strength critical.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:** "About 85% singletrack, with most of the singletrack being loose over hard with a lot of chatter" - tgoods [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Climbing Pattern:** "The first half of the course is going to be non stop climbs and fast descents, averaging around 130 ft of climbing per mile" - tgoods [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Surface Conditions:** "Mostly gravelly limestone dry dirt mixture. Even the big berms are made of gravel with occasional baseball size baby-heads" - MN_XC [COMPETITIVE] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Technical Features:** "You'll be flying through some creek beds at full speed past rocks and trees one inch from your handle bars and there's a hard turn and now climb over these roots up the other side. Over and over." - SoonerNate [UNKNOWN] (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Trail Systems by Section:**
- East vs West side character differ: "The East side is definitely the preferable side if you are picking between the two" - MN_XC [COMPETITIVE]
- West side: "Only one 2-3 mile section that I would consider pedally. Continuous 'up-a-holler' climbs and 'down-a-holler' ripping descents" - MN_XC [COMPETITIVE]
(https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/little-sugar-mtb/86468)

**Official Course Description (100K):** 
- Distance: 101.8k / 63.3 miles
- Elevation Gain: ~5,600'
- Trail systems: Little Sugar Creek, Back 40/Blowing Springs, Slaughter Pen, and Coler MTB Preserve
(https://www.bigsugarclassic.com/mtb/)
