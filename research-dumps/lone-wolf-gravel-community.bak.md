# LONE WOLF GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & History

**Race Creator James Morrow [UNKNOWN]** explained the origin: "The Lone wolf route was created by me and my brother-in-law while searching to find as much remote gravel as possible that could be accessed from downtown Iron Mountain. Once the route was found, it quickly became one of our favorite rides to do leaving from our houses. We could get a solid 50 mile loop of pristine, hard pack, fast gravel, accompanied by chunky steep, climbs, and rowdy descents. While on one of our spring rides, we were both bar to bar climbing out of a big trough when he looked up and whispered to me 'Wolf'-'Wolf'. At the crest of the hill trotted one of the largest gray wolves I've ever seen, and as being spring time was losing much of its coat, giving it a mangy rough appearance. As soon as we laid eyes on it, it looked over its right shoulder back at us and kept a steady trot up and over the crest. Thankfully It stepped off into the forest and let us pass. That route was dubbed the Lone wolf route from there on." (https://www.upsportandspoke.com/lonewolfgravelrace)

The race required rerouting in year 2 (2022). **James Morrow** explained: "With Fumee Lake area not allowing their gates to be opened for our event this year we had to do some serious recon and planning to figure out how we could bypass the area while still making the route as great as it was the first year. While also needing a new start/finish line too. We turned to the DNR for support and usage and they delivered. We were able to reroute the course while adding 10 miles of amazing U.P. Gravel, a few miles of which are brand new thanks to the DNR, and host the start/ finish at the gravel pit just around the corner from Carney Lake. All these changes became a silver lining through the clouds." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-lone-wolf-sequel-2022-reroutes-peak-colors-a-perfect-day-in-dah-u-p)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The race features "pristine, hard pack, fast gravel, accompanied by chunky steep, climbs, and rowdy descents" according to the route creators (https://www.upsportandspoke.com/lonewolfgravelrace)

**Conditions in 2022**: "Conditions were pretty dry and loose for gravel biking in the Iron Mountain area." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-lone-wolf-sequel-2022-reroutes-peak-colors-a-perfect-day-in-dah-u-p)

From a YouTube description: "Once we made the turn back onto gravel, the gps route had us going directly into the woods without not even a hint of a trail. So I zoomed out on my head unit and directed the group to take about..." (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D7dMuD_U3Ho) - indicating navigation challenges and potential route confusion.

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Navigation required [ALL LEVELS]**: "The courses will be minimally (or not at all) marked, a .gpx-compatible device is required for navigation." (https://www.upsportandspoke.com/lonewolfgravelrace)

**Required equipment [ALL LEVELS]**: "Helmets and rear flashing lights are required, as is following all traffic laws and rules of the road." (https://www.upsportandspoke.com/lonewolfgravelrace)

## Weather Experienced

**2022 Race Day**: "Just over 100 registered. Just under 70 showed up. 27* in the morning 55* in the afternoon. Partly sunny. Peak color change. A perfect fall day in dah U.P." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-lone-wolf-sequel-2022-reroutes-peak-colors-a-perfect-day-in-dah-u-p)

The race is timed for fall colors in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, typically late September.

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Aid station information**: "We had one aid station on course hosted by Embark Maple with Laura the fox and her rad Dad dishing out all the maple goodness." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-lone-wolf-sequel-2022-reroutes-peak-colors-a-perfect-day-in-dah-u-p) - Only one aid station for 50+ mile courses, plan self-sufficiency accordingly.

## DNF Risk Factors

Navigation appears to be a significant challenge with minimal or no course marking and riders needing to problem-solve route-finding in real-time (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D7dMuD_U3Ho)

**Timing issues in 2022**: "Only downfall of the race is that we had some disconnects with the manual timing and weren't able to log everyone's times. This will be our main focus to correct for next years race." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-lone-wolf-sequel-2022-reroutes-peak-colors-a-perfect-day-in-dah-u-p)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event is described as holding "to the tradition of grass-root origins that helped create the popular cycling discipline" in contrast to commercialized gravel racing (https://komcycling.com/blogs/product-blogs/episode-95-the-lone-wolf-gravel-race)

Race proceeds support local community: "With the proceeds from the race we were able to purchase a rescue device for the fire department that will help with single track rescues in the Iron Mountain area." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/the-lone-wolf-sequel-2022-reroutes-peak-colors-a-perfect-day-in-dah-u-p)

**Presented by**: Iron Hills Cycling Team, "a nonprofit organization that strives to promote a sense of community and support among local residents of Dickinson County and the surrounding areas through a shared passion of cycling." (https://www.upsportandspoke.com/lonewolfgravelrace)

The race allows both competitive and recreational approaches: "We offer 36, 50, and 100-mile course options that you can fiercely compete in, or slow roll and enjoy the beautiful scenery." (https://www.upsportandspoke.com/lonewolfgravelrace)

**Notable competitors**: Dan Teaters is mentioned as a three-time winner, and Mitch Edbauer as a three-time competitor (https://komcycling.com/blogs/product-blogs/episode-95-the-lone-wolf-gravel-race). Andrew Knickerbocker of Diablo Cycling placed 2nd in the 100 Mile in 2024 (http://www.diablocycling.com/races-events--blog/the-lone-wolf-gravel-race9964023)
