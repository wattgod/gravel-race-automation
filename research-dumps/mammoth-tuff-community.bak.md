## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Dave Malwitz [UNKNOWN]:** "What makes Mammoth Tuff deceivingly difficult is its unique set of challenges: the starting altitude of 8,000 feet, long stretches of sandy and loose terrain, and the near absence of course markings. Riders are well warned to rely on their own navigation rather than following the course blindly." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/mammoth-tuff-2024-through-sand-and-sky)

**Dave Malwitz [UNKNOWN]:** "That first year, my buddy Danny and I tackled the TUFFER course—80 miles and 5,000 feet of climbing that, on paper, seemed reasonable. However, the combination of high elevation, loose gravel, and unrelenting wind quickly redefined my sense of 'tuff'." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/mammoth-tuff-2024-through-sand-and-sky)

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "I managed to catch them just as they crested the top and promptly watched them disappear at the bottom of the descent when an aggressive patch of washboard bounced my chain off. Back to chasing." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "I had been concerned with my tire choice of 40mm Pirelli Cinturatos after pre-riding parts of the course and despite not having too many issues on the day so far, I began to struggle. Other riders in my group were primarily on knobbier, larger tires. I watched them plow through and float over sand while I was sinking, slipping and pushing a lot of watts to keep up." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "Staring into the breathtaking backdrop of Mount Morrison and Laurel Mountain the hamstring cramps started. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Amanda Nauman (race co-creator) [RECREATIONAL]:** "The variety of volcanic rock from pumice to obsidian means that we recommend the widest tire you can fit in your frame and comfortably ride." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "In each of the three distances at Mammoth TUFF, one feature that ties them all together is that the first half of the ride is primarily descending and the second half of the ride is primarily climbing back to town. Because of this, it's often easy to forget to eat and drink while you're having a blast in the downhill sections and it will come back to haunt you on the way back to the finish." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Recommendations:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** Used 40mm Pirelli Cinturatos and struggled in sand compared to riders on knobbier, larger tires. Finished 8th overall but noted: "I watched them plow through and float over sand while I was sinking, slipping and pushing a lot of watts to keep up." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Dave Malwitz [UNKNOWN]:** "The Revel titanium frame does an excellent job at smoothing out rough terrain, while the wider tires helped me float across many sandy sections." Used Soma Cazadero 700x50 tires with Orange Seal Endurance. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/mammoth-tuff-2024-through-sand-and-sky)

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "We recommend the widest tire you can fit in your frame and comfortably ride" due to volcanic pumice stone. Her favorites include "Rene Herse's 700x 42 Hurricane Ridge, 44 Manastash Ridge, 44 Corkscrew Climb TC Semi-Slick or 48 Oracle Ridge." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "As wide as your frame will allow! 38's will suffice, but we recommend 40 and above. The more surface area you have, the better. Knobby tires that roll well are recommended." (https://www.tuff.ventures/mtuff/info)

**Gearing Recommendations:**

**Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "If 2x we recommend something like a Shimano GRX crankset, or similar, with a 48/31 tooth chainrings and a 11-34 cassette. If 1x we recommend a 40 tooth chainring with at least a 11-40 cassette but an 11-46 would be better. We are saying a 1:1 ratio or easier. It is a long day at altitude." (https://www.tuff.ventures/mtuff/info)

**Dave Malwitz [UNKNOWN]:** Used "42t chainring and 10-44 cassette" with SRAM Force XPLR eTap AXS groupset. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/mammoth-tuff-2024-through-sand-and-sky)

**Complete Bike Setup:**

**Dave Malwitz [UNKNOWN]:** Revel R+ Titanium, SRAM Force XPLR eTap AXS, 42t chainring/10-44 cassette, Revel carbon wheels, Soma Cazadero 700x50 tires, Orange Seal Endurance, Ergon SR Allroad saddle, Easton cockpit, Wahoo Roam GPS, Topo Designs Top Tube Bag, small Topo handlebar bag, Outershell Saddle Bag. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/mammoth-tuff-2024-through-sand-and-sky)

**Hydration/Storage:**

**Official FAQ [UNKNOWN]:** "It is recommended you carry a pack or some sort of bags on your frame, bars, etc. that allow you to carry a minimum of 2 Liters (67OZ) of fluids, 4hrs of nutrition, and extra clothing (you will be in the Owens Valley and temps can change quick)." (https://www.tuff.ventures/mtuff/info)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Sandy/Pumice Sections:**

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "Pumice stone is prevalent on the forest roads and it is a relatively soft, porous rock that is usually formed by the compaction and cementation of volcanic ash or dust. If you've ridden through pumice before, you know it mimics the feel of sand in areas because of how light and airy the material is." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "The climb back up from the aid station was by far the hardest part of the race. This stretch of road had been regraded and then scraped which is to say, there were sandy sections everywhere." On the final climb: "the mile and a half dirt climb is a sandbox from hell." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Course Profile:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "The next portion of the Tuffer course features a very sandy bit of rolling terrain... A five mile dirty descent led us to our oasis [aid station]... The climb back up from the aid station was by far the hardest part of the race." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**UCI Course Description [UNKNOWN]:** "The hardest part of the course comes right after the halfway point where riders start a 16 miles climb at 4.3% average with especially the narrow Red Rock Canyon climb between 52-54 miles." For 89-mile course: "63% of the course is gravel, 37% paved." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/mammoth/)

**Descent Challenges:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** On Old Highway 395 descent: "The pace on this section simply intimidated me as potholes, sand and other land mines seemingly appeared out of nowhere between the intermingled beams of sunlight peaking between the pines. I played it safe and wagered I'd reconnect on the next short climb." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Highway Section:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "This portion of the event briefly puts you on Highway 395 which offers a wide shoulder and a really spectacular view of the eastern Sierra." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Altitude Management:**

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "The best suggestion is to lower your expectations of power output and fitness capabilities. Everyone will be operating at a lower capacity than normal at altitude, and it's okay to accept that fact. Don't get down because you feel like you're slower because everyone else will be feeling it too! Coming in with that mindset will allow yourself to just do your best and have a good time." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Nutrition/Hydration Timing:**

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "Because of this [descending first half, climbing second half], it's often easy to forget to eat and drink while you're having a blast in the downhill sections and it will come back to haunt you on the way back to the finish. Make a plan and set reminders for yourself to take care of the fueling and hydration." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "People get dehydrated quicker at altitude so properly hydrating is important in avoiding the negative side effects from high elevation. Make sure you're getting both plain water and some sort of drink mix or tab with electrolytes, especially surrounding activity." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Early Race Pacing:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "I knew I wanted to try and be with the front group so I notched up my pace heading up the first climb. I managed to catch them just as they crested the top and promptly watched them disappear at the bottom of the descent when an aggressive patch of washboard bounced my chain off." Later: "I jumped into a doomed two-man breakaway that lasted for 15 miles. We were caught at the bottom of a paved 3-mile climb and eased back into the pace of the group hoping to save some legs for later." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Cramping from Altitude:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** "Despite the warning signs of impending leg cramps, I was still with the group 10 miles later when we hit the penultimate climb on Hot Creek Hatchery Rd. Two riders attacked the group with about 10 miles to go and I spun up to catch them. At least I tried to. Staring into the breathtaking backdrop of Mount Morrison and Laurel Mountain the hamstring cramps started." Later: "I was now cramping almost every time I tried to remain seated." Still finished 8th overall. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

**Navigation Issues:**

**Dave Malwitz [UNKNOWN]:** "Riders are well warned to rely on their own navigation rather than following the course blindly. Even the shorter TUFF course demanded full attention and preparation, as one mistake could easily lead to getting lost in the maze of forest roads." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/mammoth-tuff-2024-through-sand-and-sky)

**Voluntary Withdrawal:**

**Graham Johnson [ELITE]:** At first aid station (halfway through): "One rider simply turned around (this was an out-and-back portion of the route) and rode back up the hill never to be seen again. He would go on to win by 8 minutes." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2023-mammoth-tuff-gravel-race-report)

## Weather Experienced

**Temperature Swings:**

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "The overnight lows start to dip in September, so prepare to be cold in the early morning and heat up throughout the day. Riders doing the TUFFEST route will be managing more heat in the valley, so layers of clothing you can remove will be critical." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)

**Amanda Nauman [RECREATIONAL]:** "When it comes to the temperature at high elevation, be sure to layer, stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and you will feel the effects of altitude less." (https://puregravel.com/blogs/reviews/mammoth-tuff-taming-volcanic-gravel-and-embracing-high-altitude-cycling)
