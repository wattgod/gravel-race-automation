# MARYS MAYHEM — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Nicole Oh [ELITE]:** "There's no doubt that the popularity of gravel riding is increasing, with more and more events appearing on the calendar. Mary's Mayhem, held on the roads around Mudgee, was Graveleur's second event of the year. Unlike Thunderbolts held earlier in May... the courses for Mary's were far less difficult and much more conducive to fun times and good vibes!" (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Nicole Oh [ELITE]:** "Now, it was the understanding of all that this event was NOT a race. The timing chips were (apparently) a trial for future events. But a timing chip and prize for the first female across the line sounds like a race to me... My racing instincts then took over, as I found myself putting in a dig over the top of the last hill to get a gap... and a little sprint to the finish to ensure I was 2nd female over the line (and as I found out later, 15th overall)." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Nicole Oh [ELITE]:** "The faster than expected ride meant we were all done by 12.30pm, leaving plenty of time to soak up the sun and enjoy the fine choice of food and refreshments on offer. We briefly flirted with the idea of visiting a gin distillery or winery (on bikes), but on arrival to our hotel room back in Mudgee, our comfy beds and watching the Olympics on TV, accompanied by some donuts and Negroni, won the day." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Nicole Oh [ELITE]:** "The route today seemed a lot more scenic, although perhaps that was due to the fact we rode much less hard, and therefore had plenty more time to look around! It started and ended with tarmac, but with a whole load of champagne gravel in between with just a few corrugations. As opposed to yesterday, there was a good 45min of stopping for photos, food, regrouping, and even just sitting in the sun." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Mike Israel (event founder) [UNKNOWN]:** "I started Graveleur back in 2017 with a few mates looking for dirt roads to ride... It grew into something much bigger - a national community of over 6,500 riders who love getting off the beaten path and supporting the regional towns they pass through." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Mike Israel [UNKNOWN]:** "Graveleur is for everyone. Whether you're twelve or seventy, on a carbon race bike or a hand-me-down hybrid, there's a route for you." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Mike Israel [UNKNOWN]:** "Mudgee was a natural choice. It's got amazing dirt roads, scenic country, a cracking food scene and great places to stay. Plus, the local cycling club and community have been incredibly supportive." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Mike Israel [UNKNOWN]:** On the Sunday finish at Rylstone - "practically every resident from Rylstone came out. We felt so welcomed. It was magic." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Day 1 Fast Pace Experience:** Nicole Oh's group completed the 60km to the feed stop in Gulgong in under 2 hours on fresh legs, aided by champagne gravel and net downhill terrain in the first section. They finished the full day by 12:30pm despite a 10-minute tea stop. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**[ELITE] Nutrition/Feed Stops:** At the Gulgong feed stop (60km mark on Day 1), riders were "greeted by a lovely spread of homemade cakes and slices, courtesy of the local Rotary club." Nicole notes "I made sure I sampled a few." CWA branches in Gulgong and Rylstone provide home baking at feed zones. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/ and https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**[ELITE] Two-Day Pacing Strategy:** Nicole's group intentionally rode "party pace" on Day 2, starting at the very back "to eliminate any temptation to go faster than planned." They spent a good 45 minutes stopping for photos, food, regrouping, and sitting in the sun. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**[UNKNOWN] Course Profile Impact:** Saturday's long course is described as net downhill in the first section with climbing concentrated in the second half after Gulgong. The region is "relatively flat" being over the Great Dividing Range, making distances "not as taxing" as other Graveleur events. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/ and https://raceroster.com/events/2026/112571/marys-mayhem-2026)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Quality:** Multiple sources describe "champagne gravel" throughout the courses, with "just a few corrugations" encountered. Day 2 route started and ended with tarmac with "a whole load of champagne gravel in between." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Course Profile - Day 1 (Saturday):** 97km with 1000m climbing. First section is net downhill, with most climbing concentrated in the second half after the Gulgong feed stop at 60km. Includes "a last hill" before the finish. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Course Profile - Day 2 (Sunday):** Same distance as Day 1 (97km) but with slightly more climbing at 1200m. Described as "a lot more scenic" with "ancient rock formations as you cruise through the valley." Route includes "a nice gentle downhill roll home on tarmac with a tailwind." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/ and https://graveleur.cc/marys-mayhem-event-page)

**Regional Terrain:** Mudgee region is described as "relatively flat" and characterized by "rolling hills" with "virtually no climbing on the courses" compared to other events. The area features "backroads wind[ing] through gum trees and gold-tinged paddocks." (https://raceroster.com/events/2026/112571/marys-mayhem-2026 and https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Wildlife Alert:** "The only downside of the day was the numerous 'sleeping' wombats by the side of the road" on Day 2. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 Event - Day 1:** "Saturday started a little on the chilly side, but once the sun had burnt through the morning fog, it was a beautiful day to ride a bike." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**2024 Event - Day 2:** "The weather for Day 2 was almost a carbon copy of day 1 — a little fog and chill, but then glorious sunshine for the rest of the day." (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Expected Conditions:** Event marketing describes "hopefully glorious Spring sunshine" and refers to the region as having "blue ribbon" weather conditions. (https://raceroster.com/events/2026/112571/marys-mayhem-2026)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Local Community Involvement:** "From the Rotary Clubs of Rylstone and Mudgee to the CWA branches in Gulgong and Rylstone, local groups have thrown their weight behind the event - staffing checkpoints, baking cakes, offering first aid, and creating a festival-like energy riders can feel every step of the way." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Post-Race Atmosphere:** Day 1 finishes at Small Batch Brewery with "post ride drinks, celebrations & re-fuelling" from 2:00-5:00pm featuring "fresh food, Live DJ, Games and loads more." Saturday night continues with an after-party at Kelly's Irish Pub with "presentations and meal discounts." (https://graveleur.cc/marys-mayhem-event-page and https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Sunday Finish:** Day 2 concludes at The Globe Hotel in Rylstone with a "bustling community market" where, according to the founder, "practically every resident from Rylstone came out" in previous years. Riders described feeling "so welcomed. It was magic." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart)

**Event Philosophy:** Described as having "good vibes" and being "designed for pure fun" with the tagline "How You Ride It Is Up to You!" The event welcomes riders 12 years and older on any bike, with significant family and group discounts available. Entry fees are "well below industry average" as the organization aims to "recover costs" and build community. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/ and https://graveleur.cc/marys-mayhem-event-page)

**Race vs. Ride Ambiguity:** There's some confusion about competitive nature. Nicole Oh notes "it was the understanding of all that this event was NOT a race" but timing chips were distributed and prizes awarded for first male/female finishers on the long course. The official messaging says "Go full gas and race it — Saturday is race day with full timing" but also emphasizes the party pace option. (https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/ and https://graveleur.cc/marys-mayhem-event-page)

**Video Recap Description:** Event described as "Two days of epic riding, endless champagne gravel, cold beers, and live music at the finish—what more could you ask for? The vibes were high and we had an absolute blast." (https://graveleur.cc/features/marys-mayhem-video-recap)

**Inclusivity Focus:** Named after Mary Ann Bugg, a Worimi woman and "Australia's first female bushranger" who "single-handedly busted [Captain Thunderbolt] out of prison on Cockatoo Island by swimming across shark infested waters of Sydney Harbour with a file held between her teeth." Event organizers note "In the spirit of Mary Ann Bugg herself, Marys Mayhem is bold, inclusive, and unforgettable." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart and https://raceroster.com/events/2026/112571/marys-mayhem-2026)

**Community Size:** The broader Graveleur community has grown to "over 6,500 riders" nationally. The 2024 Marys Mayhem had "about 250 starters for the event, most doing the long course." (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart and https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)

**Regional Tourism Focus:** Event explicitly aims to "support the regional towns they pass through" with strong partnerships with local breweries (Three Tails Brewery, Small Batch Brewery), The Globe Hotel, local businesses, and tourism organizations. The founder emphasizes riders can enjoy Mudgee's "cracking food scene" and local attractions like gin distilleries and wineries. (https://www.midwesternliving.com.au/blog/marys-mayhem-gravel-grit-and-a-whole-lot-of-heart and https://thebodymechanic.com.au/marys-mayhem-gravel-ride-australia/)
