# MID SOUTH — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Joe Laverick [ELITE]:** "This weekend has been the best bike race experience of my life. A pro race, an amateur race, a concert, a party, an expo, a community. The list can go on, but Mid South is the true spirit of gravel." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Joe Laverick [ELITE]:** "We swing a right-hand turn out of neutral and immediately it's race on. There's dust everywhere. It's impossible to see. It's a race of positioning for the first half an hour to the river crossing." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Joe Laverick [ELITE]:** "I'm one of, if not the first rider not to make it across. All the top guys make the split, and with history showing the early move is usually the one that wins at Mid South, I go deep to close it. I'm fully in no-man's land. Too far behind the front group to catch it, too far ahead of the chase to sit up. I go deep, probably too deep." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Joe Laverick [ELITE]:** "The group was that awkward size. Sometimes we'd get a little gap, then it'd sit up and groups would come back. There were a few promising moves, but each time the elastic looked like it'd snap, riders would sit up, and the chase group come back." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Joe Laverick [ELITE]:** "I'm claiming it as a winning a sprint, I don't get to do many of those. I get a bear hug from organiser, Bobby Wintle. He hugs every single person who finishes the running race on Friday, and the bike race on Saturday. That's an awful lot of hugs." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Dirt Tan Bike Club author [COMPETITIVE]:** "In 10 years of racing gravel, I've never known where to fit Oklahoma. The Mid South tempted me toward Stillwater each March, but I knew the horror stories. Red mud deep enough for every letter of murder. Chains chewed. Derailleurs gargled and spat. Some springs: hypothermia." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Dirt Tan Bike Club author [COMPETITIVE]:** "Marty and I were lucky to stay in Stillwater with the family of our teammate, Sam Kiddoo. Sam couldn't come, but his dad and brother (Scott and Ean) were both racing. Scott and Michelle welcomed us to join them at their VRBO... They fed us as if we were the kings of health-conscious countries. Grilled salmon and more salmon marinated to perfect sweetness." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Dirt Tan Bike Club author [COMPETITIVE]:** "Up each climb, I repeated Bobby's invitation to keep it in the here and now. Enjoy this part right here for the joyful part that it is... I didn't need anybody's OK to be happy riding in this complicated state. That honesty about the past doesn't disqualify any of us from the joy we find in the present." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**John Borstelmann [ELITE]:** "The 30 mph crosswind gusts for most of the first hour let me convince myself that drilling it was a good idea. Careening over the red dirt prairie at record speed, working the relentless push of the wind, I attacked again and again, knowing I would be followed but hoping to soften up the contenders ahead of the decisive sections later in the race." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/the-mid-south-race-report-john-borstelmann-2023)

**John Borstelmann [ELITE]:** "The crazy tailwind for most of the second hour kept the pace high enough that the group was down to 12 by the halfway checkpoint, with no chance of anyone catching back up." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/the-mid-south-race-report-john-borstelmann-2023)

**John Borstelmann [ELITE]:** "McElveen took off early, with 600 meters to go, and I gave it everything I had. But after 25 seconds, we were still 2 blocks from the line. I was completely empty, my legs were both seized up with cramps, and all I could do was watch as he rolled into the champagne shower with time to post up." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/the-mid-south-race-report-john-borstelmann-2023)

**Anurag Saxena [UNKNOWN]:** "Friday, March 14, was an eventful day. There was a forecast of strong gusts of winds for the day, with warnings of up to 60 mph gusts. There was also a 'Red Flag warning' - meaning warm temperatures, low humidity, and high winds increase the risk of wildfires... By late evening, it was becoming highly likely that the race would be canceled. I went to bed in preparation, knowing that if things got worse overnight, there might be police knocking on our doors asking us to evacuate. At 4 a.m. I checked my phone to see that the race had been canceled." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/3/18/recap-mid-south-2025)

**Trix8806 [COMPETITIVE]:** "When we got to the expo at 3, it was just shutting down due to the 40 mph sustained winds and 60 mph gusts. Portapotty's were tipped over, tents flying, etc. very unsafe. Went back to our campground at Lake Carl Blackwell. Around 4 pm police knock on our camper door. Wild fires in the park, immediate evacuation... Campers were already burnt to the ground and fire on both sides of the road." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/mid-south-gravel-2025-thread/96156)

**Trix8806 [COMPETITIVE]:** "Happy the family is safe, got pretty scary at a few points. Super disappointed as this was my first 'big race' and conditions tomorrow were shaping up for perfection." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/mid-south-gravel-2025-thread/96156)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Early Race Positioning [ELITE perspective]:** Joe Laverick describes the opening as critical: "It's a race of positioning for the first half an hour to the river crossing. Stay far enough forward not to go into the 'washing machine' of the peloton, all while staying far enough back not to burn any matches." The river crossing at the start is a decisive moment where the front group forms. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Early Break Dynamics [ELITE]:** "History showing the early move is usually the one that wins at Mid South" - Joe Laverick notes that making or missing the early split can determine the race outcome. He went deep trying to chase back on after missing the river crossing clean. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Key Tactical Sections [ELITE]:** Joe Laverick describes bridging to an early breakaway before a "dodgy double track section" to avoid traffic and dangerous terrain: "There are what can only be described as craters on the right hand side. If you'd have hit one, it wouldn't just be a race over but it'd be a visit to the hospital." This strategic move saved energy. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Single Track Section at Mile 89 [ELITE]:** "With 15km to go, there's a single-track section where the race usually splits. I knew that I was going to lose time there, and I knew there was going to be a split. I hoped that getting a little gap would put me on the right side of that split." - Joe Laverick attempted to position ahead of this decisive section but was caught just before it. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Wind Strategy [ELITE]:** John Borstelmann used the 30 mph crosswind gusts in the first hour to attack repeatedly, aiming to "soften up the contenders." By halfway, the group was down to 12 riders. The wind direction matters significantly for race dynamics. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/the-mid-south-race-report-john-borstelmann-2023)

**Finish Sprint Timing [ELITE]:** "McElveen took off early, with 600 meters to go" on an uphill finish stretch with tailwind. John Borstelmann notes the finish is a "drawn out drag race, with the timing of the initial jump being the only useful tactic." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/the-mid-south-race-report-john-borstelmann-2023)

**Recreational Strategy Note [COMPETITIVE]:** The Dirt Tan Bike Club author focused on "riding in the here and now" and enjoying each section, a mindset Bobby Wintle encouraged pre-race. This contrasts sharply with elite tactical concerns. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**River Crossing:** Multiple sources mention the Cimarron River crossing as a critical early feature. Joe Laverick describes it as "a sweeping left downhill into the river crossing. The jostling for position gets worse as the rutty red dirt that characterises Oklahoma starts to come into play... There's a grassy line to the right that is possible to ride if you get there in a good enough position... Some people thought it'd be faster to hop back on the bike mid-grass bank. They were wrong." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Red Dirt and Dust:** Joe Laverick notes immediately after neutral: "There's dust everywhere. It's impossible to see." The characteristic red Oklahoma dirt/clay is mentioned repeatedly across sources as defining the terrain. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Double Track Section:** Joe Laverick describes a "dodgy double track section" mid-race with "craters on the right hand side. If you'd have hit one, it wouldn't just be a race over but it'd be a visit to the hospital - either for yourself or your poor bike." Getting through this section ahead of traffic was strategically valuable. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Single Track at Mile 89:** The race features "a single-track section where the race usually splits" at approximately 15km to go. Joe Laverick describes "swooping hairpin turns" and notes that short course riders are often in this section, requiring front riders to "part the sea." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Course Description (2025):** Official course details: "106.9 miles... You're in the Cross Timbers eco region, one of 12 distinct regions of Oklahoma... descending and then crawling out of the countless contours... weaving your way through Postoak and Blackjack Oak forests. These resilient, scrubby trees cling to massive sandstone ledges that will often be exposed by the sunken and shaded roadbed." (https://www.midsouthgravel.com/2025/project-one-f4fe3)

**Elevation (2025 course):** "106 miles and 800'+ of climbing" - notably different from previous years. The 100-mile route includes approximately 2,000 feet of climbing per the aid station description ("33 miles and 1900' of climbing" in one sector). (https://www.midsouthgravel.com/2025/project-one-f4fe3 and https://source-e.net/rider-resources/pre-race-meeting-the-mid-south-gravel-race/)

**Cimarron River Crossings (2025 route):** The 100-mile course crosses the Cimarron River twice: first on Pleasant Valley Rd in the fast rolling sector, and again "at mile 81... using the new highway bridge not yet open to vehicles." (https://www.midsouthgravel.com/2025/project-one-f4fe3)

**Bovine Bypass:** Near the end of the course is "the Bovine Bypass, a private land section of rowdy double track" just before the return to Stillwater. (https://www.midsouthgravel.com/2025/project-one-f4fe3)

**Specific Roads:** Multiple sources mention College Road and Triple XXX roads as "local favorites" in the Guthrie sector (miles 61-82 approximately). (https://www.midsouthgravel.com/2025/project-one-f4fe3)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mud Conditions:** Multiple sources cite red mud as a historical race-defining factor. The Dirt Tan Bike Club author mentions "horror stories. Red mud deep enough for every letter of murder. Chains chewed. Derailleurs gargled and spat." A photo caption from 2020 reads "Believe me when I say: This is a picture of a bicycle" showing a bike completely caked in mud. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Rescue Infrastructure:** "Down one street closed to traffic, we saw a long row of rear bike racks resting on their chins... This was the prepositioning of assets for a mass rescue operation. If Oklahoma's mud hit the fan, this was where teams of jeeps would come to rack up and fetch the DNFs hand over muddy fist." The race has professional extraction plans for wet years when roads become impassable. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Weather Variability:** Historical issues include "some springs: hypothermia" according to the Dirt Tan Bike Club author. A photo caption notes "Some years, you just have to enjoy your walk" showing bike-hiking in difficult conditions. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Wildfires (2025):** The 2025 race was completely cancelled due to wildfires. Trix8806 described an emergency evacuation from Lake Carl Blackwell campground with "fire blowing over the road" and "campers already burnt to the ground." The Air Quality Index at the halfway point reached 500+. (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/mid-south-gravel-2025-thread/96156)

**Mechanical Failures:** The terrain with "craters" and rough double track sections poses significant mechanical risk. Joe Laverick mentions that hitting certain craters "wouldn't just be a race over but it'd be a visit to the hospital... for your poor bike." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Equipment Failure [ELITE]:** Joe Laverick's Di2 "randomly dies in the last few kilometres" during his race, though he was able to finish. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Split Positioning [ELITE perspective]:** Missing the early split at the river crossing or the late split at the single track section can effectively end competitive aspirations, as "history showing the early move is usually the one that wins." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**The Bobby Hug:** Joe Laverick describes: "I get a bear hug from organiser, Bobby Wintle. He hugs every single person who finishes the running race on Friday, and the bike race on Saturday. That's an awful lot of hugs." This is cited across multiple sources as signature to the event. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**DFL (Dead Fucking Last) Party:** Joe Laverick: "From the first elite racer to cross the line, to the DFL (Dead Fucking Last) party for the thousandth plus rider who comes in hours later. Mid South is a party, and it's how you put a bike race on... The party goes long into the night as the DFL crosses the line some time around midnight. I'm tucked up in bed, but you can be sure that they got the biggest of hugs, and the biggest of parties." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Inclusive Atmosphere:** Joe Laverick: "World Tour winner or first time rider, every person matters at Mid South Gravel - that's why it's great... Away from the racing, Mid South really got me thinking - How the hell do I show this to the rest of the world? The US Gravel scene is unique. The event is set-up for the masses to enjoy, there just happens to be a pro-race at the front." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Pre-Race Experience:** The Dirt Tan Bike Club author describes: "A full brass band fired up and marched straight through the chute, front to back. All 1,200 riders split a tuba's width as King Cabbage shuffled past to reach a stage on our left. And those guys gave it a hefty blow." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Crowd Support:** Dirt Tan Bike Club author: "My first line skirted the chute's right edge, bringing me almost cheek to cheek with the party going on across the barrier—all cowbells and shouts. A woman I could not see screamed, 'LINK UUUUUUUN!' A second later, I spotted Jason Strohbehn in full crouch, arms flexed. 'GO GET IT, ABES!'" (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Finish Line Energy:** Joe Laverick: "After the race, everyone is hanging out at the finish line drinking beers, exchanging war stories and cheering on those further down the field... A beer gets put into my hand, and I down it instantly." (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel)

**Weekend Events:** The race weekend includes: Thursday afternoon Denim Ride (now drawing "more participants than the first few editions of the gravel race"), Friday 50km run and half-marathon, music, organized pre-rides, Saturday 100-mile and 50-mile races, expo, and parties. (https://www.joelaverick.com/p/mid-south-gravel and https://riptonco.com/blogs/rippedin-journal/the-mid-south-that-wasnt)

**Denim Ride Scale:** Ripton notes the Thursday Denim Ride with Salsa Cycles is "now a mainstay of the Mid South calendar" and has become "what has to be the world's largest gathering of denim-clad cyclists." (https://riptonco.com/blogs/rippedin-journal/the-mid-south-that-wasnt)

**Host Community Hospitality:** Dirt Tan Bike Club author stayed with local family who "treated us well... cleared the owners' suite and set us up in there so we'd have the biggest bed and a bathroom to ourselves... fed us as if we were the kings of health-conscious countries." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Recognizing People:** Dirt Tan Bike Club author: "We weren't in Stillwater 15 minutes before we were bumping into friends and acquaintances from all over: Emporia, Fairbury, Lincoln, Omaha, Beatrice, Minneapolis. These friendly greetings would keep coming all weekend... I cannot tell you how many free calories each little shout out gives a guy. All right, Abes! And, Here we go, Lincoln!" (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Bobby Wintle's Pre-Race Talk:** Dirt Tan Bike Club author: "I weirdly expected Bobby to squeeze a history lesson into his pre-race pep talk... He didn't do that. He didn't talk about Oklahoma's past at all. Instead, he urged us to spend the next hundred miles riding in the here and now—to race out there and take in the gift of the present... As much as folks love Bobby's white-hot enthusiasm, I respect him for the regard he shows Oklahoma's history." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Historical Context:** Bobby Wintle renamed the race from "Land Run" to "Mid South" after learning the fuller history of Oklahoma land runs and their impact on indigenous peoples. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**Moots Owner Perspective:** "The experience of meeting riders that love our bikes is never taken lightly by us. Here I was back in my college town where I had my first 'industry job' at a bike shop, riding alongside folks that have amazing stories from riding their Moots all over the country and beyond... The smiles, the cramps and the tales of the day were inspiring and real. This is what bikes are about for us." (https://moots.com/blogs/news/the-mid-south-gravel-a-spring-gravel-classic-and-so-much-more)

**Season Opener Status:** Bobby Wintle: "I think we know that the pros, ever since they've really started coming, have looked at Mid South as the season opener. It's the litmus test of where your fitness level is at." (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/mid-south-splits-pro-and-amateur-race-days-to-prioritise-safety-while-preserving-the-gravel-spirit)

**2026 Format Change:** Starting in 2026, Mid South will split into a Friday afternoon pro-only 100-mile race (5:15 PM finish) and Saturday morning amateur mass-start race. Bobby Wintle: "We're not doing it to garner more attention for ourselves, and we're not doing it to attract more pros. We're doing it because the pros are already showing up, and the pro numbers keep growing. So what do we do about it? How do we keep it safe? But more than anything, how do we give everything happening throughout the weekend the attention that it truly deserves?" (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/mid-south-splits-pro-and-amateur-race-days-to-prioritise-safety-while-preserving-the-gravel-spirit)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 Race Day:** Joe Laverick and Dirt Tan Bike Club author both raced in 2024 with favorable conditions. Dirt Tan Bike Club: "Looking to be a pretty perfect race day. No rain for over a week and reasonable temps." The race was characterized by significant wind. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**2024 Pre-Race Wind:** Dirt Tan Bike Club author: "Wind tomorrow is going to be wild, steady 20's-30's with gusts in the 50's. Should settle down Saturday morning." (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)

**2023 Race Day:** John Borstelmann experienced: "The 30 mph crosswind gusts for most of the first hour... The crazy tailwind for most of the second hour... the brutal headwind at mile 51... the wind had shifted around to our backs by then, and our uphill finishing stretch would be a drawn out drag race." Wind was a dominant race factor. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/the-mid-south-race-report-john-borstelmann-2023)

**2025 Cancellation:** Anurag Saxena: "Friday, March 14, was an eventful day. There was a forecast of strong gusts of winds for the day, with warnings of up to 60 mph gusts. There was also a 'Red Flag warning' - meaning warm temperatures, low humidity, and high winds increase the risk of wildfires." The expo was shut down Friday afternoon due to 40 mph sustained winds and 60 mph gusts. Wildfires broke out and the race was cancelled early Saturday morning. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/3/18/recap-mid-south-2025)

**2025 Fire Conditions Detail:** Trix8806: "When we got to the expo at 3, it was just shutting down due to the 40 mph sustained winds and 60 mph gusts. Portapotty's were tipped over, tents flying, etc. very unsafe... Around 4 pm police knock on our camper door. Wild fires in the park, immediate evacuation... The sky turned grey and then orange... the AQI at the halfway point is at 500+ right now." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/mid-south-gravel-2025-thread/96156)

**Historical Wet Conditions:** The Dirt Tan Bike Club author references "horror stories" from past years including "Red mud deep enough for every letter of murder... Some springs: hypothermia." Photos from 2020 show completely mud-caked bikes and rescue jeeps on standby. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2024/03/29/mid-south-2024-the-durability-of-joy/)
