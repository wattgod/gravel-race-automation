## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**

- **Mega Mid South**: Approximately **98% dirt roads** with "about 0% singletrack"[2][3]. The official course description states **99.9% unpaved**[3].
- **Standard Mid South 100**: Approximately **91% off-road**, with roughly 9% tarmac sections[5].

**Road Surface Characteristics**

The dirt varies significantly with weather: "fast clay hardback, loose gravel over hardpack, deeply rutted dry clay, and even sand in some areas"[2]. These "dirt roads change with the weather"[3].

**Key Course Features & Named Sectors**

- **Brethren Hill** (mile 33): Identified as the featured climb on the Mega Mid South[2].
- **Cross Timbers region**: The bulk of elevation gain comes from "countless rollers" in this section[2].
- **Gnarnia** (course mile 157 on Mega Mid South): "One-mile section of unmaintained road may be overgrown and require some hike a bike"[2]. Described as "the most technical section of the route"[2].
- **College and Triple XXX roads**: "Local favorites" described as gorgeous with "some of the fastest rollers on the route"[4].
- **Hackleman Road**: Described as "a gorgeous challenge"[4].
- **Bovine Bypass** (around mile 82): "A private land section of rowdy double track"[4].
- **Cimmarron River crossing**: Crossed using "the new highway bridge not yet open to vehicles"[4].
- **Wild Horse Creek**: Crossed during the course[4].

**Technical Difficulty**

- Mega Mid South: Rated **6 out of 10** difficulty using the Bikepacking.com rubric[3]. Technical difficulty is rated "Fair" (4/10)[2]. Physical demand is rated "Difficult" (7/10)[2].
- Standard Mid South 100: Described as a **Black Diamond expert route**[1].

**Elevation Profile**

- **High point**: 1,222 feet (Mega Mid South)[2]; 1,035 feet (standard 100-mile route)[1].
- **Starting elevation**: 899 feet[1].
- **Minimum elevation**: 835 feet[1].
- **Grade characteristics**: Max grade -9.9%, min grade 15% (note: these figures appear inconsistent in the source data)[1].
- **Distance breakdown**: 62 miles climbing, 39 miles descending, 1 mile flat on the standard route[1].

## CLIMATE & CONDITIONS

**Typical Weather for Race Season**

The search results reference the race taking place with weather conditions including sun and wind exposure. One source notes "It's been a hot, hot summer here in OK and these roads will dry you out!"[3], suggesting warm conditions.

**Historical Weather Incidents**

- **Mud**: "The Mid South is known for the mud. The mud here is particularly sticky, and a few years back, many riders had to abandon the race as they couldn't get their wheels to spin due to mud!"[5]. The course description warns: "If there is any sustained rainfall, much of the western side of the route could be impassable mud"[2].
- **Wildfires**: The 2025 Mid South 100 was cancelled due to wildfires. Chase (a rider) "chose to stay in town to support the relief" when the event was cancelled[3].
- **Wind exposure**: "The first 100 miles are the most challenging due to the sun and wind exposure in the areas between Stillwater and Carney"[2].

**Altitude**

Starting elevation: 899 feet. Maximum elevation: 1,035-1,222 feet depending on route. This is relatively low elevation riding in the High Plains/Cross Timbers region.

## COMMUNITY & REPUTATION

**Race History**

The event was **originally called the Land Run 100**. "The first Land Run 100, now known as The Mid South linked 107 miles of red, rutted roads and featured more than 6,000 feet of climbing with Carney, Oklahoma as its halfway point. 121 riders braved the course that first year"[5]. The **Mega Mid South is the result of over 10 years of planning and route building** for The Mid South, with the inaugural grand depart event recently held[3].

**Founder/Promoter**

The race is based at **District Bicycles in Stillwater, Oklahoma**, which appears to be the primary organizer[3].

**Notable Past Winners & Notable Riders**

- **Jill Martindale**: Holds the Mega Mid South fastest known time (FKT) of **25 hours 12 minutes**[3].
- **Ted King**: Attempted an FKT on the Mega Mid South with support from rider Chase, who "started an hour after Ted" to provide support[3].
- **Luke Hall**: Mentioned as "a heck of a strong rider" encountered during one race report[5].

**Community Perception**

The race is described as featuring "picturesque farmland scenery and raucous gravel roads offering riders a rewarding challenge"[3]. It is characterized as "a stunning and challenging 300-mile gravel bikepacking loop"[3]. The course is designed to "highlight the unique landscapes of Oklahoma"[3]. One reviewer called it a "perennial route"[6], suggesting it has established status in the gravel calendar.

**Equipment Recommendations**

"We recommend a tire of at least 38mm setup tubeless"[3]. The rationale: "As with any Mid South route, these roads are ever changing. The route is 99.9% unpaved and the dirt will change with the weather conditions"[3].

**Water/Hydration Emphasis**

"Don't underestimate your need for water. It's been a hot, hot summer here in OK and these roads will dry you out!"[3].

**DNF Rates**

Not specified in available search results, though historical mud conditions have caused riders to abandon: "many riders had to abandon the race as they couldn't get their wheels to spin due to mud!"[5].

**Difficulty Insights**

Course difficulty is heavily dependent on pace and weather: "Weather and road conditions will drastically affect the physical difficulty. If there is any sustained rainfall, much of the western side of the route could be impassable mud. Pace will also greatly affect the difficulty. Doing this route in three days will be far more challenging than breaking it into five days, even for a fit rider"[2].

**Notable Landscape & Cultural Features**

The course passes through areas with historical significance, including **Mulhall, Oklahoma, the namesake of Lucille Mulhall, "a competition cowgirl in early Oklahoma Territory. She was the first woman to compete in roping, and to perform in and produce rodeos"**[4]. The landscape features "Eastern Red Cedar, a species that has become dominant in the area with the elimination of natural of prairie fire" and "Northern Harrier and Redtail hawk" habitat[4].
