# MIGRATION GRAVEL RACE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

**Haley Hunter Smith [ELITE] (2025 Winner):** "Her full-suspension MTB, fitted with a gravel handlebar—a so-called «gravelized Supercaliber»—proved perfect for the rugged Maasai terrain." **Maddy Nutt [ELITE] (2024 & 2025 Podium Finisher):** "Migration gravel race is without doubt the highlight of my cycling career to date... On Stage 2: "I crossed the line in 3rd in the women's field, with a smile across my face declaring it to be the best bike course I have ever ridden. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/migration-gravel-race-results-sarah-sturm-and-hans-becking-celebrate-overall-victory-in-the-maasai-mara/)

**Federico Damiani [ELITE] (2024):** During Stage 1 mud section: "Porca troia!" (Italian expletive showing frustration with conditions). (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Ian Boswell [ELITE] (Unbound Winner):** "The hardest thing for me was probably Stage 1 because it was so early in the race and I had a puncture cause the course was so… and of course I didn't realize what this course was gonna be so the first day was really the toughest, just accepting what we were racing across… This race was completely different from anything I've ever done. But in hindsight, a gravel bike is able to ride all these surfaces, it's just a mindset, being able to accept the conditions and not try to fight it cause that's when you start to have a miserable time." **Ian Boswell [ELITE] (continued):** "I think what I've learned is that bicycles and humans are much more resilient than we think… We shouldn't complain as much as we do about the road or the terrain, or the adversity we face, seeing some of the African riders and what they did just to be able to finish is inspiring, coming from a pro background you want everything to be perfect you know, the bike should be able to shift and brake, everything should be working perfectly, you complain if it's not, these guys don't complain, they just get off and keep riding, it's very inspiring to see." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/migration-gravel-race/)

**Samuel Kagiri [ELITE] (African rider, 2023):** "I think it is a big privilege for African riders like me to ride in such races. **Nancy Akinyi Debe [ELITE] (2021 Women's Winner):** "It's a big event and the Kenyans are able to, especially the men, they're able to show that they can race against the big boys, and they can see the level they need to work towards... For the future, I know what I need to do and where I need to improve. Especially the simple things, like how to set up the bike, the tyre pressure, to check the route and feeding on the bike." **Suleman Kangangi [ELITE] (2021 2nd Place, Kenyan rider):** "I think the MGR was very important in more ways than one, especially for the African riders. (https://journal.brooksengland.com/migration-gravel-race-followup/)

**David Millar [ELITE] (2024):** "Everything comes at a price, money is the cheapest way to get the lowest kind of experience, blood, sweat and tears, flat tires and broken souls, that's the price here I guess and it is still a bargain." **Nathan Haas [COMPETITIVE] (Former WorldTour rider):** Listed Migration as bucket list: "OK, this is absolutely on my bucket list. It's a four-day stage race and the racing looks intense, but even more hardcore is the way in which you travel around. You sleep in tents (provided and set up by the organization every night)." ## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Haley Hunter Smith (2025 Winner):** Full-suspension MTB with gravel handlebar ("gravelized Supercaliber") - specifically noted as "perfect for the rugged Maasai terrain." Won 3 out of 4 stages with this setup. (https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

**[ELITE] Factor OSTRO Gravel Setup (David Millar & Rob Gitelis, 2024):**
- Frame: Factor OSTRO Gravel (rose gold with teal fork signature edition)
- Wheels: Black Inc 34 gravel aero wheels
- Tires: Schwalbe Overland 45mm front/rear (Rob) OR Vittoria Terreno Dry 47mm front/45mm rear (David)
- Tire pressure: "approximately 25 psi" with foam inserts
- Bars: Black Inc Integrated Aero Barstem system with double roll bar tape
- Saddles: WTB Ti Silverado (David, prioritizing comfort, same as his MTB) OR Selle Italia SLR 3D (Rob)
- Pedals: SPD, David used MTB trail pedals for bigger platform
- Bags: Saddlebag rescue pack, top tube frame bag bolted to frame, third bottle cage mount under down tube for gilet/arm warmers
- Hydration: USWE hydration packs (3 liters total water carrying capacity)
- Kit: CHPT3 SCC3 shorts and jersey (lightweight road aero kit with cargo pockets on shorts)
- Protection: POC OMNE helmet and Devour glasses with face shield
(https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one)

**[UNKNOWN] Chris McCormack (triathlon legend, 2024):** Used a mountain bike from 2012, never unboxed until week before race. "Neither has a bike computer, nor do they even own one." Also unaware of tubeless tires and proper tire pressure (was running 80 psi instead of ~25 psi). Bar-ends on mountain bike. This setup represents what NOT to do. (https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one)

**[ELITE] Maddy Nutt's observations:** "My stomach was certainly not used to the food I was feeding it, but I knew that it was better to suffer slightly with an unhappy stomach if it meant I was getting enough fuel in me for the racing. With days over 8 hours in the saddle, this didn't leave too much of the day for faffing before the next stage." Notes the need for both bike and body maintenance in self-supported format. (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

## Weather Experienced

**2024 Edition - Stage 1 (Pre-race):** Heavy rain the evening before Stage 1. "As the organizers started wrapping up their speech, a heavy rain began. We didn't know the impact it would have, but it was clear that the terrain would not dry on time." This created thick mud/clay conditions throughout Stage 1. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**2024 Edition - Stage 2:** "The camp was located at 2,300 meters of elevation... It was quite cold, with thick fog that only went away a few minutes before the start." Rain during the night before Stage 3, with riders discovering they left kit and shoes outside to dry. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**2024 Edition - Stage 3:** "We've just got now. The weather gods were on our side, and the forecast was no clouds, mild temperatures, and a tailwind all the way." First half of stage had tailwind that turned to headwind on return loop. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**2024 Edition - General:** "The region lies on the equator and is at an average altitude of 1,900 metres." Race characterized by "intense heat and heavy rainfall" combination. (https://journal.brooksengland.com/migration-gravel-race/)

**2025 Edition - Stage 1:** "The vast savannah, lined with red dirt roads and scattered acacia trees, offered little shelter from the crosswinds that would shape the day." (https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

**2025 Edition - Stage 2:** "With over 2,000 meters of climbing and 170 kilometers of racing, the second stage through the Loita Hills broke legs and broke plans... the long climbs and high heat" were noted as decisive factors. (https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Stage 1 - Mud Section (2024):** "The thick mud we found at around km 20 would put us even more out of our comfort zone. The first patches of mud were ok and could be avoided by choosing a different line. Not long after, there was no possible choice. We had to ride, or walk, through something that was more like clay, with our wheels unable to keep turning." Described as requiring hike-a-bike through knee-deep mud. After mud: "block headwinds, which lasted for more than two hours." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Stage 1 - Opening Section:** "After about half an hour, I passed Ian Boswell fixing a flat on the side of the road." First 15km in 2024 saw "15 punctures or so, in less than 5 km into the race." Described as "a 50 km section of Paris Roubaix on rocks. Imagine each cobble has a faceted side as sharp as a blade." One rider described it as "30km of Carrefour de l'Arbre on rocks." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/, https://www.reddit.com/r/gravelcycling/comments/1dn7dj3/brutal_gravel_wildebeests/)

**Stage 2 Climb (2024):** "The first half was on smooth gravel and the gradients were mellow, but as soon as we turned right after water stop 1 it got steeper and through narrower paths." 18km main climb starting at 1,800m reaching summit at 2,630m. "It was a relentless up and down with barely any moment to recover." After the climb: "50 km long section of singletracks and double tracks, including several technical segments and water crossings... we honestly think it was a bit too much for bikes without suspension." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Stage 2 Obstacles:** "It wasn't until water stop 2, located at kilometer 114, that we finally found proper gravel paths, but the hurdles did not stop there as this time what prevented us from keeping a constant pace were the many cows and sheep on our way." 50% of Leopard riders took the escape route on Stage 2 due to difficulty. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Stage 3 (2024):** "Stage 3 was mainly gravel, the real gravel. That allowed for higher speeds and therefore more smiles during the day." Described as a loop course with tailwind first half, headwind second half. "Several consecutive short climbs in the latter part of the stage." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Stage 3 (2025):** "Perhaps the most tactically complex day, Stage 3 was a mix of narrow singletrack, cattle crossings and unpredictable river fords. Riders waded through knee-deep water, navigated herds, and dodged thorn bushes as nature took center stage." Called the "mud stage" after serious rainfall. (https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

**General Terrain Description:** "The course itself also presented many challenges, with several riders finding it particularly technical in certain sectors. Beyond the technical single track descents, which I found to be exciting and enjoyable, albeit a little on the cusp of gravel/MTB territory, the terrain was incredibly rough in parts, which meant that the actual riding gave your whole body a hammering. This was without a doubt, the most challenging terrain I've ridden in a race." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

**Wildlife Encounters:** "Riding through an amazing landscape and passing wildebeests, zebras, giraffes, antelopes." Riders regularly pass wildlife on course. "We literally just stopped to look at an elephant" during transfer. "There was a lion yesterday not far from camp... About 500 meters." (https://bikepacking.com/plog/migration-gravel-race-kenya/, https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one)

**Road Surface:** "This laterite ground develops over time thanks to a combination of intense heat and heavy rainfall. The sesquioxides of iron and aluminium are what gives the ground its rich colour, and the dust which will no doubt coat the racers' bikes." Characterized by "rough, hard packed gravel trails, stretches of tricky singletrack and the continent's famous red clay roads." (https://journal.brooksengland.com/migration-gravel-race/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Maddy Nutt's Pacing Strategy:** "My target for the week was a strong podium finish, and I knew that I needed to control my enthusiasm to race fast each day and pace my efforts across the week, so on the third day I reeled in my effort towards the end of the stage, thinking towards the final stage (188km) the next day." On Stage 4: "With no further stages to pace towards, I completely gave it me all from the start of the stage and slipped away from the women's field through a rough and muddy section... Crossing the line over half an hour before the next woman." Took 31 minutes out of 2nd place on final stage to move from 4th to 2nd overall. (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

**[ELITE] Stage Winning Times (2025, for reference):**
- Men's overall: Lukas Baum 14:57:12 (650km, 4 stages)
- Women's overall: Haley Hunter Smith 17:57:54 (650km, 4 stages)
Note: These are elite times; recreational riders should expect significantly longer. (https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

**[ELITE] Logistics & Daily Schedule:** "Start times as early as 6:30am, which required pre 5am alarms." Self-supported race requires efficiency: "With days over 8 hours in the saddle, this didn't leave too much of the day for faffing before the next stage, so efficiency definitely was key." Daily routine: "Alarm at 5.15 AM. Queue for breakfast. Fill bidons and hydration pack. Get changed. Warm-up (this part only lasted for a day, as for the following stages we squeezed our time in the tent)." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt, https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**[ELITE] Altitude Adaptation:** "The race was all at altitudes above 2000m, with climbing on stage 2 taking us closer to 3000m. This presented another challenge to riders, with less oxygen in the air severely impacting the ability to push out power. I found that initially my body struggled with the altitude, but that it quickly adapted and I was no longer as conscious of how it inhibited my performance." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

**[ELITE] Nutrition Challenges:** "Another challenge faced by participants was adapting to the food, and simply consuming enough to fuel the racing and recovery each day, as well as being careful with consuming only clean water. My stomach was certainly not used to the food I was feeding it, but I knew that it was better to suffer slightly with an unhappy stomach if it meant I was getting enough fuel in me for the racing." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

**[ELITE] Race Mentality:** Ian Boswell: "Race is gone but well, I can enjoy now!" (after early flat). David Millar on approach: "We're not here to race. Rob and I are here for the experience. We want to have the bandwidth to appreciate and absorb everything that happens." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/, https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanicals - Stage 1:** "15 punctures or so, in less than 5 km into the race, even the best riders end up still for a while." Sharp rock section claimed multiple victims early. Ian Boswell lost significant time to early puncture. Lukas Baum punctured with 25km to go on Stage 4 but recovered. (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/, https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

**Mechanicals - Catastrophic:** "On day 2, I broke my rear hanger which forced me to ride single speed, it was a bit challenging cause the course started to get really hilly after 160 km, that's when I told myself: I have fought all the way to here, I can't quit 15 km from the line." Samuel Kagiri finished Stage 2 single-speed after hanger failure. (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/migration-gravel-race/)

**Escape Routes:** "50% of the Leopard riders followed this escape route" on Stage 2 due to extreme difficulty. Organizers "facilitated by the organizers, aware of the toughness of the stage." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Stage 2 Attrition:** "At this point fatigue from earlier stages appeared in riders who had taken the first few days harder." Stage 2 described as where "The peloton fractured early as the ascents took their toll." (https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/, https://gravelearthseries.com/2025/06/25/four-days-through-the-wild-the-spirit-of-migration-gravel/)

**Cumulative Fatigue:** "With four stages, this was definitely something that added to cumulative fatigue." The rough terrain "gave your whole body a hammering" across multiple days. (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

**Food/Water Safety:** Need to be "careful with consuming only clean water" and adapting to unfamiliar food. Risk of food poisoning mentioned by one rider who had to stop every half hour. (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt, https://www.albioncycling.com/log/no-word-for-stranger-the-migration-gravel-race/)

**Wildlife Risk:** "As we're traversing an area that is home to a huge amount of wildlife, it would be too dangerous to ride at night." Maasai protection needed. "There was a lion yesterday not far from camp... About 500 meters." Buffalo noted as particularly dangerous: "the buffalo is stupid, he charges. all the time, no matter what, no matter why he's stupid and charges all the time!" (https://bikepacking.com/plog/migration-gravel-race-kenya/, https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one, https://www.albioncycling.com/log/no-word-for-stranger-the-migration-gravel-race/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Cultural Connection:** "At points this was even rather overwhelming and I found myself tearing up behind my sunglasses, as I was cheered by huge crowds of children as I pushed my body to its limits." Constant greetings: "riding through the Mara, we had constant children cheers of 'jambo' (hello) and were only greeted with huge enthusiasm and celebration." Along Stage 3 route: "there were thousands of kids shouting 'Jambo!', making our day on the saddle even more pleasant." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt, https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Podium Ceremony:** "The three of us stood grinning but rather overwhelmed, as the Maasai warriors dressed us in their blankets and celebrated our result with chanting." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt)

**Maasai Cultural Insight:** "There is no word for stranger in the Maasai language." This philosophy permeates the race atmosphere. Maasai community integral to race: "As a result, AMANI consult Maasai tribesman to make them part of the process and help in the conservation of these unspoiled lands." (https://www.albioncycling.com/log/no-word-for-stranger-the-migration-gravel-race/, https://journal.brooksengland.com/migration-gravel-race/)

**Camaraderie:** "On crossing the line, I was overwhelmed by hugs and celebrations from friends new and old, and those who had believed more in my abilities than myself!" Chad Haga working for Mattia de Marchi on Stage 4: "I knew I was out of the GC race but Mattia has worked super hard all week... I can call him a friend now and I wanted to do everything I could." (https://www.parcours.cc/blogs/news/migration-gravel-race-report-contributed-by-maddy-nutt, https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/migration-gravel-race-results-sarah-sturm-and-hans-becking-celebrate-overall-victory-in-the-maasai-mara/)

**Post-Race Sentiment:** "From Cape Epic champions to former WorldTour pros, everyone who competed at this year's Migration Gravel Race said that the event was one of the hardest they'd ever done. Nevertheless, the bonds forged over four days of riding, camping, and commiserating in the Maasai Mara helped ease the painful parts. As the Maasai men who worked as motorcycle drivers and support staff gave their final blessing by the Mara River on Friday evening, smiles trumped scars." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/migration-gravel-race-results-sarah-sturm-and-hans-becking-celebrate-overall-victory-in-the-maasai-mara/)

**Life-Changing Experience:** One previous participant: "I asked how it was. 'The most amazing experience of my life,' he said. I asked why. 'It's simply incredible: wildebeest running next to you, elephants crossing in front…' He was racing, yet he quickly understood it was so much more than a race." (https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one)

**Mission & Purpose:** "The aim of the Amani Project is, simply put, to help create racing opportunities for cyclists from East Africa. With the MGR, we thought we'd turn the tables on the traditional model of sending a select few (East African riders) to a pressure-packed European race environment and instead have them enjoy home-field advantage for once and invite the best from the West over to race in Kenya." (https://www.albioncycling.com/log/no-word-for-stranger-the-migration-gravel-race/)

**Camping Experience:** "You sleep in tents (provided and set up by the organization every night). You're treated by cultural experiences around campfires in the night." Camp setup: "Mechanics, masseurs and all the local Maasai community involved in looking after us at the camp would spend the rest of the day, and a big share of the night, making sure that we recover as much as possible from the effort exerted." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/blogs/nathan-haas/nathan-haas-2023-gravel-guide-for-new-races-toughest-races-and-wild-adventures/, https://rawcyclingmag.com/migration-gravel-race-2024-when-finishing-is-a-remarkable-feat/)

**Transfer Adventure:** "We've been on the road for seven and a half hours, the last two and a half at a crawl on rain-damaged dirt roads. We've been stuck twice, seen baboons, zebras, and warthogs... And we literally just stopped to look at an elephant. This is a very special transfer... Made it to camp, eight and a half hours later. Two trucks got stuck... The Migration Race hasn't even started, and I already feel like we've been on an adventure." (https://factorbikes.com/news/the-migration-diaries-part-one)
