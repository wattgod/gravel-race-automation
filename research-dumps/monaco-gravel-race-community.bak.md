## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Jan Stöckli [UNKNOWN]:** "The course was brutal — 120 kilometers with 2,600 meters of climbing. A true mountain test... the relentless vibrations and shocks during the climbs were tough on the muscles." He noted that "the field was amazingly strong on the ascents." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-monaco-gravel-race-uci)

The course starts from Peille Village at 650 meters elevation and features the iconic Col de Braus (famous from Tour de France 2024), tackled on gravel tracks. The climb is divided into two parts: Col des Boeufs with views of Mercantour mountains, followed by the Farghet Plateau through forest. After the Col de Braus summit, riders encounter "a series of exhilarating descents, both on asphalt roads and gravel tracks" including the Col du Castillon and passing near Saint Agnès (the highest hilltop village on the Mediterranean coast). (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/monaco/)

The finish includes "the same 8km loop that was used in the start." The route goes through Mercantour National Park with "farmland and sandy trails along the Guadiana River." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/monaco/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Jan Stöckli [UNKNOWN]:** Started strong staying with the front group on the first climb and was fighting for 5th place. "After about a lap and a half, the effort caught up with me. I started to fade and couldn't maintain the early pace... from then on, my only goal was to make it to the finish." This suggests the race gets extremely demanding after ~60km. Pre-race nutrition: "pasta for me, muesli for Simon" at 7:00 a.m. (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-monaco-gravel-race-uci)

**Elite-level pacing [ELITE]:** Mads Wurtz Schmidt finished in 3:51:32 (120km with 2400m climbing), averaging roughly 31.10 km/h. Lukas Pöstlberger was 3:43 back at 3:55:15. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/04/22/uci-monaco-gravel-results-schmidt-aguirre-dominate-to-earn-solo-victories-and-qualify-for-worlds/)

**Elite women's pacing [ELITE]:** Morgan Aguirre finished in 4:35:55, with a 5-minute advantage over second place Nicole Frain (4:40:53). (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/04/22/uci-monaco-gravel-results-schmidt-aguirre-dominate-to-earn-solo-victories-and-qualify-for-worlds/)

## Race Logistics & Planning

**Jan Stöckli [UNKNOWN]:** Travel complications were significant. Planned Friday departure was delayed when "the Simplon Pass was closed. After hours of monitoring road conditions, we decided to stay the night in Valais and leave early the next morning — 4:30 a.m. sharp." Even with the early Saturday departure, they "finally reached the course in Monaco by midday," highlighting the remote location. They did a 60km recon loop Saturday afternoon. (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-monaco-gravel-race-uci)

The race offers a shorter 83km course (1800m climbing) for men over 60 and women over 50 as the qualifier distance, using two local 33km laps instead of the northern section. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/monaco/)

## Historical & Cultural Context

Peille has a fascinating historical connection to Monaco: "In the 12th century, Peille held dominion over a vast territory, including the Rock of Monaco. In 1179, Peille ceded its rights over Monaco to Genoa, marking a turning point in the history of the Principality." From the 14th century until the French Revolution, Peille was under the rule of the House of Savoy. Princess Grace of Monaco often sought solace in the princely estates of Rocagel and Fontbonne, located within Peille's territory. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/monaco/)

The village is described as "a captivating medieval village" perched on a rocky outcrop with "narrow, winding streets, ancient stone houses, and impressive fortifications" providing "stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and the Mediterranean coast." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/monaco/)
