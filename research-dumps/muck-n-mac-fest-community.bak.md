## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Composition:** The event name itself describes the terrain: "Muck" refers to mud, grit, and gravel roads; "Mac" refers to tarmac sections. The routes consist of approximately 65% gravel sections and 35% tarmac. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Terrain Mix:** Routes include "muddy forest trails, technical tracks, and country roads, all topped off with steep gradients" with "a mix of technical single tracks, endless forest trails, country roads without a soul." (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Location Characteristics:** The Tweed Valley is described as having "remote forest roads, flowing gravel descents, and hidden singletrack" through "ancient woodlands" and "high moorland passes." The area hosted the 2023 UCI Cycling World Championships. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Specific Features:** The courses pass through "farmed land" with gates that need to be closed after passing through. Riders encounter woodland trails including "Lady Louisa's walk which takes you past the Quair water and on to the River Tweed." (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Route Format:** The event uses an "enduro-style" format with multiple timed stages (3-4 per day depending on distance option) interspersed with "linking zones" or transfer stages between segments where riders can recover and socialize. (https://outsiderevents.eventrac.co.uk/e/muck-n-mac-fest-13569)

**Elevation Details:**
- Long Course Weekend (75km): Approximately 1,600m elevation gain per day
- Short Course Weekend (45km): Less elevation, specific numbers not provided
- BORDER.200: 200km with 3,500m elevation gain total
- Day Rider Pass: 1,700m ascent noted
(https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Festival Atmosphere:** Community ratings indicate 87% of reviewers describe the atmosphere as "Small & Intimate" rather than large festival vibes, and 83% describe the location as "Remote Nature." (https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Challenge Level:** 74% of reviewers rated the event as "Challenging" in terms of difficulty. (https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Event Entertainment:** The weekend includes "an eclectic mix of live music on the Saturday night," outdoor film screenings, outdoor yoga classes, breathwork sessions, nutrition talks, massage services, and a ceilidh (Scottish traditional dance). (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Family-Friendly:** Children under 14 can enter for free with a "full programme of kids activities both on and off the bike." Activities include bike workshops and games. The venue features a maze on the grounds of Traquair House for all weekend ticket holders. (https://www.mucknmacfest.co.uk/)

**Venue Appeal:** The event is staged at Traquair House, "Scotland's oldest inhabited house" (900 years old), with camping available in the grounds. The house has "Bear Gates" that have been closed since 1745 when the 5th Earl vowed not to reopen them until a Stuart returned to the British throne. The grounds include "ancient woodlands" and "some of the oldest yew trees in Scotland." (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Local Culture:** The venue includes "the oldest operating brewery in the United Kingdom" (brewing since 1965) on-site at Traquair House. (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Camping Experience:** Multiple reviewers praised camping facilities with "clean toilets and hot showers," described as "excellent gravel weekend! Everything was well thought of." A "quiet zone" is available for those who want to sleep away from the music. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Accessibility:** The event has partnered with Adaptive Riders Collective (ARC) to provide accessible routes for off-road adaptive cycles (40km on Saturday, two 20km guided rides on Sunday), with accessible paths throughout the camping area and event village. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Overall Ratings:** The event has a 4.4/5 overall rating based on 24 reviews, with particularly high marks for:
- Supporting Staff: 4.88/5
- Event Safety: 4.79/5
- Event Logistics: 4.71/5
- Facilities: 4.67/5
- Pre-event Communication: 4.67/5
Lower ratings noted for:
- Swag: 2.00/5 (only 6 ratings)
- Aid Stations: 3.87/5
(https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Sustainability Focus:** The event emphasizes environmental sustainability with a "leave no trace" policy. Reviewers rated sustainability at 3.89/5. The organizers stress: "Absolutely no littering, under any circumstances" and respect for landowners and other trail users. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Format Understanding [ALL LEVELS]:** The event uses a gravel enduro format rather than traditional mass-start racing. Riders complete timed stages with untimed transfer sections between them, allowing for recovery and social riding between competitive segments. (https://outsiderevents.eventrac.co.uk/e/muck-n-mac-fest-13569)

**Pacing Note [UNKNOWN LEVEL - from Mark's review]:** One rider noted being able to "take twice as long to get value for money," suggesting the format accommodates a wide range of pacing abilities without time pressure between stages. The event works well "for the pros and great for people like me who take twice as long." (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Course Timing:** Start times vary by format:
- BORDER.200: 07:00 start on Saturday
- Long/Short Weekender: 08:00 starts
- Day 1 ride window: 09:00-10:30
- Course sweeper arrival: 17:00
- First finishers expected: 12:00
(https://www.outsiderevents.com/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Stage Structure:** 
- Long Course Weekend: 75km per day with multiple enduro-style timed stages (Day 1 has one large circuit, Saturday includes 3 timed enduro stages)
- Short Course Weekend: 40-45km per day with 4 timed stages total
(https://outsiderevents.eventrac.co.uk/e/muck-n-mac-fest-13569)

## Race Options & Entry Details

**Distance Options for 2026:**
1. **Full Weekend Long (75km/day):** ~150km total, 1,600m elevation per day, timed enduro stages both days, includes festival access and camping - £120-125
2. **Full Weekend Short (45km/day):** ~80km total, 4 timed stages, includes festival access and camping - £110
3. **BORDER.200:** 200km single-day challenge on Saturday, 3,500m elevation, GPS tracking provided - £72-80 (Saturday only) or £110-125 (includes Sunday long course)
4. **Day Rider Pass:** Single day option (Saturday), choice of short or long course - £60
5. **Adaptive Riding:** 40km on Saturday with 1 timed stage, 20km options on Sunday - £80
6. **Festival Village Pass:** Non-riding option for festival access only - £35
(https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest and https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Entry Includes:**
- Marked course both days
- Electronic timing for stages
- GPS tracking (BORDER.200 only)
- Festival village access
- Parking and weekend camping
- Shower access
- Event goodie bag
- GPX files for both days
- Safety and first aid teams
- Public liability insurance
(https://outsiderevents.eventrac.co.uk/e/muck-n-mac-fest-13569)

**Entry Limits:** Full weekend riding entries are limited to 400 spaces; day riding limited to 50 per day; BORDER.200 limited to 200 riders. (https://outsiderevents.eventrac.co.uk/e/muck-n-mac-fest-13569)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Course Navigation [ALL LEVELS]:** Community ratings show course navigation scored 4.12/5, with some noting navigation could be improved. GPX files are provided for both days, and the course uses marking plus self-navigation materials. (https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Aid Stations [ALL LEVELS]:** Reviewers rated aid stations at 3.87/5, noting this as one of the lower-rated aspects of the event. Riders should plan accordingly for self-sufficiency between stations. (https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Bike Type:** Event is specifically designed for gravel bikes on mixed terrain (65% gravel, 35% tarmac). The terrain includes "muddy forest trails, technical tracks" which would require appropriate gravel tire clearance and tread. (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)
