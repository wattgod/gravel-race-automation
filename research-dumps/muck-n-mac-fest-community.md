## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The course is described as **65% gravel/muck sections and 35% tarmac** across the routes. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

The terrain includes a mix of **muddy forest trails, technical tracks, and country roads, all with steep gradients**. (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

Riders experience **remote forest roads, flowing gravel descents, and hidden singletrack** through the Tweed Valley. The area features **ancient woodlands** and **high moorland passes**. (https://www.mucknmacfest.co.uk/gravelriding)

The area is described as having **centuries-old forests** and the **famous Tweed Valley**, which hosted the **UCI Mountain Bike World Championships in 2023**. (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Course Format [ALL LEVELS]:** The weekend features an **enduro-style format with timed stages** interspersed with **linking/transfer zones** where riders can chat, enjoy scenery, and catch their breath. There are **2 timed stages per day** (for weekender formats), with the Saturday taking **3 timed enduro stages**. (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest, https://outsiderevents.eventrac.co.uk/e/muck-n-mac-fest-13569)

**Distance Options:**
- **Short Course Weekender:** ~45km per day (80km total over weekend)
- **Long Course Weekender:** ~75km per day (150km total), with **1,600m elevation gain per day**
- **BORDER.200:** 200km single day challenge with **3,500m elevation gain** (Saturday only)
- **BORDER.200 Weekender:** 200km Saturday + 70km Sunday
(https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest, https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Pacing Consideration [ALL LEVELS]:** One rider noted the format is "Great for the pros and great for people like me who take twice as long to get value for money," suggesting the timed stage format accommodates varying speeds while keeping everyone engaged. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The event consistently receives high marks for atmosphere. According to Racecheck ratings: **87% of reviews describe the atmosphere as "Small & Intimate"** with **83% noting the "Remote Nature" location**. (https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Overall rating: 4.4/5 based on 24 reviews**, with particularly high marks for:
- Supporting Staff: 4.88/5
- Event Safety: 4.79/5
- Event Logistics: 4.71/5
- Facilities: 4.67/5
(https://racecheck.com/races/muck-n-mac-fest/)

**Festival Elements Beyond Riding:**
- **Live music** on Saturday night (eclectic mix including blues, rock, electro)
- **Outdoor film screenings**
- **Yoga and breathwork sessions** in the morning
- **Massage services** available
- **Nutrition talks**
- **Finnish saunas**
- **Ceilidh** (Scottish traditional dance - particularly praised by riders)
- **Local catering** with pizza, ice cream, and coffee mentioned specifically
- **Bar areas** with DJ sets
(https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest, https://www.mucknmacfest.co.uk/)

**Family-Friendly:** All kids **under 14 enter free** with a **full programme of kids activities** both on and off the bike. The venue includes access to **Traquair House grounds** with a **maze** available to explore. (https://www.mucknmacfest.co.uk/)

**Camping:** Free weekend camping included with all weekend tickets, with **clean toilets and hot showers** available. A **quiet camping zone** is available for early-to-bed attendees away from music areas. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest, https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Unique Venue:** Staged at **Traquair House**, described as **Scotland's oldest continuously inhabited house** (900+ years old), with legendary **Bear Gates closed since 1745** when the 5th Earl vowed not to reopen them until a Stuart returned to the British throne - they remain closed today. The house also features the **oldest operating brewery in the United Kingdom** (since 1965). (https://www.finishers.com/en/event/muck-n-mac-fest)

**Inclusivity:** The event has partnered with **Adaptive Riders Collective (ARC)** to provide accessible 40km routes suitable for off-road adaptive cycles, with **accessible paths throughout camping and event village** and ARC support staff on hand. (https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)

## Sustainability & Course Etiquette

The event emphasizes **"Leave No Trace" principles** with strict policies:
- Be cautious of landowners and other trail users
- Respect privacy and working livelihoods of locals
- **Close all gates** after yourself when passing through farmed land
- Act with care for your own safety and others
- **Absolutely no littering under any circumstances**
(https://www.timeoutdoors.com/events/muck-n-mac-fest)
