# NATCHEZ TRACE GRAN FONDO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**The Natchez Trace as a Training/Racing Venue [UNKNOWN]:** The Natchez Trace Parkway itself is described as "a lot like riding the trainer. 50' of grass on both sides then trees. Up a roller, down a roller. Repeat til you get to Fly where it starts to get flatter." The Trace is noted as being "good in the winter cause there is very little shade and most of it is on a ridge which makes it warmer" compared to valley roads which "can be brutal." (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/recent-move-to-nashville-tn/597008)

**Paulo_Sousa [UNKNOWN]** recommends: "From that area you can get to Natchez Trace very easily. That's the best riding in the area." (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/recent-move-to-nashville-tn/597008)

**hammydad [UNKNOWN]** notes: "The Trace is probably good for riding in the aerobars" but suggests "the best riding is either the backroads surrounding the Trace (particularly Natchez Trace Rd and Liepers Creek Rd both of which roughly parallel Natchez Trace Parkway and have good offshoots and connectors." Specific route suggestions include taking "the trace to Hwy 96 then get off and head south on either Old Hwy 96 or Waddell Hollow then work your way past Lieper's Fork." (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/recent-move-to-nashville-tn/597008)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Gran Fondo Bike Shop (Nashville area) [UNKNOWN]:** Community feedback on the Gran Fondo bike shop near Bellevue suggests it's described as a "Snobbish roadie shop but they did solid work" and "A little pricey." Another comment notes: "They are snobs and probably the only bike shop that can survive even when they sell over retail price." However, Paulo_Sousa clarifies his review "was not negative… and it was quite accurate." (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/recent-move-to-nashville-tn/597008)
