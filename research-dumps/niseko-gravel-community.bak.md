# NISEKO GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Kae Takeshita [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was slightly skeptical as to what kind of courses the organizer had prepared. The event website had very little information available... I was pleasantly surprised how lucky we got and found such a jagged quiet road without too much effort." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Kae Takeshita [COMPETITIVE]:** "The start was really relaxed and nothing intense. So chill that literally everyone started riding in the opposite direction at the very first corner!?!" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Kae Takeshita [COMPETITIVE]:** "There was one very long gravel descent in the deep woods. The ground was noticeably soft and greasy at some spots. I was expecting something more twisty and winding, but surprisingly it was fairly straight and really long. What is this road usually used for??" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Kae Takeshita [COMPETITIVE]:** "I love racing but also really enjoy the non-competitive events as well. No pressure and a lot of time to enjoy the scenery and good company. It reminds me of why I started riding gravel to begin with many years ago." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Kae Takeshita [COMPETITIVE]:** "The event organizer did a really good job creating the course. It was not monotone at all but very dynamic. Some technical areas, nice and open areas, some nasty climbs, long descents, and one last gravel climb no one probably expected getting so close to the end." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Takahito Kishi [ELITE]:** "The scenery and the roads here are something that I wouldn't normally encounter in Tokyo, and the entire mountain pass – both ascent and descent – being a gravel section, is something you cannot find elsewhere in the archipelago. The whole experience was amazing – in every sense of the word." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

**Takahito Kishi [ELITE]:** "Since this was my first gravel event, I decided to simply just enjoy the whole 100-kilometre ride. It was surprisingly long so I took it easy from the start and enjoyed the scenery and road while riding." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

**Takuya Matsuhashi [UNKNOWN]:** "It is totally different type of bike. It is not just the middle point of a road bike and a hardtail, it's a completely new category of bike!" (https://rhythmjapan.com/our-stories/niseko-gravel-biking)

**Takuya Matsuhashi [UNKNOWN]:** "To understand gravel biking, you must visit Niseko and try some nice gravel courses! There are so many scenic gravel courses and gravel enthusiasts around here, as well as the Twin Peaks MTB trails, where you can try your gravel bike handling skills on the track!" (https://rhythmjapan.com/our-stories/niseko-gravel-biking)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**From competitive rider [COMPETITIVE]:** Writer's companion flatted three times before the finish line during the 2022 event on gravel sections. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**General safety recommendations [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Essential items listed include: emergency repair equipment, spare tube, snacks and water, correct cycling attire (lightweight material recommended over casual clothes), helmet, correct bike type (gravel bike specifically for gravel terrain), and a paper map for areas without cell reception. (https://rhythmjapan.com/our-stories/niseko-gravel-biking)

**Event observation [COMPETITIVE]:** "Background of the riders was diverse: popular cycling YouTuber & influencers, current and former professional riders and national champions, industry people, and weekend racers and recreational riders. Gravel bikes, mountain bikes, fat bikes, commuter bikes, a dog in a backpack, etc. Yes, it seemed like everyone was there." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

## Weather Experienced

**2023 Spring Ride:** Event experienced rain, cold temperatures, and gusty winds. Description notes "rain-soaked gravel tracks," "biting cold," "chilled fingers," and "relentless downpour." Despite challenging conditions, riders completed the event. (https://www.summerjapan.com/en/niseko-gravel-2023-spring-ride/)

**2022 September Event [COMPETITIVE]:** "The weather could not be better, it seemed like all went perfectly. Everyone had big smiles at the end." First week of September in Hokkaido - one month before first snow typically arrives. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**2024 Spring Ride:** Over 200 participants completed the event. "Although rain was forecast, the weather cleared before the event, a notable contrast to the 2023 spring ride during which participants pushed through a downpour." (https://www.powderlife.com/blog/over-200-cyclists-joined-niseko-gravel-spring-ride-2024/)

**2025 Autumn Ride:** Event experienced "some periods of unfavorable weather" and "heavy rain" that led to the closure of the Higuma Course. (https://nisekogravel.com/category/news_en)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course composition [COMPETITIVE]:** "The courses consisted of gravel sections connected by paved roads. Gravel roads were mainly forest roads where no one would usually go into, and private gravel paths in between rice fields (thanks, the rice farmers for letting us ride in your lands for the event!)." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Gravel characteristics [COMPETITIVE]:** "The area was thickly wooded, lots of green, and the gravel was dry, fast, and chunky." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Course profile [COMPETITIVE]:** "The first few kilometers were nice and fast downhill. I started in the mid pack, but quickly came up to the front once the road went up." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Descent section [COMPETITIVE]:** "There was one very long gravel descent in the deep woods. The ground was noticeably soft and greasy at some spots... it was fairly straight and really long." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Late-race surprise [COMPETITIVE]:** "One last gravel climb no one probably expected getting so close to the end." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Paved road quality [COMPETITIVE]:** "Paved roads in Japan are very clean and in good shape in general, and there are no potholes like I'm used to seeing in the US Midwest." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**2022 Course distances and elevation:**
- Extra Long: 72 miles (115.3 km), 7,303 feet (2,226 m) elevation, 49% gravel, 4 checkpoints
- Long: 58 miles (93.5 km), 5,883 feet (1,793 m) elevation, 48% gravel, 4 checkpoints  
- Medium: 40 miles (63.4 km), 3,990 feet (1,216 m) elevation, 43% gravel, 3 checkpoints
- Short: 26 miles (41.4 km), 2,572 feet (784 m) elevation, 22% gravel, 1 checkpoint
(https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**2024 Spring Ride distances:**
- Short: 50-60km
- Long: 90-100km, extending down to Toyoura Town near Lake Toya
(https://www.powderlife.com/blog/over-200-cyclists-joined-niseko-gravel-spring-ride-2024/)

**Trail access [ELITE]:** "The entire mountain pass – both ascent and descent – being a gravel section, is something you cannot find elsewhere in the archipelago." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

**Special terrain features:** 2024 Spring Ride included "a special forest area, not usually accessible" that was "opened for a limited time." (https://www.nisekoareaguide.com/events/niseko-gravel-spring-ride)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite approach [ELITE]:** Pro cyclist Takahito Kishi on his first gravel event: "Since this was my first gravel event, I decided to simply just enjoy the whole 100-kilometre ride. It was surprisingly long so I took it easy from the start and enjoyed the scenery and road while riding." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

**Pre-ride reconnaissance [COMPETITIVE]:** "On Friday, the day before the two-day event, I met up with cycling friends from Sapporo and went for a spin" to scout terrain. Rider also did "another quick spin" on Saturday on paved roads. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Personal motto from elite cyclist [ELITE]:** Takahito Kishi's pre-competition routine centers on: "Just have fun!" (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

**Event format note:** Although described as "not technically a race" but a "fun ride," participation included "current and former professional riders and national champions" alongside recreational riders. "The start was really relaxed and nothing intense." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical issues [COMPETITIVE]:** One rider "flatted immediately and was getting help. He told me to go ahead... And he flatted two more times before the finish line. Ouch!" Total of three flats on gravel sections. Narrow gravel double tracks meant "There was no backtracking option for me because the gravel double tracks were narrow and crowded and did not allow two way traffic." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Weather-related course changes:** In 2025 Autumn Ride, the Higuma Course was officially closed "due to heavy rain." (https://nisekogravel.com/category/news_en)

**Elite coaching advice [ELITE]:** Takahito Kishi on common mistakes: "Things beyond riding a bike, such as bike maintenance and body care, tend to be overlooked by amateur cyclists but are actually often key points (in cycling)." His advice: "Have fun and keep up with your bike's maintenance. It's easy to let our excitement get ahead of us and that could lead to dangerous situations while gravel riding." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

**Physical management [ELITE]:** "Cycling tires you out more than you think, so remember to ride within your limits. If you are tired, don't force yourself to continue riding." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event vibe comparison [COMPETITIVE]:** "During this Panaracer Niseko Gravel event, the atmosphere felt something like the American gravel from 10 years ago, but with all the gravel specific equipment in the market, and information and knowledge to ride gravel easily available." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Spirit of event [COMPETITIVE]:** "Chatting, riding, encouraging others, riding, and repeat! What a great day!... Taking in the scenic views and savoring the moments for many hours to come." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Participant diversity [COMPETITIVE]:** "Background of the riders was diverse: popular cycling YouTuber & influencers, current and former professional riders and national champions, industry people, and weekend racers and recreational riders. Gravel bikes, mountain bikes, fat bikes, commuter bikes, a dog in a backpack, etc." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Event scale:** First year held on "much smaller scale." 2022 increased participation cap to 400 riders - "This was for sure the largest gravel event in Japan to date." Border was still closed, so no international participants at that time. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**2024 Spring Ride:** "Over 200 gravel-riding enthusiasts descended on Niseko" (https://www.powderlife.com/blog/over-200-cyclists-joined-niseko-gravel-spring-ride-2024/)

**Weekend format:** "Saturday was a day of packet-pickup, outdoor expo, live music, and industry & guest panels... A number of food trucks were there." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Challenging conditions bonding [UNKNOWN]:** During 2023 Spring Ride with rain/cold/wind: "Amidst these challenging conditions, the sense of camaraderie among riders shone through. Shared laughter, encouraging words, and the collective determination to overcome the elements created an unbreakable bond among participants." (https://www.summerjapan.com/en/niseko-gravel-2023-spring-ride/)

**Regional appeal [COMPETITIVE]:** "I could tell the Japanese people who come from outside of Hokkaido, the northern island (especially from populated cities) were all greatly impressed to see and experience the open space and wide roads." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Growing gravel scene in Japan [COMPETITIVE]:** "Japan's bike industry is finally catching up, and more and more riders are hearing and learning about gravel scenes from overseas. The media is really pushing gravel and there are quite a few Japanese riders signing up for popular US events as well." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Event potential [COMPETITIVE]:** "This event has a lot of potential. Although it's not on the mainland, the access is fairly easy... what's different from other remote villages of Japan is that Niseko has lodging options because it's a ski town, and the people are used to receiving both domestic and foreign tourists." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/japan-ride-report-panaracer-niseko-gravel-by-kae-takeshita/)

**Historical context [ELITE]:** Elite cyclist Takahito Kishi notes: "Niseko is one of a kind... being able to hold big and large-scale events like the Niseko Gravel is something really unique to Niseko and that's what really draws cyclists to come here." He also comments on terrain: "There is no other location in Japan that surpasses Niseko and Hokkaido as a whole when it comes to location and gravel riding." (https://setsuniseko.com/en/blog/takahito-kishi-interview/)
