# NOOSA GRAVEL ENDURO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Specifications [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Gravel or Cyclo Cross bikes are perfect. You could do on a mountain bike if you wish but it will be very slow. E bikes allowed"
- "Beginner riders may have to dismount on a few occasions for some very short sections and as such, gravel or mountain bike shoes are recommended. Experienced off-road riders shouldn't need to during the ride as a general rule"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**Tire Recommendations [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Tyres – bigger is better! 34mm + knobby robust tyres are recommended"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**Gearing [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Gear ratio's – the terrain is hilly, so gear ratios should be selected on individual ability. While not super steep, is still long so it's recommended to go smaller if in doubt of where you're fitness is at"
- For mountain bike courses: "For the steeper climbing sections, it is recommended to use a compact chain ring and a minimum of 32-tooth rear cassette"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/ and https://www.accommodationinnoosa.com.au/noosa-enduro-2025-a-challenging-riding-experience/)

**Self-Sufficiency Requirements [UNKNOWN]:**
- "It's always recommended that riders carry their own phone, pump, spares and basic tools"
- "Riders should bring their own spare parts and kits"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**Unusual Equipment Note [UNKNOWN]:**
One rider participated on "the road bike with 23mm Maxxis Refuse tyres" on gravel and single trail (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eKgU9fRY6-4)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Gravel Enduro Course Description [COMPETITIVE]:**
- "This course is designed 100% for gravel bikes, just be prepared, it's not your typical gravel ride"
- "We have removed a few of the most difficult trail sections from last year though, making it a course that you should be able to ride around with no dismount sections required"
- 90km length with approximately 1250m of climbing
- "Classic multi-terrain feel of the Noosa Enduro taking in all of Noosa's epic hinterland along some premium graded gravel roads, sealed roads, fire roads, farms, track and trails"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**MTB Course Breakdown [UNKNOWN]:**
- 60km course: "approximately 20% single track, 50% forest road, 30% bitumen or other (concrete bike path etc)"
- 100km course: "approximately 40% is single track, 40% gravel roads, and 20% bitumen or other (concrete bike path etc)"
- (https://www.accommodationinnoosa.com.au/noosa-enduro-2025-a-challenging-riding-experience/)

**Surface Variety [RECREATIONAL]:**
- "The courses are extremely varied and contain mixed surfaces like sealed, gravel and private roads, fire roads, single track, pitted fields, rocky sections, rapid downhills and some very technical sections"
- (https://localsintheloop.com.au/listing/noosa-enduro-2024/)

**MTB 100km Route Details [UNKNOWN]:**
- Almost 2300m of climbing over 100km
- "Much of the riding is on the Noosa Trail Network, a series of multi-use trails in the Noosa Hinterland"
- "Sometimes it's a rainforest singletrack, other times a dirt road on a ridgeline"
- Route includes major climbs at "Mt Tinbeerwah, Sheppersons Hill and Cooran Tablelands Range"
- "Majority of climbing done around the 60km mark"
- (https://www.ambmag.com.au/video/noosa-enduro-bike-race-preview-577071/)

**Gravel-Specific Challenge [ELITE]:**
- Includes "a 6km up Mother Mountain" climb
- (https://www.accommodationinnoosa.com.au/noosa-enduro-2025-a-challenging-riding-experience/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Time Estimates by Course [UNKNOWN]:**
- MTB 100km: "can take even the most experienced riders up to ten hours to complete"
- (https://www.accommodationinnoosa.com.au/noosa-enduro-2025-a-challenging-riding-experience/)

**Cut-Off Times [COMPETITIVE]:**
- Gravel Enduro: "12.00pm at Woondum National Park"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**Aid Station Locations [COMPETITIVE]:**
Gravel course has "three food and water stations across the course"
- Checkpoints at: "Pomona, Woondum NP, Ringtail Forest"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**MTB Aid Stations [UNKNOWN]:**
- MTB 100km: "Pomona is the first check point with food and water – so fill up"
- (https://www.ambmag.com.au/video/noosa-enduro-bike-race-preview-577071/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Support Available [UNKNOWN]:**
- "Unrepairable bike problems or injuries and we can arrange a pickup shuttle if you have your phone on you to communicate with us"
- "Comprehensive trail signage, check points, food & water stations, medical, mechanical and sag vehicle support"
- Note: "While we have the support crew there to help... due to the large volume of needs sometimes" riders should be self-sufficient

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Character [COMPETITIVE]:**
- "This timed gravel ride is a non-competitive event with just one category for all riders and no prizes given"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/adventure-gravel/)

**Festival Atmosphere [RECREATIONAL]:**
- "With all courses departing from the Festival Village participants can take advantage of the festival hub post ride with bars, food, entertainment an industry expo and more"
- "Turning the Noosa Hinterland into one giant playground"
- (https://localsintheloop.com.au/listing/noosa-enduro-2024/)

**Event Positioning [UNKNOWN]:**
- "This isn't a cycling event… it's an all terrain adventure on the bike that puts you amongst our outrageously spectacular playground"
- "Don't miss the Sunshine Coast's premier gravel and mountain biking festival"
- (https://noosaenduro.com.au/ and https://www.sunshinecoastpoint.com.au/events/noosa-enduro/)
