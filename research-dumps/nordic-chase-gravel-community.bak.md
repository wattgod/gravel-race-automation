## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Copenhagen to Oslo Gravel Edition (800km):** Route is "Over 80% gravel, more inland, and undeniably more demanding" compared to the road version. Riders experience "gravel climbs to silent lakesides" and "the raw heart of southern Scandinavia." Terrain includes "forest tracks, open gravel, Baltic winds" with "dense woods and quiet farmland before reaching the Baltic sea." (https://veloatlas.com/ultra_cycling_events/nordic-chase-gravel/)

**Route verification:** "Organizer Gregory Sanders personally verified the route in summer 2025. The photos below were taken during his trip. Please note that the final route is subject to minor changes and will be included in the race manual, released 3 weeks before the race." (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Course description from organizer:** "Start in Copenhagen, roll north through Denmark, hop the ferry to Sweden, and grind your way over gravel roads, forest trails, and through quiet Nordic villages — all under the endless glow of the Nordic summer — until you hit Oslo." (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Berlin to Copenhagen Gravel Edition (730km):** "+60% gravel" with course winding "through dense forests and quiet farmland, before reaching the Baltic Sea. After the ferry crossing into Denmark, riders tackle the gravel roads of Sjælland, continuing all the way to Copenhagen." (https://bikepacking.com/event/2026-nordic-chase-chapter-two/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Cut-off times provided:**
- Copenhagen to Oslo: 103 hours from 7:00 AM Friday start = Tuesday 2:00 PM finish deadline (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)
- Berlin to Copenhagen: 100 hours from 7:00 AM Saturday start = Wednesday 11:00 AM finish deadline (https://nordicchase.com/ber-cph-gravel)

**Self-supported requirements:** "In self-supported racing, riders complete the entire route without external assistance such as private support cars or friends handing over supplies. You carry your own gear, fix your own bike, and handle your food, water, and logistics independently using services available to all." However, "riders are welcome to draft and travel in groups, making it both a personal test and a shared experience." (https://bikepacking.com/event/2026-nordic-chase-gravel/)

**Race philosophy:** "Is it a race or a ride? It's both. Some push for fast finish times, others take their time and enjoy the journey." (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Pre-race social activities:** Social ride and briefing the day before with "drinks and snacks" followed by casual gathering. For Berlin-Copenhagen: "pasta party" included. (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel and https://nordicchase.com/ber-cph-gravel)

**Start line atmosphere:** "Coffee & Croissants provided" at 6:00 AM meet-up, group photo at 6:45 AM, Grand Départ at 7:00 AM. "Our Media team will follow the route in two cars, capturing moments along the way." (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Finish line:** "Every participant will be welcomed with cheers, photos and refreshments. We will be waiting at the finish line until every last rider rolls - all the way until the cut-off time." Post-ride ceremony includes drinks and food. (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Optional party boat (Copenhagen to Oslo edition):** Partnership with Go Nordic Cruiseline offers "special cabin rates for the ferry from Oslo to Copenhagen on August 18th" with "Collective dinner party on Ferry with the Nordic Chase participants." Departure Oslo Tuesday 4 PM, arrival Copenhagen Wednesday 10 AM. (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Community connection:** "Entry to WhatsApp group chat to connect with the other participants" included in registration. Riders can "coordinate with fellow participants in the WhatsApp group to share accommodations." (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Event size:** Limited to 100 riders per edition. (https://nordicchase.com/cph-osl-gravel)

**Organizer background:** "Gregory Sanders - Cycling has always been a passion of mine, and I've taken part in several ultra-distance events myself. After living in Denmark for the past three years, I've come to know the region inside out. In 2025, I launched the first edition of the Nordic Chase — a 400 km ultra-distance race starting and finishing in Copenhagen." (https://nordicchase.com)
