# NORTH WALES GRAVEL X — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Jon in Sweden [ELITE]:** "Preparation running up to the race was very good. Volume was high enough, intensity was there, and the bike set up was on point. I ended up going with my Grizl SL6 with SRAM Apex XPLR with 44t front and 10-44 back. Schwalbe Racing Ray/Ralph, front and back, 2.1"... At the 40 minute point, my normalised power was about 420w... I crossed the line in 3hrs 38, for 104km, with an average power of 336w and 370w NP. I finished in 3rd place" (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Jon in Sweden [ELITE]:** "I was the strongest of the five, and I wasn't sure (with this being my first mass start race) whether I should hold back a little to try to keep the group or whether to push on. I am a very strong climber for my size, and was dropping everyone on the climbs despite not really pushing very hard (I was typically climbing at 100-115% of FTP). The descents on the other side were almost designed for me with them being not too steep, but fairly twisty, with technical cornering and rough terrain." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Jon in Sweden [ELITE]:** "In a stroke of tremendous luck, I punctured on the final straight and didn't even notice until I tried to ride up to the race village afterwards and the tyre went completely flat. A big gash in the rear, which I attempted to plug later on but it wouldn't hold air. Having seen literally dozens of guys at the side of the road covered in sealant, I realise that I'm incredibly lucky." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**rareposter [UNKNOWN]:** "I did a substantial portion of that loop a few months back in a gravel sportive, the Glorious Gravel North Wales Gravel X. I wasn't hanging around (and I hooked up with a very fast rider inside the last 30km) but there were a few people in the event really pushing it, obviously using it as training for the qualifier. It's a great ride, some properly remote bits." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Tim o's son [COMPETITIVE]:** "My son rode that race, same age group as you, his first gravel race. Made a schoolboy error of riding around warming up only to find that he was at the back of the pen for the start, so went over the startline pretty well at the back. Missed the fast groups that formed and did a lot of the first half riding solo. Eventually found some others to ride with and later the faster riders from the next age group caught him and he latched on. Came in something like 16th which got a qualification for Maastricht, which was the objective. He loved every minute of it." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Jon in Sweden's setup:** Canyon Grizl SL6 with SRAM Apex XPLR, 44t front chainring with 10-44 cassette. Schwalbe Racing Ray/Ralph 2.1" tires front and back. He noted: "the 2.1" tyres gave me a lot of grip" on the technical descents. (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**[UNKNOWN LEVEL] Official recommendation:** "A standard all-rounder gravel tyre (35-45mm) will be ideal for both routes." The course is described as "mostly smooth with a couple of soft spots which could be muddy if wet but overall this is an off-roader suitable for all abilities." Technical difficulty rated 2/5 stars for both routes. (https://gloriousgravel.com/product/gravel-sportives/north-wales-gravel-x)

**[UNKNOWN LEVEL] Bike compatibility:** "This route is designed for gravel bikes but you will be fine on a hardtail MTB." (https://gloriousgravel.com/product/gravel-sportives/north-wales-gravel-x)

**[UNKNOWN LEVEL] Packing list includes:** "2 x inner tubes suitable for the size of your tyre. If running a tubeless set up, have a tubeless repair kit including plugs and spare sealant. Multitool | Chain link specific for your bike chain. Small pump | Brake pads (especially in wet conditions)" (https://gloriousgravel.com/product/gravel-sportives/north-wales-gravel-x)

## Weather Experienced

**April 2024 race conditions [from Jon in Sweden, ELITE]:** "It was cold and windy, but at least it didn't rain. The evening saw enough rain to make the track quite wet, and then race day for me saw intermittent showers and 30kph wind. 16-17c. Miserable weather really, but suits me so much better than hot." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Opening section [ELITE perspective]:** "The opening 500m is flat along a dam wall, with a tight and punchy little 10% climb at the end of it. That split the group a bit. The course goes around a reservoir initially, with some climbs to split the field." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Descents [ELITE perspective]:** "The descents on the other side were almost designed for me with them being not too steep, but fairly twisty, with technical cornering and rough terrain... I descended generally on gravity, giving myself a little rest and the others a chance to catch so we could work together on the flats. With hindsight, there weren't really enough flats to warrant this." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Overall character [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "The gravel in this part of North Wales is mostly smooth with a couple of soft spots which could be muddy if wet... The forest section is lumpy but the sections around the reservoirs is flatter and flowing." (https://gloriousgravel.com/product/gravel-sportives/north-wales-gravel-x)

**Route specifics:** Long route includes "a tough climb to Alwen" at the start, circuits around Llyn Alwen and Llyn Brenig reservoirs with "flowy singletrack and smooth gravel," then into Clocaenog Forest at 27.5km where "the riding gets spicier with plenty of short, sharp climbs." The route includes "an exact lap of the Graean Cymru UCI course" including "a stretch of singletrack" before "mostly downhill all the way back to a finish on the karting track." (https://gloriousgravel.com/product/gravel-sportives/north-wales-gravel-x)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Jon in Sweden's approach:** "I pushed off fairly hard at the start, but didn't go absolutely all out... At the 40 minute point, my normalised power was about 420w... I was typically climbing at 100-115% of FTP... By the end of the first lap (52km), the group has gone and I did the second lap solo. I caught 4 or 5 more of my age group over the course of the lap, despite having throttled back a bit. I had it in my head to save something for the final half hour." Final stats: 3hrs 38min for 104km, average power 336w, normalized power 370w. (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**[ELITE] Starting position matters:** Jon noted a rider who "made a schoolboy error of riding around warming up only to find that he was at the back of the pen for the start, so went over the startline pretty well at the back. Missed the fast groups that formed and did a lot of the first half riding solo." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**[ELITE] Jon's strategy reflection:** "I am already planning to go back next year... with a bit of weight loss (6kg), a lighter and more aero bike and a better race strategy... Had I done the second lap as fast as the first, I'd have been only 4 minutes behind [the 19-34 category winner]." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical failures [observed across all levels]:** Jon in Sweden noted: "Having seen literally dozens of guys at the side of the road covered in sealant, I realise that I'm incredibly lucky" after his own rear tire punctured with a "big gash" that wouldn't hold air with plugs. (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Chain drops [ELITE experience]:** "By 13km... my chain sneaked past the catcher and came off. I got it back on quite quickly, and chased back on." (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Cramping [ELITE experience]:** "For the last 25km, I had (for the first time in my life) some pretty unpleasant cramps." This occurred at high intensity (336w average, 370w NP over 3:38). (https://forum.cyclinguk.org/viewtopic.php?t=166324)

**Remote sections:** The route takes in "some remote sections" and organizers note "We have support vans available but we recommend you are as self sufficient as possible" with a detailed packing list. (https://gloriousgravel.com/product/gravel-sportives/north-wales-gravel-x)
