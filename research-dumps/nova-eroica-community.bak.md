# NOVA EROICA — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**JOM [COMPETITIVE]:** "One of my best days on a bicycle" - describing the 2025 Nova Eroica Buonconvento experience after completing the 95km route (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/2025-nova-eroica-italy-one-of-my-best-days-on-a-bicycle/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE]:** "We managed to make the initial front group of 20 or so which formed in the first km and spent that first hour before the first actual timed gravel sector of Monte Sante Marie with the lead group. We simply rode at our own pace towards the front of the pack. Enjoying the food stops and scenery, we rode tempo until the finish. It was a really fun day on the bike and we were happy to finish the long route with the top cyclists." - 2021 Nova Eroica Buonconvento, 82 miles/7800ft (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE]:** On weather conditions in 2021: "Typical weather for Tuscany in July is hot and dry, but this year's event saw cooler temperatures and a very light drizzle for most of the day. This was a very welcome relief for our participating cyclists as it does make for and more enjoyable ride - both the filtered sun and the misted gravel provided great conditions." (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

**Anonymous blogger [UNKNOWN]:** "Race, laughs, relaxation" - described as the motto of Nova Eroica's approach to gravel cycling (https://www.thevermilion.com/eroica-nova-when-the-gravel-respects-the-story-antonio-ruzzos-blog/article_9659/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike variety observed [UNKNOWN]:** At the 2025 event, riders were spotted on various setups including: T'Red brand bikes, Simoncini Steel, Megamo Gravel Bike, Kona, Officina Battaglin Matera, and notably a "Sweet drop bar Klein MTB conversion" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/gravel-bike-check-what-people-are-riding-at-the-2025-nova-eroica-italy/)

**Bike suitability [UNKNOWN]:** "Gravel bikes are the ideal option given the hybrid road surfaces, but given the routes are over 70% asphalt, they are easily doable with a road bike, as well." - specifically about Nova Eroica Prosecco Hills (https://cyclinghero.cc/special-event/nova-eroica-prosecco-hills)

## Weather Experienced

**2021 Buonconvento (July):** Event was rescheduled from April to July. Instead of typical hot/dry conditions, riders experienced "cooler temperatures and a very light drizzle for most of the day" with "filtered sun and misted gravel" (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

**2025 Buonconvento (June 22):** Photos show "Early wake up event day, storms nearby" but "the bad weather blew through quickly" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/2025-nova-eroica-italy-one-of-my-best-days-on-a-bicycle/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Buonconvento course specifics [COMPETITIVE]:** "Many of the famous sectors of Strade Bianche are used including the Monte Sante Marie (Fabian Cancellara), San Martino in Grania just to name a couple." - 2021 long course was 82 miles with 7800ft elevation (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

**Steep descent conditions [UNKNOWN]:** Photos and captions from 2025 show "Dodgy descent during Segment 1" followed by "Ruh roh, steep grade, loose conditions, everyone walking" and "Hike-A-Bike - Barring the front group, everyone walked" during Timed Segment 2 (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/2025-nova-eroica-italy-one-of-my-best-days-on-a-bicycle/)

**Prosecco Hills terrain [UNKNOWN]:** Routes feature "abundant vineyards and steep 'sterrate' or gravel roads" with routes being "over 70% asphalt" and described as "hybrid road surfaces" (https://cyclinghero.cc/special-event/nova-eroica-prosecco-hills)

**Istria terrain [UNKNOWN]:** Course winds "through historic villages, vineyards and olive groves until they intersect with the legendary Parenzana, the old railway line that has now been transformed into a picturesque cycle route" with "technical sections, scenic climbs and spectacular descents" plus "an exclusive seaside refreshment stop" (https://www.eroica.cc/en/the-istria-race/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Timing system [UNKNOWN]:** "The official results of this race are determined by the riders cumulative time over several timed sections. While the rest of the route is 'untimed' and 'neutral', the race does play out somewhat like a traditional race." (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

**Event format [UNKNOWN]:** "There are several timed uphill segments that you are welcome to 'race' that count towards a sector and overall standing. The overall finishing time is not really important, so you can just 'ride' the other parts of the route tranquillo." Described as "Race. Ride. Relax." (https://cyclinghero.cc/special-event/nova-eroica-prosecco-hills)

**Pacing approach [COMPETITIVE]:** JOM's group "spent that first hour before the first actual timed gravel sector of Monte Sante Marie with the lead group. We simply rode at our own pace towards the front of the pack. Enjoying the food stops and scenery, we rode tempo until the finish." This was sufficient to "finish the long route with the top cyclists." (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Festival atmosphere [UNKNOWN]:** "The festival atmosphere of the event's start/finish town of Buonconvento is great to walk around the day before the event as well as after the race." (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)

**Post-race scene [UNKNOWN]:** "There is a big party at the finish line, with tasty food and a festive environment where you can 'relax' and share your experience with others. All in all a wonderful and inclusive day out on the bike." (https://cyclinghero.cc/special-event/nova-eroica-prosecco-hills)

**Event philosophy [UNKNOWN]:** Nova Eroica is described as "a middle ground between the tourist pedaling of heroic and the desire, from time to time, to get up on the pedals and to open the gas in the timed sections. A successful formula that also added music, meetings, tastings and appointments for children to the races, as heroic family, which make Nova Eroica a festival." (https://www.thevermilion.com/eroica-nova-when-the-gravel-respects-the-story-antonio-ruzzos-blog/article_9659/)

**Aid stations [COMPETITIVE]:** Photos from 2025 show extensive food offerings at aid stations, described as "gourmet food, wine, and the culture of Italy, provides a cycling experience that is like no other." Labeled as "The scene & vibe at Aid Station 1" with multiple food tables visible. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/videos/2025-nova-eroica-italy-one-of-my-best-days-on-a-bicycle/)

**Class A event [COMPETITIVE]:** JOM rated it as "Definitely a Class A event added to the memory book" noting it was "a really fun day on the bike" despite being competitive. (https://www.biciitalia.com/bici-blog/heroic-gravel-at-nova-eroica)
