## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**All routes start with paved climbing:** "All routes will leave and finish at the farm. The gravel is approximately two miles from the start line, and one mile of that is a paved climb, once you are on the ridge, the dust shall start flying! All routes will descend this paved section upon return to the finish line." (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/)

**20-mile Butter Valley route:** "At mile two you will have a climb that is about a mile long, it is paved, so this is great for people less sure about their climbing and descending skills on the gravel. Once on top of the ridge, you can expect scenic views, and rolling twisty roads. At about mile eight, you will have a mellow descent for approximately two miles, then you head up another one-mile climb." With 1,300 feet of climbing over 18 miles. (https://oldfashionedgravel.com/routes/butter-valley-20 and https://www.thenxrth.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-old-fashioned-gravel-race-this-october)

**40-mile Crazy Corners route:** "This route offers gravel lovers minimal pavement, over 2,000 feet in elevation gain, and breathtaking descents." (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/)

**60-mile Winnie Cooper route:** "Over 4,000 feet in elevation gain" with "an additional descent and major climb" beyond the 40-mile route. (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/)

**100-mile 99 Proof route:** "Over 6,000 feet in elevation gain" (official site) but the race director quoted "8200 feet of climbing" in an interview. (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/ and https://www.thenxrth.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-old-fashioned-gravel-race-this-october)

**Driftless terrain characteristics:** The race takes place in the Driftless region with "rolling hills once on top of the bluffs, lots of farms (and animals), and a good amount of wide open to wooded descents. All of the routes have spectacular views." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-old-fashioned-gravel-race-this-october)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical issues at mile 40:** Uriah Matznick reported a mechanical that caused a DNF around mile 40 of the 100-mile course. (https://www.raceentry.com/old-fashioned-gravel/race-reviews)

**Navigation issues:** Eric Cleveland noted "Maps were a bit wonky" and Carolyn Franzone got lost on the shake-out ride due to "one of the directions on the cue sheet was wrong." (https://www.raceentry.com/old-fashioned-gravel/race-reviews)

**Course support limitations:** "This is not a supported ride" and "There will not be any mechanical support on the road, you are responsible for arranging a ride if you have issues." Riders advised to bring "some sort of tire plug, inner tube, and Co2." (https://oldfashionedgravel.com/routes/butter-valley-20)

**No rest stop on 20-mile route:** "Due to the short distance, there will not be a rest stop on this route." (https://oldfashionedgravel.com/routes/butter-valley-20)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Grassroots quality standout:** Ian Breitlow, who has "done so many grassroots events in the Midwest," rated this "#1 for quality and hospitality" citing "hot coffee and pastries in the morning" and "super clean, not-porta potty bathrooms." (https://www.raceentry.com/old-fashioned-gravel/race-reviews)

**Farm venue and post-race:** The event takes place at a family farm "nestled in a valley with beautiful views" with "the barn is going to be the center of the event, it is a cool old structure with a rustic feel." Post-ride includes "food, drink, and hangs with new friends. Awards, raffle, and prizes (everything from ridiculous to awesome)." (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-old-fashioned-gravel-race-this-october and https://www.playeasy.com/events/96a7b497-659b-4f60-bf4f-792ba4eec96c)

**Car camping available:** Erin Sullivan noted "Being able to sleep in my car in the pasture was very helpful due to high prices of hotel rooms at that time." (https://www.raceentry.com/old-fashioned-gravel/race-reviews)

**Race philosophy:** Race Director Paul Reardon: "if you want to race, we will be timing, and we wish you luck. If you want to come challenge yourself, or just go for a ride with other rad people, make new friends, eat, drink, and laugh, that's what it is all about!" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-old-fashioned-gravel-race-this-quarter)

**Community partnership:** "A portion of your entry fee will go to a local entity that could use a little financial help" and they're "working with the State of MN to develop a sustainability plan for the property" with a goal to "restore and invigorate the land." (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/ and https://www.thenxrth.com/post/everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-old-fashioned-gravel-race-this-october)

**Local business support:** "We will also be using local businesses to feed and hydrate us. Hokah is a super cool little town, and we would love to help keep our money local." (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/)

**Sells out:** "Event registration IS CLOSED! We've maxed out capacity and will NOT have day of registration." Cap appears to be 300 participants. (https://www.oldfashionedgravel.com/ and https://www.playeasy.com/events/96a7b497-659b-4f60-bf4f-792ba4eec96c)

**Minor organizational growing pains:** Ryan Greene noted "the only thing lacking was some organization and that big finish line feel that you get at some of these other races" but still rated it among his favorite races of the year. (https://www.raceentry.com/old-fashioned-gravel/race-reviews)
