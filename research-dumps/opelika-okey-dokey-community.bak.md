## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] - Trek Checkpoint setup:** "I rode a Trek Checkpoint gravel bike with stock 'team issue GR1' tires at 50 psi. These tires held up well but struggled with mud and loose-gravel climbs. My pressure was probably too high, but I am not running tubeless and I am a heavier rider so I tend to push the pressure limits. The Checkpoint's IsoSpeed couplers provided comfort, and the bike felt stable on descents, easily reaching speeds of 28-30 mph on loose gravel." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**[ELITE] - Hydration capacity:** "I carried four water bottles—two on the frame and two on the forks. Despite the cool day, I sweated heavily and needed all the water. A Camelback might have been better, since the rough course made accessing bottles difficult." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**General note:** Official event page states "No gravel bike? No worries! Any bike in good safe working condition can be used. However, you do you. Just remember, there may or may not be an award for poorest bike choice!" (https://gravelroll.com/opelikaokeydokey)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Road surface and navigation:** "The frequent unexpected turns often led onto the 'least likely' road at the intersection (you know, the road that looks like nobody ever drives down it). The route crossed three bridges closed to vehicles, requiring trust in the GPS to head down a road with signs of 'Bridge Closed 5 Miles Ahead' and just ride over the berm blocking the road." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Post-rain conditions:** "Recent heavy rains left many gravel roads muddy, and logging activities had filled some roads with large rocks, making a mountain bike (or a 4-wheel drive Jeep) more suitable in some spots." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Red clay:** "The area is known for 'red clay,' and these roads had a lot of it." The blog post includes photos showing significant mud from rains 2 days prior to the race. (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Course character:** Official description states "just hard red dirt interspersed with a sprinkling of gravel across miles and miles of twisting and rolling terrain. You'll ride through endless timberland and spooky hollows and be rewarded with occasional views of the Fall Line as it stretches across the landscape." (https://gravelroll.com/opelikaokeydokey)

**102-mile route details:** "71% unpaved, with constant rolling hills" (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Elevation profiles:**
- Short: 34 miles - 2,150' elevation
- Intermediate: 65 miles - 4,300' elevation  
- Long: 102 miles - 6,300' elevation
(https://gravelroll.com/opelikaokeydokey)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Race format:** "This is a 'Gran Fondo' style race with timed sections, allowing riders to enjoy a relaxed pace between the all out efforts... The race was judged based on the cumulative times for the timed sections only, not your overall course time." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Timed segments structure:** "Minimally, the Short course usually has one timed-segment, Intermediate course two timed-segments, and Long course will have three, covering up to 30-40% of the course distance. Segments starts and finishes will be marked on the GPS courses when we release them." (https://gravelroll.com/opelikaokeydokey)

**Navigation requirements:** "Emphasizing 'self-reliance,' the organizers didn't include route turn signs so having the GPS route was essential. They did update the route just days before the event, ensuring accuracy." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Aid station note [ELITE rider experience]:** "I missed the rest stop at mile 69, likely because it was a block off the actual route. The rural route had no gas stations or retail outlets (other than the Dollar General I missed), so carrying all necessary food and hydration is crucial." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Training comp:** "If you are looking to put some finishing touches on your Unbound training this is the perfect course. While Unbound will lack the curving roads, the short and recurring undulations of the Okey Dokey mimic much of what you will experience at Unbound." (https://gravelroll.com/opelikaokeydokey)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Rough terrain impact:** "The rough terrain left my shoulders very sore by day's end... the rough course made accessing bottles difficult." [ELITE] (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Mud crashes:** "There were a few heavy mud patches on the course, causing a crash for one tough 'ole Roller. Luckily, our team was right there when it happened and were able to get our dedicated medical staff on the scene in 12 minutes." Note: The organizer clarifies this specific crash actually happened at their Pecan Shaker event, not Okey Dokey. (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/opelika-okey-dokey-moments)

**Tire choice for conditions:** "[ELITE] These tires held up well but struggled with mud and loose-gravel climbs." The rider noted his tires worked but had limitations in specific conditions. (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Finish line experience:** "I was impressed that they announced riders as they finished. Despite finishing second-to-last, it was gratifying to hear cheers and my name announced as I rode through the finishing chute." [ELITE] (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Post-race scene:** "My favorite part of every one of our events is always and will forever be the after race hangout raffle and awards ceremony. It's so much fun getting to chop it up with all you Rollers, seeing the stoke at an all time high as you roll through the finish. It's always such a blast to see, whether it's people celebrating their win or just hyped for a day on the dirt roads, everyone is in it together and having a Super Serious Good Time." (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/opelika-okey-dokey-moments)

**Event size:** "Well over 200 riders showing out" in 2024, the first year of the event. (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/first-stop-opelika-okey-dokey)

**Support quality:** Medical and SAG support included. "We manage our own team of on-course medical support in order to reduce the load on rural EMS resources... Our roving support vehicles carry mechanical assistance in addition to medical support. No matter what happens on-course, we've got you covered." (https://gravelroll.com/opelikaokeydokey)

**Medical response:** "Luckily, our team was right there when it happened and were able to get our dedicated medical staff on the scene in 12 minutes, and comfort the fallen rider while keeping him out of the mud and water. Another team member put down bark, sticks, and other solid debris to create a path through the mud and guided other riders around our fellow Roller In Arms." (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/opelika-okey-dokey-moments)

**Photographer presence:** "There were professional photographers on course taking pictures you could buy." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Registration value:** "The registration fee was reasonable, excluding a t-shirt (available for purchase) but including handmade finisher awards." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)

**Camping option:** "If you're looking for the sweetest camping spot, be sure to go ahead and hit up Chewacla State Park. Those campsites are primo and usually sell out before we even get our promos put out there." (https://gravelroll.com/gravel-roll-blog/opelika-okey-dokey-moments)

**Late registration trend:** "About 25% of the riders signed up in the final week, complicating planning and organization." (https://onemileanhour.wordpress.com/2024/05/19/okey-dokey-ride-in-opelika-al/)
