# OREGON TRAIL GRAVEL GRINDER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** "I have honestly never been so terrified of starting a ride. But it's better to go into a ride over prepared than over confident. We were told tales of 'body bags' should a descent be miscalculated, nearly 10,000 feet of elevation gain and a significant chunk of very technical wagon road for good measure." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** "It turns out that the wagon trail was the best part of the entire 5 days. Maybe we got lucky with the previous day's rain, giving us more traction and less dust. Either way it was an absolute blast. Certainly technical, but in a way that makes you want to cheer and whoop the entire time." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** "A seemingly endless sandpit came early, and we laughed at the ridiculousness of trying to ride in a straight line while the bikes wanted to do anything but. Thankfully someone eventually pointed out to me that my front axle had come completely loose, so I had a scapegoat for the amount of flailing I had been doing." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Anonymous Elite Rider [ELITE]:** "Stage 3: A 30km climb to start the day, the group nervously rolled out. In hindsight, probably not the best day for me to start with the weight of five bottles (2L pack + 2 bottles), although in my defense I thought we'd be out there riding for 7-8h." (https://sagetitanium.com/2019/07/25/oregon-trail-gravel-grinder-stage-3-race-report/)

**Flo Costa [ELITE]:** "After an easier stage the day before, I was mentally and physically ready to bury myself on stage 4. In a similar fashion to the other days, the pace of the group slowly ramped up as we began the first climb. About 10 minutes in, the top 5 riders broke away. Given the difficulty of this stage and given that the first climb was well over 1 hour, I made sure to ride within my limits." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Flo Costa [ELITE]:** "I woke up the next morning in my tent with my legs more sore than they'd ever been after a race. I had been over-eager the day before which led to poor decisions and bad luck. I had to be more patient and race my own race in order to make time up on the riders ahead of me in GC." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** "My dearest Bridget, it has been 3 days since we departed Bend on our grand adventure. One of the oxen has already fallen ill… OK, maybe the oxen weren't ill. Hell, maybe there weren't any oxen, unless I was hallucating them. But my legs were definitely feeling the burn on day 3." (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

**Michael van den Ham [ELITE]:** "It's no secret that gravel racing has become pretty serious business these days. In the big, one day races, people have their routines, plans, things that they need to stick to to do well and, well, you don't actually get to spend a lot of time just hanging out. That's very different at Oregon Trail. It's not that the racing isn't serious, it's just that when it ends, you are all in the same place with hours to spare before the next day. A perfect place to just hang out with fellow bike people and make some new friends." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/oregon-trail-gravel-grinder-all-you-need-to-know-about-life-on-the-trail/)

**Becca Fahringer [UNKNOWN]:** "Multiple stages means you can target racing hard for the overall, or focus on one day. And if you have a bad day on the bike the camp vibes make up for it. **Sarah Max [UNKNOWN]:** "The racing is hard, and interesting, and challenging, but the whole experience is next level." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/oregon-trail-gravel-grinder-all-you-need-to-know-about-life-on-the-trail/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** Trek Checkpoint SL6 with 34-34 easiest gear, which "saw a lot of use." Front: 38mm Schwalbe G One tubeless. Rear: Panaracer Gravelking SK+ 43mm tubeless ("burly and bombproof, and at 540gr per tire I'd hope so!"). Carried hand pump, large saddle bag with two tubes, two CO2 canisters. Partner carried plugs. Used hydration pack with 2L bladder for 3 of 5 stages. Oakley Windjacket glasses recommended for "keeping the dust out of my eyes due to the enormous frames and the seal at the top." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Cassius Anderson [ELITE]:** ENVE SES 4.5 wheels, Schwalbe RX Pro front/RS Pro rear, SRAM RED and XX1 mullet drivetrain, ENVE one-piece barstem + seatpost, CeramicSpeed OSPW + BB, Arundel cages. "Built for high-speed efficiency and stability in unpredictable terrain... light, stiff, and nimble—perfect for those long accelerations through deep moondust." (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** Duke Racing Wheels, Schwalbe RX Pro front and rear, SRAM RED XPLR 13-speed with UDH, ENVE one-piece barstem + seatpost, CeramicSpeed OSPW + BB. "The SRAM 13-speed drivetrain gave him the range to tackle steep, rutted climbs with surgical precision, with plenty of cushion for bombing descents at mach 5." (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Cassia Boglio [ELITE]:** Argonaut D33 with Carbon-Ti hubs, Pirelli Cinturato M front/RC rear, SRAM RED XPLR 13-speed with UDH, ENVE one-piece barstem + seatpost, CeramicSpeed OSPW + BB. "The lightest of the three, focused on pure responsiveness and explosive acceleration." (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** Recommends bringing extra wheelset (race transports one free): "one being a different flavor of rubber you can swap to if you prefer a different tread or volume, and the other being an insurance policy. With all the rough terrain, tires (and rims) are subjected to lots of stress and being in the mountains with one broken wheelset and no backup can suck the fun out of your trip." Also recommends floor pump with accurate gauge for dialing tire pressure. (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** Pack "whatever you plan to eat (or anticipate needing to fix your bike) while on the trail. And maybe a little extra, especially since there are no easy places to purchase bars, gels, and the like. At a minimum: Flat/puncture repair kit, Multi-tool, On-the-bike nutrition for each day, Chamois cream (if you, like me, need that to get through a long day)." (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** Sleeping setup recommendations: "Good sleep is key to recovery... There can be uneven ground at many of the campsites, so a sleeping bag and pad might not cut the mustard. Some folks I saw brought lightweight cots; others had full-blown air mattresses." (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

## Weather Experienced

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - 2019:** Stage 2 started with rain in the morning: "On a chill morning we were woken up by the sound of rain hitting our tent, somewhat unexpected for Central Oregon in the summer with a forecast of around 30C/85F and sun every day. I opted for arm warmers and my 7mesh lightweight shell... Fortunately, stage 2 began with a 30km gravel climb… only to be greeted at the summit with lightly falling snow." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** "Seasoned adventurers know — you can never trust the weather in the mountains. And OTGG has experienced everything from extreme heat to rain and snow. (Yes, it has snowed in the Cascades in June in previous years!) Be ready for all of it both on and off the bike with a range of (ideally packable, breathable) clothing." (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

**Jorge Padrones [ELITE] - 2024:** "We had mild temperatures always around the 20 degrees [Celsius, ~68°F]." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-racing-the-pioneer-trails-in-oregon)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Stage 1 Santiam Wagon Road:** "I can best describe the wagon road as a sandpit/rock garden hybrid through 10+km of rolling hills. How 19th century wagons ever made it through there is beyond me. There was a fair amount of tipping over and dismounting on my part, and around 40-45km in we popped out onto a gravel road that felt like a smooth highway." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Stage 2:** "On one fast descent some unexpected deep ruts on an otherwise smooth gravel road took out contender Sarah Sturm among others." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Stage 3:** "What followed was a little more soul crushing. A solid stretch of gravel road with nothing but washboard and a bunch of large vehicle traffic drowning us in dust. With another sandpit over the last 10km we made it to the finish after more than 6h of riding!" (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Stage 4:** "It was relatively flat compared to other stages, but the constant battling through loose sandy terrain meant zero respite. The effort reminded me of a TT, constantly pushing a big gear with no rest." Stage involved "about 80km of sand that varied in colour, consistency and depth. Embedded rock gardens even kept the 3h sandpit fresh!" (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Stage 5:** "We were warned at the start about foot deep 'moon dust', and I shot quizzical looks at people around me wondering what that meant. It turns out that moon dust is to sand what powdered sugar is to regular sugar. Riding through it creates a fog of dirt, limiting visibility to the riders around you." Also: "massive snow melt puddles and snow banks before a very short descent to the mountain top finish." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Anonymous Elite Rider [ELITE] - Stage 3:** "A short descent led to another, smaller, climb. The effort was beginning to wear on me, but there was no time to ease up. Every section of the course was an obstacle to be dealt with one at a time... I kept waiting for someone to catch me on the six mile wagon road section. Up, down, puddles, snow bank, rut, rocks. This road had plenty of opportunities to ruin my day." Final 15 miles: "The road had soft spots and washboard sections which forced me to move side to side to find a good line." (https://sagetitanium.com/2019/07/25/oregon-trail-gravel-grinder-stage-3-race-report/)

**Flo Costa [ELITE] - Stage 2:** "About halfway up [first climb], I was pretty close to my limit so I had to ease up in order to have any hope of surviving the stage... I ended up setting a 1 hour power PR up this first climb. After the first descent, I started climbing the next (deceptively steep) small hill. As I started going up, my legs felt empty and I immediately knew it was going to be a long day." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Flo Costa [ELITE] - Stage 4:** "We made our way up this gravel road along a cliff which offered us incredible views on the surrounding mountains and forest." First climb exceeded 1 hour duration. (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Argonaut Factory Racing [ELITE] - Stage 1 2025:** "Loose, dry, unpredictable moondust blanketed the roads like talcum powder, reducing visibility to mere feet ahead. You couldn't see the rider in front of you until you were practically kissing their rear wheel." The course "isn't part of the typical Bend gravel canon, the roads locals know by heart." (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Jorge Padrones [ELITE] - Stage 1 2024:** "120 kms with only 900m of climbing but what in the end was a tough stage as it was so fast. We also faced some sandy terrain that made it harder, having to put extra watts through our pedals to keep our momentum up." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-racing-the-pioneer-trails-in-oregon)

**Jorge Padrones [ELITE] - Stage 2:** "160 kms with almost 3000 meters accumulated elevation, but this time also lots of descent! While the climbing was only 1500 meters, the terrain was faster and we started to get to know the Oregon gravel - fast, smooth and sometimes too loose for the really fast descents where you could pass easily the 60 km/h mark." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-racing-the-pioneer-trails-in-oregon)

**Jorge Padrones [ELITE] - Stage 3:** "For the descent, the Oregon trail team had re-conditioned an old, abandoned dirt road and made it cyclable. You could find everything from fast gravel to single track sections on the same descent through a beautiful forest." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-racing-the-pioneer-trails-in-oregon)

**Jorge Padrones [ELITE] - Queen Stage:** "Two big climbs of more than 20 km were on the menu with almost the totality of the 3000 elevation meters completed in the first 70 km." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-racing-the-pioneer-trails-in-oregon)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** "After conservative and very social riding for the first 3 days, we decided to pick up the pace a little for the last two." Stage 4 approach: "After the first day I began to find my groove in the sand. In particular when I realized momentum was my friend, on stage 4 I tried to big-ring the sections as much as I could." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Flo Costa [ELITE] - Stage 3 Time Trial Strategy:** "Knowing that the second half of the climb was the steepest, I planned for a negative split meaning I would conservatively pace myself for the first half. This also meant that I had to drop myself from the group earlier than I would've liked and had to be strict about maintaining a pace I knew I could hold for 45 minutes. As the climb got steeper, I slowly increased my power and saw that I was catching back up to riders that had started out too hot." Result: 12th fastest time with "a solid 45 minute power PR." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Flo Costa [ELITE] - Overall Strategy:** "I had planned to save some energy during the first few days, so that I could arrive fresh to the last two stages, a plan that, as usual, did not work! I always try to plan like this, but the stamina of the competition makes me forget and go hard from the start." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** "Even experienced riders of multi-day events know that pacing can be tricky. Keep the big picture in mind so you still have some gas in the tank on the final days. This means knowing yourself, listening to your body, and metering your efforts with regards to your goals for the event. For example, it may be tempting to jump into a fast-moving group on day one or two, but if the pace becomes too uncomfortable, saving your energy so you're still strong on days four and five may be the better move." (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE] - Hydration Planning:** "At a minimum, bring... On-the-bike nutrition for each day... There are no easy places to purchase bars, gels, and the like." Pack "maybe a little extra" beyond what you think you need. (https://www.bicyclelaw.com/newsletter/critical-infrastructure/a-field-guide-to-the-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder/)

**Argonaut Factory Racing [ELITE] - Stage 1 Strategy:** "Cassius Anderson thrived in the chaos. Reading moves with laser precision, he surfed wheels like a seasoned pro and trusted his Schwalbe tires to hook up through blind corners." Result: "hard-fought top-5 finish in the day's general classification." (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Anonymous Elite Rider [ELITE] - Stage 3 Mental Strategy:** "I had clawed my way back into the top 10 and was feeling really good on the day. Now all I needed to do was get over the real challenge of the day: the final 8 mile climb. I could slowly feel my efforts throughout the day creeping up on me. About halfway up, I caught a glimpse of 6th & 7th place up ahead. My motivation was higher than ever, but both of my legs were starting to cramp. I slapped them and kept going... For some reason I thought of Gino Mäder, who had passed away in the Tour de Suisse the previous week." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** Race director comments on 2024 course change: "Changing the course route, the main purpose was to keep it fresh. But also change the dynamics so that it's a more exciting race... giving more opportunities for people who are not super strong climbers for a chance of a stage win or a chance in the GC." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/oregon-trail-gravel-grinder-all-you-need-to-know-about-life-on-the-trail/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Mechanical Issues:** Stage 1: "A few minutes into the descent my heart sank as I felt my rear wheel going flat. I prayed for it to seal as I continued following the rider in 7th place ahead of me, but by the time we reached the bottom of the descent, it was completely flat. I suspected it had gotten sliced by the sharp lava rock on the technical descent. I quickly hopped off, put a CO2 in, and tried to get it to seal... I had 'only' lost 3 minutes." Later on Stage 1: "I was ready to bomb the descent... and opted for a rocky (but faster) line. I lost a bit of control and hit my front wheel pretty hard on the final big rock. A few seconds later it was completely flat... In this process I somehow broke the hand pump, which left me without a means for air. Luckily there was an aid station at the top of the climb about a mile back. I had no choice but to hike my way back to the top of the climb." Finished 40 minutes behind 7th. (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-civic-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** Stage 1: "Despite running a similar setup, a rear derailleur issue struck at the worst possible moment—just as the field fractured on the final descent. What followed was a 30-mile solo grind into a punishing headwind." Still finished top-10. (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Cassia Boglio [ELITE] - Crash:** Stage 1: "She was flying—literally—when disaster struck. Following a move from none other than Sofia Gomez Villafañe, she hit a perfect storm: a low-hanging branch, a tangle of ruts, and a split-second decision that ended in a high-speed crash. The camp thought she'd broken her arm. Medics were on scene fast, and fortunately scans ruled out serious injury. But pulling from the course meant Cassia was now out of GC contention." (https://argonautcycles.com/inside-the-argonaut-factory-racing-setup-at-oregon-trail/)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Stage 2 Crash Site:** "On one fast descent some unexpected deep ruts on an otherwise smooth gravel road took out contender Sarah Sturm among others. We rolled through carefully, witnessing that most injuries turned out to be relatively minor." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE] - Camp Fire:** Stage 2: "The excitement for the evening came when some boxes of oatmeal caught fire from a space heater, causing a small building to also catch on fire where meals were being served (and more critically, where the bathrooms were). After some commotion it was well handled, and we curled up in our tents lulled by the soothing lights of a fire truck." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** "Unlike most stage races, the Oregon Trail Gravel Grinder entry fee included camping and meals for the entire event. Once you paid your entry fee and traveled to the event, you wouldn't have to pay for anything else (excluding the on-site daily massages)." Also: "My favorite part of camp was the Autobahn coffee van, a one-man operation serving up to 47 gallons of amazing coffee per day. There was also the option to purchase espresso drinks, cold brew and delicious kombucha." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Morgan Cabot [ELITE]:** "Being a first year event, the OTGG is exploring new territory and seemingly trying to find their identity. While marketed as an adventure ride, once the event started it felt more like a pro race with a fondo following." (https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/2019-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Flo Costa [ELITE]:** "The shorter stage was also by far the most productive off the bike - I had extra time to sleep, eat, stretch, wash my bike, and jump in the river at camp. The camp vibes on this day were great; we had plenty of down time to hang out with other athletes and were even treated to a beautiful Oregon sunset." (https://www.altovelo.org/blog/race-report-oregon-trail-gravel-grinder)

**Zachary Morvant [COMPETITIVE]:** "Five days of stage racing traversing 350 miles of Oregon's most scenic and punishing gravel roads has professional and amateur racers packing their bags and heading west for a chance of glory along the old wagon trail.
