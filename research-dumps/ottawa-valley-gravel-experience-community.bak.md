# OTTAWA VALLEY GRAVEL EXPERIENCE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Format & Logistics

The race offers three distance options with staggered start times:
- **Epic Course:** 97 km, starts 9:00 AM
- **Medio Course:** 55 km, starts 9:30 AM  
- **Petit Course:** 34 km, starts 9:45 AM

**Start/Finish Location:** Calabogie Community Hall, 574 Mill Street, Calabogie, ON, K0J 1H0

**Registration:** Closes Friday before the event at 12pm. Under 19 registration is FREE. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience-2024-bmjx3)

**What's Included:** Chip timing, ATV support, route markers, aid stations, free event photos, podium presentations and medals for all three distances, post-ride meal, and a free beer. Each participant is entered into a draw to win a Felt Breed Carbon Gravel Frame. (https://gravelcup.com/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience)

**Race Series:** Part of the Ontario Gravel Cup series and Gravel Cup Canada; day permits are required and easy to obtain. (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Description:** "From picturesque Calabogie this event uses a spectacular network of single lane maintained old logging roads. There'll be a few unmaintained sections including a lane through a farm field. The final section uses the K&P Trail and Grant Rd before arriving back in Calabogie finishing on the K&P Trail." (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience-2024-bmjx3)

**Highlight Sections:** Gorra Hill, Sammon, Grant Roads (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience-2024-bmjx3)

**Terrain Character:** "All the courses are entirely rideable, flowy, fast, and extremely fun." The routes include "old logging roads of this rural and bucolic area" and pass "alongside farms." (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience-2024-bmjx3)

The course features "rolling country roads of West Carleton" and uses the "famous K&P Rail Trail." (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events)

**Surface Mix:** Combination of single lane maintained logging roads, unmaintained sections, farm field lane, K&P Trail, and Grant Road. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience-2024-bmjx3)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Director Perspective:** Shane Hotchkiss [UNKNOWN], race director of the Tour of Kincardine, was photographed "enjoying the ride" at an event, suggesting organizers who are riders themselves. (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events)

**Event Culture:** Described as having "TONS of laughs, beautiful trails, and all around good vibes" in the 2024 edition. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hzepaOSfj9M)

**Post-Race:** The event includes an "awards ceremony and meal post-ride" along with the free beer included in registration. (https://www.ovcata.ca/rides/runs-and-rides-in-the-ottawa-valley-in-2025-5341.html)

**Field Size:** The 2025 event had 94 total participants across all categories (31 Epic, 40 Medio, 23 Petit), with relatively low DNF rate (4 total) suggesting good course support and manageable difficulty. (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**DNF Rates by Course (2025):**
- Epic: 2 DNFs out of 30 starters (6.7%)
- Medio: 0 DNFs out of 40 starters (0%)
- Petit: 2 DNFs out of 22 starters (9.1%)

The Medio course had perfect completion rate despite being mid-distance, suggesting it may offer the best difficulty-to-support ratio. The Petit course surprisingly had the highest DNF percentage despite being shortest. (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)
