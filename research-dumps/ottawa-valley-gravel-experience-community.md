## Event Overview
The Ottawa Valley Gravel Experience takes place in Calabogie, Ontario, typically in mid-April. The race offers three distance options: Epic (97-125km depending on year), Medio (55-80km), and Petit (34-40km). The event is part of the Gravel Cup Canada series and starts at the Calabogie Community Hall.

**Course Description:** From picturesque Calabogie, the event uses a spectacular network of single-lane maintained old logging roads, with some unmaintained sections including lanes through farm fields. The final section uses the K&P Trail and Grant Road before finishing back in Calabogie on the K&P Trail. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Conditions:** The course features rolling country roads of West Carleton, scenic rail trails including the famous K&P Rail Trail, old logging roads, and backwoods trails. Highlight sections include Gorra Hill, Sammon, and Grant Roads. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events)

**Technical Character:** "All the courses are entirely rideable, flowy, fast, and extremely fun." (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events)

**Terrain Features:** Routes pass alongside farms and through rural, bucolic areas. The course includes "rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs" with moderate difficulty rating. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12529/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience-)

**Course Evolution:** The Epic course distance has varied by year - 125km in 2024, reduced to 97km in 2025 with adjusted routes. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events and http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**2025 Epic Course Results [ELITE]:**
- Winning time: 3:01:37 for 97km (32.04 km/h average) by Josh Tyers [ELITE]
- Top 5 finishers all within 47 seconds, finishing between 3:01:37 and 3:02:24
- 6th place: 3:03:25 (31.73 km/h)
(http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)

**2024 Epic Course Results [ELITE]:**
- Winning time: 3:45:59 for 125km (33.19 km/h average) by Mark Andrews [ELITE]
- Top 7 finishers all within 2 seconds
- Elite field maintained very tight pacing throughout
(http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2024.html)

**Start Times:** Staggered starts help manage field size - Epic at 9:00am, Medio at 9:30am, Petit at 9:45am. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events)

## Weather Experienced

**April Timing Considerations:** As an early-season April event, conditions can vary significantly. One source notes: "Be ready for the elements as this challenging race can be mucky, depending on the weather." (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events) - though this quote refers to a different May event, it highlights the variability of spring gravel events in Ontario.

**Bug Warning:** "Bug repellent is recommended!" for spring Ontario gravel events in similar terrain. (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Requirements:** Day permits are required and described as "easy to obtain." Part of the Ontario Gravel race series. (https://northernontario.travel/cycling/ontario-gravel-bike-events)

**Support Provided:** Event includes ATV support, aid stations, chip timing, and route markers. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-Race Amenities:** The event includes:
- Awards ceremony and meal post-ride
- Post-race food and beverages
- Free event photos
- Podium presentations and medals for all three distances
- "A free beer"
(https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events and https://ovcata.ca/rides/runs-and-rides-in-the-ottawa-valley-in-2025-5341.html)

**Prize Draw:** A Felt Breed Carbon Gravel Frame giveaway - each participant is entered into the draw. (https://gravelcup.com/ottawa-valley-gravel-experience)

**Event Philosophy:** Created by Brendan Gorman, who is "Based in the Gravel Cycling Mecca that is Canada's National Capital Region" and "created Gravel Cup to share his passion for gravel riding, and to provide a platform for gravel cycling enthusiasts to explore new routes, and challenge themselves on a variety of gravel genres. He's spent countless hours literally lost in the woods, curating the best gravel routes that Ottawa and it's surrounding counties have to offer." (https://ontariocycling.org/tag/gravel/)

**Registration Details:** Event typically closes registration the day before at 12pm. Series registration available at 25% off (until early March), 15% off (until late March), or 10% off (until mid-April). U19 riders receive 50% off. (https://gravelcup.com/gravel-cup-events)

## DNF Risk Factors

**2025 DNF Data:**
- Epic Course: 2 DNFs out of 35 starters (5.7% DNF rate)
- Medio Course: 0 DNFs out of 44 starters
- Petit Course: 2 DNFs out of 26 starters
(http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)

**2024 DNF Data:**
- Epic Course: 1 DNF out of 31 starters (3.2% DNF rate)
- Medio Course: 0 DNFs out of 43 starters
- Petit Course: 0 DNFs out of 49 starters
(http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2024.html)

The low DNF rates suggest the course is challenging but manageable across all distances, with most DNFs occurring on the longest Epic course.

## Participant Demographics

**Field Size:** 2025 event had 94 total finishers across all categories (Epic: 30, Medio: 40, Petit: 22, plus U19 categories). (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)

**Gender Breakdown [2025 Epic]:** 3 women, 29 men in Epic course. Francesca Seal [ELITE] was first woman in 11th overall (3:18:38). (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)

**Age Categories:** U19 categories available for all distances, showing youth participation. Josh Tyers [ELITE] won the Epic U19 with an overall victory time of 3:01:37 in 2025. (http://www.timeral.info/Archive/A1-2025.html)
