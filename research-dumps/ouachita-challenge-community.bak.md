# OUACHITA CHALLENGE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I never know what to expect from the competition any time I head west of the Mississippi... I knew I was dialed, my bike was dialed, the weather was looking super hot (which is good for me), and the course would be brutal for all!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "My race was almost over before it had really begun. Just as I entered the single track, my bike chain-sucked BIG TIME! Luckily I did not try to pedal through it as that would probably have cost me a broken chain. It took me several minutes to work it out from between the big ring and the chain stay." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "After seeing this [9 flats on Brushy Mountain] I took it a bit easier on the descents. I would rather be a couple minutes slower than on the side of the trail throwing a tube in." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "The 8 mile gravel road/pavement section that takes you from the Ouachita Trail to the Womble trail was no man's land for me today. With no one in sight I was on my own. In previous races, I was able to hook up with a group, take my share of pulls, and then tuck in behind and coast." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I got my second wind on the Womble and slowly reeled in one racer after another. Each was a grape and I, the hungry fox!" Finished in 5:33:46, first place female. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Jess Nankman [ELITE]:** "60 miles of gravel racing on my Devote Advanced Saturday and 60 miles of mountain biking on some amazing single track on my Piqued Advanced Pro 29 Sunday... Get your climbing legs ready, both days have significant climbing; they make your legs hurt but are worth the effort." Finished 5th female gravel race, 3rd female mountain bike race; 2nd for combined races. (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**Jess Nankman [ELITE]:** "Creek crossings were numerous at both races, be ready for wet feet. Temperature swings are quite possible. For this year racers woke to frost on the ground both days and with an 8am start it was still quite cold in the mornings but warmed up to the 60s/70s by the end of the race." (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**Andrew Evans [ELITE]:** "At the very least it would be good training. For the last 45 miles of the race I rode solo, getting in the aero bars as much as I could." After an 8-minute flat repair, finished 4th in gravel-only category, 14 seconds off podium. (https://andrewevans.bike/2022/03/30/ouachita-challenge-recap/)

**Arkansas Outside reporter [COMPETITIVE]:** "Years ago when I did the race, I was feeling totally beat when I hit this section [the Womble] and suddenly felt rejuvenated by the pure joy of the ride…at least that's how I remember it." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**Arkansas Outside reporter [COMPETITIVE]:** "The curse of the race and tour are the checkpoints, they have cutoffs. Miss a cutoff and you're done... The checkpoint at Gaston Mountain is notorious for ending the race for many participants." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Tire Pressure/Selection:** Carey Lowery saw 9 flats just on Brushy Mountain section in 2012, leading her to ease up on descents. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**[ELITE] Mechanical Issues - Chain Suck:** Carey Lowery experienced severe chain suck twice: "Just as I entered the single track, my bike chain-sucked BIG TIME!... This chain suck happened as I was shifting the rear derailleur to an easier gear, the shifting was a bit slow to respond, and my shock was compressing at the same time too." Later revealed chain was "too short." Bike: Niner Jet 9 RDO with 26x36 low gear. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**[ELITE] Aero Bars for Gravel:** Andrew Evans experimented with clip-on aero bars for the gravel race: "My belief was that the clip-on bars would aid me in sustaining a long range solo attack over the climb at mile 24, or help me in a drag race to the finish." Used them extensively during 45-mile solo chase. (https://andrewevans.bike/2022/03/30/ouachita-challenge-recap/)

**[ELITE] Flat Repair Kit Essential:** Andrew Evans sliced a nearly inch-long hole in tire sidewall at creek crossing: "A plug wouldn't work, but for some reason I tried that anyway. By the time I had a tube in the tire and was ready to go, I had been stopped for 8 minutes." (https://andrewevans.bike/2022/03/30/ouachita-challenge-recap/)

**[ELITE] Gearing Recommendations:** Carey Lowery wished for 24x36 instead of her 26x36 on Chalybeate Mountain's steep initial climb. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**[ELITE] Tire Sealant:** Jess Nankman notes: "Make sure your tire sealant is fresh and you carry flat fixing items; flat tires were numerous both days including Joel who did get two flats on the gravel day." They carried extra tubes and sealant from Saucon Valley Bikes. (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

## Weather Experienced

**2012:** Carey Lowery reported "super hot" conditions, temps in mid-80s, with heavy dew overnight making rocks "slicker n snot." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**2013:** Arkansas Outside reported water "well above the low water bridge for Saturday's tour and still several inches deep on Sunday" at Big Brushy Creek crossing. (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**2021:** Jess Nankman experienced "frost on the ground both mornings and with an 8am start it was still quite cold in the mornings but warmed up to the 60s/70s by the end of the race." Below freezing both mornings. (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**2022:** Andrew Evans noted "It was cold at the start, but it promised to warm up quickly and be a beautiful day in the Ouachitas." (https://andrewevans.bike/2022/03/30/ouachita-challenge-recap/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Brushy Mountain:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "Funny, several of them [guys] dug really deep to pass me back so they would not get 'stuck' behind a girl on the single track. Needless to say, I eventually passed most of them back." Saw 9 flats on this section alone. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Blowout Mountain:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "Blowout Mountain was its usual toughness with an added element of 'slicker n snot' rocks because of the heavy dew the night before. No cleaning this trail at race pace today! All the guys that I was among were off their bikes too. Faster and safer to run the rock slide sections, for sure." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Chalybeate Mountain:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "By far the tamest. The hardest part was the initial climb, steep enough that at times I was wishing for a 24 x 36 instead of my 26 x 36. All the tourists the day before kind of 'horsed it up' with their hike-a-biking. This is where I started to see some racers blow up." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Womble Trail:** Arkansas Outside [COMPETITIVE]: "The Womble Trail is known as a fast, flowy single track trail that is primarily along a ridge in the Ouachita Mountains. It is arguably among the best trails in the country and for many riders, getting to the Womble section of the race brings a sense of relief and anticipation of the relative delight of riding that flow after the ruggedness of the Ouachita climbs." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**Big Brushy Creek Crossing:** Arkansas Outside [COMPETITIVE]: "The rolling start leaving the school is fast and punctuated by crossing Big Brushy Creek... Watching the mass of bikes at the creek crossing was reminiscent of a stampede of horses ripping through the water." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**Creek Crossings on Gravel (2022):** Andrew Evans [ELITE]: "As I rode out of the water I felt the rim on my front wheel making contact with the rocks" at mile 16 creek crossing where "smooth gravel surface gave way to large rocks." (https://andrewevans.bike/2022/03/30/ouachita-challenge-recap/)

**Gravel Road Descent to Womble:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "The last gravel road climb back up to the final aid station was a kick in the behind as the sun just baked my backside. Talk about HOT! Once I crested the hill, it was mostly down hill with a nice tail wind." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Grass Finish:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "Instead of finishing up the pavement to the school, they made you grind up a steep grassy climb. I imagined myself cramping with only yards to the finish a midst a hundred + spectators." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Heart Rate Management:** Carey Lowery relies on HR monitor as "a pretty important tool for me on race day. My heart rate keeps me from not blowing up as well as not getting to pacified." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**[ELITE] Start Position Matters:** Carey Lowery: "Mid-pack was not a good place for me to be as I had wanted to get into the first or second groups for the 9 mile pavement/gravel section up to Brushy Mountain. Instead I had to slowly pace myself back up to the front over the next 5 miles all the while keeping my 'feelers' out for those who are not as experienced racing in tight packs on loose gravel." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**[ELITE] Risk Management on Descents:** Carey Lowery adjusted strategy after seeing numerous flats: "After seeing this I took it a bit easier on the descents. I would rather be a couple minutes slower than on the side of the trail throwing a tube in." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**[ELITE] Solo Pacing on Gravel Sections:** Andrew Evans: "For the last 45 miles of the race I rode solo, getting in the aero bars as much as I could" after 8-minute mechanical. Went into "chase mode, trying to catch as many racers as I could." (https://andrewevans.bike/2022/03/30/ouachita-challenge-recap/)

**[ELITE] Nutrition Strategy:** Jess Nankman: "With the races being no more than three to six hours for us we easily carried all we needed including all our Honey Stinger nutrition in our back pockets." (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**[COMPETITIVE/RECREATIONAL] Checkpoint Cutoffs:** Arkansas Outside: "The curse of the race and tour are the checkpoints, they have cutoffs. Miss a cutoff and you're done. This agony is something else I remember from my last chance at the race... The checkpoint at Gaston Mountain is notorious for ending the race for many participants." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Failures - Chain Suck:** Carey Lowery [ELITE] lost several minutes twice to severe chain suck that couldn't be back-pedaled out. Eventually had to "rethink my whole shifting thing and unfortunately that slowed me down a bit." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Flat Tires:** Carey Lowery [ELITE] counted 9 riders fixing flats just on Brushy Mountain section. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Time Cutoffs:** Arkansas Outside [COMPETITIVE]: "The checkpoint at Gaston Mountain is notorious for ending the race for many participants. Even those who make it face more climbing on the loop to Norfolk Lake and with a tough climb back up the gravel on Gaston Mountain as a reward for making all the checkpoints." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**Crashes:** Carey Lowery [ELITE] mentions Andrea Wilson "had a bad crash on the Womble" after Carey said "Keep the rubber side down!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Rider Blow-Ups:** Carey Lowery [ELITE] on Chalybeate Mountain: "This is where I started to see some racers blow up. I passed about 5 guys on this section." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Local Community Support:** Jess Nankman [ELITE]: "The local community of Oden, Arkansas and surrounding towns are very supportive of the race, tour and participants. Food, free camping, use of the high school are all great but it was the kindness and smiles that struck me. Good old Arkansas hospitality." (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**Volunteer Quality:** Jess Nankman [ELITE]: "Every person involved with this race was just so nice, caring, involved, and friendly. One could tell they all just want to race to be a success... Some locals along the course had water bottles for racers. Race volunteers were even moping the showers and refilling toilet paper. That's dedication." (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**Aid Station Atmosphere:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "Coming down into the Big Brushy aid station, I was greeted with what seemed like hundreds of screams and well wishes. By far the largest crowd that has been there since I started doing the race in 2006 ... a real motivator!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**Immediate Prize Distribution:** Carey Lowery [ELITE]: "No waiting on podiums at the OC. As you cross the line, they hand you your winnings. Sweet!" First place netted cash and an Ellsworth wheelset. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2012/04/ouachita-challenge-race-report.html)

**On-Site Camping:** Jess Nankman [ELITE]: "Camping Friday and Saturday night was allowed directly on the school campus, with racers parking their vans and setting up their tents at various locations around the grounds. The school gym was even open for sleeping if needed (and being it was below freezing both mornings people who slept in the gym were warm and cozy)." Showers available in gym. (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**Food Included:** Jess Nankman [ELITE]: "There was post race food on Sunday of personal pizzas for each racer which hit the spot perfectly after 60 miles of tough mountain biking. In addition, there was a hot breakfast for racers on Saturday and Sunday of pancakes, oatmeal, eggs, fruit, coffee and more along with and a pasta dinner on Saturday night. All the food was cooked on site in the school cafeteria and it was quite tasty. Honestly the best oatmeal I have ever had." (https://nankmancycling.blogspot.com/2021/03/ouachita-challenge-race-beauty-and.html)

**Race Popularity:** Official website notes 250 slots "sold out in about 13 minutes" for recent years. (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**Fundraiser for School:** Arkansas Outside [COMPETITIVE]: "This community has welcomed the race for 13 years now and the race attempts to repay the kindness of the community by making the weekend a fundraiser for the needs of the school and the community." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)

**Post-Race Scene:** Arkansas Outside [COMPETITIVE]: "Riders are greeted with cheers and pizza at the finish, pats on the back, a bike wash and the ultimate reward of finishing one of the toughest races in the state." Final challenge: "a grassy knoll leading up to the finish line with all the spectators watching, even with worn out legs and burning lungs, no one wants to walk this." (https://arkansasoutside.com/guts-and-glory-on-the-ouachita-challenge/)
