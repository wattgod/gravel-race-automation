# OZARK GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE - 2nd place overall 2025]:** "I'd nod off while pushing my bike up the hills and swerve wildly before continuing. Even riding down rough double-track descents wasn't enough and I'd begin to fall asleep while descending, too." (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE]:** "Having a super capable full suspension bike in a field that consisted of mostly hardtails and rigid bikes paid off and I was in the top 10% by the time we were back on dirt roads around mile 38 of the race after nearly 5 hours of racing." (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Tom Schwemberger [ELITE - 2025 Winner]:** His derailleur system malfunctioned such that "his AXS batteries would drain, irrespective of use, within a few hours. To shift, Tom would have to get off his bike, and perform an odd, complex ritual of dead and live battery swapping in order to change one gear." (https://bikepacking.com/news/tom-schwemberger-wins-ozark-gravel-doom-2025/)

**Tom Schwemberger [ELITE]:** Started "roughly 30 minutes after everyone had endeavored on those first, brutal climbs" because he "forgot his headphones and spare AXS batteries" and had to double back twice. (https://bikepacking.com/news/tom-schwemberger-wins-ozark-gravel-doom-2025/)

**Nic [UNKNOWN]:** "At the fifth or sixth river crossing, the Buffalo River, which had reached flood stage due to the rains that preceded the event, took me down and my shoes with it. Having successfully shouldered the bike over the last crossings, I'd become too confident and allowed the river to take one of my shoes." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Nic [UNKNOWN]:** "After a few minutes of searching, I figured my race was run and retraced my steps to the aid station at the start of the headwaters loop." Lost his shoe at mile ~40 and scratched. (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** "At mile 119...I stayed right at the fork when I should have gone left to Falling Water Church for a water spigot and then a few more miles to Hillbilly Slims BBQ...My computer didn't tell me I was off route, and I found myself re-routing on the Ozark National Forest hiking trail." (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** "I found that my previously tested filter was no longer filtering and needed a new filter! FML... This was the first of many water intakes that were not filtered (fingers crossed to no giardia)" (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** "After eating and shopping, I was able to 'UsE ThE ReStRoOm' if you catch my drift, and re-applied chamois stuffs for round two." - at McCormick's One Stop at mile 196. (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** "After the dreaded 20 miles of single track, I.WAS.COOKED. and certainly realized I was yet again underbiked. Many folks didn't make it through without hiking most of the section." (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE]:** Rode a Starling Murmur full-suspension with "Drop bars, aero extensions, a bar-end shifter, mechanical brakes, 2.2 inch fast rolling tires, and a custom frame bag by Merz Bags." Joe (Starling owner) said it "made him feel funny" - suggesting this was an unconventional build. (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE]:** "I was only carrying an emergency blanket as my sleep kit in hope of pushing through the night but after a couple of hours of fighting sleep it was clear that wasn't going to work." Later upgraded to: "MSR E-Bivy, Nemo Tensor sleeping pad, Nemo Fillo, and a Sea to Summit Traveller Down Sleeping Bag" (mentioned in gear list, though timeline unclear). **Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** Bike: "58cm Enve Mog Sand...Sram Force levers, crankset, calipers & Sram Transmission with a 10-52 Cassette" with "42cm Enve Gravel bars." **Ed Rogers [ELITE]:** "Bike setup in 2023 vs 2024 – from gravel to full-suspension MTB" suggesting terrain benefits significantly from suspension. (https://komcycling.com/blogs/product-blogs/dirty-chain-podcast-episode-101-ozark-gravel-doom-with-ed-rogers)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Race:** "biblical rains" preceded the event, causing a one-day delay. Race started April 6 (delayed from April 5). "Misting, frozen rains" on race day with temperatures "barely above freezing." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**2025 Race:** "Days of biblical rains and brutal conditions made even the shortest courses in this year's event to be gargantuan undertakings." Freeze warning on the first night. "Conditions will not significantly improve here in northwestern Arkansas until midday on Monday." (https://bikepacking.com/news/2025-ozark-gravel-doom-dismay-winners/)

**2025 Race:** Nic described: "Underneath pavilion and car camper, just over 200 cyclists collectively questioned their sanity. We knew what we'd signed up for. In the best of times, it was one of the most challenging courses on offer. In the worst of times? Lord knows." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**2025 Race:** Buffalo River "had reached flood stage due to the rains that preceded the event." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**2024 Race:** Angry Catfish rider notes "the heat and sun got to me pretty bad" on Day 2 after sunrise. (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE - 53hr finish]:** "After moving for nearly 20 hours straight I began to fall asleep while riding...I was only carrying an emergency blanket as my sleep kit in hope of pushing through the night but after a couple of hours of fighting sleep it was clear that wasn't going to work." Slept ~1 hour at mile 190 (22 hours in) and ~1 hour at mile 338. (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE]:** "I managed to eat about 400 calories per hour" with goal of 500 cal/hr. Carried "over 20,000 calories in an attempt to avoid getting caught out." "Hard foods like the cookies and chips began to be too difficult to eat as time continued and the leftover quesadilla and a half were too bland that far into the race to choke down." (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Jacob Ashton [ELITE]:** "Sticking to my strategy of moving consistently, I avoided stops whenever possible and ate while riding or walking up the steeper hills." (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Tom Schwemberger [ELITE - 48hr 44min winner]:** "up until this race, Tom hadn't ever used caffeine for performance on ultra races. Not being a coffee drinker, it seems the 100mg pills he used to power through each night worked like a charm." Did NOT sleep at all during the race. (https://bikepacking.com/news/tom-schwemberger-wins-ozark-gravel-doom-2025/)

**Tom Schwemberger [ELITE]:** Won despite starting 30 minutes behind the field due to forgetting gear and having to double back. (https://bikepacking.com/news/tom-schwemberger-wins-ozark-gravel-doom-2025/)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** "I was super confident coming into Doom this year. I had almost double the saddle time compared to last year but only a little climbing and virtually no MTB training." (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** At McCormick's One Stop (mile 196): "For my first meal of the race, I ordered two large servings of biscuits and gravy with bacon on top and a sausage egg and cheese croissant to go." This was the halfway point and "our last known resupply point of the race. Easter Sunday in the south, virtually all businesses are closed." (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Angry Catfish rider [UNKNOWN]:** "After riding alone for a large portion of day two, a few single-speeders going back and forth were a welcome sight." At mile 370 Kent General: "With only 37 miles left, I ordered two full double cheeseburgers and a chicken sandwich." (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Ed Rogers [ELITE]:** "The myth of big training volume – Ed's results with ⅓ of last year's volume" and "The episode also underscores...ultra-racing isn't just about fitness—it's about planning, preparation, mental resilience, and learning from experience." (https://komcycling.com/blogs/product-blogs/dirty-chain-podcast-episode-101-ozark-gravel-doom-with-ed-rogers)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Water filter failure:** Angry Catfish rider: "I found that my previously tested filter was no longer filtering and needed a new filter!" Had to drink unfiltered water for remainder of race. (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Lost shoes in river crossing:** Nic lost a shoe at Buffalo River crossing (mile ~40) and had to scratch. "After a few minutes of searching, I figured my race was run...walking five miles of rocky singletrack as the car weaved its way through the ridges of the Ozarks." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Electronic shifting failure:** Tom Schwemberger's "derailleur system malfunctioned and made it such that his AXS batteries would drain, irrespective of use, within a few hours" yet still won. This suggests mechanicals don't always mean DNF but require adaptation. (https://bikepacking.com/news/tom-schwemberger-wins-ozark-gravel-doom-2025/)

**Frame bag zipper failure:** Tom "broke the zippers on his frame bag very early on, making for an even harder time moving through the course." (https://bikepacking.com/news/tom-schwemberger-wins-ozark-gravel-doom-2025/)

**Sleep deprivation:** Jacob Ashton: "After moving for nearly 20 hours straight I began to fall asleep while riding" and "Even riding down rough double-track descents wasn't enough and I'd begin to fall asleep while descending, too." Required indoor sleeping stops. (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Navigation errors:** Angry Catfish rider at mile 119: "My computer didn't tell me I was off route, and I found myself re-routing on the Ozark National Forest hiking trail. Clearly a wrong turn..." This caused them to miss the only BBQ restaurant resupply. (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Resupply timing:** "A store that's open from 8 AM to 6 PM is no issue at all; in an ultra race, that means it's closed for 14 hours a day." Multiple riders mentioned risk of missing resupply windows, especially on Easter Sunday when "virtually all businesses are closed." (https://www.starlingcycles.com/a-murmur-of-doom-jacobs-story/)

**Being underbiked:** Angry Catfish rider 2023: "I was underbiked with 700 x 40's under geared, and overly confident." Multiple riders mentioned the 20-mile late-race singletrack section destroyed gravel bikes. (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Gremlin Bell Ceremony:** "Andrew passes out gremlin bells 15 minutes before the race starts, and EVERY rider walks around their bikes ringing them to ward off evil spirits. We then attach the bells to our bikes and depart promptly at 7:00 am." (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Nic [UNKNOWN]:** "6:27 a.m., Friday the 13th, 2022. Twenty-four grown-ass adults are walking around in circles ringing bells and there is a dude wearing a Scream mask counting down the minutes until a bike ride begins. What the F%#k is going on?" (https://theradavist.com/tag/ozark-gravel-doom)

**Nic [UNKNOWN]:** "We charged the small Gremlin Bells by ringing them while walking around our bikes before attaching them to our rigs to ward off misfortune." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Race Director's commitment:** Ed Rogers podcast notes: "The episode also underscores the cultural magic of Doom, thanks in large part to race director Andrew's commitment to each finisher—waiting in the dark, offering hugs and beers at the barn, embodying the soul of grassroots endurance racing." (https://komcycling.com/blogs/product-blogs/dirty-chain-podcast-episode-101-ozark-gravel-doom-with-ed-rogers)

**Andrew Onerma on race aesthetic:** "When asked about the semi-satanic, doom-y aesthetic of the promotional material, Andrew said, 'When people see a race out here in Arkansas, the general feeling is usually the same...There are no mountains or anything. But anyone who has ridden out here knows it's brutal. I want to show people that, and I also want to accurately represent what people are doing.'" (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Andrew Onerma [UNKNOWN]:** "Bikepacking and cycling are sort of slow-motion sports. It's high performance with a sense of style. I think what we're doing out here is badass. And I want to communicate that." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Horseshoe Canyon Ranch:** "Upon entering and traversing the 1.5mi descent into the valley to get to the trading post and restaurant, it's immediately evident that this is a special place. Beautiful bluffs, loblolly pines, creeks, and critters surround the Ranch." The venue moved from Oark General Store to Horseshoe Canyon Ranch. (https://www.angrycatfishbicycle.com/articles/doom-gravel-race-recap-pg374.htm)

**Growth:** "In 2025 it stood as the largest ultra-distance bikepacking event in the United States apart from the Tour Divide, and its reputation continues to grow as riders experience what Arkansas terrain demands." (https://www.ozarkgravelcyclists.com/doombikepacking)

**Post-race atmosphere:** Nic: "Returning to Horseshoe Canyon felt like purgatory. As a combination of Dread riders and scratches rolled in, we shared in stories and commiserations. Eventually, finishers of varying distances rolled in. Looking like they'd been through hell..." (https://bikepacking.com/news/doom-2025-scratch-report/)

**Ed Rogers podcast [ELITE]:** "The culture of Doom – community, growth, and why it's worth doing" and comparison to "the 'Mid South' of bikepacking." (https://komcycling.com/blogs/product-blogs/dirty-chain-podcast-episode-101-ozark-gravel-doom-with-ed-rogers)
