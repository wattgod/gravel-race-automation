## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile:** The first 25 miles stays relatively flat on the ridgetop with minimal elevation changes. The race ends with approximately a 12-mile hill/climb. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/lynns-story-parker-dam-gravel-grinder, https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Surface Conditions:** Mostly hard-packed gravel with very little loose gravel. Some double track sections that can be muddy depending on conditions. One group encountered a "roaring spring" where they refilled bottles around mile 13. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Notable Sections:** The Quehanna Highway section features a 3-mile downhill. Medix Run Road is a long climb on rail grade up the valley and then onto Shaggers Inn Rd (it's a pond, not a motel/hotel) over the ridge. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Course Features:** Route passes through the campground immediately onto double track at the start. The course includes private camps tucked into the countryside and very beautiful scenery. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

## Race Logistics & Course Details

**Distance Options:**
- Full Series Route: 71.7 miles, ~5,707 feet of elevation
- Half Series Route: 51.9 miles, ~4,188 feet of elevation  
- Short Series Route: 23.1 miles, ~1,680 feet of elevation
(https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Aid Stations:**
- Full Series: Aid stations at miles 25 and 48
- Half Series: Aid station at mile 28
- Short Series: No aid station
(https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Cutoff Times:**
- Full Series Aid Station #1: 11:30 AM
- Full Series Aid Station #2: 2:30 PM
- Half Series Aid Station #1: 12:00 PM
(https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Course Marking:** "Well Marked Course" is included with registration (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Difficulty Rating [COMPETITIVE]:** Listed as "Moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10051/parker-dam-gravel-grinder)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Features:** The event includes chip timing, post-race meal starting at 11:00 AM, event shirt (if registered by deadline), fully stocked aid stations, and awards for top 3 in each category. Limited to 300 participants. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Location:** Race is hosted at S.B. Elliott State Park in Penfield, PA, with camping available. The organizer has personal connection to the area: "I can account for that firsthand, I did call this county home for 20 years. It's still my home at heart, even though I no longer live there." (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Series Context:** Part of the Pennsylvania Gravel Series with multiple races throughout the year. Organized by Trans-Sylvania Productions. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Camping Experience [UNKNOWN]:** Group camping experience described with full kitchen setup for cooking breakfast (eggs, toast, sausage, fresh coffee) and dinner (brats, burgers) plus bonfires. "The campsites in our area looked like a lot space but usable tent space was really at a minimum and required strategic placement." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Group Ride Atmosphere [UNKNOWN]:** The PEC Public Lands Ride associated with the area brought together riders from Ohio and Pennsylvania, including staff from Pennsylvania Environmental Council promoting exploration of public lands. Group included stop at Big Knob fire tower. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)
