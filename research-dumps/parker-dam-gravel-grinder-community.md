## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course profile:** The Full Series route is 71.7 miles with 5,707 feet of elevation gain. Half Series is 51.9 miles with 4,188 feet of climbing. Short Series is 23.1 miles with 1,680 feet. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**The finishing challenge:** The race ends with approximately a 12-mile hill according to Lynn Botelho's race report. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/lynns-story-parker-dam-gravel-grinder)

**Ridgetop riding:** The first 25 miles stays up on the ridgetop with minimal elevation change (only a couple hundred feet up or down). (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Descent section:** The course includes a 3-mile downhill on the paved Quehanna Highway section. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Surface conditions:** The gravel is mostly hard packed with very little loose gravel sections. Two double track sections could be muddier depending on conditions. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Route markings:** Maps show dirt/gravel roads in red and paved roads in black. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Terrain character:** Described as "relatively flat course for this part of the world" by one rider, though it still features significant elevation gain. GranFondoGuide categorizes it as "Moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/lynns-story-parker-dam-gravel-grinder, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10051/parker-dam-gravel-grinder)

## Race Logistics & Format

**Aid stations:** Full Series has aid stations at miles 25 and 48. Half Series has one aid station at mile 28. Short Series has no aid station. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Cutoff times:** Full Series has cutoffs at Aid Station #1 (11:30 AM) and Aid Station #2 (2:30 PM). Half Series cutoff is 12:00 PM at Aid Station #1. Short Series has no cutoff. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Start time:** Race starts at 9:00 AM with racer meeting at 8:45 AM. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Field limit:** Limited to 300 participants. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10051/parker-dam-gravel-grinder)

**Late starters:** The race allows some riders to start late (mentioned in Lynn Botelho's report where a faster friend "was allowed to start the race late"). (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/lynns-story-parker-dam-gravel-grinder)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Scenic quality:** The route "passed by a lot of private camps tucked into the countryside" and sections were "very beautiful." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Group riding:** The Ohio Gravel Grinders group had 11 riders who rode together, with some faster riders rolling ahead while others "enjoyed the day." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2023/7/24/recap-parker-dam-gravel-adventure-via-ev)

**Post-race meal:** Included with registration, begins at 11:00 AM. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Series context:** Part of the Pennsylvania Gravel Series with points standings across multiple races. (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Local support:** Event sponsored by Visit Clearfield County. The race organizer notes: "I can account for that firsthand, I did call this county home for 20 years. It's still my home at heart, even though I no longer live there." (https://www.trans-sylvaniaproductions.com/pdgg)

**Advocacy connection:** BIAPA (Brain Injury Association of Pennsylvania) has presence at the race with board member Noah Sideman riding to raise awareness, with a BIAPA booth on site. (https://www.biapa.org/get-involved/gravel-grinders)
