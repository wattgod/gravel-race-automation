# PICKEREL BUCK40 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Shane Hitz [ELITE]:** "This route is hard, no doubt about it. It has two sections of old school singletrack the first being four miles long...It has about 20% roads that are gnarly chunk or unrelenting sand. The sand is worse this year because of the drought. This area hasn't had meaningful rain since the beginning of May so in a normal year the sand would be a bit tamer." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Shane Hitz [ELITE]:** "Definitely had a tough go at this on day one. Ended the first night at around 10:00. I was pretty gutted but thanks to a friend for inspiring me to get rest and rethink my situation in the morning. We had a long conversation that night and he told me it never always gets worse. So Tuesday I woke up, felt renewed, went through the first set of muddy two track having fun and was back in a better set of mind." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Chris Schotz [ELITE]:** "Shane is upfront about how tough some of this terrain can be. It's an adventure with a few grinds and great rewards. Riding the SoNic in one go is a lot like the infamous Crusher up in Michigan - enhanced gravel with truck road surprises." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Chris Schotz [ELITE]:** "Around mile 150 there's a ragged two-track off of Knowles Creek Road that takes you to an even more ragged scramble up to the Big Rock overlook that might be the best view on the ride...I saw a fairly good sized bear at dusk on the muddy mile made famous in the Bear 100 gravel race. The bear went away when I told it where to go." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Nick Holzem [ELITE]:** "The section after Lakewood shares the steep ATV trails as the Pickerel Buck 40, these were even harder at night despite the ATV club doing some trail repairs since earlier this year. Night riding was slower than I imagined." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Nick Holzem [ELITE]:** "I started the day with a fun singletrack warmup through Strong Falls. Then pushed my new full suspension rig up Big Rock and had a blast riding down the steep technical chute. A climb up Thunder Mountain then a quick cheeseburger and fries at Thunder Mountain Valley Inn. Butler Rock area was tough, loose big rock, and plenty of sand." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Shane Hitz** rode a bone stock Surly Karate Monkey single speed with 32/20 gearing and 2.25 tires, carrying four liters of water capacity on the related SoNic 1.2 route which shares terrain with Pickerel Buck40. (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**[ELITE] Chris Schotz** used a stripped down gravel bike with 700x50 tires for speed, noting these were "the minimum to get it done" on the technical SoNic route. He normally runs a loaded Fargo with 29x2.6 tires. (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**[ELITE] Nick Holzem** rode the new Trek Checkout full suspension bikepacking bike on the Tour de Nicolet route which shares sections with the Buck40. (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

Navigation requirements [UNKNOWN LEVEL]: "Use paper cue sheets, gpx, or your phone to navigate because the course is not marked." (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Route composition [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "For a gravel route, it is high in pavement with 33% paved but with all the difficult sections you will need those easy miles to get this route done in under 10 hours." (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

**Technical sections [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "The route is designed to take riders to remote and hard-to-get-to and unknown places often traveling through overgrown vegetation and sometimes shouldering your bike over downed trees." (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

**River crossing [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** River ford at mile 28. (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

**ATV trail conditions [ELITE - Nick Holzem]:** "The section after Lakewood shares the steep ATV trails as the Pickerel Buck 40, these were even harder at night despite the ATV club doing some trail repairs since earlier this year." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Butler Rock area [ELITE - Nick Holzem]:** "Butler Rock area was tough, loose big rock, and plenty of sand." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**ATV traffic [ELITE - Chris Schotz]:** "On the Nicolet Trail around Lakewood the side-by-side ATVs start to become abundant. Just smile and wave. They're nice enough and always have a cooler. They might offer a beer if you show off or something. It was just a Miller Lite." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Sub-10-hour target [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** The course description mentions getting the route done "in under 10 hours" as a benchmark, noting riders "will need those easy miles" from the 33% pavement to achieve this. (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

**Resupply strategy [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Be prepared to support yourself with only two resupply options, Pickerel or Waubee Lodge." (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

**Bikepacking option [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "This is a free gravel/adventure ride that can be ridden in one day or you can even bikepack it. Both options will get you eligible for the Ironbear 1000." (https://www.ironbull.org/pickerel-buck40)

**Food stops on related routes [ELITE riders]:**
- White Lake Market: "Has the best sandwiches anywhere" (Shane Hitz) and "an amazing fresh custard filled donut" (Nick Holzem)
- Northern Delites in Lakewood: burger and ice cream stand
- Waubee Lodge: "great soup and salad bar with a big menu" (Shane Hitz); "clutch at mile 140 for their soup and salad bar" (Shane Hitz on Tour de Nicolet)
(https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)

**Recovery strategy [ELITE - Shane Hitz]:** After struggling day one: "We had a long conversation that night and he told me it never always gets worse. So Tuesday I woke up, felt renewed, went through the first set of muddy two track having fun and was back in a better set of mind." (https://www.ironbull.org/fkt-leaderboard)
