## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Corey Godfrey (Race Director) [ELITE]:** "The gravel is primarily of the pea-sized variety near Lincoln, though there will be stretches of larger, chunky cherry-tomato-sized gravel on occasion. Some of the roads have become hardpack and are as fast as concrete. There's also a lot of minimal maintenance and dirt roads in the area." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** "You can be in the middle of Lincoln and in 10 to 15 minutes be on gravel in the country. It's a beautiful thing. The elevation can also vary. To the northwest of Lincoln are the Bohemian Alps, which have a lot of rolling hills." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Course Profile:** 150-mile route typically features 8,000-11,000 feet of climbing depending on year and measurement device. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Ari's Group [UNKNOWN]:** Iowa gravel near Lincoln described as "extra dry with sandy bowls everywhere" during their September 2012 ride. (https://ari-fixed-gear-pages.blogspot.com/2012/09/iowa-iowa-iowa-sunday-september-2nd-2012.html)

**2021 Specific Course Data:**
- Gravel Worlds 150: 151.1 miles, ~12,000 ft elevation
- Privateer 75: 77.8 miles, ~5,500 ft elevation  
- Buccaneer 50k: 31.6 miles, ~2,000 ft elevation
(https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**Route specifics (2021):**
- Mile 95.4: Randy Gibson Climber's Jersey awarded at this point
- Malcolm, NE appears as optional stop on course
- Valparaiso, NE appears as optional stop
(https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**Minimum Maintenance Roads:** Malcolm Tassi notes these are "forgotten double-track wagon trails that turn into nearly-impassable mud bogs if it rains." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/tag/gravel-worlds)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Colin Strickland [ELITE]:** Overcame early flat tire and still came back to win 2018 race, demonstrating the race can be won even after significant mechanical setbacks. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/tag/gravel-worlds)

**Alison Tetrick & Kae Takeshita [ELITE]:** Sprinted for finish in what was described as "the most exciting finish ever at Gravel Worlds" (2017 or 2018). Elite women's race came down to final sprint. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Self-Support Strategy:** Race is fully self-supported. Riders "must either carry everything they need for the day or resupply at our mandatory checkpoints, optional oases, or small rural town convenience stores." No outside support from friends/family allowed. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Checkpoint Strategy (2021):**
- 150-mile route: Checkpoint 1 at mile 61.7, Checkpoint 2 at mile 116.1 (cutoff 6pm)
- Optional oases and town stops provide additional resupply opportunities
- Checkpoints provide: water, Gu/Saltstick nutrition, free hot dogs, porta potties
- Food truck available (bring cash)
(https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**Start Times (2021):**
- Gravel Worlds 150: 6am start, 9pm cutoff
- Privateer 75: 7am start
- Buccaneer 50k: 8am start
(https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

## Race History & Event Details

**Event Origins:** Started in 2007 as "Good Life Gravel Adventure" hosted by Lincoln's Pirate Cycling League. 25 riders showed up the first year based on a blog post, 50 riders the second year. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Philosophy:** Corey Godfrey explains the pirate theme: "the whole pirate theme revolved around promoting and participating in events without an association to a sanctioning body or a cycling team. If you wanted to join the team or participate in an event, all you had to do was show up and ride. Don't have a team? Join the Pirate Cycling League!" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Rainbow Jersey Issue:** The UCI contacted race organizers in 2018 about using rainbow stripes, asking them to stop. Race now awards "Gravel Worlds Champion's jerseys that have rainbow-esque lettering but no stripes." Corey Godfrey: "I'd like to visit Switzerland someday, but not because I'm getting sued in international court." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Course Secrecy:** Route is not released until the week of the race. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Past Elite Winners Include:** Alison Tetrick, Colin Strickland, Kae Takeshita, Rob Evans, Rebecca Rusch, Neil Shirley, Mat Stephens (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**2020 Cancellation:** Event was cancelled due to COVID-19 safety concerns and unknowns. "We ultimately did everything we could to host a safe event, but due to ongoing safety concerns and too many unknowns we could not in good faith host our event this year." Moved to August 21, 2021. (https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/uncategorized/cancelling-gravel-worlds-2020)

**L'Eroica/Vintage Category:** Added in 2019 for pre-1988 steel bike enthusiasts. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/tag/gravel-worlds)

**Long Voyage 300:** Ultra-distance option (~300 miles, 35+ hours estimated time) added for "extra crazy Pirates." Requires GPS tracking via TrackLeaders. (https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Corey Godfrey [ELITE]:** "Riders must either carry everything they need for the day or resupply at our mandatory checkpoints, optional oases, or small rural town convenience stores. We force the riders to interact with volunteers and locals in order to sustain themselves. That helps keep the vibe grassroots and in my opinion, adds to the experience." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**"Polished, Grassroots Vibe":** Godfrey said the goal was to create an event with this specific character—professional execution while maintaining grassroots authenticity. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Nine Basic Rules:** Gravel Worlds has only 9 rules (vs. Dirty Kanza's 25). Rule #1: "Don't be lame." Godfrey explains it "covers a lot of behaviors that we feel are antithetical to the spirit of gravel grinding. Since we're pirates, we can and will disqualify those that exhibit any lame behavior." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/preview-2018-gravel-worlds-race-nebraska-lauf-true-grit)

**Event Weekend Activities (2021):**
- Wednesday: Route Discussion Party at Cycle Works
- Thursday: Demo Day, early check-in, rider hangout at Schilling Bridge Cork & Tap House
- Friday: Check-in, Rider Expo, Women's Panel, Women's Lead Shake Out Ride, Afternoon Panel, Shake Out Ride, Welcome Speech from Lincoln Mayor, Long Voyage 300 Send Off
(https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**Post-Race:** Free showers at Fallbrook YMCA from noon-9pm on race day with shuttle service from finish line. (https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**Pirate Themed Awards:** Overall winners are recognized as "Captains of the Gravel Seas" and receive swords. (https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**Community Support:** Malcolm Softball and Baseball team sells goods at ball diamonds. Valparaiso Boy Scouts sell goods. Local gas stations and stores serve as resupply points. (https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)

**#GravelFamily Culture:** From the community perspective, Joe Billesbach (riding after surviving hit-and-run with drunk driver) is described as "happiest riding where cars can't follow." The essay discusses how gravel cycling creates community by "riding away from our communities" and serves as a healthy "third place" outside of home and work. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2022/10/18/the-victory-of-third-places/)

**Robert Putnam's Research (cited in Dirt Tan article):** "If you belong to no groups but decide to join one, you cut your risk of dying over the next year in half. If you smoke and belong to no groups, it's a toss-up statistically whether you should stop smoking or start joining." Social engagement through cycling appears to provide significant health benefits beyond physical fitness alone. (https://dirttanbikeclub.com/2022/10/18/the-victory-of-third-places/)

## COVID-19 Protocols (2021)

**Safety Requirements:**
- Masks/face coverings REQUIRED at check-in
- Masks/face coverings REQUIRED at start/finish line and checkpoints
- Masks HIGHLY RECOMMENDED at all indoor locations and when unable to maintain social distancing
- Custom Gravel Worlds face buff provided to all riders
- If symptomatic or had direct COVID contact, riders asked not to attend (or get negative rapid test 24-48 hours before)
(https://www.gravelworlds.com/blog/2021-gravel-worlds-rider-info)
