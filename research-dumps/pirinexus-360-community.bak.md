# PIRINEXUS 360 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Alberto's group [UNKNOWN]:** "We only could sleep 4 hours the night before the race but we tried to be ready on time for start line and keep our mind aware for the first part of the ride where we were riding with more riders... After the first checkpoint, we took the decision to be more conservative than faster and save energies for the harder part of the route." (http://www.therawstories.com/the-pirinexus-360/)

**Alberto's group [UNKNOWN]:** "50 kilometers of hard climbing (23 km of them by gravel) were waiting for us, from Ceret (Km 180) until the top of Col d'Ares (Km 230). It was really hard and a huge nightmare for a lot of participants, and some of them left the race on that point." (http://www.therawstories.com/the-pirinexus-360/)

**Alberto's group [UNKNOWN]:** "Col d'Ares was our main goal in our minds. If we achieved it, we knew that we could finish the race on time. And finally, we did it! We arrived at the top, we wait for our friend Alberto that he did an awesome effort and then we started a crazy downhill to Camprodon." (http://www.therawstories.com/the-pirinexus-360/)

**Tom Owen [ELITE]:** "Running 25c tyres, mismatched wheels and a metal fork with an outrageous rake to it, it proved anything but nimble; it felt practically lethal on rocky descents... My water bottles froze both nights. On the first night my breakfast banana also froze." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-culture/bikepacking-the-pirinexus-an-eye-opening-ride-through-catalunya/)

**Tom Owen [ELITE]:** "After what seemed like an eternity, punctuated only by more of those tuneful boopedy-beeps, the two policemen exchanged words, then, from the Spanish he murmured into his radio, I made out the one in charge saying, 'He's sleeping' and something about a bicycle... The doors clunked open and closed again. The car drove away." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-culture/bikepacking-the-pirinexus-an-eye-opening-ride-through-catalunya/)

**Timo Rokitta [UNKNOWN]:** "On a gravel track 'Via Verde', formerly a railway line now a cycle path, deep puddles led to wet feet for 62km towards Olot. Soon after, the first of several long climbs began. In just 11 kilometers, we were over 1,000 meters above sea level, and surrounded by morning fog." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-pirinexus-challenge-gravel-ride-across-the-pyrenees-the-emporda-the-costa-brava-by-timo-rokitta/)

**Timo Rokitta [UNKNOWN]:** "After more than 13 kilometers, the borders of France were reached. An ice-cold wind was blowing but the views were fantastic. During the long descent that followed, the temperatures began to warm... Once at the summit, we rolled along a bumpy trail to the second feed station in La Jonquera." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-pirinexus-challenge-gravel-ride-across-the-pyrenees-the-emporda-the-costa-brava-by-timo-rokitta/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Choice [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "For the test all types of bikes are admitted, although the most recommended are the gravel or cyclocross bicycles. It can also be done with a road bicycle with 25 or 28-inch tires, or a 29-inch mountain bike with flat tires." (https://www.viesverdes.cat/en/noticies_actualitat/arriba-la-5a-edicio-de-la-pirinexus-360-challenge/)

**Route-Specific Bike Advice [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "The Pirinexus starts and finishes on Via Verde, a smooth gravel custom-built bike path... The next major climb of the route, the Col de Ares, is also a road climb – painfully slow on a mountain bike." (https://www.eatsleepcycle.com/bikepacking-the-pirinexus/)

**Technical Section Warning [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "After climbing a quiet lane to reach the border the road quite simply disappears and a fairly technical, awesome stretch of gravel unfolds. Here a mountain bike or an adventure gravel bike would make this section loads of fun. Anyone on tyres under 40c would be on foot. There is, of course, an unofficial alternative route on the road – the perfect choice for anyone lacking confidence with their bike skills or riding a road bike." (https://www.eatsleepcycle.com/bikepacking-the-pirinexus/)

**Bike Recommendations [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
- "Ridley Kanzo Adventure – perhaps the perfect choice for the Pirinexus. A lightweight carbon frame with tyre clearance up to 42c. Make sure your gear ratio is up to the steeper sections of the route."
- "Reilly Gradient – a great choice and better on the technical sections than the Kanzo. This is a bike which absorbs the bumps of the road for optimum ride comfort."
- "Ridley Ignite MTB – an awesome lightweight hardtail. You'll sacrifice speed on the paved road sections but you'll fly along the technical stuff." (https://www.eatsleepcycle.com/bikepacking-the-pirinexus/)

**Cautionary Experience [ELITE]:** Tom Owen rode on "a steel frame Decathlon road bike from the 1990s... Running 25c tyres, mismatched wheels and a metal fork with an outrageous rake to it, it proved anything but nimble; it felt practically lethal on rocky descents." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-culture/bikepacking-the-pirinexus-an-eye-opening-ride-through-catalunya/)

## Weather Experienced

**September 2022 [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "The night before the event there was heavy rain and some thunderstorms. This didn't prevent the ride from starting at 6am sharp in the dark... deep puddles led to wet feet for 62km towards Olot... An ice-cold wind was blowing [at the summit]... From this point, about 40 kilometers remained to the finish in Girona." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-pirinexus-challenge-gravel-ride-across-the-pyrenees-the-emporda-the-costa-brava-by-timo-rokitta/)

**Early Spring [ELITE]:** Tom Owen reported: "My water bottles froze both nights. On the first night my breakfast banana also froze. On the second night I slept with my snacks inside my sleeping bag... I rode past a hiker's hostel with a vacancy sign on the way – on the same street in fact – to the roadside ditch where I eventually drew the attention of the fuzz." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-culture/bikepacking-the-pirinexus-an-eye-opening-ride-through-catalunya/)

**Starting Strategy [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "The bad point was that, apart from the main race, there was a half race modality with 145 km and we started all together. That means that the first phase of the race was really fast if you wanted to go in front of the peloton. After the first checkpoint, we took the decision to be more conservative than faster and save energies for the harder part of the route." (http://www.therawstories.com/the-pirinexus-360/)

**Mental Goal Setting [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Col d'Ares was our main goal in our minds. If we achieved it, we knew that we could finish the race on time." (http://www.therawstories.com/the-pirinexus-360/)

**Night Riding Strategy [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "We did the latest 49 km during the night, riding really fast thru a single-track direction Girona and trying to earn some minutes." (http://www.therawstories.com/the-pirinexus-360/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Philosophy [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Most participants don't think of this event as a race, but rather a personal challenge where first and last finishers, are just as important." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-pirinexus-challenge-gravel-ride-across-the-pyrenees-the-emporda-the-costa-brava-by-timo-rokitta/)

**Pro Participation [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "For the 2022 edition, almost 100 participants were at the start in Girona on September 24th, 2020. Three professionals from the Movistar team also joined in the fun, Carlos Verona, Ana Dillana, and Hayley Simmonds." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-pirinexus-challenge-gravel-ride-across-the-pyrenees-the-emporda-the-costa-brava-by-timo-rokitta/)

**Origin Story:** "When Jordi Cantal traversed the Pirinexus route of 340km in the Summer of 2014, he did not know that at the end, deep in the night, he would have created a new challenge that has already become a legend... In 2015, Jordi Cantal turned the event into a race, which has been held every since, except for 2020 due to Covid." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-pirinexus-challenge-gravel-ride-across-the-pyrenees-the-emporda-the-costa-brava-by-timo-rokitta/)

**Local Culture Encounter [ELITE]:** Tom Owen's wild camping experience with local police: "Rather than upset the children by investigating further, the parents walked quietly past and then called the cops from a discreet distance... After what seemed like an eternity... I made out the one in charge saying, 'He's sleeping' and something about a bicycle... The doors clunked open and closed again. The car drove away... Just imagine how that scene would play out where you live. For me, a Brit, it's unthinkable. Even in the towns, Spain has a wilder sensibility, a more permissive attitude." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-culture/bikepacking-the-pirinexus-an-eye-opening-ride-through-catalunya/)

**Regional Context [ELITE]:** "Catalunya is struggling for identity. It is a place torn apart by divisions, by perceptions of being unjustly controlled and unfairly treated by a larger outside entity... there didn't appear to be a single building unfestooned with pro-independence banners or the twisted yellow ribbon that has become the independence movement's emblem." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-culture/bikepacking-the-pirinexus-an-eye-opening-ride-through-catalunya/)
