# PISGAH MONSTER CROSS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Although this was the 'easiest' of Eric Weaver's events, it was the hardest win of the season for me. The last time I 'chased' this long was the 2007 Cohutta 100 where I chased Danielle for 99 miles and ended up coming in second by a wheel." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "After the race, I talked to him about his bad luck. And no, he did not learn his lesson. It took about 70 racers passing him before he was able to fix the flat." Regarding Jacob from I-9 who had flats at multiple races. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I was riding a friend's Cannondale Super X with a 46/36 front and 11/28 cassette. I am by nature a spinner and the 36/28 was making my legs feel like concrete blocks. About 1/2 way up the climb I was sitting in 3rd place." On the 10-mile Highway 215 climb. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Just before the last aid station at the intersection of the BRP and FS 5000, there is a 6-7 mile descent with just a couple of short ups. It was here that I started shivering. As much as I like carving the turns, I was ready to done with this section and eagerly looking forward to the Yellow Gap climb." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I rolled under the finish line in a time of 4:36." For the 71-mile course. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE]:** "My experience was more like this: [suffering]. I started with no warm up, immediately went up the rocky climb for miles, past the scene of last year's crash, then down the rocky descent, up and down gravel roads by babbling brooks and rushing streams. All the time my legs were cramping and I absolutely could not believe that my brakes were not maladjusted and stuck against my rims, which would account for how goddamn slow I felt." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/2016-pisgah-monster-cross-race-report-by-jom/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE]:** "About to catch Pfaff Daddy of Gravel Cyclist. I offered up a friendly wave as I slowly rode past Pfaff Daddy, and an 'Oi!' to K-Dogg as I crawled along beside him. He was surprised to see me there, usually I'm well dropped by his 61yo arse." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/2016-pisgah-monster-cross-race-report-by-jom/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE]:** "My next goal was to create as much of a gap on my teammate on every descent, in the hope I would have a large enough buffer on the Blue Ridge Parkway, to hold K-Dogg's wheel when he ultimately caught me. There were no if's and but's about it… K-Dogg would catch me, it was just a matter of when?" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/2016-pisgah-monster-cross-race-report-by-jom/)

**K-Dogg [COMPETITIVE]:** "I love climbing. You suffer a lot but it's peaceful. You can hear birds chirping and the deep, labored breathing of fellow sufferers around you... For the last 3 years at extreme cx races I've tried hard to man up and go faster down the cliffs….but every time I get the courage to 'let it go'… I crash spectacularly. At 59 (and living in Florida) drastic improvement is just not in the cards." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE]:** "Though rain was promised, it held off until my last few miles on pavement. I started with no warm up, immediately went up the rocky climb for miles, past the scene of last year's crash, then down the rocky descent, up and down gravel roads by babbling brooks and rushing streams, and campers waking to the gorgeous morning. All the time my legs were cramping." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE]:** "Oh yeah, my friend Roger was with me. He crashed in a well-marked rut on a descent. His bike survived and he had some bleeding, but nothing serious. Fortunately he was in front of me. Otherwise I would have certainly crashed in his place." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE]:** "So yeah, I'm a Florida flatlander going up the biggest hill ever AND I've got to take a shit. That happened." On the Blue Ridge Parkway climb. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] on 2019 race:** "Historically, Pisgah Monster cross has been suited to anyone riding a hardtail mountain bike, or a gravel bike shod with a 45mm wide tyre. Seeing as not every gravel bike can squeeze in a tyre that big, that leads to compromises in tyre width and increases the requirement for good skills when it comes to descending." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] on 2019 race:** "Warming up properly cuts into socialization time, so you can probably guess how I fared on the first climb. D'oh!" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] on 2019 race:** "My teammate Jimbo rolled slowly by, and happened to set the perfect tempo for me to glob onto his wheel. Mind you, wheelsucking at 7mph / 11km/r isn't exactly saving a lot of energy, but it is nice having a teammate pace you up the climb." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] on 2019 race:** "Somewhere during the descent of 475B, Jimbo's front wheel slid out on a corner and he hit the deck. The sound of a bike and rider hitting any hard surface is unnerving at best, but I'm pleased to say Jimbo popped up almost immediately, straightened his bars and took a mental collection of things." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**merlinextraligh [UNKNOWN]:** "The climb up NC 215 to get on the Blue Ridge Parkway is steep and goes on for ever. the gravel sections are relatively smooth, although there are some substantial washboards. We used 700x40 Schwalbe Marathons. Basically a road touring tire. They did fine, and the lower rolling resistance for the paved sections was much appreciated. Gave up a little traction, but that only manifested as some rear wheel slippage climbing out of the saddle which only occurred on really steep rough sections, and was manageable." (https://www.bikeforums.net/cyclocross-gravelbiking-recreational/1114649-pisgah-monster-cross.html)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE] on modified P99+ route (115 miles, 19k ft):** "After a few of those I started to feel the flow again and got myself in gear. Down to Pressley gap, we actually rode a bit of the climb up to Hickory Knob before making one last push. Then it was finally all downhill from here. There were no PR's set on the way down, but I had a good, clean, smooth, ride down middle/lower black to roll into the parking lot just before midnight." (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE] on P99+ route:** "Andy and I had just under 19 hours total time 110.5 miles and 18,600 ft of elevation... Anyhow, the route was tough, the stats don't really do it justice. It's the hardest single pull effort I've done to date and to be honest when we were down by the fish hatchery it was a little tough to make that left to go back up 475b instead of just rolling back to the parking lot on 276." (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** Used a Cannondale Super X with 46/36 front chainrings and 11/28 cassette. She noted: "I am by nature a spinner and the 36/28 was making my legs feel like concrete blocks" on the 10-mile Highway 215 climb, suggesting she would have preferred lower gearing. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race:** Rode a 3T Exploro LTD with SRAM Red eTap AXS 12-speed, 46/33 chainrings, 11-33 cassette, ENVE G23 rims with Berd spokes, and WTB Resolute 700c x 42mm tires with Orange Seal Endurance formula. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race on tire choice:** "Historically, Pisgah Monster cross has been suited to anyone riding a hardtail mountain bike, or a gravel bike shod with a 45mm wide tyre. Seeing as not every gravel bike can squeeze in a tyre that big, that leads to compromises in tyre width and increases the requirement for good skills when it comes to descending." He understood 42mm WTB Resolute was on the narrower side for this course. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race on teammate's tire choice:** "I believe Jimbo was rolling the Schwalbe G-One tyre in 700c x 40mm, which I felt wasn't the best choice for this course." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE]:** Rode an aluminum Blue Norcross SP cyclocross frame with Velocity A23 wheels, WTB Nano 40mm front tire and MSO Explorer 40mm rear tire, at ~40 PSI front and ~45 PSI rear, with tubes (not tubeless). He noted: "I've never ridden tubeless. But I've never had problems with flats and I am afraid to try new things." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

(https://www.bikeforums.net/cyclocross-gravelbiking-recreational/1114649-pisgah-monster-cross.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Descending the BRP. Photo credit Brad O Allen. I popped off their wheels a couple times on the steeper descents and had to reel them back in on the climbs. ## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] - 2013 race, clockwise direction:** "The descent off Gloucester was tricky technical as it was riddled with stutter bumps. Bottles were being 'yard saled' right and left. Fortunately, mine stayed put." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "The three of us along with a handful of guys eventually regrouped on the 10 mile climb up Hwy 215... This race is not only about fitness, but about tactics as well. More of a road race than MTB race, you need to be able to find a group that you can hang with, sit in the draft, take pulls, and thereby conserve energy. The wind is your enemy and if you get caught on the BRP all by your lonesome (happened to me last year), you will eventually wilt." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] on Blue Ridge Parkway descents:** "In some of the steepest parts, the guys were coasting and pulling away from me, even though I was spinning my hardest gear at 120 rpm+!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] on FS 5000 descent:** "It was so bumpy that my eyeballs were bouncing around in my skull, making it difficult for me to see the roadbed. After a couple squirrely moments, I had to slow down in order to focus." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] on Yellow Gap climb:** "After flushing the lactic out of my legs in the beginning mile, I was able to ramp it up and make short work of this 3 mile climb. I spied Jane and reeled her in. I felt like I was flying up this climb." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE] on first gravel climb:** "The gravel surface resembled the aftermath of a B-52 carpet bombing, potholes and ruts everywhere. Pandemonium!" He was advised pre-race to position on the left or right side of the road, not the middle. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE] on Blue Ridge Parkway climb:** "This is a tough sector of the route, climbing from about 1000 metres / 3,000 feet up to 1,450 metres / 4,750 feet in the space of 13 kilometres / eight miles... Along the way, grades average anywhere from 8% to 13%." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/2016-pisgah-monster-cross-race-report-by-jom/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race on first climb:** "Pictured above is the elevation profile of the first climb, courtesy of my Garmin 830. One mile of warm-up followed by about five-ish miles of sustained climbing. Red = getting nice and steep." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race:** "Remember earlier when I spoke about this race favoring bigger tyres? This is when they come in handy." Referring to descents after the first climb. "During the course of this descent, we caught and passed K-Dogg, who was descending like his spirit animal, the plucked chicken." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE] on modified P99+ route (115 miles):** "After a bit of hiking in the dark, the click of bike shoes in pedals and we've got a rider. Time to really get the party moving. After the hike of Black, we turned onto Turkeypen descending into the glow of the rising sun. The trail was in great shape and we were having a blast." (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE]:** "Then onto 140A where we were greeted with freshly bulldozed doubletrack road like surface. Which made for some slow going and choice vocabulary. Then in one of the rolling sections I picked up just enough speed to catch a root and stop my bike instantly sending me bouncing off the top tube before hitting the rocks below. Elbow and knee a little banged up but merely a flesh wound." (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE]:** "The descent down to the Buckhorn gap shelter is one I don't get to do very often but man is it a hoot, and in the dark no less, I was starting to feel alive again. Then we had to hike back up Black mtn for the second time of the day." (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] - 4:36 finish time:** "When the gun went off, there was no need to go anerobic, as we had a police escort all the way up Hwy 276. It was a nice start to what was eventually going to be hammerfest. Once we turned off onto the Fish Hatchery road, the race really began. My legs were quite happy climbing up to Gloucester Gap, but so were everyone else's. Immediately 3 women passed me at warp speed. I had to let them go, knowing that I could not sustain that kind of pace." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I did not stop at the first aid station as I was lucky enough to be in a good group on this 24 mile stretch [Blue Ridge Parkway]. By no means was it easy-peasy, but it did allow me to recover from the climb. I still had to get on the gas ... on the descents!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] on cramping:** "On one of the last climbs, my right hamstring decided my effort was a bit too much and began to cramp. I let off the gas, grannied down, and it went away pretty quickly. Assessing my nutrition, I realized I was not drinking enough. So over the next 15 minutes I killed a bottle." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE] on final push:** "I spoke to her briefly as I passed and then raced like a 'scalded dog,' hoping she would not hang onto my wheel, as she has a big engine. I was afraid to look back, but after a few minutes took a glance and saw her in the distance. I did not let up, but hunkered down and treated the last 12 miles like a TT." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2016 race:** "I reminded myself of a few things. Don't look at the heart rate monitor – relax – breathe deep – look at the riders ahead – ride by feel – and *try* to hang onto the rear of the front group for as long as reasonably possible, but without going into the red." Early in the first climb. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/2016-pisgah-monster-cross-race-report-by-jom/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2016 race:** "I always start conservatively and my legs felt A-M-A-Z-I-N-G – as amazing as a bloke of my age and lack of ability can feel 🙂 – I threw caution to the wind, upped my tempo, ignored the pounding of my heart in my chest and closed the gap to my teammates." After deciding to bridge up to teammates mid-climb. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/2016-pisgah-monster-cross-race-report-by-jom/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race:** "My teammate Jimbo rolled slowly by, and happened to set the perfect tempo for me to glob onto his wheel. Mind you, wheelsucking at 7mph / 11km/r isn't exactly saving a lot of energy, but it is nice having a teammate pace you up the climb." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race:** "I took my sweet time refilling bottles and imbibing in the free food on offer – half a PB&J sandwich, some cashews, a couple of Fig Newtons and a top up of both bottles with flat Coke... it was at this point I realized fueling at the aid stations would be my preferred modus operandi for the remainder of the race." At Aid Station #1, 31 miles in. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE] on 115-mile P99+ route:** "Ryan, Andy, and I started up the climb, knowing that we were only about 1/3 of the way done we had to conserve a little, but after all that trail it was hard not to roll that pavement a little more quickly. I felt like we did a pretty good job of dialing it back, then I watched some of the video I took and It looked like we were moving a little more quickly than I thought (my legs would end up agreeing with that consensus later)." On 276 to Blue Ridge Parkway climb. (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

**Matt Sweeney [COMPETITIVE] on late-race strategy:** "I had stashed a small cooler at the bottom of 477 with some cokes and oreos knowing that the spirits might be a little low at that point. Andy and I stopped and grabbed a quick coke, a few oreos before setting out on the last real leg." Around 11.5 hours in. (https://racingbikesraisingkids.com/2021-pisgah-99/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Seeing smiling Shanna, I knew I was indeed in Pisgah!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Once again, I must commend all the volunteer efforts as this race was well organized. NASCAR style aid stations are what I like and that is what I received!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2013/09/pisgah-monster-cross-race-report.html)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE]:** "Gravel races are a much friendlier affair than the roadie races I did all those years ago. Do you reckon I'd be able to squeeze into the front row, last minute, to line up for the race? Admittedly, my place there was undeserved, but sometimes, you have to go where you shouldn't, all in the name of capturing good images for the race report and video! Thanks to everyone who let me sneak in." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**Dave Jordan [COMPETITIVE]:** "I will certainly do this race again. As a bonus the new Oskar Blues Brewery is only two miles from the park entrance. You should go there. They had a cask-conditioned imperial red with ginger and lime that I recommend. The brewery is a shrine to beer and bike love." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pisgah-monster-cross-challenge/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race:** "In the days leading up to the race, I conversed with one of the locals concerning tyre choice... I socialized with many of the friendly people I've gotten to know who are regulars to this race, or residents of the area." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race:** "Race promoter Eric Wever politely reminded to keep it fun and be kind to motorists on the Blue Ridge Parkway – always good advice – and a few minutes later, released the horde!" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2019-pisgah-monster-cross-challenge-western-north-carolina/)

**JOM [COMPETITIVE] - 2019 race on aid stations:** "The volunteers at this race were great. You didn't even have to stop. I hung out for a bit longer to watch Chris finish it up and had a celebratory beer with him as well. It's always really exciting to share that experience with friends, even though you may not ride together all day it still really feels like a shared experience (at least in my mind)."
