# POLISH GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Paweł Pieczka [ELITE]:** Won the 2017 Race Through Poland in 3 days, 2 hours, and 49 minutes as the defending champion from the first edition (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)

**Ben Davies [ELITE]:** Finished third in the 2017 Race Through Poland, arriving a full day after the runner-up (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)

**Adam Toupalik [ELITE]:** 29-year-old Slovenian won the 2025 UCI Gravel Adventure Poland (128km) in 3:51:19 (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/06/08/uci-gravel-adventure-poland-results-350-hardcore-riders-battle-mother-nature/)

**Hanna Nilsson [ELITE]:** 33-year-old Swedish rider who switched from road racing to gravel in 2025, won the women's category at Gravel Adventure Poland in 4:56:01 (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/06/08/uci-gravel-adventure-poland-results-350-hardcore-riders-battle-mother-nature/)

**Pontus Tidemand [ELITE]:** "Rally Poland is a rally that I really like. Last year we were in the lead when we got technical problems, but we took with us the fact that we lead for most of the event. The year before, 2015, we finished second so I have good memories from here" (https://www.pontustidemand.se/tidemand-looks-forward-to-polish-gravel-action/)

## Weather Experienced

**2017 Race Through Poland:** The weather was brutal and the primary cause of DNFs. Started sunny, then deteriorated into "przelotne deszcze, mżawki z mgłami, nocne ulewy" (passing rain, drizzle with fog, nighttime downpours), and finally "niewielkie śnieżyce i mocne opady deszczu" (light snow and heavy rain). Temperatures in higher mountain sections frequently dropped below 0°C. The majority of participants withdrew due to "wyziębienia" (hypothermia/cold exposure). (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)

**2025 Gravel Adventure Poland:** "Although the race started in dry conditions, that quickly changed. Dense pea-soup fog enveloped the mountainous course as wind whipped through the dense forest and rain turned the gravel route into a series of mud puddles." (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/06/08/uci-gravel-adventure-poland-results-350-hardcore-riders-battle-mother-nature/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Poland Gravel Race (500+ km format):** The route is over 500km with over 10,000m of elevation gain, running through Przemyśl foothills, Bieszczady, Western Beskids, Pieniny, and Tatras. Start is in Przemyśl at Niepodległości Square; finish at Głodówka chalet in Bukowina Tatrzańska. The first climb from Przemyśl old town to Tatar Mound is described as "foreshadowing of the challenges that await." (https://pgr.kolo-ultra.pl/en/)

**Rally Poland characteristics [ELITE perspective]:** "The Masurian Lake District and the city of Mikołajki... characteristic sandy roads hide many challenges. Potentially devastating stones are hidden not only by the sand, but also by the tall grass linings, which also gives the drivers limited visibility when approaching the corners and cutting too much is a big risk... The roads are fast and rhythmic but the sand can easily get rough during the day." (https://www.pontustidemand.se/tidemand-looks-forward-to-polish-gravel-action/)

**Rondo MYLC design philosophy:** Polish brand Rondo designed the MYLC for "wide doubletrack gravel with long sweeping corners and straight lines" - indicating this represents a common Polish gravel terrain type. (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/products/rondo-launches-super-slack-descent-oriented-mylc-gravel-bike)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Race Through Poland format [ALL LEVELS]:** Self-supported ultramarathon with mandatory checkpoint sections that must be completed in order and within time limits. Routes between checkpoints can be chosen by riders (avoiding national roads), creating varied strategies. The 2017 event had a ~5 day time limit for 1300-1500km depending on route choice. (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)

**Checkpoint structure [ALL LEVELS]:** The Poland Gravel Race included mandatory sections at: Wrocław-Tąpadła, Świeradów-Zdrój-Stóg Izerski, Czech Praděd, Pieniny section with Trzy Korony shelter, Jurassic section through Ojców, and Wzgórza Trzebnickie with Prababka before the finish. (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)

## DNF Risk Factors

**2017 Race Through Poland catastrophic attrition:** Of 74 starters (including 7 pairs), only 8 finished - all solo riders. The majority withdrew due to "wyziębienia, otarć, kontuzji, awarii większych i mniejszych" (cold exposure, chafing, injuries, major and minor mechanical failures) or after "patrząc na prognozy pogody i oceniając własne szanse" (looking at weather forecasts and evaluating their own chances). This represents an 89% DNF rate. (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-polske)

**2025 Gravel Adventure Poland:** Of 350 competitors attempting to qualify for the UCI Gravel World Championships, Mother Nature was described as showing "no mercy" with the combination of fog, wind, rain, and mud. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/06/08/uci-gravel-adventure-poland-results-350-hardcore-riders-battle-mother-nature/)

**Hidden hazards [ELITE perspective]:** "Potentially devastating stones are hidden not only by the sand, but also by the tall grass linings" - cutting corners carries significant puncture/damage risk. (https://www.pontustidemand.se/tidemand-looks-forward-to-polish-gravel-action/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Through Poland organization:** Described as having "doskonałą organizację imprezy: od biura zawodów, przez monitoring, przejazd przez Wrocław w asyście policji, organizację punktów, stały kontakt z dyrekcją wyścigu, ekipy foto-video na trasie" (excellent event organization: from race office, through monitoring, passage through Wrocław with police escort, checkpoint organization, constant contact with race direction, photo-video teams on the route). The main organizer Paweł 'Pik' Puławski brought in top European ultramarathon competitors, allowing Polish riders to compete "na własnym podwórku" (on their own turf). (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)

**Finish rewards:** All finishers at Głodówka chalet receive "Champagne and breathtaking views of the Tatra mountains." Race packs include "cycling cap with your race number, drop bag transfer from start to finish point (limited 1pc., 8 kg, 15 litres), live tracking for dot-watchers, personal accident insurance, media service, giveaways." Finishers within time limit receive "hot meal, finisher medal and finisher t-shirt." (https://pgr.kolo-ultra.pl/en/)

**Gravel growth in Poland:** "Ultramaratony rowerowe, w szczególności w formule samowystarczalnej, zyskują coraz większą popularność w naszym kraju" (Ultramarathon cycling, particularly in self-supported format, is gaining increasing popularity in our country). Events mentioned include Wisła1200, Carpatia Divide, and Polish Gravel Race. (https://velonews.pl/posts/6256-race-through-poland-samowystarczalnie-nie-tylko-przez-polske)
