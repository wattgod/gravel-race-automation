# PONY EXPRESS 120 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Sue Hageman [UNKNOWN]:** "My brain and body are tired today! Pony Express 120 Gravel Dash was my longest and hardest event this year! Weather was perfect. ❤️❤️... I did the 65 miler with 3793 feet of elevation. The first third and last third were definitely the toughest. Hills were steeper but not hill after hill like Gravel Worlds. We actually had several 'flat' sections... Sometimes I wanted to cry when I saw a hill!!! No crying happened!!! The weather couldn't have been more perfect! My time wasn't what I had hoped for. But happy with my result. I'm finally learning not to compare myself to the other riders. I need to ride my ride and not someone else's." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sues-story-pony-express-120-gravel-dash)

**Anonymous blogger [UNKNOWN]:** "The first 21 miles bump along toward the small town of Barnes for the first checkpoint... The day heats up and the dirt roads get rougher. By the time I reach the second checkpoint at mile 43, I'm ready for a break. I plop down in the air conditioned Palmer Cafe for a cheeseburger, fries, and coke. No, that's not racing food. That's bikepacking food... The short cut blasts 14 miles straight east on moderately thick county gravel right through a relentless progression of rolling hills in the shadeless 95 degree heat. It's reminiscent of Gravel Worlds with its never-ending waves of hills across a vast sea of gravel." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Same blogger on Saturday's conditions:** "At the first hint of daylight, the camp stirs to life under threatening skies and a steady northerly wind. With heavy rain forecast, Race Director Mark Hoffman sends us on the 'wet' route, an alternative route home that replaces miles of dirt roads with good gravel and pavement... at 7:00 am with our first pedal stroke from camp, the first rain drops fall. With only 28 miles to ride back to Marysville, the 50-some degree rain is not a big deal, but the rain does not stop all morning. Nor does the wind. It is a wet, windy, cool, muddy ride all the way home." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Carter (teenage first-time bikepacker) [UNKNOWN]:** "Carter looks absolutely shelled when arriving at camp, slumps over in a chair, and moves not a muscle for maybe an hour. Eventually, he revives. By morning, he's a colt busting out of the corral. Carter ultimately finishes second in the race home and is a living example of the recuperative powers of youthful exuberance. And sleep." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Sue Hageman [UNKNOWN]:** "I drank and ate very well. First time ever I had to go to bathroom 20-30 miles in. Mentally I did surprisingly well. Even though the last 15 miles were rough... I'm finally learning not to compare myself to the other riders. I need to ride my ride and not someone else's." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/sues-story-pony-express-120-gravel-dash)

**Bikepacking strategy [UNKNOWN level riders]:** Multiple riders took the "short cut" option at mile 43 (Palmer Cafe checkpoint) rather than continuing the full 120-mile route on Day 1. The blogger notes: "Do I continue on the 120 mile route for another 46 overheated, hilly, rough miles? Or do I ride an alternative route 16 miles straight to camp? Until the very recent past, that question would not have entered my mind. I would have simply continued the 120 mile route without a second thought. No longer. Today I decide to spin the short cut to enjoy several more hours hanging out with everyone at camp. That still means a 59 mile day on a loaded gravel bike on a whole lot of rough roads." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Checkpoint locations:** Barnes at mile 21, Palmer Cafe at mile 43 (air-conditioned cafe closes after lunch service - plan accordingly). (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Anonymous blogger [UNKNOWN]:** "Race Directors Renee and Mark Hoffman of Backroads Bicycles pull together a committed team and a legion of volunteers to create an energetic, festive event for everyone. Their gravel family continues to grow and this event is a big reason why... Boisterous volunteers Peggy and Tiffany, both of whom will race on Saturday, re-supply riders with water, snacks, and encouragement." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Thursday Bikepacking Swap Meet:** "New this year is a Bikepacking Swap Meet on Thursday night, followed by Q&A sessions by veteran bikepackers Peggy Waite Bradley (Cannonball 550), Aaron Apel (Great Plains Route), and Paul Brasby and me (Great Divide Mountain Bike Route). Lively interactions between passionate bikepackers extend past sunset and into the evening. It all ends far too soon." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Friday night camp atmosphere:** "With the backdrop of a fiery sunset, we gather around the camper over burgers, snacks, and drinks. New friends connect. Old friends re-connect. All share experiences of the day, of days past, and of days to come. It's a bikepacker rendezvous. This unique event attracts both bikepacking veterans and new comers. The old timers may have more stories, but not necessarily better ones... Nineteen bikepackers gather at camp, all told, and elevated chatter fills the air well into the evening." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Host camp experience:** "Our host Jon Naaf marks our campsite with a cozy oasis of chairs and coolers wedged into the camper's sliver of shade. He enthusiastically hails riders as they trickle in, first from the short cut and then from the main route. All are hot, tired, and grateful for relief. In addition to a hearty greeting, Jon offers cold drinks and snacks, cooks gourmet burgers, and gathers all to share a peaceful evening." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**First-time bikepacker experiences:** "Jamie and Richard ride with their Boy Scout sons Noah and Charles, all experienced hikers and campers, but first time bikepackers. Their excited, joyful tales of their day long ride to camp bring smiles and laughter all around. Competitive triathlete Jeff shifted to bikepacking to ride with his teenage son Carter, again both first time backpackers." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Finish line amenities:** "Notwithstanding the conditions, I see nothing but smiles and hear nothing but good cheer from all the bikepackers at the finish. A hot shower certainly helps, along with homemade pie and ice cream, other food and drinks, a Finisher's towel (appropriate), and my door prize (concentrated bike cleaner, again appropriate)." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Event assessment:** "For beginners, the Pony Express is a low risk introduction to bikepacking. For veterans, it's a celebration and gathering of kindred spirits." (https://blackhillsbackbone.blogspot.com/2022/09/2022-pony-express-120-bikepack.html)

**Official event description:** "Our goal for the Pony Express 120 Gravel Dash is to provide the Gravel Grinder community with a challenging ride and an opportunity to explore some of the history of the great mid-west. Modeled after other great gravel rides/races in this area, this event is for the self-sufficient minded cyclist who enjoys being pushed to their limits. At times this is a remote area but with enough towns along the way to resupply with support crew locations." (https://onemarysville.com/pxgd120/)

**Series affiliation:** "Part of the Great Plains Gravel Cup race series" (https://onemarysville.com/events/pony-express-120-gravel-dash/)
