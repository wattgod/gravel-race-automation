# PONY XPRESS GRAVEL 160 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course composition:** 96% gravel county roads and 4% blacktop county roads winding throughout northwest Las Animas and southern Huerfano County. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Starting elevation:** Cokedale staging area at 6,300 feet. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Initial climb:** First 13 miles gain 1,200 feet elevation. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Major descent:** 12-mile downhill after mile 13 through the Sangre de Cristo foothills, featuring weathered homesteads, mining ruins, windmills, and views of snow-dusted Spanish Peaks. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Highway of Legends section:** Fast descent around mile 45 leading to the second aid station. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Remote section:** Parallels a babbling brook with mining ruins in bear country. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Biggest challenge:** 20-mile climb up to 8,800 feet summit starting around mile 55-60. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Final 30 miles:** Mostly downhill from the 8,800-foot summit back to finish, though with some "gotcha!" climbs mixed in. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Gravel quality:** "Fine gravel roads, perfect for light tires" according to official race details. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Elevation range:** 6,000 feet to 8,800 feet. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Navigation issues:** A 160-mile rider got lost multiple times, going off-course at mile 10, again later in the race, and a third time, reportedly following a malfunctioning Garmin GPS. Tony Huston encountered this same rider three separate times throughout the course. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Navigation requirements:** "All participants must have a GPS, as the course is isolated with limited markings. Maps will be available. No sag support is provided." (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Course marking:** "Limited course marking arrows" combined with GPS and handlebar-mounted map required for successful navigation. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Remote sections:** "Areas of the course are very remote with limited mobile phone reception." Portions of the course have no mobile phone reception, meaning calls or texts cannot get through. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Minimum pace requirements:** Riders must maintain a minimum speed of 10 MPH for the 160K race. For the 85K and 48K, riders must maintain a minimum speed of 8 MPH. Participants cycling slower will find feed stations closed and will receive a DNF. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Self-sufficiency warning:** "This is one event where you cannot ride till your too tired and then pull over and expect someone to ferry you home. No support crews on race course." (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Altitude for flatlanders:** Tony Huston and Pat Smith traveled from Houston (elevation -34 feet) to race start at 6,300 feet with a high point of nearly 9,000 feet with zero acclimatization time. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

## Weather Experienced

**2015 race conditions:** Start temperature was 45 degrees at 7:00 AM in Cokedale. Tony Huston noted it was "quite a smack in the face since we'd just slugged out of the soul-wilting 85 degree sauna of Houston." He removed his undershirt and leg warmers as the day warmed. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Typical conditions:** Race organizers state typical temps range from 45 degrees to 78 degrees. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Wind protection:** "Most of the course is in calm sheltered areas providing protection from high winds." (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Aid station locations [ALL LEVELS]:** Feed stations at miles 25, 50, and 75 for the 160K. The 50-mile feed is at the start/finish where a drop bag can be left. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Aid station supplies [ALL LEVELS]:** Each fueling station stocked with 2.75 water bottles per rider. Hammer Nutrition supplies energy drinks, energy gels, and assorted energy/granola bars. Also water, bananas, and cookies available. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/ and https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Crew access [ALL LEVELS]:** Support crews can only give support at fueling stations and must use Interstate route to reach the 25/75 mile station (approximately 30-minute drive). No crew vehicles allowed on race course. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Mental approach [ELITE - Houston flatlanders]:** Tony Huston: "We didn't come to Colorado to compete. We came to ride and to gawk at gorgeous mountain scenery." They stopped multiple times to take pictures during descents. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Recommended equipment [ALL LEVELS]:** Head and tail light (micro light acceptable), helmet, and GPS for navigation. Maps should be mounted to handlebars for easy navigation. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Tire suitability [ALL LEVELS]:** Course described as "fine gravel roads, perfect for light tires." (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Clothing strategy [ELITE - Tony Huston]:** Started with undershirt and leg warmers at 45 degrees, removed them as day warmed. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race size (2015):** In its second year, approximately 15 cyclists at the staging area for the 7:00 AM start of the 90-mile distance. Participant info was marked as "private" on registration site. Tony Huston noted: "I'm betting this thing gets bigger over the next several years." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Race organizer:** Phil was described as incredibly relieved and happy when the last riders came through, giving "bro-hugs" at the final aid station. His son Jesse also helped man aid stations. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Start times:** 160-mile riders start at 6:00 AM, 90-mile at 7:00 AM, 50-mile at 10:00 AM. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Event evolution:** For 2027, race format changed to make the 160K/100 mile event 2 laps of the 85K/50 mile course instead of the previous iconic 160K/100 mile loop. Added a new 50K/30 mile distance and single speed class for 85K/50 mile. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Historical theme:** Event themed around challenges Pony Express riders faced in the 1860s, requiring speed, endurance, mental toughness, navigation, weather reading, and food management with only self-reliance. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Scenic highlights:** Course showcases Sangre de Cristo Mountains beauty with rolling foothills, forests, open plains, views of Fisher Peak and Spanish Peaks, and historic mining areas including the nationally recognized Ludlow Memorial. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Historic landmarks:** Course passes weathered homesteads, mining ruins, charming windmills, coke ovens, and a small mostly-intact jailhouse with rusted iron bars that likely housed miners who had too much whiskey. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/pony-xpress-gravel-160-ride-report-by-tony-huston/)

**Traffic:** "With only 20 kilometers of blacktop road and few cars, all race distances boast entirely new terrain and quiet riding." Roads open to public vehicles and wildlife, though little traffic in the area. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Camping option:** Las Animas County fairgrounds available for weekend camping at $25 per night, includes restrooms and very limited electricity/water use. Address: 2000 N Linden or 2020 N Linden, Trinidad, CO 81082. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Awards timing:** Lunch starts at 1 PM. Awards at 2 PM for 85K/50 mile state championship, 2:30 PM for Pro class state championship, 5 PM for 160K/100 mile distance. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/pony-xpress/details)

**Race status:** Event paused for 2026, returning May 2027. (https://coloradogravelgrinderchampionship.com/)
