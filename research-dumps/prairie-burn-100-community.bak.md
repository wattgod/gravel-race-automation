# PRAIRIE BURN 100 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**From The Scarlet & Black article [UNKNOWN]:** The Prairie Burners emphasize inclusivity and community building. **Laura Ferguson '90:** "It's community building, but it's also getting people out together, both in an activity and in supporting commerce." After Tuesday rides, the group visits Grinnell Craft Brewhouse together and orders from local restaurants. (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/)

**Tim Stahl, president of Imagine Grinnell [UNKNOWN]:** "There's always a place for help and mentorship, and we just try to thread that needle carefully so we're not being too bossy." He also notes: "You can talk to people, hang out and talk, whereas on a bike path, even a Rails-to-Trail, you can't get more than three or four people riding at the same time." (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/)

The event is explicitly described as "not competitive" in nature: "It is chip-timed with competitive options, but riders and runners can get out of it what they desire. This event is about having a good time, making connections and promoting all the good that comes from cycling." (https://www.prairieburn100.com/)

Post-race activities include:
- Beer garden with Solera beer and wine (11 AM - 3 PM)
- Food trucks
- Live music by The Surf Zombies (12 PM - 2 PM)
- Yoga in the park (9:15 - 10:15 AM)
- Farmer's Market (9:30 AM - 12 PM)
- Storytime Art in the Park for families (10:30 AM - 12 PM)
(https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Tim Stahl [UNKNOWN]:** "We think of Iowa as flat, but you leave town, there are some impressive hills." The terrain is described as "Moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs." (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/ and https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/7925/prairie-burn-100)

Iowa has approximately 75,000 miles of gravel road statewide, with over 700 miles in Poweshiek County alone. **Tim Stahl [UNKNOWN]:** "Iowa has some of the best gravel roads in the world." (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/)

The course is described as looping, with all routes returning to Central Park at various points for refueling. The 25 and 50 mile routes have marked turns with arrows, and all routes are available as Ride With GPS files. (https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tim Stahl [UNKNOWN]:** "What makes a gravel bike is the geometry. It's pretty forgiving, with low, thick tires, and then gearing so you can climb the hills." Traditional road bikes are possible but not recommended. (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/)

For winter riding, the most experienced Tuesday riders use "fat, studded tires for traction on icy roads around town" when temperatures drop to 5-10 degrees. (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/)

**Official rules [UNKNOWN]:** "Front & rear blinky lights are a good idea." Helmets are required to ride. (https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

Free gravel bike rentals are available at Bikes to You for those who want to try the sport. (https://www.prairieburn100.com/ and https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

Aid stations are located at:
- 10 miles (with van bailout option)
- 25 miles (Central Park)
- 35 miles (on route)
- 50 miles (Central Park)
- 75 miles (roving aid station)
(https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

**Official timing structure [UNKNOWN]:** Official timing ends at 3:30 PM and bailout support for riders ends at 5:00 PM. Riders should prepare accordingly. (https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

The event includes "Burner Challenge Segments" — two segment challenges located on the first and second 25 miles. Winners in male, female, and non-binary categories receive $50. Results are verified via Strava. Winners are determined by 3 PM. (https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Official rules [UNKNOWN]:** "Know how to fix a flat and/or ride with someone who does. Please have a cell phone and a bailout plan as we'll have limited ability to pick up riders with mechanicals." The race director can be reached at 641-990-0216 for assistance during the race. A final sweep of the entire course is provided. (https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

**Safety concern noted by Laura Ferguson [UNKNOWN]:** "For me personally, I think I know of three cyclists who have been killed on Poweshiek County roads, one person who is paraplegic and so, you know, the paved roads don't feel very friendly." This is cited as a reason the gravel roads are safer — the course is open to vehicle traffic but has less traffic than paved highways. (https://thesandb.com/53911/sports/prairie-burners-group-ride-blazes-through-iowas-gravel-roads/ and https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

**Weather policy [UNKNOWN]:** "The PB100 will occur rain or shine! All bike routes will have hard surface rain re-routes if needed." (https://www.prairieburn100.com/event-info)

## Weather Experienced

No specific race day weather reports were found in the sources. The event typically occurs on the second Saturday in June (June 6, 2026 and June 14, 2025 dates confirmed). (https://www.prairieburn100.com/ and https://www.imaginegrinnell.org/events/prairie-burn-100-gravel-bike-ride-and-fun-run)
