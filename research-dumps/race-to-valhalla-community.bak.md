## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Winding Stairs Road Climb:** The signature climb starts from scenic Lake Cherokee on a steep winding gravel road that "can take the better part of a half hour to climb" and comes in the early stages of the course. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Surface Conditions [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** From a course recon video description: "Lots of long punishing climbs and tricky, fast, loose descents." The surface composition is described as "very similar" to Valley of the Giants, featuring "some smooth crush, some chunky" gravel representative of U.S. Forest Service road system mountain gravel. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jNxglHo4qu0 and https://gravelroll.com/theholler)

**Course Character:** Long gravel roads with no traffic, riding along the Chattooga River. The route is described as "the most demanding and scenic course" in the Southeast Gravel Series. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Downtown Finish:** Riders finish coming down Main Street (closed to traffic) for "an amazing final mile stretch to the finish" while the town comes out to watch and cheer. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Town Support:** The entire town of Walhalla comes out for the event, which is held in conjunction with the Mother's Day Art Walk festival (note: some sources also mention April timing). Main Street is closed to traffic for the finish. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Post-Race:** Free hot meal and cold beer awaiting at the finish line. The event has "a festival type atmosphere" with riders encouraged to stick around and cheer on fellow finishers. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Family-Friendly:** With camping, bustling downtown, shopping, and festival activities, it's described as having "fun for the whole family" with options to come for the day or stay the whole weekend. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Pro-Like Experience:** "Feel like a pro as you ride down Main Street all the way to the finish line while the town comes out to watch and cheer." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Organizer Commitment:** Southeast Gravel's stated goals include providing "a safe, fun, and challenging event," instant scoring and results, high-quality race photos for social media, GPS and signage for navigation, and creating an experience that makes participants want to return. (https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/)

## Weather Experienced

**Ideal Timing:** Late April is described as "the best time to ride in the Carolinas. The temps will be cool to start and won't be too hot yet." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Lodging & Camping

**Camping Options:** Oconee State Park and South Cove County Park both offer camping spots very close to the start. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Other Accommodations:** Downtown cottages, equipped lofts, and vacation rentals are available. Specific mentions include a "sweet container house with a view" and "Lofts in Downtown Walhalla." Hotels are available 10 minutes away in Seneca, SC. (https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/ and https://gravelroll.com/theholler)

## Aid Station Support

**Long Course:** Aid stations at mile 19.3 and 57.5
**Short Course:** Aid stations at mile 19.3 and 32.8

**Stock includes:** Water, carb drink mix, gels, pickle juice, Coke, and "lot's of other fun snacks." Mechanical and SAG support available on course. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Important Course Access Notes

**CRITICAL - Private Property:** "When pre-riding this course DO NOT ride Coppermine road. This is private property and is only used on race day." Separate pre-ride routes are provided that exclude this section. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Area Attractions

**Mountain Biking:** Newly constructed Stumphouse Mountain Bike Park offers "miles of world-class mountain bike trails." (https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/)

**Water Activities:** The Chattooga River (featured in the movie "Deliverance") offers whitewater kayaking with rapids ranging from beginner to advanced. Lake Keowee provides fishing (bass and trout), boating, and swimming. (https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/)

**Historic Sites:** Stumphouse Mountain Tunnel, Issaqueena Falls, Oconee History Museum, Blue Ridge Railroad Historical Trail. (https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/)

**Dining:** Local favorites include Delish Sisters, Steph's Steaks, and Paesano's Italian Restaurant. (https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/)

## Series Context

Race to Valhalla is Event 2 (or 3, depending on source) of the 6-race Southeast Gravel Series, which runs March to August and includes: Gravel Battle of Sumter Forest, Saluda Redbank 100, Race to Valhalla, Greenwood Gravel Grinder, Tryon Gravel Gallop, and Kershaw Gold Rush. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla and https://strambecco.com/event/race-to-valhalla/)
