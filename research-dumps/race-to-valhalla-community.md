# RACE TO VALHALLA — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Winding Stairs Road Climb:** The course features a signature climb called Winding Stairs Rd that comes in the early stages. It's a steep, winding gravel road that climbs from scenic Lake Cherokee and "can take the better part of a half hour to climb" according to course description. This is described as part of their "most demanding and scenic course." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Chattooga River Section:** Long gravel roads with no traffic and sections riding along the Chattooga River. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Private Property Restriction:** Coppermine Road is private property and can ONLY be ridden on race day — riders are explicitly warned not to pre-ride this section. Pre-ride routes are 70 miles (long course) and 48 miles (short course), while race day routes are slightly longer at 72 miles and 48 miles respectively. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Downtown Finish:** The final mile is down Main Street in Walhalla, which is closed to traffic for the race, creating a "feel like a pro" finish experience. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Town Support:** Walhalla "goes all out to welcome" riders with a downtown festival concurrent with the cycling event. The entire town comes out for the event, and Main Street is closed to traffic for riders to finish through downtown while spectators watch and cheer. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Post-Race Amenities:** Free hot meal and free beer await finishers. The event encourages riders to stick around and cheer on fellow riders finishing. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Family-Friendly Weekend:** The event is positioned as family-friendly with camping nearby, downtown shopping, and festival activities. Oconee State Park and South Cove County Park both offer camping close to the start. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Series Event:** Race to Valhalla is Event 2 of 4 in the Southeast Gravel Series. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Weather Experienced

**Late April Timing:** The race is positioned for "the best time to ride in the Carolinas" with temps described as "cool to start and won't be too hot yet." The 2026 race is scheduled for April 25, and the 2025 race occurred on April 26. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

## Race Details & Logistics

**Aid Stations - Long Course (72 miles):** Located at mile 19.3 and 57.5, stocked with water, carb drink mix, gels, pickle juice, Coke, and "lots of other fun snacks." (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Aid Stations - Short Course (48 miles):** Located at mile 19.3 and 32.8 with the same amenities. (https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla)

**Course Options:** Three distance options available:
- Long Course: 72 miles (race day route)
- Short Course: 48 miles
- Fun Course: 20-23 miles

(https://www.southeastgravel.com/race-to-valhalla, https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=387568&groupid=385552)

**2025 Field Size:** 271 total racers participated, with 116 on the long course and 151 on the short course. (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=387568&groupid=385552)

**Start Format:** Interval wave start, with wave grouping by distance. Race uses RFID chip timing. (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=387568&groupid=385552)
