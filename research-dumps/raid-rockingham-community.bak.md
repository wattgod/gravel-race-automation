# RAID ROCKINGHAM — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Nick Digangi [COMPETITIVE]:** "I had the opportunity to do some recon rides of the course, which worked out in my favor as I often go too hard too soon and end up in the gutter for the remainder of my rides. Knowing how my body works, it takes me a solid 10+ miles to wake up and really get moving. So my strategy was clear: Pace myself." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Nick Digangi [COMPETITIVE]:** "I have a love/hate relationship with all gravel/dirt roads, as they tend sap a lot of energy out of me... I was taking it nice and slow as we came up to the first dirt section, a rail trail. I entered this long, flat section with a small group of about seven other riders. Seth, obviously feeling fresh, took off and blitzed through the section." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Nick Digangi [COMPETITIVE]:** "I found myself in some trouble early on [on Nottingham Square Road climb] and wasn't able to generate as much power as I normally have, but even still I finished with my second best time out of my four total trips up the segment." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Nick Digangi [COMPETITIVE]:** "I was feeling good up until mile 50 when I started to fade. My lack of fitness and having spent the majority of the last 40 on my own slowed me down... I had been starting to cramp up a bit... I ripped open some Clif blocks and carried on. Once I hit the segment I was welcomed with a searing pain in my left quad, I went to stretch it out and then felt my hamstring cramp as well, then my right quad…and so on and so forth." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Bikearama [UNKNOWN]:** "The course is surprisingly challenging. Living on the coast in Portsmouth, I have dipped into a few of these gravel sections here and there on far-flung road rides, but I had no idea you could link so many together to form such a rolling, challenging loop in forty miles!" (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**Bikearama [UNKNOWN]:** "The most memorable section for me came just before the finish on the wonderful Dame Road in Durham Point, which in late May was in the process of being graded. I got the bipolar experience of ripping up some solidly-packed, smooth-as-silk dirt road (like carving alpine ski turns on fresh corduroy just after dawn with nobody else around), but then had to slog through an extended section covered in two inches of beach-soft sand/dirt/gravel!" (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**throwawaycxer [UNKNOWN]:** "If you don't mind mixing some gravel in, I would recommend the Deerfield/Nottingham area, there's a road called Deerfield rd that's the northern border of pawtuckaway... Awesome roads out there. You might also look at the Raid Rockingham route... that's a 65 mile loop that's super fun, but I would recommend larger tires or a CX bike, if you have one." (https://www.reddit.com/user/throwawaycxer/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Width [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Official event guidelines state: "A properly prepared, gravel-ready bicycle is the key to your enjoyment" and "Wide 32 – 35mm (or wider) tires will be most suitable." (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**Tire Width [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "A properly prepared gravel-ready bike is the key to your enjoyment, anytime!" (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**Tire Pressure & Setup [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** One rider from mcccxxv blog reports: "Every single route I completed on this list was on BMC Roadmachine X, 32mm Gravel King tires, tubed, inflated 70–80psi." (https://mcccxxv.wordpress.com/2021/03/29/ne-gravel/)

**Bike Type [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Bikearama rode the preliminary course on cyclocross bike with "34c cyclocross tubular tires" and found even those struggled on some sections: "This was the 'grumpiest' section of the entire route, and even with my 34c cyclocross tubular tires I was more than ready to get back on the pavement." (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**Bike Type [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** throwawaycxer recommends "larger tires or a CX bike" for the route. (https://www.reddit.com/user/throwawaycxer/)

**eBikes:** "Yes, but only Class 1 (pedal assist to 20mph), no Class 2 or Class 3 bikes allowed. Due to their weight and technology, we may not be able to provide on-route support. Know your battery range!" (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**Essential Gear per Event:** "You'll want to bring at least one tube (2 would be better), patches, and a pump. A multi-tool, chain-breaker, and link would be a good idea... A helmet is required." (https://www.crwheelers.org/content/gravel-raid-rockingham)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Rail Trail Section [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Bikearama describes: "After 3-4 miles of relatively smooth packed dirt and gravel that you can easily navigate on a road bike with 28, 25 or even 23c tires, the last 2-3 miles, at least at the end of May, were severely chewed up by what looked to be at least a half-dozen sets of horse tracks. This was the 'grumpiest' section of the entire route." The rail trail is the "eastern terminus of the Rockingham Recreational Trail (a 25.3 mile rail trail that extends all the way to Manchester)." (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**Cat 5 Climb:** "The 'Cat 5' climb is tougher and seems to go on longer than the vertical would lead you to believe." (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**Dame Road Section [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "The most memorable section for me came just before the finish on the wonderful Dame Road in Durham Point, which in late May was in the process of being graded... had to slog through an extended ungraded section covered in two inches of beach-soft sand/dirt/gravel! Hopefully the grading will be completed long before the Raid." (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**Nottingham Square Road:** Nick Digangi describes this as one of "two Strava segments set out on the course and it stung riders with a mix of spongy dirt roads and punchy gradients. Even as a rider in the back half of the full group, I saw riders dropping off as soon as things got steep." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Course Mix:** Bikearama notes: "The course does a great job of interspersing 1-3 mile long sections of dirt/gravel road with longer pavement sections." (https://raidrockingham.blogspot.com/)

**Overall Composition:** Both routes are "approx. 30% gravel." 60-Mile Route has "2,800′ climbing" while 45-Mile Route has "1,900′ climbing." (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Self-Pacing Strategy [COMPETITIVE]:** Nick Digangi: "My strategy was clear: Pace myself. I have a love/hate relationship with all gravel/dirt roads, as they tend sap a lot of energy out of me." After the rail trail section, "my plan was to ride recovery until we ventured back on to Nottingham, New Hampshire's dirt road climbs." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Finding Groups [COMPETITIVE]:** Nick describes being "in no-man's land for a bit but eventually I met up with a group of about four others. Eventually I split off with another rider. This is one of those times on a ride where you're grateful for the company—not to mention the ability to trade off, bring up each others average, and save a little energy in the process." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Late-Ride Cramping [COMPETITIVE]:** At mile 50 of the 100k, Nick "started to fade" and experienced severe cramping. He found relief with Clif Blocks: "I ripped open some Clif blocks and carried on... Luckily, the Clif Blocks I had taken 10 minutes earlier must've started to kick in." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Route Choice Flexibility:** "I signed up for the 60-Mile route but decided to ride the 45-Mile route, OK? Sure! If you make that decision before the ride let us know; otherwise, you can decide while you ride. The 2 routes split right after the first rest stop at Mile 20 and the split will be prominently marked. For those riders who reach that rest stop after 11:30AM we make the decision for you, it's the 45-Mile route for you!" (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**Staged Start:** "The staged start sends groups of similarly-trained riders off at the same time." Registration includes "a self-seeding question that you must answer during the registration process – enter your typical road riding pace. This helps us with rider release at the start!" (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**CRW Group Ride Pacing [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Charles River Wheelers planned a group ride at "~14mph with 2 stops for food. This is a no-drop ride, but riding gravel is harder than riding paved roads. If you can't average 14mph on dirt with some climbing (17mph paved road equivalent) then this ride may not be for you." (https://www.crwheelers.org/content/gravel-raid-rockingham)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-Race Party:** "The ride finishes at The Stone Church Music Club and they'll host a great post-ride party for us. The party starts around 11AM and starts winding down at 2PM, plan accordingly!" Features "cold craft beer on tap and some tasty grub to match, all at the legendary Stone Church Music Club!" (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**After-Party Details:** Nick Digangi: "Afterwards, Arlon set up a nice little shindig with plenty of food, water, and 'adult sodas' (all thanks to Smuttynose). Everyone was able to relax and chat it up with our spandexed brethren." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Rider Interaction:** Nick: "Thanks to everyone who came and said 'Hi' to us as well. It was pretty cool hearing that people actually read our stuff." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-dreams-realized-hampshire-rider-diary)

**Event Philosophy:** "Absolutely not; this is a personal challenge ride. Riders of all abilities are encouraged to participate and to complete the route at their own pace." (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**Event History:** "Launched in 2012, Raid Rockingham has grown to be one of New England's premier gravel events. In our first year we operated out of a construction zone behind the historic Newmarket Mills which were undergoing redevelopment at the time, we had about 75 riders. We now host more than 450 riders each year." (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)

**Independent Fabrication Connection:** "Our good friends down the hallway at Loco Sports organize and promote just about every road running event in the general area that is worth attending, so we partnered with them to create a local challenge ride, the RAID Rockingham." They note: "There's beer, bikes, and bike swag involved, so… yeah, kind of hard for it not to be [fun]." (https://ifbikes.com/other/raid-rockingham-race-or-ride/)

**Intro to Gravel Category:** "Our 'Intro to Gravel' category uses the 45-Mile route and is perfect for those riders new to gravel. Our experienced Ride Guides and 'no-drop' approach make for a welcoming environment!" (https://www.grvl.net/raid-rockingham)
