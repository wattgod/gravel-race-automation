# RASPUTITSA — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Adam Roberge [ELITE]:** "Being the 'favorite' in a race comes with the responsibility to 'make it happen,' or to 'make it hard,' because the longer you wait, the higher your chances of ending up on the wrong side of luck... Given the nature of this course, I knew I had to use the very technical first 3km to make it hard from the beginning." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

**Adam Roberge [ELITE]:** "The start was, without a doubt, one of the craziest I have ever raced. It featured a 3km downhill, weaving in and out of a golf path with two long and deep mudslides... The last climb was a 20-25 minute effort through very loose and muddy conditions... The combination of deep mud and occasional snow made this final section particularly challenging (and very enjoyable)." Won in 2:45 with IF: 0.789, TSS: 357, 5,971 calories burned. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

**Lea Davison [ELITE]:** "That was the second hardest thing I've ever done in my life. The only thing harder was La Ruta [in Costa Rica]" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "For a trail runner moonlighting as a cyclist getting across the finish line is a big deal. Well, for me at least. I was well within the time cutoff, so I totally won at life." (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "The climbing. It felt like it never ended. Seriously. If you want to challenge your climbing legs get up here. It's a BEAST. My granny gear and I hung out together all day. And I'm pretty sure my running time would be very close to my biking time #noshame" (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**Heidi Myers (co-founder) [RECREATIONAL]:** "A lot of calls cannot be made until the last minute. That adds fun and is a popular topic of conversation up until race day. What are people riding for tires? What bike choice? Am I going to bring my fat bike? Should I bring my cyclocross bike?" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Heidi Myers [RECREATIONAL]:** "We probably had a little under 200 racers who did not finish, and we were picking them up at feed zones. They were cold and hypothermic. We had eight vehicles on course just picking up riders." (2019 race conditions) (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Heidi Myers [RECREATIONAL]:** "I've never heard anyone say, 'Oh, I wish it was harder'" (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Adam Roberge [ELITE]:** Used the same bike setup as MidSouth for his winning Rasputitsa ride. Noted that equipment setup at BWR California was "a tricky one equipment-wise, as you are forced to underbike the technical single-track sector because 60% of the course is road." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "I rode that shit on my mountain bike" - explicitly chose MTB over road/gravel bike (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**General trend [MIXED LEVELS]:** "These last couple of years more folks have opted for a mountain bike. There was a crew of 3 or 4 guys this year all riding steel fully rigid mountain bikes, a couple full suspension mountain bikes and another guy on a fully rigid carbon 29er but with narrow tires." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-irreverent-road-ride-8-vermont-challenge)

**Adam St. Germain (IRR director) [UNKNOWN]:** "I like to tell people that every bike is the wrong bike. And, I feel like that is mostly true. The most important thing is that it has low gearing and you'll be comfortable being on it for 8-plus hours." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-irreverent-road-ride-8-vermont-challenge)

## Weather Experienced

**2019:** "Officially, it was in the upper 30s, low 40s. There was hail at the start. There was snow on the course. There was a downpour of rain. Pretty much all the shit you could possibly imagine." ~200 DNFs due to cold/hypothermia. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**2016:** "Dry, sunny, high of 60. PERFECT weather to spend hours outside on your bike. There was mud of course – but lots of sun as well... weather that started in the cold, low 30s in the morning, and turned crisp and sunny during the day." (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**2024 (Adam Roberge's year):** Deep mud and occasional snow on final section. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Cyberia Section:** "Those roads are not maintained at all. They're not plowed, they don't lay sand, they're not salted. They are unmaintained roads. There is extensive snow cover in there... you can see people literally walking their bikes through. They're unrideable." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "Cyberia. After climbing for what feels like approximately 11 days straight you will reach cyberia. And then climb for another day until you reach the top. This is hands down my favorite part of the course. Mostly because I knew I would finally get to descend some. And also because they handed out maple syrup shots in tiny little shot glasses made of ice. SHOT GLASSES MADE OF ICE! FULL OF MAPLE SYRUP!" (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**Adam Roberge [ELITE]:** "The start was, without a doubt, one of the craziest I have ever raced. It featured a 3km downhill, weaving in and out of a golf path with two long and deep mudslides." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

**General terrain:** "The challenge is the road conditions. The washboard back roads are exhausting on your upper body this time of year. Mentally, just having to deal with the weather, I saw people out there who just broke down mentally." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**2019 specifics:** 41 miles with 4,900 feet of climbing. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**2024 specifics:** 88km (approximately 55 miles) with significant climbing including "the first long climb of the day" and "the last climb was a 20-25 minute effort through very loose and muddy conditions." (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Adam Roberge [ELITE]:** "I knew I had to use the very technical first 3km to make it hard from the beginning. My commitment was clear; I aimed to create a small lead group with the challenging start. In the event it didn't work, I was then okay with being patient and playing all my cards on the final climb." Successfully went in early 4-person break that whittled to 2, then soloed on final climb. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

**Adam Roberge [ELITE] - Nutrition Strategy:** "Especially after a long training day on the bike, cooking a big and tasty dinner is a challenge... a lot of the recovery process is about eating enough, and without delicious food, meeting my calorie needs during intense training can be difficult." Uses prepared meals from Plaisirs Gastronomiques to optimize recovery. (https://adamroberge.com/2024/05/597/)

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "My granny gear and I hung out together all day" - stayed in easiest gears, focused on finishing rather than racing. (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Cold/Hypothermia (2019):** "We probably had a little under 200 racers who did not finish, and we were picking them up at feed zones. They were cold and hypothermic. We had eight vehicles on course just picking up riders." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Mental breakdown:** "Mentally, just having to deal with the weather, I saw people out there who just broke down mentally." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Bike choice:** Wrong bike choice can lead to suffering - conditions dictate whether gravel, cyclocross, fat bike, or mountain bike is appropriate. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "Rainbow-colored, grinning unicorns jump up and down, cheer, and offer you homemade chocolate chip cookies as you pedal past. Are you delirious? Dreaming? Bonking? No, you've likely reached an aid station at Rasputitsa Dirt" (https://theradavist.com/2022-rasputitsa-dirt-gravel-cycling-event)

**Chris Hadgis [COMPETITIVE]:** "The volunteers. Cheerful, supportive, out there spending their day helping a bunch of crazy bike riders have the best experience possible. THANK YOU! You made the day wonderful." (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**Heidi Myers [RECREATIONAL]:** "Honestly, we have made a really big effort to know our racers by name. I know this sounds ludicrous, but I'd say we can walk up to about half the people who attend and say 'Hey Jen.' We get to know them and get to know what they have going on in their lives." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Women's participation growth:** "Over the past 6 years, we've grown our Women's field from 10 percent to 21 percent this year. That we've done through pure encouragement." Non-competitive Bittersweet events put pros and amateurs together: "Sometimes we make the pros do things out of their comfort levels. Like we have a Bittersweet event where there was skiing." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Race philosophy (official site):** "For the most part, the race is against yourself with the largest award going to the Lanterne Rouge recipient" - the last person to cross the finish line gets the biggest prize. (https://rasputitsadirt.com/results)

**Charity focus:** All money raised goes to Little Bellas (mentoring girls on mountain bikes). 2018 donated $19,000, aiming for $21,000+ in subsequent years. (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Post-race:** "Thank you to the Burke Publick House for welcoming a bunch of dirty, smelly bikers with a fantastic post-race spread with tons of vegetarian options. Carrot ginger soup. Poutine with mushroom gravy. YES! And great beers, with plenty of local Vermont options." (https://adventuresneaker.com/2016/04/25/rasputitsa-2016-race-report/)

**AC/DC cover band, gravel confessional booth with Bobby Wintle and Mid-South crew, "problem-child" kids-care zone, hair-do station, gear repair, vegetarian-friendly food truck, and Dirt-Church VT beer garden.** (https://theradavist.com/2022-rasputitsa-dirt-gravel-cycling-event)

**Growth:** Started with 300 registrants in 2014. By 2022, over 2,000 participants. (https://theradavist.com/2022-rasputitsa-dirt-gravel-cycling-event)

**Three C's founding principles:** Cycling, Community, Charity (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Money-back guarantee:** "Not hard enough for you? Rasputitsa will return your entry fee. Thus far, everyone has gone home happy." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Special guest 2019:** Lars Boom (2008 cyclocross world champion) raced his first-ever gravel race at Rasputitsa. Organizers vetted him with Tim Johnson and Jeremy Powers first: "We reached out to them and asked, 'What do you think about this Lars Boom character?' and they were like, 'He's a class act.' And he truly truly was." (https://www.cxmagazine.com/gravel-race-2019-rasputitsa-vermont-cycling-community-charity-2)

**Anthony Moccia support:** Following Anthony Moccia's stage 4 cancer diagnosis, the community rallied with "A Day at the Races" (#adayattheraces) and a Queen Tribute Benefit Concert at Jay Peak Resort, with all proceeds going to the Moccia Family. (https://rasputitsadirt.com/)
