# Rasputitsa Gravel Race - Raw Research Dump

## Overview

Rasputitsa is an annual gravel bike race held in Vermont's Northeast Kingdom (NEK). It has earned a legendary reputation for its brutal mud conditions, unpredictable spring weather, and the sheer suffering it inflicts on participants. The race is often called "Vermont's Spring Classic" and has developed a cult following among Northeast gravel enthusiasts.

**Official Website:** [rasputitsadirt.com](https://rasputitsadirt.com/)

---

## 1. Name Meaning and Etymology

### Rasputitsa - Russian "Mud Season"

The name "Rasputitsa" (Russian: raspútitsa) is a Russian language term that literally translates to "season of bad roads" or "quagmire season."

**Etymology breakdown:**
- The word consists of two parts: "ras" (prefix meaning "dissolve" or comparable to English "un-"/"dis-") and "put'" (path/road)
- Alternative translation: "crossroads" or "dissolved paths"

**Historical Context:**
Rasputitsa refers to two periods of the year in Eastern Europe when travel on unpaved roads becomes extremely difficult due to muddy conditions from rain or thawing snow. This applies to both:
- **Spring Rasputitsa**: March-April thaw of frozen ground
- **Autumn Rasputitsa**: Heavy October rains

**Military Significance:**
- Credited with hampering Napoleon's French Empire campaign in 1812
- Hindered Nazi Germany's invasion in 1941
- Known as "General Mud" or "Marshal Mud" - a defensive advantage in wartime
- The Mongol invasion of Kievan Rus' in the 13th century was also affected by rasputitsa

**The Process:**
During winter, ground freezes to a depth of 8 feet or more with several feet of ice/snow accumulated on top. When spring thaw begins, it melts from the top downward, creating a progressively deepening layer of water and mud on top of still-frozen ground. Water cannot drain until the lowest frozen soil layer has completely thawed - a process taking 5-6 weeks.

**Climate Change Impact:**
Rasputitsa used to last 2-3 weeks but now reaches two months in some areas due to warming winters.

## 2. Event History and Founders

### The Beginning (2013-2014)

**Founders:** Anthony Moccia and Heidi Myers

**Origin Story:**
- Both founders had previously ridden the Almanzo 100 in Minnesota (a free-to-enter gravel grinder)
- "That was the motivator for us to embrace something different," Myers said
- They staged a precursor race called "The Dirty 40" in fall 2013
- They decided to push the event into spring and give it a new name: Rasputitsa

**The Vision:**
- Create a race in the most untouched, remote section of Vermont - the Northeast Kingdom
- "We started with $50 in our pocket and a dream"
- Guided by the "3 C's": Cycling, Community, and Charity

**First Race (2014):**
- The first Rasputitsa had approximately 300 registrants
- Course was mapped during winter using online GPS software
- When founders checked the roads a few weeks before the race, they discovered some were Class IV unmaintained public highways - completely impassable
- They had already published the course, so they went with it anyway
- The toughest segment - a wet, icy riverbed - was named "Cyberia"
- Myers described the first edition as "a shitshow"

### Growth Timeline

| Year | Participants | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 2014 | ~300-400 | First Rasputitsa |
| 2019 | ~1,500 | Pre-pandemic peak |
| 2020 | Cancelled | COVID-19 pandemic |
| 2021 | Cancelled | Burke select board declined due to COVID concerns |
| 2022 | 2,000+ | Return from hiatus |
| 2024 | ~1,500 | 10th Anniversary at Jay Peak Resort |
| 2025 | 1,500 (cap) | East Burke location |

### Recognition and Awards

- Named **"Best Cycling Event in the Northeast"** by Competitor Magazine
- Listed in **"7 Challenging, Fun Spring Classics to Ride"** by Bicycling Magazine
- Described as "a psychotic 45-mile, insane, drop to your knees and cry sufferfest"

### COVID Hiatus and Founders' Personal Challenges

During the race's two-year hiatus (2020-2021):
- COVID's unpredictable shutdowns created obstacles with partners, vendors, and the town of East Burke
- **Anthony Moccia** was diagnosed with tonsil cancer around the beginning of the pandemic
- **Heidi Myers** had brain surgery to address symptoms of Young Onset Parkinson's disease
- The stress of the unknowns was reason enough to fold the race indefinitely

The 2022 return was a resurrection for both the race and its founders.

## 3. Race Details and Course Information

### Current Format (as of 2024-2025)

**Location:**
- Jay Peak Resort (2024 10th Anniversary)
- East Burke, Vermont (2025)
- Address: 830 Jay Peak Road, Jay, VT 05859

**Date:** Mid-to-late April (typically 3rd Saturday)
- 2024: April 20
- 2025: April 19

**Distance Options:**
- **Classic Distance:** ~53 miles with 5,300+ feet of climbing
- **100K Option:** Well north of 7,000 feet of climbing
- **69K Option:** Medium distance
- **40K Option:** Shortest route

**Participant Cap:** 1,500 riders

**Wave Starts:** 5 pre-determined waves

### Course Terrain

**Road Composition:**
- Approximately 50% Class 1 roads
- Approximately 45% Class 2 roads
- About 5% more technical terrain
- ~110 feet of climbing per mile

**Geographic Features:**
- Loops around Burke Mountain on mostly dirt roads
- Northeast Kingdom lies just east of the Green Mountains
- Valley edges of Connecticut River provide only flat areas
- "Flat does not exist here no matter which direction you are headed"
- Roads are almost always named after a hill
- Terrain changes from wooded hills and farms to lone dirt roads through massive bogs

### The Infamous Cyberia Section

**The Signature Suffering:**
Cyberia is the highlight (or lowlight) of the course - a notorious 5-kilometer section that defines the Rasputitsa experience.

**Specifications:**
- Class IV road (unmaintained public highway)
- Ratchets up to an unforgiving 23% grade
- Ascends 1,140 feet via a rutted track
- Deep mud and steep grades

**Founder Quote (Heidi Myers):**
> "Those roads are not maintained at all. They're not plowed, they don't lay sand, they're not salted. They are unmaintained roads. There is extensive snow cover in there. If you look at the pictures, you can see people literally walking their bikes through. They're unrideable."

## 4. Weather and Conditions

### The Defining Characteristic: MUD

Rasputitsa has earned its reputation for punishing conditions:

> "In Vermont's Northeast Kingdom, Rasputitsa is far more peaceable but no less merciless on wheeled transport. The annual gravel bike race has its own well-earned reputation for vanquishing even the fiercest competitors with its often capricious weather and treacherous course conditions."

### Year-by-Year Conditions (Examples)

**2019 - The Brutal Year:**
- Snow and temperatures in the 30s
- Conditions so gnarly, muddy, and frozen that everyone had to hike-a-bike at certain sections
- Some course sections completely unrideable
- Over 400 of the ~1,500 starters did not finish (27% DNF rate)
- People abandoned expensive bikes on the side of the road
- Riders thumbed rides just to escape hypothermic conditions

**Pro Rider Lea Davison's Quote:**
> "That was the second hardest thing I've ever done in my life. The only thing harder was La Ruta [in Costa Rica]."

**2022 - The Mild Year:**
- Flurries a few days before race
- Race day: pristine conditions
- A little mud but mainly dry, hard-packed gravel
- Started cold (low 30s), turned crisp and sunny

**2024 - 10th Anniversary:**
- Mud from the start
- Riders sent onto thawed XC ski trail that devolved into ankle-deep, greasy, smelly mud
- First two miles forced entire pack off bikes
- Ankle/axle-deep mud "where a surfboard would have been most efficient"
- Winner time: 2 hours 45 minutes

**One Year:**
- 70 degrees and dry
- "Spring in Vermont is beyond unpredictable"

### Temperature Ranges

Typical race day conditions:
- High 30s to low 40s
- Light-to-medium consistent rain possible
- As low as 27°F at higher elevations with snow
- Conditions can border on dangerous if unprepared

### The Unpredictability Factor

> "Every year the intensity of the route differs—not due to the 57 miles and over 7K feet of climbing, but due to the mixed terrain of gravel, rocks, and mud which can change in a flash depending on Vermont's wintery temperatures and amounts of rain and snowfall."

> "Spring in Vermont is beyond unpredictable."

## 5. Community and Culture

### The Cult Following

**Demographics:**
- Riders from 46+ U.S. states
- 5+ Canadian provinces
- European participants
- Now attracts nearly 2,000 competitors including Olympians and WorldTour racers

**The Vibe:**
> "At heart, Rasputitsa is still the quirky fondo it always was – business in the front, party in the back."

**Inclusivity:**
- Open to all individuals and all types of bicycles
- Podiums for top male, female, AND nonbinary finishers
- The race's most exalted prize: **The Lanterne Rouge** - goes to the rider who finishes LAST

- Maple syrup handed out in shot glasses made of ice

## 8. Equipment and Gear Recommendations

### Bike Choice

**The Eternal Debate: Gravel vs MTB**

> "No matter what kind of bike you ride, it's going to be the wrong bike at some point during the course."

**Recommendations:**
- Gravel or cross bike with near 1:1 gearing ratio for climbs
- Many riders opt for mountain bikes as "safe bet" given unpredictable conditions
- Ted King has ridden 40-44mm WTB Nano tires successfully

### Tire Recommendations

**Size:**
- 40-44mm tires work well
- File-tread or light gravel tires often sufficient

**Tread Pattern:**
> "No matter how wet Rasputitsa gets, mud tires (or really tires with a serious amount of tread) aren't necessary. The dirt/sand/gravel roads in the Northeast Kingdom drain surprisingly well."

**Successful Tire Choices:**
- WTB Nano 40-44mm
- Donnelly X'PLOR MSO 40mm
- Panaracer Gravelking SK 32c

**Setup:**
- **Tubeless is essential**
- Lower pressures dramatically improve ride experience
- Sealant saves many flats

### Essential Gear Checklist

**Repair Supplies:**
- Patch kit and boot
- Multi-tool with chain tool
- Mini-pump and CO2
- Spare tube(s) - even with tubeless
- Tubeless plug kit

**Storage:**
- Seatbag or handlebar bag
- Water-resistant handlebar bag recommended (keeps camera/extra socks dry)
- Hydration pack or bottles

**Navigation/Safety:**
- GPS unit
- Lights

**Weather Considerations:**
- Prepare for temps from 27°F to 70°F
- Rain/snow gear essential
- Remove fenders if grassy/freezing mud expected (unless huge clearance)
- Extra warm layers for 30s temps with rain/sleet

## 9. Race Experience Quotes and Stories

### The Start

> "Within the first two miles of the race, the entire pack was forced off their bikes for ankle/axle deep mud where a surfboard would have been the most efficient. Starting in the back of the pack forced a slow maneuver through the slop, following the leader until the tromping landed on a paved path."

> "The start was 'one of the craziest' ever raced, featuring a 3km downhill weaving in and out of a golf path with two long and deep mudslides."

### The Cyberia Section

> "Rasputitsa 2017 was a foggy, chilly, wet load of mess and fun. Highlights were the hike-a-bike in the ice and snow up Cyberia and the rest stop with the ice cups of maple syrup."

### The Finish

> "Riding together until we got to the 2 foot deep mud bog that introduced the 'surprise' CX course finish. Not an ideal finale."

### The Verdict

> "After chatting with riders at the finish, there was a collective consensus that the race was an absolute blast. Although they encountered conditions that were unrideable, it made it that much more special to get through it all."

### The Best Advice

> "Giving advice for Rasputitsa seems like a fool's errand. It's a race that defies definition and every year the conditions are completely different."

> "The best advice is simply to embrace the vibe. Rasputitsa is going to be a blast whatever comes your way. Mud, snow, a cookie, a shot of Fireball, just open your heart and embrace it."
