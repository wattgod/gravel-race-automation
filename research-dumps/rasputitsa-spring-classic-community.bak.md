# RASPUTITSA SPRING CLASSIC — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Heidi Myers [ELITE]:** "I struggled with balance, memory, behavior, depression, and movement disorders. My ability to ride a bike is limited because my right shoulder is so stiff I typically need to ride one-handed. I was never an extreme athlete, but I quickly went from doing century rides and half-marathons to barely being able to last 10 miles on the bike... The gravel racing community has been my redemption. What separates cycling from almost any other sport is community, and this one is like a family." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Anonymous blogger [RECREATIONAL]:** "Rasputitsa is Russian for the mud season when roads become difficult to traverse. This 47 mile (31 miles of gravel) road race travels some of Vermont's coldest and barren landscapes." (https://nyvelocity.com/rasputitsa/)

**Anonymous rider [COMPETITIVE]:** "I originally signed up for the 75 miler, but downgraded to 63 the morning of based on the previous day's feeling. Knowing all routes go to the first rest stop, I knew I had an out if I needed/wanted to end my ride early." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Same rider [COMPETITIVE]:** "After the first 13 miles, there are almost no flat portions – you are either riding up a hill or down a hill. I also got sunburned because I didn't get as much sunscreen on my arms and legs as I thought." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Same rider [COMPETITIVE]:** "Every time a group of men passed me, at least one said 'good job' as they floated by. I know it's not condescending and intended to be supportive but it always irritates me. Do they say that to other men when they pass them? I doubt it. I may not be fast but I am strong and I can ride bikes all day long and not be completely useless at the end of the day." (https://wifemotheawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Phil [UNKNOWN]:** Describing the 100k route as "A journey through the Burke Mountain area of Vermont's Northeast Kingdom, Rasputitsa has become a staple of Vermont's gravel season... making friends along the way." (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=91O6dSEUFhk)

## Race Details & Format

**Distance options:** The race has evolved over the years. Early descriptions mention "47 mile (31 miles of gravel)" route (https://nyvelocity.com/rasputitsa/). More recent editions offer multiple distances: "40, 75, 100 miles" with the 63-mile option also mentioned (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/ and https://granfondoguide.com/Events/NorthAmericanSpringClassics).

**Registration cost:** "$85" for the event mentioned by the competitive rider (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Field size:** Originally started with "a field limit of 500 riders" in 2016 (https://www.volunteersignup.org/LFM4A), has grown to "1500 people" in more recent years according to Heidi Myers (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Charity component:** "The event raises money for the Mary E. Wright Halo Foundation" (https://nyvelocity.com/rasputitsa/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Cyberia section:** Described as "a Class IV road that a former race participant described as 'a scary backwoods where at any time a Yeti or Bengal tiger may jump out of the woods, bludgeon you with a blunt object, and either leave you for dead or drag you back into the woods for a nice little Saturday brunch.'" (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Climbing profile [COMPETITIVE]:** The first major hill "arrived at mile 13 and was a model for most of the hills to come. It was about 8/10ths of a mile averaging 8.5%. Many were already walking." Total climbing on 63-mile route not specified, but "After the first 13 miles, there are almost no flat portions – you are either riding up a hill or down a hill." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Final sections [COMPETITIVE]:** "Only 20 miles left in the ride and only 3 more significant climbs before an epic descent back into Pittston" (note: this appears to be from Lu Lacka Wyco Hundo description mixed in, but similar terrain characteristics) (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Course infrastructure:** "Rolling back into town, I overshot a turn and saw the photographer waving his hands at me from the side of the road. I doubled back and the route put us on a trail of railroad-grade gravel next to the tracks that eventually gave way to a grassy doubletrack trail." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

## Weather Experienced

**2022 conditions:** Based on video title "Rasputitsa Spring Classic 2022 - The Return" suggesting it was held after COVID cancellations (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k2P1FtVHD64)

**Typical April conditions [COMPETITIVE]:** "It's cloudy and misty as I roll up to the Jenkins Township firehouse. The temps are in the mid-50s*F, which felt nice for my running-hot tendencies." By mid-race: "By now, the sun is out and many of us are taking off warmer clothing from the start. I ditched the vest and switched to fingerless gloves." The rider noted "Sweat up the hill, cool down on the descent" and that they "got sunburned because I didn't get as much sunscreen on my arms and legs as I thought." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Mud season:** The race name "Rasputitsa is Russian for the mud season when roads become difficult to traverse" - indicating spring conditions with potential mud. One blog compared it to "Rospuutto in Eastern Finland, in Russia as Rasputitsa. In Sweden they use Menföre or in other parts of Finland there is Kelirikko, in New England it is called the Filth Season, and in Ukraine Bezdorizhzhia." (https://veloklubhaus.com/2025/02/27/february-2025-rides/)

**Weather contrast noted:** "Last weekend at Rasputitsa (admittedly a very different latitude), it was cool (40s*F) and it snowed. Yesterday it was 80*F and brilliantly sunny" - indicating April weather in Vermont can vary dramatically (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Clothing setup [COMPETITIVE]:** "I decided I probably only needed a vest over my lightweight wool t shirt. Paired with my trail shorts, mesh bib liners, and a zero-weight baselayer, I was ready for a long day in the saddle. I was also one of Very Few not wearing a full road cycling kit." Rider started with vest and full-finger gloves, switched to fingerless gloves mid-race. (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Bike choice note [ELITE]:** Heidi Myers mentions "Lea Davison set me up with a mountain bike because she thought it would help my rigid upper body feel more comfortable" and personally uses a "Specialized Women's Diverge" noting "The most comfortable bike I've ever ridden. The Future Shock suspension under the handlebar helps relieves stiffness." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**General observation [COMPETITIVE]:** "Most of the Very Serious Cyclists on the hundred-miler had already left at 8am" - suggesting elite riders on different equipment/approach than recreational field. (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start time structure:** "Mass start had the founder, Pat, leading us out in his truck which was helpful due to the first few miles rolling through downtown Pittston. The first hill arrived at mile 13" suggesting neutral/easy first section. "I had planned to go out with the 9am Mass Start. The mass start... Most of the Very Serious Cyclists on the hundred-miler had already left at 8am" (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Route flexibility [COMPETITIVE]:** "The routes had various offshoots from a core route, making it possible to see people who had gone out before you on a longer distance later in the day. I originally signed up for the 75 miler, but downgraded to 63 the morning of... Knowing all routes go to the first rest stop, I knew I had an out if I needed/wanted to end my ride early." Decision point at mile 25: "Looking at the 40, I would be going back on roads I've already ridden on; looking at the 63, I'd get to see the other side of the river and hit all three counties... Plus, it's only 20 miles to the next aid station and only two or three big hills. I decided to keep moving on the 63." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Pre-race guidance:** "The pre-event communication included logistics, but also a pace sheet so you could make sure the aid stations were open for your given pace and route. 'take pictures and enjoy the ride. If today isn't your day, just follow the next shorter route. Don't use Google to get back to the start – it will put you on roads that will be a very bad time. We won't leave anyone out there.' It felt like a friend of a friend was giving pro tips on a route they created. The only time limit was to be done by dark." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Walking climbs [COMPETITIVE]:** "The first hill arrived at mile 13 and was a model for most of the hills to come. It was about 8/10ths of a mile averaging 8.5%. Many were already walking." Later: "I even got off and pushed my bike up a short but steep hill to see if that helped. (it didn't, but it did give me a chance to work different muscles for a bit)" (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Nutrition challenges [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'm still dealing with GI issues, and Saturday had been one of the 'bad' days for nausea. This, of course, triggers my anxiety because I know hard efforts require good fueling strategies, and I was having issues just eating enough for baseline caloric needs." At mile 25: "I wasn't nauseous, but I also wasn't feeling hungry. I ate the rest of my Clifbar and the banana." At mile ~53: "I stopped to eat some fruit snacks and almost immediately felt queasy. I dialed back the pace even more." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

## Aid Stations

**First aid station (mile 25):** "well stocked with bananas, pb&js on white bread, oranges, and plenty of water and Hammer nutrition electrolyte mix. I grabbed a banana, refilled my water bottle" (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Second aid station (taco stop, mile ~45):** "Lots of people sprawled out on the grass across the road from the tent with sodas and tacos; other offerings included oranges, bananas, pb&js, and other typical aid station fare." Note: This rider did not eat due to stomach issues. (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Volunteer support structure (2016):** Medical tent, DJs, multiple feed zones including "FIRST Feedzone MILE 16 Top of Victory" (9:00-11:30 AM) and "Cyberia Feedzone / White Witches" (10:30-2:00 PM), extensive traffic marshals at intersections, broom wagon, photography, and post-race food (pizza or pasta). Volunteers received "long sleeve event jersey, meal at the Apres-Velo party" (https://www.volunteersignup.org/LFM4A)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event philosophy [ELITE - Race Co-Founder]:** "Rasputitsa is more than a gravel cycling race; It's a homegrown self-supported challenge of the mind, body, and soul that instills people with a connected sense of place and purpose. It's an event that celebrates exploration, discovery, and the pursuit of the natural world through cycling." (https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCbw1cbkgOPggMB63iotxCPw)

**Heidi Myers [ELITE - Race Co-Founder]:** "The gravel racing community has been my redemption. What separates cycling from almost any other sport is community, and this one is like a family. Race day for me is a zillion hugs and handshakes... The event has helped me pull me through my own internal struggles. With Parkinson's, the combination of the inability to ride and my mental symptoms, which include cognitive impairment, often lead to a feeling of isolation. But because of Rasputitsa, one day a year I feel that I, too, belong." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Community support:** "At Rasputitsa last year, I watched a group of girls decorate their bikes with #imwithheidi decals. Olympian Lyne Bessette donated all the proceeds from 100b7, a gravel ride she puts on in Canada, to me. Olympian Lea Davison set me up with a mountain bike... Over drinks at last year's Rasputitsa, I connected deeply to 2017 Dirty Kanza champion Alison Tetrick" (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Professional participation:** "Its reputation has attracted pros like Ted King, Alison Tetrick, Tim Johnson, Lyne Bessette, Jeremy Powers, Anthony Clark, and more." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Vibe description [COMPETITIVE]:** "The only time I needed it was down-to-earth the ride was intended to be... 'take pictures and enjoy the ride. If today isn't your day, just follow the next shorter route... We won't leave anyone out there.' It felt like a friend of a friend was giving pro tips on a route they created." Post-race: "It looked like they were offering pizza or pasta and beverages. Perhaps if I had a friend I would have stuck around to talk about the day and how nice the ride was." (https://wifemotherawesomegirl.com/tag/gravel-century/)

**Family involvement:** "Even my family recognizes this. When I was first diagnosed, they implored me to scale back on my duties with the event, to accommodate for my handicaps. But now my husband, sons, parents, siblings, and in-laws have come to recognize the power this race has on me, and they show up on race day to do any volunteer job I delegate to them. My sons Nolan, 12, and Thayer, 8, are growing up at gravel events. Nolan is our staff photographer and he supplies all our images on social media. Thayer runs our retail sales at the event. To them, April means Rasputitsa as much as December means Christmas." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Gravel culture note:** "Unlike disciplines like cyclocross or road racing, most gravel events are devoid of categories, and thus elitism. It's a mass start that ends in a shared beer at the finish line regardless of your ability. That's why gravel breaks down barriers. It doesn't isolate. Pros line up with amateurs, and everyone celebrates together afterward." (https://www.bicycling.com/rides/a25061218/gravel-racing-and-parkinsons/)

**Special elements:** The race features "Yeti's, maple syrup shots and, of course, Cyberia" (https://granfondoguide.com/Events/NorthAmericanSpringClassics)
