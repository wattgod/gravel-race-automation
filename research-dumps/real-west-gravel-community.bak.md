## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Choice - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** 45mm Schwalbe G-One RS. "Very happy with this choice today. Was descending better than anyone else in the mud since most people ran narrow, low tread tires." He noted that Max chose "little tires" which disadvantaged him in the mud section. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Bike Setup - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** 
- Argonaut GR3 frame
- SRAM XPLR Transmission with 48T front, 10/46 rear gearing
- 45mm tires
(https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Clothing - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** "I go bare legs and lots of embro, it's gonna be fun." For mid-30s temps with rain. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**General Gear Advice [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Eastern Oregon can be unpredictable, so bring a packable jacket and a solid base layer to stay comfortable." Weather forecast showed Low 35°F | High 52°F. (https://groups.google.com/a/obra.org/g/obra-chat/c/sN854zTvRPs)

**Mechanical Issue - Shawn Petersen [ELITE]:** GRX shifter locked into crash mode after a fall. Had to reset it mid-race, losing his group. Also experienced drivetrain issues that prevented shifting into easiest gears. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile:** 67 or 47 miles with "60 ft of climbing average per mile" with 70% unpaved. The long course has "over 5K climbing." (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/real-west-gravel and https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o9gsZbjy11A)

**Early Attack Point - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** "Mile ~6ish... it's a nice steep kicker before a truly long and arduous false flat starts." This is where he launched his early race-defining attack. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**False Flat Section:** "Over the top of the riser I pull off and then there were only three of us. We set off onto the long false flat that had a few kickers thrown in for the next 20ish miles." (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Exposed Plateau:** "Our group of three gets to the top of the false flat onto a large, exposed plateau. Here the temps dipped down into the mid 30s with a very strong headwind." This is where Stefano Barberi pulled out due to cold. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Technical Mud Section Before Aid 2:** "We send the gnarly mud section into Aid 2" - this section significantly advantaged wider tires and caused gaps in the lead group. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Dangerous Descent - Shawn Petersen [ELITE]:** "Insane, fast, technical downhill through slick mud and deep ruts... threading the needle, basically praying my tires would hold because braking was not an option. Somehow made it. Morgan and another guy crashed. Later heard someone broke a femur here." One rider required 911 medical evacuation. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Mid-Race Climb:** After Aid 2, "the bottom of the descent and the lowest point on the course before crossing a couple train tracks and turning left onto another ~10 mile false flat 'climb' section with some kickers stuffed in there." (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Final Approach:** "13 mile false flat descent into town" followed by "the final .75 mile steep finishing climb." (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Surface Quality:** "The course itself was a mix of crushed gravel and rolling hills, deceptively smooth but riddled with hidden challenges." (https://argonautcycles.com/racing-roundup-real-west-gravel-grit-strategy-and-sheer-determination/)

**Train Crossings:** "Train Crossings at Approx. Miles 42 & 43 (Long Loop) Just past Aid 2/3 'The Hub' - Trains may pass—be prepared for a possible stop." (https://groups.google.com/a/obra.org/g/obra-chat/c/sN854zTvRPs)

**Guard Dogs Warning:** "Lambing Season & Guard Dogs: Possible guard dogs between miles 46-52 due to lambing season. If pre-riding, wait for the dogs to retreat before proceeding—do not be seen as a threat to livestock." (https://groups.google.com/a/obra.org/g/obra-chat/c/sN854zTvRPs)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Race Dynamics - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** "Course is only ~65 miles long too so with a smaller group that means basically any major move will be important." Early attacks matter more on shorter courses with small fields. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Early Attack Strategy - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** Launched attack at mile 6 on steep kicker before long false flat. "With only three of us really rotating on the front at this point and the rest of the field playing passenger I figured I would give it a good wack here to see who was serious." Attack successful - split to three riders. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Positioning Critical - Shawn Petersen [ELITE]:** "Positioning in the wind is everything. Fighting at the back instead of being in the middle in my group cost me." Also: "Decisive moves happen FAST. That split at the feed zone! I should've seen it coming." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Feed Zone Dynamics - Shawn Petersen [ELITE]:** "Feed Zone Mess-Up: One guy pulled off, I eased up, lost focus for a second, and boom—the group surged. Morgan and I were suddenly off the back. We chased hard but never made it back. Later I heard... the group attacked itself and blew up anyway." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Nutrition - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** "(2) bottles of 100g MNSTRY powder. (4) MNSTRY gels. Did not use (1) gel, consumed everything else." For a 3:39:40 finish time over ~65 miles. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Neutral Rollout Reality - Shawn Petersen [ELITE]:** "Supposed to be neutral… did 333w for 4:45 minutes just to stay up there with the top 15ish." The neutral section wasn't actually neutral for those trying to stay with the front. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Final Climb Tactics - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** "In hindsight I think I should have lead into the climb and laid down a hard pace right from the start - I think that would have played to my strengths a bit better" rather than sitting in and trying to sprint. (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Conservative Pacing - Cory Sullivan [ELITE]:** "Took a calculated approach, working within a larger group to conserve energy for the final push." (https://argonautcycles.com/racing-roundup-real-west-gravel-grit-strategy-and-sheer-determination/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Cold-Related DNF:** Stefano Barberi, a "seasoned veteran" in the lead group of three, was "forced to withdraw" due to "bitter cold" on the exposed plateau section. (https://argonautcycles.com/racing-roundup-real-west-gravel-grit-strategy-and-sheer-determination/)

**Crash-Related Mechanicals:** Ben Farver's GRX shifter locked into crash mode after a fall, causing him to lose his group while resetting it. Shawn Petersen also experienced a crash-related shifter issue. (https://argonautcycles.com/racing-roundup-real-west-gravel-grit-strategy-and-sheer-determination/ and https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Serious Crash Injury:** One rider broke a femur on the technical descent and required 911 medical evacuation. Multiple other crashes reported on this section. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Drivetrain Issues in Mud:** Shawn Petersen couldn't shift into easiest gears, dropped his chain 4 times on the hardest climb, had to dismount and run. "Poured a bit of my bottle onto my derailleur—fixed it enough to keep going." Later couldn't shift into biggest gear on descent, "had to coast instead of hammering as I would spin out." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/shawns-story-real-west-gravel)

**Weather-Driven Course Changes:** "As conditions worsened, many riders opted for the short course, demonstrating their resilience in the face of adversity." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8431/brutal-conditions-mark-2nd-edition-of-real-west-gravel-race)

**Course Evaluation:** "The challenges faced during this year's race have prompted Mudslinger Events to reevaluate the course layout for future events. Plans are already underway to shift the race to the north side in 2026, where a more maintained road system will help mitigate the muddy conditions." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8431/brutal-conditions-mark-2nd-edition-of-real-west-gravel-race)

**Community Values:** Ryan's selfless decision to stay behind and assist an injured rider rather than chase back: "That's the kind of integrity we're proud to see in the sport." (https://argonautcycles.com/racing-roundup-real-west-gravel-grit-strategy-and-sheer-determination/)

**Grassroots Vibe - Matthew Wiebe [ELITE]:** "From everything I could tell leading into race weekend the vibe would be pretty similar to the Grasshopper series that I and everyone else in NorCal is familiar with." (https://argonautcycles.com/real-west-gravel-from-matthews-eyes/)

**Local Character:** The motto is "Let R Ride" and Pendleton is "3 hours away from PDX, Boise, and Spokane, and 4 hours from Bend." The town is "known for sheep, wool, and a long-standing rodeo." **Event Inclusions:** "Real West Gravel T or Socks, Custom Race Plate," fully stocked aid stations with Hammer Nutrition Heed, water, PB&J, single-serve cookies, pretzels, pickles, gluten-free options. Mechanical support at venue and aid stations 1, 2, 3. (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/real-west-gravel)

**Vendor Presence:** USWE hydration packs on site with 20% discounts, Argonaut Cycles present showcasing custom bikes. (https://groups.google.com/a/obra.org/g/obra-chat/c/sN854zTvRPs)

**Award Structure:** "Top 3 Awards for all distances, plus a top team award for the best team time on the long course for your top 3 riders. Age group champions get 50% off for next year's event!" (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/real-west-gravel)
