# RELIANCE DEEP WOODS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Saturday's course was burly: 66 miles and 9200 feet. And with a good bit of chunky gravel/double track, my 2016 Niner RKT RDO with 2.2 Race Kings was the right bike for the gnar. It had dumped rain the night before, so sections of the course could be particularly spicey." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "I rolled into the finish line, 1st Master's and 2nd woman, with a time of 6:07. Chelsie Tan had finished in 6:04. I had heard about her and finally got to meet her. Tiny, she's lucky is she stands 5'1", but I bet her watts/kg is phenomenal. Previously a World Tour level athlete, she has found that mountain biking is much more fun." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "If you want champagne gravel, head to the middle of the country. If you want to do hard things and destroy your limits, then this race is for YOU!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]:** "The race website sum it up perfectly: do not expect champagne gravel, flat roads or easy routes but do plan on awesome scenery, a deep backwoods feel and solid climbs….they definitely delivered!" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]:** "Well then the race takes a turn. We then hit many miles of climbs and descents where a mtb would (and did) shine. Basically the gravel turned to eroded dirt roads with endless embedded rock outcrops-not bad going up, but definitely served a beating for anyone on a gravel bike (including me) going down." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]:** "Managed to take 1st place in Masters and 5th overall for the race! And, as a podium bonus, we got podium HUGS! Basecamp Winter Program played a big role in ensuring I had a super solid foundation-stoked on that!" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "After a restless night's sleep (achy legs), Sunday morning I drove back to the venue with my Trek Checkpoint sporting 45mm Continental Terra Speeds. They would be a little sketch in some areas of the course, but with 23 miles of pavement and mostly smooth'ish gravel (relatively speaking), this bike was gonna be fast, as long as I had the legs to power it." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** "Along the second half of Fingerboard, with all those short punchy climbs, the wheels started to come off. My low fuel light came on. But I could smell the finish... Rolling past the JMT trailhead, I was at 4:55. I challenged myself to get to the finish under 5 hours. It hurt like hell, but I managed to squeak it in at 4:59." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Bike Choice for Stage 1 (Technical Day):**
- **Carey Lowery:** 2016 Niner RKT RDO mountain bike with 2.2" Race King tires - "the right bike for the gnar" with chunky gravel/double track. "Wish I woulda tried these tires years ago!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)
- **Jon Livengood:** Lauf gravel bike with 2.2" tires - "the promoter suggested a hardtail as being the best bike for todays gravel. Well since I had my fancy new Lauf with 2.2 tires on it, I figured that's hard tail enough." Notes that MTB riders caught him on technical descents: "I started seeing the mtb's catch me" and "lost 4 minutes there!" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Bike Choice for Stage 2 (Less Technical Day):**

(https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)
- **Jon Livengood:** Notes Stage 2 had "gravel bike being the proper bike for today" and "The Lauf crush the course today!" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Tire Performance:**
Carey specifically praised 2.2" Race King tires for wet, muddy conditions on chunky double track: "This section was quite wet and a little muddy, but the Kings were hooking up well." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Stage 1 - Specific Climbs and Sections:**

**Childer's Creek and Power House Road:** "Five miles of mostly free speed on pavement ... I will take it! Just that one stiff little climb." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Swinging Bridge Climb:** "The 3 mile climb after the swinging bridge went by quickly." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Smith Mountain Climb:** "6 miles of chunky double track... quite wet and a little muddy" after rain. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Kimsey Descent to Piney Flats:** "This descent is WAY more fun after only 20 miles in your legs as opposed to 120 miles when doing the Dirty 130. Piney Flats was wet but grippy. There were a couple of severely rutted out sections where you had to carefully pick your lines." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Wolf Creek-McFarland Climb (10 miles total):** "The Wolf Creek section was smooth, 6-8 % average grade... From there, I made the right turn onto McFarland and continued climbing another 5 miles, but with horribly steep pitches, washed out road beds, and chonky bedrock to negotiate." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Lost Creek Section:** "The last 13 miles seemed to fly by, standing and hammering the climbs and raging the descents." Carey PR'd this section. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Stage 2 - Specific Climbs and Sections:**

**Start on Hwy 30:** "Fast start uphill on Hwy 30... After some initial rollers, I felt my legs begin to come around." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Kimsey Mountain Climb:** "10 mile" climb with a "60 minute" duration mentioned. Goes "through the low hanging clouds" to sunny conditions above. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html, https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Ditney Descent:** "Those 4.4 miles I settled into a conservative 'don't wanna flat' speed" - chunky conditions. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Hiwassee River Road:** "Dirt road as smooth as a baby's bottom." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Unicoi Turnpike (Joe Brown Hwy):** "3.5 mile climb up to Unicoi Gap where the aid station was." Descent described as "fast and furiously smooth." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Duckett Ridge/Ironsburg Road:** "Ahhh ... fresh buttery smooth pavement!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Fingerboard Section:** "All those short punchy climbs" - where Carey's energy depleted. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Childers Creek:** "Who the f*ck put all those rollers on Childers? I didn't remember those! Yep, I was tired and ready to be done. Up and over, up and over, up and over I pedaled until my legs filled with lead." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Stage 2 Final 20 Miles:** Jon notes "multiple 'Flanders steepness' hills...so like 3-5 minute efforts on 15%+ grades." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Stage 1 - Fast Start Decision:**
**Carey Lowery:** "The start was faster than my legs wanted to go this early on, but I knew I needed to hang on to enjoy the draft going up Childer's Creek and Power House Road. Five miles of mostly free speed on pavement ... I will take it!" Notes she "pulled away from a few ladies" on the stiff climb as "the diesel engine was warmed up." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**[ELITE] Early Race Group Tactics:**
**Jon Livengood:** "Race stated relatively chill, but on the early paved climb it exploded and the peloton was reduced to small groups of five. I settled into the second group as I knew going deep this early would crush my legs later in the day and drafting does not come into play today." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Descending Strategy:**
**Jon Livengood:** On technical descents with MTB riders: "I hung on the best I could on the uphill, but decided not to push it on the down and let the two mtb riders fade away and drop me on the downhill (lost 4 minutes there!)" - conscious decision to preserve equipment/safety over time. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Carey Lowery:** On Stage 2's Ditney descent: "I settled into a conservative 'don't wanna flat' speed." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**[ELITE] Multi-Day Recovery:**
**Carey Lowery:** "I tried to stay conservative, as tomorrow was another hard day, but I was having so much fun." Left early after Stage 1: "I could not stay and join in the afternoon camraderie and festivities, as I had to go home to let the kids out... But it also gave me good reason to get home and try to recover for tomorrow's stage." Still had "a restless night's sleep (achy legs)". (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html, https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**[ELITE] Stage 2 - Leading from Behind:**
**Carey Lowery:** "Having a 45 minute lead over 2nd place, I took it easy and settled in behind a group of ladies." Notes missing the days of quick recovery: "I miss those days where just one good night's sleep and I was ready to hammer again the following day." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**[ELITE] Aid Station Efficiency:**
**Carey Lowery Stage 1:** "I stopped briefly to eat a banana and refill one bottle" at mile 32. "I passed by the aid station without stopping" on the second pass. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Carey Lowery Stage 2:** "At the aid station, Matt partially filled a bottle for me. I was in and out in less than 20 seconds." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Jon Livengood Stage 2:** "At maybe mile 40 I almost reeled in 3rd place coming into the aid station, but unfortunately he rolled through it while I had to stop to get some for fluids…heartbreaker as his tiny <10 second lead now jumped to 40 sec." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Training Stress Scores:**
**Jon Livengood:** Stage 1: "5:20 hrs on the bike and 338 TSS!" Stage 2: "4 hours on the bike today and 278 TSS." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**[ELITE] Fueling Issues:**
**Carey Lowery:** On Stage 2's Fingerboard section: "Along the second half of Fingerboard, with all those short punchy climbs, the wheels started to come off. My low fuel light came on." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Navigation Errors:** Carey witnessed another rider's costly mistake: "As I turned right off of the Smith Mountain descent, Randy Kerr came riding up behind me. WTH?!? Apparently he had ridden the Piney Flats section twice instead of coming down Smith. Man, I hated it for him. When the course has lots of criss-crossing, you have got to be astute in doing your homework, as well as watching for the upcoming turns, so that you don't end up repeating a section or going backwards on the course. Ask me how I know ... Gravel Worlds 2023 😣" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Flats:** Carey encountered "two ladies, one helping the other who had flatted" on the chunky Ditney descent during Stage 2, which influenced her to slow down. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Wrong Bike Choice:** Jon notes the disadvantage of gravel bikes on Stage 1's technical terrain: "I started seeing the mtb's catch me" and "lost 4 minutes" on one technical downhill section against MTB riders. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Festival Atmosphere:** "This was as much a festival as it was a race" with options to "clean up at the campground, chow down on some grub, and have a beer or NA beer, or two." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Growth and Improvements:** "Shannon Burke, who operates TennesseeGravel.com, started this 2 day gravel race last year. With a few tweaks this year and a lot more sponsors, he had upped the game and I had a feeling this was gonna be a great weekend to race, swap stories, and even party a little." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Course Improvements:** "This year's Day 1 course was so much better than last year's. Long climbs and technical descents made for a great day of racing and adventure in my backyard." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Grassroots Vibe:** Multiple sources describe it as "grassroots gravel grinding" with emphasis on "grassroots camaraderie." Event advertises: "What to Expect: Amazing Scenery, Grassroots Camaraderie, Good Food & Tasty Beer! What NOT to Expect: Flat Roads, Easy Routes, Champagne Gravel" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html, https://www.tennesseegravel.com/reliance)
