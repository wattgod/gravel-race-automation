## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Stage 1 (66 miles/9200 ft per Carey, 65 miles/8900 ft per Jon) [ELITE perspectives]:**

- Starts with 5 miles of mostly pavement including Childer's Creek and Power House Road with "just that one stiff little climb"
- 3-mile climb after the swinging bridge
- Smith Mountain climb: 6 miles of chunky double track that was wet and muddy in 2025
- Kimsey descent to Piney Flats: "WAY more fun after only 20 miles in your legs as opposed to 120 miles when doing the Dirty 130"
- Piney Flats: "wet but grippy. There were a couple of severely rutted out sections where you had to carefully pick your lines"
- Aid station at mile 32
- 10-mile Wolf Creek-McFarland climb: Wolf Creek section was "smooth, 6-8% average grade," then McFarland continued another 5 miles "with horribly steep pitches, washed out road beds, and chonky bedrock to negotiate"
- Smith Mountain descent (returning via different route)
- Course has "lots of criss-crossing" requiring careful navigation to avoid wrong turns
- Short climb on Lost Creek
- Last 13 miles flew by with climbs and descents

(https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]** described Stage 1 as "95% gravel (errâ€¦not paved)" with terrain that "turned to eroded dirt roads with endless embedded rock outcrops-not bad going up, but definitely served a beating for anyone on a gravel bike (including me) going down." Included "45 minute climb up, then another 30 minute downhill" followed by "another hour-ish climb and then a super tech downhill." Last 10 miles was "pristine gravel (for Tennessee)." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Stage 2 (60 miles/7300 ft per Carey, 60 miles/6300 ft per Jon) [ELITE perspectives]:**

- Fast start uphill on Hwy 30
- 10-mile Kimsey climb described as relatively steady
- 4.4-mile descent down Ditney (chunky, ridden up the previous day)
- 5-mile stretch of rolling pavement heading down to Farner ("very lonely")
- Hiwassee River Road: "a dirt road as smooth as a baby's bottom"
- Shuler Creek Road (flats)
- 3.5-mile climb up Unicoi Turnpike (Joe Brown Hwy) to Unicoi Gap where aid station was located, climbing through low clouds
- Fast, smooth descent down Joe Brown to Cooper Hollow
- Fresh, buttery smooth pavement over to Ironsburg Road
- Duckett Ridge climb with struggle-inducing sections
- Second half of Fingerboard with "short punchy climbs"
- Final "tar n chip climb"
- Towee Pike descent to Childer's Creek with multiple rollers ("up and over, up and over")
- Last segment includes "multiple 'Flanders steepness' hillsâ€¦3-5 minute efforts on 15%+ grades"
- Final paved segment to finish

(https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html and https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Stage 2 mix:** "23 miles of pavement and mostly smooth'ish gravel (relatively speaking)" with one technical dirt road sector after the Kimsey climb. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** On Stage 1, deliberately didn't chase the fast early start: "The start was faster than my legs wanted to go this early on, but I knew I needed to hang on to enjoy the draft going up Childer's Creek and Power House Road. Five miles of mostly free speed on pavement ... I will take it!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** Managed effort across two days: "I tried to stay conservative, as tomorrow was another hard day, but I was having so much fun" during Stage 1's final 13 miles. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** Stage 2 start strategy with 45-minute lead: "Having a 45 minute lead over 2nd place, I took it easy and settled in behind a group of ladies." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]:** "Race stated relatively chill, but on the early paved climb it exploded and the peloton was reduced to small groups of five. I settled into the second group as I knew going deep this early would crush my legs later in the day and drafting does not come into play today." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]:** Used wheel-following strategy: Found a rider "riding just slightly harder than me on this climb, so I stuck to his wheel and let him tow me up the mountain. A nice comfortably hard pace, but not one that required any match burning." On Stage 2, employed similar tactics on the Unicoi Turnpike climb. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Jon Livengood [ELITE]:** Stage 2 chase dynamics: "At maybe mile 40 I almost reeled in 3rd place coming into the aid station, but unfortunately he rolled through it while I had to stop to get some for fluidsâ€¦heartbreaker as his tiny <10 second lead now jumped to 40 sec. **Carey Lowery [ELITE]:** Final effort on Stage 2: "Rolling past the JMT trailhead, I was at 4:55. I challenged myself to get to the finish under 5 hours. ## DNF Risk Factors

**Navigation errors:** Carey observed: "When the course has lots of criss-crossing, you have got to be astute in doing your homework, as well as watching for the upcoming turns, so that you don't end up repeating a section or going backwards on the course." She witnessed Randy Kerr accidentally ride the Piney Flats section twice instead of coming down Smith Mountain. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Mechanical risk on technical descents:** Carey noted on Stage 2's Ditney descent: "Those 4.4 miles I settled into a conservative 'don't wanna flat' speed. Halfway in, I came across two ladies, one helping the other who had flatted. I think I may have slowed down a bit more after that." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)

**Bike choice consequences [ELITE perspective]:** Jon Livengood on Stage 1: Gravel bikes with 2.2" tires took "a beating" on technical descents with embedded rock outcrops, losing significant time to mountain bikes. Lost 4 minutes on one super technical downhill to MTB riders. (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/jons-story-reliance-deep-woods-gravel-grinder)

**Bonking risk [ELITE perspective]:** Carey described Stage 2 struggle: "Along the second half of Fingerboard, with all those short punchy climbs, the wheels started to come off. My low fuel light came on." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-2.html)
