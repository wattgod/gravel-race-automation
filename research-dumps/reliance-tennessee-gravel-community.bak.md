Well, hello there, sparkly legs! The Smith Mountain climb was a beast at 6 miles of chunky double track... I ended up PR'ing the Lost Creek section, too. ## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE - Carey Lowery]:** "2016 Niner RKT RDO with 2.2 Race Kings was the right bike for the gnar" for the chunky gravel/double track sections. She noted "the Kings were hooking up well. Wish I woulda tried these tires years ago!" (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**[ELITE - Mike]:** Course description suggests full suspension mountain bikes would be appropriate: "Saturday saw some sections that were more fit for a full squish mountain bike than a gravel bike." However, he completed it on a gravel bike. (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**[COMPETITIVE - Beth Lofgren]:** For hot conditions, carried 2.5L on back plus 2x 24oz bottles on bike for hydration on Tellico Highlands route. (https://www.tennesseegravel.com/ride-reports)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Saturday Race Route (65-66 miles, ~8,800-9,200 ft):**

**[ELITE - Carey Lowery's breakdown]:** 
- First 5 miles: "faster than my legs wanted to go" on pavement including Childer's Creek and Power House Road
- Mile ~5-8: 3-mile climb after swinging bridge
- Smith Mountain: "6 miles of chunky double track" - described as "a beast"
- Kimsey descent to Piney Flats: "WAY more fun after only 20 miles in your legs as opposed to 120 miles when doing the Dirty 130"
- Piney Flats: "wet but grippy. There were a couple of severely rutted out sections where you had to carefully pick your lines"
- Aid station at mile 32
- Wolf Creek-McFarland climb: 10 miles total - "Wolf Creek section was smooth, 6-8% average grade. From there, I made the right turn onto McFarland and continued climbing another 5 miles, but with horribly steep pitches, washed out road beds, and chonky bedrock to negotiate"
- Smith Mountain descent on return
- Lost Creek: "short climb" in final 13 miles
(https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**[ELITE - Mike's experience]:** "5 miles in we started our first of MANY climbs... As our route took us near the top of a mountain those of us doing the race route were sent to the right to continue our way up the mountain, then back to the base of the climb to do it all over again." Descent speeds hit "nearly 40 mph in a few spots." Found "loose gravel on some slick-rock" on one switchback. (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Sunday Race Route (50-60 miles, ~6,300 ft):**

**[ELITE - Mike's breakdown]:** "In the first 16 miles we climbed for all but 3 minutes. At 5 miles in we started an 11 mile climb. There were a couple of sections where it flattened, but there was no descending for 11 miles... At one point we were sent up a 5 mile climb filled with switchbacks that arrived at the top of Buck Bald where we were greeted with an aid station and a 360° view of the surrounding mountains." (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Course Navigation Challenge:** 

**[ELITE - Carey Lowery]:** "As I turned right off of the Smith Mountain descent, Randy Kerr came riding up behind me. WTH?!? Apparently he had ridden the Piney Flats section twice instead of coming down Smith. Man, I hated it for him. When the course has lots of criss-crossing, you have got to be astute in doing your homework, as well as watching for the upcoming turns, so that you don't end up repeating a section or going backwards on the course." (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE - Carey Lowery's approach]:** Started conservatively: "The start was faster than my legs wanted to go this early on, but I knew I needed to hang on to enjoy the draft going up Childer's Creek and Power House Road. Five miles of mostly free speed on pavement ... I will take it!" 

At aid station (mile 32): "stopped briefly to eat a banana and refill one bottle."

Final 13 miles strategy: "I tried to stay conservative, as tomorrow was another hard day, but I was having so much fun."

Finished Day 1 in 6:07, 3 minutes behind 1st place woman. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**[ELITE - Mike's pacing note]:** On Sunday, working with another racer: "I would drop her on the short little climbs, and she would hang on the descents. We only had a couple of miles left and we hit pavement. Since we had been going along for a while we rolled across the line together." (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**[COMPETITIVE - Gus Wolf's ambitious plan]:** "I got up at 5 am, worked a full day and drove out to the start point. Started Pedaling around 6pm ish. My plan was to complete in 24 hours..." (https://www.tennesseegravel.com/ride-reports)

## DNF Risk Factors

**High Attrition Rate:** "100 registrants, 80-some started, and only 38 finished" - only 38% completion rate for those who started. (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Crashes on Technical Descents:** [ELITE - Mike] crashed at ~20 mph on loose gravel over slick-rock during a switchback, doing a "face-first slide for a few feet down the chunky gravel road." His bell was rung and he worried about concussion but continued. Finished with "blood on my hands, legs and one arm." (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Navigation Errors:** [ELITE - Randy Kerr] accidentally repeated the Piney Flats section instead of descending Smith Mountain due to course complexity. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Day 2 Injury Impact:** [ELITE - Mike] on Sunday morning: "wasn't sure if I would be able to do the race route due to scabs and stiffness from my injuries. Not to mention the lack of sleep due to inability to get comfortable." Still completed Day 2 and finished 21st overall. (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Grassroots Vibe:** Official description emphasizes "grassroots gravel grinding" with "Music ~ Prizes ~ Food ~ Beer ~ Camping" and explicitly states what NOT to expect: "Flat Roads, Easy Routes, Champagne Gravel" (https://www.tennesseegravel.com/reliance)

**Venue:** Event based at Hiwassee Outfitters, 155 Ellis Creek Rd, Reliance, TN. Camping available on-site. (https://www.tennesseegravel.com/reliance)

**Race Director Engagement:** [ELITE - Mike] noted: "As I rolled into the finish line in 23rd place the race director came to ask me if I was OK. He had gotten word on the radio of my wreck." (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Cell Service Warning:** "Cell phone service ceased as we got about 3 miles from the campground." (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Course Support:** Moto course patrol present - two patrol riders checked on Mike after his crash and verified his bike was rideable. Aid stations on both days, including one at Buck Bald summit on Sunday. (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Post-Race:** Saturday included "afternoon camraderie and festivities" though some riders had to leave for home responsibilities. (https://careylowery.blogspot.com/2025/04/reliance-deep-woods-gravel-race-stage-1.html)

**Scenic Journey:** [ELITE - Mike]: "Friday we spent the day finding our way to Hiwassee Outfitters in Reliance Tn. Winding roads took us through beautiful forests and cool little towns... We wound through some AMAZING roads... If it had not been a race I would have stopped to take pics of some of the stuff we rode on those bikes or the beautiful views." (https://gumbi1.wordpress.com/2024/06/01/reliance-gravel/)

**Benefits Local Organization:** Event benefits SORBA Chattanooga (Southern Off-Road Bicycle Association). (https://www.tennesseegravel.com/reliance)
