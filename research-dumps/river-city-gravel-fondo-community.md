# RIVER CITY GRAVEL FONDO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Results

The River City Gravel Fondo is the kickoff event of the Trek BC Gravel Series on Vancouver Island, held in Campbell River, BC. The event takes place in April and features routes through gravel roads managed by Mosaic Forest Management, BC Parks, and local First Nations territories.

**2024 Event:** 178 participants competed across three distances (https://www.panachecyclingsports.com/post/river-city-gravel-fondo-a-huge-success)

**2025 Event:** 216 participants competed, with the race held on April 20, 2025 (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=386987)

### Distance Options:
- **Impulso:** ~35-40km (shorter route, out-and-back staying south of Gold River Highway)
- **Intermedio:** ~70-71km (loop including roads north of Campbell Lake)
- **Esperto:** ~115-120km (extended loop around gravel roads and lakes north of Campbell Lake)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

Course designer John Vanderveen, Canadian Masters Gravel Champion, created routes described as featuring "muscle aching terrain" but also "awe-inspiring views that can only be found on the West Coast of BC." (https://www.mosaicforests.com/news-views/continuation-of-great-partnership-results-in-2025-edition-of-epic-race)

The terrain is characterized as "moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12111/river-city-gravel-fondo)

Routes utilize "exclusive private gravel roads in the remote wilderness" through logging roads in Campbell River and Mosaic Forest Management lands. (https://www.mosaicforests.com/news-views/continuation-of-great-partnership-results-in-2025-edition-of-epic-race)

The race takes place through "pristine area West of Campbell River" including "trails and logging roads in Camp[bell River]" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dm41yeTkcEs and https://www.panachecyclingsports.com/post/river-city-gravel-fondo-a-huge-success)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The inaugural 2024 event "was met with huge success and praise from participants" according to the race organizer. (https://www.panachecyclingsports.com/post/river-city-gravel-fondo-a-huge-success)

The event grew from 178 participants in 2024 to 216 in 2025, indicating strong community response. (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=349372 and https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=386987)

Race director Jon Watkin stated: "For 2025, we are taking things to a new level with the continuation of our great partnership with Trek Bicycles and Mosaic Forest Management. With the expansion of the series to 4 epic events, we want to highlight the Island as the best gravel cycling destination in the world and give a ride experience that will create lasting fond memories." (https://www.mosaicforests.com/news-views/continuation-of-great-partnership-results-in-2025-edition-of-epic-race)

The event features riders "of all ages" with categories including open (19-34), youth (13-18), masters (35+), plus e-bike and vintage bike categories. (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=349372)

**[UNKNOWN]** The event description emphasizes it's designed "for those not wanting to race" and invites "those who are simply looking for a challenge and to share their experiences with colleagues, friends, and family." (https://cyclingbc.net/event/river-city-gravel-fondo-2025/)

Start location is at Pedal Your World Bike Shop (2024) or We Wai Kai Sports Field (2025), with participants encouraged to "visit the local host cities and take advantage of promotions from select hospitality partners and restaurants." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12111/river-city-gravel-fondo and https://campbellriver.travel/news/upcoming-cycling-events-in-campbell-river/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] 2024 Esperto Winners:**
- Fabian Merino completed 120km in 3:53:05 (average speed ~30.8 km/h)
- Katja Verkerk completed 120km in 4:41:10 (average speed ~25.6 km/h)

**[ELITE] 2025 Esperto Winners:**
- Michael Van Den Ham completed 120km in 3:16:10 (average speed ~36.7 km/h) — notably faster than 2024
- Sofia Donnecke completed 120km in 3:59:27 (average speed ~30.1 km/h)

(https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=349372 and https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=386987)

The race uses mass start format with manual wave starts grouped by distance. (https://www.webscorer.com/race?raceid=386987)
