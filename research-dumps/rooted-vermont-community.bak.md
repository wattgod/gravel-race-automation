# ROOTED VERMONT — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Adam Roberge [ELITE]:** "The men's Sip of Sunshine race was decided on a section of steep fourth-class road with chicken-head gravel surface. The relatively short course distance — 85 miles versus 100 or 200 — allows a lot more guys to be in the mix, so you need to go hard from the beginning. Rooted can get very strategic. It's such a fast course. The race played out in the technical sections." He also praised "the post-race meal of pulled pork sliders, salmon and pork poke bowls, and maple creemies. Rooted still feels like a race, but everything about the event is about enjoying yourself." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Katie Kantzes [ELITE]:** "Clear sunshine, killer climbs, gravel and gnar – Rooted Vermont was an epic day. The race played out over almost 9,000 feet of climbing (with a massive kick in the teeth, cat 4 climb at only mile 3) and technical single track with plenty of rocks and, of course, roots. The lead group of 3 women went clear around the first class 4 dirt section at mile 20. With long steady climbs in between, by mile 50 (the second class 4) it was down to 2. I'm not a gifted technical rider by any stretch, but I'd put my 3T through its paces and I was grateful and confident that the bike could handle anything. I took my shot to climb away from Sarah Lange who had been putting in massive pulls." (https://us.3t.bike/blogs/news/the-race-winning-racemax-from-rooted-vermont)

**Samantha Fox [ELITE]:** "Rooted provides progression. You can come as a total newbie, and leave the clinic with skills. You can show up at Rooted and you know people, you aren't alone. There is no other race event where that is the case." Fox noted she signed up "so she could hang out with a bunch of other riders in a fun environment. She chose the short course because she didn't have time to train for the long one." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Matt Wilson [UNKNOWN]:** "I cried and smiled more this weekend than ever before. Seeing the community in action, being there, I understood, I felt why Moriah was so fond of and excited to be part of it. The community that Ted and Laura and Kristin are shaping with the Rooted race and in gravel in a larger way highlights the fact that relationships and friendships and loving each other and supporting each other is what life is really about." (This was Matt's first gravel race, ridden in honor of his sister Mo Wilson) (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**jwellford [COMPETITIVE]:** "What an amazing event! I'd highly recommend it to any of you northeast gravel racers/riders. Incredibly well-organized, huge number of volunteers, fun 'mullet protocol' that cares as much about the back of the pack as the pros in the front, and we were treated to a beautiful Vermont morning with only a bit of rain several hours in." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/rooted-vermont-anyone/61531)

**Mark Ouellette [ELITE]:** "The goal was a top 10 overall finish. I came very close last year with 14th male overall, and several incredibly strong female riders smoked me too. The highlight of the ride was bumping into fellow BaseCampers Larry Rose, Andrea Troy, and Sarah Porch. It is always more fun to share events with others... Here is a big win for BaseCamp and a picture of me making the podium for a 3rd place finish. Old guys can still give the younger guys a run for their money!" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/marks-story-rooted-vemont)

**Anurag Saxena [UNKNOWN]:** "This year, I signed up for the Big Sip route (82 miles with 8,000 ft of climbing) but I ended up riding the Little Sip course (48 miles with 4,200 ft of climbing). I think that is my limit for climbing for the distance ridden. The course traversed through beautiful Vermont country side with some difficult sections of climbing. Every time I come to Vermont I love the scenery even more... For me, Rooted Vermont is a summer camp with a gravel race on the side." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2022/8/26/recap-rooted-vermont-2022)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile — Sip of Sunshine (Long Course):**
- 85 miles with 9,500 feet of climbing (2021 measurement) / 8,000-9,000 feet reported by various riders
- "Massive kick in the teeth, cat 4 climb at only mile 3" [ELITE perspective]
- Heavy on climbing with "sections of steep and bouldery class 4 road"
- "Uphill singletrack finish after blasting through a wall of soapy foam courtesy of the folks from Vermont's Rasputista"
- Multiple Class IV sections: "first class 4 dirt section at mile 20" and "second class 4 by mile 50"
- Technical single track with "plenty of rocks and, of course, roots"
- Vermont's gravel characterized as "Vermont Pavé" — dried mud creating rough surfaces
(https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/ and https://us.3t.bike/blogs/news/the-race-winning-racemax-from-rooted-vermont)

**Little Sip (Short Course):**
- 49 miles with 4,300 feet of climbing (2021) / 48 miles with 4,200 feet (2022)
(https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Surface Conditions:**
Adam Roberge [ELITE] described "steep fourth-class road with chicken-head gravel surface" as the decisive section where the men's race was won. He noted "It's such a fast course" despite the technical sections. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Aid Stations:**

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**The "Mullet Protocol" Philosophy:**
The event promotes #mulletprotocol — "business at the front, party at the back" — emphasizing that both elite racing and recreational participation are equally valued. Co-race director Kristin Motley explained: "We're here to support and cheer on each other; we want Rooted to be both an event for elite riders and an event for the people. A lot of races do one or the other. But most people who ride Rooted come for more than just a start line." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Gender Parity Achievement:**
2022 marked the achievement of the organizers' goal: gender parity on the start line with 800 riders from 42 states and 8 countries. The event featured "a non-binary podium and a podium for para-athletes in addition to traditional men's and women's podiums." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Last Finisher Celebration:**
Laura King described her "goosebump moment" when more than 100 people welcomed the last place finisher with chants and cowbells: "It's not just about the race and the ride. The party is just as important." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Mullet Podium:**
In addition to race results podiums, there's a "mullet podium for those nominated by fellow riders for exemplifying the spirit of gravel." 2022 had 40 nominees. Winners included Ian Boswell (2021 race winner who "rode the short course this year and stopped repeatedly to assist other riders with mechanicals and flat tires. He also pulled creemees all weekend") and Mike Geehan "for his selflessness and community service." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Women's Clinic:**
A free women's gravel clinic is held two months before the race. The inaugural 2019 clinic had 50 participants; by 2021 it hosted 100 riders with 240 applicants. Laura King stated: "We don't actually care if the women who came to the clinic register for Rooted. We care about getting more women on bikes... When we think about the most authentic way to get more women into the sport, it means putting in the work to help people feel like they belong there in the first place." (https://www.singletracks.com/community/rooted-vermont-gravel-race-laura-ted-king/)

**Weekend Festival Atmosphere:**
The event is a three-day gravel festival with Friday night party at a local restaurant, all-day Saturday expo with extensive programming including shakeout rides, women's forum, and gravel community chat. SRAM provided mullet haircuts; Cannondale set up a slip n' slide. Post-race included pulled pork sliders, salmon and pork poke bowls, and maple creemies. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/ and https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2022/8/26/recap-rooted-vermont-2022)

**Memorials:**
The event has honored deceased community members including Moriah Wilson (murdered gravel racer whose brother Matt rode his first gravel race at Rooted in her honor) and Willem Jewett (local rider who died from cancer; his wife Ellen hosted an aid station with live music and a freshly built singletrack trail in his honor). (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/race-report-at-rooted-mullet-protocol-is-more-than-marketing/)

**Burlington Area:**
The race is based in Richmond, VT, with many riders staying in nearby Burlington. FergusYL noted Burlington is "like half an hour from my house" and recommended: "Maple creemies with maple dust (soft serve ice cream made with maple syrup and rolled in maple sugar). Red Hen Bakery in Middlesex has awesome creemies... Beer - Lawson's is a title sponsor, but the Alchemist in Stowe makes Heady Topper and Hill Farmstead in Greensboro was voted best brewery in the world five times." (https://www.trainerroad.com/forum/t/rooted-vermont-anyone/61531)

**Event Status:**
