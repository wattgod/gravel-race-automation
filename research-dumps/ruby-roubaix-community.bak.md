# RUBY ROUBAIX — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Ruby Loop (120.5 miles) - Specific Sections:**

The **Woods Lane/Lee section (mile 22)** is consistently called out as particularly challenging: "At mile 22 you will come off a gravel road at Woods Lane, cross over a paved highway and roll on to a dirt/bug dust (loose fine dirt), two-track road that presents significant challenges. Watch for large washouts and deep ruts. Riders should be especially cautious in this area as the terrain can be unpredictable and difficult to navigate... approximately 4.5-mile portion of the course." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

The **final 18 miles** are repeatedly noted as brutal: "At the community of Ruby Valley, you'll continue north on a lightly traveled paved road toward the rather deceptive summit of Secret Pass... you'll turn south on the Fort Halleck-Lower Lamoille gravel road for the final 18 mile grind over a number of deceptive rolling hills with some of the roughest gravel of the route" (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Harrison Pass descent** features "twisting, often loose and wash-boarded descent" after the 1,760' climb from Jiggs. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Water crossings** are possible "depending on the winter-spring snowpack" on multiple routes, particularly the southern sections. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Ruby Valley Road section** is noted as surprisingly smooth: "This section of 'gravel' is well maintained, and often smoother than some of the pavement we are accustomed to riding." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Lee Loop descent into Lee** includes "a short, steep, sometimes loose 2-track road – use caution and keep an eye out for your fellow cyclists." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Surface variety across all routes:** "Road Surfaces for all routes include smooth pavement, smooth compacted dirt/gravel, loose gravel, washboards and firm, rough surfaces." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Aid Station Strategy [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Many riders will bypass the first 2 or 3 aid stations if they're 'racing' the course and have sufficient training, fluids and food, however the final 20 miles can be the most grueling part of this ride. Don't forget to leave the Ft. Halleck aid station with plenty of water." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Route-specific elevation gain reality:** The Lee Loop is noted as having "deceptive" elevation at 2,015' total gain, with "most of the climbing is on the return leg." The Pleasant Valley Loop has 870' gain but riders are warned "Don't let the short distance fool you" - suggesting the rolling nature makes it harder than stats indicate. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Self-sufficiency required [ALL LEVELS]:** "In the mountain bike race tradition, please be prepared to complete any needed repairs on your own. Our sag support and aid stations will have pumps, chain tools and limited hex wrenches, but will likely not have trained bicycle mechanics on hand." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race and https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10441/ruby-roubaix-gravel-fondo)

**E-bike options available:** E-bike categories are offered for Secret Pass, Lee, and Pleasant Valley Loops (not the Ruby Loop). (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Charitable mission:** "Proceeds from this event support youth leadership development, exchange programs, local and global community improvement projects, and programs that encourage healthy behaviors and inspirational service to others" through the Rotary Club of Elko Desert Sunrise. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/)

**2024 community impact:** The event "helped the Rotary Club of Elko Desert Sunrise: Sponsor six students for Rotary youth leadership camps in Portola, California; Award $6,500 in scholarships to students pursuing college or technical education; Host an international exchange student from Italy." (https://elkocommunityhub.com/blog/ruby-roubaix)

**Event scale:** "Over 200 cyclists" participated in 2025. (https://elkocommunityhub.com/blog/ruby-roubaix)

**Event format:** "Fondo-style format means participants can choose to race for the podium or ride at their own pace, soaking in the vast expanses, challenging terrain, and the unparalleled small-town hospitality that Lamoille and the surrounding communities have to offer." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/events/discover-adventure-and-scenic-beauty-at-the-2024-ruby-roubaix-gravel-fondo/)

**Setting description:** Event is held in "rural northern Nevada" with "spectacular scenery, vast expanses of lightly traveled roads, challenging terrain and a healthy dose of small-town hospitality." Start/finish at "historic Lamoille School House" in Lamoille, Nevada. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/ and https://www.cyclingwest.com/events/discover-adventure-and-scenic-beauty-at-the-2024-ruby-roubaix-gravel-fondo/)

**Timing:** Event held late June (June 27, 2026; June 28, 2025; June 22, 2024). Wildflowers noted as "should be peaking over the summits and may be lingering at the lower elevations" and "should be near their annual peak on Secret Pass." Sandhill Cranes mentioned as wildlife to watch for. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Medical support:** "Silver State Race MedX joined us in 2025 to meet everyone's needs on the course." (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical preparation critical:** The repeated emphasis on self-sufficiency and "mountain bike race tradition" suggests mechanical failures could easily lead to DNFs without proper preparation and tools. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Water management on Ruby Loop:** The specific warning about leaving Fort Halleck aid station with plenty of water for the final 18-mile rough gravel section suggests dehydration risk in the closing miles. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)

**Underestimating shorter routes:** Multiple warnings not to be fooled by "short distance" on Lee and Pleasant Valley Loops, suggesting riders may start these undertrained and struggle with cumulative elevation and rough surfaces. (https://www.rubyroubaix.com/the-race)
