## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Tire width/mud clearance:** "You needed laser focus here cause if you veered 5 inches to the left or right, you were done for" on packed mud lanes "wide enough for a 45-50MM tire to teeter on." (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**[ELITE] Mud management tool:** "The race crew provided these paint mixer sticks the day before, but to be honest, the most effective way to shed the mud was to wait until you reached a long and dry enough downhill and just bomb down it, flinging the clumps in to oblivion." (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**[ELITE] Chain issues in mud:** "I dropped my chain twice, but caught up. I just kept pedaling." — Alex Marr (https://www.themountainmail.com/news/salida-76-mud-jams-race/article_c38f859e-265a-45ca-8b4b-a38d03aea0ba.html)

**[COMPETITIVE] Gearing for steep climbs:** "There were long sections of 15-17%. Since I was riding my wooden Renovo I had low enough gears so I was able to ride all of it. Not everyone was so fortunate." — Wendy Skean (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)

**[COMPETITIVE] Clothing for elevation:** "I chose to wear an undershirt and vest. On the climb I did get a little warm but was glad for all the clothes riding the loop at the top" (top at 10,000'). (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)

**On-course support:** "Absolute Bikes will provide on-course mechanical support." (https://strambecco.com/event/salida-76/)

Mid-race: "Not long after Aid 2 is when the rain started up again." (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Opening climb:** "The course started right at Riverside Park in town and quickly went up a road that quickly turned to dirt and the start of a 12-13 mile 3500' climb. No warmup but it wasn't insanely steep and it was nice to get almost 50% of the vert out of the way in the first 2 hours." [ELITE] (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**Area character:** "I honestly knew nothing about the area NE of Salida... While a nice area with rolling hills, it appears to be a part of the state where a certain portion of the population reenacts their favorite Breaking Bad scenes. Definitely a plethora of bombed out trailer meth labs galore, some of which still appeared to be occupied." [ELITE] (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**Mud sections:** "Immediately after leaving Aid 1, the mud started and forced people off the 'road' on to whatever alpine tundra was dry enough to get through... roughly 170-180 riders had gone before us, providing packed down mud lanes." [ELITE] (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**76K specific terrain:** "Most of the course here was on soft, sandy double track. I had never ridden in that much sand before so it was a little bit of a challenge. You had to keep changing lanes to find the hardest track. I was proud of myself for riding all of it." [COMPETITIVE] (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)

**Final climb:** "The final climb beyond Aid 3 was annoying, moderately hellacious – but it was short. I got in my absolute easiest gear and crawled up a 12-13% headwall." [ELITE] (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**Untimed finish:** "The race directors intelligently made the final 13 miles of the course untimed, since its all downhill and deterred anyone from going full bore and risking death to not even finish on the podium." [ELITE] (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**76K finish structure:** "The top was reached at mile 35 which was the finish. I know this sounds weird. With the next 12 miles being the long, steep descent on loose gravel the organization didn't want racers bombing down the road which could be very dangerous, especially since it was open to traffic. Your race time was recorded at the top but you were required to finish the ride back to Salida to be a finisher." [COMPETITIVE] (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)

**Hike-a-bike:** The 76K "includes access to private land and features a short hike-a-bike section at mile 22." (https://strambecco.com/event/salida-76/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Starting position for mud:** "roughly 170-180 riders had gone before us, providing packed down mud lanes" — suggests starting in first wave helps with mud management. (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**[ELITE] Elite group tactics:** "We topped out of the climb together… we knew we probably needed to stay together because it gets windy on the second half. The three stayed close to each other after this point." — top 3 finishers working together. (https://www.themountainmail.com/news/salida-76-mud-jams-race/article_c38f859e-265a-45ca-8b4b-a38d03aea0ba.html)

**[COMPETITIVE] Nutrition strategy:** "I've implemented an electrolyte heavy fluid plan to my rides this summer, along with 3-4oz of maple syrup added and a Honey Stinger waffle ingested every hour. At the aids I slam whatever fluids remain and house half a banana and a handful of peanut M&M's." [ELITE] (https://www.exploringtherockies.com/2025/09/30/salida-76-a-ride-to-a-bygone-era/)

**[COMPETITIVE] Wendy Skean's fuel (80+ age group winner):** "The fuel I used for this race was a mixture of Sustained Energy and Heed. I also had Race Caps Supreme, Mito Caps, Anti-Fatigue Caps, and Endurance BCAA+. Immediately after the race I had Vegan Recoverite." (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)

**[COMPETITIVE] Aid station strategy:** "When I reached the first check point I refilled with water and ate two Oreo cookies. So tasty. I had two more when I returned a second time before the last steep climb." (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)

**[COMPETITIVE] Physical management:** "I taped my back and shoulder" to prevent weakness issues experienced in previous race. "I only felt it a tiny bit on the climb and worked on relaxing my shoulders. It never bothered me again." (https://wendyskean.com/wendyskean.com/Blog/Entries/2024/10/18_SALIDA_76K_GRAVEL_RACESEPTEMBER_14,_SALIDA,_COLORADO.html)
