# SANTA FE CENTURY GRAVELÓN — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Key Stats

The **Gravelón** is a 54-mile timed gravel race that starts and ends on Rowe Mesa, southeast of Santa Fe, New Mexico. The course features **3,100 feet of climbing** across a mix of rough dirt roads, 9 miles of pavement (Highway 34), and rolling gravel roads through ponderosa groves, grasslands, and piñon/juniper forest.

**2024 Race Conditions:** Partly cloudy, 70-75°F (from official results)

**Participation trends:** The event saw 1,400 riders in 2024, down from 1,800 in 2023. Race director Hadji Corona attributed this to "economic insecurity" and people being "uncertain if they will have jobs... This is kind of a luxury." (santafenewmexican.com)

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Robert Splinter, 59 [RECREATIONAL]:** "You always have to pay attention to what's right in front of you. On the road, you can kind of relax a little bit." He previously raced the 54-mile gravelón, calling it "the hardest ride I've ever done." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Ana Splinter, 54 [RECREATIONAL]:** Describing her first mountain bike race in Mexico a decade ago: "It was pretty tough. I cried. I was cold. I was miserable. I was doing three miles an hour." When she reached the top: "I was just like dancing and shaking — I didn't care about anything because I finished." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Sophie Bair, 16 [UNKNOWN]:** Near the front of the 25-mile race, when asked about her preparation: "Honestly, I know nothing." She was primarily a runner who had done "a few bike races — but never a gravel race." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Hadji Corona (Race Director) [N/A]:** On the course difficulty: "People think they can be talented in different sports and go to cycling. But this will humble you in an instant." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Ramon Sarason, 63 [RECREATIONAL]:** A lifelong cyclist riding casually with his 12-year-old German shepherd: "It's not like his older, adrenaline-fueled days anymore... but I still get to have that youthful freedom of kind of flying on a bike — like a bird." (santafenewmexican.com)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course layout:** "Starting on Rowe Mesa, the first 14.8 miles of the clockwise loop begins on Forest roads that wind through the Ponderosa groves and grasslands of the transition zone typical of northern New Mexico. The right turn onto Highway 34 marks the beginning of 9 miles of a fast paved section without any big climbs and a change into the shorter trees of the piñon/juniper forest. The remaining 30 miles are all on improved dirt/gravel roads with a mix of rolling terrain with some moderate but longer climbs back to the start/finish." (santafecentury.com)

**Surface conditions:** The gravel trails were "edged with cholla and yucca — was pockmarked with car track ruts nearly a foot deep and strewn with rocks ranging from pebbles to large chunks." The terrain is described as "uneven and gravel-covered." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Wet weather contingency:** "In the event of wet conditions, it may become necessary to modify the course for the Gravelon due to the formation of mud and clay on Rowe Mesa, which creates extremely difficult riding conditions... Therefore, in order to ensure the safety of the Gravelon, an alternative route that provides better and safer riding conditions may be chosen." (santafecentury.com)

**Location challenges:** The start/finish is "located just inside the boundary of the Santa Fe National Forest off of Forest Road 326 on Rowe Mesa, a 20 minute drive southeast of Santa Fe" with "no cell service." Communication relies on ham radio operators. (santafenewmexican.com, santafecentury.com)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event scale:** "Hundreds of cyclists set out on the jagged terrain of Rowe Mesa... It's now the second-largest event in New Mexico by area, behind only the Albuquerque Balloon Fiesta." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Support infrastructure:** The race requires "about 100 volunteers, six aid stations and support from local businesses and government... Among the most essential is Chris Kemper, 72, a volunteer with the Amateur Radio Emergency Service. Wearing a bucket hat and large sunglasses, Kemper stood beside a DIY antenna linked to a ham radio in his car — a vital link to other aid stations in an area with no cell service." (santafenewmexican.com)

**Event weekend:** The Gravelón is part of a larger cycling weekend. Saturday features the Gravelón (54 miles) and Gravelito (25 miles) on gravel. Sunday includes the Century (106-mile tour), Gran Fondo (106-mile timed), Half-Century (54-mile tour), Medio Fondo (54-mile timed), and a 20-mile route, all on roads. (santafenewmexican.com)

**Saturday festivities (2025):** "Bike Swap & Expo: 12 PM – 7 PM; Beer Corral: 2 PM – 9 PM; Gravel Event Awards: 5 PM; Live Music: 7 PM – 10 PM featuring Brooklyn's Kaleta & Super Yamba Band... FREE ENTRY. Food Trucks & DJ all day!" (thesantafevip.com)

**Awards:** "Gravelon Prizes/awards will be given to the first 3 finishers in every age group for men and women. The top 5 overall will be excluded from age group awards... To be eligible to receive the prize money, the rider must be present at the podium ceremony." (santafecentury.com)

**Finisher rewards [ALL LEVELS]:** "Finishers in every category will receive a commemorative finisher's medal" (santafecentury.com)

**Pre-race logistics:** "Pick up number for Gravelito and Gravelon will be at the Railyard, Friday May 15th from 2pm to 7pm." (santafecentury.com)

**Parking:** "Parking will be available in a pull-out off of the forest road and on the shoulder of the road." (santafecentury.com)
