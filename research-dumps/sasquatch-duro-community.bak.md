# SASQUATCH DURO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Location Changes

**IMPORTANT CONTEXT:** Sasquatch Duro has undergone significant changes in location and format:

- **Pre-2024:** Event held in Oakridge/Westfir area (east of Eugene, OR) on logging roads with substantial elevation gain
  - Monster Squatch: 54 miles, 7,100+ ft elevation
  - Big Squatch: 47 miles, 6,700 ft elevation  
  - Little Squatch: 32 miles, 4,500 ft elevation

- **2024:** Event cancelled due to fire danger

- **2025-Present:** Event relocated to Carlton, Oregon (Yamhill County wine country, 45 minutes from Portland)
  - Long Squatch: 67 miles, ~5,000 ft elevation
  - Short Squatch: 40 miles, ~2,900 ft elevation
  - Route described as "Yamhill 8" figure-eight pattern through vineyards, forests, and farmland

**Sources note:** Most race reports and results are from the old Oakridge location. Current Carlton route is described as less technical with "smooth gravel to rugged paths" but significantly different terrain than the historic logging road format.

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Event recommendation [SOURCE: OFFICIAL]:** "Suggested Tire: 38mm to 45mm, cross bike, gravel bike or hardtail mountain bike. 62% Gravel and mellow Climbs and Fun Descents" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/7795/sasquatch-duro)

**E-bike allowances [SOURCE: OFFICIAL]:** Short loop allows Class 1 (pedal-assist only, 20 mph max) and Class 3 (pedal-assist only, 28 mph max) e-bikes. Maximum 50 e-bike participants. Class 2 (throttle-assisted) not permitted (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Carlton route specifics [SOURCE: OFFICIAL]:** "Mass Start with a 2.8 mile slow roll west out of town to reach the gravel" - neutral rollout heading west from Carlton's Upper City Park on West Main Street and NW Meadow Lake Road for nearly six miles before the figure-8 begins (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Surface description [SOURCE: OFFICIAL]:** "62% Gravel" with mix of "smooth gravel to rugged sections, rolling vineyards, forests, and farmland" (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Course format:** Long course riders complete two laps of the figure-8; short course completes one lap. Both finish with timing stopped 3 miles from Carlton (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Cut-off policy [SOURCE: OFFICIAL]:** "Hard Cut for Long Course lap 1pm or 3 hrs" - riders who don't complete first lap within 3 hours (by 1:00 PM with 10:00 AM start) are automatically re-scored to short course. Event states: "NOT YOUR DAY? Well it better be! We are in this together. If you dont make the cut off or want to downgrade at the lap point we will re score you!" (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Start format [SOURCE: OFFICIAL]:** 10:00 AM mass start with 2.8 mile neutral rollout. "Even when we have flaggers please look and listen for the best outcome" (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Elite pacing data [ELITE level]:** 
- Aris Sophocles: 3:04:14 for 67-mile Big Squatch = ~21.8 mph average (https://results.bikereg.com/race/20753)
- Nicholas North: 3:14:44 for 67-mile (Men 40-49) = ~20.6 mph average (https://results.bikereg.com/race/20753)
- Edward Dudlik: 1:56:00 for 40-mile Little Squatch = ~20.7 mph average (https://results.bikereg.com/race/20753)

## Historical Context & Event Evolution

**Venue history:** Event supported Oakridge Trails Alliance pre-2025, whose mission was to "preserve and protect the Alpine Trail system and trails throughout the Willamette National Forest." Old location known as "The Kingdom of Gravel" and "Mountain Bike Capital of the Northwest" (https://strambecco.com/event/sasquatch-duro/)

**2024 cancellation:** Event cancelled due to fire danger in Oakridge location (https://strambecco.com/event/sasquatch-duro/)

**January 2025 relocation announcement:** "Sasquatch Duro moves to new home in Carlton, Oregon" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/7795/sasquatch-duro)
