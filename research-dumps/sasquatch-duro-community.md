## Race Overview & Location Changes

The Sasquatch Duro has changed locations over its history. Originally held in Oakridge, Oregon, the event moved to **Carlton, Oregon in 2025** (45 minutes from Portland, in Oregon wine country). The Carlton location features "rolling vineyards, lush forests, and serene farmland" with routes showcasing the Willamette Valley (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro).

The 2023 race in Oakridge featured significantly different terrain, with routes through "forested timber lands that were once the lifeblood of surrounding communities" (https://bikeportland.org/2019/05/20/oakridge-and-westfir-host-sasquatch-duro-gravel-event-299959).

## Course Options & Elevation

**Carlton Location (2025-2026):**
- **Short Squatch (Little Squatch):** 40 miles, 2,897 ft elevation gain
- **Long Squatch (Big Squatch):** 67 miles, 4,976 ft elevation gain
- E-bikes allowed on short course only (Class 1 & 3 pedal assist)
- Mass start at 10:00 AM with 2.8 mile neutral rollout
(https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Oakridge Location (2019-2023):**
- **Little Squatch:** 30-43 miles depending on year
- **Big Squatch:** 43-60 miles depending on year
- **Monster Squatch:** Longest option (available in some years)
(https://obra.org/events/28413/results, https://bikeportland.org/2019/05/20/oakridge-and-westfir-host-sasquatch-duro-gravel-event-299959)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Oakridge Course (2019):**
The Big Squatch was "about equal parts pavement and dirt" with surfaces ranging from smooth gravel to technical descents. The course went off-road at mile 7, climbed to approximately 2,800 feet (starting elevation 1,200 ft), then descended on National Forest Road 1928 before joining the Aufderheide Scenic Byway along the Willamette River.

The major climb was NF 1912: "six grueling miles" gaining "over 2,600 feet of climbing to get to Windy Pass" followed by "an eight mile descent" back to the river. The course crossed First, Second, Third, Fourth, Fifth, Sixth, Seventh, Eighth, and Ninth creeks. The last two miles were "paved and flat on the Aufderheide Byway." (https://bikeportland.org/2019/05/20/oakridge-and-westfir-host-sasquatch-duro-gravel-event-299959)

**Carlton Course (2025+):**
Described as "smooth gravel to rugged paths" through "rolling vineyards, forests, and farmland" with quieter roads than the Oakridge location. (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Aid Station Timing (Carlton 2025+):**
- Mile 18 and Mile 30 (both courses)
- Mile 46 (long course only)
- **Hard cutoff for long course lap: 1 PM or 3 hours**
- Timing stops 3 miles from Carlton
- Downgrade option available at lap point with re-scoring
(https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

## Weather Experienced

**2019 Oakridge Race:** "The weather was moody in the days leading up to the event with showers and dark skies... The sky gods smiled on the Sasquatch Duro and it turned out to be sunny and dry all day." The descent conditions included "damp dirt" suggesting recent precipitation. (https://bikeportland.org/2019/05/20/oakridge-and-westfir-host-sasquatch-duro-gravel-event-299959)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Post-Race Experience (Carlton 2025+):**
- Live music 12-3 PM (11 AM-4 PM in some descriptions)
- Awards ceremonies at 2 PM (short course) and 2:45 PM (long course)
- $14 food voucher for local Carlton businesses or on-site quick-serve meal
- Custom event socks included
- Top 3 pint glasses for all age groups
- Post-race atmosphere in Ladd Park with local food trucks
(https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/PopUp/8473/gray-and-sophocles-fastest-at-sasquatch-duro)

**Carlton Town Appeal:** Known as "Oregon's wine capital" with "nearly two dozen tasting rooms, a charming historic downtown, boutique shops, and excellent farm-to-table dining." Downtown features spots like Carlton Wine Studio and Cana's Feast. (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Packet Pickup:**
- Friday: 3-6 PM at City Hall (191 E Main St, Carlton)
- Saturday: 7:30-9:45 AM at race site
(https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Oakridge Post-Race (2019):** "The after-party and free food were about four miles away in Oakridge" from the Westfir finish line. Participants received "a complimentary beverage and post-event fun including LIVE music, podium awards and a raffle." The finish area at Westfir Lodge featured patio seating where riders could stop before heading back to Oakridge. (https://bikeportland.org/2019/05/20/oakridge-and-westfir-host-sasquatch-duro-gravel-event-299959)

## Event Details & Logistics

**Part of Oregon Triple Crown Series** - riders can compete for overall series prizes including a set of Rolf Prima Wheels (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Accommodations:** McMinnville is the recommended base (10-15 minute drive to Carlton), with 200+ wineries nearby. Local vacation rentals available via VRBO and Hipcamp. RV camping available 16 minutes from Carlton. (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro)

**Age Groups:** 15-24, 25-39, 40-49, 50-59, 60-64, 65-69, 70-74, 75+, plus Non-Binary and Elite categories. E-bike categories include Men 50+ and Women 50+. (https://www.mudslingerevents.com/sasquatch-duro, https://obra.org/events/28898/results)
