# SCRATCH ANKLE GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Course Details

**Location & Setting:**
The race takes place in the Blackwater River State Forest (Florida) and extends into Conecuh National Forest (Alabama), starting and ending at Bear Lake Campground in Milton, Florida. According to the official site, riders "traverse through the majestic longleaf pine forests, cross pristine waterways like Coldwater Creek, Juniper Creek, and Blackwater River, and navigate diverse dirt roads that wind through the heart of the woods" (https://www.porc.org/scratch-ankle).

**Course Distances:**
- Small: ~20 miles (fun ride)
- Medium: ~60 miles (race)
- Large: ~100 miles (race)
- XL: ~200 miles (self-supported ride with live tracking)

**Ecological Significance:**
The bikepacking.com description notes that "combined with Conecuh National Forest to the north and Eglin Air Force Base to the south, the area forms the largest contiguous longleaf pine/wiregrass ecosystem in the world, a vital habitat for rare and endangered species" (https://bikepacking.com/event/scratch-ankle-gravel-2025/).

**Terrain Character:**
The Gran Fondo Guide lists difficulty as "Moderate terrain with rolling short steep hills or moderate, sustained climbs" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12212/scratch-ankle-gravel). The official description emphasizes "fast flats" alongside "rugged terrain" and mentions "gently rolling hills, winding watercourses, and expansive floodplains" with "sandy trails and creek crossings" (https://scratchanklegravel.com and https://bikepacking.com/event/scratch-ankle-gravel-2025/).

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Mission & Beneficiaries:**
The event is organized by Pensacola Off-Road Cyclists (PORC) volunteers. According to the official site, "all proceeds from this event support local trail maintenance and improvements" (https://scratchanklegravel.com). More specifically, the PORC site states proceeds benefit "trail maintenance, advocacy efforts, and fostering the development of young talents through the Gulf Coast Youth Mountain Bike Association" (https://www.porc.org/scratch-ankle).

**Post-Race Experience:**
The PORC site mentions "our esteemed host, Chef Brandon, will serve mouthwatering delights, ensuring an unforgettable gastronomic experience" (https://www.porc.org/scratch-ankle).

**Family-Friendly:**
The event description emphasizes it "invites families to immerse themselves in nature. Camping options allow for a weekend of exploration and shared experiences in these serene surroundings" at Bear Lake Campground (https://www.porc.org/scratch-ankle).

**Event Philosophy:**
The official tagline states: "GRAVEL IS WHERE WE CONNECT... Welcome to Scratch Ankle Gravel. Let the forests guide you, the roads challenge you, and the community fuel your spirit. Together, we ride not only for the thrill but to support and give back to the local trail community" (https://scratchanklegravel.com).

## Logistics & Registration

**Registration:**
- Limited to 200 participants according to Gran Fondo Guide (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12212/scratch-ankle-gravel)
- 2025 registration opened October 19, 2024 at 9am (https://scratchanklegravel.com/news)
- 2024 event sold out (https://www.porc.org/scratch-ankle)

**Date History:**
- 2024: March 16
- 2025: March 8
- 2026: March 21

**Camping:**
Available at Bear Lake Campground and Krul Lake. The PORC site provides links to "Reserve a site" (https://www.porc.org/scratch-ankle).

**Facilities:**
Gran Fondo Guide lists: Parking, Changing Rooms, Toilets, On-the-day Registration, Pre-Packet Pickups, First Aid, Community Volunteers, Bike Festival, Fundraising, Finishing Medals, Photos, Downloadable GPS files, Post Ride Shower, Feed/Aid Stations, Post Event Meal(s) Provided (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/12212/scratch-ankle-gravel).
