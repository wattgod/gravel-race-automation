## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Location:** The race starts at Bear Lake Road in Milton, Florida, in the Florida Panhandle. The course runs through **Blackwater River State Forest** and **Conecuh National Forest**, offering one of the Southeast's most distinctive gravel settings. The terrain features rolling short steep hills and moderate sustained climbs through pine flatwoods and longleaf pine forest. (scratchanklegravel.com)

**Elevation:** The course sits near sea level in the Florida Panhandle, with elevation changes coming from the rolling hill terrain characteristic of the region's sand hills. The moderate terrain profile makes the event accessible while still providing challenge through accumulated climbing over longer distances.

**Distance Options:** The event offers multiple distance options including routes of approximately 20, 60, 100, and 200 miles, with staggered start times throughout the morning. The XL distance starts at 7:30 AM, the Large at 9:00 AM, and the Medium at 10:00 AM, with an overall start at 7:00 AM. (granfondoguide.com)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:** Scratch Ankle Gravel promotes community connection and trail advocacy. The event supports local trail maintenance through its operations, connecting riders with the forest and conservation landscape of the Florida Panhandle. (scratchanklegravel.com)

**Reputation:** The race is described as "one of the Southeast's most distinctive gravel racing experiences," leveraging the unique terrain of the Blackwater River State Forest and Conecuh National Forest that most riders would not otherwise access. (elitewheels.com)

**Event Amenities:** Registration includes race timing, a post-ride meal, event t-shirt (if ordered by the cutoff date), awards for top finishers, finisher medals, aid stations, SAG support, and event photos. The comprehensive support package reflects a well-organized event despite its relatively small profile. (scratchanklegravel.com)

**Trail Advocacy:** The event positions itself as more than just a race, emphasizing its role in supporting trail maintenance and conservation in the national and state forest lands that host the course. This aligns with a broader trend in gravel racing where events contribute to the preservation of the roads and trails they use. (scratchanklegravel.com)
