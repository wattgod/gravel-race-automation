## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course characteristics described by elite winner [ELITE]:** The course was described as "rough, hot, rocky" with demanding climbs that allowed for early selection. The first climb was noted as decisive where Esmée Peperkamp "set a savage pace" that riders needed to match or get dropped. (https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/nicole-frain-wins-sea-otter-europe-gravel-race/)

**Official course profile:** 107km with 1828m of elevation gain in the Les Gavarres massif. The course starts with a 5.4km climb at 6.3% average gradient. The hardest section comes between km 41-48 with two consecutive climbs reaching the highest point at 409m elevation. A technical descent follows the major climb to Puig d'Arques at the halfway point. The second half has less elevation with one final climb peaking 10km before the finish. Final section runs along the Ter River before finishing at Girona-Fontajau Municipal Pavilion. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/girona/)

**Surface conditions:** Wide, flat tracks through rural areas mixed with technical descents and rolling hills. Fast, flat sections present in the final portions. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/girona/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite women's race dynamics [ELITE]:** The women's race was decided on the first climb where Nicole Frain and Esmée Peperkamp attacked together and rode clear. Frain then went solo at roughly the halfway point and held the gap to the finish. The winning time was 3:40:00 for 107km (29.2 km/h average). Second place finished nearly 6 minutes back at 3:45:54. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/09/22/sea-otter-europe-gravel-results-soete-frain-sweep-podium-for-ridley-racing/ and https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/nicole-frain-wins-sea-otter-europe-gravel-race/)

**Elite men's race dynamics [ELITE]:** The men's race stayed together longer with three riders (Daan Soete, Dorian Goddon, Stan Dewulf) contesting the finish. The race came down to a sprint finish with only 1 second separating first and second place. Winning time was 3:10:49 for 107km (33.7 km/h average). Notable that 2023 Gravel World Champion Matej Mohoric finished 23rd. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/09/22/sea-otter-europe-gravel-results-soete-frain-sweep-podium-for-ridley-racing/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

Sea Otter Europe is described as "the cycling festival in Europe" held in Girona at the end of September. The festival drew approximately 64,000 visitors over three days, with over 6,000 participants in sporting events and 402+ exhibiting brands. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/girona/ and https://bicyclingaustralia.com.au/news/nicole-frain-wins-sea-otter-europe-gravel-race/)

The event combines elite UCI Gravel World Series racing with a major industry bike show, demo circuits for testing bikes, and activities for industry professionals. Described as having an "enthusiastic crowd" at the finish. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/RaceDashboard/UCIGravelWorldSeries/1/8727/nicole-frain-and-daan-soete-take-victory-at-2025-uci-sea-otter-europe-gravel-girona)

The festival atmosphere provided a "lively backdrop" as riders, teams and fans converged in Girona, which is noted as "one of Europe's cycling hotbeds." In just seven editions, the event has become "a necessary and must-attend event for the cycling community" with "worldwide recognition among the cycling industry and cycling enthusiasts." (https://www.granfondoguide.com/RaceDashboard/UCIGravelWorldSeries/1/8727/nicole-frain-and-daan-soete-take-victory-at-2025-uci-sea-otter-europe-gravel-girona and https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/girona/)

**Location benefits:** Girona province and Costa Brava region offers "good weather, good gastronomy and a first-class natural environment" with a Mediterranean climate "ideal for practicing sports all year." The city offers routes from Pyrenees mountains to gentler coastal routes. (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/girona/)

**UCI Worlds qualification:** Over 800 riders participated in this final qualifying race before the 2025 UCI Gravel World Championships in Limburg, Netherlands. Those finishing in the top 25% of their UCI age/gender category automatically qualified for Worlds. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/09/22/sea-otter-europe-gravel-results-soete-frain-sweep-podium-for-ridley-racing/ and https://www.granfondoguide.com/RaceDashboard/UCIGravelWorldSeries/1/8727/nicole-frain-and-daan-soete-take-victory-at-2025-uci-sea-otter-europe-gravel-girona)
