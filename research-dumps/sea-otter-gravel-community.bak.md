# SEA OTTER CLASSIC GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Steph Hart [ELITE]:** "Goal number one was to keep the rubber side down, anything else was a bonus... After grabbing way too much brake, watching two super sprinkles riders crash, and contemplating why I took a day of PTO and paid $175 to do this race, I finally settled into some flat(ish) gravel where I could collect myself... Within the group, I clearly had the fitness advantage, but managed to get gapped at every sandtrap, loose corner, and single track section- that's gravel newbie life." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Steph Hart [ELITE]:** "I had assumed there would be neutral bottles in this race, so didn't bother to bring a hydration back, however this was not the case. So with only two bottles and a pretty warm day, this was a decision I had to pay for at the 50 mile mark where I had to stop and fill a bottle." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Steph Hart [ELITE]:** "At that point I was back in road race mode, where I was wondering what on earth I should be doing tactically to drop this lady and try to win. I shouldn't have worried, because after riding together for 5 or so miles my lack of hydration & 3900 KJ of work caught up with me, and what ensued was the worst adductor cramp of my life." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Simon Pellaud [COMPETITIVE]:** "The jet lag hit me pretty hard, and my sensations during training in the days leading up to the race were not good... The morning of the race, I was really nervous — fear of the unknown, I'd say. But as usual, all that stress disappeared with the first pedal stroke, turning into focus and concentration." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-sea-otter-classic)

**Simon Pellaud [COMPETITIVE]:** "After one well-paced lap, there were only 8 of us left at the front, but I quickly realized I wasn't having a great day. So I decided to take some risks and use a headwind section to anticipate the upcoming difficulties, which allowed me to pace my effort better. I rode 30 kilometers solo at the front, before being caught by the same 5–6 riders." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-sea-otter-classic)

**Simon Pellaud [COMPETITIVE]:** "On the technical side, the progress I made between the first and last lap was huge. My bike was amazing! Very happy with my setup for this race... Gravel is a world of its own in every way, and the technical level is no exception. Every race has its own unique characteristics, and riders have to adapt their equipment, tire choices, gearing, etc., to the terrain." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-sea-otter-classic)

**Keegan Swenson [ELITE]:** "It was a fast and really tactical day. Ultimately every lap, the last climb was really hard — I knew that was where the race was going to be won or lost. So, I was just trying to play my cards right and come into that with relatively good legs and try and sneak away there... There were a lot of attacks, so the key was for me to be really patient and save the legs." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/from-the-ground-at-the-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race/)

**Haley Batten [ELITE]:** "I had no race plans coming in to this. This is my first gravel race, so I wanted to see how the riders were riding and working together. I felt like they were hesitating a lot and not really in sync as a group, so I thought that maybe if I surprised everybody no one would be expecting it. I hoped when I went — I attacked on the single track on lap two — that maybe a rider or two would come with me to help keep it away, but I just pushed really hard and I knew if they hesitated at all I could probably hold it." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/from-the-ground-at-the-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race/)

**Haley Batten [ELITE]:** "I will say on the last lap I regretted my decision. I was deep in the pain cave for sure." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/from-the-ground-at-the-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race/)

**Haley Batten [ELITE]:** "They didn't seem to be in sync so I thought, if I surprise everyone, no one would be expecting it. I had hoped a rider or two would come with me so there would be more chance of making the move stick. I pushed really hard though, and knew if anyone hesitated for a second behind that I could get away, and I just kept going. During the last lap, I was regretting my move as I was deep in the pain cave, but it turned out well." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/sea-otter-classic-gravel-2025/ltgp-1-elite-women-and-men/results/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[ELITE] Bike Setup:** Steph Hart rode a gravel bike purchased in February 2025 after falling off 9 times on first ride. Successfully completed 90 miles with no crashes on race day. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**[COMPETITIVE] Equipment Adaptation:** Simon Pellaud noted: "Every race has its own unique characteristics, and riders have to adapt their equipment, tire choices, gearing, etc., to the terrain. I still have so much to learn — and I'm keeping my eyes and ears wide open!" (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-sea-otter-classic)

**[ELITE] Braking:** Multiple riders mentioned brake management as critical. Steph Hart: "After grabbing way too much brake, watching two super sprinkles riders crash... I finally settled into some flat(ish) gravel." Later: "That was enough motivation for me to loosen the deathgrip on my brakes during the long descent." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Overview:** 3 laps of a 30 mile course, 2,700 ft vertical per lap. Course is ~70% gravel. Total distance 90 miles with over 8,000 feet of elevation gain. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Technical Challenges:** Steph Hart [ELITE]: "not terribly technical, but lots of loose descents/corners, and a pretty rough climb leading into Lookout Ridge." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Lookout Ridge Section:** "Near the end of the lap, we came to a 0.25 mile stretch of pretty technical single track that averaged a 15.5% grade. I was worried about this, because I took a bit of a crash pre-riding this section of the course a few weeks prior." The climb is described as the decisive section where races are won or lost. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Start Section:** "At the gun, everyone went out very hard to climb out of the racetrack and onto half a mile of single track, and then a fast but loose 3 mile descent." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Laguna Seca Loop:** "The start/finish of each lap loops back through the Laguna Seca racetrack. This sounds uneventful, but included two 3-4 minute climbs peaking at 12%, and 2 descents through gravel pits that my roadie bike handling skills were wildly insufficient for." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Technical Skills Required:** Simon Pellaud [COMPETITIVE]: "The Sea Otter Classic Gravel Race, first round of the Life Time Grand Prix, proved to be an incredibly savage race with relentless twists, turns and steep puncy hills." (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yg5P_NMYIyY)

**Surface Conditions:** Course described as having "sandtraps, loose corners, and single track sections" that challenged even fit riders. "Long rutted and potholed Lookout climb near the end of the course." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race and https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/sea-otter-classic-gravel-2025/ltgp-1-elite-women-and-men/results/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Nutrition - 6 Hour Finish:** Steph Hart consumed 380g carbs over 6 hours using combination of Maurten, shot blocks, and Neversecond gels. Started with 2 bottles and 0g sodium. Had to stop at 50 mile mark to refill bottle. Total work output: 3900 KJ. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**[ELITE] Tactical Approach - Elite Men:** Keegan Swenson: "There were a lot of attacks, so the key was for me to be really patient and save the legs... I knew that [Lookout Ridge climb] was where the race was going to be won or lost. So, I was just trying to play my cards right and come into that with relatively good legs." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/from-the-ground-at-the-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race/)

**[COMPETITIVE] Aggressive Racing:** Simon Pellaud rode 30 kilometers solo at the front, got caught, recovered briefly, then attacked again with less than 10km to go before being caught on the final climb. Final result: 10th place (6th Life Time rider). "I'm proud of my race and the way I raced — aggressive cycling is my specialty. I didn't get rewarded for it, but I made a name for myself." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-sea-otter-classic)

**[ELITE] Women's Race Dynamics:** Haley Batten attacked on single track on lap two when she sensed group hesitation and lack of coordination. Rode solo for final two laps to win by over 5 minutes. Behind her, Sofia Gomez Villafañe and Cecily Decker broke away together with Decker doing "a huge amount of work at the front" before Villafañe attacked on the final climb for second place. (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/from-the-ground-at-the-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race/ and https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/sea-otter-classic-gravel-2025/ltgp-1-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**When Race Gets Hard:** Multiple sources identify Lookout Ridge climb (0.25 mile at 15.5% grade of technical single track) as the decisive section each lap. The climb occurs near the end of each 30-mile lap. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**[ELITE] Drafting Strategy:** Steph Hart: "The pace felt very manageable, but I sat in taking the free draft while I could. Within the group, I clearly had the fitness advantage, but managed to get gapped at every sandtrap, loose corner, and single track section- that's gravel newbie life." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Crashes:** Multiple crashes observed during race. Steph Hart watched "two super sprinkles riders crash" on the early descent. She also "took a bit of a crash pre-riding" the technical single track section of Lookout Ridge. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Cramping from Dehydration:** Steph Hart [ELITE] experienced "the worst adductor cramp of my life" at mile 75 due to insufficient hydration (only 2 bottles, no hydration pack, no neutral support). "I physically had to get off my bike and walk off the cramp while watching the race leader ride up the road. After an eternity (probably like 2 minutes) of frantically trying to regain control of my limbs, I was able to get back on my bike. I spent the next 10 miles limping back to the start, watching my average power plummet." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Hydration Planning Error:** No neutral bottle service available despite some riders' assumptions. Riders need to plan accordingly with sufficient bottles or hydration pack. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Technical Skills Gap:** Riders from road racing backgrounds struggled with loose descents, gravel pits, and technical sections even when having superior fitness. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Post-Race Physical Impact:** Keegan Swenson "threw up at the finish line thanks to his huge effort" and was described as "exhausted." (https://www.slowtwitch.com/gravel/from-the-ground-at-the-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Festival Scale:** "Sea Otter is much more than just a gravel race — it's a true cycling festival. All the brands are there on-site at Laguna Seca." Simon Pellaud noted: "After the race, I got to meet most of my sponsors (BMC, Schwalbe, SRAM, Assos among others) and finally 'put faces to the names' of the people who have supported me for years." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-diary-sea-otter-classic)

**Spectator Experience:** "These features were well suited for spectators heckling riders, which I found at least a little entertaining." The Laguna Seca racetrack section provides good spectator viewing. (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Growth and Participation:** Record participation in 2025 with over 700 riders, which is 35% up on 2024 entries. Combined women's start for 60 and 90 mile routes had 67 riders total. Elite men's field had more than 100 riders. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8466/batten-and-swenson-triumph-at-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Event Scale:** Described as "North America's largest cycling gathering" and "the largest bike festival in the world" hosting "nearly 10,000 athletes and over 70,000 fans" at Laguna Seca Raceway. (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/IndexFull/8466/batten-and-swenson-triumph-at-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race and https://www.gravelstoke.com/gravel-cycling-gear-blog/tag/sea+otter+classic)

**Race Day Organization:** Women's-only start for 60 and 90 mile routes. Elite women started at 10:50 AM, 20 minutes behind elite men's 10:30 AM start. Multiple distance options (30, 60, 90 miles) allow different ability levels. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/sea-otter-classic-gravel-2025/ltgp-1-elite-women-and-men/results/)

**Post-Race Amenities:** Registration includes complimentary post-ride showers, secure bag drop, secure bike valet, post-race lunch and complimentary Sierra Nevada beer at Casa Amigos Rider Lounge (must be redeemed same day). (https://www.seaotterclassic.com/gravel/ and https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/5370/life-time-sea-otter-classic-gravel)

**Morale Challenges:** Steph Hart [ELITE]: "At the start of lap 3 I heard the gap the lead was 90 seconds. In theory, I should have drilled it to catch the lead group, but at this point the 30 and 60 mile riders were finishing up and I basically saw 3 people on course over the next 15 miles- morale was low." (https://www.altovelo.org/race-reports/race-report-sea-otter-classic-gravel-race)

**Life Time Grand Prix Integration:** Sea Otter serves as opening round of prestigious Life Time Grand Prix series with $5,000 prize for winners stepping down to $1,000 for fifth place in Elite categories. Also serves as UNBOUND Gravel qualifier with at least 50 qualifying spots available. (https://www.seaotterclassic.com/gravel/)

**Junior Development:** Part of USA Cycling Junior Gravel National Series for ages 15-18, racing the 60-mile distance. "In partnership with USA Cycling, we are thrilled to have the Sea Otter Gravel Race return as part of the Junior Gravel National Series." (https://www.seaotterclassic.com/gravel/ and https://www.cyclingnews.com/news/sea-otter-gravel-and-unbound-gravel-100-decide-selection-for-life-time-grand-prix-u23-program-offer-junior-series-races/)
