## 3. Course Terrain & Technical Details

### Fort Ord National Monument
- **Size:** 14,000+ acres (managed by Bureau of Land Management)
- **History:** Former U.S. Army facility (1917-1994); served as leading training center during Vietnam War; up to 1.5 million American troops trained here
- **Designated:** April 20, 2012 by President Obama as National Monument
- **Trails:** 86+ miles of trails, 96 numbered trails
- **Wildlife:** Sanctuary for 44 species of rare plants and wildlife

### Course Characteristics
- Mixed terrain: flowy singletrack, wide-open fire roads, paved sections
- Sandy climbs and rocky pinch points
- Rolling roads with constant elevation changes
- Loose over hard on descents
- Jeep trail leading to Lookout climb
- Technical singletrack sections (some years)

### Notable Course Features
- Starts with nearly 4-mile descent on gravel road (typically headwind)
- Lookout Ridge climb (final major climb)
- Barloy Spur Rd: 0.25 mile averaging 15% grade
- Two 3-4 minute climbs peaking at 12%
- Approximately 70% gravel surface

### Course Evolution
- **2023:** 60 miles, 7,000 ft elevation
- **2024:** 50 miles (two 25-mile loops), 5,600 ft climbing
- **2025/2026:** 30+ mile laps, 3,500 ft per lap, three distance options

### Equipment Recommendations
- **Gravel bike:** 40-42mm high volume tires minimum
- **MTB:** 2.2" full knobbies acceptable
- **Suggested:** 45-50mm tires with minimal center tread
- Course can have ruts from rain running parallel on trail tread

## 4. Weather Patterns (April in Monterey)

### Temperature
- **Average High:** 62°F (17°C)
- **Average Low:** 49°F (10°C)
- **Daytime feel:** Moderately chilly and breezy
- Nights remain quite chilly (high 40s°F)

### Precipitation
- **Monthly average:** ~35mm (1.4 inches)
- **Rainy days:** ~8 days per month
- **Daily rain chance:** ~13%
- **When it rains:** Light rain, averaging 0.38 in (9.7 mm)

### Sunshine
- **Daily sunshine:** Average 8.77 hours (highest month of year)
- **Monthly total:** ~263 hours
- **Clear/sunny conditions:** 13.3 hours (55% of day)

### Cycling Conditions Summary
- Moderate temperatures ideal for racing
- Low rain probability but prepare for possibility
- Good sunshine hours
- Pack layers for cool mornings/evenings
- Potential for fog/marine layer

## 5. Forum Discussions & Community Insights

### TrainerRoad Forum Highlights

**Course Observations (2025):**
- "Gravel course is running fast and should provide some great racing"
- "Lots of loose over hard on descents, paved climbs, a jeep trail leading up to the Lookout climb"
- "Section of uphill, sandy, washout singletrack"

**Historical Context:**
- 2022 complaints: Course was too hard (basically ran XC course)
- Started hour before XC waves, led to gravel riders in over their heads on singletrack
- Course has been modified to be more accessible

**Life Time Grand Prix Integration:**
- 2025: First time LTGP kicked off with gravel race instead of MTB
- 2024: Used Fuego XL (MTB) as LTGP opener
- Gravel race replaced Fuego XL for 2025 series opener

### Rider Experience Reports

**First-Timer Experience (Alto Velo Racing Club):**
- "I bought a gravel bike on a whim in February, and after falling off my bike 9 times on the first ride, I decided to sign up for the 90 mile route"
- "At the gun, everyone went out very hard to climb out of the racetrack and onto half a mile of single track, then a fast but loose 3-mile descent"
- "Within the group, I clearly had the fitness advantage, but managed to get gapped at every sandtrap, loose corner, and single track section - that's gravel newbie life"

**Technical Challenges:**
- Sandy sections cause field separation
- Loose corners require skill
- Pre-riding recommended for technical sections
- Some sections historically required hike-a-bike

**Course Changes Over Years:**
- Early editions had technical singletrack (loved by some, challenging for beginners)
- Recent editions eliminated most technical elements
- 2024 reduction from 60 to 48 miles
- Two-lap format provides good option for beginners
