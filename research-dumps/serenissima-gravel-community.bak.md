## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Technical course characteristics:** The route features "very narrow roads and so many corners" according to 2021 winner Lutsenko. The 132km course includes only 25% paved surfaces, with 110km of off-road routes out of 132km total. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/alexey-lutsenko-wins-the-first-ever-pro-only-gravel-race-as-racers-welcome-new-format/ and https://journal.wilier.com/en/the-serenissima-gravel-the-race-that-is-all-the-rave)

**Route location:** The race runs from Jesolo to Piazzola sul Brenta, passing through Treviso (a city of 100,000 inhabitants) entirely on gravel roads. The course traverses "the dirt roads of Parco Naturale Regionale del Fiume Sile and the Greenway and Treviso-Ostiglia bike paths." (https://journal.wilier.com/en/the-serenissima-gravel-the-race-that-is-all-the-rave)

**Not like US gravel:** Based on the route's single-track and back-country lanes, "there were few wide-open stretches of stones like those found in races Stateside." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/alexey-lutsenko-wins-the-first-ever-pro-only-gravel-race-as-racers-welcome-new-format/)

**Finale setting:** The race finishes in "the spectacular setting of magnificent Cittadella" with spectators concentrated in the winding streets. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nBpJo3K8vQ8 and https://www.idlprocycling.com/cycling/alpecin-deceuninck-says-goodbye-to-team-legend-with-win-on-italian-gravel-del-grosso-joins-world-champion-on-the-podium)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[ELITE] Winning attack timing:** Lutsenko attacked with 80km to go and soloed to victory, maintaining approximately a one-minute gap to the chasing group. His winning time was 3.5 hours at "100 percent full gas" for the 132km distance. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/alexey-lutsenko-wins-the-first-ever-pro-only-gravel-race-as-racers-welcome-new-format/)

**[ELITE] No team support strategy:** "After sprinting away from his nineteen breakaway companions, [Lutsenko] soloed 80 kilometres over rocks and dust, pushing as hard as he could and repeating to himself: 'Alexey, go as fast as you can'." Without radio communication or team car information, riders had to make tactical decisions independently. (https://journal.wilier.com/en/the-serenissima-gravel-the-race-that-is-all-the-rave)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Pro-only format (inaugural edition):** The 2021 race was "the first-ever pro-only gravel event on the calendar" with a small but talent-stacked field including WorldTour teams (Astana-Premier Tech, Cofidis, Intermarché-Wanty-Gobert Materiaux) and several Italian ProTeam squads. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/alexey-lutsenko-wins-the-first-ever-pro-only-gravel-race-as-racers-welcome-new-format/)

**Unique race regulations:** The race features distinctive rules: "no radios, no team cars, only limited mechanical assistance" and riders must repair bikes themselves if they have mechanical problems. Per the official site: "niente radioline, assistenza tecnica ridotta al minimo, solo te, la tua bici e la strada davanti" (no radios, minimal technical assistance, just you, your bike and the road ahead). (https://journal.wilier.com/en/the-serenissima-gravel-the-race-that-is-all-the-rave and https://www.serenissimagravel.com/)

**Race philosophy:** Official site describes it as: "Polvere, sudore e coraggio" (Dust, sweat and courage). "La Serenissima Gravel è nata per chi ama uscire dalle regole ordinarie e buttarsi nell'avventura" (Serenissima Gravel was born for those who love to break out of ordinary rules and throw themselves into adventure). (https://www.serenissimagravel.com/)

**Part of larger event series:** The race is part of the "Ride the Wonderland" series organized by former Milano-Sanremo champion Filippo Pozzato and bike designer Jonny Moletta, which includes the Giro del Veneto road race, Granfondo VENEtoGO mass-participation event, and Veneto Classic pro road race. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/alexey-lutsenko-wins-the-first-ever-pro-only-gravel-race-as-racers-welcome-new-format/)

**Pro and amateur categories:** The race features both "PROFESSIONISTI ED APPASSIONATI INSIEME IN UNA GARA UNICA" (professionals and enthusiasts together in a unique race), with separate categories and hospitality packages available. (https://www.serenissimagravel.com/)

**Stadium atmosphere goal:** Organizer Pozzato aims to "create a stadium atmosphere" where "spectators to be able to see more stages of the race, a bit like the Tour of Flanders." Future plans include potentially starting from Piazza San Marco in Venice. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/alexey-lutsenko-wins-the-first-ever-pro-only-gravel-race-as-racers-welcome-new-format/)

**UCI calendar status:** The race was "inserted for the first time in the UCI calendar in 2021" and continues to be sanctioned. (https://www.serenissimagravel.com/)
