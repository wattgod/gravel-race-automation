# SOLDIER CUTOFF HILLDURO — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Gary Wenzl [RECREATIONAL]:** "This was my first race after recovering from a crash in June. Due to rain, they went with the 'very wet route,' which took out all MMRs and some very scenic roads. There was still plenty of nice views. Only 2720 feet of recorded elevation, but it was some of the most challenging (afterwards eating lunch, someone said the one climb was 17% at a certain point near the top). The race was fun and started out well, but with 30 miles left to go, I started getting leg cramps, and on the last steep climb, it was like they locked up on me, and I even had a hard time getting them to unclip. Luckily I didn't fall over and walked the bike up the rest of the climb. Last 5 miles was torture. It was cool and overcast (starting out at 47° and finishing at 57°); hydration and electrolytes was if anything a little over, so not sure why I was cramping so badly. Overall I came in 16 out of 19 (I think a lot of folks didn't show due to weather)." (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro)

**Shawna Young [ELITE]:** Per video description, she "has been chasing a podium on the Iowa Gravel Series for a little while. It alluded her at Soldier Cutoff Hillduro thanks to a wrong turn" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IRDmhDusWss)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The course features **significantly more climbing than typical Iowa races**. While the promotional materials estimate "over 4,000 ft" of elevation gain, Gary Wenzl's 2025 wet-route experience recorded only 2,720 feet but he noted it was "some of the most challenging" climbing, with **one climb reportedly hitting 17% gradient near the top** (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

The race winds through the **Loess Hills State Forest area**, around Preparation Canyon State Park, and through valleys of the Little Sioux River and Soldier River Cutoff. Multiple sources emphasize this features "hills like you've never seen in Iowa" and serves as "great preparation" for western climbing (https://www.racecenter.com/cycling/soldier-cutoff-hillduro, https://iowabicyclecoalition.org/event/soldier-cutoff-hillduro-iowa-gravel-series/).

**Weather-dependent route variations exist**: The 2025 edition used a "very wet route" that eliminated "all MMRs and some very scenic roads" due to rain (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

## Weather Experienced

**2025 race conditions [RECREATIONAL rider]:** Started at 47°F, finished at 57°F. Cool and overcast throughout. Rain before the race forced route changes to a "very wet route" (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

## DNF Risk Factors

**Late-race cramping** appears to be a significant risk factor. Gary Wenzl [RECREATIONAL] experienced severe cramping starting with 30 miles to go, culminating in legs that "locked up" on the final steep climb, forcing him to walk. Notably, this occurred despite what he described as adequate or even excessive hydration and electrolyte intake in cool conditions (47-57°F), suggesting the **steep climbing intensity may cause cramping independent of typical heat/hydration issues** (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

**Navigation errors** can cost podium positions, as evidenced by Shawna Young's [ELITE] wrong turn preventing a podium finish (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IRDmhDusWss).

**Weather-related attrition**: Gary noted "a lot of folks didn't show due to weather" in 2025, with only 19 starters in his category (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Cramping management [RECREATIONAL perspective]:** Gary Wenzl's experience suggests that **standard hydration/electrolyte protocols may not prevent cramping on this course**. Despite "a little over" adequate hydration and electrolytes in cool conditions, he still experienced severe cramping in the final 30 miles. The steep, repeated climbing appears to create cramping risk beyond typical flat gravel races (https://www.joinbasecamp.com/post/garys-story-soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

The race is **described as friendly to both new and experienced racers**, marketed as "a ride that you can invite your family or buddy to that's new to gravel grinding" with well-marked routes and friendly people (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10857/soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

**Venue facilities**: Pisgah City Park offers camping and showers, making it accessible for weekend race travel (https://www.racecenter.com/cycling/soldier-cutoff-hillduro, https://iowabicyclecoalition.org/event/soldier-cutoff-hillduro-iowa-gravel-series/).

**Post-race included**: Registration includes post-race meal ticket, event photography, and "some good memories and fun" (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Events/Index/10857/soldier-cutoff-hillduro).

The race is **part of the Iowa Gravel Series**, with riders like Jessie Combs competing for series points as part of team efforts (Prairie Bloom Racing Team) (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0UHgsDA94HA?hl=en-US).
