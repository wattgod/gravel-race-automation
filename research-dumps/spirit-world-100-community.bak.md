## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Don Scheese [COMPETITIVE]:** "After the first 5 miles on paved roads heading out of town south into the Coronado National Forest (a neutral start led by the local fire department), the gravel roads become, at times, dusty, rutted, rocky & washboardy." The route traverses three ecological zones at 4,000-5,000 feet elevation: "the cottonwood and sycamore lined, ruggedly beautiful Harshaw Canyon; the thickly canopied oak-juniper-pinyon pine forest of the higher elevations; and the Serengeti-like high desert grasslands of the wide-open San Rafael valley."

"It's a 12-mile and 1000-foot gradual climb to start the race" through Harshaw Canyon, where "the canyon alternates between rocky defiles and open meadows where the sun pours down like honey." After twelve miles, "we top out onto the high desert plain with incredible views of the Borderlands mountains. A sharp right takes us up, over, and down into Apache Canyon, where the oak-juniper-pinyon forest predominates." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/entering-the-spirit-world-an-arizona-gravel-event/)

**Karl [UNKNOWN]:** "The gravel terrain added to the challenge, with the ground shifting under his tires and keeping him on high alert. Karl had to navigate each bump, climb, and curve carefully to avoid slips and falls, especially on downhill sections where speed made balance even more critical." (https://www.thebetterbar.co/conquering-80-miles-karls-journey-through-the-spirit-world-100/)

**Water crossings:** Multiple YouTube videos reference water crossings on course, titled "COLD Water Crossing at The Spirit World 100" and "First Water Crossing at The Spirit World 100" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zgQYyP7nSLk and https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iCp7ovqTi10)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**From Official Description:** "We believe The Spirit World is more about the adventure than the race" and describes it as having a "Total adult summer camp vibe" with riders noting they "Traveled to the event solo but never felt alone." One testimonial: "The Spirit World is in a class by itself. It is a gathering of people who love bikes and people. Every single person at the event was absolutely, simply goodness!" Another: "Felt like a small community of friends who happen to ride 100 miles of gravel. The vibe was amazing!" (https://www.thespiritworld100.com/)

**Don Scheese [COMPETITIVE]:** "The cultural diversity of the riders is one of the feature attractions: bros, dudes, hipsters, MAMILs, fashionistas, skinny-legged cyclists & wide bodies, mingle with cowboys, cowgirls, ranchers—a veritable potpourri of people." Event is "deliberately kept small (250 riders)" with "all festivities and meetings are now held out of the funky complex of buildings known as the Patagonia Lumber Company, which serves local coffee, beer, and wine." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/entering-the-spirit-world-an-arizona-gravel-event/)

**Included amenities:** Live music and entertainment throughout weekend, group ride Friday, western-themed gravel family dinner by The Cyclist's Menu, post-race meal, handmade Spirit World ceramic mug, stocked aid stations, post-race Sunday group ride to "the Boomshakalaka," medical & safety support. (https://www.thespiritworld100.com/)

**Pre-race activities [UNKNOWN]:** "Happy hour meet and greet at the Wagon Wheel Saloon," shakedown spins, "'I Just Want To Ride' film screening at the Tin Shed Theatre." (https://theradavist.com/sometimes-you-meet-the-people-and-the-animals-racing-the-spirit-world-100)

**Velo Magazine perspective:** "Tequila feed zones? Mariachi bands? The Spirit World 100 and other grassroots gravel events succeed by putting the experience ahead of the competition." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/the-spirit-world-100-and-other-grassroots-gravel-events-offer-more-than-just-a-finish-line/)

**Event Philosophy:** "The Spirit World is a shared space for all to enter, where you can feel the magic of adventure and its impact on your mind, body, and soul. But you can only arrive there when you break through physically tough moments...This event is less about racing, and way more about camaraderie and sharing in each other's journeys throughout the weekend. Let the land, the animals, the winds, and the silence of this high desert be your guide, as we ride all day and dance the night away." (https://www.thespiritworld100.com/)

**Registration:** Event sells out quickly. "2026 REGISTRATION GOES LIVE AT 8AM MST ON SUNDAY, JANUARY 4, 2026" with entry limited to 250 riders. (https://www.thespiritworld100.com/)
