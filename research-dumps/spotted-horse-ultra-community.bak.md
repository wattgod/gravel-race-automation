# SPOTTED HORSE ULTRA — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Scott [COMPETITIVE]:** "I had not been riding much long distance since Gravel Worlds in late August as cyclocross season started the week after, so my training rides have been 30 minutes to an hour for the last month. I think long distance riding helps my cross endurance, but cyclocross does not help my long distance endurance." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Scott [COMPETITIVE]:** "It always takes me 35-45 miles for my legs to get into the groove and by mile 35, I knew it was going to be a struggle to keep up with my normal pack... I was 20 miles in and started to have hunger twangs, so an RX bar to the rescue followed by a salted nut roll 10 miles later." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Scott [COMPETITIVE]:** "Nine miles later, I saw the 'Big Picture'. Mr. Garmin was telling me to get into Highway 92... I had kinda questioned my self a few miles sooner if I was on the right route... the screw-up cost me 2.5 hours." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Elite Finisher [ELITE]:** "You WILL see the sunrise to your left shoulder, welcoming you to the day ahead... If you are able to welcome the sun and realize it will be your companion for the day, time loses it's meaning and then it's power. You are free to ride all day long, free of the 'when will this be over?', free from the 'god, how much farther?'." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

**Elite Finisher [ELITE]:** "My GPS showed close to 15,000 [feet of elevation gain] for the 200. I'm told it is closer to 12,000." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

**Elite Finisher [ELITE]:** "It is as stout a test and at the same time stunningly beautiful a ride as you will find ANYWHERE in these United States. It is every bit the equal of the Almonzo 162, The Dirty Kanza 200, The Heck of the North and I say this having ridden them all." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

**Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]:** "After that first hour went by and the sun started to come up, I began to roll by people here and there. The rain lightened. We were certainly damp and dirty and chilled, but rolling. I had surgical gloves on my hands inside Pogie Lites, and my hands were functional. Hands and feet are my kryptonite." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]:** "I knew I was there when I rolled up to an intersection. Before I even saw the stretch of mud ahead of me, I saw a muddy gentleman poking at his very, very muddy bike with a stick. 'Is that a fat bike?' I said. 'It was a fat bike,' he said." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Scott [COMPETITIVE]:** Fat bike setup: "I was signed up for the 200 miler on my fat bike. I've been racing the same (fat) bike for a few years now and have the bike and gear dialed in pretty well for all sorts of conditions." On Level B roads with a fat bike: "I was able to ride probably more than most since I was on a fat bike. After the first mile of Level B road, I saw the cross-road thinking 'whew - it's over', but then looked down at the GPS and noticed we were going straight again.. right into another Level B. Same for the next cross-road too. So 3 (or maybe 5) miles of Level Bs and I was able to ride maybe 3/4 of a mile." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]:** Cold/wet weather gear: "I had surgical gloves on my hands inside Pogie Lites, and my hands were functional." Shoe covers failed: "I had some yellow Gore shoe covers on over my Giros... I was getting ready to declare them DOA at checkpoint 2... I had a mini-funeral for my shoe covers (which were no longer even covering my toes, and had an inch of mud under them) and tied plastic grocery bags over my shoes instead." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]:** On bike choice for muddy conditions: "There is a fine line between 'bike that will give me the best shot at riding through the mud-pocalypse' and 'bike that will be very difficult to carry if can't ride through the mud-pocalypse.'" Rode a Salsa Cutthroat but chose to walk B-roads after seeing someone on a fat bike unable to ride them. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Navigation equipment required:** Multiple sources confirm GPS device with mapping capabilities is highly recommended/required as routes aren't marked. Course maps, cue sheets, and/or GPX files must be downloaded prior to race day. (https://strambecco.com/event/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra/, https://spottedhorsecycling.com/course/)

**Lighting requirements:** "All riders are required to have at least one clear front headlight and one red taillight for the duration of the event and must wear reflective clothing." (https://strambecco.com/event/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra/)

## Weather Experienced

**2018 Race:** "The 6:00 am race started in a light drizzle that got heavier up until the 1st checkpoint in Orient at 59 miles. It would rain off and on for the race duration. It rained in the Winterset area all last week and some heavier rains on Friday already forced a couple of re-routes before the race started." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**2018 Race - Adrienne Taren's experience:** "I found myself driving north from Oklahoma... it seemed that the second I hit the Iowa border, the rain-pocalypse broke loose. Also, the temperature dropped 20-some degrees... I set my alarm for a little before 4am, and was awoken around then anyway by the creep of water onto my sleeping pad... it was still raining." Race day continued with rain and cold throughout. (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**2018 Race conditions:** "By Checkpoint #1, the race had already claimed a few derailleurs and hypothermic conditions forced many others to drop from the race." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**2021 Race:** "For the first time in a few years, the weather did not disappoint! It was a great day to be outside and on a bike." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/14467/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**B-Roads (Level B roads) are a defining feature:** Scott [COMPETITIVE]: "I got to the Level B roads and was able to ride probably more than most since I was on a fat bike. After the first mile of Level B road, I saw the cross-road thinking 'whew - it's over', but then looked down at the GPS and noticed we were going straight again.. right into another Level B... So 3 (or maybe 5) miles of Level Bs and I was able to ride maybe 3/4 of a mile. Some stretched had ditches that was walkable/rideable, the next did not, so Mr. Sling came into play." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**2018 B-road conditions in wet weather:** Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]: "I rolled up to an intersection. Before I even saw the stretch of mud ahead of me, I saw a muddy gentleman poking at his very, very muddy bike with a stick. 'Is that a fat bike?' I said. 'It was a fat bike,' he said. And that is how I decided to not try and ride my Salsa Cutthroat down that road... Congratulations, you've made it through the last 2 or 3 or 17 miles of hiking ankle-deep mud." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Course reroutes happen:** "Friday already forced a couple of re-routes before the race started" due to heavy rains. "The Race Director had changed the course to route us around the vast majority of the B-roads in the original course (as well as one water crossing that had changed to rapids), but there were a few miles that she could not get around." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap, https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Course characteristics:** Elite finisher [ELITE]: "You will NOT find long extended sections of flat gravel roads that have you begging for an end to the monotony... There will NOT be a single course marking. Not even an occasional 'left turn here' where only a nondescript dirt road travels quietly off into the distance, hill after bloody hill hiding it's terminus... hundreds...yes hundreds of hills this race gifts to all who dare." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

**Course composition:** "Hilly, scenic, and somewhat remote, with many turns and few flat or straight sections. Expect dirt roads, bridges, a water crossing, and maybe some hike-a-bike sections." Course described as "95% unpaved" with gravel and dirt roads. (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/14467/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra, https://www.bikeiowa.com/Event/16255/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra)

**Wildlife encounters:** Scott [COMPETITIVE]: "I had 2 friendly farm dogs follow me at different times during the day. Both followed for 5-7 miles and they were having a ball... One even followed me though a town and across highway 34... Wildlife was plentiful too. I saw too many deer, rabbits, raccoon, possum and a few cats in this dark-thirty stretch." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Scott [COMPETITIVE] - 180 miles in ~18 hours total:** Navigation lesson: "I have my GPS set to a .5 mile window. This way I can see the turns and watch the curves in the road... I had my GPS set to 'recalculate route' option... This setting had to be on for years... In hindsight, I should have double-checked the cue cards against the GPS after the re-calculation in Orient." After wrong turn cost 2.5 hours, he turned off the recalculate feature. (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Scott [COMPETITIVE] - Final 30 miles pacing:** "30 miles doesn't sound like much, but it is still a 3 hour window in the dark at a 10mph avg with semi-moist clothes and 150 miles on your legs already... I started to playing mental games with myself to see if I could shave off some minutes each of the 3 hours by pushing my pace. The first 10 miles was exactly an hour. The 2nd 10 miles was 61 minutes and the last 10 miles was 59 minutes, so 3 hours for the win." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Checkpoint decision points:** "At Checkpoint #2, racers were given the option to drop to the 150 or advance to the 200." Scott [COMPETITIVE]: "I arrived at 8:50pm at a Caseys [at mile ~150]. Pizza or Chicken Noodle Soup didn't sound that good and the deli area was closed, so I pleaded with the lady to make a fresh double-turkey wrap (which she did - THANK YOU!). I had a set-down meal there." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Mental approach from elite finisher [ELITE]:** "If you are able to welcome the sun and realize it will be your companion for the day, time loses it's meaning and then it's power. You are free to ride all day long, free of the 'when will this be over?', free from the 'god, how much farther?'." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**2018 Race attrition was severe:** "Attrition was the name of the game on race day. By Checkpoint #1, the race had already claimed a few derailleurs and hypothermic conditions forced many others to drop from the race. The final results are not up yet, but only 3 people finished the 200 miler, and 30-40 finished the 150. Maybe 60-70 started?" (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Hypothermia risk:** "Hypothermic conditions forced many others to drop from the race" at 2018 event. Scott [COMPETITIVE] at Checkpoint #2: "I said goodbye to the volunteers and a few racers that were dropping out and I continued on with a little shivers that subsided when I started to pedal again." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Mechanical failures:** "By Checkpoint #1, the race had already claimed a few derailleurs" in 2018. (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Navigation errors:** Scott's wrong turn cost 2.5 hours and nearly led to quitting: "I had a few options running through my mind. The one I settled on was: 'just ride back to the Winterset Winery and DQ myself and have a good afternoon.' So I started riding North for 4-5 miles when my conscious kicked in and said 'Scott - you aren't a quitter'." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Time cutoffs exist:** Scott [COMPETITIVE]: "The reality was I would never make the 2am cut off if I attempted the full 200. I was already WAY behind and had already ridden an extra 30 miles and my legs were pretty fatigued." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race Director reputation:** Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]: "Because Sarah Cooper is responsible for Spotted Horse's existence and direction, and she's a badass." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Pre-race camping:** Adrienne Taren [UNKNOWN]: "After the appropriate amount of swearing at iPhone Maps, I found Sarah Cooper's house (appropriately off a gravel road), where she was graciously allowing racers to camp on the extensive front lawn. I bonded with the handful of other crazy people there over bicycles and whether or not we were going to die in a thunderstorm overnight... It was quite pleasant, and I would happily almost be flooded out of my tent with these people again." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-2018-spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-the-wet-edition-by-adrienne-taren/)

**Volunteer support:** Scott [COMPETITIVE]: "Late-morning, cresting a hill, there were a couple of volunteers roadside with Fireball and Reese's cups. Perfectly normal to have a friendly stranger stick a Reese's inside your mouth for you because your hands are too filthy, by the way... I left at 9:44pm and I started to playing mental games with myself... Newbs (Scott Newbury - one of the race volunteers and good friend) walked in and we chatted while I ate. After supper, I filled my water-bladder... Newbs also sent her [his wife] a text when I left Murray." (https://www.bikeiowa.com/Review/1709/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra-race-recap)

**Race philosophy:** Elite finisher [ELITE]: "Here is what you will NOT find at Sarah Cooper's Spotted Horse Ultra. You will not find long extended sections of flat gravel roads that have you begging for an end to the monotony. Nor will you find cheering sections lining the roads, broken up by an aid station every two miles... There will NOT be a drop bag waiting for you at the Casey's (Mile 70) or the local C- store (Mile 135). There is NOT a single course marking... What there IS. A true individual test, equal parts in and out." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

**Event philosophy:** "The Spotted Horse offers a space to clear the mind and get lost in the moment. It offers a true challenge, one of both body AND spirit... If you come to Iowa, to The Spotted Horse Ultra, hoping for a true test of put the bit firmly into your mouth, no frills, no excuses, no BS, it's me vs. the distance gravel grinding, you will come away completely satisfied." (https://expandyourpossible.blogspot.com/2016/11/200-miles-and-people-that-made-it.html)

**Self-supported nature:** "Spotted Horse Gravel Ultra is a self-supported adventure, and no crews are allowed on the course. There will be opportunities to resupply at convenience stores... The routes aren't marked, so a GPS device with mapping capabilities is highly recommended... Riders must be self-reliant, carrying necessary food, water, equipment, or money for convenience stores... caching supplies along the course are strictly prohibited." (https://strambecco.com/event/spotted-horse-gravel-ultra/)
