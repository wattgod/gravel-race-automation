Decreasing speed, increasing suffering the whole way and hoping that the body only turns to ash as the finish line comes into sight. Sometimes the ashes end up on the side of ride, maybe sleeping. ## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Gerasimos G. [COMPETITIVE]:** Ran tire pressure at "precisely 38.7 (probably a bit too high)" for the 2021 race. Notes this was likely too high, suggesting slightly lower pressure may be better for the gravel conditions. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Gerasimos G. [COMPETITIVE]:** Strongly recommends having navigation loaded before the race. Failed to load route into Strava on mobile and had no cue sheets, creating navigation challenges when the field spread out. "Cell service is patchy in the valleys which we would be going in and out of all day." (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

## Weather Experienced

**2021 (June 5):** Temps in the low 90s (94°F confirmed), winds gusting up to 25mph. Date was moved from traditional May timing to June, which contributed to extreme heat. Weather forecast caused roughly half of registered riders (350 registered, 150 showed up) to skip the event. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html and https://www.reddit.com/r/gravelcycling/comments/ntbm30/spring_valley_100_94_degrees_today/)

**2018 (May):** "Ideal" conditions allowed for a PR finish around 6:18 for 100 miles. [COMPETITIVE] (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Historical weather variance:** "The date of the event, normally the first Saturday after Mother's Day (in May) meant the weather had varied from the 30s to the 80s, cold rain to hot sun, mostly wind and sometimes calm." (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Specific climb:** "Oriole Road at mile 91" is described as "soul-breaking" — a notorious late-race climb that hits when riders are already depleted. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html and https://springvalleyeda.org/spring-valley-100-gravel-bike-race-recap/)

**Terrain pattern:** "Preston, like most towns in the area, is on a river. The South Branch of the Root River in this case. The course follows the river into the valley in the covered coolness of the forest. Then there's a bridge and you roll in toward town. It's a familiar pattern that repeats itself dozens of time on the course: downhill, valley, river, bridge, then uphill, open rolling plateau of farmland, downhill, etc." [COMPETITIVE] (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Gravel surface:** "Who doesn't love 45 mph downhills on two wheels over misshapen marbles?" — describes the loose, freshly graded gravel conditions that create fast but technical descents. (https://springvalleyeda.org/sv100-race-report-the-graders/)

**Route description:** "Rolling hills and challenging Bluff Country USA" in southeastern Minnesota, "where the prairies meet the bluff and where Superheroes come to be humbled." (https://www.thenxrth.com/events/spring-valley-100)

**Training recommendation for terrain:** ">10% grade" climbs are recommended for preparation, with multiple repeats necessary. (https://springvalleyeda.org/sv100-race-report-the-graders/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Gerasimos G. [COMPETITIVE]:** Lead group held "about 20mph average with some tailwind" in the opening miles. "Nothing extreme, and nobody really pushing too hard. Everyone was playing things a bit safe. There was no PR happening today, that was for sure" — referring to the extreme heat conditions forcing conservative pacing. Arrived at Preston (mile 40) in about 2:06 with tailwind assistance. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Gerasimos G. [COMPETITIVE]:** In 2021's extreme heat, started with lead group of about 15 riders, but field split early with "5-7 riders off the front" by mile 20 when hitting headwind sections. Fell back with two others (Ben and Phil) to survive the conditions. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Start time:** 8:00 a.m. rollout for both 60-mile and 100-mile races. The 2021 race moved start from traditional 9:00 a.m. to 8:00 a.m., likely to beat afternoon heat. (https://www.bikemn.org/events/spring-valley-100-2025/ and https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Hydration strategy [COMPETITIVE]:** At Preston aid station (mile 40), Gerasimos G. "required 5 bottles to get my three water bottles and my belly topped off" plus ate "a pickle wrapped in salami" in the 90+ degree conditions. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Extreme heat:** 2021's 94°F temps caused roughly 200 of 350 registered riders (57%) to DNS (did not start) before even reaching the start line. "Definitely a tough day to be on a bike for 6-10hrs." Weather can be a major DNF factor. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Navigation challenges:** In 2021, with reduced field size, tire tracks were minimal. "Getting lost in 'no man's land' in between groups of riders was now more than a lonely ride, unprotected from the wind, it was a near guarantee for getting lost and adding miles on a day that was already setting up to be dangerously hot." Cell service is "patchy in the valleys." [COMPETITIVE] (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Late-race climbing:** Oriole Road at mile 91 is described as "soul-breaking" — hitting when riders have already covered 91 miles and are depleted. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race format:** "Barrier-free" and "free to public, self-supported gravel road races" — no registration fee mentioned in some years, though 2026 shows $35 registration fee. Self-supported means no aid except what organizers provide at limited stops. (https://www.thenxrth.com/events/spring-valley-100 and https://springvalleyeda.org/spring-valley-gravel-bike-races/)

**Historical context:** "This year marks the 16th consecutive year barrier-free gravel racing events have started and finished in our host city of Spring Valley" (for 2025). The event has roots in the Almanzo 100, "one of the first gravel events in the midwest to be attracting 1,000+ people." Went through name changes over years (Almanzo 100 → Spring Valley 100 → Spring Valley Wilder 100 & 60). (https://www.bikemn.org/events/spring-valley-100-2025/ and https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Finish area:** "Both routes finish in Spring Valley Willow Park where you'll find an 18-hole Frisbee golf course, a perfect setting and activity for riders to relax after the race or for supporters to wait for their riders to cross the finish line." (https://www.thenxrth.com/events/spring-valley-100)

**Pre-race atmosphere:** "It was electrifying to be back in that grassy lot with everyone prepping their stuff and nervously bantering. There was a lot of CRo-designed kits from the Day Across Minnesota (DAMn) and the Filthy 50 amongst the group." [COMPETITIVE] (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**Volunteer support:** At Preston (mile 40), volunteers provided water support with "a truck and a bunch of water" plus food like pickles wrapped in salami. (https://gravelnewsnetwork.blogspot.com/2021/06/race-report-05jun2021-spring-valley.html)

**2021 sponsors:** VFW Post 4114, Lupulin Brewing, Sunshine Foods, TJs Liquor, Valley Butchery and Meat Market, Ody's Country Meats and Catering, Root River Auto, Bus and Diesel. (https://springvalleyeda.org/spring-valley-100-gravel-bike-race-recap/)

**Series affiliation:** 2026 race is "excited to be part of Gravel 'Sota, a Minnesota cycling series, and our event will be kicking off their season." Connection to Gravel National Championships: "With the 2025-2026 Gravel National Championships being held in the Fall of both years from nearby La Crescent, what a great way to test your training and get familiar with the unique conditions the courses have to offer." (https://springvalleyeda.org/spring-valley-gravel-bike-races/ and https://www.thenxrth.com/events/spring-valley-100)
