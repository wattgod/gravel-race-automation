# STANDARD DELUXE DIRT ROAD CENTURY — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**JOM [ELITE]:** "We quickly learned Alabama isn't flat, and the gravel is very real. Not pea-sized gravel, but more like chunky rocks in the trickier spots. Tyre punctures could be an issue for some come race day. There is also the dust… fine red dust." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**JOM [ELITE]:** "Between mile 25 and the first aid station stop of the day is a blur. I stuck my nose towards the front once or twice, but mostly hid in the dusty draft while Brian, John, Nic, Dr. Pain and others toiled away at the front, stretching the group out." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**JOM [ELITE]:** "The pace lifted once more and I was immediately in trouble. I was now clinging to the back and routinely performing the 'fat man fade' – where one moves towards the front of the group on a hill, only to drift slowly to the rear as the hill begins to bite. Ideally you slot onto the rear of the group as the hill is crested." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "This is weird I thought… weird but I like it! Must be an Auburn thing. Is this how they play against the Gators?" [referring to the group slowing to 10 mph on pavement for snacks and conversation mid-race] (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "At some point along here, a big guy on a single speed Fatbike (John Schwab) accompanied by our youngest and sometimes teammate, Brian Rogers, rolled up from behind after chasing for several miles. 'Where the heck you been?' I queried. 'I told you I was in tourist mode' he said grinning." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "Soon all the Green Eggs and Hambone-ing in the world couldn't save me as I watch Sam and Diesel slowly grind out of sight. Great! I am dropped and now lost. I pulled out the emergency cue sheet but most of the marked roads weren't actually marked on the actual road. WTF?!" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "Everybody's faces were smeared with so much red dust they looked like irradiated Native Americans." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Race organizer perspective [RECREATIONAL]:** "The roads of Chambers County aren't exactly gravel, but more red-clay Alabama dirt. Each route is at least 60% gravel/dirt. You should expect some chunk, some smooth, and some unmaintained county pavement. If it rains, it will be muddy (2019 IYKYK)." (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Recommendations:**
- **Race organizers [RECREATIONAL]:** "You can ride this race on whatever you want, but don't come crying to us if you show up on 700x25c and get a double flat in the first mile. We recommend at least 700x38c gravel tires, mountain bikes are cool too." (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

- **EmperorSnu [ELITE]:** "Like last year, it's recommended that you run a bike with at least 700x35c tires. Cyclocross (with wide tires) or mountain bikes would be preferred. It is also recommended that you bring spare tubes and CO2 with you in the event of a flat. The roads on the route are very rural, and are very chunky, so a standard road bike setup will not be able to complete the route." (https://www.bikeforums.net/19727562-post1.html)

**Bikes Used by Elite Riders:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** Rode a Litespeed T5G flat-mount disc (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **Brian Rogers [ELITE]:** Rode a Black Cat bicycle (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **John Schwab [ELITE]:** Rode a single speed Fatbike with 34 x 17 gearing and finished with a sub 8:40 Leadville 100 background (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**Tire Sealant:**
- **Dr. Pain [ELITE]:** Used Orange Seal Endurance Formula successfully - "His rear tyre was punctured by a nail, but the sealant had done its job" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Characteristics:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "Alabama gravel is the real deal. Not pea-sized gravel, but more like chunky rocks in the trickier spots." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **Race organizers [RECREATIONAL]:** "The roads of Chambers County aren't exactly gravel, but more red-clay Alabama dirt... some chunk, some smooth, and some unmaintained county pavement." (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

**Specific Course Features:**
- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "Up and down, left and right. Virtually no flat or straight sections. A few smooth sections but still lots of stones and punctures." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "One excessively hilly section" where single speed riders had to "attack over and over again" to work their momentum (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

- **JOM [ELITE]:** Course includes riding "through a cemetery" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **JOM [ELITE]:** "A horribly washboarded dirt and gravel road" appeared in the course (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **JOM [ELITE]:** "This bridge caused concern for some" - specific bridge noted on course (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**Climbing:**
- **Race organizers [RECREATIONAL]:** "The routes have rolling hills... beautiful country scenery" (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

**Dust Conditions:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "There is also the dust… fine red dust" during pre-ride (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "The gravel became a dusty red haze with fist sized rocks everywhere" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Pavement Sections:**

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Race Dynamics:**

**Early Race Pacing [ELITE]:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "At around 8:10am, the race kicked off. Once past the controlled neutral, it didn't take long before the pace lifted and riders began stretching the group out. The pace wasn't ballistic hard, but it was hard enough to put several riders into difficulty." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "After the pre-race rider meeting, the fifty something rider pack comprising of riders from both distances was gently paced out of town not by a car, but by two guys on bicycles who pulled off to the side once the first bridge was crossed... It was hard to tell. No body jumped or surged. No shoving or high speed jockeying for position at the front. More like a Gran Fondo, we sort of moseyed down the road." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Pace Fluctuations [ELITE]:**
- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "Thirty minutes later we hit a flat stretch of pavement and EVERYBODY suddenly threw it into neutral… down to 10 mph! Snacks were opened. Drinks were drunk. Conversation began... Soon enough the race pace cranked back up and it started to hurt again." This pattern repeated: "Along one excessively hilly section... we hit another flat stretch of pavement and it was Deja Vu again… candy and conversation… water and whinging (Oz slang for whining) then eventually back to race speed." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Aid Station Strategy [ELITE]:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "The much reduced group rolled through a cemetery – seriously – and into the first rest stop at around Mile 47. This is where a gravel cycling event is markedly different from your average road-going event. Everyone stopped, nobody kept rolling. Everyone refueled. We chatted for a few minutes before casually starting off again." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "Another hour of race pace and K-Dogg needed to pee. Once again the dozen our so lads obliged with no argument and gratefully lined up by the road side. Then once again the pace became uncomfortable until the second aid station (we blew past the first one) and yet another mandatory but grateful group stop for delicious PB & J and Gatorade. Such a civilized group!" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Group Dynamics [ELITE]:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "Approximately 20 miles into the race, the pace slowed. Formerly disorganized and chaotic, the group assembled into a two abreast paceline, casually taking in food and conversation." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "The pack whittled down to 15 due to fatigue and flats" by mid-race. Later: "The pack was whittled down to eight or nine grim and grimy racers." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Late Race Tactics [ELITE]:**
- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "With about two hours left in the race, Dr. Pain and the Belgian Diesel were slowly being reeled in by the mob while I was surfing the back waiting for the planets to align themselves. They did and I went… hard... Soon 31 year old, Cat 1 mountain biker Sam Gwin came around me even harder... The die was cast. Time for a team time trial – we all took pulls to the best of our ability which wasn't much compared to Sam." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

**Positioning Strategy [ELITE]:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "My clever teammates Lambert and K-Dogg were hanging around comfortably at the back" while others worked at the front (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Issues:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "Predictably, tyre punctures were a problem, ending the chances for several riders early" within the first 20 miles (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "People began to flat" as soon as the pace picked up and terrain became chunky (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

- **JOM [ELITE]:** "Nic Patterson had punctured a tyre rig[ht before the finish]" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**Navigation Issues:**
- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "My greatest fear was that I would get caught just before the finish and lose 3rd place. But despite a few wrong turns I managed to find my way home cramps and all." He noted: "I pulled out the emergency cue sheet but most of the marked roads weren't actually marked on the actual road." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

- **EmperorSnu [ELITE]:** Noted 2017 improvements included "A clearly marked course (last year's route wasn't marked well... or really at all)" (https://www.bikeforums.net/19727562-post1.html)

**Attrition:**
- **K-Dogg [ELITE]:** "The pack whittled down to 15 due to fatigue and flats" then later to "eight or nine grim and grimy racers" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-k-doggs-take-on-the-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century/)

- **JOM [ELITE]:** "The reinvigorated pace and rough gravel roads of Alabama continued to whittle the group down – about 12 riders remained in the front group" at Mile 47 (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Character:**
- **Race organizers [RECREATIONAL]:** "We designed this for fun, not for profit. We couldn't find a gravel race that fit our style so we made our own." (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

- **Race organizers [RECREATIONAL]:** "We still aim to keep the experience homegrown, lighthearted, and fun." (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

**Race Origins:**
- **Race organizers [RECREATIONAL]:** "The original Standard Deluxe route was developed as a Bikepacking Overnighter by the late John Little (RIP). A few members of Terra FIrma rode the route one winter and the idea for the race was born. That was 2016, and our first race was that fall with a whopping 75 riders in attendance." (https://www.dirtroadcentury.com/about-the-race)

**Organizer Background:**
- **JOM [ELITE]:** "The Standard Deluxe Dirt Road Century is the brainchild of Colt Fetters and Mark (sorry, didn't catch your surname), two lads who have lived in the area a while and ridden a ton of the local dirt and gravel roads. They are passionate about gravel cycling and racing at a grass roots level, and worked hard to assemble this event with the help of friends and volunteers from the local community." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/race-reports/race-report-standard-deluxe-dirt-road-century-waverly-alabama/)

**Venue Description:**
- **Official race website:** "The population of Waverly is not much over 100, and the whole town isn't even three square miles. It's about 15 minutes northwest of Auburn and about 15 minutes east of Lake Martin. If you open up Google Maps and input 'Waverly, AL,' there is a lone red dot, just one business on the main drag of Patrick Street – It's called Standard Deluxe."
