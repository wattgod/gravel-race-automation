# SBT GRVL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "The first hour was fucked... We had all agreed amongst ourselves that we wouldn't go too deep too early, having come straight from sea level. We weren't sure what the altitude would do to us deeper into the race. That said, in that first hour, you had to go crazy hard just to stay with the group. Our power was barely lower than it would be in a 75-minute crit." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "Around this point, we hit a section that the organizers had warned us about where a local landowner had laid down thick, loose gravel on a downhill. It was like riding through sand at a cyclocross race where you just sort of follow your wheel and hope for the best… except you're going like 40mph… and surrounded by other people… and the sand is rocks." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "I was absolutely wrecked at the finish this year. At Steamboat Gravel this year, my co-worker Adam and I were hoping to improve on our 2022 results (Adam - 8:23, Bruce - 8:58). If things went extremely well, we were even hoping to finish in under 8 hours. So how did we do? Here are our 2023 SBT GRVL finish times: Adam - 9:12:17, Bruce - 9:26:01. Needless to say, we missed our stretch goal, and we didn't even PR." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "Around mile 60, I saw a rider on the side of the road fiddling with a grey Trek Checkpoint. It was Adam. He had suffered a bad puncture in his rear tire and was trying to plug it... Adam ended up having to stop a total of 4 times to deal with his leaky rear tire. In the end, he ran out of spare tubes and had to borrow a tube from a generous racer who stopped to help." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "I hit the halfway point (71 miles) in pretty much exactly 4 hours. I was stoked about this but I was starting to struggle... Even worse, the heat was getting serious. The high for the day was 90° F, and by 10 AM, I felt like I was cooking in the sun. My power was dropping and I had to go into the red just to stay with the group I was riding with. Finally, at mile 85, I completely came apart." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Alison Powers [COMPETITIVE]:** "One thing I didn't post on the blog because I was embarrassed by it, is the fact that I lowered my saddle on Tuesday. With the encouragement of a friend, he suggested I lower it 3mm to help ease the stress on my back. Lowering ones saddle 5 days before a race is not ideal. I thought this will either be extreme self-sabotage or just the ticket I need to limit my back pain. Turns out, it's just what I needed. My back did great!" (https://www.pactimo.com/en-uk/blogs/alisons-blog/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Alison Powers [COMPETITIVE]:** "The Poo Stop - It happened. Despite all the planning, and fiber forgo-ing, I had to stop to poo... Around 3 hrs into the race, I was starting to get cramps from having to go to the bathroom so badly. But, I was in a group that was rolling along nicely that also had 2 women in it (my competition). I finally cracked and at the next porta-potty, I had to stop. It was like euphoria and after, I felt like a whole new rider and energy and good vibes." (https://www.pactimo.com/en-uk/blogs/alisons-blog/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Alison Powers [COMPETITIVE]:** "Basic Group Riding Skills - I remembered from my experiences at Leadville, that basic pack riding skills and how to ride in a rotating paceline are not common skills... So many times, I would be with a group and try to encourage 'working together', paceline, etc, and it just wouldn't work out. If we all worked together to get to the finish, not only would we finish faster, we would save energy while doing so. 90 miles into the race, I gave up any hope of riding nicely with someone and rode solo for the next 45 miles." (https://www.pactimo.com/en-uk/blogs/alisons-blog/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Alison Powers [COMPETITIVE]:** "Overall, I finished 5th. 4th Pro woman. One age group rider was very fast. I stayed strong both physically and mentally (my mental mantra when I was alone for so long was 'I am better, I am faster, I am stronger'). I stayed cool thanks to dumping water on my head and over my body. I never felt in the hole with hydration or nutrition. My time was 7:24 hours." (https://www.pactimo.com/en-uk/blogs/alisons-blog/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Ben Delaney [ELITE]:** "I won the 100-mile Blue course in a three-man sprint... 20.8mph average, 100 miles in 4:48:03. 272w Normalized Power, 3.33 watts/kg for the 4 hours and 48 minutes, Intensity Factor of .83 (I averaged 83% of my threshold power for 100 miles), TSS of 326." (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/winning-sbt-grvl-blue-power-analysis)

**Ben Delaney [ELITE]:** "The Steamboat Lake climb was where a critical selection was made. I did 356w normalized power for 11.5 minutes [4.4 w/kg] — and could not keep up with Nick Gould (Mazda Lauf) and another rider. Behind that pair, eight of us fell into a chase group over the top. My peak five-minute power of 375w was also on that climb [4.7 w/kg]." (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/winning-sbt-grvl-blue-power-analysis)

**Ben Delaney [ELITE]:** "On the final climb, I averaged 279 watts for 20 minutes from miles 85.5 to 90. There, the three of us were just trying not to cramp as we were out of water. It was a completely different effort from the all-out climb earlier in the day. Nonetheless, 279w after 3,167kJs feels pretty hard — much harder than that same effort when you are fresh!" (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/winning-sbt-grvl-blue-power-analysis)

**Micah Ling [UNKNOWN]:** "Steamboat Springs, Colorado smells amazing. Maybe not the hot sulphur springs themselves, but the hills surrounding the area are packed with sage and fields are dotted with fresh bales of sweet hay... Sunday morning at 6am, I still wasn't sure I could do this. I'm the kind of person who has to practice something over and over." (http://micah-ling-dofs.squarespace.com/blog/2019/8/27/race-report-sbt-grvl-black-course-140-miles)

**Micah Ling [UNKNOWN]:** "By mile-85, I was having the time of my life. I planned my nutrition right, drank plenty of water, and was ready to keep going. And maybe I was heat-delirious, or just happy that I was feeling so good, but the second-half seemed even better than the first. The loop around Oak Creek that started at mile 93, took riders up perfectly packed dirt roads." (http://micah-ling-dofs.squarespace.com/blog/2019/8/27/race-report-sbt-grvl-black-course-140-miles)

**Reggie Miller [COMPETITIVE]:** "At 56 years old, I must treat my body with so much care, unlike when I was 26. The dreaded interval day!! I suggested to Coach Jason that I wanted to do these. I wanted to wake up the engine, so to speak, and make sure all was working fine. We decided on only doing 3 sets of the 30/30 seconds instead of our normal 5, just because we were close to race/ride day on Sunday. I quickly recognized a decrease in power output, which was to be expected because of the altitude." (https://trainright.com/reggie-miller-sbt-gravel-100-mile-gravel-race/)

**Stephanie Holbrook [COMPETITIVE]:** "I followed a training plan that incorporated short intervals, long intervals, and lots of climbing at elevation. Being an Arizona resident, I wasn't entirely prepared for the race's altitude, which stood at 7,000 feet above sea level, despite having trained on weekends in Flagstaff at around the same elevation. My Garmin Edge 530 let me know I was acclimated to 3800 feet. Not near enough for the hills." (https://stephanieholbrook.com/blog/the-thrilling-steamboat-springs-gravel-race-my-experience-at-sbt-grvl)

**Peter Abraham [COMPETITIVE]:** "The course and the terrain: The route and environment are the foundations of a great outdoor sports experience... In Steamboat this means smooth 'champagne gravel,' which allows you to travel faster and see more with a less jarring ride. And the chances of flat tires and crashes go down significantly compared with some other events on extremely chunky gravel roads." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/sbt-grvl-the-best-bike-race-in-north-america/)

**Peter Abraham [COMPETITIVE]:** "This year there were, anecdotally, more riders of color present than at any bike race I've been to in my 40 years in the sport. It was astonishing; SBT looks and feels different from every other cycling event in the country. And it's not only riders of color... What I've learned by participating in and working at hundreds of endurance events is that a race is much more fun for everyone if it feels like 'the whole world' is there." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/sbt-grvl-the-best-bike-race-in-north-america/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Ben Delaney [ELITE]:** Used Zipp 202 wheels and Donnelly X'Plor MSO 700 x 36 tubeless tires on a Moots Routt RSL: "I probably rode 800-miles on my new setup before the race, but in the 140-miles around Steamboat is where I felt like I truly got to know my bike. There's nothing like a day-long ride up and down more than 9,000 feet of elevation, to make you super comfortable on dirt and gravel." (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/winning-sbt-grvl-blue-power-analysis)

**Stephanie Holbrook [COMPETITIVE]:** Rode Liv Devote with Praxis 2x crankset and 11x40 cassette, but had significant shifting issues: "Halfway up the first climb, I couldn't get my front chainring into the small gear. The cables are constantly being stretched or breaking. I was left with three gears until the second aid station... Planning for my next hilly gravel race, I'm saving up for a 1x, pairing a 10-44 cassette with a front chainring in the 38-42T range." (https://stephanieholbrook.com/blog/the-thrilling-steamboat-springs-gravel-race-my-experience-at-sbt-grvl)

**Stephanie Holbrook [COMPETITIVE]:** Used Giant Crosscut 1 700x38c front and Goodyear Connector Ultimate 700x40 rear: "The Silca Pro Tire Pressure Calculator has never let me down. I plugged in my numbers, and voila gave me my tire pressure. The app recommended 44.5 psi in the back and 42.5 in the front. The PSI worked perfectly." (https://stephanieholbrook.com/blog/the-thrilling-steamboat-springs-gravel-race-my-experience-at-sbt-grvl)

**Ben Delaney [ELITE]:** "I broke a spoke at mile 15 of this year's race. Although this happened on a smooth uphill and the mishap was unrelated to the route, my day was over in less than an hour." (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/winning-sbt-grvl-blue-power-analysis)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "I do want to mention that I saw more ejected bottles on this course than I have ever seen anywhere. To me, ejected bottles are the worst kind of race litter. If you don't have bottle cages that hold your bottles securely, it's relatively cheap and easy to fix!" (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Alison Powers [COMPETITIVE]:** "My equipment - also did great! Not one problem with my bike, kit, bibs, etc. One thing I didn't expect was how dirty and dusty my glasses got, so I had to clean them off a few times." (https://www.pactimo.com/en-uk/blogs/alisons-blog/sbt-grvl-recap)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "The first gravel section... lasts about 30 miles. After the first gravel sector, we came out onto the tarmac for a short while before cutting back through a hay field for an impromptu bit of CX. This made for sapping riding and I was worried I was going to rip my derailleur off." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "The first serious climb arrives at around mile 40; it's a 4-mile, 9%, gravel situation that goes from 6.5k to around 9.5k... Cresting the hill, I really did start to notice the altitude 'I'm breathing heavily but it'll pass… ok I'm still breathing heavily… ok STILL…' It was taking me about 10 minutes to finish every bite of Clif bars because I needed to breathe so hard." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "For the next few miles, Matt and I rode along a long section of bare plateau, which was beautiful and then… BAM out of nowhere— singletrack. The singletrack consisted of a fairly straightforward jeep trail. What made it tricky was that it was covered in a thick layer of fine, powdery dust... We ripped through this section and then into the woods where we crossed a creek." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "About 10 miles in, there's a steep and twisty descent after a small climb. The surface here is loose and bumpy and there was a minefield of ejected bottles in the middle of the road. I was going too fast and completely overcooked the first turn, so I went straight off into the grass, dropped my front wheel into a ditch, and I had to bail by jumping over my handlebars." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "The road leading up to the mile 95 aid station is one of the hardest parts of the course. The surface is super loose and slow, and you have to grind up an endless set of 3-7% kickers. My power pretty much evaporated here and I was moving super slow, getting passed by a ton of riders." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweigows-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Micah Ling [UNKNOWN]:** "The descent from mile 105 to 109 was probably my favorite section of the entire race... The loop around Oak Creek that started at mile 93, took riders up perfectly packed dirt roads. Views of ranches, the creek, impossibly green hillsides, and mountains in the distance, were just serene." (http://micah-ling-dofs.squarespace.com/blog/2019/8/27/race-report-sbt-grvl-black-course-140-miles)

**Peter Abraham [COMPETITIVE]:** "The terrain surrounding Steamboat does not fit into the popular perception of 'Rocky Mountains.' The mountains are rounded, the valleys wide and filled with cattle ranches, and the climbs not too severe. The terrain is far more rolling and generally enjoyable than you'd expect from a race in Colorado." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/sbt-grvl-the-best-bike-race-in-north-america/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Ben Delaney [ELITE] - 100-mile Blue Course winner:** "I had planned to start by eating solid food but it was immediately clear that I would not be removing my hands from the bars for a while so I had to settle for furiously guzzling gels whenever it was safe." On the first major climb at mile 40: "Matt and I made a pact that we wouldn't go too hard given that there was still 100 miles to go." (https://fascatcoaching.com/blogs/training-tips/winning-sbt-grvl-blue-power-analysis)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** Planned to "reduce aid station stops to a single stop at Aid 4 (mile 95)" and "pace as evenly as possible to average ~18 mph over the whole course." Result: "With Adam's fitness, it was probably doable for him. For me, it was more of a stretch." Spent "around 30 minutes total stopped last year, taking my time to eat, refill bottles, and relax." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Bruce Lin [ELITE]:** "I hit the halfway point (71 miles) in pretty much exactly 4 hours. I was stoked about this but I was starting to struggle. With several very tough climbs still to come, it was pretty clear at this point that I wasn't going to hit my 8-hour goal." (https://www.theproscloset.com/blogs/news/sometimes-ambition-outweighs-talent-my-2023-sbt-grvl-recap)

**Alison Powers [COMPETITIVE] - 7:24 finish:** "I had a race plan, hydration plan, and nutrition plan and I stuck to them. I raced my own race. Because my breakfast was compromised due to poor timing, I knew I had to eat early and often so I didn't get hungry." (https://www.pactimo.com/en-uk/blogs/alisons-blog/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "We had all agreed amongst ourselves that we wouldn't go too deep too early, having come straight from sea level. We weren't sure what the altitude would do to us deeper into the race. That said, in that first hour, you had to go crazy hard just to stay with the group." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Micah Ling [UNKNOWN]:** "Race organizers sent out an email letting black-course riders know that if they reached mile 85 and weren't having the time of their lives, they could make the turn and call it a day and join the folks riding 100-miles. So that was my goal: get to mile 85 and make the call." (http://micah-ling-dofs.squarespace.com/blog/2019/8/27/race-report-sbt-grvl-black-course-140-miles)

**Reggie Miller [COMPETITIVE] - Race week training:** "Coach Jason and I were lucky to have ridden the champagne gravel trails of Steamboat in early June for the Moots Ranch Rally ride, where we got a glimpse of 50-60% of the trail system... Today was a 53-mile ride. We did the infamous Cow Creek Trail, but backwards. Sometimes it's good to see the trail from another direction, to gauge better sight lines." (https://trainright.com/reggie-miller-sbt-gravel-100-mile-gravel-race/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Micah Ling [UNKNOWN]:** "I've done a few century-ride events and something I've always noticed is that feeling of solitude... SBT GRVL never felt that way. This race felt like just around each bend there was someone encouraging racers. I can't say enough about the aid stations. The sponsors were awesome, the volunteers were awesome, the food/drink selection was awesome." (http://micah-ling-dofs.squarespace.com/blog/2019/8/27/race-report-sbt-grvl-black-course-140-miles)

**Micah Ling [UNKNOWN]:** "We were met with cold soaked towels and race-specific trucker hats. After a much needed shower, I was thrilled to sit down to some street tacos and a cold drink that didn't involve electrolyte powder. As far as I'm concerned, SBT GRVL can rightfully claim their tagline: the greatest gravel roads on earth." (http://micah-ling-dofs.squarespace.com/blog/2019/8/27/race-report-sbt-grvl-black-course-140-miles)

**Reggie Miller [COMPETITIVE]:** "The highlight for me on that ride was meeting Marcus and Quincy from Ride For Racial Justice. I've been big fans of RFRJ. Our thought process aligns and messaging is pretty much the same. It was cool to tell them I appreciate them. Bikes bringing people together once again." (https://trainright.com/reggie-miller-sbt-gravel-100-mile-gravel-race/)

**Reggie Miller [COMPETITIVE]:** "Friday's ride was probably the highlight of my time in Steamboat. Castelli sponsored a Junior Ride of about 30-40 kids ranging in ages from 8 to 17. I got to lead out this group with SBTGRVL founder Amy Charity, and we quickly found out that these kids are not only fearless, but strong climbers." (https://trainright.com/reggie-miller-sbt-gravel-100-mile-gravel-race/)

**Peter Abraham [COMPETITIVE]:** "What makes this so fun is you see everyone from the event all the time throughout the weekend. The casual collisions with old and new friends make for a spectacular community experience that exists alongside of the bike race." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/sbt-grvl-the-best-bike-race-in-north-america/)

**Peter Abraham [COMPETITIVE]:** "This year there were, anecdotally, more riders of color present than at any bike race I've been to in my 40 years in the sport. It was astonishing; SBT looks and feels different from every other cycling event in the country... What I've learned by participating in and working at hundreds of endurance events is that a race is much more fun for everyone if it feels like 'the whole world' is there." (https://www.cyclingwest.com/racing/gravel-racing/sbt-grvl-the-best-bike-race-in-north-america/)

**Ben Brown [COMPETITIVE]:** "Every single time we heard a 'Fuck yeah TBD!' [from teammate Daghan shooting the race]. After the singletrack, everything had completely split. I had found a group of a few guys and I was sure that I wouldn't see Matt again but he popped up almost immediately once we were back on the tarmac." (https://www.tobedetermined.cc/journal/sbt-grvl-recap)

**Amy Charity (Founder):** "We're really excited about the new format. It's something unique, and we're eager to see how it plays out this year. It'll be interesting to see if other events start adopting this model and how it evolves... We're working on making the event as family-friendly as possible. We're partnering with local businesses in Steamboat and Hayden to have activities like a Kid's Zone, meetups in the on-course Spectator Zone, food trucks, music, and more." **Ryan Steers (Director of Marketing):** "Steamboat is a ski town, but it's also a ranching town. We have more than 600 miles of gravel roads, and these aren't just empty roads, they're active, working ranches... There's a rodeo every weekend in the summer. A lot of our participants finish the hill climb and then just walk 200 yards to the rodeo to watch an amazing Colorado tradition."
