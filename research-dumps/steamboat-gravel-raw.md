# SBT GRVL (Steamboat Gravel) - Comprehensive Research

## Overview

SBT GRVL presented by Wahoo is a world-class gravel cycling event held annually in Steamboat Springs, Colorado. Known for its iconic "champagne gravel" roads, the race has become one of the premier gravel cycling events in North America since its inaugural year in 2019.

**Official Website:** https://www.sbtgrvl.com/

**Social Media:** @sbtgrvl on Instagram

## Course Details

### 2026 Course Options (New Routes)

#### BLACK Course (Race)
- **Distance:** 108 miles (single-lap)
- **Elevation Gain:** 8,189 feet
- **Gravel Percentage:** 70%
- **Format:** Elite and amateur race with rolling enclosure and safety bubble

#### GREEN Course (Ride)

- **Elevation Gain:** Not specified (beginner-friendly)

## Cutoff Times

### Time Requirements
- **Minimum Average Speed:** 8 mph elapsed time
- Riders not maintaining 8 mph average will be redirected to a shorter course
- **Black Course:** Must average over 9 mph to finish before dark (sunset ~8:00 PM)
- Lights are NOT required; all participants must finish by dark

### Black Course Specific Cutoffs
- **Mile 20 cutoff:** 8:30 AM - riders who miss this will be redirected to Blue Course
- **Mile 37 cutoff:** Additional checkpoint where riders can be redirected (takes 17 miles off but continues on Black Course)

### Time Cutoff Enforcement
- Cutoffs strictly enforced for Blue and Black courses
- Red and Green courses have more relaxed timing

- Various snacks
- Hydration options

### Aid Stations by Course (Traditional)
- **Black Course:** 5 aid stations
- **Blue Course:** 4 aid stations
- **Red Course:** 3 aid stations
- **Green Course:** 2 aid stations

### Aid Station Spacing
- Approximately every 20-25 km (12-15 miles)

### 2026 New Feature
- Outside/team nutrition support available at Hayden Gun Range aid station and spectator zone

### Support Notes
- Some mechanical support available at aid stations
- Riders should be prepared to be self-sufficient
- Only neutral aid stations may assist riders
- NO direct outside support on course (friends, family, sponsors prohibited)

## Rules and Requirements

### Equipment Requirements
- **Helmet:** ANSI certified helmet required at ALL times while on bike
- **No aero bars:** Access to brakes required for dirt descents
- **No e-bikes on Black Course** (except approved paracyclists)
- **E-bikes allowed:** Blue, Red, and Green courses

### Prohibited Items/Actions
- Headphones or earbuds while riding
- Texting while riding
- Direct outside support on course
- Aero bars/extensions

### Race Categories
- Elite Men/Open
- Elite Women
- Amateur categories
- Junior categories
- Para-cycling categories

## Unique Features and Characteristics

### "Champagne Gravel"
- The signature feature of SBT GRVL
- Smooth, high-quality gravel roads unique to Routt County
- Referenced as "the greatest gravel roads on Earth"

### Ranch Country Heritage
- Routes pass through working ranches
- Riders encounter livestock management and heavy machinery
- Steamboat's ranching heritage predates the gravel-riding phenomenon
- Respectful relationship with local landowners emphasized

### High Altitude Challenge
- Starting elevation: ~6,700 feet
- Max elevation: ~8,500 feet
- Altitude acclimatization recommended
- Unique physiological challenge for riders from lower elevations

### Community Focus
- Founded by accomplished cyclist Amy Charity
- Strong emphasis on respect for surroundings and local community
- Equal prize purses for men and women since inaugural year
- Known for friendly atmosphere and world-class organization

### Course Variety
- Four distinct courses catering to all ability levels
- From beginner-friendly 25-mile Green to challenging 108+ mile Black
- Options for e-bikes on most courses
- Para-cycling resources and inclusive participation

### Economic Impact
- Estimated $4.5 million impact on local economy
- One of the largest gravel bike races in the world
- Draws riders from across the globe

### Notable Route Features
- Zipp Cow Creek section (infamous finish)
- Dunkley Pass (significant climb on longer courses)
- Stunning rural scenery through aspen, ranch land, and sage hills

## Key Takeaways for Participants

1. **Register early** - Lottery system fills quickly (October registration window)
2. **Acclimate to altitude** - Arrive early to adjust to 7,000+ feet elevation
3. **Prepare for self-sufficiency** - Limited mechanical support on course
4. **Know the cutoffs** - 8 mph minimum average, specific checkpoints on Black course
5. **No race day packet pickup** - Must pick up Friday or Saturday
6. **Bring appropriate nutrition** - Aid stations stock Honey Stinger products
7. **Check e-bike rules** - Not allowed on Black Course
8. **Book accommodation early** - Limited availability in Steamboat Springs during event
9. **Attend rider meeting** - Mandatory for racers, essential course information provided
10. **Respect ranch country** - Routes pass through working ranches
