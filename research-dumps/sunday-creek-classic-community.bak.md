# SUNDAY CREEK CLASSIC — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "The best gravel race you've never heard of, the Sunday Creek Classic serves up 121 kilometres of gravel of varying quality over 3,000 metres of brutal vert in the pristine, glorious Sunshine Coast hinterland." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "Eve and I settled in together with the second or third group of men, Eve bombing the descents while I hung back wondering what the limit of my Maxxis Ravagers' tread was, then nudging the dial on the climbs... At about an hour in we were sitting pretty, taking selfies under a cloudless, sunny sky surrounded by paddocks of little calves and ponies." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "Most of it [Mt Buggery] is rideable, but near the top comes a series of three or four steep, loose, rutted ramps, barely achievable even for the MTBs, and punctuated with rocky descents... I cleaned the first three ramps but walking the last." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "With a severe shortage of gels, having sucked down four in the first hour alone, I stuffed handfuls of jelly snakes up my jersey and ate them like spaghetti for the next 40 kilometres." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "Sunday Creek Road isn't a road so much as a collection of sharp rocks held together like extreme nougat, and which I hear was a challenge even for riders on dual suspension mountain bikes... From what I can tell, gravel racing means you set your bike up as well as you can, then completely abandon any caution, ignore the fact that you can't actually see anything you're hitting, and hope for the best." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "I got rescued instead. Also, I may have cried a bit... I got a lift back to race central feeling silly and ashamed, regretting what I'd tried to do, and utterly, totally spent after 6.5 hours of riding, an extra 400m vert of hike a bike, and a TSS of 425 on zero solid food." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Race Organizer Chris Dixon [UNKNOWN]:** "The goal of the event is two fold – firstly, I love Kenilworth and believe the area is an underrated adventure playground. As they grow, events like the Sunday Creek Classic can have a real positive impact on small town economies like Kenilworth. The second goal is to build an event that becomes a must-do classic on the Australian cycling calendar." (https://wildnco.tumblr.com/post/651955101774249984/welcome-to-the-inaugural-sunday-creek-classic)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "At 5.3km, and gaining nearly 500m elevation, Mount Buggery is the most notorious feature of this Big Ride... Things get out of hand pretty quickly, though, with each ramp increasing the gradient until we give up and dismount. I've ridden it on my mountain bike, but even my 12-speed Shimano GRX is no match for gradients that top out just a whisker under 30%." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]:** "The middle Jimna section can get a bit monotonous, and is best spent deep in conversation and deep in snacks, especially because the last few kilometres into 'town' get increasingly rutted and pockmarked. With few smooth lines, and with the route's one proper pit-stop just out of reach, it can feel inordinately hard to reach Jimna." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]** rode a Trek Checkpoint SL 5 gravel bike with Maxxis Ravager tires on her first gravel race. She picked up the bike on Wednesday, tested it briefly Thursday, and raced it Saturday. (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE]** using Shimano GRX 12-speed could not ride the steepest ramps of Mt Buggery (approaching 30% gradient), noting she had ridden it on her mountain bike previously. This suggests even modern 1x gravel gearing may be insufficient for the steepest sections. (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

**Official race guidance [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "It is suitable for gravel bikes, cycle cross bikes, mountain bikes or road bikes with clearance for gravel tires (35mm plus recommended). Best leave those carbon soled road shoes behind you will be pushing the bike at some stage!" (https://www.visitsunshinecoast.com/event/6792e93919a59dbd60d36d09/sunday-creek-classic-gravel-ride)

**Official race guidance [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Avoid this route after prolonged rain. Red dirt makes the going very hard, and a lot of rock balast has been added to help make the road more weatherproof. This can create a lot of fatigue even for experienced riders, and the rocky balast can cause pinch flats and tyre cuts for those ready to send it all the way back to Kenilworth." (https://bq.org.au/where-to-rides/sunday-creek-gravel-loop/)

**Mandatory gear requirements [ALL LEVELS]:** "At least two water bottles, Your Phone, have enough battery to navigate and make a call to emergency services, The Map, Enough food and supplies to get you through to the next stage, Highly recommend that you use the Strava route or add the GPX file to your garmin or GPS bike computer." (https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

## Weather Experienced

**Imogen Smith [ELITE] - 2023 race:** "At a chilly 7am, stripped down to jerseys and bibs and toed the start line... At about an hour in we were sitting pretty, taking selfies under a cloudless, sunny sky." The day started cold but warmed to sunny conditions. (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE] - Winter reconnaissance ride:** "It's so [expletive] cold... It's [expletive] freezing... It's four degrees [Celsius]" at the 4am start. By the time they reached gravel "blue sky – pierced by dizzying pinnacles of bunya pines – is already starting to appear, and the fog breaks up and swirls around eye-watering explosions of sunbeams that are the signature of pretty much every winter's day in Queensland." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Water crossings [ELITE experience]:** At about 60km in, riders encounter a deep causeway. Imogen Smith describes: "I came to a deep causeway. The guy at the front shook his head and dismounted 'I'm not riding into that'. Water flooded the road in every direction, so there was no option to get around it." Eve rode straight through, followed by Imogen, noting "I had just enough momentum to follow her wheel, realising that no matter what, I was going to get my feet wet." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Mt Buggery climb specifics [ELITE]:** Located at 40km mark. "The climb is quite simply an unlovable and difficult lump that doesn't finish when it should. Most of it is rideable, but near the top comes a series of three or four steep, loose, rutted ramps, barely achievable even for the MTBs, and punctuated with rocky descents." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Mt Buggery gradients:** "5.3km, and gaining nearly 500m elevation... starts out manageably, with ramps of about 10 or 12%, lasting 100 metres or so. Things get out of hand pretty quickly, though, with each ramp increasing the gradient... gradients that top out just a whisker under 30%." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

**Sunday Creek Road descent [ELITE]:** "The most painful, unrewarding descent of my life. Sunday Creek Road isn't a road so much as a collection of sharp rocks held together like extreme nougat... Throughout the day I'd been getting better and better at sending the gravel bike and opting for a 'come what may' attitude to downhills, and I decided to send the heck out of Sunday Creek Road, pushing waaaay into the 'out of control' no-go zone." Imogen notes it was challenging "even for riders on dual suspension mountain bikes." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Hike-a-bike finale:** The race features "the dismount-and-grovel-up-an-insanely-steep-monstrosity" near the end. When Imogen went back to complete this section, she found "four dismount of about 10 minutes, hauling my bike over impossible head-high moto ruts" where she expected only two short sections. (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Forest sections:** "Upon leaving Kenilworth you climb into the Imbil State Forest, beneath towering native pines... a plantation of native hoop pine, dark and moody, has been planted in the rainforest's stead. Mature and standing over 30 metres, the trees block out virtually all light." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

**Middle section character:** "The middle Jimna section can get a bit monotonous... the last few kilometres into 'town' get increasingly rutted and pockmarked. With few smooth lines, and with the route's one proper pit-stop just out of reach, it can feel inordinately hard to reach Jimna." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

**Navigation challenges:** Multiple sources note the importance of GPS. The course uses "lots of pink tape in the difficult sections usually placing three pieces along the direction of travel at intersections. Also lookout for small blue arrows and Cycle Safety signs." However, Imogen experienced a navigational error that had them "wandering around some bushland for a while." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/ and https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Imogen Smith [ELITE] - Nutrition disaster:** "With a severe shortage of gels, having sucked down four in the first hour alone, I stuffed handfuls of jelly snakes up my jersey and ate them like spaghetti for the next 40 kilometres... utterly, totally spent after 6.5 hours of riding, an extra 400m vert of hike a bike, and a TSS of 425 on zero solid food." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Note:** This represents a FAILED nutrition strategy from an elite rider - consuming 4 gels in the first hour was far too aggressive and left her depleted. This is a cautionary tale, not advice to follow.

**Elite pacing [ELITE winners]:** 2024 winners Finn McKenzie and Oliver Stenning finished in 4:06:38. Split times show: CP2 Mt Buggery at 1:36:53 (07:00 start = 08:36:53), CP3 Jimna at 2:34:50 total (11:34:50), Finish at 4:06:38 (11:06:38). (https://barefootsport.bbtiming.com/event/1254/results)

**Elite women's pacing [ELITE]:** Imogen Smith finished in 5:09:40 in 2024. (https://results.trackmelive.com.au/event/sundaycreek2024)

**Strategic rest point:** Jimna at approximately 74-75km is the only resupply point. Jimna Base Camp offers "coffee, snack food to buy, plus toilets, showers, camping and accommodation" but is "only open on the weekends." (https://bq.org.au/where-to-rides/sunday-creek-gravel-loop/)

**Hydration carrying [ALL LEVELS]:** "Carry at least 1.5L of water as you start this ride – if you have greater capacity, carry more. This is an advanced ride so if you plan to take all day to get to Jimna, carry sufficient supplies for the day." (https://bq.org.au/where-to-rides/sunday-creek-gravel-loop/)

**Official cut-off:** "5:00 cut-off is tight, if you're not going to make it, that's ok, you are welcome to keep going, just make sure you have lights for riding at night. We'll be here until everyone is over the line... 5:00 is when the safety vehicle is arriving back at HQ." Note: This appears to be referring to 5:00 PM (10 hours after 7am start). (https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Navigation errors [ELITE experience]:** Imogen Smith missed a crucial left-hand turn near the finish, rode an extra section, and when she went back to complete the correct route, got lost in increasingly narrow trails with "no tyre marks. No footprints. Quite a bit of lantana... I'd been going uphill for nearly 40 minutes and had one gel left, and I was… lost... Long story short, I didn't get to ride the end of the course. I got rescued instead." (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Mechanical issues observed [ELITE]:** "Climbing and climbing, passing people with flats and a variety of existential crises" on the final climbs. (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Flats on descents [ELITE]:** "The surface of Sunday Creek Road varies a lot depending on when the grader has been through – and while this time we're lucky, we still manage three flats when we get competitive on the descents." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)

**Remote sections:** "There are long stretches away from any support. This is also the beautiful part of the event." With only one resupply at Jimna (74km), there's a 46km final stretch with no support. (https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

**Bonking risk:** The combination of 3,000m climbing, remote terrain, and only one resupply point creates significant nutrition/bonking risk. Imogen's experience of being "utterly, totally spent" with TSS of 425 on inadequate food demonstrates this risk even for elite riders. (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Race philosophy:** "Like the great gravel races gone before, the Sunday Creek Classic is as much a personal feat of epic endurance as it is a race. And far out is it a race." The event is "Inspired by the great gravel events around the world, by European classics that feature wild weather and brutal courses and more recent events in America like Barry-Roubaix, Dirty Kanza and the Belgian Waffle Ride." (https://www.visitsunshinecoast.com/event/6792e93919a59dbd60d36d09/sunday-creek-classic-gravel-ride and https://wildnco.tumblr.com/post/651955101774249984/welcome-to-the-inaugural-sunday-creek-classic)

**Imogen Smith [ELITE] on the racing spirit:** "We're kind to strangers. We share suffering. We build each other up. That's what was cool about racing at Sunday Creek... The ladies got me up on the podium anyway for a photo. Character!" (https://marathonmtb.com/2023/06/02/sunday-creek-classic-goosebumps-and-gravel/)

**Small town atmosphere:** "Everyone in town is excited to have you here, buy a coffee and a donut, grab some cheese at the cheese factory." Post-race: "Thankfully the pub was open and ready with the finest cold beverages in the valley!" though "the last rider was in by 4:00pm just missing the Kenilworth Bakery closing time of 3:00pm." (https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

**Camping culture:** The Kenilworth showgrounds offer camping for $20 unpowered sites with "toilets, showers and you're right next to all the amenities of Kenilworth." Hot showers at race HQ require bringing "$1 coin." (https://bq.org.au/where-to-rides/sunday-creek-gravel-loop/ and https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

**Growth trajectory:** 2025 had 174 registered riders. The event has evolved into "Gravel Classics" branding, described as "bringing together Sunday Creek Classic and future events under one name. Defined by place, terrain and character, each event stands on its own while sharing a common approach to course design, delivery and experience." (https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

**Safety culture:** "The people around you are your first point of call – help each other out – we will credit any time spent helping people." (https://www.findglocal.com/AU/Unknown/212924220754831/Sunday-Creek-Classic-Gravel-120km)

**Mid-race oasis:** Jimna Base Camp is "known to moto riders, bikers and four-wheel drivers as the place to stop for a snack, a coffee, or the night in the middle of any off-road adventure." Owner Peter Cusack provides "excellent hot coffee, cookies, chocolate bars, coke, water and ice creams." (https://cyclist.com.au/big-ride-kenilworth/)
