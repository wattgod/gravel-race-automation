## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Course Description & Route**

The 120 km route is "almost entirely gravel" and follows this path: starting in Kenilworth → towards Kandanga and Bella Creek → west to Jimna → returning through Sunday Creek back to Kenilworth[4][5]

**Surface Breakdown**

The course features fire trails, gravel roads, and some single track[4][5]. The search results do not provide a percentage breakdown of each surface type.

**Terrain Character**

The route traverses diverse landscapes: dry eucalyptus forest, sub-tropical rainforest, and open cattle grazing country[4][5]

**Named Climbs & Key Features**

- **Mt Buggery:** Described as a "notorious climb" and one of the defining features of the course[4][5]
- The course includes 3,000 meters of climbing distributed throughout the 120 km route[4][5]

**Technical Difficulty**

Described as "one of the toughest one day gravel events on the Australian calendar"[4][5]. The difficulty is high enough that riders are advised: "Best leave those carbon soled road shoes behind – you will be pushing the bike at some stage!"[4][5]

**Bike Requirements**

Suitable for gravel bikes, cyclocross bikes, mountain bikes, or road bikes with clearance for gravel tires (35mm plus recommended)[4][5]
