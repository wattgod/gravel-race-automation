## Race Overview & Key Details

The Takelma Gravel Grinder is a 55-57 mile gravel race with approximately 6,000 feet of elevation gain, held annually in late June near Canyonville, Oregon. The race is part of the Oregon Triple Crown Series, produced by Mudslinger Events, and is limited to 200-250 riders.

**Unique Feature:** The race literally starts by riding through the doors of Seven Feathers Casino Resort (https://www.takelmagravelgrinder.com/, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7641/)

**Current Course Records (as of 2022):**

- Fastest man: Stefano Barberi, 2h 37m 51s [ELITE]
(https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7641/)

**2025 Winners:**

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Breakdown:**
- Approximately 55% gravel, 45% pavement
- First 13 miles on flat pavement along Tiller Trail Highway
- Main KOM segment: Mile 15-21 (6 miles, 2,200 vertical feet of climbing)
- First major gravel climb: 1.3 miles at 9% average grade
- Gradients reach up to 15%
- Passes through farm and ranch landscapes along South Umpqua River
- Crests to "spectacular valley view of Days Creek"
- Route passes Chief Miwaleta Park and along Galesville Reservoir
- Returns to Seven Feathers Casino Resort
- Timed portion ends 13 miles from the casino
(https://www.takelmagravelgrinder.com/, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7641/, https://strambecco.com/event/takelma-gravel-grinder/)

**Course Character:**
Described as having "sustained and unrelenting climbing" after the flat start, with the Bold Route defining the "Takelma Tough" attitude (https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7641/)

**Cultural Context:**
Takelma is the traditional language of the Cow Creek Band of Umpqua Tribe of Indians. The race name and associated Takelma Roasting coffee company represent "an extension of their cultural connection to people and place." Every roasting profile has a Takelma word associated with it as "a way to honor our history and culture, as well as spark a new conversation with every cup of coffee." (https://takelmaroasting.com/pages/our-story, https://www.granfondoguide.com/Contents/Index/7641/)
