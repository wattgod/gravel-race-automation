# TEXAS HELL WEEK — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & History

**Event Type:** Multi-day training camp with daily ride options (NOT a gravel race as initially categorized)

**Key Details:**
- Running since 1991, founded by Nick Gerlich (West Texas A&M professor)
- Originally held in San Marcos, moved to Fredericksburg in 1994
- Went on hiatus after 2017, revived by Fred and Rick Boethling (RAAM organizers)
- 7 days of riding with 3 daily route options: approximately 30-50 miles, 50-80 miles, and 60-100 miles
- Takes place mid-March to mid-April during wildflower season (bluebonnets and Indian paintbrush)
- Followed by Stampede 200 and Longhorn 500 (a RAAM qualifier)
- Includes optional evening seminars on coaching, nutrition, and crewing

**Location Details:**
- Based in Fredericksburg, Texas (Texas Hill Country)
- Seven counties south of Colorado River
- Nearly 1,000 miles of paved roads with little/no traffic
- Terrain: Edwards Plateau with multiple river valley crossings
- German heritage town with 100+ wineries, 100+ restaurants, extensive lodging options

(https://www.raamchallenge.com/about, https://avidcyclist.com/event/texas-hell-week/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Elevation Characteristics:**
From the Longhorn 500 course (which uses the same road network):
- **Camp Wood Loop:** Characterized by "steep climbs and river crossings" - four major climbs (two 800 ft, one 600 ft, one 500 ft) dropping into and climbing out of Medina, Sabinal, Frio, and Nueces River valleys. 54 ft/mile elevation gain.
- **Spicewood Loop:** "Numerous water crossings, all up on the Edwards Plateau" - includes "a short unpaved section of road." 53 ft/mile elevation gain.
- **Center Point Loop:** Mixed terrain - "smaller farms and ranches in the northern portion, up on the Edwards Plateau, and larger, greener, more productive farms and ranches in the southern portion." 47 ft/mile elevation gain.
- **Crabapple Loop:** "Smaller farms and ranches, with many cattle guards and water crossings. There are no gas stations/c-stores on this loop. This is the Hill Country at its best." 53 ft/mile elevation gain.

**Livestock Hazards:**
Video evidence confirms loose livestock on roads during the event.
(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x_R3KsVsojQ, https://www.raamchallenge.com/races)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:**
Original purpose was for cyclists from the Upper Midwest to "ride themselves into shape by logging as many miles as they could in a week" after a long winter. Informal, self-directed training camp rather than competitive race format.

**Attendance History:**
Grew to attract "over 300 riders annually from across the USA" by the time of its 2017 hiatus. Event was significant enough that participants "were devastated" when it ended.
(https://www.raamchallenge.com/about)

**Recumbent Community Participation [COMPETITIVE]:**
The rbent Forum discussion from 2008 shows active participation from competitive recumbent riders. **bikerteam** noted this was his "5th Hell Week" and that he'd "been to Fredericksburg 50 times," indicating strong repeat participation. Riders discussed rooming together, coordinating schedules around the event, and planning to ride different route options (A ride, B ride, or 200k options).
(https://rbentonline.org/YaBB.pl?num=1200283740/29)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Type Discussion [COMPETITIVE]:**
**FlyingLaZBoy (Paul):** Discussed potentially riding a Ti Schlitter factory bike instead of his "steel SXP" to get "something a bit lighter," though the loaner didn't materialize. He ended up "chugging up those Fredericksburg mountains on the SXP, after all." This suggests the climbing is significant enough that bike weight matters to competitive riders.

Multiple forum participants mentioned upgrading to carbon fiber recumbents before the event, with **bikerteam** noting: "First Bustin' Bud, then Gregarious Greg, and now Pistol (fast) Paul. Three make a conspiracy ya know... Two carbon bents thus far..."
(https://rbentonline.org/YaBB.pl?num=1200283740/29)

**Required Equipment (for associated Longhorn 500/Stampede 200):**
All riders must have "functional front and rear lights attached to the bike (+ spare batteries)" and "reflective clothing and/or reflective tape on your bicycle" recommended.
(https://www.raamchallenge.com/races)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Multi-Day Strategy [COMPETITIVE]:**
**bikerteam:** Planned to "ride the A or B ride every day, with the exception of Monday, where I'll ride the 200k" - indicating riders mix distance options throughout the week based on fitness and goals.

**aikigreg:** Expressed concern about "lack of current conditioning and base miles" making a 200k inadvisable, preferring shorter Sunday/Monday options. This suggests riders self-select distances based on current fitness rather than racing all-out daily.
(https://rbentonline.org/YaBB.pl?num=1200283740/29)
