# THE CRUSHER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Greg Gentle [RECREATIONAL]:** "I've never ridden that far before. Horrible idea or not, I'm committed. The Crusher Gravel Race will send about 150 brave souls across the wilderness of the Keweenaw Peninsula in route to Marquette. The Crusher brags 225 miles of 'enhanced gravel' promising to deliver one of the hardest days on the bike you'll ever experience." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Greg Gentle [RECREATIONAL]:** "My goal was to ride straight through aiming for 20 hours. To do that I need to ride about an average of 13 mph knowing there will be some stops along the way." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Greg Gentle [RECREATIONAL]:** "After about 10 miles we hit some gnarly downhill sections. I know to get myself to the front of these situations and was able to bomb it with another rider leaving the group behind. There were water bottles and other 'I'm going to need that later' items littered yard sale fashion all over the trail for the next few miles ejected from bikes and bags from other riders ahead of us. It was steep, rocky, and fast. Pick the wrong line and you are in trouble." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Greg Gentle [RECREATIONAL]:** "At this point, I was feeling much stronger than Chicago Steve, so I kept the pressure on all the way to the second and final aid station in L'Anse at 160 miles at 6:00 with about 14 hours of riding complete... I asked Lisa how many riders had come through. She wasn't sure, but one of the guys there was checking in riders. He heard my question and did a quick count and said, 'About 14'. Holy shit!!" (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Greg Gentle [RECREATIONAL]:** "I don't care if that's your race game, or not. If you're in a group for 5+ hours and you don't take a turn you're dead to me. Okay…that's a little harsh. Let's just say I don't have a lot of respect for you as a rider. Do some work. Pull your weight." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "Almost immediately we found ourselves on single track and soon after pushing our bikes up Hogback Mountain. The trip up wasn't terrible, but it certainly wasn't easy. Basically it boiled down to a rock scramble, carrying/pushing 30-pound bikes up the mountain. Honestly going up was easier than coming down, but the view was well worth it." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "Coming down was where we hit our first snag, about 2.5 miles in. Unfortunately, as we neared the bottom Jeff slipped on a root and as his bike landed on its side, he caught himself on the bike, pressing a large rock into the fork with a loud cracking sound." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "Soon we find ourselves on an overgrown railway, seriously overgrown and seriously a railway. The rails may have been removed, but the timbers and many nails were still there, buried beneath the brush. With fresh dew on the grasses it was like riding through a car wash, we made it to the end of the trail, drenched head to toe." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "Many of the unpaved roads were recently graded so there were a lot of sections that the only thing we could do was follow the tire tracks to have a semi-smooth ride. Over time we were getting beat up and struggled to maintain any good power." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "By this point though we had learned to be cautiously optimistic about any pre-conceived notions as we rolled along. Not knowing the terrain, many times we felt great on a stretch of road that would quickly disappear into a single track, barely." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "We had expected temps to be in the mid-60s through the night and we were starting to dip into the upper 50's. I could not warm up for anything. We stopped to put layers on, but neither of us had anything for our legs." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Chris Spurrier [UNKNOWN]:** "We were cold, tired, and had no idea what was ahead. We decided to take a short detour to get a cell signal and call Ken so we could get a little sleep and re-asses... While we were confident we could have forged on and finished, we did not know what the cost would have been. The risk was significantly increasing for injury (Jeffs fork was cracked after all), so we decided to call it." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Required Gear [ALL LEVELS]:** According to the race passport, all riders must carry required gear including "a snorkel" among other items. Gear checks are conducted at registration. (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Bike Durability Concern [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Chris Spurrier's teammate Jeff cracked his fork 2.5 miles in after slipping on a root coming down Hogback Mountain. They taped it up and continued riding. (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**GPS Requirement [ALL LEVELS]:** "Signage: There is none. GPS is required." (https://906adventureteam.com/mountain-bike-events/the-crusher/)

**Satellite Communication [P2P RIDERS]:** "Satellite communication devices are recommended for this event. I.E. Garmin Inreach, etc." for the Point-to-Point format. (https://906adventureteam.com/mountain-bike-events/the-crusher/)

**Trackleaders Beacons [EX FORMAT]:** "Timing: Trackleaders. Beacons are required for this event." (https://906adventureteam.com/mountain-bike-events/the-crusher/)

In hindsight, I wish I had about 12 riders on that section to paceline. I averaged about 18 mph, but a group would've done it 20+." **L'Anse Climb [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "This diminished quickly after we took off to climb out of L'Anse. This climb was long, and we knew it, but it would have been the last real climb to deal with." Located after the mile 144 aid station. (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Peshekee River Section [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Night riding section heading south along the Peshekee River. "We rolled at 5mph looking left with our headlamps, and then ahead to make sure we weren't veering off, it felt like an eternity. We both thought we would see things in the woods like buildings and animals. The lights reflecting had us seeing things and our minds were starting to drift." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Terrain Variety [ALL LEVELS]:** Official description: "Forest roads, B-roads, Jeep trails, Roads that used to be roads. Hard packed, sandy, chunky, mud. Wild, remote, wilderness. Some is fast, a lot is not, all of it taking you deep into Upper Michigan forests, away from the comforts of nice gravel." ## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Pacing Goal - 20 Hour Finish [RECREATIONAL]:** Greg Gentle: "My goal was to ride straight through aiming for 20 hours. To do that I need to ride about an average of 13 mph knowing there will be some stops along the way." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Aid Station Strategy [RECREATIONAL]:** "The Marji has prepared me for a few things. First, don't lag around the checkpoints and aid stations. Keep moving." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Group Riding Dynamics [RECREATIONAL]:** Greg Gentle rode with a group of 15-20 that he and "Chicago Steve" pulled for 45-50 miles into Houghton. The group left without them during their aid station stop, but they caught them again later. (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Checkpoint Photography Strategy [RECREATIONAL]:** At "The Cliffs" checkpoint, the group of 15 stopped together for required selfies. Later at Freda ruins: "Chicago Steve and I quickly grabbed our selfies of the Freda smokestack and began the chase to rejoin the group." They minimized time at photo stops. (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Mileage Miscalculation Impact [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "We did however feel confident enough to make it by dark, after all we were at mile 111 about 5 and only needed to get to 134. Or so we thought... As we neared mile 133 we felt it was right around the corner. Mile 134, mile 135, mile 136, surely we can't be off that much? Finally, at 144 we rolled into L'Anse." A 10-mile navigation error had significant psychological impact. (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Night Riding Preparation Gap [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "We had no idea what to expect for the last 70 miles. It could have been a flat road or Armageddon. At best we anticipated another 7 hrs of riding." The unknown terrain in darkness was a major decision factor in their DNF. (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical Failures:** Fork damage from technical descents - Jeff cracked his fork at mile 2.5 on Hogback Mountain descent. (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Cold/Inadequate Clothing:** "We had expected temps to be in the mid-60s through the night and we were starting to dip into the upper 50's. I could not warm up for anything. We stopped to put layers on, but neither of us had anything for our legs. We were both still shaking from 47-degree weather." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Fatigue and Sleep Deprivation:** "Jeff had actually started to doze off a couple of times, he would catch himself and veer back on the road... I was starting to fall asleep." Both riders were hallucinating in the woods at night. (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Unknown Terrain Risk:** "We had no idea what to expect for the last 70 miles... At best we anticipated another 7 hrs of riding. We were both still shaking from 47-degree weather, exhausted, and beyond our limits. While we were confident we could have forged on and finished, we did not know what the cost would have been. The risk was significantly increasing for injury." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Checkpoint Navigation Challenges:** Finding the outhouse checkpoint: "We stopped at a parking lot and looked for the outhouse but didn't see it... Now we had lost an hour just looking for the waypoint. I put the lat/long into the Garmin again and knew it had to be back where we pulled off, so we backtracked to the lot. Looking back I couldn't see Jeff's light anymore, it was pitch black out... there, behind the sign, a brown outhouse in the middle of the woods." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

**Preparation Gaps:** "We still did not go in as prepared as either one of us would have liked. Experiencing such a diverse terrain over 24 hrs took its toll on us." (https://kylecoaching.com/race-report-the-crusher-race-report-chris-spurrier/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Self-Supported Ethos:** "You are on your own. No one is out there to save you. GPS is required. You must observe and obey the 'rules of the road.' We do not sign the course. You should not approach wildlife. In an emergency, dial 911." (https://906adventureteam.com/mountain-bike-events/the-crusher/)

**Event Philosophy:** "Crusher is about the journey, adventure, personal challenge, and teamwork." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-crusher-enhanced-gravel-michigan-upper-peninsula-usa/)

**Pre-Race Experience:** "At this point, we are a full 30 minutes off the scheduled 5:30 start. We received some final tips from Todd and hit the pre-dawn roll out." Greg Gentle also "found the opportunity to say hello to mountain bike legend Tinker Juarez. Tinker has been to a few events up here. He's a true gentleman and continues to crush the competition." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**Aid Station Atmosphere:** At Houghton mile 70: "Houghton was an oasis of joy. There was a huge spread of food and drink to load up on, and more importantly, a bathroom to finish some #unfinishedbusiness. The crew of volunteers was super friendly and supportive." At L'Anse mile 160: "My friend Lisa Thompson was running the show here and did an awesome job making sure all of the riders were topped off." (https://fat-bike.com/2019/08/the-crusher-race-report-by-greg-gentle/)

**No Podiums Culture:** "Racers are tracked through the weekend on large boards, as they finish we place a green dot by their name, if they DNF they receive a red dot. No podiums... By the end of the weekend, everyone wanted to know 'what color is your dot'? Bikes did not have number plates." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-crusher-enhanced-gravel-michigan-upper-peninsula-usa/)

**Finisher Awards:** "Finishers of the 2019 Crusher received a poster designed by Christopher Schmidt, and a camp mug. For the 225-mile event it was a Titanium camp mug, for shorter events steel." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-crusher-enhanced-gravel-michigan-upper-peninsula-usa/)

**Mission Statement:** "Our mission is to help you find your best version." With hashtags: "#enhancedgravel, #uncivilized, #crushorbecrushed" (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-crusher-enhanced-gravel-michigan-upper-peninsula-usa/)

**Camping Community:** "We camp together. We cry and laugh together. We CRUSH together." At Trail's End Campground in Copper Harbor for Mass Start events. (https://906adventureteam.com/mountain-bike-events/the-crusher/)

**Event Origin:** "Established in 2014 as the Huron Mountain Crusher, and known for a short time as HAMR, we've returned the event to its original format and intention, offering men and women a unique opportunity to #findtheirlimits solo or as part of a team." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-crusher-enhanced-gravel-michigan-upper-peninsula-usa/)

**Gender Equity Commitment:** "We want to show endurance races can welcome and equally represent men and women, showing our commitment 275 spots have been reserved for women and 275 for men." (https://www.gravelcyclist.com/training-rides/the-crusher-enhanced-gravel-michigan-upper-peninsula-usa/)

**Facebook Community:** "Join the Crusher Facebook group. It's the best source for up to date Crusher information and help from the community." Repeated multiple times as the primary information source. (https://906adventureteam.com/mountain-bike-events/the-crusher/)
