# THE DIVIDE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Jochen Böhringer [COMPETITIVE]:** "My longest race so far was the Silk Road Mountain Race in Kyrgyzstan, which took just under 8 days. The Tour Divide will likely take me twice as long, and I'm really curious to see how this extended duration affects me—especially mentally. In shorter events, I've managed to stay focused despite exhaustion and lack of sleep. But over two or more weeks? That's uncharted territory." (https://ultra-jo.com/2025/06/09/td2025/)

**Jochen Böhringer [COMPETITIVE]:** "The Tour Divide means two things to me. First and foremost, it's the realization of a lifelong dream to ride this legendary route. That means finishing is my top priority—like in most races I do. But everyone who knows me also knows: I wouldn't sign up for a race without trying to go as fast as I can." (https://ultra-jo.com/2025/06/09/td2025/)

**Shannon Allison [UNKNOWN]:** "What surprised me the most about the Tour Divide? I think the friendships that I forged throughout the tour took me off guard. You're not supposed to have emotional support as a racer but I chose to make friends and leave behind the notion of racing. I'm still going up the same mountains as the racers but at least I had someone to commiserate with." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/7/14/five-questions-with-tour-divide-rider-shannon-allison)

**Shannon Allison [UNKNOWN]:** "What I learned about myself: I learned I have zero desire to actually race anymore. What I want most in my life is adventure and new roads." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/7/14/five-questions-with-tour-divide-rider-shannon-allison)

**Karin Pocock & Alexandera Houchin [COMPETITIVE]:** "We rode it in together! However Karen started a wave behind me (15 min later) so we agreed she beat me by 15 minutes for 3rd." (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Eddie Clark [ELITE - photographer embedded with race]:** "I found it imperative to share some goodwill with my northern neighbors and friends. It couldn't have been a more pleasant reminder of just how nice our closest allies are... When we think at a base level that most people are good people, it becomes a lot easier to listen and empathize with them, to be friendly, and to work toward a common good." (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2025-tour-divide-eddie-clark-part-1/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Jared Dunham [UNKNOWN - 3rd overall 50mi]:** Rode 42cc tires but felt he would have been fine on 40cc tires. "The sand made a few of the hills more challenging but you don't need a big tire to ride the course." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**Terry Ritter [UNKNOWN - 1st in 50+ class, 50mi]:** Raced on 36cc tires. Felt course conditions were excellent with "right direction and plenty of heavy rain the day before." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**Hunter Post [UNKNOWN - 1st age group, 4th overall 50mi]:** Raced 40cc tires. "The rain helped firm up the sand, but the depth was still energy draining. Particularly on the 2nd lap, once the sand was chewed up by other riders." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**Melanie Post [UNKNOWN - 1st age group, 34mi]:** Raced on 40cc tires. "The sandy climbs were definitely the most challenging part of the course, aside from just the elevation gain in general." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**Christina Vipond [UNKNOWN - 2nd overall women, 50mi]:** Raced on 36cc tires. "Choosing lines on the edge of the two-tracks was helpful but I still did my fair share of walking some of the deeper sand." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**Shannon Allison [UNKNOWN]:** "The bungee webbing that I bought to go over my rear rack was the best piece of gear. I would stuff rain gear/wind breaker in it for quick changes, I've stuffed several burritos and pizza boxes under it and extra bottles of electrolytes! When I purchased it I wasn't sure if it would work out but I'll never do a bike pack trip without it now!!" (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/7/14/five-questions-with-tour-divide-rider-shannon-allison)

**Kathy Schoendoerfer [ELITE - regarding Robin Gemperle, 2025 winner]:** "He has the most minimalist bike I've seen." (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

## Weather Experienced

**2025 Tour Divide - Eddie Clark [ELITE]:** "It's worth mentioning just how nice the weather was so far compared to previous years, and I doubt any of the veteran racers would say otherwise." Described conditions as sunny with clear views in the Swan Valley area of Montana. (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2025-tour-divide-eddie-clark-part-1/)

**2025 Tour Divide - Eddie Clark [ELITE]:** At Red Meadow Lakes: "On my very first trip past Whitefish in the 2011 Tour Divide, this entire section of the route was snowed in and required quite the hike-a-bike effort from the racers." In 2025, conditions were clear. (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2025-tour-divide-eddie-clark-part-1/)

**2025 Tour Divide - Eddie Clark [ELITE]:** Near Brush Mountain Lodge in Colorado: "The sky was black and lightning crashing down every few seconds... It started to rain and get cold." (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2025-tour-divide-eddie-clark-part-1/)

**The Divide Michigan - July 2020:** Rain the day before the race "helped firm up the sand" according to multiple riders. Course had been recently brined, making the outer loop roll "faster than ever." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**The Divide Michigan - July 2020:** A July 24th update reported that "rain had made the washouts on Gilbert Corners a little bigger." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Tour Divide 2025 - Robin Gemperle [ELITE - 1st place, 11d:19h:14m]:** Covered 310 miles in the first 24 hours. Arrived at Ovando, Montana (mile ~530) at 2:45 a.m., nine hours ahead of the 2024 record pace. Had a five-hour nap in the tipi there. According to Kathy Schoendoerfer: "despite the ease in which he seems to be crushing it... he is having problems finding places to sleep." (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Tour Divide - Eddie Clark [ELITE]:** Red Meadow Lakes area: "There's not much for views on these roads as the trees are tall, and the undergrowth is just as dense as the grizzly population." (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2025-tour-divide-eddie-clark-part-1/)

**The Divide Michigan - Multiple riders [UNKNOWN]:** Gilbert Corners described as a 3-4 mile section of "sandy two-track that keeps racers guessing about their bike choice." The 34-mile riders ride this section "mostly uphill on their way back into town." The 50-mile riders experience it both out and back with "sketchy downhills" on the way out and "sand at the bottom of those downhills will zap your legs before the punchy uphills challenge your will power." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**The Divide Michigan - Jared Dunham [UNKNOWN]:** "The Divide may be 50 miles but it's probably the most memorable 50 mile race course I've done so far... very hilly with some sand thrown in." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**The Divide Michigan - Course description:** One section dubbed "the redneck roughneck" is a slightly rougher downhill. The 19-mile course features "short punchy climbs" including the "Amish Sawmill" section and "Gilbert Corners." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**The Divide Michigan - Outer Loop:** "Out here the hills are much bigger but will have a lower incline, except for El Diablo on E. 35 Rd. which is both steep and long." The loop direction alternates each year - even years clockwise, odd years counter-clockwise. (http://hardracingevents.com/events/the-divide/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Tour Divide 2025 - Nathalie Baillon [ELITE - 1st place women, 16d:10h:17m]:** Was first woman to reach Ovando, Montana at approximately mile 530. "Stopped for dinner and then continued on and has now reached Lincoln, around 30 miles away." Rode "neck and neck" with Lael Wilcox the entire way south from Canada, both well ahead of Lael's 2025 record. (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Tour Divide 2025 - Robin Gemperle [ELITE - winner]:** Stopped at Blackfoot Angler shop in Ovando at 2:45 a.m., had a five-hour nap, then departed at 8:20 a.m. after eating a sandwich. (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Shannon Allison [UNKNOWN - left course near Breckenridge after 1700+ miles]:** Target finish was 27 days. Rode over 1700 miles with 70,000+ feet of elevation gain before leaving the course. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/7/14/five-questions-with-tour-divide-rider-shannon-allison)

**Hunter Post [UNKNOWN - 4th overall, 50mi Michigan race]:** Noted that sand was "particularly energy draining on the 2nd lap, once the sand was chewed up by other riders" - suggesting first-lap advantages and cumulative fatigue on multi-lap courses. (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Tour Divide 2025:** High-profile scratches occurred by Day 7 of the race. (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Tour Divide 2025 - Fire Conditions:** The Indios Fire and later the Antone Fire in New Mexico affected the route. The Antone Fire grew to 7,000 acres at zero percent containment, closing Mangas Road directly on the Tour Divide route. Race organizer Matthew Lee had to make last-minute decisions and had "boots on the ground to scope out alternatives in real time." (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Tour Divide 2025:** Brush Mountain Lodge was not open during the race, which was described as "heartbreaking" by preview coverage. (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2024-tour-divide-preview/)

**Tour Divide - Sleep Issues:** Robin Gemperle, despite crushing the race, was "having problems finding places to sleep" according to Kathy Schoendoerfer. (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**Shannon Allison [UNKNOWN]:** Left the Tour Divide course near Breckenridge, Colorado after riding 1700+ miles. (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/7/14/five-questions-with-tour-divide-rider-shannon-allison)

**The Divide Michigan - Course Hazards:** Race organizers posted "when you see a caution sign, we mean it!" regarding washouts on Gilbert Corners that got bigger after rain. (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Shannon Allison [UNKNOWN]:** "Act of kindness: Fun story... I was on my way to Brush Mountain Lodge 18 miles over the Colorado border and the sky was black and lightning crashing down every few seconds... About 12 miles from the lodge a truck pulled up and a man, his wife and their grandkid were inside. He said 'Shannon, do you need a ride to the lodge?' I immediately said yes and we loaded our bikes into the truck and they delivered us safely to the lodge... Either way, they saw us in trouble and went way out of their way to help and it was the 4th of July." (https://www.ohiogravelgrinders.com/blog/2025/7/14/five-questions-with-tour-divide-rider-shannon-allison)

**Eddie Clark [ELITE]:** On crossing into Canada: "I had a pleasant conversation with a Royal Canadian Mounted Police officer, and we both were not happy with tariffs or antagonistic messaging coming from Washington, but we could easily agree that it was just politics and we were still friends... While waiting for a lead racer to cross the Elk River into Fernie that evening, another complete but very friendly stranger walked up to see what I was photographing, and we must have had a 20+ minute conversation about everything from bikes to history and, of course, politics." (https://bikepacking.com/plog/2025-tour-divide-eddie-clark-part-1/)

**Kathy Schoendoerfer [ELITE - Blackfoot Angler shop owner]:** Greeted racers at her shop in Ovando, Montana for the sixth time greeting Lael Wilcox. Acts as an unofficial checkpoint where racers stop for food and rest. (https://bikepacking.com/tracker/2025-tour-divide-tracker/)

**The Divide Michigan - Christina Vipond [UNKNOWN]:** "Jeff, Don and their volunteers (including the cross country and track teams) were top notch with ice cold drinks and freeze pops at all aid stations. The course was well marked with signs and volunteers were stationed throughout the course to make sure racers stayed on course. Photographers volunteered their time and posted over 1000 photos that racers could share for free." (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**The Divide Michigan - Purpose:** Started in 2015 by Jeff Harding and Don Passenger as a fundraiser for Manton Public School's Cross Country and track teams. All proceeds go to the teams. (https://www.athleticmentors.com/the-divide-gravel-road-race/)

**Jochen Böhringer [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'm beyond excited for the challenge—and equally for the incredibly diverse and beautiful landscapes along the way... I'm really curious to see how this extended duration affects me—especially mentally... I'm just incredibly excited to finally arrive in Banff, to get to the start line, and to immerse myself in the special community of riders. These events always bring unforgettable encounters with like-minded adventurers from around the world." (https://ultra-jo.com/2025/06/09/td2025/)
