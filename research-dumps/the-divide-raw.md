# THE DIVIDE - RAW RESEARCH DUMP

## OFFICIAL DATA

**Race Dates**

The Divide is held **on the last Sunday of July each year**[1][2]. For 2026, this falls on **July 26, 2026**[3]. The 2025 race occurred on **July 27, 2025** (which was the last Sunday of July that year)[4].

**Start Time and Location**

The race starts in **downtown Manton, Michigan**[2][5]. For 2025, registration opened at 9:00 AM at Railroad Park in Manton, with the race start at **noon**[4]. The 2026 race is scheduled for **9:00 AM - 2:00 PM**[3], suggesting an earlier start than 2025.

**Distance Options**

The Divide offers **three distance options**[1][2]:

- **19 miles** — characterized by short punchy climbs and fast downhills
- **34 miles** — includes the challenging Outer Loop section
- **50 miles** — the longest option, taking the long way to and from the Outer Loop through Gilbert Corners

All three routes are **very hilly** and include a mixture of gravel, seasonal, and forest roads, with some sandy spots[1][2].

## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**

The course uses **primarily gravel roads with a sand base**[1]. The Standard (19-mile) course uses "mostly solid gravel roads with only a little sand and one slightly rougher downhill section"[2]. The exact percentage breakdown of gravel vs. pavement is **not provided**.

**Key Course Features and Named Sections**

- **Amish Sawmill** — the 19-mile course includes short punchy climbs from this area[2]
- **Gilbert Corners** — described as a "challenging section"; the 19-mile course experiences the best half, while 50-mile riders take the long way through it[2]
- **The Outer Loop** — an 11-mile section featuring much bigger hills with lower inclines (except one exception); accessed by 34 and 50-mile riders[2]
- **El Diablo on E. 35 Rd.** — described as "both steep and long"; a notable climb within the Outer Loop[2]
- **Redneck Roughneck** — a slightly rougher downhill section on the Standard (19-mile) course[2]

The Outer Loop direction alternates each year: clockwise on even years, counter-clockwise on odd years[2].

**Technical Difficulty**

The course is described as **"doable by any rider"** on the Standard 19-mile option[2], though the 34 and 50-mile courses with the Outer Loop are significantly more challenging. Roads are **rideable at high speed with skill and high-volume, fast-rolling tires**[1].

**Elevation Gain**

The search results do **not provide specific elevation gain data** for any of the three distances. However, all routes are characterized as "very hilly."

**Course Direction**

The race starts in downtown Manton and heads **south and west towards dirt roads that skirt the Manistee National Forest**[2][5].
