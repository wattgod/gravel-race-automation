# THE EQUALIZER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Key Details

**The Equalizer** is a gravel race held in Marthasville, Missouri, featuring two distance options:
- **50K (actually 43 miles)**: 1,800 feet of elevation
- **100K**: 3,786-4,100 feet of elevation (sources vary slightly)

The race starts at 7:30 AM and rolls through Warren County's rolling hills, featuring a mix of hard-packed crushed limestone, loose gravel, large rock sections, paved roads, and creek crossings. Both distances share the first 5-12 miles before splitting (sources vary on exact split point).

**Key Features:**
- Free onsite camping at Wessel Park and KT Caboose (Friday/Saturday night)
- Showers and bathrooms available across the street
- Saturday afternoon pre-race recon ride offered
- Aid stations on course (100K riders see it twice)
- CX-style finish
- Post-race food, music, raffle
- Part of the Show Me Mo Gravel Series
- Missouri State Championship race for gravel (USAC sanctioned, 100K distance only)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Variety:**
"Gravel consistency ranges from fast hard packed crushed limestone, to loose gravel, to big brown Charette Creek river valley rock, to buttery smooth asphalt" (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)

**Creek Crossings:**
The 100K features "3 creek crossings" described as "the most awesome and refreshing creek crossings" (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/). The 50K includes "a 5 mile out and back through a few of the famous creek crossings" (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)

**Course Character:**
"The course takes you through roads that look untouched by time, into the beautiful and beastiful rolling hills of Warren county highland farm country" (https://irace.ai/events/the-equalizer-gravel-race)

**Technical Sections:**
- "The Equalizer is fast enough for the pavement pounder, yet technical enough for the gravel grinders" (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)
- Features "The Big Watts Big Rocks Segment" and "Kersten Krusher" KOM/QOM climbs (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)
- New KOM/QOM Gravel Strava segment climb added for 2024

**Route:**
Both the 50K and 100K routes loop through Marthasville and Warrenton, with "fast road sections peppered by limestone gravel through the rolling countryside" (https://trailnet.org/calendar/the-equalizer-gravel-race-2024/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Course Support:**
- Aid stations provide water, ice, and sports drink
- 100K riders see aid station twice
- Sag bag service available at aid stations
- Trailing/roaming sag support provided
- Course is "well marked" with "orange arrows on the road roadside directional stakes" and "several corner marshalls present" (https://irace.ai/events/the-equalizer-gravel-race)

**Pacing Considerations:**
The race is described as having enough variety to accommodate different rider types: "fast enough for the pavement pounder, yet technical enough for the gravel grinders, and most important… Fun and safe for everyone" (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)

No specific pacing or nutrition data with rider level attribution was found in the sources.

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Pre-Race Experience:**
"Saturday Afternoon, the day before, there will be a fun, free, chill partial short course recon ride. Bring your friends, stay the night, we will be serving dinner (tbd) go take a shower ya filthy animal, get a plate of food, camp out, and get ready for the 7:30 start. All right there in Racers Village." (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)

**Post-Race:**
"Post race food, snacks, beverages, music, podiums, pics, Equalizer Black T-shirt" (https://trailnet.org/calendar/the-equalizer-gravel-race-2024/)

Post-race raffle includes prizes, with I-9 Hubs mentioned as raffle items in multiple years.

**Awards:**
- Custom hammer trophies for 1st place
- Custom hammer medals for 2nd and 3rd place
- Top 5 overall finishers get their names printed on next year's t-shirt
- State Championship winners receive Roy Doskal Realty State Championship Jersey and medals
- KOM/QOM awards for two Strava segments (must be completed during race)
- Awards for top 3 in all age groups (10-year increments) and overall M/F

**Event Vibe:**
"It's gonna be a good day" and "Glory awaits" are recurring phrases in official race descriptions, suggesting an enthusiastic, welcoming atmosphere. The race emphasizes being "Fun and safe for everyone" (https://terrain-mag.com/event/the-equalizer-50k-and-100k-gravel-race/)

**Charity Component:**
Trailnet is the charity partner, receiving 100% of donations.

## Related Context: Lifetime Fitness Equalizer (2002-2004)

**Note:** Several sources reference a different "Equalizer" race - the Lifetime Fitness Equalizer triathlon (2002-2004) - which was a battle-of-the-sexes format where women started ahead of men. This is NOT the same event as the Missouri gravel race.

**Barb Lindquist [ELITE]** won the first Lifetime Fitness Equalizer in 2002: "I was super excited to participate in the first equalizer in the sport. I went in confident: I was racing well, and I figured I had a good shot at winning... With about a half-mile to go, I just launched into a full-on sprint... I beat Becky by 10 seconds, but most importantly, I got Craig by 15. Winning $50,000 was amazing" (https://finance.yahoo.com/news/recalled-great-equalizer-205308580.html)

On the race's impact: "Did the equalizer race change the scope of women's triathlon? I believe it did... Having a woman win it three years in a row definitely inspired the next generation of great triathletes. Those races showed that women can go out and kick butt, race tough and with integrity--and keep a smile on our face while at it." (https://finance.yahoo.com/news/recalled-great-equalizer-205308580.html)
