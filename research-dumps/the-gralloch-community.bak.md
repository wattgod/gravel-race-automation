# THE GRALLOCH — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE]:** "The Gralloch is tough. There is no denying that. The course is brutal. To paint the picture, the first 12 km is nearly all uphill, right out of the blocks... This year the 110km long course was extremely dry and dusty and even though you climb just over 1,700 metres, the course was running extremely quickly. So fast in fact, I averaged nearly 32 km/h!" (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE]:** "Standing on the start line I had one goal. Qualify for the World Champs. I was quietly confident I could achieve this in my 19-34 category, but with gravel racing, anything can happen. One over cooked corner, one puncture or even one too little energy gels and your race can be over just like that." (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE]:** "With the first 12km being nearly all uphill I had one goal, set a pace hard enough that it would dispatch enough riders but not too many that no one would be left to help work together. The starting climb starts off very steep and so there is little advantage following someone's wheel so I knew I wanted to be on the front to set the pace to my liking. Gradually increasing the pace the front group slowly got smaller and smaller and as we crested the first climb there were around 30 to 40 riders left." (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE]:** "I've raced the Gralloch UCI Gravel Race before and with this in mind I knew a technical, narrow section was fast approaching as we hit the 40 km mark. Moving up through the group I used my cyclocross skills to my advantage, pushing the pace through this narrow section to go clear. With it being such a narrow few kilometres the hope was that not many would have seen me slip off the front." (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE]:** "Another thing a lot of people find unusual is I don't have a power meter and ride completely off of feel. I've been doing this for the past 2 years and find it really beneficial for situations such as this and knowing what your body can and cannot handle." (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)

**Michael Newton [UNKNOWN]:** "After last year's puncture issues, I took descents a little bit steadier than normal which seemed to pay off as I had no mechanical issues throughout the race. I kept pushing on the pedals and fuelled what I thought was going to keep me going strong throughout... At the 75km mark I had a lack of energy but thankfully the legs came back 5km later with the help of a group that dragged me to the feed zone at 87km point. I topped up the water bottle and squeezed a few oranges down my neck which was a refreshing change to gels etc." (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)

**Michael Newton [UNKNOWN]:** "Around 100km we hit some tarmac, oooooh buttery smooth tarmac mmmmmm. Thankfully it was pretty much all downhill to the finish which would normally mean cranking over the pedals and powering to the line...... not this time. Every small group that went past my brain said get on the back and hold on, my legs said, no thanks I'm cooked. I cruised over the line knowing I probably hadn't done enough to qualify for Worlds but I was happy with my effort non the less." (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)

**Michael Newton [UNKNOWN]:** "The atmosphere at the finish was fantastic but unfortunately I couldn't enjoy it as my stomach was feeling dodgy after all the energy food..... definitely need to change my fueling strategy for future events." Finished in 4hrs 12mins, 48/113 in 35-40 category. (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)

**George McHollingsworth [UNKNOWN]:** "George burst out of the starting gates with all the fury of a caffeinated greyhound, rocketing up the ranks from 293rd to a top 150 spot. The crowd was surely watching in awe—until disaster struck. A suspected carbohydrate overload dragged him into a brutal slowdown, leaving him prey to the ruthless onslaught of older, wiser riders... By the final feed station, George had only one move left: a dramatic mid-race purge, a tactical chunder of legendary proportions." (https://www.rlscyclingclub.com/post/the-gralloch-a-uci-gravel-race-in-scotland)

**Gerry McScott [UNKNOWN]:** "Everything was going brilliantly—until bottle gate struck. His Temu bottle cage failed him, leaving him with only one water bottle for the remainder of the race. It didn't matter. Gerry was unstoppable. With grit and sheer stubbornness, he blasted his way into the top 25%, securing his place at the UCI Gravel World Championships in October." Finished 4 hours 8 minutes, 75th place in Male 50-54. (https://www.rlscyclingclub.com/post/the-gralloch-a-uci-gravel-race-in-scotland)

**Emily McNash [UNKNOWN]:** "Emily's flapjack-fuelled charge put her in prime contention, climbing into the top 25 of her category by the halfway point. But then fate intervened, and her second water bottle took flight, joining Gerry's in the void of gravel heaven. Dehydration began to take its toll, forcing a slow and painful slog to the next checkpoint... she rallied to finish second-fastest among the team—but just shy of qualification. The jersey she wore across the finish? Decorated with a coat of sweat and salt stains, the mark of a true fighter." Finished 4 hours 28 minutes, 66th place in Female 35-39. (https://www.rlscyclingclub.com/post/the-gralloch-a-uci-gravel-race-in-scotland)

**Matt Buckley [COMPETITIVE]:** "It was around 75km that the cramp in my legs started to set in; every gradient became a balancing act between pushing on hard enough and avoiding my left quad spasming, which then required a few minutes off the bike, drinking as much as I could, and pulling some interesting stretches." (https://www.t3.com/active/cycling/i-rode-the-uks-most-brutal-gravel-race-and-lived-to-tell-the-tale)

**Matt Buckley [COMPETITIVE]:** "The descent from Loch Grannoch was a different beast, however. Fist-sized bits of gravel and relentless corrugations made for very tough going on my already tired body. Choosing the fastest line down the hill had turned into letting go of the brakes and hanging on for dear life, my hands cramping onto the bars with a vice-like grip." Finished in just over 5 hours. (https://www.t3.com/active/cycling/i-rode-the-uks-most-brutal-gravel-race-and-lived-to-tell-the-tale)

**Ben Thomas [ELITE]:** "With still 70 km to go, pacing was going to be crucial. I knew that this later part of the race would suit a group better than a solo rider thanks to the wider, smoother gravel tracks and so knew I just had to go all in to establish a gap." After Petr Vakoc's puncture at 80km: "Suddenly from the race being completely under control I'm now out front on my own leading a UCI gravel race. I've gone from thinking about a top 2 result to thinking I could win." Finished 4th, just 1 second behind the podium. (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/gralloch-almost-a-dream-ride)

**Ben Thomas [ELITE]:** "The games really begin, no one wants to lead, no one wants to be at the back, we almost come to a standstill. We enter the high street and the speed ramps up again sprinting for the next turn, fighting to be in a good position, I get elbow bumped back to fourth. My quads are cramping and I cannot move back up the positions before we turn into the arena." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/gralloch-almost-a-dream-ride)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE] - Complete Setup:**
- Bike: Ribble Gravel SL with Sram Force AXS groupset, 44T chainring front, 10-44 cassette rear
- Wheels: Hunt 40 Limitless Gravel Aero Wheelset ("engineered to be the fastest and most versatile gravel race wheelset on the market")
- Tires: Pirelli Cinturato RC 45mm with tyre inserts to protect rims
- Clothing: Sportful Fast Not Fun gravel-specific skinsuit with cargo pockets
- Note: "Sharp dagger-like rocks line the course and the wrong gravel bike setup for racing could make or break your race" (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)

**Puncture Protection - Critical for All Levels:**
- "Punctures were a common theme once again at this year's edition of The Gralloch" (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)
- "Lost bottles, punctures and dropped chains were a common occurrence" (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)
- General tire trend: "While wider tyres – or even MTB tyres – are generally becoming a thing in gravel, the majority is still rolling on 45 mm tubeless tyres, preferably with a tyre liner. Swapping the front tyre to a gripper tread pattern was common, as was running slightly higher pressures for the rough course." (https://escapecollective.com/tech-gallery-gravel-gear-at-the-gralloch/)
- Terrain classification: "The flinty type of rocks that seem to be native to this area are sharp, and if we go by the categorisation of Silca's tyre pressure calculator, most of it is firmly in the 'class 4' category." (https://escapecollective.com/tech-gallery-gravel-gear-at-the-gralloch/)

**Matt Buckley [COMPETITIVE] - Hydration:**
- "Heat, Hydration Packs, and Hope... I became increasingly pleased with my last-minute purchase of a hydration pack. At around 50km in, I'd already drained both bottles on my bike and was getting through the 2L of water in the hydration pack; without it, I'd have been in serious trouble." Temperature was nudging 28°C. (https://www.t3.com/active/cycling/i-rode-the-uks-most-brutal-gravel-race-and-lived-to-tell-the-tale)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile:**
- Distance: 110-111km with 1,700-1,761m of climbing
- "The first 12 km is nearly all uphill, right out of the blocks" including the iconic Fuffock Hill (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)
- "From there, you descend the rough gravel tracks before you're treated with a brief bit of rest with a few kilometres of smooth tarmac to catch your breath before you again dive back onto the gravel" (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)
- "For the next 80 kilometers the course dips and dives through the Galloway Forest, taking in the sights before you finally return to the tarmac for the final 10km stretch to the finish line" (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)

**Key Technical Sections:**
- "A technical, narrow section was fast approaching as we hit the 40 km mark" - ideal for making a break (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)
- "The descent from Loch Grannoch was a different beast... Fist-sized bits of gravel and relentless corrugations made for very tough going" (https://www.t3.com/active/cycling/i-rode-the-uks-most-brutal-gravel-race-and-lived-to-tell-the-tale)

**Surface Conditions:**

- "The next 20 kilometers were fast and flowy" after the opening climb (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)
- "Wider, smoother gravel tracks" in the latter part of the race (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/gralloch-almost-a-dream-ride)
- Feed stations at approximately 87km mark (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)
- "Around 100km we hit some tarmac, oooooh buttery smooth tarmac... pretty much all downhill to the finish" (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)

**Scenery:**
- "One minute, I was riding through wide open glens with views stretching miles, and the next, I was picking the smoothest line through a lochside path with the deep blue water looking so inviting" (https://www.t3.com/active/cycling/i-rode-the-uks-most-brutal-gravel-race-and-lived-to-tell-the-tale)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE] - Front Group Strategy:**
- "With the first 12km being nearly all uphill I had one goal, set a pace hard enough that it would dispatch enough riders but not too many that no one would be left to help work together. The starting climb starts off very steep and so there is little advantage following someone's wheel so I knew I wanted to be on the front to set the pace to my liking." (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)
- "Gradually increasing the pace the front group slowly got smaller and smaller and as we crested the first climb there were around 30 to 40 riders left" (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)
- "The next 20 kilometers were fast and flowy and with there still being a big group at the front it allowed me to surf the wheels and save my energy" (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)
- Attack timing: "I knew a technical, narrow section was fast approaching as we hit the 40 km mark. Moving up through the group I used my cyclocross skills to my advantage, pushing the pace through this narrow section to go clear" (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)

**Tom Couzens [COMPETITIVE] - Nutrition Strategy:**
- "90 grams of carbs an hour is my magic number. It's something I've tried a lot in training to find out what works best for me and this is it. Little and often is also key. I've found a Veloforte energy gel every 15 minutes is the perfect gravel race nutrition strategy." Each gel = 22g dual sourced carbs + electrolytes. (https://www.huntbikewheels.com/blogs/news/racing-the-gralloch-with-tom-couzens)
- Pacing: "I don't have a power meter and ride completely off of feel. I've been doing this for the past 2 years" - Average heart rate for the race: 164 bpm (https://laka.co/gb/blog/uci/racing-the-gralloch-my-experience-at-the-uci-gravel-world-series-in-scotland/)

**Michael Newton [UNKNOWN] - Mid-Pack Experience:**
- "In the moment with fresh legs, adrenaline pumping and great atmosphere I went off a bit quick which I paid for later on in the race" (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)
- "I rode with a number of small groups but found going at my own pace at times was a better approach as I wasn't tempted to push too hard" (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)
- "At the 75km mark I had a lack of energy but thankfully the legs came back 5km later with the help of a group that dragged me to the feed zone at 87km point" (https://www.barnsleyroadclub.co.uk/post/gralloch-uci-gravel-2024-111km-race-report)
