## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Martin Criminale [UNKNOWN]** (pre-rode 2022 L course):
- **Bike:** Thesis OB1 AXS
- **Gearing:** 42/52 low gear — "would not want to try this course with anything bigger"
- **Tires:** 650x48mm Rene Herse Switchback Hill tires — "would recommend at least 45mm tires"
- **Group comparison:** Two others had 650x2.1" tires, one had 700x38mm tires BUT notes: "1) our pace was only moderate and 2) this person's rims are wide and 3) we had perfect conditions/traction. If it's hot and dry 38mm tires will get swallowed by the sand."
- **Hydration:** Carried two large bottles but filled one before the last climb. "One of us drank two big bottles and a 70oz reservoir they carried on their back. If it's hot, bring a filter and perhaps bring a third bottle or pack."
- **Fenders:** "I almost never take my fenders off but for this ride, I kept bending the front one when I tried to ride over logs... I recommend not using fenders for this ride because of all the obstacles even though it's nice to keep the mud off. Or just walk your bike over every log."
(https://martin.criminale.com/2022/06/pre-riding-grit-l-course.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Martin Criminale [UNKNOWN]** describes three major climbs on the L course:
- **First climb:** "comes right out of the gate and is heading out on the stick of the lollipop and was super steep and I never had the energy to take my hands off the bars to take a picture"
- **Second climb:** "in the loop/body of the lollipop and is not quite as steep and is a little smoother (fewer washboard)"
- **Third climb:** "the return trip on the stick of the lollipop and is the most fun. Assuming you still have the energy to enjoy it of course. And assuming it's not a really dry day which would turn this climb into a giant sandbox."

**First descent:** "So good! You head down a short stretch of dirt road and then turn right into the woods. From here to the bottom it's mostly doubletrack, singletrack, and FUN. You are also in the woods so it's not quite as exposed as the rest of the course. At the bottom of this descent, you cross a creek which is a good source of water in case you need it. I do recommend filtering."

**Second descent:** "Even better! Is that possible...?! The first part is steep but then it's mostly gradual with lots of water bars, tiny creek crossings, eroded trails, and lush green meadows. You really need to pay attention here as some of the dips and bumps are bigger than you think so don't let it rip."

**Views:** "If the skies are clear, you can see for miles in every direction."

**P2C trail:** Riders "will ride P2C trail along the lake. Please be aware of and respectful to other trail users." (https://thegrit.bike/course-details/)
(https://martin.criminale.com/2022/06/pre-riding-grit-l-course.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Martin Criminale [UNKNOWN]** and group (moderate pace, ~5 hours for 45 miles):
- **Nutrition:** "2 large bottles each with 1.5 scoops HEED and .5 scoops Endurolytes Extreme Powder, half a bottle of water, 1 almond butter and jam sandwich"
- **Hydration strategy:** Refilled at creek crossing before final climb
- **Note:** This is a MODERATE pace strategy for a recreational/pre-ride scenario — approximately 9 mph moving time including stops
(https://martin.criminale.com/2022/06/pre-riding-grit-l-course.html)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Water troughs (hazards):** "Be careful as you approach these as some only have a very narrow section of rideable trail that goes around them and some will require you to get off and walk your bike as the transition at the bottom of the trough is too sudden to ride." (https://martin.criminale.com/2022/06/pre-riding-grit-l-course.html)

**Last descent danger:** "Be warned, this is very steep and you'll be tempted to let go of the brakes on the straight sections but each corner invariable has some washboards and my heart rate spiked more than once when my rear tired locked up as I got air off of a bump. Also, the last part of the descent is paved so you feel like you have ultimate traction but some of the corners are sharp. Watch out." (https://martin.criminale.com/2022/06/pre-riding-grit-l-course.html)

**General course warnings from organizers:** "All the roads are open to traffic including cars, trucks, side-by-sides, and so on. You must obey all rules of the road at all times! Failure to do so might results in serious injury – we can't keep everything safe for you so be smart and ride safely. Expect rough descents and keep your speed under control at all time... This is adventure, not champagne, gravel racing to expect rough roads." (https://thegrit.bike/course-details/)

## Course Details & Distances

**2026 Course Options (from official site):**
- **Maximus:** 63 miles, 7,100 feet of climbing (Latin for "greatest")
- **Fortis:** 42 miles, 4,700 feet of climbing (Latin for "strength")
- **Velox:** 31 miles, 3,100 feet of climbing (Latin for "speed and agility")
- **Primus:** 20 miles, 1,300 feet of climbing (Latin for "first" — "a great first gravel ride," new for 2026)

**Location:** Start/finish at Crystal Spring Sno-park, exit 62 on I-90 near Snoqualmie Pass, WA
- "So close to the Seattle area and so close to I-90" (Mark C)
- Described as taking place in "rugged North Cascade mountains" and "remote forest roads & trails in the North Cascades Wilderness"
(https://thegrit.bike/ and https://thegrit.bike/course-details/)
