# THE INSAYNER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

The InSayner is described as uncategorizable—"It can't be classified as a road race, a mountain bike race or a gravel race – it's in its own category of adventure as it includes fire roads, snowmobile trails, gravel roads, paved road, and even a few deer and foot trails." (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

**Course terrain mix includes:**
- Snowmobile trails (multiple sections throughout)
- Single track at Musky Mountain bike trails
- "Sweet stretches of downhills"
- An old ski hill run
- Logging roads
- Gravel roads
- "A small stretch of blacktop"
- Deer and foot trails
- Creek crossings (including Plum Creek—crossed twice on some routes, once with a bridge, once without)

**Specific challenges mentioned:**
- "Climbing Mus-Ski mountain"
- "Slogging through mud"
- "Fending off mosquitoes and deer flies"
- Creek crossings
- "Suicide Hill" (long course riders go UP it instead of down)

**Course routing:** Starts in Sayner at the Community Center, travels down snowmobile trails to Musky Mountain, continues down Plum Vitae Road to another snowmobile trail, hits Vandercook Road multiple times, continues to Klondike Road and Ross Lake Road before following snowmobile trails back to the finish. (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

**Wildlife encounters:** "The pack leaders caught sight of a bear crossing the course" during the inaugural 2021 race. (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**[UNKNOWN LEVEL]** Race directors' advice from 2022: "The InSayner will be a challenging, but fun race that to the bitter end will throw you an occasional gut punch... What kind of bike you ask? Ha Ha Ha Ha wouldn't you like to know! But know this: one bike's advantage won't last forever." (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

**Cut-off enforcement exists:** For the longer course, race directors warn: "this is NOT an all day event. YOU have a cut off to make, otherwise it's black top for you!" (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

**From 2026 race details [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "This is tough—but it's doable if you pace yourself and keep the wheels turning. There's a cutoff: make it in time and you stay on course; miss it and you'll take the alternate route back. No dramatics required. Hydrate, fuel, and keep moving—future you will be grateful." (https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

**Long course psychology warning [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** "Just hammer those hills and don't get off your bike because it can sense fear – shed a tear and it's over." (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Course conditions:** "It levels the playing field for adventurers – folks who never raced a bike event before finished and placed in the top 3, while active racers dropped like, well, flies!" (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

**Long course self-sufficiency requirement [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Race directors warn long course riders: "And don't count on any race support! You had the chance to sign up for the short race, the 25ish. Maybe you should re-think your decision, buttercup?" (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

**Physical challenges from inaugural race [ELITE]:** "One of our local, legendary mountain bikers had to lay down in the middle of a creek to relieve a leg cramp!" (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

**Cutoff enforcement:** Missing the time cutoff results in being redirected to "black top" alternate route rather than continuing the full backcountry course. (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Inaugural 2021 event [ELITE]:** "Over 120 people gathered in the small town of Sayner, WI, on Saturday, June 12th, for an adventure to remember, the first race of its kind in the Lakeland Area... 'It came together just as we hoped,' said Olkowsi. 'I was told the pack leaders caught sight of a bear crossing the course and one of our local, legendary mountain bikers had to lay down in the middle of a creek to relieve a leg cramp!' But, for all involved, it was a blast." (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

**Registration growth:** First year drew "nearly 80 registrations" with "visitors from all over the MidWest" and promised to grow with additional course options. (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

**Charitable purpose:** "The InSayner has a bigger purpose, though, than just getting folks up to the Northwoods, bringing like-minded silent sports enthusiasts together and just having a fantastic amount of fun. It's serving to introduce a significant new LAMBO project – Mus-ski Mountain Trails, with a 5-year plan and a $350,000 price tag." All proceeds support trail building and maintenance. (https://lambo.rocks/news/the-first-ever-insayner-rocked)

**Race day schedule (2026):**
- 8:00–9:15 AM — Bib Pickup / Check-In / Registration
- 9:15 AM — Rider Meeting
- 10:00 AM — 25-mile InSayner + 15-mile Swarm Starts (separate waves)
- 10:05 AM — No-See-Um Family Fun Ride Start
- 1:30 PM — Awards + Raffles
(https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

**Race director personality:** The event features humorous, challenging language in its descriptions. Example from long course description: "Oh, you're one of THOSE people! The 25ish isn't enough? You think you're better than that, don't you?" and ends with "Remember, you chose this. No cursing, and don't forget to sign the waiver. Yours truly, Danny and Mike O., The InSayner Race Directors." (https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

**Family-friendly options:** The event includes a 3.5-mile "No-See-Um Family Fun Ride" described as "short, scenic, and still a little bit InSayner" with volunteer assistance available at creek crossings for families and first-timers. (https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

**Part of larger race series:** Listed as an eligible race in the "Ironbear 1000" challenge, which strings together 1000 miles of Wisconsin gravel races. (https://www.ironbull.org/ironbear-1000)

**Trail advocacy recognition [COMPETITIVE]:** Michele Underwood's Wisconsin trail series highlights InSayner as one of the "Mountain bike races that build and sustain our trails," noting "All proceeds from the event support trail maintenance, new trail builds, and the addition of fun features like berms and drops that make riding the Sayner trails exciting and challenging." (https://www.overlandgirl.com/mountain-biking/wisconsin-bike-races-supporting-trails)

## Race Format & Distance Options

**Current format (2025-2026) offers three distances:**
- **25-mile InSayner Back Country Race:** "The signature event: challenging, fast, and wildly fun"
- **15-mile Swarm Adventure Race:** "The 'highly recommended' sweet spot of challenge + fun"
- **3.5-mile No-See-Um Family Fun Ride:** "Short, scenic, and still a little bit InSayner"
(https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

**Historical format (2022) offered two distances:**
- 23-mile route: "The brutal 23 mile backwoods biking adventure"
- 50+ mile route: "The savage, barbaric 50+ mile torture on a bike" with minimum registration requirement of 20 riders, otherwise long course riders would "run the short course twice"
(https://runtrimag.com/race-events/insayner-mountain-bike-race-musky-mountain-bike-trails-fund-raiser/)

**Earlier formats (2021-2022) mentioned:**
- 12-mile option (listed as "12 (ish) mile")
- 25-mile option (listed as "25 (ish) mile")
(https://www.ironbull.org/ironbear-1000)
