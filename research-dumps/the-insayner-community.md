# THE INSAYNER — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Race Overview & Mission
The InSayner is a **fundraiser mountain bike race** supporting trail building and maintenance in Northern Wisconsin, specifically benefiting LAMBO (Lakeland Area Mountain Biking Organization) and the Musky Mountain trail system. The race takes place in the **Northern Highland-American Legion State Forest** near Sayner, WI, and is described as "three events in one" with varying difficulty levels.

**Race Directors:** Danny and Mike O. (https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

**Mission:** All proceeds go directly to trail maintenance, new trail builds, and features like berms and drops at LAMBO trail systems. (https://www.overlandgirl.com/mountain-biking/wisconsin-bike-races-supporting-trails)

## Race Format & Distances

**Three Race Options:**

1. **25-mile InSayner Back Country Race** — "big adventure, big effort, big bragging rights" (https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

2. **15-mile Swarm Adventure Race** — described as "the highly recommended sweet spot of challenge + fun" and "a 12-mile backwoods biking adventure" (note: sources list both 12 and 15 miles) (https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race, https://www.nwpulse.com/insayner-bike-race-to-deliver-big-adventure-this-saturday/)

3. **3.5-mile No-See-Um Family Fun Ride** — "short, scenic, and still a little bit InSayner" (https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

**Early Editions:** The race originally offered 23-mile and 53-mile options in earlier years (2021-2022). (https://fat-bike.com/mc-events/insayner-adventure-bike-race/?mc_id=1004)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**25-Mile InSayner Back Country Race Route:**
- Starts at Sayner Community Center
- Heads southwest on snowmobile trails to Musky Mountain bike trails (singletrack)
- Continues down Plum Vitae Road to another snowmobile trail
- Hits Vandercook Road "a few times"
- Continues to Klondike Road and Ross Lake Road
- Returns on snowmobile trails to finish
(https://fat-bike.com/mc-events/the-inthe-insayner-23-mile-adventure-bike-rice/, https://www.raceplace.com/events/162810/insayner-mountain-bike-race)

## Trail System Context

**Musky Mountain Trail System:**
Located between Boulder Junction and Sayner, off Highway N on Plum Vitae Road, near Crystal Lake and Musky Lake campgrounds. When completed, expected to offer 15 miles of trail for all skill levels.

**Phase One (completed October 2023):** 5 miles of machine-built, flowy trail with "smooth lines and some faster sections," optional jumps, tall berms, and hand-built technical trail.

**Phase Two (under construction):** 3 miles of "gravity-fueled downhill trails" with "steep descents, mandatory jumps, and large berms" including a trail that challenges riders to "fly over Crankbait."

**Phase Three (planned 2026):** Final 6.8 miles providing beginner/intermediate trails to glacial valleys and views of West Plum Lake.

(https://www.nwpulse.com/insayner-bike-race-to-deliver-big-adventure-this-saturday/)

**Other LAMBO Trail Systems:**
- Raven Trail at Clear Lake (Woodruff)
- Zip Trail (Minocqua)
- Shannon Lake Trail (St. Germain)
- Sucker Springs (Eagle River)

These include "hand-built technical single track and machine-built flow, trails for all skill levels, groomed winter fat-biking trails, skills park and pump track, epic elevation (at least for northern Wisconsin) and scenery." (https://www.nwpulse.com/insayner-bike-race-to-deliver-big-adventure-this-saturday/)
