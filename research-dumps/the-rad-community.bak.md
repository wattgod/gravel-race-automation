## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Race Dynamics [ELITE level]:**

**2024 Men's Race:** "At mile 46 the group started to break up with Lachlan Morton and Nathan Spratt about three minutes ahead of a large chase group that included Alexey Vermeulen, Alex Howes, Peter Stetina, Matt Beers and more. After 55 miles of gradual climbing, the course began to descend. At mile 65, Stetina, Vermeulen and Brendan Johnston led with Morton, Howes, Spratt and Eric Brunner not far behind." At the halfway point the lead group was charging up the rolling climbs led by De Crescenzo, Paige Onweller, Skarda and Anthony. ## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Profile (2024):** Three distances offered:
- 112-mile Stubborn Delores course: 10,400 feet of elevation gain
- 68-mile Anteloop course: 6,500 feet of elevation gain  
- 47-mile Frijole course: 4,200 feet of elevation gain

"All three courses were new for this year's race and featured a mix of pristine and punchy gravel in what was essentially a lolly pop out and back." (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Course Layout:** "The course started with a lap around gorgeous Trinidad Lake State Park, where the lead men stayed in a large pack." After the State Park section, riders faced "After 55 miles of gradual climbing, the course began to descend" before "the final sections" and "a pretty brutal climb on blacktop" followed by "the sporty descent in the last quarter mile" back into downtown Trinidad. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Finish Circuit:** "gnarly climbs through town to finish downtown at the expo" with "a pretty brutal climb on blacktop" and "a sporty descent in the last quarter mile." The technical finish allowed for late-race position changes. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Surface Quality:** Described as "pristine and punchy gravel" with "impressive views of the Spanish Peaks" and "miles of smooth gravel." The organizers characterized it as "arguably one of the most beautiful courses in the country." (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/ and https://gearandgrit.com/life-time-introduces-the-rad-dirt-fest-a-new-gravel-bike-and-trail-run-event-in-trinidad-colorado/)

**Course Changes:** Significant year-over-year variation: "the courses changed significantly from last year to this year." The Rad was chosen as the "wild card event of the Life Time Grand Prix series" in 2023. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Participation vs. Completion:** In 2023, "Of the 120 bikers to enter the race, only 90 were able to finish this year's grueling ride" — a 25% DNF rate in the alleycat race format, though this was a different Oakland-based event also called "Rad." (https://oaklandnorth.net/2010/07/12/cyclists-race-in-the-rad-massaker-alleycat/)

**Weather Challenges:** "As the day went on the wind picked up and made for a challenging end of the race" — wind conditions deteriorate as the day progresses, potentially causing later starters or slower riders more difficulty. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Elevation and Distance:** The 112-mile course with 10,400 feet of climbing is described as being for "the hardiest." The combination of high elevation gain and distance poses significant challenges. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Town Transformation:** "Downtown Trinidad was completely revitalized from the last time I had been through here many years ago... Usually these little towns go downhill, not up, and it was a welcomed surprise. It seemed as if all of the dispensaries had brought in a bit of money and life back to the area, and there were way more breweries and other renovated store fronts throughout the quaint, historic downtown." (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Event Scale and Diversity:** "more than a thousand of seemingly every type of rider, pros and amateurs alike from 46 states and nine countries" attended in 2023. The event draws international participation across all skill levels. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Local Support:** "we persevered with mostly smiles and high fives" during the race. The finish was "downtown at the expo" suggesting integrated community celebration. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Cultural Integration:** The event "coincides with a town celebration commemorating the 200th year of the Santa Fe Trail" and includes "festivities celebrating all of the cultures that make Trinidad who we are, including activities like cowboy poetry, Aztlan Native American performance, fandango dancers and so much more!" (https://gearandgrit.com/life-time-introduces-the-rad-dirt-fest-a-new-gravel-bike-and-trail-run-event-in-trinidad-colorado/)

**Local Dining:** "Rino's, where you'll eat authentic Italian food and be serenaded and swooned with Elvis and Neil Diamond classics by the staff during your meal. It's really something else." A recommended pre-race carb-loading spot. (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/)

**Prize Distribution:** "There were so many prizes that even those who came in nearly dead last, were still able to get at least some sort of memento from the race." The event rewards participation broadly, not just podium finishers. (https://oaklandnorth.net/2010/07/12/cyclists-race-in-the-rad-massaker-alleycat/)

**Town Vision:** Trinidad officials "have welcomed us with open arms and an invitation to join them on their journey toward becoming the next big outdoor recreation location in Colorado. The gravel and trail running community is rooted in successful partnerships." The town is actively developing as an outdoor recreation destination with a "newly established Office of Outdoor Recreation" working to create "a new mecca for outdoor exploration, quickly developing a number of new trails in the community." (https://www.elevationoutdoors.com/go-outside/why-the-rad-dirt-fest-is-rad-and-the-gear-that-helped-get-the-job-done/ and https://gearandgrit.com/life-time-introduces-the-rad-dirt-fest-a-new-gravel-bike-and-trail-run-event-in-trinidad-colorado/)
