**Distance Options and Elevation Gain**

The race offers **three distance options**:[2][4]

- **110 Mile** course
- **70 Mile** course  
- **40 Mile** course (47.2 miles actual distance with 4,540′ elevation gain and highest point of 7,221′)[2]

All three courses incorporate a 2-mile technical gravel section on South Shore Trail near Trinidad Lake State Park with "pitchy hills with loose gravel, coupled with mostly smooth gravel."[2]

**Field Size**

**Prize Purse**

Search results do not specify a monetary prize purse. However, the race offers **UNBOUND Gravel qualifier coins** with the following structure:[4][5]

- **50% Performance-Based:** Top riders in each category receive coins
- **50% Random Drawing:** Half the coins distributed via random drawing
- **Winners:** Receive guaranteed entry to Life Time UNBOUND Gravel presented by Shimano (entry fee not included) for either the 200 or 100 mile distances

For the 110 and 70-mile distances, **awards are 3 deep in each category**.[2][5]

**Aid Station Details and Cutoff Times**

- **40 Mile:** 4:15pm support at Mile 62.5 (Trinidad Lake State Park) — no drop bags
- **70 Mile:** 6:30pm support at Mile 90.3 (Burro Canyon)

## TERRAIN & COURSE

**Surface Breakdown**

The exact percentage breakdown of gravel vs. pavement vs. singletrack is not provided in search results. However, the race is categorized as a **gravel grinder**[3] and incorporates a notable **2-mile technical section on South Shore Trail** with loose gravel and pitch climbs.[2]

**Key Course Features**

The race is set in the **Spanish Peaks region** of Southern Colorado with courses featuring "towering Spanish Peaks and expansive mesas" as the backdrop.[4] The 40-mile course includes:

- Trinidad Lake State Park (appears on multiple course distances)
- Burro Canyon (70-mile aid station at Mile 90.3)
- South Shore Trail (technical section with loose gravel and pitchy climbs)

**Technical Difficulty Level**

The race features a designated **2-mile technical section** described as "more technical gravel" with "pitchy hills with loose gravel, coupled with mostly smooth gravel."[2] The overall difficulty level is not explicitly rated in available sources.

**Elevation Profile**

For the 40-mile course: 4,540′ elevation gain with highest point of 7,221′[2]. Complete elevation profiles for the 70 and 110-mile courses are not provided in search results.
