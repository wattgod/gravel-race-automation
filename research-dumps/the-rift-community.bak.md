# THE RIFT — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Nina "Machina" [UNKNOWN]:** "I set my max power record for an hour and a half, a testament to my effort... Dark lava fields turned to sand and washboard gravel, and while the Grit fork on my Lauf Seigla combined with 2.1 inch MTB tires and GGA 38 GULO gravel wheels helped with some of the shock absorption, my arms, neck, and back also got destroyed. I was also quite alone at that point, with no women to ride with around and I was working by myself in the headwind all the way to Aid 4. I almost considered defeat, but I quickly pushed that thought out of my mind and fought harder... 127 miles in 9 hours, and I found myself in 12th place for the Elite Women's field." (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)

**Thor Dieringer [UNKNOWN]:** "The descent was pure carnage, huge ruts, baby-head rocks, and deep sand that sapped energy and demanded focus. Thank everything for the RockShox Rudy fork, it was an absolute game changer... One more climb, a splash through the same river crossings from earlier, then onto the tarmac for the final push. A final fourteen minutes of full gas effort... After 6 hours and 30 minutes, 200 km and 6500 kilojoules burned, it came down to a sprint for the top 10." (https://routewerks.cc/blogs/the_dirt/trip-report-the-rift-gravel-race-2024)

**Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "Dire. Not the first word that one would typically hope to describe a 'vacation,' but that's not how we roll over here. Dire. Fcks were given... 'It's awful, but you're a sick b*tch, you'll love it.' – this from my friend who did the race in 2019... I will have nightmares about the next 15 miles. The highest point of the ride was at mile 43, we foolishly thought that we would now be rewarded with some downhill. No. From mile 60 to 75 we rode nothing but the most miserable, sharp, awful washboards with the rain and wind picking up. Just frame shattering horror." (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)

**Mikey [COMPETITIVE]:** "I would probably look to work a bit more on my topend 1-3 minute efforts, so I can get myself over the steeper climbs a bit better. Last year I punctured and broke a wheel early on, so I ran wider 45mm tyres as well as a wider and bigger tyre liner to mitigate the effects of any impacts or rocks and holes." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)

**Tom [COMPETITIVE]:** "Unfortunately, my legs didn't feel the best on race day. With that in mind, I backed off a little, which gave me time to take in the sights as we raced around Hekla, one of the most active volcanos in Iceland! I then picked up the pace in the second half of the race, picking off the riders who had gone too deep too early to ultimately finish 25th." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)

**Metheven [COMPETITIVE]:** "It was an absolutely incredible experience racing through volcanic landscapes and across freezing river crossings. It's a race that must be experienced to be believed. Early in the race, I was just behind Mikey when a crash occurred... The only fundamental mistake I made was missing a couple of gels during a rough section of the course, which I paid the price for later when I ran out of energy." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)

**Geerike Schreurs [ELITE]:** "Iceland's raw, volcanic landscape is incredible stunning, but it's hard to capture how brutal it is to race a bike across this terrain. Very impressive ride by Rosa Klöser who was flying early in the race. And even that I tried, there was no way to catch her. After riding 120km solo I'm grateful to come away with second place at The Rift." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/gravel-earth-series-2025/the-rift-10-event-elite-men-women/results/)

**Magnus Bak Klaris [ELITE]:** "The course was harder, rougher, and more rocky than expected, the climbs steeper. It was super exhausting...and the scenery? Unreal." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/gravel-earth-series-2025/the-rift-10-event-elite-men-women/results/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tires - Wide is Better (Multiple Levels Agree):**
- **Nina [UNKNOWN, Elite Women 12th]:** 2.1 inch MTB tires on GGA 38 GULO gravel wheels with Lauf Grit fork "helped with some of the shock absorption" (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)
- **Thor [UNKNOWN]:** Maxxis Reaver 700c x 40, but noted "if I had this to do over, I would ride on 42 or 45s for just a bit more comfort" (https://routewerks.cc/blogs/the_dirt/trip-report-the-rift-gravel-race-2024)
- **Mikey [COMPETITIVE]:** "ran wider 45mm tyres as well as a wider and bigger tyre liner to mitigate the effects of any impacts or rocks and holes" after puncturing and breaking a wheel the previous year (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)
- **Metheven [COMPETITIVE]:** "settled on a 2" (50mm) Continental Race King. They have an incredibly low rolling resistance, and the extra volume smooths out rough terrain, making you go faster with less risk of puncturing." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)
- **cmscat50 [ELITE]:** "Biggest tires possible will help. I rode 38 which back then wasn't tiny but I did puncture (snake bite)" (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/the-rift/1284306)

**Suspension Forks:**
- **Thor [UNKNOWN]:** "Thank everything for the RockShox Rudy fork, it was an absolute game changer" (https://routewerks.cc/blogs/the_dirt/trip-report-the-rift-gravel-race-2024)
- **Nina [UNKNOWN]:** Lauf Grit fork on Lauf Seigla helped with shock absorption (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)
- **cmscat50 [ELITE]:** "You will then quickly learn why they invented the Lauf fork as the washboard is worse than I have seen anywhere else" (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/the-rift/1284306)

**Gearing - CRITICAL Level-Specific Advice:**
- **Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE, 10th place]:** "The 46-tooth chainring I had fitted turned out to be my undoing. With the gradient and loose terrain climbing out of the saddle as I prefer wasn't possible with limited grip available from the loose ground. It was brutal." He notes "with different gearing that might have been possible" for a higher finish (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-rift-volcanoes-and-gravel)
- **Abraham Soler [RECREATIONAL]:** "switched up the gear ratio, opting for a 10-50t cassette and with a SRAM Eagle AXS rear derailleur and a 40t chainring. This proved a perfect ratio for those steeper gradients" (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/the-rift)

**Bottle Cages:**
- **Mikey [COMPETITIVE]:** "I would also probably fit some different bottle cages to hold the bottles more securely on the rougher sections. The only unexpected event I experienced was losing both my bottles on the 'Washboard' gravel roads." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)
- **Metheven [COMPETITIVE]:** "Make sure you have secure bottle cages as so many people lose bottles over the rough terrain." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)

**Complete Setups:**
- **Thor [UNKNOWN]:** Salsa Warbird, Route Werks Handlebar Bag, Garmin 840 Solar, Maxxis Reaver 700c x 40, Velocio kit, USWE Outlander Pro hydration pack (https://routewerks.cc/blogs/the_dirt/trip-report-the-rift-gravel-race-2024)
- **Abraham Soler [RECREATIONAL]:** FiftyOne Assassin, René Herse Manastash Ridge 44c tires, 10-50t cassette with 40t chainring (https://signaturecycles.com/journal/the-rift)
- **Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** Ribble Ultra Grit with RockShox Rudy fork, 46t chainring (later regretted) (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-rift-volcanoes-and-gravel)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**The Washboard Section (Miles 60-75 approx):**
- **Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "I will have nightmares about the next 15 miles... From mile 60 to 75 we rode nothing but the most miserable, sharp, awful washboards with the rain and wind picking up. Just frame shattering horror. Every time you thought you saw a better line it would disappear. The 'gutters' were ten inch deep sand with fixed, sharp lava rock to punish you if you made the slightest error. The demon washboards had to be ridden. There was no way around it... Definitely the most miserable 90 min I've ever experienced on a bicycle." She saw "a sign that said 'Stop crying like a child'" during this section. (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)
- **Nina [UNKNOWN]:** "Dark lava fields turned to sand and washboard gravel... my arms, neck, and back also got destroyed" in a section called "Oli's Loop" (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)
- **cmscat50 [ELITE]:** "The washboard is worse than I have seen anywhere else" (https://forum.slowtwitch.com/t/the-rift/1284306)

**River Crossings:**
- **Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "this is a river, not the little creeks we are used to back home, like a hubs deep, 15 pedal stroke freaking river... I had walked this same river in knee deep water 90 min ago and now I'm riding eight or so inches deep" (found a better line on return). Friend Echo "went in the drink, all but her left shoulder and head, big hip bruise too. That was mile 60." (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)
- **Metheven [COMPETITIVE]:** "freezing river crossings" (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)
- **Adrian Ong [COMPETITIVE]:** "water coming up your legs so cold you're reminded why this place is called Iceland" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/adrian-vs-the-rift-iceland-s-volcanic-wilds-fights-back)
- **Thor [UNKNOWN]:** Notes "numerous glacial river crossings" and a "Sock Drop (spare socks to change into after the numerous glacial river crossings were complete)" (https://routewerks.cc/blogs/the_dirt/trip-report-the-rift-gravel-race-2024)

**The Hike-a-Bike Climbs:**
- **Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "We saw riders ahead walking up an extremely steep climb. Ah! There it is, the hike-a-bike we heard about. We decided to see how far up it we could get... we congratulated ourselves, we were punished for our arrogance. Around the next bend and saw the actual hike-a-bike... Half a mile later…… THE hike-a-bike." (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)
- **Nina [UNKNOWN]:** "some of the steepest pitches of the course, some with over 23% grades and we grunted as we pushed our bikes over top of them" near mountain Sléttafell (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)
- **Adrian Ong [COMPETITIVE]:** "16-30% gravel climbs, one with an inclination so severe that everyone (even the pros) had to dismount to get to the top" (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/adrian-vs-the-rift-iceland-s-volcanic-wilds-fights-back)

**Hekla Volcano Climb (2025 New Course):**
- **Ben Thomas [COMPETITIVE]:** "This year's new course didn't mess around. Instead of skirting Hekla, we climbed it... Long, steep, loose, and relentless... As we ground our way toward the snow line, the race shattered. I could see riders dotted up the volcano ahead. Eventually we were off the bikes hiking through snow with numb legs and burning lungs." The descent: "pure carnage, huge ruts, baby-head rocks, and deep sand that sapped energy and demanded focus." (https://www.benthomascoaching.com/post/the-rift-volcanoes-and-gravel)

**Technical Terrain:**
- **Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "The climb to the highest point of the race at mile 43 was getting more and more technical... riding on the side of a volcano, but INSIDE a volcano, across a caldera, down cracked black lava descents... I got to the point where I knew I had two options, crash or let both brakes go and hope the bike and tires would just figure it out despite me. They did." (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)
- **Thor [UNKNOWN]:** "a banked rolling, twisting black lava road with technical rocks and a glacier below" (https://routewerks.cc/blogs/the_dirt/trip-report-the-rift-gravel-race-2024)

**Course Navigation:**
- **Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "We came upon a left hand turn with three 4x4s and four volunteers urging us left. Turns out the front group had made a wrong turn there and ended up on the actual glacier" (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Start Intensity (Multiple Levels Report Similar Pattern):**
- **Casey Cohenmeyer [ELITE]:** "After the neutral roll-out, it was full gas to the top of mountain Sléttafell... I set my max power record for an hour and a half" (https://bicycle.spinergy.com/blogs/news/the-rift-2021-race-report-i-mean-survival-report)
- **Nina [UNKNOWN, Elite Women 12th]:** "After the neutral roll-out, it was full gas to the top of mountain Sléttafell" (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)
- **Mikey [COMPETITIVE, 13th place]:** "I was happy with my start this year and in a reasonably good position going onto the first climb... Over the next hour or so, I managed to stay with the front of the race as it whittled down to about 15 riders towards its highest points." (https://www.ribblecycles.co.uk/blog/the-rift-2024-round-up/)
- **Tom [COMPETITIVE, 25th place]:** "Although I made the front split over the first few climbs I was very much aware that I still had 160km of tough racing to go and really didn't want to blow up! **When the Race Gets Hard:**
- **Multiple sources identify Mile 60-75 washboard section as the crux**
- **Nina [UNKNOWN]:** "I almost considered defeat" during the headwind section after the washboards but "I caught a second wind on the road after chugging some energy drink at the feed zone and felt more hopeful once the chunky, slow-moving gravel turned to paved road" (https://gulocomposites.com/blogs/journal/the-rift-race-recap-2024)
