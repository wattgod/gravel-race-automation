# TRAKA 360 — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "It starts in Girona, heads towards Cap de Creus, takes in Cadeques and Roses, goes south towards Pals and Llagostera, before circling back to Girona. There's almost 5,000m of climbing with most of it off-road. It's rough and rugged, steep and stunning, and while the views are spectacular in places, it's a hard day out." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "When I decided I wanted to do the race months ago my aim was to be in by midnight. Closer to the race I could see the shape I was in and looking at the list of women racing I thought maybe anywhere from 3-8 was both possible and realistically depending on how the day played out, if it went well 3-5 was definitely in reach." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "Coming in just after 10pm meant there were still people around, the LaBiciletta team were there, my dad and some friends from town. I may as well have won the race the way we were celebrating when I crossed the line. To say it was an amazing feeling doesn't quite cut it." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "Despite a series of mechanical mishaps and a race route ripped apart by a week of heavy rain, his refusal to throw in the towel saw him push through for the win." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/traka-360/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "I love the Grand Prix and what they're doing for pro-racing in the US but they're not the only show in town. You've got Mid South, Steamboat, BWR and TRAKA, to name but a few, that are all super relevant. So I can't just give myself solely to the Grand Prix because there's just too much cool shit going on and I want to do it all." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "I'm not entirely sure what I've gotten myself into. Last year, I raced the 200k, followed by 100k the next day, but that was spread out over two days. This year, I've signed up for the 360k route—a non-stop race with just a few aid stations." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "Early mistakes like these can ruin your whole race, and these early bursts of effort are going to come back to bite later on." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "I want to stop. I want to call Hanna and have her come pick me up. But I don't. I keep going, riding as if I'm out on a recovery spin." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Ulrich Bartholmoes [ELITE]:** "Although I didn't crash, another rider crashed into my back and tore open my hydration pack - and completely soaked me in 2 liters of sticky liquid. Not exactly what you want after an hour of racing - when you know you have at least 12 more to go...." (https://www.kogel.cc/blogs/kbba/ulrich-bartholmoes-vs-the-traka-360)

**Ulrich Bartholmoes [ELITE]:** "On the last big climb about 80 kilometers before the finish it was clear: I have to attack here if I want to have a chance... With the worst line and probably the smallest / non-existent sprint talent of myself, I ended up in 5th place overall." (https://www.kogel.cc/blogs/kbba/ulrich-bartholmoes-vs-the-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "The season is so long in gravel. Traka and Unbound are the real ones that matter to me. Everything else is noise." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "You can't replicate a 12-hour effort in training. If you try, you'll just hurt yourself." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'm 31 now. I've been doing this for over a decade. I used to chase numbers. Now, I chase a feeling." Threshold for Freddy is around 370 watts. "I don't look at power when I ride. I just lap it at the start of a climb and check it after." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "Last year, Freddy had a DNF—but not because of fitness. After 9.5 hours of racing and with just 90 minutes to go, a mechanical forced him to pull out." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Timo Rokitta [COMPETITIVE]:** "After 17 hours and 28 minutes, I finally made it. The efforts of the past 5 months have paid off!" (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-the-traka360-from-dawn-till-dusk)

**Timo Rokitta [COMPETITIVE]:** "The subsequent descent is on a 'corrugated road' - my hands are now hurting intensely." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-the-traka360-from-dawn-till-dusk)

**Jan Stöckli [ELITE]:** "When I signed up for the Traka360, I honestly didn't realize what I was getting into. On the form, they asked how long you thought you'd need to finish. The options started at 11 hours. That's when it hit me – this wasn't 360 degrees, it was 360 kilometers!" (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-heroes-the-traka-360)

**Jan Stöckli [ELITE]:** "Honestly, it was the best day I've ever had on a bike. I never felt like I hit my limit, and my head was in the game the whole time... There's always someone doing something even crazier. Like the people riding 360 km today and another 200 km tomorrow. So what I'm doing? Peanuts." Finished 3rd place. (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-heroes-the-traka-360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "On the very first ride, my mech hanger snapped, flinging itself into the rear wheel and leaving the bike in pieces. In that moment, I genuinely thought the race might be over before it had even started." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "I'd never cycled 360 kilometres in one go. The furthest I'd ever ridden in a single day was 230 km, back in Iceland in 2018... just six days before race day, I had taken on a challenge of an entirely different nature, running all eight main hiking and walking paths up Mount Snowdon (Yr Wyddfa) in a single day... For the next four days, I could barely walk." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "The opening section was a dream. Dry gravel, golden light, cool morning air. I quickly found rhythm, linking up with a group of German riders. We moved efficiently, covering the first 135km in just under five and a half hours." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "The fatigue caught up with me. It felt like I was sleepwalking. Not asleep, but barely alert... I started thinking maybe I should call Sarah. Maybe take a shortcut. Maybe I didn't need to finish... But then I caught myself. What would I think? Would I be okay with quitting? The answer came fast and sharp: absolutely not." Finished in 18 hours, 15 minutes, 23 seconds. (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "I'm on the 3T Exploro RaceMax. 40 on the front, with 44 11 on the back. I could probably have done with something smaller on the front or bigger on the back. I could ride everything on the recce's with that set-up but by 275km I was definitely looking for more gears on the climb. The bike is SRAM E-Tap AXS and the tyres were Rene Herse Hurricane Ridge 42mm tubeless – they feel good on every surface." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "I&#8217;m running DT Swiss GR1400 wheels paired with Challenge Getaway 45 tires, and they feel just right on the road." On brand-new Felt Breed Advanced. (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Ulrich Bartholmoes [ELITE]:** "Heavy Usage: My bikes are in daily use and roll a lot. Usually over 30,000 kilometers a year. I put great importance to durability - and it makes a difference to me when parts last a whole season and don't have to be constantly replaced in between. Especially the tough requirements in gravel races do not make it easy for the bearings: Dust. Mud. Water. Constant hard knocks. Kogel bearings have convinced me: ceramic bearings with Cross Seals last a whole season for me!" (https://www.kogel.cc/blogs/kbba/ulrich-bartholmoes-vs-the-traka-360)

**Ulrich Bartholmoes [ELITE]:** "Marginal Gains: How much wattage does an oversize pulley save? Honest answer? I don't know. But let's do an example calculation: Assuming the more efficient path of the chain saves 1-2 watts - that could theoretically make a difference of 100m per hour more I can cover with the same power." (https://www.kogel.cc/blogs/kbba/ulrich-bartholmoes-vs-the-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "Battery life is a huge one. I've had units die mid-race before. With DURA [COROS], I know it'll last the distance." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** Running an "aero frame bag" for the 360. (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/traka-360/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "There are five feed stops/aid stations on the course. The first is at 90km, with the distance decreasing between each stop." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "After that, the toughest part of the course followed. From the feed station to Roses, it was either steep uphill or steep downhill. The (exhausting) highlight was a 7km long singletrack with many loose rocks... The final climb before the second refreshment station in Roses was so steep that almost everyone had to push their bikes due to the 15% gradient and a strong headwind." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "Shortly before Pals, there was an exhausting climb in the forest on extremely sandy ground. A fast section then brings me to the final ascent, which climbs to over 450 meters. The subsequent descent is on a 'corrugated road' - my hands are now hurting intensely." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Timo Rokitta [COMPETITIVE]:** "Traka is never easy. The profile promises massive climbs that you know are going to hurt, but in between, it's a constant rollercoaster of twisting trails. Keeping your momentum is key, but it's also exhausting. Every micro-effort chips away at your energy storage. And then there's the ever-present risk of punctures—or worse, a crash." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-the-traka360-from-dawn-till-dusk)

**Timo Rokitta [COMPETITIVE]:** "A short section of trail runs alongside a stream and then it's full throttle towards the finish." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-the-traka360-from-dawn-till-dusk)

**Jan Stöckli [ELITE]:** "After a quick descent, we catch my American friend Griffin Easter. But soon enough, the two of us are forced to let the others go. Another low point in the race, and I'm barely moving forward... With 10 km to go before the last big climb, I made my move. The group split, and six of us hit the climb together. After the descent, just three of us were left." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-heroes-the-traka-360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "The next stretch was flatter, 100 km of consistent grind. I aimed to get through it in about four and a half hours... Garmin Hill, the hardest climb of the entire route. It lived up to the hype. Long, steep, and completely unrelenting. I crawled up it, running on fumes. Even a rider on the 560km route said it was the worst hill of the whole thing... From there, a brutal descent, one final kicker of a climb, and then the last 50 km." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "My weeks generally followed a similar pattern: a mix of easy short/long rides/and intervals during the week with one to two gym sessions, and then Saturday and Sunday are long rides... My longest training session was just under 9 hours. Three of us got on the train one Saturday morning and recced some of the hardest sections of the course before riding back. About 180km in total. It was a long day. Then the next day was 4.5 hours. Both gravel, so it's a bit more unforgiving on the legs." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "Of the top 5 women I'm the only one who didn't have someone in each zone with a prepped vest or bag for them. I carried most of my food with me, and picked up bits at the feed stops... I had maltodextrin mixed with the water in the pack on my back, and also in the bottle on my bike. I had a smaller bottle with no water and just the powder for later on. I made a some rice-cakes, which I managed to eat a few of before they escaped their packaging and covered EVERYTHING in my bags in gooey chocolate rice… I'd bananas with me, and plenty of gels and jellies." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "What would you do differently if you did the event again? I would make sure to fill up the water bladder in my backpack at the first feed stop, as well as the bottle. And I'd bring chamois cream with me." (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "We're barely 40 kilometers into the race, with more than 320 to go. Early mistakes like these can ruin your whole race, and these early bursts of effort are going to come back to bite later on." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "From the feed station I descended cautiously to avoid damaging my tyres. Many gravel bikers were standing on the side of the road here, repairing their tyres." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "I'm stocking up, but something's off—we've already covered 135 km, yet this aid station was supposed to be at 130 km." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Ulrich Bartholmoes [ELITE]:** "Mental Tricks & Motivation: I spent a lot of time beforehand thinking about how I'd mentally get through such a huge distance. My motto was: There's always someone doing something even crazier. Like the people riding 360 km today and another 200 km tomorrow. So what I'm doing? Peanuts. I also tried to keep the vibe light in our group – cracking jokes, keeping the mood up." (https://www.kogel.cc/blogs/kbba/ulrich-bartholmoes-vs-the-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** Building through "six- to eight-hour rides at mid-Zone 2, with sub-threshold sprinkled in." Rides just below threshold, typically around 370 watts. "Going in undercooked is fine. Going in overcooked is not." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "I only have the map screen on race day. No distance. No time. If you give yourself too much info, you can talk yourself out of it." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Freddy Ovett [COMPETITIVE]:** "You've got to have an internal governor. Don't get dragged into someone else's race. Ride your own." (https://coros.com/stories/d/freddy-ovett-traka-360)

**Timo Rokitta [COMPETITIVE]:** "After participating in The Traka 200 last year, it was clear that I would ride the 360-kilometer route in 2023. The preparation began on December 1, 2022... During the preparation phase, I trained an average of 80-90 hours per month. In addition to cycling sessions, I also ran a marathon and did other runs to add some variety to my training." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-the-traka360-from-dawn-till-dusk)

**Jan Stöckli [ELITE]:** "My Race – Looking Back: I came up with a plan: get into an early breakaway and try to build a gap, just in case I couldn't keep up on the longer climbs. But surprisingly, I ended up being the strongest climber in my group. On the second big climb, I pulled away and rode my own pace. But then came a super long flat stretch – over 100 kilometers. Riding that solo? Not smart. So I waited for the chase group." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-heroes-the-traka-360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "At each feed station, I gave myself permission to slow down. Eat properly. Refill bottles. Take a moment to breathe." First 135km covered in just under five and a half hours. "At 180 km, I saw Sarah for the second time. A moment of calm in the storm. I devoured orange segments like they were going out of fashion... by the end of the race, I must've eaten a whole tree's worth (okay, at least 13 whole oranges!)." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** On mental struggle around 180km+: "I started thinking maybe I should call Sarah. Maybe take a shortcut... What would my clients think if I didn't finish? What would Sarah think?... But more than any of that, what would I think? Would I be okay with quitting? The answer came fast and sharp: absolutely not." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "On the very first ride, my mech hanger snapped, flinging itself into the rear wheel and leaving the bike in pieces. In that moment, I genuinely thought the race might be over before it had even started." Pre-race mechanical prep critical. (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "The next day brought a different kind of challenge. Not soreness. Not stiffness. But deep, lingering mental fatigue. I'd run a mountainous ultra up Snowdon just a week before. My taper had been a mountain marathon. I was cooked." Mental fatigue as significant as physical. ## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Emma Porter [ELITE]:** "The Traka is run by a group called Klassmark. Any Klassmark event I've been to is always brilliant. It's not only well run, but the atmosphere is excellent. It's fun, social, they value the environment and community above everything else, but they also attract some of the best athletes in the world. Lorena Wiebes won the 100km this year!" (https://tritalkingsport.com/race-report-tackling-the-traka-360-by-emma-porter/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "The day before the race, the city of Girona was full of gravel bikers from around the world. Everywhere you went, you would encounter the stars of the scene, whether it was in a café, restaurant, or one of the bike shops - gravel everywhere." (https://cyclespeak.com/tag/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "The Traka started in 2019 and has been ongoing since then. The first year of The Traka saw an impressive turnout of nearly 100 participants. In 2020 and 2021, even with the pandemic, the number of participants increased to 200 and 400, respectively. In 2022, The Traka experienced a revival with 800 participants and this year's event was a phenomenal success, with 1900 cyclists from all around the globe taking part in The Traka!" (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "The Traka stands out from other gravel events for several reasons... the event has a strong focus on sustainability and minimising its impact on the environment, with measures such as the use of compostable products and a 'leave no trace' policy. Another unique aspect of The Traka is its inclusive and community-oriented atmosphere, which encourages riders of all levels to participate and challenge themselves. The event also features a range of activities and entertainment options, such as music performances and food trucks, creating a festive and enjoyable experience for participants and spectators alike." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Jonas Orset [UNKNOWN]:** "– ¡Felicidades, Jonas Orset! the announcer shouts as I cross the finish line. I made it. What a feeling! I'm welcomed by the Felt team with smiles and pats on the back." (https://nordictrailblazer.cc/races/traka-360/)

**Timo Rokitta [COMPETITIVE]:** "More than 400 participants started on time at 6:00 am on the 360-kilometer track." (https://www.gravelunion.cc/article/ride-report-the-traka360-from-dawn-till-dusk)

**Jan Stöckli [ELITE]:** "The atmosphere at the start line was electric. Everyone knew what was coming: 360 kilometers of gravel." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-heroes-the-traka-360)

**Jan Stöckli [ELITE]:** "Wednesday, April 30: That evening, we were invited to the SRAM Athletes Dinner – amazing food and good chats. The excitement was definitely building." (https://www.tudorprocycling.com/post/gravel-heroes-the-traka-360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "Girona is a playground for cyclists, and everywhere I looked, I saw riders who looked stronger, faster, more prepared. Pro-level kit, pristine bikes, lean bodies, and focused expressions. It's easy to feel out of place in that environment." (https://benturneradventure.mykajabi.com/blog/traka360)

**Ben Turner [COMPETITIVE]:** "Thankfully, Girona came through. Jordi, a fellow Vielo rider and an absolute hero in cycle clothing, came to my rescue with the exact Vielo parts I needed.
