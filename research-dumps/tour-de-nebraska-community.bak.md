## Community Feel & Atmosphere

Tour de Nebraska is explicitly described as "like a family reunion on wheels" with strong emphasis on loyalty and repeat participation. The event is intentionally limited to 500 riders to "allow for meaningful interaction between riders and the quaint communities we visit" and to provide "excellent service to each and every cyclist." (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/)

The event offers scholarships and discounts including: $30 loyalty discount for past riders, $100 off for all students under 30 years of age (before end of year), aimed at creating a multi-generational "Tour de Nebraska family atmosphere." (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/)

Founded in 1988, the tour emphasizes a no-pressure experience: "You are truly on your own—no schedules, no deadlines, and no kids (unless you want to bring them)!" (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/)

The gravel adventure component explicitly states "This is not a race, it's a tour" with oasis-style regroup points approximately every 10 miles. (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/gravelroad-adventure.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Main Tour:** 5-day circle tour (Wednesday-Sunday) covering approximately 250 miles total, with daily mileage ranging from 45-70 miles. Routes described as going through "beautiful, scenic rural Nebraska" in east central Nebraska. (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/ and https://www.nancecountyjournal.com/news/tour-de-nebraska)

**2024 Route:** Springview • Bassett • Ainsworth • Valentine (https://bellbike.clubexpress.com/content.aspx?page_id=4091&club_id=489982&item_id=2201475)

**2026 Route:** Hartington - Vermillion - Yankton, described as "Fresh and new space to explore!" (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/)

**Gravel Adventure Option:** 2-day add-on limited to first 30 riders. Routes feature "incredible views along the Missouri River" with "some challenging climbs on both days." Minimum average pace requirement of 10 mph to ensure support crews can reach designated locations. (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/gravelroad-adventure.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Gravel Adventure [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** Riders expected to maintain minimum 10 mph average pace. Support provided with oasis-style regroup points approximately every 10 miles with water, snacks, and hydration mix. Routes subject to changes. (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/gravelroad-adventure.html)

The main tour is described as "an especially perfect multi-day tour for first-timers or seasoned veterans" with the 45-70 mile daily distances, suggesting accessible pacing for recreational riders. (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**[UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** The event has been celebrating "10 years of hosting a limited gravel adventure," suggesting both gravel and road bikes are appropriate depending on route chosen. (https://www.tourdenebraska.com/gravelroad-adventure.html)
