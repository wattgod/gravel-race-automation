# Trans Am Bike Race - Comprehensive Research

## Overview

The Trans Am Bike Race (TABR) is the premier self-supported ultra-distance cycling race across the United States. As of 2023, the event has been rebranded as **Trans Am Bike Nonstop** and is operated by Ride Yr Bike LLC, run exclusively by Nathan Jones.

At approximately 4,200-4,300 miles (6,800 km), it is one of the longest ultra-distance bike races in the world, traversing the entire continental United States from the Pacific to the Atlantic coast.

## Course Details

### Distance
- **Total Distance:** Approximately 4,182-4,300 miles (6,735-6,900 km)
- **Route Version Variations:** Different years/routes may vary slightly (e.g., 2024 route: 4,287.8 miles)

### Elevation
- **Total Elevation Gain:** Approximately 165,000-183,920 feet
- **Highest Point:** Hoosier Pass, Colorado at 11,542 feet (3,518 m)
- **Terrain Challenge:** Virginia has more total elevation gain than any other state on the route

### Route Characteristics
- **Surface:** Approximately 90% paved roads
- **States Crossed (10):** Oregon, Idaho, Montana, Wyoming, Colorado, Kansas, Missouri, Illinois, Kentucky, Virginia

### Terrain Breakdown by Region

#### Rocky Mountains (Oregon to Colorado)
- Long but generally not steep passes
- River valleys with some flat stretches
- Highest pass at Hoosier Pass (11,542 ft)
- Snow possible at any time at high elevations
- Central/Eastern Oregon: dry, mountainous - carry extra water

#### Great Plains (Kansas)
- Generally flat terrain
- Constant wind, potential dust storms on high plains
- Beautiful but monotonous
- City park camping is common

#### Ozarks and Mid-West (Missouri, Illinois)
- Roller-coaster terrain
- Short, steep climbs
- More developed services

#### Appalachian Mountains (Kentucky, Virginia)
- Much harder than Rockies despite lower elevation
- Steep, roller-coaster grades
- 15,000 feet MORE climbing in last 1,150 miles than first 1,150 miles
- Virginia has most total elevation gain of any state

## Cutoff Times

**There is NO finish time cutoff!**

The Trans Am Bike Race/Nonstop has no hard time limit. The checkpoint format introduced in recent years was designed to foster safer pacing without imposing time cutoffs.

### Typical Finishing Times
- **Record:** 16 days, 9 hours, 56 minutes (Abdullah Zeinab, 2019)
- **2025 Winning Time:** 14 days, 13 hours, 2 minutes (Jonas Majerski)
- **Average Completion:** Approximately 20 days
- **Racing Pace:** Generally under 35 days
- **Touring Pace:** Over 35 days
- **Range:** 17 to 50+ days

## Aid Station Information

**There are NO official aid stations.**

This is a fully self-supported race. All provisions must be obtained from commercial sources along the route.

### Self-Support Rules
- All food, accommodation, repairs must be purchased from commercial sources
- No private resupply or private lodging allowed
- No pre-arranged support
- No mailing packages ahead (caching)
- No support from friends, family, or other racers
- No drafting or pacing by non-participants
- Services must be equally available to all participants

### What Riders CAN Do
- Resupply food/equipment at commercial stores
- Rent hotel/motel rooms
- Launder clothing at laundromats
- Service bikes at commercial bike shops
- Accept help from "trail angels" if equally available to all

### Services Along the Route

#### Oregon Coast to Kansas
- Northern Oregon: flush with camping and services (tourist area)
- Central/Eastern Oregon: sparse services, carry extra water
- Montana (Lost Trail Pass to Dillon): sparse services
- Wyoming (Lander to Rawlins): sparse services
- Near Yellowstone/Grand Teton: increased options
- Kansas: city park camping common, friendly to cyclists

#### Missouri to Virginia
- Food, water, overnight accommodations abundant
- Missouri: roller-coaster terrain through Ozarks
- Kentucky: ferry across Ohio River, city park camping
- Virginia: Troutville Fire Department offers showers/laundry for cyclists

### Trail Angel Culture
- Long history of cyclist support along TransAmerica Trail
- Warm Showers app for homestays
- Many towns known for hospitality
- Adventure Cycling maps list official services

## Accommodation and Travel Logistics

### Pre-Race (Astoria, Oregon)

#### Getting to Astoria
- **Nearest Major Airport:** Portland International Airport (PDX) - approximately 100 miles
- **Transportation:** Rental car, shuttle services, or cycling from Portland
- **Bike Shipping:** Ship bike ahead to local bike shop or hotel

#### Pre-Race Activities
- Virtual briefing (day before race)
- On-site bike inspection available near start line
- Registration process primarily virtual

### During the Race

#### Accommodation Options
1. **Hotels/Motels:** Available in most towns along route
2. **City Parks:** Common camping option, especially in Kansas and Missouri
3. **National/State Parks:** Available near Yellowstone, Grand Teton
4. **Cyclist Hostels:** Some towns have dedicated facilities
5. **Commercial Campgrounds:** Throughout route
6. **Bivy/Wild Camping:** Where legal

#### Key Town Services

**Oregon:**
- Astoria: Full services (start)
- Mitchell: Checkpoint 1 location

**Montana:**
- Missoula: Adventure Cycling headquarters, "cyclist's lounge," full services

**Wyoming:**
- Moran: Checkpoint 2 location
- Near Yellowstone/Grand Teton: Increased services

**Kansas:**
- State intentionally caters to cyclists
- Water, showers, bike tools, Wi-Fi, charging stations
- Guaranteed cyclist campsites

**Kentucky:**
- Ferry across Ohio River
- Rolling farmland and woodlands
- Berea: Gateway to Appalachians

**Virginia:**
- Troutville Fire Department: Showers, laundry, camping space for cyclists
- Full services approaching Yorktown

### Post-Race Logistics
- Return transportation from Yorktown, VA or Washington D.C.
- Nearest major airports: Norfolk (ORF), Richmond (RIC), or Washington D.C. area (DCA, IAD, BWI)
- Consider shipping bike home

### International Rider Considerations
- Must secure U.S. visa independently
- Plan for extended stay (3-6 weeks minimum)
- Consider time zone adjustments
- Cell phone/data coverage along route varies

## Unique Features and Characteristics

### What Makes Trans Am Unique

#### The Longest of Its Kind
At approximately 4,300 miles, Trans Am is one of the longest ultra-distance bike races in the world, significantly longer than events like:
- Transcontinental Race (~4,000 km / 2,500 miles)
- Tour Divide (~2,700 miles)

#### Pure Self-Sufficiency Philosophy
Unlike Race Across America (RAAM) which allows support crews, Trans Am enforces complete self-sufficiency:
- No support vehicles
- No pre-arranged aid
- No private lodging
- No drafting
- All services must be commercially available to everyone

#### Historic Route
- Based on Adventure Cycling Association's TransAmerica Trail
- Created for 1976 Bikecentennial (America's 200th birthday celebration)
- Nearly 50-year cycling heritage
- Rich network of trail angels and cyclist-friendly services

#### Non-Stop Format
- Clock runs continuously from start to finish
- No stages, no designated rest periods
- Riders choose their own riding/sleeping strategy
- Pure individual time trial format

#### No Time Cutoff
- Riders can finish at their own pace
- Racing vs. touring pace distinction
- Encourages participation from broader skill range

#### Mixed Terrain Option (Trans Am Bike Nonstop)
- Hybrid of paved TransAmerica Trail and mixed-terrain Bike Nonstop US route
- Choice of classic (Yorktown) or modern (D.C.) finish
- Designed to use lower traffic roads and cycling infrastructure

### Event Philosophy and Culture

#### Origins
- Born from ultra-endurance bikepacking community
- Techniques and ethics based on events like Tour Divide
- Middle ground between supported RAAM and off-road Tour Divide

#### Community Spirit
- Strong dotwatching culture (following via GPS trackers)
- Trail angel network
- Post-race fellowship
- Memorial for Mike Hall continues at events

### Technical Requirements

#### Mandatory Equipment
- Satellite GPS tracker (SPOT or Garmin inReach)
- 10-minute tracking interval minimum
- Lights (front and rear)
- Helmet
- Insurance coverage

#### Bike Specifications
- Any type of bicycle allowed
- Recumbent bicycles permitted
- Velomobiles have been allowed (controversial in 2018)
- Road bikes most common for paved route option

### Challenges and Risks

#### Physical Demands
- 250+ miles per day at racing pace
- Sleep deprivation (top racers sleep 3-4 hours per night)
- Extreme temperature variations (desert heat to mountain snow)
- Altitude sickness risk in Colorado

#### Common DNF Reasons
- Injuries
- Mechanical failures
- Exhaustion
- Medical issues
- Mental fatigue

#### Completion Statistics
- Typical completion rate: 50-70%
- 2017: 70% completion (175/250)
- 2018: 50% completion (75/150)
