## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Vincent Nadon (TCR No.6, 2018):**
"Instead of aiming for a specific ranking, I decided to focus on experiencing the journey. Otherwise, I would have been anxious the whole time, unable to appreciate what I was doing... Mistakes are human, and they are part of the race. I told myself I just wanted to visit Europe by bike and reach the finish line in one piece." (panoramacycles.com)

"During this ride, I cried tears of joy for completing this difficult challenge, which marked a turning point in my health (after being diagnosed with Crohn's disease a few months prior). I was proud of pushing my limits, despite the chronic illness I didn't want to be defined or handicapped by." (panoramacycles.com)

**Martin Cox (TCR 2015):**

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Martin Cox (TCR 2015):**
- Swapped to **Schwalbe Tubeless One in 28mm** after puncture issues previous year: "they coped eminently well – the one issue I had was from a big glass patch in Bosnia which damaged the tyre too much for the sealant, however I put my spare inner tube in and was away again... after this experience, I cant see myself going back to tubes again!" (road.cc)
- Used **Shimano Hydraulic braking with Ultegra Di2**: "Hydraulic discs = my new favourite thing!... I never had any issues with discs in the wet, and it rained a great deal the first couple of days." (road.cc)
- **Shutter Precision SP8 dynamo hub paired with Sinewave Cycles Revolution unit**: "As long as I was riding faster than 13km/h it would give a steady power output... below 13km/h and it was no joy – the lesson being to ensure everything is fully charged before you hit the mountains!" (road.cc)
- Used **Rapha Aerosuit**: "combining lightweight, ventilation, and performance – very much recommended!" (road.cc)
- **Alpkit Numo mat** for sleeping: "great bit of kit which did a much better job of keeping me warm at nights... an inflatable mat adds 15 minutes to a sleep break, and the act of blowing I found had the effect of waking me up" (road.cc)

**Vincent Nadon (TCR No.6, 2018):**
- **Panorama Cycles Katahdin**: "Even with the luggage on the bike for crossing Europe, its handling on descents was really top-notch! Although I did most of the race on 32mm road tires, the Katahdin also allowed me to use gravel tires for the rougher sections." (panoramacycles.com)
- Used **double chainring and large cassette (11-42T)** for wide gear range (panoramacycles.com)
- **Clip-on aero bars**: "a must to have more position options and maintain comfort over long distances" (panoramacycles.com)
- **Apidura Expedition and Race series**: 14L + 2L + 4L (panoramacycles.com)
- "I had a relatively low iron level... so he prescribed Feramax" for Crohn's disease management (panoramacycles.com)

**Guy Bowden (TCR 2023):**
- Brought gravel bike with **42mm tires**: "Having brought my gravel bike with some 42mm tires all the way from Belgium with me, I was looking forward to the challenge and enjoyed the entire 40km [Albanian parcours], passing quite a few riders who had to nurse their bikes across the rough terrain." (muchbetteradventures.com)
- Both tires "blew off the rims in quick succession covering me and my bike in tubeless sealant after I had accidentally over-inflated them" (muchbetteradventures.com)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Croatian inland route (TCR 2014):**
"The inland roads were better than we'd ever imagined – easy riding, with a gentle tailwind, and almost empty of traffic because there was a new motorway nearby that most of the vehicles were using... After summiting, we entered another valley and saw the most awesome sight in front of us – a gentle downhill that went on for what looked like 10 or 15 km. It was truly bike touring heaven." (ridefar.info)

**Val Mora off-road option (TCR 2015):**
"Either go over the Stelvio pass, or take the off-road option through Val Mora, which would cut out over 2,000m of climbing, but would be slower going due to the rocky terrain. As a mountain biker at heart, this was an easy choice." (road.cc)

**Passo della Spluga (TCR 2015):**
"A super fun, technical descent from the Passo della Spluga with it's 50+ switchbacks and numerous narrow tunnels." (road.cc)

**Col della Assietta (TCR 2015):**
"Picture a mountain bike track, descending off a mountain, on a road bike... the brakes brought me back with ease!" (road.cc)

**Albanian SH36 parcours (TCR 2023):**
"The third mandatory parcours was a full on dirt track affair: the SH36 'road' to Peshkopi, in northeastern Albania. This was the first real test of bike setup and off-road handling. It would punish those who brought pure road bikes with them, and reward those who opted for a more gravel oriented set-up... I enjoyed the entire 40km, passing quite a few riders who had to nurse their bikes across the rough terrain." (muchbetteradventures.com)

**Belgium start:**
"Fire torches 🔥, bell man 🔔 and slippery cobblestones on the climb. One lap around the Mur, then on the final section of the first Parcours somebody ended up blocking the way so I had to unclip. Unfortunately, I couldn't get traction with my road shoes on the slippery 🧼 cobblestones in the steepest section so my foot went under the bike and my knee scrapped the sidewalk." (thevelocitynerd.com)

**Gravel sections (TCR No.9, 2023):**
Vincent Nadon: "After about 100km I started to doubt my itinerary which seemed to lead me systematically to weird gravel trails where I didn't see many riders, and sometimes no trails at all 🚧. I felt I was losing time, going a bit slower in offroad tracks and also re-routing on the fly." (thevelocitynerd.com)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Sleep strategy (Martin Cox, TCR 2015):**
"I had come across Alpkit Numo mat... adds 15 minutes to a sleep break, and the act of blowing I found had the effect of waking me up – so perhaps it's better (in a RACE) to forego the inflatable and go for closed cell, or nothing at all – and just toughen up for the few nights you need it before the nights warm up?" (road.cc)

"I had done some decent miles in the latter part of the spring and early summer... a 900km ride two weeks before the race, allowing me to test both my legs and my kit." (road.cc)

**Pacing insights (TCR 2014):**
"We had a fair amount of holiday traffic... we'd already planned to take the inland route instead of the coast road once we had the option, and our 30 km on the coast told us that this was a good plan." (ridefar.info)

"I suggested that once we'd done 275 km (which was my new daily goal), then we should look for the next good place to stop." (ridefar.info)

"I was feeling great, and was sensing that riding with two other guys was hindering progress a bit because they weren't always riding as fast as I would and stops always take more time when there are more people. I therefore decided to press on without them." (ridefar.info)

**Marginal gains philosophy (Martin Cox):**
"An average of 5 minutes per hour off the bike adds up to 2 hours per day, which over the race length can add a full day to your ride! So efficiency savings are certainly the way to go!" (road.cc)

**Mindset shift (Vincent Nadon, 2018):**
"After pushing harder in the Swiss mountains to improve my position in the race, I completely missed the first checkpoint! I was only 2 km away... I ended up losing an hour trying to find the Checkpoint 1 hotel... Frustrated with my situation, I finally changed my mindset and tried to enjoy the journey as much as possible... Instead of aiming for a specific ranking, I decided to focus on experiencing the journey." (panoramacycles.com)

**Christoph Strasser (TCR No.9, 2023) — winning strategy:**
"He covered the 3,950 km / 2,454 miles in 8 days, 16 hours, 30 minutes total, riding for 84% of that time and covering an average daily distance of 454km / 282 miles." (strava.com)

Last place finisher comparison: "James Vernon, reached the finish line in almost exactly 15 days, riding for 60% of that time and with an average daily distance of 264km / 164 miles." (strava.com)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical issues:**
Martin Cox (2014): "Last years race didn't go well for me, multiple punctures saw me take 36 hours just to get to Paris, and from then on it didn't go as well as I'd planned." (road.cc)

"Multiple punctures causing no end of grief – I flew down the hills, and didn't sustain any troubles at all [with tubeless]." (road.cc)

"I had to make a few equipment adjustments, such as a tool bag that fell off and a 3D-printed mount for my Garmin Varia that gave way under the vibrations of the Flemish cobblestones." (road.cc)

**Navigation problems:**
Vincent Nadon: "My phone ran out of battery and even though I saved back all segments of my route as road rides plus looked for kinks in the route, the GPS got me to a location that wasn't the first checkpoint (CP1)... What the f**k Komoot?... I probably lost all the gains in position I made throughout the day." (thevelocitynerd.com)

**Mental challenges:**
"The first thoughts of scratching entered my head... I was barely 12 hours into a two week race." (road.cc)

Vincent Nadon: "The worst was after the race when I had a hemorrhoid flare-up. Probably a reaction to the reduction in effort, possible dehydration in the last days, and the beginning of relaxation from the stress/adrenaline of the race." (panoramacycles.com)

**Heat exhaustion:**
"The mid-afternoon climb up to the CP3 in Burrel was brutal. I lost count of the number of stops I had to make to buy water, coke and ice-cream along the way. I was stopping at nearly every roadside water spring I passed." (muchbetteradventures.com)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Start atmosphere (Martin Cox, 2015):**
"There was masses of waiting, either soaking up the atmosphere or getting more anxious about the coming night. There was a lot of checking out each others bike – having your twitter tag on the bike seemed to have paid off as a name badge, with several people recognising my bike and name from this site. As we all assembled on the Muur at 23.30 the tension was evident, although the mood was happy there was clearly a lot of thought being given to the hours and days ahead... for most of us, it was a trip in to the great unknown." (road.cc)
