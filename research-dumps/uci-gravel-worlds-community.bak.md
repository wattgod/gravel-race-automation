# UCI GRAVEL WORLD CHAMPIONSHIPS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Joy A. Franco, PhD [COMPETITIVE]:** "An epic adventure warrants a proper report. Qualifying for and getting to race my age group at UCI Gravel World Championships this year, representing my country as a member of Team USA, was the privilege of a lifetime... This race was unlike anything I've ever done in my 18 years of bike racing experience in all the best ways possible and I'd do it all over again in a heartbeat." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Joy A. Franco, PhD [COMPETITIVE]:** "When we hit that first real climb in Raar, the first real attack came and shattered things apart. It was the last I would see of the leaders as my legs imploded... For the next couple of hours it would be repeated efforts to catch onto trains of riders as they passed me, struggling each time to maintain their pace through my pain." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Joy A. Franco, PhD [COMPETITIVE]:** "The cramping was absolutely due to a lack of high-end fitness (more on that in a moment) and not because of a lack of electrolytes... Within thirty minutes of racing I was starving, and that really shouldn't happen." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Paige [ELITE]:** "I participated in USAC Gravel Nationals last month and as many of you have heard me say, this was the safest gravel event that I have ever done... With that, similar rules for road closures, standards and safety protocols were in place at UCI Gravel Worlds. The course is closed to traffic and I never once felt like I had to worry about oncoming traffic, stop signs or traffic lights interfering with my race." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

**Paige [ELITE]:** "I was really disappointed in the fact that the UCI did not think through or recognize that the men's age group fields would impact the women's race... From my personal perspective, it did impact my race. After the first few climbs, you entered a longer 30km section of flatter terrain... About 10km into this 30km flatter stretch, a large group of age group males caught up to us hauling. A lot of the women jumped the wheels and suddenly our group became a smash fest of who could hang onto the guy's wheels to get pulled along and it was strung out. It felt weird." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

**Paige [ELITE]:** "The course was really a highlight of the weekend. This race perfectly paired so many disciplines into one. It was like a road race paired with a mountain bike race as well as some tight loose gravel sectors. And let's not forget the plethora of corners and turns and narrow Italian alleys…err I mean roads." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

**Marianne Vos [ELITE]:** "I didn't think about the previous 13 to be honest. Of course, it [gravel] is something fairly new but it's a World Championships so you try to do your best... There was a pretty big group but then on a harder part Lotte set a really high pace and then we were the four of us, with Lorena Wiebes and [Italy's] Soraya Paladin, but then with another hard part we were left with two." (https://www.velouk.net/2024/10/07/report-result-world-gravel-championships/)

**Mathieu van der Poel [ELITE]:** "It's super nice to add another rainbow to the collection, in another discipline... I wanted to make the race as hard as possible because I knew with a big group it could be a difficult situation for me, so I tried to put everybody on the limit. When Florian Vermeersch went, we had good cooperation. It was a super hard race, but I enjoyed it and I'm, of course, super happy with the title." (https://www.velouk.net/2024/10/07/report-result-world-gravel-championships/)

**Daniel Oss [ELITE]:** "We are here really to discover, I think, a new world. A new way of riding a bike. Nobody knows at the start line what is going to happen, and we discover kilometer by kilometer. The rule is attack, because when you don't know what you can do, just attack and look at what is going on." (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/daniel-oss-uci-gravel-world-072614775.html)

**Daniel Oss [ELITE]:** "I was in shape because it is the last period of the season. I stopped in July and then I work out for the last races. This is new. I had really nice company during this long, long breakaway, I think it was the longest in my life. But he [Vermeersch] was stronger, just this. So he deserved it." (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/daniel-oss-uci-gravel-world-072614775.html)

**Tom Pidcock [ELITE]:** Admitted before the start he was "coming into this race as a novice of the format." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025/elite-men/live-report/)

(https://www.bikeradar.com/features/pro-bike/2025-uci-gravel-world-championships-pro-bike-gallery)

**Tom Pidcock [ELITE]:** Pinarello Dogma GR, SRAM Red XPLR AXS groupset, Vision SC 48 i25 wheels, Vittoria Terreno Pro T30 tyres in **only 35mm width**. (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/pro-bike/2025-uci-gravel-world-championships-pro-bike-gallery)

**Lorena Wiebes [ELITE] - 2025 Winner:** Specialized S-Works Crux, SRAM Red XPLR AXS groupset with large 1x aero chainring, Roval's Rapide integrated cockpit, Roval Terra CLX II gravel wheels, Specialized Pathfinder TLR tyres. (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/pro-bike/2025-uci-gravel-world-championships-pro-bike-gallery)

**Marianne Vos [ELITE] - 2025 Second Place:** Cervélo Áspero-5 with integrated handlebar from Cervélo R5, **"mullet" tire setup: grippier Vittoria Terreno T70 45mm up front, slick 42mm-wide Corsa Pro Control TLR out back** for lower rolling resistance. Used Gravaa KAPS hubs allowing tire pressure adjustment while riding via bar-mounted remote. (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/pro-bike/2025-uci-gravel-world-championships-pro-bike-gallery)

**Joy A. Franco, PhD [COMPETITIVE]:** Pre-race nutrition failure: "I didn't realize it would be so hard to find gluten free carbohydrates in France and the Netherlands... I was limited to smaller items like gluten free meusli bars and sandwich bread. It's just not the same." At home she relies on calrose rice with maple syrup and Greek yogurt. (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Note on tire choices:** Elite riders in 2025 predominantly used relatively narrow (35-45mm), fast-rolling gravel tires like Continental Terra Speed TR and Vittoria Terreno, not XC mountain bike tires, due to the hardpack nature of the courses. (https://www.bikeradar.com/features/pro-bike/2025-uci-gravel-world-championships-pro-bike-gallery)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**2025 Netherlands Course (Limburg):** "The terrain was fast and tidy, roughly 70 percent unpaved by the organisers' definition, though much of it resembled firm farm roads or compact dirt. There was little of the unpredictable, loose, or technical surface that defines gravel racing elsewhere." 50km circuit, 131km for elite women, 180km for elite men. (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025)

**Joy A. Franco [COMPETITIVE] - 2024 Course:** "The first few miles of the race were on city roads as we made our way to the dirt. It felt much like a road race... As we hit the double track that weaves between plots of farmland things became more strung out but manageable. All of the women in the field were excellent bike handlers and never once did things feel sketch." First major climb was in Raar, with "a steep dirt downhill with washboard section littered with water bottles from the groups ahead." Course passed through start area twice, providing two opportunities to drop out. (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Paige [ELITE] - Italy Course:** "This race perfectly paired so many disciplines into one. It was like a road race paired with a mountain bike race as well as some tight loose gravel sectors... There was a longer 30km section of flatter terrain. At this point, I had a solid group of about 8-10 women. We had somewhat of a rotation going... About 10km into this 30km flatter stretch, a large group of age group males caught up to us." Course included "very rocky dried out creek bed," "tight loose gravel sectors," and "plethora of corners and turns and narrow Italian alleys." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

**2025 Course Description:** "The course is closed to traffic and I never once felt like I had to worry about oncoming traffic, stop signs or traffic lights interfering with my race." Multiple sources note courses were "relatively flat" with 800m climbing for men (2022), course was "50% gravel/off-road, rest on hardcore." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap, https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/uci-gravel-world-championships-10-riders-to-watch-in-the-mens-race/)

**2025 Limburg Terrain Style:** "The climbs are not huge, in fact, they rarely top 150m in altitude, but there are a lot of them, which take their toll, especially when ridden at near pro road race pace... It's nonstop in terms of intensity and with ever-shifting wind direction." Terrain described as "fast and open gravel farm roads, pathways, and with a smattering of semi-technical forested climbs and descents." (https://off.road.cc/content/feature/uci-gravel-world-championship-2025-everything-you-need-to-know-16933)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Joy A. Franco [COMPETITIVE]:** Race duration: 4h 52min. Blew up approximately 25 minutes into the race on first major climb: "By the time we hit that main climb in Raar for the first time, I had already put in about 4-5 'all watts combined' efforts to stay with the group. That's not much compared to a typical cyclocross race, but it was a lot compared to what I've done over the past two months." Strategy shifted to "stay with that group. Stay on that wheel" for mid-race survival. In final 10km, "kept an eye on my power meter readings to help myself push harder than I felt possible." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Joy A. Franco [COMPETITIVE] - Nutrition Strategy:** "Early in the season my Cycle-Smart coaches made it very simple for me. We broke down the numbers for how many calories I could actually digest during a race, what that meant in terms of carbs, and how to time the delivery. On my own, I tested what foods worked best during a race over multiple events and came prepared with exactly the race foods I needed to prevent bonking." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Elite Race Dynamics [ELITE]:** "The race is very much on. Multiple attacks are made out of the peloton." Early selection happens quickly - in 2025 men's race, peloton reduced to about 100 riders with 150km still to ride, then down to about 60 riders, eventually to about 25 riders, finally to about 20 riders. Winning move (Vermeersch) went approximately 80km from finish. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025/elite-men/live-report/)

**Daniel Oss [ELITE]:** "The rule is attack, because when you don't know what you can do, just attack and look at what is going on... I was in shape because it is the last period of the season. I stopped in July and then I work out for the last races." He and Vermeersch attacked approximately 80km into the race and "rode together for 154km" before Vermeersch dropped him 8km from finish. (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/daniel-oss-uci-gravel-world-072614775.html)

**Paige [ELITE] - Team Tactics:** "All of the women riders opted for the team tactics, and that spoke volume to the group of riders we had with us... My assignment (in a perfect world) would be to help Lauren in the flatter sections during the middle part of the course but early on I could see we needed to get her to the front. I started to be aggressive to move up... Going from the very back of the start to first and second wheel in the peloton took a pretty large effort." She did a "hero pull" to bridge Crystal back to front group, expending massive energy. (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Joy A. Franco [COMPETITIVE] - Cramping:** "Not long after the muscles in my legs started to get torn apart by cramps, which was only halfway through the race... When I got out of the saddle they reacted with anger, forcing me to cry out in pain. I had to back off." Cramps were "absolutely due to a lack of high-end fitness... and not because of a lack of electrolytes." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Preparation Issues:** Joy lost "about two weeks of essential training" one month before the race due to illness, then was "on honeymoon trying to find time for rides amidst just living up tourist life in Paris... It was far from ideal race prep." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Mechanical Issues:** During 2025 men's race, "Politt's dropped, and lost more ground when needing to briefly stop to sort a gearing issue." (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025/elite-men/live-report/)

**Tim Melier [ELITE]:** "Pulled out of the race before it began" in 2025. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025/elite-men/live-report/)

**Romain Bardet [ELITE]:** "Despite impressing on the gravel circuit since his retirement from the road earlier this year, including a few wins, the Frenchman won't be competing for a high finish at his Worlds debut" - dropped from peloton early with 150km to go. (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025/elite-men/live-report/)

**Crashes:** "I was caught behind multiple crashes, and Crystal was as well" during early race positioning. (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Joy A. Franco [COMPETITIVE]:** "Qualifying for and getting to race my age group at UCI Gravel World Championships this year, representing my country as a member of Team USA, was the privilege of a lifetime... This race was unlike anything I've ever done in my 18 years of bike racing experience in all the best ways possible." Had USAC support at feed zones and transportation help. "All riders were active participants and it felt respectful." (https://neurojoy.net/blog/ucigravelworldsracereport/)

**Paige [ELITE] - Media Coverage Disappointment:** "Rather than doing an entirely separate blog about this, I think many of you should just go listen to the Girls Gone Gravel Podcast that came out this week where we talk about this specifically. I will be honest in admitting that I never even thought it wouldn't be broadcasted, it was more of who was going to broadcast it. So when the news came out of no coverage, it was really disappointing." European women's road racing noted as having "a long way to go in gender equity." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

**Safety & Organization:** "This was the safest gravel event that I have ever done. Granted, my racing history only spans 2 years but it's still worth noting. With that, similar rules for road closures, standards and safety protocols were in place at UCI Gravel Worlds. The course is closed to traffic." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap)

**Participation Scale:** "Around 2,500 riders took part across all categories, from WorldTour professionals to age-group qualifiers" in 2025. "A total of 23 titles of UCI Gravel World Champion were awarded across the weekend with a record participation: 2,613 riders registered in the different men's and women's age groups." (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025, https://www.velouk.net/2024/10/07/report-result-world-gravel-championships/)

**"Spirit of Gravel" Debate:** Community discussions note tension between traditional gravel culture and UCI formalization. One comment about Lutsenko: "In many ways Lutsenko is the antithesis of whatever the 'spirit of gravel' is. You just can't imagine him rocking up in a camper, busting out a BBQ with Laurens ten Dam, and talking about pale ale brewing with Peter Stetina." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/uci-gravel-world-championships-10-riders-to-watch-in-the-mens-race/)

**Criticism Theme:** "The event once again raised familiar issues about what a 'Gravel World Championship' should look like. Not enough gravel. Riders and media described the course as too manicured and too much like a road race... WorldTour dominance. The top ten in both elite races were made up entirely of WorldTour riders... Dedicated gravel specialists were largely absent from the fight for medals." (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025)

**Age-Group Interference Issue:** "I was really disappointed in the fact that the UCI did not think through or recognize that the men's age group fields would impact the women's race... The men did not impact the podium racers, but I did hear it likely impacted 10/11th placing and positions after that (there was a bridge back from an amateur male)." Multiple riders and commentators called for "stricter separation — either through time gaps or completely separate race days." (https://www.paigepowered.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-race-recap, https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025)

**For Mass Participants:** "However, for the mass participants it provides not only a focus for their season but a reward for all the sacrifices they made to get there." (https://www.progravelmagazine.com/post/uci-gravel-world-championships-2025)
