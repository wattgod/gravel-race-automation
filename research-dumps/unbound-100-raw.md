## Event Overview

Unbound Gravel (formerly known as Dirty Kanza) is the world's premier gravel cycling event, held annually in Emporia, Kansas. The event comprises gravel racing covering various distances (25, 50, 100, 200, and 350 miles) held during late spring on rural roads in the Flint Hills region of the Great Plains.

The **Unbound Gravel 100** is a 100-mile (160 km) race that was introduced in 2013. Added to the lineup as a launchpad to the 200-mile main stage event, the 100 quickly became a race in and of itself. It is perfect for those looking for a long day in the saddle, with all the challenges of Flint Hills gravel goodness, and just a bit less pain than the 200-mile event.

### Event History
- The event began with 34 participants in 2006 as "Dirty Kanza"
- Rebranded to "Unbound Gravel" in 2021
- The 100-mile distance was added in 2013
- Now the "World's Premier Gravel Event" with thousands of participants

## Course Details

### Distance
- **Official Name:** 100 miles
- **Actual Distance:** Approximately 103-108 miles (varies by year)
  - 2023: 103.1 miles
  - 2025: 108.5 miles

### Elevation
- **Total Climbing:** Approximately 3,600-4,373 feet (varies by year)
  - 2022: 1,914 ft climbing, max elevation 1,658 ft
  - 2023: 4,373 ft climbing
  - 2025: Approximately 3,600 ft climbing

### Terrain Characteristics
- **Surface:** Primarily gravel and dirt roads with minimal blacktop
- **Road Condition:** Many roads receive little to no maintenance and can be quite primitive
- **Gravel Type:** Notorious flint-strewn gravel that is brutal on tires
- **Course Format:** Single-loop format
- **Notable Features:**
  - Tire-slicing flint and chunky rocks
  - Washed-out gullies
  - Punchy climbs
  - Sun-drenched prairie
  - Rolling hills that are deceivingly challenging
  - Rivers to ford
  - Sticky "peanut butter" mud if it rains

### Route Direction
The 100-mile route leaves downtown Emporia under police escort, traveling **north** into Wabaunsee and Morris Counties. The routes pass through Lyon, Wabaunsee, and Morris counties before returning to Emporia.

### Weather Considerations
- Temperatures can exceed 100째F (38째C) in the afternoon
- Rain and hail possible
- Severe weather risk in Kansas during late spring
- 2023 was notable for extreme mud and weather conditions

## Location and Start/Finish

### Race Venue
- **City:** Emporia, Kansas, USA
- **Region:** Flint Hills, Great Plains
- **State:** Kansas

### Start Location
- **Address:** Granada Theatre, 807 Commercial Street, Downtown Emporia
- **Start Time:** 6:00 AM (for 200, 100, 50, and 25 mile events)
- **Format:** Mass start with police escort out of downtown

### Finish Location
- **Location:** Commercial Street, Downtown Emporia
- **Approach Route:**
  1. Final punchy climb on Highland Hill (tarmac) along Emporia State University
  2. Turn left on 12th Avenue for two blocks
  3. Turn right for the final straightaway on Commercial Street
- **Finish Line:** 700 & 800 blocks of Commercial Street

### Finish Line Experience
- Curb-to-curb finish chute on Commercial Street
- Final 1.5 miles barricaded for rider and spectator safety
- Finish line party, beer garden, and music starting at 10 AM
- Hosted by Emporia Main Street

## Registration and Entry

### Registration Process
The Unbound Gravel 100 is a **lottery-only event** due to high demand.

#### Lottery Timeline (2026)
- **Lottery Opens:** November 1 at 12:00 PM CT
- **Lottery Closes:** November 15 at 11:59 PM CT
- **Winners Announced:** November 21 via email
- **Registration:** Free to enter lottery; credit card charged only if accepted

#### Requirements
- **Minimum Age:** 14 years old
- **Entry Limit:** One entry per person
- **Credit Card:** Required at registration (charged only if selected)

### Categories
- Open (Men/Women)
- Single-speed (bicycles with a single gear ratio)
- Tandem (bicycles designed for two riders)
- Pro Rider classifications

### Alternative Ways to Gain Entry

1. **Volunteer Hours**
   - Volunteer 10+ hours at UNBOUND Gravel to skip the lottery
   - Friends and family can donate their volunteer hours toward your total

2. **Life Time Membership**
   - Club-Access membership for 6+ months prior to lottery
   - Eligible for early-access registration or guaranteed entry

3. **Charity Partners**
   - Choose a charity partner to support
   - Pay or fundraise for guaranteed entry
   - Limited spots available, first-come first-served
   - Cost includes guaranteed entry plus registration and service fees

4. **Training Camp**
   - UNBOUND Gravel Training Camp presented by Shimano
   - April 3-6, 2025 camp (dates vary by year)
   - Participation includes automatic race entry
   - Cost: $2,200 (includes entry to XL, 200, or 100)
   - Minimum $400 deposit required

5. **Qualifying Events**
   - Various qualifying events throughout the year
   - Each event has at least 50 qualifying spots
   - Half go to top riders in each category
   - Half awarded by random draw of eligible participants

6. **Coaching Packages**
   - Select coaching packages include guaranteed entry
   - Available for the race distance of your choice

## Cutoff Times

### Overall Time Limit
- Based on an average speed of **10 mph**
- For 100 miles, this equals approximately **10 hours**
- With a 6:00 AM start, finish cutoff is approximately 4:00 PM

### Checkpoint Cutoffs
- **Cutoff times are strictly enforced** for rider safety
- If you reach a checkpoint after the cutoff time:
  - Your time will not be scored
  - You will no longer be an official participant

#### Example Cutoff (2023)
- **Checkpoint 1 (Mile 79, Eureka, KS):** 2:00 PM

### Enforcement
- Any participant not reaching a checkpoint before the established arrival cutoff time will be pulled from the event
- Cutoff times are based on 10 mph average pace

## Aid Stations and Checkpoints

### Checkpoint Structure for 100-Mile
- **Number of Checkpoints:** 1 official checkpoint
- **Location:** Council Grove, Kansas
- **Distance:** Approximately mile 50 (mid-course)

### Checkpoint Services
- Neutral mechanics
- Neutral water
- Minimal nutrition items
- Support crew access point

### Support Crew Rules
- **Checkpoints are the ONLY locations** where support crews can assist riders
- Support crews are **NOT allowed on course** except to pick up an abandoning rider
- If a support crew vehicle is spotted on course, the rider will be **disqualified**

### Crew For Hire Program
- "Crew For Hire powered by GU" program available
- For riders without personal support crew
- Available for both 200 and 100 mile events

### Self-Sufficiency Requirements
The Flint Hills region is extremely remote:
- Rarely pass through any towns
- No convenience stores between checkpoints
- Riders must carry all supplies needed between checkpoints
- Participants are solely responsible for their personal well-being

### Allowed Support
- Athletes may help other athletes (mechanical, navigation, etc.)
- Resupply at local businesses/stores along the route is permitted
- "Neutral" support from local residents at stationary locations is allowed
  - Must be available to any other participant

| Airport | Distance | Details |

| **Wichita Mid-Continent Airport (ICT)** | ~100 miles | 10 carriers, 30 daily flights |

#### By Car
- Located midway between Topeka and Wichita on I-335
- Between Kansas City and Wichita on I-35
- Key crossroads location

#### Local Transportation
- **"Need A Ride?"** - Service to/from Wichita, Topeka, and Kansas City
- **LCAT (Lyon County Area Transportation)** - Local bus service within Emporia and Lyon County
- Limited public transport between airports and Emporia
- Facebook group for ride-sharing/transport help

### Accommodation Options

#### Lodging Challenges
- Emporia's population swells by ~50% during race weekend
- Extra 12,000+ people flood the town
- Hotels and B&Bs sell out months in advance
- Housing costs are notably high during race weekend

## Unique Features

### What Makes Unbound Gravel 100 Special

#### The Flint Hills Experience
1. **Notorious Terrain**
   - Brutally hard, flint-strewn gravel roads
   - Tire-slicing flint and chunky rocks
   - Sharp gravel that "eats tyres for breakfast"
   - Washed-out gullies and primitive roads

2. **Deceptive Difficulty**
   - No mountain peaks on the horizon
   - Relentless rolling hills
   - Elevation gain adds up significantly
   - Punchy climbs throughout

3. **Extreme Remoteness**
   - Rarely pass through towns
   - No convenience stores between checkpoints
   - True self-sufficiency required
   - "Do not call us" policy for breakdowns/injuries

4. **Variable Weather**
   - Can exceed 100째F (38째C)
   - Risk of rain, hail, and severe weather
   - "Peanut butter" mud in wet conditions
   - Rivers to ford

#### The Race Experience

1. **Mass Start Format**
   - Amateurs and elites roll out together
   - Creates unique atmosphere unlike any other race
   - Big-name pros line up alongside everyday gravel fans
   - Starts at 6 AM with police escort

2. **The Finish Line**
   - Iconic finish on Commercial Street
   - Final punchy climb on Highland Hill
   - Curb-to-curb finish chute
   - Huge crowds and celebration atmosphere

3. **Community Atmosphere**
   - "Like Glastonbury for bikes"
   - Town transforms for race weekend
   - Strong local support and hospitality
   - Finish line party with beer garden and music

#### The Challenge

1. **Stepping Stone Philosophy**
   - Designed as preparation for the 200-mile event
   - Still provides "all the challenges of Flint Hills gravel goodness"
   - "Just a bit less pain" than the 200

2. **Self-Reliance**
   - Only one checkpoint on the 100-mile course
   - Must carry supplies for 50+ miles between aid
   - Mechanical self-sufficiency crucial
   - Navigation skills important

3. **Competition Quality**
   - Attracts elite professional gravel racers
   - Highly competitive fields
   - Sprint finishes common among top contenders
