## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Neil Fortner (Wild Card Cycling, 2024):**
- "Unbound had been a bucket list item for me for a while, so I was stoked to finally get selected in the lottery this year for the 200. Knowing that it's basically a Pro Tour classic at this point, my only goal was to have a good race and beat the sun."
- On mile 113-148: "I became very concerned that I would be unable to finish due to this, and slowed down considerably... I was totally unable to eat anything, and barely able to drink."
- On the finish: "When I saw Highland Hill, a short steep climb in the city of Emporia, and saw the banner/arch at the top, I, in my half delirious state, assumed it was the finish line and sprinted up the hill at 585 W for 35 seconds then sat up. It took me about 10 seconds to realize my mistake."

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Kaw Reserve Road (Mile 27-28 area):**
- Neil Fortner: "Around 30 miles in we hit the first gnarly section, which had dirt, larger rocks, and uneven ground... I can see how they would be a problem for the elite riders though, riding fast in a tightly packed group."
- Bertn05: "When we hit Kaw road this proved to be the right strategy but I should have been even further ahead because around me they started to crash. 3 times I avoided these."
- "There were bottles everywhere and people pulled over to fix their bikes" (Reddit user, 2024)

**Divide Road (around Eskridge, mile ~40):**
- Reddit user (2024): "This was the roughest section of the day. Steep, rocky, pointy, and it went for a handful of miles. This was one of the few times a front suspension and wider tires would have really helped."
- Cyclingnews (2025): "At mile 40, over 150 riders hit Divide Road, a minimally maintained, rutted and muddy stretch infamous for derailing races"
- SILCA report (2024): "The lead group of four were still off the front... Mohorič's teammates off the back on Divide Rd and not together"

**Little Egypt Road:**
- Neil Fortner: "This segment included the famous Little Egypt Road, which was fun but challenging"
- Reddit user (2024): "After Volland was Little Egypt. This was rockier than Divide but only half the distance"
- Described as starting after Alma checkpoint around mile 71

**General Gravel Character:**
- Neil Fortner: "Almost all of the gravel outside of Lyon County (which contains Emporia) was rougher than that in Lyon County, with an occasional bedrock surface that was pretty interesting"
- Neil Fortner: "The first few miles were on fast, packed (even by Illinois standards) gravel"
- Mettle Cycling course description: "If it's not that, there's washout or exposed bedrock. In others, where Jeep drivers decided to tear it up while still wet, the giant ruts left over created literal gutters and curbs"

**Specific Hazards:**
- Neil Fortner: "At one point I had to lock up my wheels when the ride in front of me didn't see the rocks until the last second, and a couple times I had to bunny hop over an unexpected mini 'gap jump'"
- Creek crossings that "dumped daring riders" (Mettle Cycling, 2025)

**Highland Hill:**
- Neil Fortner: "When I saw Highland Hill, a short steep climb in the city of Emporia, and saw the banner/arch at the top, I, in my half delirious state, assumed it was the finish line"

**Lake Kahola area (near end):**

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Starting Position Strategy:**

*Be at the front early:*
- SomeMayoPlease: "My goal was to position myself well and avoid surging after corners, picking my own lines, and bombing it on every downhill. Mentally, I had marked Mile 28 as the first important point on the course, and I surged to make sure I led the entire pack."
- Bertn05: "Started at the first line aiming for 10 hours, I was immediately out in the front, planning to stay clear from most of the danger."
- Mettle Cycling: "My only concern is getting through the next 2 hours without a spell of bad luck or poor line choice... Maintaining a conservative mindset in most cases paid dividends to many"

*Being at back has consequences:*
- Neil Fortner: "By the time I got to the start chute it was packed full and I had to start at around the 15 hour (predicted) mark. The first few miles were on fast, packed... gravel, and speeds were fast even though I was trying to limit my effort."

**Pacing Philosophy:**

*Zone 2 all-day power:*
- SomeMayoPlease: "Being a well-studied Dylan Johnson follower, I know this race is all about pacing... From the moment I was on my own, for roughly six hours, I averaged 246 watts and my normalized was 255, showing how important it is to not surge on climbs and use momentum when climbing."
- Neil Fortner (miles 0-71): "I kept an eye on my average power and tried to tone it down whenever it got close to 200W"
- OBED Bikes: "If your goal is to finish strong, then your riding pace should feel almost too easy in the beginning... you want to be riding in the low-to-middle of your Zone 2—this is your ALL-DAY power"

*Common pacing mistakes:*
- Reddit user (2024): "One thing I noticed is how surgy most people seemed to be though. Push the uphill, then coast the downhill. I was aiming for consistency, so I'd get dropped going uphill, then catch them on the downhill."
- OBED Bikes: "The biggest mistake almost EVERY racer makes, even pros, is going out too hard"

**Group Dynamics:**

*Finding the right group:*
- SomeMayoPlease: "Coming out of Alma, I saw a small group up the road and a small group behind me. I kept a steady pace and got caught by the guys behind me, but they were riding too hard on the climbs, and I had to let them go."
- Reddit user (2024): "I joined up with a couple guys and we rode together until mile 134"
- OBED Bikes: "The 'right' group is one where you can maintain your all-day pace while staying sheltered behind other riders"

*Non-cooperative riders:*
- Bertn05: "We continue, we are getting close to the hills of Little Egypt, we catch more and more riders and drop them almost instantly... the last hill before Little Egypt we regroup again a bigger group and I am going immediately to the front. Putting my own pace and staying clear of trouble... but only one rider follows.. and following is all what he does"
- Bertn05 near finish: "right before the finish I pick two Age groupers who again dont take turns... yet one of the women warns me they are planning to sprint from behind"

**Technical Sections:**
- Neil Fortner: "I found myself looking forward to these sections, as I could generally ride clear of the group I was riding with and take my time working up to the next one, since it's much easier to ride through the rocks alone"
- Mettle Cycling: "When approaching technical sections, I gave plenty of room to other riders. If I had enough room, I typically rode to the front to choose my line and eliminate any chance of someone else influencing that decision"

**Aid Station Strategy:**

*Speed is important:*
- SomeMayoPlease: "Stopped time: 3 minutes and 22 seconds" over entire race
- Neil Fortner: "stopped briefly in Alma at mile 71... I rolled through the first water stop at mile 41"

*What worked:*
- Reddit user (2024): "At the aid station my wife had our car set up. I ate an Uncrustable, some wheat thins, swapped bottles and refilled my hydration pack. I reapplied sunscreen and chamois cream... I was in and out in five minutes."
- SomeMayoPlease: "ice socks, and I put three on myself at both aid stations, which was a huge game changer"
