## Course Details

### Distance and Elevation
- **Official Distance:** 350+ miles (approximately 352-358 miles depending on year)
- **Elevation Gain:** Approximately 13,500-14,500 feet (4,100-4,400 meters)
  - 2022: 14,219 feet over 352 miles
  - 2025: Approximately 13,450 feet over 358 miles

### Terrain
- Gravel roads through the Flint Hills region of east-central Kansas
- Sharp, tire-slicing limestone gravel
- Rolling prairie terrain with constant elevation changes
- Primarily single-loop format starting and ending in Emporia
- Not technical singletrack, but rough terrain with challenging line choices
- Roads not marked - GPS navigation required

### Course Direction
- Routes vary by year (north loop vs south loop)
- 2024-2025: Counter-clockwise, north loop extending beyond Interstate 70
- Course passes through small Kansas towns for resupply opportunities

### 2025 Route Specifics
- First 45 miles share track with 200-mile course
- Moves away from 200-mile course beyond Eskridge
- Proceeds north to Maple Hill
- Passes through Alma after approximately 110 miles
- Passes through Pillsbury Crossing (most northerly section)
- Heads south and reconnects with 200-mile course for approximately 75 miles
- Through Council Grove, then diverges from 200-mile route

### Towns Along the Route (Approximate)
- Eskridge (Mile ~40-45)
- Maple Hill
- Alma (Mile ~110-118)
- Alta Vista (Mile ~112)
- Pillsbury Crossing
- Council Grove (Mile ~148)

## Registration and Entry Fees

### Entry Fee Structure
Based on recent years:
- **XL (350 miles):** $250 (2023 pricing)
- Historical: $200 (2022 pricing)
- Entry fee includes Trackleaders registration for GPS tracking

### Registration Process
1. **Lottery System:** Registration is via lottery (not first-come, first-served)
2. **Free to Enter Lottery:** No cost to submit name for lottery
3. **Credit Card Required:** Card info collected but only charged if accepted
4. **One Entry Per Person:** Only one lottery entry allowed per individual
5. **Additional Fees:** Processing fee, state sales tax, and add-on items charged upon acceptance

### Alternative Entry Methods

#### Life Time Membership
- Life Time members with Club-Access membership for 6+ months prior to lottery qualify for:
  - Early-access registration
  - Guaranteed entry into XL, 200, and 100 events

#### Volunteer Entry
- 10+ volunteer hours at UNBOUND Gravel earns guaranteed entry
- Friends and family can donate volunteer hours

#### Charity Entry
- Partner with official charity organizations
- Fundraise for a cause and receive guaranteed entry
- Cost includes entry fee plus registration and service fees

#### Training Camp Entry
- UNBOUND Gravel Training Camp offers Camp + Entry packages
- $400 minimum deposit (non-refundable)
- Includes guaranteed race entry

## Cutoff Times

### Official Time Limit
- **36 hours** from race start to complete the course
- Race starts at 3:00 PM Friday
- Must finish by 3:00 AM Sunday (36 hours later)

### Average Speed Requirement
- Cutoffs based on average speed of approximately 10 mph
- Riders who miss cutoffs will not be officially scored

### Checkpoint Considerations
- Unlike shorter distances, the XL has **no official checkpoints**
- The 36-hour limit is the primary cutoff
- Riders are responsible for self-managing their pace

## Aid Stations and Resupply

### Official Aid Stations
- **NONE** - The XL is completely self-supported
- No official checkpoints or aid stations
- No crew support allowed at any point

### Self-Supported Rules
- Riders must NOT receive outside help from support crews
- Support crews cannot provide resupply or mechanical assistance
- Riders are solely responsible for their own well-being
- If breakdown or injury occurs, riders must contact their own crew for extraction

### Resupply at Local Merchants
Riders ARE allowed to use local businesses accessible to all:
- Gas stations
- Convenience stores
- Local shops

### Key Resupply Locations (Approximate - varies by year)
1. **Eskridge** (Mile ~40-45) - Water/oasis point
2. **Alma** (Mile ~86-118)
   - Gas station at approximately mile 86
   - Stop 2 Shop (extended hours for event - until 3 AM)
3. **Alta Vista** (Mile ~112) - Water/oasis point
4. **Council Grove** (Mile ~148)

### Resupply Spacing
- Approximately 75 miles between resupply opportunities
- Riders must plan to carry sufficient supplies between stops
- Some gas stations have limited hours; plan accordingly

### Strategic Considerations
- Fast resupplies are key to competitive times
- Winners often stop only 2-4 times total
- Know layout of gas stations in advance if possible
- Be prepared for "zombie apocalypse gas station raids"

#### Ground Transportation
- Rental cars available at major airports
- Uber/Lyft available
- Lyon County Area Transportation (LCAT): $45 per trip for airport pickup
  - Must schedule in advance
  - Limited availability during event weekends

#### Local Airport
- Emporia Municipal Airport (EMP)
- 5 miles south of Emporia on Highway 99
- 5,000 ft x 100 ft runway
- Private/general aviation only (no commercial flights)

### Accommodation Options

#### Hotels
- **Hampton Inn Emporia** - ~$139/night (popular option)
- Various chain hotels in Emporia
- Book through HotelPlanner for exclusive event rates

#### University Housing
- Emporia State University Residence Halls
- Often sells out quickly (waitlist available)

#### Camping

**Lyon County Fairgrounds**
- RV and tent camping available
- Event-approved camping area

**Emporia Senior Center**
- Tent camping available

**Unbound Gravel Ranch (Hipcamp)**
- 10-15 minute drive from Emporia
- Barn shower access
- Shared bathroom
- Bike wash
- Complimentary race morning coffee, juice, water
- Breakfast options available

**Basecamp Flint Hills**
- Located in Allen, Kansas (20 miles north)
- On the Flint Hills Trail State Park
- Hammock trees and tent camping
- Bunkhouse available
- Two back-in camper sites
- Seasonal cold water shower and port-a-potty (April-October)

#### Premium Options

**RocketCamp**
- All-inclusive camp experience
- Guaranteed race entry included
- Gourmet meals by professional chef
- Large rooms with 4 single beds and full bathroom
- 2026 Dates: Check-in Wednesday May 27 (6 PM), Checkout Sunday May 31 (10 AM)

#### Alternative Lodging
- Airbnb/VRBO options (many Emporia residents host during race week)
- Homestays with local residents
- Van/car camping (some participants choose this)
- Facebook groups for short-stay options

### Lodging Tips
- **Book Early** - Accommodations sell out quickly
- Emporia population increases by 50% during race week (~12,000 visitors)
- Housing costs rise significantly during event
- Consider splitting accommodations with other riders
- Camping is most affordable but weather-dependent

## Unique Features and Characteristics

### What Makes XL Different

#### Self-Supported Format
- Unlike the 200, 100, 50, and 25-mile races which have checkpoints and allow crew support
- No official aid stations or checkpoints
- No outside assistance allowed
- Must be completely self-sufficient for 350+ miles

#### Night Riding
- Race starts Friday afternoon at 3:00 PM
- Riders spend 9+ hours riding in darkness
- Good lighting is essential
- Experience Flint Hills at night - one of the highlights
- Watch sunrise Saturday morning on course
- Many finish late Saturday night or Sunday morning

#### Sleep Deprivation
- Most riders complete in 17-36 hours
- No sleep during race for competitive riders
- Tests mental and physical endurance
- Different from multi-day bikepacking races

#### GPS Tracking Requirement
- Mandatory GPS tracker (SPOT, Garmin inReach, or Zoleo)
- Must register with Trackleaders.com
- Live tracking available for spectators and safety
- Cost included in entry fee
- Rental devices available through Trackleaders

### Race Character
- "Tests body, mind, and spirit"
- Blurs line between single-day race and multi-day bikepacking
- "Zombie apocalypse gas station raids" for resupply
- Rough terrain with challenging line choices at night
- Sharp limestone gravel can slice tires
- Remote Kansas prairie environment

### Support Crew Role (Limited)
- Cannot provide direct assistance during race
- Must be prepared to extract rider if they DNF
- Can track rider via Trackleaders
- Waiting at finish line

### Shared Finish Experience
- XL riders finish Saturday, same day as 200-mile riders
- Share the Commercial Street finish line atmosphere
- Downtown block party celebration

## Equipment Requirements

### Mandatory Equipment

#### GPS Tracker
- **Required:** SPOT, Garmin inReach, or Zoleo device
- Must be registered with Trackleaders.com
- Periodic location updates throughout race
- Rental available if you don't own one

#### Navigation
- Course is NOT marked
- GPS files provided by race organization
- Maps and cue sheets available
- GPS bike computer highly recommended

### Recommended Equipment

#### Lighting
- High-quality front lights (multiple recommended)
- 9+ hours of night riding requires reliable lighting
- Backup lights/batteries essential

#### Hydration/Nutrition
- Large water carrying capacity (75+ miles between resupply)
- Nutrition for extended riding
- Electrolytes

#### Bike Setup
- Gravel/adventure bike capable of handling rough terrain
- Wider tires recommended for limestone gravel
- Tubeless setup common
- Bikepacking bags for carrying gear

#### Tools and Spares
- Multi-tool
- Extra tubes
- Tire plugs
- Pump/CO2
- Chain quick links
- Paint stick for mud clearing (conditions dependent)

#### Clothing
- Layers for temperature changes (afternoon to night to morning)
- Rain gear
- Extra clothing for night riding

#### Other
- Portable phone charger
- Emergency contact information
- Credit card/cash for gas station purchases
