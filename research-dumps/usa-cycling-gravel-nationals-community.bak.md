# USA CYCLING GRAVEL NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "The race was really heating up when, at mile 27, a rider in the top 10 crossed wheels and went down at high speed. This created a domino effect and mass pileup. I was one of the early victims and became a landing pad for other riders and their bikes. All told maybe 20 went down, and half wouldn't continue on... I pushed on for 30 more minutes where I got to a neutral aid station, dismounted, called my wife to let her know that I was OK, and called Wayne for a pickup." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**Pete Stetina [ELITE]:** "This felt like a classic race in every sense of the word. USAC hadn't read the room... The event gave off the vibe of a race, and the riders who came were there more for the competition and less about the mass gathering." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**Bradyn Lange [ELITE]:** "It was an interesting race. It didn't start out too hard, a break had about four minutes, and then it just kind of turned into survival on the final climbs. I bridged to the final group with about ten miles to go, and there were five of us heading into the final section, which suited me well as a mountain biker. I hit it on the first kicker, got away, and held it to the finish." (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

**Lauren Stephens [ELITE]:** "The race was the hardest one we've had so far. Every climb, the group would splinter and kind of have a selection. Finally, at the southern end of the course, we were a selection of six or seven riders. What made the race was quite a muddy, slick section where a few riders went down, and that was the point that made the race. The following climb, Sarah Lange and I pulled away from Emily Newsome, and from then on we worked together all the way to the final steep cyclocross kicker, and I was able to pull off the win out of that section." (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**2023 Nebraska Course (Gering):** 131 miles with significant wind and sand pits. The course featured a steep climb 20 miles from the finish and a technical section in the final three miles suitable for attacks. Described as "fast" with enough hills and short two-track sections to break things up. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**2025 Minnesota Course (La Crescent):** 110-mile course with nearly 7,800 feet of climbing (elite/open 19+). The route is described as "relentless" with almost no flat ground — riders are either grinding up sustained 400-500 foot climbs or descending. Locals call it "champagne gravel" because of smooth surfaces in the fall, but the speed and pitch make it difficult. The course winds through hardwood forests, farm fields, trout stream valleys, and ridge-top gravel roads in the Driftless region. Final technical section features a steep climb 20 miles out and technical bits in the final 3 miles. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gravel-nationals-2025-preview-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-race-routes-watching-festivities)

**2025 Course Specifics:** Minnesota course favored tire setups closer to 40mm rather than the 2.2-inch widths common at Unbound, Big Sugar or Mid South. The 74-mile course (Juniors 15-18 and Masters 50+) has 5,000 feet of climbing. The 31-mile loop (Juniors 11-14) has 2,000 feet of climbing. (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite Race Dynamics [ELITE]:** "Without live coverage or many updates from the course... a large breakaway of seven riders got away early, with key riders like Chase Wark, the local favourite for the race, and Michael Garrison driving the pace... Lange was one of these riders who jumped across the gap to the breakaway as he moved just after the midpoint of the race, with Wertz and Daxton Mock making quick work of the gap towards the leaders." (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

**Women's Elite Selection Process [ELITE]:** The lead women's group dwindled from the whole peloton to just 7-8 riders, then was reduced to three (Stephens, Lange, Newsome) after a crash in the middle third of the course in a muddy section. Final selection happened on a steep cyclocross-style kicker near the end. (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

**Mountain Bike Skills Advantage [ELITE]:** Bradyn Lange specifically noted the final technical section "suited me well as a mountain biker" for making the winning move. The technical finale rewarded bike handling skills. (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mass Crash at Mile 27 (2023):** A rider in the top 10 crossed wheels at high speed creating a domino effect with approximately 20 riders going down, and half of those involved did not continue. This occurred early in the race at mile 27. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**Mechanical Failures Post-Crash:** Pete Stetina's bike sustained "catastrophic" damage in the crash that made it unsafe to ride aggressively even after roadside repairs, forcing his first-ever gravel DNF. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**Muddy Section Crashes (2025):** A muddy, slick section in the middle third of the 2025 course caused crashes that split the lead women's group and became a decisive race moment. (https://www.cyclingweekly.com/gravel/us-gravel-nationals-lauren-stephens-powers-to-third-straight-title-bradyn-lange-wins-with-breakthrough-performance)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Pete Stetina [ELITE] on Event Culture Shift:** "Three years ago, the idea of a sanctioned USA Cycling Gravel National Championships was a cringeworthy idea. Race organizers and racers (myself included) publicly denounced the idea and need for any such type of event. We claimed it was the antithesis of what our discipline stood for. This past weekend, most of my colleagues and I renewed our USAC licenses for the first time in years and toed the line eager to fight for a stars and stripes jersey." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**Race-Focused Atmosphere:** "The expo was quite sparse — there was a major lack of industry presence... I didn't feel the sense of impending two-wheeled adventure with the masses that one does at events like Unbound or Mid South. The official USAC 'blue shirt' vibe was present but seemed more friendly and relaxed rather than stiff and regulatory... This weekend was to be about performance only." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)

**La Crescent 2025 Community Integration:** The championship coincides with La Crescent's Apple Fest, a long-standing local celebration that includes a parade, carnival rides, and a 5K. Friday shakeout rides and an expo area add to the weekend vibe. The event depends heavily on volunteers, local bike shops, and town cooperation. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gravel-nationals-2025-preview-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-race-routes-watching-festivities)

**Spectator Experience:** Start and finish in downtown La Crescent make it spectator-friendly. Fans can reach multiple vantage points on climbs using paved roads without interfering with racers. No confirmed livestream as of 2025 preview, making in-person attendance important for viewing. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gravel-nationals-2025-preview-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-race-routes-watching-festivities)

**Prize Structure & Motivation:** The 2023 event had a $60,000 prize purse ($30k male/female) — "the biggest single day purse in gravel. Ever" at that time. USAC had never had prize money at road or mountain bike nationals before. However, Pete Stetina notes "There isn't a large prize purse driving participation here. The real reward is the stars-and-stripes jersey and the prestige that comes with winning the national championship." (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/ and https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gravel-nationals-2025-preview-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-race-routes-watching-festivities)

**Entry Requirements & Accessibility:** Unlike many mass-start gravel events, this race requires an annual USA Cycling race license; one-day licenses aren't allowed. This makes it less accessible to casual participants but ensures a field of committed racers who already compete in gravel, road, MTB, or cyclocross. (https://www.thenxrth.com/post/gravel-nationals-2025-preview-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-race-routes-watching-festivities)

**Event Safety & Organization:** The event featured the safest gravel racing experience for elite riders, with a rolling full road closure for the front group and prominent traffic control. Impressive finish line arch, fencing, signage en route, and dialed communications. Neutral aid stations were available. (https://velo.outsideonline.com/gravel/gravel-racing/usac-gravel-national-championship/)
