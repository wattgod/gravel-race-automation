# WAUCHEESI BIKE RACE — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Design by Chris Joice:**
The event features terrain through Cherohala Skyway, Cherokee and Nantahala National Forests with "long climbs, fast descents, great forest roads, rowdy gravel, and amazing vistas" (https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

**Specific Climbs:**
- Basin Gap climb (included on both 63 and 88-mile routes)
- North River Road climb (included on both 63 and 88-mile routes)  
- Farr Gap climb (88-mile route only, described as "spectacular")
- Bald River section (45-mile route)

**Elevation Profiles:**
- 88-mile "Wily" route: 11,500+ feet of climbing
- 63-mile "Wondrous" route: 8,800+ feet of climbing
- 45-mile route: 4,100 feet of climbing
(https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

**Navigation Requirements:**
"Course will not be marked" - riders must download and use provided GPX files. Maps available via RideWithGPS and raw GPX downloads. (https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Mandatory Time Cutoffs [ALL RIDER LEVELS]:**
- 24-mile split: Must reach by 1:00 PM or forced onto 45-mile route
- 45-mile split: Must reach by 2:30 PM or forced onto 63-mile route
- Overall finish: Must complete before sunset
- Lighting requirement: If completing 88-mile course and at rest stop 4 after 6:00 PM, lights are mandatory
(https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

These cutoffs indicate approximately 5 hours to reach mile 24 (assuming 8:00 AM start) and 6.5 hours to reach mile 45.

**Aid Station Coverage:**
Four fully stocked aid stops provided on course (https://thewaucheesi2025.itsyourrace.com/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Forced Route Changes:**
The mandatory time cutoffs at miles 24 and 45 serve as DNF prevention by redirecting slower riders to shorter routes rather than allowing them to continue on courses they cannot complete before sunset. (https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

**Navigation:**
With no course marking, riders dependent on GPS devices face DNF risk if devices fail without backup navigation. (https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Start/Finish Venue:**
Tellico Grains Bakery (105 Depot Street, Tellico Plains, TN) serves as race hub with:
- Police-escorted neutral roll-out from bakery
- Saturday packet pickup (6-8 PM) and race morning pickup (starting 6 AM)
- Post-race celebration with wood-fired pizzas and craft beverages
- Finish line atmosphere with spectators "cheering on finishers"
(https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html, https://thewaucheesi2025.itsyourrace.com/)

**Awards:**
Prizes given to top three finishers in each category for 88-mile and 63-mile distances. No prizes for 45-mile recreational route. (https://rapidexpeditions.com/waucheesi-bike-race.html)

**Event Merchandise:**
Optional event t-shirts available for $25, described as "high quality, super-comfy triblend that will quickly become your favorite tee" - advance ordering recommended to ensure availability. (https://thewaucheesi2025.itsyourrace.com/)
