# WILD GRAVEL — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Overview & Location
Wild Gravel is a gravel cycling and running event held in the Stirling Ranges (traditionally known as Koikyennuruff), Western Australia. The event is organized by the Shire of Gnowangerup, located approximately 3.5 hours from Perth and just over 1 hour from Albany. The inaugural event was held October 5, 2024, with the second edition on October 11, 2025.

The Stirling Ranges cover nearly 1,200 square kilometres and are a biodiversity hotspot with over 1,500 plant species. The area features the highest peaks for thousands of kilometres and is home to dozens of native mammals and many bird species. October timing coincides with wildflower season. (https://stayhappening.com/e/wild-gravel-2024-E2ISWUK2D3T)

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Karel (Trimarni Coach) [COMPETITIVE]:** "We both felt that the race was challenging but not hard. This is because we expected it to be hard and our expectations met our reality. We were mentally prepared for a very long day of riding and that helped us throughout all 9 and 11 hours of racing." - Belgium Waffle Ride NC 131-mile gravel race (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Marni (Trimarni Coach) [COMPETITIVE]:** "I couldn't stop thinking about all the riders who were in the event doing something really hard. It's so easy to become a creature of habit and avoid new or different experiences due to fear, fear of failure, worrying about what others may think or complacency... Comfort often leaves to contentment but it doesn't lead to growth." - Belgium Waffle Ride NC (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Marni (Trimarni Coach) [COMPETITIVE]:** "Around mile 70, we hit a very muddy road that clogged up the wheels until they wouldn't turn. I was with two other guys and we were walking through the mud, carrying our bikes. Thankfully I have a good amount of tire clearance so my bike was more rideable at times compared to others. But I still had to stop several times and pull the mud out of my bike (or use a stick to push it through)." - Different gravel race, but relevant to terrain challenges (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**2025 Wild Gravel 150km rider [RECREATIONAL]:** "My first 150km race. Was a pretty tough day out on the Canyon Grail, but the scenery was spectacular" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnQcaAdzHrw)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Tire Clearance - [COMPETITIVE]:** Marni noted that "good amount of tire clearance" made her bike more rideable than others during extremely muddy sections where wheels were clogging up. This was critical for being able to continue riding when others had to walk. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Bike Platform - [RECREATIONAL]:** The 150km Wild Gravel race was completed on a Canyon Grail (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnQcaAdzHrw)

## Weather Experienced

**Belgium Waffle Ride NC (June 2023) [COMPETITIVE]:** Multiple severe weather events during the race. Marni reported: "Then the first storm hit. Thunder, pouring rain... The gravel sections made me stay focused but it was hard to see with the mud and rain... After the rain stopped, I was able to find myself with another guy or two and we all rode together on and off for the next 30 miles. Then another storm hit... Finally the last storm hit. I was getting a bit cold from the rain so when the sun finally came out I was feeling much better." Three separate storm systems passed through during the 10+ hour event. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Wild Gravel Trail [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:** The year-round Wild Gravel Trail is a 440km loop that is over 90% gravel. The route begins in Gnowangerup and travels through Ongerup, past Bluff Knoll, along Stirling Range Drive, south to Mount Barker, then northward via Kendenup, Cranbrook, Tambellup, Broomehill and Katanning before returning to Gnowangerup. (https://www.gnowangerup.wa.gov.au/news/announcing-the-wild-gravel-trail-a-new-adventure-for-gravel-riders/10238)

**Clay-Based Mud Conditions [COMPETITIVE]:** Karel and others warned Marni about "a lot of mud because of the clay-like roads" - this type of mud is particularly sticky and problematic for wheel clearance. One section was described as "a very muddy road that clogged up the wheels until they wouldn't turn" requiring dismounting and carrying bikes. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Unpredictable Terrain [COMPETITIVE]:** Marni noted: "Gravel riding requires such a different mindset than on-road riding as the miles go by a lot slower at times and it's very unpredictable." She also described "taking some risks riding through some mud puddles, not knowing how deep they were. One puddle was deeper than I anticipated and I felt in the mud." (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Belgium Waffle Ride NC Specific (comparable event) [COMPETITIVE]:** The 131-mile course covered 14,692 feet of elevation, including "long uphills and flowing descends and rode on rocks, roots, gravel (of course), road, single track, sand and grass. We even biked through a barn!" Three private roads included "rocky, rooty segments [that] were so steep that we were often hiking our bikes up the unrideable sections." (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Mental Approach [COMPETITIVE]:** Karel and Marni both emphasized expectations meeting reality: "We expected it to be hard and our expectations met our reality. We were mentally prepared for a very long day of riding and that helped us throughout all 9 and 11 hours of racing... Going into the event were both a little nervous for the day because of all of the 'firsts' that we would be experiencing but as soon as the race started, we were mentally ready for whatever the day would bring." (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Breaking Down Distance [COMPETITIVE]:** Marni's strategy for 10+ hour solo sections: "I tried to stay positive by only focusing on one gravel segment at a time and trying to push the pace on the paved segments." After reaching mile 50 and realizing she was only halfway, this segment-by-segment focus became critical. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Nutrition Planning [COMPETITIVE - 10-11 hour finish]:** For the Belgium Waffle Ride NC, Marni carried: "two bottles on the frame, each with 250 calories (1 packet) of GU roctane and in my hydration pack I had 2 bottles worth of 90g each (360 calories) of NeverSecond. I also carried a pack of Powerbar chews, 2 gels and an apple cinnamon fig bar." Total: approximately 1,260 calories carried. She drank from frame bottles on smooth road sections and from hydration pack on gravel. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Pacing Reality [COMPETITIVE]:** Marni noted: "I had a few low moments after reaching mile 50, thinking that I was only half way." Long solo sections are mentally challenging - "It was hard being alone for so long as anytime I saw another rider, I was relieved to have some company." (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Navigation Issues [COMPETITIVE]:** Marni experienced GPS failure: "My Garmin had the route loaded when we started but after a few miles it would show the message that it was taking a while to find satellite. This meant that the route was no longer there." Even with a well-marked course, she noted anxiety about this: "I figured this was ok since I was with a group, the course was well marked and I assumed that eventually it would pick up the satellite." When she lost the main group and rode alone from mile 30-100+, navigation became more critical. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Mud-Induced Mechanicals [COMPETITIVE]:** Extreme mud can completely stop forward progress: "we hit a very muddy road that clogged up the wheels until they wouldn't turn... we were walking through the mud, carrying our bikes." Bikes with insufficient tire clearance were at greater disadvantage. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

**Flat Tires [COMPETITIVE]:** Marni experienced a flat tire during her event, listed as one of the "setbacks throughout" in her race report. (https://www.trimarnicoach.com/trimarniblog/tag/gravel+race)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Positioning:** Wild Gravel is described as part of Western Australia becoming "a gravel cycling hotspot" alongside established events like Seven, Backroads, and Bike it to Ballidu Gravel, all noted as "huge hits and growing quickly." (https://stayhappening.com/e/wild-gravel-2024-E2ISWUK2D3T)

**Inclusive Design:** The event explicitly caters to "all fitness levels" with 15 different participation options in 2025: Walk (5km, 12km), Run (5km, 12km, 21km half marathon, 42km marathon), Ride (12km, 30km, 60km, 100km, 150km), Run+Ride combinations, and volunteering. (https://wildgravel.com.au/)

**Official Race Status:** For 2025, the 100km and 150km distances were sanctioned as official races by Auscycling (Australia's peak cycling body). The announcement noted: "For the less competitive types, all other event distances are not officially deemed 'a race', however, if you want to try beating your friend over the line, go for it... bragging rights at the After Party will be well worthwhile🤣😉" (https://wildgravel.com.au/)

**Regional Significance:** Gnowangerup Community Gravel (the Friday warm-up event) was selected as the major regional event for 2025 WA Bike Month, featuring a "bike obstacle course, learn to ride gravel, free giveaways, free bike mechanic and more" plus timed 5km and 10km runs. (https://wildgravel.com.au/)

**FKT Attempt:** To celebrate the launch of the Wild Gravel Trail, "renowned ultra cyclist, and 2022 winner of the Indian Pacific Wheel Race, Colin Ottaway" attempted a Fastest Known Time record on the 440km trail starting April 22, 2025. (https://www.gnowangerup.wa.gov.au/news/announcing-the-wild-gravel-trail-a-new-adventure-for-gravel-riders/10238)
