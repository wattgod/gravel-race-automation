# WISH ONE MILLAU — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Rider Quotes & Race Reports

**Axel Zingle [ELITE]:** "It's more fun than the road. But it's still competition and the rhythm is the same as among the pros" (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)

**Andréa Vendrame [ELITE]:** Held the leading group as long as possible, finishing 18th overall and qualifying for the Elite category at Gravel World Championships (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)

**Julian Lino [ELITE]:** "In the US, gravel is a big part of cycling, but in Europe it was kind of a niche discipline. Now, with the UCI World Series and the creation of the UCI World Championships, gravel is growing everywhere. This makes it easier to create access for everybody." **Russell Finsterwald [ELITE]:** American rider finished third in 2023, "ventured to France to get a feel for European gravel racing coming off a sixth place in the pro men's Unbound 200" (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/gravel-world-series-carolin-schiff-182907370.html)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course changes and challenges:**
- The exact route was kept under wraps until twenty-four hours before the start due to it passing through a military zone (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)
- Route features 50/50 split between gravel and road surfaces (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)
- The course is "designed to be fast" (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)
- Includes "rustic roads, rolling tracks, single-track, forest paths and the exceptional tracks of the Larzac military camp" (https://wishonegravelrace.com/en/accueil-english/)
- "Another hard climb awaits between kilometers 30 and 40. And with technical sections throughout the course, there is little time to rest" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- Finish is a "paved 2 km climb to the finish" under the Millau Viaduct (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- In 2023, a new section called Côte du Rouquet at 59.5km from finish was where the winning attack was launched (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/gravel-world-series-carolin-schiff-182907370.html)

**Course specs by year:**
- 2023: 146km with 2,200m elevation gain for men 19-59/women 19-49; 101km with 1,600m climbing for women 50+/men 60+ (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- 2025: New 130km route (86km for women 50+/men 60+) with 2,000m D+ for 130km, 1,200m D+ for 80km (https://wishonegravelrace.com/en/accueil-english/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Elite-level pacing patterns [ELITE]:**
- 2023 men's winner Toby Perry [ELITE]: Solo attack at 59.5km to go from reduced front group, winning with 7+ minute gap in 4:34:43 total time (https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/gravel-world-series-carolin-schiff-182907370.html)
- 2023 women's winner Carolin Schiff [ELITE]: Solo effort finishing 12+ minutes ahead in 5:13:20 (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-2023/world-series-10-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/results/)
- 2024 men's race [ELITE]: Sprint finish with Florian Dauphin winning, top 3 all same time at 4:25:26 (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-2024/wish-one-millau-grands-causses/results/)
- 2024 women's race [ELITE]: Battle between Unbound 200 winners came down to 1-second sprint finish at 5:06:40 (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-2024/wish-one-millau-grands-causses/results/)

**Key tactical observations [ELITE perspective]:**
- "After the start, riders have to tackle a long and steep climb out of the valley" - early selection point (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-ush-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- "A challenging course like this will quickly split the field and rewards strong efforts" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- Significant gaps between following riders typical - "Last year, Tessa Neefjes (NLD) and Niki Terpstra (NLD) won a few minutes ahead of their opponents, while there were also significant gaps between the following riders" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Mechanical issues reported:**
- Noah Adhemard [ELITE] forced into DNF due to double puncture (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)
- Rosa Kloser [ELITE] suffered double puncture at km 95 in 2023, separating her from second place but still finished third (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-2023/world-series-10-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/results/)
- Petr Vakoc [ELITE] had "ill-timed puncture" in 2025 but fought back to fourth (https://www.cyclingnews.com/races/uci-gravel-world-series-events-2025/66-degres-sud-pyrenees-catalanes-gravel-tour/results/)

**Concentration demands:**
- "The main difference is, in their minds, the level of concentration required. While zoning out or zoning into autopilot, relatively speaking, is possible out on the road it is down right impossible when racing on the rough stuff. Gravel is unforgiving and demands concentration for every moment of the five plus hours in the saddle" (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event characteristics:**
- 600 riders from 20 countries participated in 2023 edition (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- "Only one day of racing" in 2025, "offering a more friendly and intense atmosphere between the riders" (https://wishonegravelrace.com/en/accueil-english/)
- Spectacular finish location under the Millau Viaduct, "the tallest bridge in the world with its height of 336.4 meters" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- Notable celebrity participation: "4 times F1 World Champion Alain Prost will also be participating – and not in an F1 car" (2023 edition) (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
- Race preparation included fishing in the Tarn river the day before - "When in Rome, or in Aveyron" (https://www.cafeducycliste.com/en-gb/blogs/rouler/wish-one-and-all)

**Organizer mission:**
- "At Wish One it's our mission to inspire a sustainable way of life through cycling... our goal is to inspire people to get out there and ride. Racing is part of Wish One DNA, we love the thrill of intense adventures" (https://wishonecycles.com/gravel-races/?lang=en)

**Pro rider crossover:**
- Notable pro road and gravel riders at 2023 start included "Jens Debusschere, Laurent Brochard, Adam Blazevic, Piotr Havik, Byron Munton, Arnaud Jouffroy, Svenja Betz, Puck Moonen, Carolin Schiff, Emma Ortiz Bonilla, Emma Porter, Jade Treffeisen" (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/600-riders-from-20-countries-to-discover-frances-gravel-playgrounds-in-wish-one-millau-grands-causses/)
