# WOLLOMBI GRAVEL FEST — COMMUNITY RESEARCH

## Event Format & Philosophy

**Not a Race:** Multiple sources emphasize this is explicitly "not a race" but rather "a celebration of gravel riding in the gorgeous Wollombi Valley" focused on "riding with friends, soaking up nature, indulging in food, wine, live music, and entertainment" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/). 

The official description states: "Wollombi Gravel Fest is not a race. Think of it more of a celebration of riding your bike in nature with family, friends (and celebrities) and enjoying live music, amazing beer, local wines and good food" (https://raceroster.com/events/2024/96526/wollombi-gravel-fest).

**Celebrity Presence:** The inaugural 2024 event featured gravel riding legend Tiffany Cromwell and Formula 1 driver Valtteri Bottas. The event included "Access to an up close and personal chat with Valtteri and Tiff, hosted by long time sports presenter and local Wollombian Jim Wilson" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

## Course Details

**Day 1 - Wollombi Gravel Fest Unbound:**
- Distance: 87.56 km (one source lists 90km)
- Elevation: 1,418 m vertical
- Estimated time: 3.5-4 hours
- Start time: 8am
- Route: "Starting in Wollombi we'll ride south along Great North road on paved road. At Murrays Run we'll turn left and this is where you leave the pavement behind and enjoy the quiet dirt roads. You'll travel up towards Bucketty through gorgeous paddocks and up a lovely climb. At the top you'll turn left and head into the Watagans, down Watagan Creek road and finish the first loop at Laguna. At this point you turn left and right and head over the hill into Yango Creek road and enjoy a scenic ride home" (https://raceroster.com/events/2024/96526/wollombi-gravel-fest)

**Day 2 - Boree Track Out and Back (Recovery Ride):**
- Distance: 47.61 km (one source lists 59.51 km)
- Elevation: 791-935 m vertical (varies by source)
- Estimated time: 2.5 hours
- Start time: 8:30am
- Route: "Starting in Wollombi we'll ride up Yango Creek Road to the Boree Valley Road turn-off at 9km. From there we ride out and back on Boree Valley road and back down Yango Creek road arriving in Wollombi for your recovery brunch" (https://raceroster.com/events/2024/96526/wollombi-gravel-fest)
- Flexibility: "If you want an easier ride this day, you can turn around at any time" due to the out-and-back format (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike Requirements [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
"The course is definitely a gravel bike course. It can also be ridden on a mountain bike. A road bike would not suffice in certain parts of the course particularly if the weather turns sour" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

**Recommended Equipment [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
From the official site: "Bring your bike, a spares kit and pump, proper bike clothing, any necessary medication (we are half an hour from the nearest chemist), ride food (although some will be provided). It'll be hot so ensure you have sunscreen and plenty of water and electrolytes. The spares kit on your bike should include a spare tube and tyre levers plus a chain joiner link and a tube/tyre patch kit. You'll also need a multi-tool and if you run tubeless then carry some plugs and a CO2 kit or a pump. If you use AXS then a spare battery is a good idea" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

**Onsite Support:** "We will have spare tubes, CO2 and other necessities for sale at the ride centre if you forget yours. We will also have a mechanic onsite for any last-minute bike issues" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

## Participant Requirements

**Experience Level:** The event expects participants to:
- "Be at a sufficient fitness level to complete the distance of the course and understand how to manage your equipment and food intake"
- "Be able to follow the gpx downloadable route files via strava or another means"
- "Be experienced at navigating and following a GPX route"
(https://raceroster.com/events/2024/96526/wollombi-gravel-fest)

**Who Can Enter:** "This event is open to cyclists from beginners with some off road experience through to the highly experienced. You will need a moderate level of fitness" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

**Youth Riders:** "Riders Under 18 years of age on event day must be accompanied by an adult guardian and should choose the Sunday Recovery ride as their ride of choice." Additionally, "It is not recommended that riders under 15 sign up for Day 1" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Surface Description:** The courses feature "champagne gravel" and "quiet dirt roads" through "gorgeous paddocks" in the Watagans region (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

**Traffic Considerations:** Day 1 starts at 8am specifically because "we are leaving at 8am before anybody in the valley likes to wake up and before tourist traffic will come in the opposite direction" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/). Regular road rules apply as "The roads will not be closed to traffic" (https://raceroster.com/events/2024/96526/wollombi-gravel-fest).

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**From YouTube Content [UNKNOWN LEVEL]:**
A rider described it as: "Plenty of likeminded people, good vibes, great food, ideal gravel riding terrain and fantastic organisation… this is part 1 of the 'Story Of My Ride' at the first Wollombi Gravel Fest" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CFOKk9gc4MA).

Another video describes it as "a dream weekend of cycling" and mentions "making cycling fun!" (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_9wru3B5CJo).

**Full Weekend Experience:**
The event is structured as a three-day festival:
- **Friday:** Ride check-in and pre-ride dinner at Myrtle & Stone Cafe
- **Saturday:** 90km ride followed by "smoked BBQ festival complete with live music, local wines and craft beer" in Wollombi village, open until 8pm
- **Sunday:** Recovery ride followed by brunch
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**What's Included in Entry:**
- Entry to supported rides
- Riding alongside Valtteri Bottas and Tiffany Cromwell
- Saturday post-ride sausage sizzle lunch
- Professional photography access
- Full ride insurance through AusCycling
- Rider pack with Wollombi Gravel t-shirt and musette
- Feed stops with water and snacks
- Mechanical assistance pre-ride and along the course
- Live music festival Saturday afternoon/evening
(https://raceroster.com/events/2024/96526/wollombi-gravel-fest)

## Support & Safety

**Course Support:** "Both Saturday and Sunday's course will be supported with a lead vehicle and a sweep vehicle. We will have feed zones for refreshments and communication opportunities at regular points around the course and we will have trained marshalls and first aid at major points on the course. Support is being provided by local RFS volunteers and qualified event drivers" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

**Check-in/Check-out Required:** "We ask that each rider signs on at the start of each ride and signs off when arriving at the finish to ensure we are accounting for every rider" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

**Mechanical Support:** "We will also have an event support mechanic onsite at the ride village for three days. The support mechanic will provide free last-minute service support for riders and if you have a problem they'll solve it with a range of spares including tyres, tubes & tubeless, chains and brakes for Gravel/eGravel and MTB/eMTB. Air and water will be available for your use plus emergency charging for AXS and Di2" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).

## Communication & Navigation

**Phone Coverage:** "There are large parts of the course that will have no phone coverage but there are definite patches of coverage throughout the ride. Water stops will all be able to communicate with ride headquarters and first aid if needed. Wollombi Valley has phone coverage for Telstra and Optus, however is you're with Vodafone, you're out of luck. Ride headquarters and accommodation venues have wifi" (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/).
