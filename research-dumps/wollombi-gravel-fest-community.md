## Event Overview & Format

**Not a race:** Multiple sources emphasize that Wollombi Gravel Fest "is not a race, it is a celebration of gravel, riding in nature with friends and enjoying a weekend away." (https://auscycling.org.au/events/wollombi-gravel-fest)

The organizers explicitly state: "It is not a race. Wollombi Gravel is all about the experience of riding through gorgeous roads with great people and enjoying the moment. Regular road rules apply at all times." (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Event duration:** Full weekend festival (December 20-22, 2024) featuring two days of riding plus social activities.

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Day 1 - "Wollombi Gravel Fest Unbound":**
- 87.56 km with 1,418m vertical gain
- Estimated time: 3.5-4 hours
- Start time: 8am
- Route description: "Starting in Wollombi we'll ride south along Great North road on paved road. At Murrays Run we'll turn left and this is where you leave the pavement behind and enjoy the quiet dirt roads. We'll travel up towards Bucketty through gorgeous paddocks and up a lovely climb. At the top we'll turn left and head into the Watagans, down Watagan Creek road and finish the first loop at Laguna. At this point we turn left and right and head over the hill into Yango Creek road and enjoy a scenic ride home."
- Characterized as "the challenging day through the Watagans"
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Day 2 - Boree Track out and back:**
- 47.61 km with 791m vertical gain
- Estimated time: 2.5 hours
- Start time: 8:30am
- Route: "Starting in Wollombi we'll ride up Yango Creek Road to the Boree Valley Road turn-off at 9km. From there we ride out and back on Boree Valley road and back down Yango Creek road"
- Described as "more of a relaxing ride" and a "leisurely recovery ride"
- "Out and back course allowing for riders to turn around at any time"
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Surface conditions:** The course is described as featuring "amazing champagne gravel" and "gorgeous roads" through the Watagans and Boree Valley. (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Bike type [UNKNOWN - from organizers]:**
- "The course is definitely a gravel bike course. It can also be ridden on a mountain bike. A road bike would not suffice in certain parts of the course particularly if the weather turns sour."
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Required spares kit [UNKNOWN - from organizers]:**
"The spares kit on your bike should include a spare tube and tyre levers plus a chain joiner link and a tube/tyre patch kit. You'll also need a multi-tool and if you run tubeless then carry some plugs and a CO2 kit or a pump. If you use AXS then a spare battery is a good idea."
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Additional recommendations [UNKNOWN - from organizers]:**
- Proper bike clothing
- Sunscreen (noted: "It'll be hot")
- Plenty of water and electrolytes
- Any necessary medication ("we are half an hour from the nearest chemist")
- Ride food (though some provided)
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Rider eligibility [UNKNOWN - from organizers]:**

Riders Under 18 years of age on event day must be accompanied by an adult guardian and should choose the Sunday Recovery ride as their ride of choice."

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Celebrity participation:** The 2024 event features "gravel riding legends Tiff Cromwell and Valtteri Bottas" as well as other Olympians including Olivia Gollan and Kate Bates. Participants get "Access to an up close and personal chat with Valtteri and Tiff, hosted by long time sports presenter and local Wollombian Jim Wilson." (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Weekend schedule:**
- **Friday:** Check-in (4pm-6pm), cocktails, and pre-ride dinner at Myrtle & Stone
- **Saturday:** Coffee and breakfast wrap, 8am ride start through the Watagans, followed by "Myrtle Smoked BBQ festival with local live music, craft beer, local wines" in the village (village open until 8pm)
- **Sunday:** Coffee and light snack, 8:30am recovery ride, ending with brunch by Myrtle & Stone and opportunity to explore Wollombi galleries
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/ and https://auscycling.org.au/events/wollombi-gravel-fest)

**What's included:**
- Entry to designated rides with ride insurance through AusCycling
- Safe ride conditions with marshalls, signage, and first aid facilities
- Entry to race festival grounds with live music and food
- Post-ride sausage sizzle after Saturday's ride (included in Saturday ticket)
- Ride t-shirt and goodie bag
- Bag check-in and pick-up at start/finish
- Water and feed zones along the ride
(https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)

**Event philosophy:** Described as "a celebration of gravel riding" where "The weekend is all about riding with friends, soaking up nature, indulging in food, wine, live music, and entertainment." (https://wollombigravel.com.au/wollombi-gravel-fest/)
