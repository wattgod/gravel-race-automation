**Anonymous rider [UNKNOWN]:** "This was the most fun lap because after the long climb I found a group of four I joined that worked for all of us. ## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Course Surfaces [UNKNOWN]:** "The track is basically a roadbike track. At least from a mountainbikers perspective. It was mostly asphalt or pretty smooth gravel. Only the long climb was a bit bumpy and partly wet." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Technical Level:** Described as not very technical by a mountain biker: "It was mostly asphalt or pretty smooth gravel." The main technical feature was "the long climb was a bit bumpy and partly wet." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Course Layout:** "One lap was 50 k long and had one 150 m climb in the first quarter. The rest was more or less flat." The course requires riding three rounds. (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Major Course Features:** "A major part of the course is designed at the borders of the Drau river and the connecting channel. There are two major climbs each lap with a first one after 20km and a second one towards the final after 60km each loop." (https://cyclry.com/worthersee-gravel-race-2024-highlights-report/)

**Starting Section:** "The first 6km are on paved roads slightly going up for the first 3km leaving town." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/veldamworthersee/)

**Elevation Details:** "Each lap has 740m of elevation which results in a total of 978m of climbing for the older categories doing one and a half laps while the younger categories have 1478m of elevation." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/veldamworthersee/)

**2026 Course Changes:** "More gravel, more challenge, more adventure! The course of the Wörthersee Gravel Race 2026 has been further developed... An adapted start phase ensures optimal spreading of the field and an even greater proportion of gravel." (https://www.woerthersee-gravel.com/en/competition/course/)

## Race Strategy & Pacing

**Group Riding Dynamics [UNKNOWN]:** "The first lap was more or less chaos. We started in a huge group consisting of age groups 35 to 44. I tried to follow a group but either the group was much too fast for me or way too slow. I could stay with others only for minutes." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Field Spread Over Time [UNKNOWN]:** "By now [third lap] the race was very split up. Only very small groups if any where riding together. I was alone most of the time for sure." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Winning Times 2024 [ELITE]:** Sebastian Schönberger won in 04:00:23.3, while Giada Borghesi won women's in 04:28:36.3 for the 136km distance. (https://cyclry.com/worthersee-gravel-race-2024-highlights-report/)

**Winning Times 2025 [ELITE]:** Magnus Bak Klaris won in 3:38:16 for 136km, with Geerike Schreurs winning women's in 4:32:21. (https://granfondodailynews.com/2025/04/07/uci-worthersee-gravel-results-klaris-schreurs-awesome-in-austria/)

**Power Data [COMPETITIVE]:** "NP: 247W (/65kg)" for 136km with ~1500m climbing at 31km/h average. Position: 267/854 overall, 81/235 in 19-34y age group. (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4C5vWlRVnxc)

**Fueling Issues [UNKNOWN]:** "I had 2.5 liters of Maurten Hydrogel with me, no water at all. That was a mistake... There were three aid stations on the lap. At each I took a small water cup. But that also was not enough. In the second lap I stopped quickly to fill one of my 0.7 liter bottles with water. But it was empty again way too soon." Recommends: "I should start with one 0.7 liter bottle of water and fill it up at the aid stations if it's empty." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Energy Management [UNKNOWN]:** "Also I didn't have much energy anymore. So lap three was just about getting to the finish line. This went ok, but it was not pleasant at all." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Scale:** 2024 edition sold out with participant limit increased from 1,000 to 1,200 due to overwhelming demand. 36 nations represented. (https://cyclry.com/worthersee-gravel-race-2024-highlights-report/)

**Organizer Response:** "The premiere of the Wörthersee Gravel Race was a complete success and we are overwhelmed by the positive response and the enormous interest in our event," - Roland Sint, Managing Director. (https://cyclry.com/worthersee-gravel-race-2024-highlights-report/)

**Race Director Thanks:** "Many thanks to all the athletes, helpers, spectators and the local population. You have all contributed to making the first Wörthersee Gravel Race such a success. Your enthusiasm, your support and your understanding for the temporary road closures have contributed significantly to the smooth running of the event." (https://cyclry.com/worthersee-gravel-race-2024-highlights-report/)

**Event Structure:** "Das Wörthersee Gravel Race erstreckte sich über drei Tage: Kärntens größte Radmesse, ein eigener Kinderbewerb und die Präsentation der Profis" (Three-day event including Carinthia's largest cycling expo, kids' race, and pro race presentation). (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S4F7SHM9dxU)

**Start/Finish Location:** "Start and finish are located in the city center of Velden am Worthersee at the Seecorso with a nice view on the lake." The event takes place "right in front of Schloss Velden, creating a truly unique atmosphere." (https://ucigravelworldseries.com/en/veldamworthersee/ and https://www.falkensteiner.com/en/schlosshotel-velden/experience/whats-on/worthersee-gravel-race)

**Qualification Opportunity:** Top 25% in each age group qualified for the UCI Gravel World Championships. (https://cyclry.com/worthersee-gravel-race-2024-highlights-report/)

## DNF Risk Factors

**Bike Suitability [UNKNOWN]:** One rider concluded: "flat gravel courses are not for me. That's more a confirmation than a learning. I just don't have the power to sprint out of every corner." Recommends courses "with elevation - a lot of it" for riders with similar profiles. (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Hydration Planning [UNKNOWN]:** Insufficient water planning caused issues: "I had 2.5 liters of Maurten Hydrogel with me, no water at all. That was a mistake." Aid stations provided only small water cups which were insufficient. (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)

**Peloton Skills [UNKNOWN]:** One rider noted: "I don't have enough road race experience to move around smoothly in peletons. The first weakness I could probably fix with a bit of dedicated training. But the second would only be fixable with quite a lot of road racing and I absolutely don't feel like doing that." (https://trailstories.net/2024/05/20/woerthersee-gravel-race.html)
