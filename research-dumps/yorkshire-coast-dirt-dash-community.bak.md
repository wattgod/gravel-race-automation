## Equipment & Gear Recommendations

**Jenny Graham [COMPETITIVE] (Round-the-World record holder):** Rode the Route YC (which the Dirt Dash follows) on a Sonder Titanium Camino gravel bike. Recommends gravel bikes for the versatility on these routes: "We rode on gravel bikes as we wanted the versatility, but you can find a route to suit whatever bike you have available." (https://us.alpkit.com/blogs/deeds/adventure-cycling-in-north-yorkshire-route-yc)

**Event Requirements [ALL LEVELS]:** Minimum recommended tire size of 45mm for mountain bikes & gravel bikes. E-bikes are allowed but must be declared at signup. (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

**Mandatory Kit [ALL LEVELS]:** Helmet, front and rear lights, sufficient water, emergency blanket or sleeping bag, waterproof jacket, first aid kit, phone or GPS for navigation, multitool, spare tube, tire levers, puncture repair kit, tent/bivvy bag/tarp. (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

**Camping Gear [ALL LEVELS]:** You need to bring your own bowl, mug, fork and spoon for meals at camp. (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

## Terrain Details (Rider Perspective)

**Jenny Graham [COMPETITIVE]:** "Best Climb: Yorkshire is full extreme undulation! But rounding the corner from Lockton and see the road drop into the ravine and remerge up the other side did it for me. Its maybe 'only' a 100m climb but with a hairpin bend and steep gradient it leaves you with a little taste of alpine." (https://us.alpkit.com/blogs/deeds/adventure-cycling-in-north-yorkshire-route-yc)

**Jenny Graham [COMPETITIVE]:** "Best Singletrack: Across Levisham Moor was properly lush! I loved being out of the woods and roaming across the Moors, such a unique landscape." (https://us.alpkit.com/blogs/deeds/adventure-cycling-in-north-yorkshire-route-yc)

**Official Route Description:** Terrain includes singletrack, gravel paths, forestry roads and public roads. (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

**Route Statistics:** Day 1: 58.8 km with ca. 840 m of climbing; Day 2: 59.5km with ca. 970 m of climbing. Total approximately 73 miles (118 km). (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

## Community Feel & Atmosphere

**Event Philosophy:** "The Dirt Dashes have a strong community vibe: our events genuinely feel like a big riding family." (https://bikepacking.com/event/yorkshire-coast-dirt-dash-2026/)

**Event Description:** "The Dirt Dashes are friendly, professionally designed gravel riding events that mix great routes, local character, and a strong sense of community. They're built around the idea that riders come first: many participants return year after year." (https://bikepacking.com/event/yorkshire-coast-dirt-dash-2026/)

**Format:** Described as "non-timed, self-supported reliability trial with overnight camping" - emphasizing it's NOT a race. Rules state: "It's not a race, it's a reliability trial! Stay cool, be nice, don't hurt yourself or others." (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

**Organizer Background:** Routes planned by Markus Stitz, "a seasoned route designer with multiple gravel and bikepacking guidebooks to his name" and round-the-world cyclist. (https://bikepacking.com/event/yorkshire-coast-dirt-dash-2026/)

**Start/Finish Venue:** YHA Whitby described by Jenny Graham as "magnificent" - "Sandwiched between the historic ruins of Whitby Abbey and the graveyard that inspired Count Dracula, it offers amazing views of the seaside town and North York Moors. There's a steep cobbled road (or 199steps) to follow into the old town. At night the streets twinkle so much you'd easily mistake it for something out of Harry Potter!" (https://us.alpkit.com/blogs/deeds/adventure-cycling-in-north-yorkshire-route-yc)

**Scenic Highlights [Jenny Graham, COMPETITIVE]:** "Most Yorkshire Scene: The North Yorkshire Moors Railway steam train cutting its way through the Moors to the coast as it travels between Pickering and Whitby." Best Sunrise: "A very early start saw us standing on Flamborough Head with the chalky white cliffs towering over us with the sun rising on the sea. Perfect!" (https://us.alpkit.com/blogs/deeds/adventure-cycling-in-north-yorkshire-route-yc)

**Support Provided:** Evening meal and breakfast included (vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free available on request), campsite with toilets and drinking water, cafe on site, support vehicle ("broom wagon") available if rescue needed. (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)

**Access & Sustainability:** "We highly encourage you to take the train or cycle to the event" - Whitby has regular train connections to Middlesbrough, and is on Eurovelo 12 and Route YC Adventure Route for those cycling to the start. (https://dirtdash.cc/yorkshire/)
